# THE OLD ONES PERSIST
## A LoreVault Set — 20 Moments in the Pane Where the Primordials Never Yielded

**Set identifier:** OOP-001 through OOP-020  
**Panes:** Old Ones Persist / Titans-Held  
**Tier spread:** I (Surface) ×8 / II (Echo) ×8 / III (Deep) ×4  
**Universe voice:** Greek Myth (primary, Doctrine §1.5); Enchanted Kingdom (secondary, for figures from Kuba/Vedic/Norse strata per §1.2)  
**Cultural-sensitivity advisory:** Near Eastern, Levantine, Egyptian, Kuba/Congolese, and Norse consultants required before public release.

---

## Set Premise

In most mythological traditions, the older gods were defeated or displaced. Cronus was overthrown by Zeus. Tiamat was split in two by Marduk, her body becoming the sky and earth. Anu faded as Marduk rose. El and Asherah were edged out by Baal, then overwritten by Yahweh. Ymir was dismembered by Odin and his brothers, his flesh becoming the world his killers then ruled.

This Set asks: what if none of that happened?

The **Old Ones Persist Pane** and the **Titans-Held Pane** are two related cosmological variants where the primordial generation never lost. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, the older divine pairs — Tiamat and Apsu, El and Asherah, Nun — remain in possession of the cosmos. In the Titans-Held Pane, the Olympian succession was blocked: Cronus's children remain swallowed, and the figures who would have inherited the world exist only as potentials, as qualities of light, as the name on no one's tongue.

**What this Pane is not:** It is not a world in ruin. The primordials are not malevolent in the way the younger gods' myths needed them to be — that framing was the victors' theology. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, Tiamat rests. Cronus governs. El sits at the source of the rivers. Nun waits at the edge of the created world. The world is different from what we know. It is simply sorted by other hands, or not yet fully sorted at all.

### MOMENT 01 — Kronos Archigenēs (the Unharvested)
**Pane:** Titans-Held | **Tier:** I (Surface) | **Mechanic tier:** Rare

In the Titans-Held Pane, the stone substitution never happened. Cronus swallowed his five children — Hestia, Demeter, Hera, Poseidon, Hades — and kept them swallowed. He rules from the peak of Othrys with the adamantine sickle that castrated Ouranos still at his belt. He acts on a sky-oracle's counsel: the child who would overthrow him will not be born into a world where that overthrow is necessary. He may be right.

**Lampblack Residue:** The sickle of sky-iron. The swallowing. The sky-throne on Othrys, not Olympus.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Kronos Archigenēs" — Cronus the First-Born (*archē* + *genēs*), emphasizing his temporal primacy over his children rather than his defeat by them. The name the oracle gave him, not the name his enemies wrote.  
**Art direction:** Othrys mountain at the height of the pre-Olympian age. A figure on a vast stone throne at the summit, Titan-proportioned, sickle at his belt. The sky is deep amber — no lightning, no storms, no weather of the Zeus-type. Below the mountain, the world is running correctly: Oceanus as the horizon-river, Hyperion's light that does not belong to Apollo. Cronus is watching it run. In his chest, a faint amber glow pulses slow.  
**Flavor text (Rare, A2 Prophecy):**

> *He did not swallow them in fear. The oracle said the world would be better that way. He is still checking.*

### MOMENT 02 — Tuphaon (the Storm-Body)
**Pane:** Titans-Held | **Tier:** I (Surface) | **Mechanic tier:** Rare

Zeus was never born to fight him. Typhon walks. He is not walking to destroy anything in particular. The earth is his body and the earth has motion.

**Lampblack Residue:** The hundred serpent-heads. The storms that precede him. The fire in his voice.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Tuphaon" — the older Hesiodic spelling, pre-Homeric, pre-Olympian. The name of the figure before he was rewritten as Zeus's defeated enemy.  
**Art direction:** The eastern Mediterranean coast. A storm-scale figure walking north from the Levant: upper body a man's, dark-skinned, massive; from his shoulders, serpents rather than wings; his lower body is the specific grey-green of sea-sky before a Mediterranean squall. The sea behind him is calm, because he has already passed. He does not look threatening. He is going somewhere.  
**Flavor text (Rare, A11 Anonymous Witness):**

> *He is not angry. He is not anything. The storm-body moves because movement is what storm-bodies do. — chorus, second-rank*

### MOMENT 03 — Tihamat (the Unsplit Ocean)
**Pane:** Old Ones Persist | **Tier:** I (Surface) | **Mechanic tier:** Rare

In the Old Ones Persist Pane, Ea made peace with Tiamat rather than fathering a champion to fight her. Marduk was never conceived. Tiamat rests, whole and unsplit: she is the salt sea and the sky-ocean simultaneously, above and below, still one water. The world is made differently in this Pane — flatter, less architecturally separated, the sky closer to the sea.

**Lampblack Residue:** The salt-dragon body. The water that is simultaneously above and below. The army of monsters she created for the war that did not happen here.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Tihamat" — closer to Proto-Semitic *tiham* ("deep, ocean"), more elemental and less personified than the Enuma Elish's narrative antagonist. She is what she was before the cosmogony required her death.  
**Art direction:** The primordial ocean from above: no sky, no earth, just water in every direction, with no horizon because horizon requires a boundary between sea and sky and here there is none. Deep below the surface, the outline of a dragon-body, resting — enormous and still. The water above her is the sky. The water below is the abyss. Both are the same water.  
**Flavor text (Rare, A2 Prophecy):**

> *She did not need to be killed to make the world. She only needed to rest. The world formed around her.*

### MOMENT 04 — Anum (the Unmoved Sky)
**Pane:** Old Ones Persist | **Tier:** II (Echo) | **Mechanic tier:** Uncommon

In Sumerian theology, Anu is formally supreme but practically passive — constitutional authority rather than executive. In the Babylonian period, he was eclipsed by Marduk. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, that eclipse never began. Anu has not moved. No god rules without his approval. He has not approved anyone new since he approved Enlil, who is still governing.

**Lampblack Residue:** The horned crown, the tallest. The extreme height. The still center from which legitimate authority radiates without requiring motion.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Anum" — the Sumerian form with the masculine grammatical suffix *-um*, the name before Akkadian smoothed it to "Anu." The sky-father before the system needed to diminish him.  
**Art direction:** Above the clouds, above weather, above anything reachable. A figure in perfect stillness on a lapis lazuli throne. The universe arranged below him as a diagram: rings of heavens, rings of earth, rings of underworld, all visible and all working. His eyes are open. He has not moved in ten thousand years. The horned crown is very tall.  
**Flavor text (Uncommon, A7 Hesiod Catalog excerpt):**

> *Legitimacy does not move. Things come to it. That is the definition.*

### MOMENT 05 — Nunu (the Primordial Deep)
**Pane:** Old Ones Persist | **Tier:** III (Deep) | **Mechanic tier:** Rare

In Heliopolitan cosmogony, Nun was supposed to recede as the created world expanded. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, he has not. Atum emerged, creation happened — but Nun remains present, lapping at the edges of the organized world. The desert is where Nun presses closest. Springs are where he leaks through. The world is smaller because he has not retreated to give it room.

**Lampblack Residue:** The darkness before light. The water without a shore. The absence of the differentiation that makes substance into *something*.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Nunu" — reduplication appearing in some Coptic-era sources, emphasizing the primordial redundancy of his nature: the deep that is simply deep, without analogy, because there was nothing to compare it to.  
**Art direction:** The edge of the created world — a sharp, clean boundary where the organized universe ends and formless dark water begins. On the created side: Egyptian civilization at full New Kingdom register. On the other side: the dark of before, not the dark of night (which has stars) but the dark of prior to. The boundary is a wall, not a shoreline. Very close to the wall, on the created side, the desert begins.  
**Flavor text (Rare, A4 boundary-binding form):**

> *He does not push. He waits. The created world is an arrangement inside him. He has all the time there is.*

### MOMENT 06 — Apsûm (the Sweet Water Father)
**Pane:** Old Ones Persist | **Tier:** II (Echo) | **Mechanic tier:** Uncommon

In the *Enuma Elish*, Apsu is killed by Ea/Enki — his rest disturbed by the noise of the younger gods, he proposes to destroy them, but Ea outmaneuvers and murders him. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, Ea made peace with Tiamat (Moment 03), which required first making peace with Apsu. The killing did not happen. Apsu lives beneath the earth, the vast freshwater ocean, the source of every well and spring. He is not asleep. He is listening.

**Lampblack Residue:** The freshwater beneath the earth. The well as a letter written downward. The patience of things that are very deep and very old.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Apsûm" — with the Akkadian long vowel marked, the form closer to the primary tablets. The long vowel was in the original; dropping it is a transliteration convenience that costs specificity.  
**Art direction:** Below the roots of everything. An enormous figure lying in a vast underground freshwater sea, in absolute darkness. Beard of river-weed; skin the color of deep-aquifer stone. His breath is the bubbling of springs. Above him, through layers of earth, the faint hum of civilization — building, arguing, praying. He can hear every well being dug.  
**Flavor text (Uncommon, A11 Anonymous Witness subterranean form):**

> *Every well is a letter to him. He reads every one.*

### MOMENT 07 — Aurgelmir (the World-Body Not Yet Used)
**Pane:** Titans-Held | **Tier:** I (Surface) | **Mechanic tier:** Rare

Without the Titans' defeat providing the template for younger-generation gods overthrowing the elder, Odin was never born. Ymir is alive. He is slowly metabolizing. The world is inside him, being digested.

**Lampblack Residue:** The enormous body that is also the landscape. The rivers of blood that are rivers of the world. The mountains that are his knuckles.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Aurgelmir" — Ymir's name in the older mythological strata (*Vafþrúðnismál*, Poetic Edda), used by the frost-giants themselves. The name from before the Odin-narrative was canonical; the name of the figure who was never killed.  
**Art direction:** A landscape that is a figure. The rolling hills in the far distance are his knuckles; the valley is the fold of his sleeping arm; the clouds are his breath. Very small in the foreground — structures, small fires, the suggestion of a civilization that does not know it lives on a body. No threat in the composition. He is sleeping. The world runs on the metabolic heat of his sleep.  
**Flavor text (Rare, A11 Anonymous Witness, Enchanted Kingdom voice):**

> *He does not know there are people in his valleys. He means no harm. He is simply large.*

### MOMENT 08 — Pān Gǔ (the Separator Unseparated)
**Pane:** Old Ones Persist | **Tier:** II (Echo) | **Mechanic tier:** Uncommon

In Chinese cosmogony, Pangu held the sky and earth apart for eighteen thousand years, then died to make the world from his body. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, Pangu has not yet died. He is still holding. The world is in a state of becoming.

**Lampblack Residue:** The holding. Sky in one hand, earth in the other. The eighteen-thousand-year labor.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Pān Gǔ" — tonal diacritics restored in Mandarin pinyin. *Pān*: coiled, ancient. *Gǔ*: solid, firm, ancient. The name announces its age when the tones are marked.  
**Art direction:** A colossal figure mid-effort: one hand pushing up toward a sky still forming (clouds not yet committed to a height, light not yet committed to a direction), one hand pressing down toward an earth still setting (mountains without defined edges). His face shows patience rather than strain — effort that has found its rhythm. Around him, the universe is provisional: sketched, not yet permanent.  
**Flavor text (Uncommon, A10 Last Word subtype):**

> *He is not struggling. Struggling implies doubt. He holds because holding is what he does.*

### MOMENT 09 — Saturnus Antiquus (Before the Roman Gloss)
**Pane:** Titans-Held | **Tier:** II (Echo) | **Mechanic tier:** Uncommon

The Roman identification of Saturn with the Greek Cronus produced one of antiquity's most successful theological glosses — the jovial festival-figure of the Saturnalia. In the Titans-Held Pane, that identification never happened. The original Italian Saturn, before Rome made him approachable, is the time-and-devouring force whose periodic return was not celebration but propitiation. The festival was not joy. The festival was tribute paid so the old power would not take more than the harvest.

**Lampblack Residue:** The sickle. The devouring. The reversal of order — which is not festivity but the return of a prior order that was never fully defeated.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Saturnus Antiquus" — the Ancient Saturn, the epithet marking the pre-festival, pre-gloss form; what the cult contained before Rome found it convenient to soften.  
**Art direction:** A hillside in pre-Roman central Italy. A figure with the standard Saturn iconography — sickle, agricultural associations — but in iron rather than gold, Titan-proportioned rather than humanized. Around him, people who are not celebrating. They are bringing the harvest in silence. The sickle has recently been used. The offerings are made with the attentiveness of people who are not yet sure the old power has accepted them.  
**Flavor text (Uncommon, A4 Curse form):**

> *The Romans made him safe. Safety is a lie told to children. The sickle remained.*

### MOMENT 10 — Brahma Prajapati (the Creator Not Displaced)
**Pane:** Old Ones Persist | **Tier:** II (Echo) | **Mechanic tier:** Uncommon

In classical Hindu theology, Brahma was eclipsed by Vishnu and Shiva — there are very few Brahma temples; his active role in the cosmos is minimal. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, that diminishment never occurred. Brahma still creates actively; the universe is in its early stages; nothing has been made permanent.

**Lampblack Residue:** The four faces, each looking in a different direction, each speaking a different thing into existence. The lotus. The creation-act ongoing.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Brahma Prajapati" — using the older Vedic epithet (*Prajapati* = Lord of Beings/Progenitor) that predates the Trimurti systematization. The creator before the division of functions into three offices. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, that division has not happened — which means nothing is yet permanent.  
**Art direction:** A four-faced figure surrounded by an active first hour of creation: from one face, animals mid-form; from one face, elements not yet committed to their natures; from one face, a language being invented in real-time; from one face, the absences that give the present-things their edges. He is not finished. The universe around him is provisional — sketched, waiting for revision.  
**Flavor text (Uncommon, A2 Prophecy form):**

> *He has not decided the shapes of things yet. He is still speaking. This is the most dangerous moment.*

### MOMENT 11 — El-Elyon and Athirat (the Original Couple)
**Pane:** Old Ones Persist | **Tier:** III (Deep) | **Mechanic tier:** Legendary

El and Asherah are the original divine couple of the Canaanite-Ugaritic world, documented in the tablets at Ras Shamra (ca. 1400–1200 BCE): El at the source of two rivers, white-bearded, patriarch of the divine council; Asherah (Athirat in Ugaritic), Lady of the Sea, creatrix of the gods, mother of seventy divine children who each govern one of the seventy peoples of the earth. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, neither Baal's mythological dominance nor Yahweh's theological displacement ever happened. The Asherah poles still stand. The sacred groves are not cut down.

**Cultural-sensitivity note:** El's name is the same word as the Hebrew word for God and appears in Elohim, El Shaddai, El Elyon. This is historically accurate and academically documented. The card should not frame these figures as "the real story behind the Bible" or in ways dismissive of Abrahamic communities. Near Eastern and Levantine cultural consultants required before lock.

**Lampblack Residue:** The white beard and the two rivers at their source. Asherah walking on the sea. The seventy children who are the seventy peoples.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "El-Elyon" and "Athirat" — using El's "Most High" title (*Elyon*, which appears in the Hebrew Bible) and Asherah's specifically Ugaritic name (Athirat, from *'ṯrt*), both found in the primary Ugaritic tablets. The forms from the Canaanite sources themselves, before the Greek and Roman intermediaries.  
**Art direction:** The source of two rivers in the Lebanon-Syria borderlands — the location the Ugaritic texts gesture toward as El's dwelling. Late afternoon. An old man, white-bearded, seated at the confluence in complete rest. Beside him, a woman in robes the color of deep Mediterranean water. Around and behind them, receding into distance: seventy figures of different human types — different sizes, faces, garments — each clearly going somewhere. The divine couple looks at the rivers. The water speaks for them.  
**Flavor text (Legendary, A2 Prophecy / A7 Hesiod Catalog):**

> *Of the divine couple at the source of the rivers were born seventy children. Each governed one people. The water spoke for them both.*

### MOMENT 12 — Bumba (the Vomiting Creator)
**Pane:** Old Ones Persist | **Tier:** III (Deep) | **Mechanic tier:** Rare

From the Kuba people (Kongo Basin): Bumba was alone in the darkness and had a stomachache. He vomited up the sun, then the moon and stars, then nine kinds of creature, then humans, then trees, then tools. Creation as expulsion from self: the world is everything Bumba contained, made external. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, Bumba has not fully emptied himself. He is still walking. He is still creating.

**Design note:** The vomiting-as-creation is the Kuba people's most serious cosmological assertion — that creation is a form of giving from within the self. The art must be luminous, not gross or comedic. Kuba/Congolese community consultation required before lock.

**Lampblack Residue:** The whiteness. The luminosity of what is expelled. The creator becoming lighter as the world becomes heavier.  
**Pratchett Tweak:** "Bumba" is given its proper Kuba context rather than an exoticizing gloss. The name is not adjusted; the framing is the tweak — a figure of the same cosmological weight as Tiamat, Ymir, and Pangu, working through a different mechanism: not death, not holding, but giving from inside the self.  
**Art direction:** A white figure against absolute darkness, mid-motion. Something luminous emerging from him — not depicted as nausea but as the parting of something interior and very bright, suggesting stars and comets more than anatomy. Darkness behind him beginning to be illuminated: points of light in the deep background, each one a world-element still coming into being. His posture suggests relief — the relief of something necessary being completed. He is lighter than he was. The darkness is lighter than it was.  
**Flavor text (Rare, A11 Anonymous Witness / cosmogonic witness form, Enchanted Kingdom voice):**

> *The world is everything he had inside him. He is lighter now. The world is heavier. This seems fair.*

### MOMENT 15 — The Wandering Merchant (Odin Before the Well)
**Pane:** Titans-Held | **Tier:** II (Echo) | **Mechanic tier:** Uncommon

### MOMENT 18 — The Intact Sophia (Wisdom Without a Fall)
**Pane:** Old Ones Persist | **Tier:** III (Deep) | **Mechanic tier:** Rare

In the Gnostic cosmological tradition, Sophia (Wisdom) fell from the Pleroma through unauthorized desire, and from that fall the material world was accidentally generated — she was scattered, grieving, made into the world. In the Old Ones Persist Pane, the Pleroma was never disrupted. Sophia did not fall. She is whole. She has never wept, never been scattered, never been made into the world. The world, in this Pane, has no Sophia-fragment in it — and is therefore, in some ways, a lesser world.

**Lampblack Residue:** The luminous completeness. The dove. The untested wisdom that has never been dismembered or fallen.  
**Art direction:** A figure of absolute luminous completeness — not the grief-struck, scattered Sophia of the Gnostic texts but her opposite: whole, radiant, unbroken. The art should be somewhat unsettling. Completeness in this tradition should look provisional and hard-won, not absolute. She looks complete in the way a thing looks complete that has never been used. Everything is in its place. Nothing is wrong. This is the problem.  
**Flavor text (Rare, A10 Last Word inverted):**

> *She has never fallen. She has never wept. She has never been made into the world. The world wonders what it missed.*

### MOMENT 19 — The First People (Made Without the Children)
**Pane:** Titans-Held | **Tier:** I (Surface) | **Mechanic tier:** Common

In most mythological traditions, humans were made by younger gods — Prometheus gave them fire, Enki gave them the blood of a god, Marduk directed their creation. In the Titans-Held Pane, those younger gods were never born. Humans were made by Epimetheus, who had already given all the useful gifts to the animals — strength to the lion, speed to the antelope, armor to the tortoise — before he got to humans and had nothing left. So humans are naked, slow, fragile, and short-lived. But Epimetheus did not take them back. So here they are.

**Lampblack Residue:** The nakedness. The insufficient gifts. The persistence in spite of the insufficient gifts.  
**Art direction:** A group of early humans on a cold hillside. Visible in the background: better-equipped animals going about their business, indifferent. The humans are huddled together, not in defeat but in the specific posture of people who have decided that nearness to each other is their gift. One of them has found fire. She is holding it carefully and not showing the animals.  
**Flavor text (Common, A11 Anonymous Witness, Enchanted Kingdom voice):**

> *Epimetheus gave everything away, then made them anyway. They are grateful anyway. There is something in that.*

### MOMENT 20 — The World Not Yet Sorted (cross-Pane)
**Pane:** Old Ones Persist / Titans-Held | **Tier:** III (Deep) | **Mechanic tier:** Legendary

A cross-Pane card. In every Pane where the Old Ones Persist, there is a shared quality: things are not yet settled. The sky and earth are not fully separated. The living and the dead share more space. The gods and their domains overlap and argue. No succession has sorted function into office. The world is older — and in being older, it is stranger, more various, less administered. The primordials have not died to become the world, so the world is not yet made from their bodies. It is still being decided.

**Lampblack Residue:** The unsettled. The pre-classification. The potential that has not yet become a specific thing.  
**Art direction:** A landscape still negotiating itself — not chaotic but provisional. The mountains and the sea have not yet decided where their boundary is. The sky is too close to the ground in one section and too far in another. Animals are larger than they should be. Light comes from multiple directions at angles that do not correspond to a single sun position. In the foreground, right at the edge, a small human figure is looking at the landscape. The expression is not fear. It is the expression of someone looking at something very large that has not yet decided what it is.
