## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He opened the journal to May 3 and closed it.

He had the journal on the table. He was well enough to sit at the table. He had taken it out of the coat (the coat had been under his pillow through the fever; it was now in the wardrobe; he had moved it at the same time he moved himself from the bed to the chair, as though both were the same kind of recovery). He opened it to the first entry: *3 May 1897. Left Munich at 8:35 PM on 1st May...*

He closed it.

He did not believe, at this moment, that he could read it. He was not certain whether the reason was illness, or something specific to the content, or something he could not name yet. He would not attempt to distinguish these for now.

He wrote at the back: *31 July — opened the journal to May 3. Closed it. Not ready. The journal is present.*

He noted: *not ready* was the complete statement. He had no more precise formulation. He wrote it in the back of the journal so that the front remained as it was.

## Tether Note

*Not ready* — Jonathan opens the journal to the first entry and closes it. The decision not to read is written at the back, not the front — so the front remains in sequence, the decision appended rather than inserted. "Not ready" is the least analytic phrase in the journal; no sub-clauses, no qualifications, no three hypotheses. One condition, acknowledged. Echo E-GH1-085: "not ready" — the journal closed at the first entry; the decision written at the back; the most compressed self-assessment in the record.
