## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He returned to the library with a different purpose.

He had looked at the dates on his first visit. Now he looked at the subjects. History, yes. Law, yes. But in the back section, which he had not reached before because of the failing light: maps. Large-format, linen-backed maps of English counties, English parishes, English railway lines. Stacked flat in a wide lower shelf, too large for the standard bookcase. He pulled them out carefully.

They were organized. Not by county — by acquisition. Each map had a pencilled notation in the same hand as the library annotations he had seen before: a date, a figure, sometimes a name. He turned through them until he found the ones with names.

Carfax.

He recognized it from the documentation Mr. Hawkins had sent for review. The Carfax estate — the property the Count was purchasing, the reason Jonathan was here. He looked at the map. The estate was circled lightly in the same pencilled hand. A notation at the margin read: *near London, Purfleet. Distance from centre: see attached.* There was nothing attached.

He put the map back precisely where he had found it. He sat at the library table. He wrote: *22 May — maps. Carfax.*

He stopped writing.
