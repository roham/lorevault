## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He wrote the brief again.

He had written it on June 1 and burned it: the summary of material facts, May 3–30, in the professional shorthand. He had burned it because it was right, and a right document was a dangerous document.

He wrote it again, longer now — May 3 through June 11. He wrote it in the same form: dates, observations, inferences marked as inferences, sources noted. He wrote it carefully. It took ninety minutes.

He did not burn it.

He had thought, on June 1, that the danger of a right document was that it could be found. He had changed his mind. On June 1 he had believed there was still a difference between the document existing and the thing it described existing. He no longer believed this. The thing it described existed whether the document existed or not. What the document was, now, was a record that had been made. A record that could not be un-made.

He folded it and put it inside the back cover of the journal.

He wrote: *12 June — second brief. Kept.*

---

## Tether Note

*The kept brief* — the inversion of June 1. Jonathan now understands that destroying the document does not change the reality it describes; what mattered about the document was that it had been written, not that it could be found. The second brief is a statement of intent: Jonathan wants there to be a record, not because it will save him, but because the act of making a record is the last thing he can do that is entirely his own. The brief is inside the journal — the only document Jonathan controls.
