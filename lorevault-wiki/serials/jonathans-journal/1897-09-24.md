## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He went to the office.

He had been to the office during the week — on the twenty-third he had gone back after writing the condolence letter. But Thursday had been a partial day, and Friday had been quiet. Today he went in for the full morning and afternoon.

He worked through the Hawkins correspondence and an instruction that had arrived on Saturday. The work was routine. He did it correctly. He did not find himself reviewing his own work more than once on any of the matters; the checking habit was not activated today.

He noted in his journal: *24 September — full day at the office. Correspondence clear. Work routine.*

He looked at this entry. He thought about whether to add anything — there was a context to the day, the week that had preceded it, the letter he had sent on Friday. He decided not to add anything. The professional entry was the correct form for a professional day. The other things were in the other entries.

He found that having done the other entries made the professional entry easier to write cleanly.
