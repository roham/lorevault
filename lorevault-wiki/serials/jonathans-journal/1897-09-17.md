## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He was on the platform at quarter past six.

The train was on time. He did not look at his watch between arriving on the platform and the train pulling in. He was aware, at some point, that he had not looked at his watch. He did not look at it then either.

Mina was on the train. She came down from the carriage and he was there and they stood on the platform in the ordinary way of two people who have been apart for three weeks. He carried her bag. They walked home from the station.

He noted in his journal that evening: *17 September — Mina arrived, quarter past six. On time. Did not time the interval on the platform.* He paused. He added: *Did not time it.* He read this back. He had written it twice — once as a negative embedded in the sentence, once as a standalone confirmation. He left both.

He found it worth saying twice: he had been on a station platform waiting for someone and he had not timed the wait. This had not been the case on every platform since May. It was the case today. He noted it both ways to be sure it was in the record.
