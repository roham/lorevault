## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He returned to the chapel on the twenty-seventh.

He went to confirm the count and to examine the soil more closely — the professional practice of verification, returning to the site with the purpose of checking the prior observation rather than extending it. He moved through the crates. In the far rank, in a crate whose lid had been partially replaced but not secured, he found the Count.

He was lying in the soil, dressed as Jonathan had seen him dressed at their first meeting in the castle. His eyes were open. He appeared, from this position and at this distance, to be neither sleeping nor waking but something that was not either. He was not dead — the term was not quite right; what Jonathan observed was an absence of the conditions that would make death a meaningful description.

Jonathan stood still for some time. He did not touch anything.

He left and returned to his rooms by the same route.

He wrote: *27 June — the Count is in one of the crates. The crate is in the chapel. I did not disturb it. I do not know what I have seen. I am recording the fact.*

He had been recording facts since May 3. The fact-record was very long. He had arrived at the limit of what fact-recording could hold.

## Tether Note

*The figure in the box* — Jonathan finds the Count lying in a crate of earth in the chapel. His response is the same as June 23 (the lizard descent, E-GH1-047): he records the fact, notes he does not know what he has seen, and declines to disturb anything. But the sentence that closes the entry — "I have arrived at the limit of what fact-recording can hold" — is a new statement. Jonathan has never before acknowledged a limit to the documenting practice. The professional register's capacity to contain the Castle is failing.
