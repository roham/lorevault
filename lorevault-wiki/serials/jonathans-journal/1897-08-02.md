## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He had a memory of a morning in the Castle that he could not find in the journal.

He had not read the journal (July 31: not ready). But he had a specific memory — a morning, early in his stay, when the light had come through the east window at an angle that created a particular shadow on the floor, and he had stayed very still watching it. He did not know when this was. He had no journal entry for it; he could not have located it even if he had been reading.

He wrote: *2 August — a memory with no entry. A morning, the light angle, the shadow, being very still. I do not know when. Not in the record.*

He noted: the record had been the accurate document. But the record could only contain what he had chosen to write. There were mornings in the Castle that he had lived and not written about. The journal was accurate within its scope; its scope was not complete.

He had known this. He had not previously written it down. He wrote it now.

## Tether Note

*The memory without an entry* — Jonathan has a memory he cannot locate in the journal: a morning, the light angle, staying still. The journal did not capture it because he did not write it. The journal is accurate within its scope; the scope is not complete. This is the inverse of July 13's "record without its memory" (E-GH1-067) — there, he had an entry he could not verify from memory; here, he has a memory he cannot find in the record. Both reveal the gap between the documentation practice and the life it documented. Echo E-GH1-087: "the memory without an entry" — the morning not written; the journal's scope acknowledged as incomplete; the inversion of E-GH1-067.
