## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

His letters said he had left.

The three letters he had copied on June 21 — in his own hand, in his own professional script — included one that said he had departed safely and arrived in Bistritz in good health. He did not know when that letter had been sent or whether it had arrived. He did not know if Mina had read it. He did not know if Mr. Hawkins had read it.

He knew that whoever had read those letters believed he was gone from the Castle, was well, and had reached safety.

He was in the Castle. He was in his room. It was June 30.

He wrote: *30 June — the letters say I left. I did not leave. There is now a discrepancy between the documentary record and the fact. The documentary record will be believed. The fact is only in this journal.*

He considered this. He had spent fifty-eight days maintaining a professional record of events as they occurred. The record was accurate. But the letters — in his own hand, sent by the Count — had overwritten the record in the view of anyone not in this room. The journal was the only place where June 30 existed as what it was.

The journal was the last accurate document.
