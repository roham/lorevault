## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He had kept a small notebook in May.

In May, traveling east by train, he had copied into the notebook a list of place names — the towns on the route, the names of stations, the larger cities. He had done this from habit, or professional instinct, or the general disposition toward documentation that had characterized him before he understood what documentation was for.

He found the notebook in his bag. The names were there in his May handwriting — clean, the hand before the Castle. He read them in order: the towns running east, Linz and Salzburg and then onward, the sequence he had traveled going toward Transylvania.

He was now traveling through the same places in the reverse order. He looked out the window at the station boards as the train slowed, and then looked at the notebook, and confirmed the correspondence. The names matched. They were in the correct reversed sequence. The route was the same route.

He does not think the place names remember him. He does not expect them to. A town on a railway line does not maintain records of individual passengers. He is the one who maintained the record. He finds that his record was accurate.

He wrote: *14 August — through Austria on the return route. The station names match the notebook. The route is the same route.*

He considered writing more and then found he had written the accurate amount. He closed the journal. Austria continued outside the window, being accurately itself.
