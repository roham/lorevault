## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The castle was quieter than usual.

Not the absence of noise he had expected to hear and did not — that had been the pattern since May. This was different: some sounds that had been present throughout, that he had not consciously catalogued because they had been continuous, were now absent. A low ambient resonance from the eastern part of the castle, which he had registered as background and stopped attending to, was gone.

He could not have described it when it was present. He could describe its absence precisely.

He wrote about it at length in the journal — far more than the event warranted — because he was aware that he was attempting to document the sound by documenting its absence, and that this was the first time the journal had been required to document a negative in this way.

He wrote in his journal: *10 June — silence changed.*

## Tether Note

*The catalogued absence* — Jonathan can describe what he did not notice precisely when it disappears. The background resonance was below the threshold of conscious attention; its absence is above it. He writes at unusual length because he is trying to reconstruct, from the shape of the absence, what the sound was. The journal is now documenting a negative: the record of something that was there and is no longer.
