## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

That afternoon the Count asked Jonathan what he had been writing.

Jonathan said: letters home. To my employer, to my betrothed.

The Count said: show me.

Jonathan said: they are private correspondence.

The Count said: show me.

What Jonathan wrote in the journal afterward: *He wished to see my letters. I showed him the professional letters. He seemed satisfied. He did not ask again.*

What Jonathan did not write: the letter to Mina was in his coat pocket throughout. He had not posted it because he had not found the post box, and he had not asked where the post box was because he was not sure he wanted to know what the answer would be. He showed the Count the professional correspondence. The Count read the three pages about the property conveyance with what appeared to be complete attention. He handed them back. He said: *Good.* He said: *You are almost finished.* He said: *Then you will be free to go.*

Jonathan wrote: *He seemed satisfied.*

He is becoming a careful editor of his own experience.

## Tether Note

*He will be free to go* — the Count's phrase, never in doubt before today. The installment's Spine-event: Jonathan has been assuming departure is possible; this is the first moment he consciously withholds information in the presence of the Count. He is now two kinds of prisoner: the physical kind (doors he hasn't tried yet) and the epistolary kind (a journal that no longer tells the whole story). Per BS-1 Deep thread D-BS1-002 (Withheld Record Pattern): the pattern of suppressed evidence runs in both Jonathan and Holmes. The character who knows more than they write is a Lattice-level Spine event, not a universe-specific one.
