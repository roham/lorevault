## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He woke with Mina's presence clear.

He could not say whether it had been a dream. The soft intervals made the distinction imprecise. What he had on waking was not a specific image or a voice but a presence — a quality that he associated with her and had not experienced since Exeter.

He sat up and reached for the journal immediately, because he understood that the precision of the experience was already fading as he moved. He opened to a page. He wrote the date.

By the time he had written the date, the specific content had softened. He could tell that it had been present; he could tell it was now less present. He could not recover the substance of it.

He sat with the journal open for some time. He wrote: *Mina.* Then he crossed it out. Not because it was wrong, but because a name without its content was not a record; it was a gesture.

He wrote in his journal: *13 June — present on waking. Lost in the writing.*

## Tether Note

*Lost in the writing* — for the first time, the act of writing is what dissipates the thing Jonathan is trying to record. The presence was clear; the movement toward the journal began the fading. The journal as the instrument for preserving experience is, in this case, the instrument by which the experience is lost. The "intervals soft" from June 9 have now produced an effect that the record cannot capture; the record only marks the failure.

The crossed-out "Mina" is the most intimate gesture in the journal — a name written and then erased not because it was wrong but because it was insufficient.
