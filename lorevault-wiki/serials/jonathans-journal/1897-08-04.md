## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

A letter arrived from Mr. Hawkins.

He had written to Mr. Hawkins — he could not precisely remember when; during the fever, or just after; he had a memory of writing and of Sister Agatha taking something to post. The letter from Mr. Hawkins was a reply, which meant the interval between Jonathan's writing and the reply had been shorter than he had estimated. Mr. Hawkins must have replied by return.

The letter said: he was relieved to hear from Jonathan. He had received the advance letter (one of the June 21 copies in the Count's hand) saying Jonathan had left the Castle safely; he had also received, subsequently, a letter in Jonathan's hand from Bistritz that had caused him considerable concern. He was glad Jonathan was under good care. He should rest. He should write when he was able.

Jonathan read this twice.

He wrote: *4 August — letter from Mr. Hawkins. He received the advance letter (the Count's) and then my Bistritz letter. He was concerned. He is glad I am being cared for.*

He noted: Mr. Hawkins had received both letters — the false one (June 21, E-GH1-045) and the true one. He had noticed the discrepancy. He had not mentioned it in his reply. He was a solicitor's principal; he knew when to note a discrepancy without naming it.
