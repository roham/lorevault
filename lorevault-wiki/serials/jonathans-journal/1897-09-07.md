## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He did not go to the office.

He read in the morning and wrote correspondence in the afternoon. The house was quiet — the chosen kind, as he had named it. He wrote his journal entry in the evening and found it was longer than usual.

He reviewed what he had written. He had written: about the morning and the book he was reading; about the correspondence and what it covered; about the Lucy entries and the unsent paragraph; about the September 1 count of forty-one days and what he thought about it now; about the difference between the journal in the Castle (the record, the instrument, the evidence) and the journal now (ordinary documentation, with occasional paragraphs that belong to it and nowhere else). He had written three pages.

He has not written three pages in the journal since August 11 — the marriage entry — and before that, not since mid-June, when he was writing the Castle entries in compressed urgency.

He noted at the bottom of the entry: *7 September — longest entry since returning. Nothing happened today. I had more to write.*

He thought about whether this was a good sign. He thought it probably was. The journal was the correct place for things, and he had more things, which meant there were more correct things. He did not write this because it was getting circular. He closed the journal.
