## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The realization came in the afternoon, not the night.

He had tried the second door — the one at the north end of the library corridor, the one that looked as if it might lead outside. It was locked. He tried the window above the writing desk: sealed from outside. He tried the passage near the kitchen, which smelled of cold air and which he had assumed was a servants' exit: locked. He tried the main door through which he had originally entered.

The lock was not a latch. It required a key he did not have and had never been given.

He sat down at the writing desk in the library. He picked up the pen. He wrote: *I am a prisoner.* He looked at the line. He thought: I have been a prisoner since the second night, at least. Possibly the first.

He thought: I was not told this.

He thought: I was told *you will be free to go.*

He thought: this is a specific kind of lie. Not the kind that says the wrong thing. The kind that says the right thing at the wrong time, which is the same result by a different method.

He wrote: *and the night is closing in.*

He put down the pen. He did not write anything else that day. He had said what could be said.
