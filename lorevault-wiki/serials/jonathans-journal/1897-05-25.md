## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He found a note tucked into the back pages of a volume of English property law — the third edition, 1883.

It was on a separate sheet of paper, folded once. The ink was old and the handwriting was not the Count's — it was a different hand entirely, smaller and more careful, with the abbreviations of a professional. It read: *Freehold clear. Title examined 1884. Adjacent ground: separate deed, not included in conveyance. Advise client to hold. — R.M.S.*

He read it three times. He took out his casebook and copied it, word for word. He noted the initials: R.M.S. He did not know who R.M.S. was. He noted the date: 1884 — three years before Mr. Hawkins had taken on the Carfax assignment; before Jonathan had joined the firm; possibly before Mr. Hawkins had been engaged on this matter at all.

He thought: there was an English solicitor here before me. He thought: that solicitor left a note but not a name.

He wrote in his journal: *25 May — note in property volume. R.M.S. 1884.*

He thought: I should find out what happened to R.M.S.

## Tether Note

*The predecessor* — Jonathan has found evidence of a previous English solicitor sent on the Count's business in 1884 — before Jonathan's firm was engaged. The initials R.M.S. and the professional notation suggest a competent practitioner who left a careful note and nothing else. Jonathan notes he should find out what happened to R.M.S.; the reader will understand that the absence of further records is itself an answer. This is the installment's Lampblack: the missing solicitor, documented only by initials and a single note, is the echo of Jonathan's own trajectory. The predecessor who left no more records.

Cross-Pane: the pattern of a professional who followed the standard process and left a competent record that no one came to retrieve is a Lattice-level type. The witness who completes the paperwork and disappears from the record.
