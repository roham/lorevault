## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He read back through the journal.

Not for the first time — he had read it retrospectively on July 1, from the beginning, and named the arc. Today he was looking for a specific entry. He had a memory of writing something on May 28 — the chapel discovery, the first time he had been below, the arched nave. He found the entry and read it.

He could not confirm that he had actually done what the entry described.

The entry was in his handwriting. The detail was specific — the dust on the flagstones, the broken window above the east door, the smell of old wood and damp stone. He had no reason to doubt it. And yet he could not, reading it now, feel the memory that should have accompanied the facts. The facts were there; the experience they described was not retrievable.

He wrote: *13 July — read the May 28 entry. The entry is accurate, I believe. I cannot verify it from memory. The memory and the record are not in correspondence.*

He noted: the journal had been the accurate document. He had called it that on June 30 (the last accurate document). He was less certain of this now.

The record and the memory are no longer in correspondence. He named the journal the last accurate document on June 30 (E-GH1-054); on July 13 he qualifies that claim.
