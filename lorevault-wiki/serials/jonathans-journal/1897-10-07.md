## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He put the old journal back on the shelf.

He had read it three evenings this week: October 3, 4, and 6. He had read March, April, and May 1. The journal had now been read up to its threshold.

He noted in the current journal: *7 October — old journal placed back on the shelf. Read: March, April, May 1. The next entry is May 3, 1897. I have not read May 3.*

He sat with this notation. He thought about May 3, 1897, which is a date he knows exactly: Munich, the Hotel Quatro Stagioni, the Golden Krone diligence arranged for the following day, the first entry written in what would become the Castle journal. He had not read this entry since he wrote it. He did not know if he had the memory of it in his own voice or if his memory had been shaped by what came after.

He closed the current journal.

He wrote one more line before closing it: *May 3 is the next entry. I will read it when I am ready. Not yet has become soon.*

He put both journals back on the shelf. He thought that *soon* was a new category — not a timeline, not a commitment, but a different quality of not-yet. He noted this and closed the journal.
