## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He picked up the first Castle journal again.

He had held it for three minutes on September 15. Today he held it for longer — he did not count, this time; he let the time pass without measuring it. He looked at the cover. He turned it over and looked at the back cover. He opened it to the first page, which has the date range written in the front: *March–May 1897* on the flyleaf in his pre-Castle handwriting. The inscription stopped there. He did not turn to the May 3 entry.

He closed it and put it back.

He noted in his journal: *19 September — held the first journal again. Longer than three minutes. Opened to the flyleaf. Did not read past the flyleaf. Not yet.*

He looked at that last phrase: *not yet.* He had not written "not ready" as he had in August; he had written "not yet," which is different. *Not ready* means the state is insufficient. *Not yet* means the time has not arrived. He thought this was an accurate distinction.

He found that the day was otherwise ordinary and that the otherwise ordinary day was a form of wealth he intended to note.
