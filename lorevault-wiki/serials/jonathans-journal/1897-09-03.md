## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Another letter from Mina.

Lucy is worse. The pallor Mina described on September 2 is more pronounced; she tires after short walks now; she has little appetite. And: she woke in the night and walked in her sleep — Mrs. Westenra found her at the window, still asleep, looking out at the harbor. Mrs. Westenra is alarmed. Dr. Seward, who has been attending, has written to his teacher in Amsterdam — a Professor Van Helsing, who has a wide practice in unusual cases. Mina does not say unusual in what sense. She says Van Helsing may come to see Lucy if Dr. Seward thinks it necessary.

He read this once. He read it again.

He wrote in his journal: *3 September — second letter, Lucy's condition. Pallor worsened. Walking in sleep. Dr. Seward has written to Van Helsing, Amsterdam. Possible visit pending.* He paused. He wrote: *Sleepwalking: new category. Not in the previous reports.*

He recognized that he was noting the new symptom as new — distinguishing it from the established presentation, the way one notes a change in a document's status. He looked at what he had written and found it accurate. He did not know what sleepwalking meant in this context. He noted that he did not know.
