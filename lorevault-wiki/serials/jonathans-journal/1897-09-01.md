## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

September.

He wrote the date at the top of the new entry — *1 September 1897* — and noted that it was the first day of September, which he has always used as a recalibration point. New month. Review of the preceding month; projection for the new one.

He had done the professional tally yesterday. He sat now with the journal and did the other kind of tally.

He had left the Castle on July 22. July 22 to September 1: forty-one days. He counted this twice. The arithmetic was consistent. Forty-one days since the Castle.

He noted this in the journal: *41 days since leaving the Castle on July 22.*

He looked at the number. It is a larger number than he had expected when he was in the Castle counting the days. In the Castle, when he had set the terminus for himself — I will leave on July 22 — he had not calculated forty-one days in the future, because forty-one days in the future had not yet existed. It was possible to understand this.

He wrote: *Forty-one days since leaving. The calendar continues.*

He found this accurate. He made the projection for September: the three in-progress matters, a new instruction expected from Hawkins the elder, the routine of the profession. He closed the journal. The day was ordinary, as projected.
