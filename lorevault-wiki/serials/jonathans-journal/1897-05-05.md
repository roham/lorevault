## Source — Bram Stoker, *Dracula* (1897), Jonathan Harker's Journal

> *5 May, the Castle.—The grey of the morning has passed, and the sun is high over the distant horizon, which seems jagged, whether with trees or hills I know not, for it is so far off that big things and little are mixed. I was not able to sleep, but lay awake thinking. The windows were heavily draped, and I could not see outside; so I set myself to try to discover what was there, and was not able. I was able, however, to find out after a very little time, that the Count slept during the day, and that I was alone in the castle with Miss Lucy and Miss Wilhelmina Harker. There is no doubt that the house is handsome, but it is not English, and it seems quite strange to me.*
>
> *[The text of this entry is fragmentary, as if several pages were cut or removed. The next complete entry is May 8th. What remains is a list in Jonathan's hand:*
>
> *Two gold coins of which I do not know the currency.*  
> *A letter in German, which I cannot fully read.*  
> *The name of the village nearest the Pass, which I have already forgotten.*  
> *The fact that the door from my room does not lock from the inside.]*

## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation

The four items Jonathan listed are not an inventory. They are evidence. He was trained as a solicitor; when he did not know what to make of something, he wrote it down. The list is what he could still name. What he could not name he did not write. The May 5 entry is the first time his handwriting changes — the letters are smaller from the third line onward, as if he was writing to fit in a space he had not expected to be small.
