## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

They wrote letters in the morning.

Mina wrote to Mrs. Westenra. He wrote to Dr. Seward. He had met Seward once, briefly, in the correspondence sense — their letters had crossed in August. He did not know Seward well; he could write a professional condolence.

He wrote: that he was sorry for the loss of Miss Westenra, that he knew his wife had found great comfort in Miss Westenra's friendship over many years, that he thanked Seward and Professor Van Helsing for their attentions during her illness. He wrote three short paragraphs. He read them back.

He found he wanted to ask Seward a question: what had been wrong with her, precisely. He did not write this.

He noted in his journal: *23 September — wrote to Seward. Three paragraphs. Condolence and thanks.* He paused. He wrote: *I wanted to ask what was wrong with her. I did not ask. This was not the letter for that question.* He thought: there may not be a letter for that question. He noted this too.

He addressed the envelope and set it with Mina's letter. Mrs. Hawkins would take them to the post in the afternoon. He went back to the office.
