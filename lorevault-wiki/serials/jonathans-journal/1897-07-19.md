## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He read the journal from the beginning.

He had done this on July 1 and had named the arc. On July 19 he did it again, differently: not reading for the arc but for the actionable items. He was reading it as a brief — not the kind of brief that had no addressee (the June 12 summary in the journal cover), but a brief for himself.

He found the following actionable items:

The east window. Descent feasible (approx. 68 ft, 16–19 inch stone courses, third course wet when wet). He had descended it once (June 25) and returned. The route was documented.

The gate. Two mortice locks, bolts engaged. Not passable without a key. The route through the gate: closed.

The mountain road. Approximately thirty minutes on foot from the gate. Accessible if the gate were open. The gate was not open.

He wrote: *19 July — read the brief. The east window route is the route. The gate is not the route. I am going to use the window.*

He noted: he had written *I am going to use the window*. This was a declaration of intent. He had not previously written a declaration of intent in this journal. He had written observations, facts, counts, and measurements. This was different.
