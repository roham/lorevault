## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Mina told him about Whitby.

She described the town, the harbor, the abbey on the cliff — he had the postcard and Lucy's description and his own boyhood visit; he could follow the geography. She described Lucy in the early days: well, if tired; cheerful; glad to have company. She described the change: the pallor, the sleepwalking, Mrs. Westenra's alarm, Dr. Seward's careful face when he examined Lucy in Mina's presence. She described Van Helsing: a large, serious man, Dutch-accented, who asked her precise questions about what she had observed. She said Van Helsing seemed to know more than he was saying.

He listened. He took no notes.

That evening he wrote in his journal: *18 September — Mina told me about Whitby. I am not setting down everything she said. Some of it is hers to keep. Some of it I did not understand well enough to write accurately. Some of it I am still thinking about.*

He looked at what he had written. He thought: the journal is the correct place for things that belong to the journal. Not everything belongs to the journal. He had known this before the Castle; he was more certain of it now.

He wrote: *Van Helsing seemed to know more than he was saying.* He added: *This is Mina's observation, not mine.*
