## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He reached a village in the early evening.

He did not know its name. He asked for food and shelter in German and was understood, or approximately understood — the man he spoke to gestured toward a building that appeared to be an inn and said something Jonathan took to mean that he should go there. He went there. He was given bread and soup. He was given a room, or what served as a room: a space with a bed and a small window.

He paid from the money he had kept in the coat through the Castle months. He had kept it as a professional reflex — keep the engagement expenses separate from personal funds — and had not spent any of it while in the Castle because there was nothing to spend it on.

He wrote: *26 July — a village. I do not know its name. Food, bed, room. I paid. The money held.*

He noted: the money from the professional engagement — kept separate, unused through eighty-three days — had now purchased his first meal and shelter outside the Castle. The engagement funds had become escape funds. He had not planned this. He had retained the professional habit and the habit had survived into a use he had not anticipated.
