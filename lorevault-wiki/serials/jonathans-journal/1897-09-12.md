## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He completed the Hawkins leasehold analysis.

The matter was a leasehold renewal query — the client's existing lease expires in January; Hawkins wanted a summary of the terms and a recommendation on whether the proposed renewal rate was fair against current market comparables. He had done the analysis in two sessions this week: comparables research on Tuesday, summary drafted today. He read through the draft.

He read it once.

He found it correct. He made two small revisions to the phrasing — nothing substantive, a matter of precision — and set the document. Done.

He thought: in August he would have read this twice. Possibly three times. He had been, in the first weeks back, re-reading his own work compulsively — not because he distrusted it, exactly, but because the habit of checking had calcified from repeated practice in conditions where checking was necessary. He had been reading his own documents the way he had read the Castle's documents: for the thing that might be different on the second reading.

Nothing was ever different on the second reading, in Exeter. His documents were correct on the first reading. He had begun to trust this.

He noted in his journal: *12 September — Hawkins leasehold complete. Read once. Trusted.* He thought this was, as a notation, unremarkable. He noted it anyway.
