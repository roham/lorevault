## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He thought about May 1 at the office.

He was working through the Hawkins correspondence — an ordinary Monday — and at some point in the mid-morning he found he was thinking about the May 1 entry. *Leave tomorrow for Munich.* He had written this as an ordinary notation, the kind of notation one makes about a professional trip. He had traveled to Munich in professional life before; he had traveled widely for the firm. This had been one more trip of that kind, as far as May 1 could see.

He thought: the man who wrote May 1 had been looking forward to it. He had been, at that point, a solicitor's clerk of good standing and adequate experience who had been given a complex and interesting foreign commission and had prepared for it thoroughly. He had been ready for the commission.

He had not been ready for what the commission became.

He noted in his journal that evening: *5 October — thought about May 1 at the office. The man who wrote it was about to take a train to Munich. He was looking forward to it. He had prepared. He was ready for the commission he was going on.*

He did not add anything further. That was the accurate statement.
