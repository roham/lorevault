## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Jonathan had not, until this morning, thought about when the Count left the castle.

He had been here nine nights. The Count had come to breakfast on four of them. He had been present at dinner on six. On the others he had sent word that he was attending to business. Jonathan had not thought to ask what business.

He tried now to remember the last time he had seen the Count after dark. He could not. He could remember the mornings with complete precision — the Count's hands across the table, the untouched tea, the conversation about England. But the nights were less clear than they should have been. Not blank. Not missing. Just softer, as if seen through gauze.

He thought: if a man owns a castle, he may keep whatever hours he chooses.

He thought: yes. That is correct.

He wrote nothing in the journal that night.

## Tether Note

The memory-blur is not amnesia; it is the first appearance of the selective forgetting that structures Jonathan's account through Chapter 5. He has been here nine nights; the count of nights is precise; but the content of the nights is not. This is the Gap Day layer of the GH-1 iceberg — what the published account does not contain because Jonathan did not know how to write it. Echo element registered: the unwritten nights.
