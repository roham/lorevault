## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He found a window he had not yet opened.

It was in a small room at the north end of the library corridor — a room he had not noticed because its door was set into an alcove and the alcove was in shadow. The door was unlocked. The room was empty except for a reading chair and a small table. The window faced east.

From the window he could see the valley in daylight for the first time. The road was visible, far below — the road on which he had arrived. The village was a smudge at the valley's edge, miles away.

He stood at the window for a long time. He thought: if I could signal, the distance would make the signal meaningless. He thought: if I could shout, the distance would absorb the sound. He thought: if I had a mirror on a clear day, the angle might carry the flash as far as the valley floor.

He did not have a mirror. He filed the thought.

He wrote in his journal: *24 May — east window.*

He did not write: and what I saw from it. He did not write: and what I considered.
