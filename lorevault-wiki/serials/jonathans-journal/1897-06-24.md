## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The Count visited in the evening.

He said: Jonathan would leave on June 29. A carriage would be arranged. The journey would be as he had come — through Bistritz, through the pass. He could rely on it.

Jonathan thanked him.

He wrote, afterward: *24 June — departure named: June 29. Five days. The Count has specified a terminus.*

He noted: an arrangement without a terminus had troubled him on June 19. He was now aware that a named terminus, in a place where the doors remained locked, had a different character than a named terminus in ordinary circumstances. The departure date did not open the doors. The departure date was a statement made by the person who held the keys.

He looked at what he had written. He added: *A departure named by a host in a locked house is not the same as a departure. I am noting the difference.*

He noted the difference. He did not know what to do with that knowledge, but he had been noting things he could not act on since May 4 and the ledger was still open. He would continue noting. The date was the date. The doors were the doors. Both facts were now in the record.
