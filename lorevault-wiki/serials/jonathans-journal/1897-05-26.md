## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He counted the entries.

He had not done this before — had not needed to, because the dates were there and the dates were reliable. But he wanted to know the number, and the number was twenty-three. May 3 to May 25: twenty-three days. He had been at the Castle for twenty-three days.

He looked at the journal and then looked at the room and then looked at the journal again. Twenty-three days did not feel like twenty-three days. The first days had been long and strange. Then something had changed — not the days, but his experience of them — and the middle period had compressed. He could not identify when the compression had begun. He found May 16 in the journal: a date, and nothing else. He remembered now that he had written the date and then could not proceed. The day in which everything had already been said.

He thought about R.M.S. R.M.S. had been here in 1884, and had left one notation in one volume. One notation. Thirteen years ago.

Jonathan had twenty-three entries and one R.M.S. He thought: more documentation is not better documentation. He thought: R.M.S. may have been more accurate.

He wrote in his journal: *26 May — twenty-three entries.*
