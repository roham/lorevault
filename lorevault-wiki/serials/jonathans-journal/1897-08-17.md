## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Exeter.

He knows this city. He was born here; he grew up here; he knows the names of the streets and the angles of the light in the morning and which drains run slow in heavy rain. He walked from the station to his lodgings by the route he always walks. He passed the office. The office was closed, correctly, at the hour he passed it.

The city did not remark on him. He had not expected it to. He is a solicitor's clerk who was traveling on professional business, which is the category he belongs to in Exeter's accounting. He belongs to it still.

At the lodgings, Mrs. Hawkins said: "You look thin." He said he had been unwell but was better. She brought him tea. She had been managing his household since he was an article clerk. She has a reliable method for assessing returning persons and her assessment was accurate: he is thin.

He sat in the chair in his parlor and drank the tea.

He has not thought about this chair since May 3. It is a good chair — straight-backed, situated near the window, the right height for writing. He knows these things because he is sitting in it. He found himself noting its specifications in his journal, without particular intention, and then reviewed the list and found it accurate.

He does not know why he needed to note the chair's specifications. He noted that he had done this. He expects he will get better at this.
