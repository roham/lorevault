## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He resumed writing on the nineteenth.

He wrote: *19 June — I am in the Castle. The doors are locked. I have been here since the fourth of May. I have not left.*

He considered this. He had said nothing in it that he did not know to be true. He crossed out the last sentence — he could not write *I have not left* without the phrase feeling like an accusation directed at himself, as though the not-leaving were his failure rather than his circumstance.

He wrote instead: *I remain in the Castle. The professional matter is concluded. The Count has indicated I may leave. No date has been specified.*

He reread this. The professional matter was concluded: the transfer papers, the property titles, the legal instruments, all signed and correct. The Count had what he had come to provide. And yet no date for departure had been specified.

He added: *An arrangement without a terminus is not an arrangement of the kind I was trained to recognise. I am noting this.*

This was the solicitor's register reasserting itself — not from confidence, but from the recognition that if he stopped recording he could not stop anything else. The documentation was the last professional act available to him. He would continue it.

## Tether Note

*The resume* — Jonathan's first entry after the June 18 absence is not about the encounter. It is about the legal structure of his situation: professional matter concluded, no departure date, an arrangement without a terminus. The visible cross-out — *I have not left* replaced by *I remain* — is the journal's most explicit self-correction. He edits the fact of captivity into a professional observation about contract terms. Echo E-GH1-043: "arrangement without terminus" — the legal shape of an engagement that has no exit clause, which Jonathan can name precisely but cannot remedy.
