## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He saw the Count in the courtyard at noon.

He had not expected this. The pattern of their interactions — dinner, the professional meetings that had grown less frequent, the sounds at night — had led Jonathan to a conclusion he had not written down. He had believed, without examining the belief, that the Count did not appear in daylight. He had no evidence for this. He had simply not seen it.

At noon he looked out of the library window into the small courtyard below. The Count was there, seated at the stone bench along the east wall, reading. The light was full and ordinary. He read for seven minutes — Jonathan counted — and then rose and went inside through the door Jonathan could not see from his angle.

The Count did not look up. He was reading with the particular attention of a person who does not expect to be observed.

Jonathan wrote in his journal: *3 June — courtyard, noon. Count, reading, seven minutes. Daylight.*

He added: *I had assumed otherwise.*

## Tether Note

*The assumption corrected* — Jonathan has been constructing, unconsciously, a theory of the Count's constraints: that the Count is a creature of night, inaccessible in daylight. The noon courtyard appearance corrects this without resolving anything. The Count can appear in daylight; the fact was simply not available to Jonathan because the Count had not appeared in daylight until now. Jonathan notes he had "assumed otherwise" — the honest accounting of a belief he held without evidence.

The seven-minute timing (Jonathan counts again — the third explicit timed observation, consistent with the previous series: 7 min, 11 min, 3 min) continues the Arithmetic of the Castle thread without needing to name it.
