## Source — Bram Stoker, *Dracula* (1897), Jonathan Harker's Journal

> *4 May.—I found that my landlord had got a letter from the Count, directing him to secure the best place on the coach for me; but on making inquiries as to details he seemed somewhat reticent, and pretended that he could not understand my German. This could not be true, because up to then he had understood it perfectly; at least, he answered my questions exactly as if he did.*
>
> *He and his wife, the Frau Wirtin, both did their best to dissuade me from going. They were so worried and so agitated that I had the idea they were acting a part. But at last, finding this of no avail, and seeing me so resolved, they said I might go, but that they would pray for my safety. The Frau Wirtin sent her maid to buy me a little silver crucifix, which she pressed into my hand. I had not the heart to refuse it, though it troubled me to take it. The crucifix has no place in Church of England practice, and I did not know how to explain it to Mina.*
>
> *The journey took several hours, and at Borgo Pass I was met by a calèche and four horses. The driver was a tall man with a long brown beard and a great black hat, which seemed to hide his face from us. I could only see the gleam of a pair of very bright eyes, which seemed red in the lamplight, as he turned to us.*
>
> *I could not help noticing what a very good looking man he was—a man of marked personality, tall and thin with long fingers; even his fingernails were peculiarly pointed. As his eyes fell upon me, I felt a strange chill, and a horrible sensation of nausea came over me, which, do what I would, I could not conceal.*

## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation

The crucifix the Frau Wirtin pressed into Jonathan's hand was silver and old — worn smooth at the edges from being passed, for reasons Jonathan was not told, through several hands over several decades. Jonathan did not give it to Mina, though he had promised. The casing that held it in his chest pocket was the one that, later, held the newspaper clippings Mina made. He only noticed the exchange when he looked for the crucifix six months afterward and found the clippings instead. By then the crucifix was somewhere else.
