## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He found the Carfax papers.

They were in the property file where he had left them — a file he had prepared himself, before May 3, in the course of the Count's real estate transaction. He had prepared this file carefully. The title abstracts for Carfax estate in Purfleet: complete. The conveyance documents: correct. The land searches, the requisitions, the professional summary of his client's requirements: thorough. He had been diligent. He had performed the work of a competent solicitor's clerk assigned a complex foreign-client transaction, and he had performed it well.

The transaction had closed. The property had transferred. Count Dracula owned Carfax.

He sat with the file in his hands and read through it. There is nothing in the file that was incorrectly done. The work is accurate. He had been asked to facilitate the acquisition of an English property for a foreign national, and he had done so, and the file reflects this: it is complete and it is correct.

He is the person who prepared this file.

He closed it and returned it to the drawer.

He wrote in his journal: *19 August — found the Carfax file. My work. Correct.* He stopped. He wrote: *Mina mentioned this morning that she has had a letter from her friend Miss Westenra, who is at Whitby for the summer.* He reviewed this second notation. He was not certain why it seemed relevant to record alongside the first. He wrote it anyway. He would probably understand later why he had done this.

He filed the journal in its place and went to find Mina.
