## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He stood on the deck.

He had crossed the channel before — twice for professional purposes, once as a boy with his parents. It is a gray crossing in good conditions. He knows this. The water between England and France is gray and it moves in a particular way and the air above it is a particular temperature. He has documentation of this in memory and it matches what is in front of him.

England was not yet visible.

He stood and waited for it to become visible. He had calculated, from the departure time and the known crossing duration, approximately when it would appear. He performed the calculation again, standing at the rail. The result was consistent with his previous calculation.

He had crossed this same water going east in May. He had crossed it without documentation, because in May crossing the channel was an ordinary business detail, the beginning of a professional journey. He had not stood at the rail. He had been reviewing the client file.

He reviewed nothing now. He stood and looked at gray water and waited for England to appear, and thought: the channel is the same channel. It crossed under the ship in the same direction it always has. The water does not know anything about the interval between the May crossing and this one. He found this useful — not comforting exactly, but useful. A thing that remains itself is a form of evidence that there is a place to return to.

England appeared. It was gray and it was England.

He wrote: *arrived.*
