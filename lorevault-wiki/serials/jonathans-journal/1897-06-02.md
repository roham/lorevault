## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He wrote about dinner.

He had not previously written about the meals — they had been adequate, and adequacy was not material to the record he was keeping. But on June 2 he wrote about dinner at unusual length.

He described the bread first: the texture, the density, the particular quality of warmth that indicated it had been baked that afternoon. He described the soup: the base (bone broth, he estimated, with three identifiable vegetables and an herb he could not name from the taste alone). He described the quality of the light on the table at the hour he ate. He described the temperature of the room — not unusual, neither cold nor warm.

He was aware, while writing, that he was taking inventory of the things that were still reliably sensory. The bread was what bread was. The soup was soup. The light was ordinary evening light. He was writing it down because he was afraid of the point at which these ordinary things would no longer produce ordinary responses.

He wrote in his journal: *2 June — dinner. The bread was still bread.*

## Tether Note

*The sensory inventory* — Jonathan's longest food description in the journal. He is not writing about dinner because dinner is interesting; he is writing because he needs to confirm that ordinary physical experience is still producing ordinary physical responses. "The bread was still bread" is the entry's closing note — not quite flat affect, but a notation that normalcy is being checked and confirmed, for now.

The checking itself is the record. By writing "still bread" he is acknowledging that he has considered the possibility of it not being.
