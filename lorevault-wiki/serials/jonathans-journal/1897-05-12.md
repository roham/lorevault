## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Jonathan watched for seven minutes. He did not move from the window. He counted.

He had been trained, as a solicitor's clerk, to record the precise duration of events. A witness who says "it happened quickly" is worse than no witness. A witness who says "four minutes and twenty seconds" is the beginning of a case. He had the watch in his hand before he consciously reached for it.

He counted seven minutes.

Then he stepped back from the window and sat on the bed. He held the watch in both hands without looking at it. He thought: I have just watched a man climb a wall face-downward. He thought: I have just timed it. He thought: I am going to write this in the journal. He thought: I am going to write exactly what I saw.

He opened the journal. He wrote: *I am all in a sea of wonders.* He closed the journal. He sat for a while in the dark.

He knows what he saw. He has not yet found the category for it.

## Tether Note

The watch is the Deep-layer anchor for this installment. Jonathan is a man who measures things — he is the same faculty that Holmes deploys, turned toward evidence he cannot classify. The seven-minute observation is Spine-layer for the GH-1 Jonathan: even in the presence of the impossible, he counts. The category will not come until Chapter 4. What he writes instead — *a sea of wonders* — is the first explicit misdirection in his own journal.
