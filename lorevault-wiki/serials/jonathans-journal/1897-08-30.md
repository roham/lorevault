## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He counted, at the end of the day.

He has been back in England since August 17 — thirteen days, or fourteen, depending on whether the seventeenth counts. He counts the seventeenth, because he was in England on the seventeenth; the date belongs to England. Fourteen days.

He noted in his journal: *30 August — fourteen days in England.*

He looked at this notation and recognized it immediately as the counting habit. He had counted days in the Castle because the days were the evidence — evidence that time was passing, evidence that the correspondence was not indefinite, evidence that the arrangement had a duration even if the Count had not named the terminus. He had kept the count because the count was what he could do.

Here, fourteen days: he does not need this count. England is not a count. He is not counting to establish duration; he is counting because he is counting.

He considered removing the notation from the journal. He did not remove it. The notation was accurate. He would note, instead, that he had made it: *I am still counting. This is worth knowing.*

He closed the journal and went to bed.
