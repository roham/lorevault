## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He reviewed the Carfax papers.

He had brought them from Exeter in the document case Mr. Hawkins had given him for the journey — a good case, brass-latched, with his initials on the corner. He opened it. The papers were in order: the title chain from 1750, the boundary survey, the schedule of encumbrances, the three correspondence files with the Count. He had reviewed them twice in the first week.

He reviewed them again. He took notes. He noted what remained to be done: one outstanding search, one title query that had not been fully answered in the correspondence. He wrote the note in the professional shorthand he always used for pending items.

He wrote in his journal: *31 May — reviewed the Carfax papers. Two outstanding items.*

He was aware, while doing this, that he was performing the function of a solicitor rather than being one. The distinction was new; he had not previously understood there was a distinction. He noted it and continued.

## Tether Note

*The performance of function* — Jonathan notices, perhaps for the first time, the gap between performing professional routine and inhabiting it. The Carfax papers are real; the outstanding items are real; the shorthand note is in the correct format. But something has changed in the relationship between the action and the state it is supposed to produce. The chapel has introduced a gap between method and experience that the method cannot close.
