## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He noted the date.

He wrote *1 July 1897* at the top of the page and paused. He had begun the journal on May 3 — the first entry, the train from Munich, the professional purpose. He counted: fifty-nine days.

Fifty-nine days was not, in his professional experience, an unusual period for a legal engagement. He had worked on property matters for longer. What was unusual was that a solicitor's engagement did not, ordinarily, change what the solicitor was.

He had arrived as a professional. He had been documenting his own transformation since May 3 without knowing it. He had the record. He could read it now with a different frame, beginning from May 3, and see the arc that had not been visible while he was inside it.

He wrote: *1 July — a new month. The record covers May 3 through June 30: fifty-nine installments. I am reading them as a document rather than a diary. I do not know what form I am in on July 1. The record does not yet include July.*

He closed the journal. He opened it. He wrote: *It will.*
