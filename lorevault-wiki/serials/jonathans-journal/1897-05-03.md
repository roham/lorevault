## Source — Bram Stoker, *Dracula* (1897), Jonathan Harker's Journal

> *3 May. Bistritz.—Left Munich at 8:35 P. M., on 1st May, arriving at Vienna early next morning; should have arrived at 6:46, but train was an hour late. Buda-Pesth seems a wonderful place, from the glimpse which I got of it from the train and the little I could explore of it. I feared to go very far from the station, as we had arrived late and would start as near the hour as possible. The impression I had was that we were leaving the West and entering the East; the most Western of splendid bridges over the Danube, which is here of noble width and depth, took us among the traditions of Turkish rule.*
>
> *I reached Klausenburg about noon, and went, on the train's being a little late, to the hotel Royale. I was tired, so I thought it would be good to go straight to bed. I ordered a few hours' sleep only, as I wanted to get on to my destination—Bistritz—without delay. After my supper I went to bed early, having locked and bolted my door.*
>
> *I slept till late in the morning, and breakfasted very solidly on a dish of paprika hendl, the recipe of which I am going to get for Mina. I had for lunch, or rather dinner, a chicken done up some way with red pepper, which was very good but thirsty.*
>
> *My landlord is a very cheery fellow, and the people here seem very content and happy. I have asked him of several things, and among others what the strange blue flame was I had seen. He merely smiled and said that it was often seen on the Eve of St. George's Day—yesterday—and that the peasants all believed it indicated the spot where treasure had been hidden. He seemed to think that it was a silly superstition, and I am sure he did not believe it. When I asked him about what I had seen, he began to cross himself and to put his two fingers up in a curious fashion. I tried to explain, but he said it was 'all imagination' and made signs to ward off the evil eye.*

## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The night before he left for Bistritz, Jonathan wrote Mina a letter and asked the hotel's night porter to send it. The porter did not. The letter was found, in an envelope addressed to Exeter but never sealed, in the hotel records of 1903, six years after the journey it described. The date on the letter was two days later than the date Jonathan recorded in his journal. He had already started forgetting.
