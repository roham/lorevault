## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

There was a stair in the east wing he had not seen before.

It was in an alcove off the main library corridor — the alcove was in shadow and the door was set flush with the wall, and he had walked past it each day without seeing it because there was no reason to look. He found it because he was moving more slowly than usual and the angle of the afternoon light had changed and caught the door's edge.

He went down. The smell changed at the fifth step: older, colder, stone that had been sealed in the dark for years. At the bottom: a door. It was not locked.

It was a chapel.

The altar was gone — removed, he guessed, long ago; there were stone sockets in the floor where the supports had stood. Along the far wall: boxes of earth, fitted and sealed, stacked in rows. He counted them to fifty and stopped counting. They were uniform, the size of a large trunk, and they smelled of the same earth he had noticed on the road coming in.

The largest box was in the centre of the room. Its lid was slightly displaced.

He looked in.

He wrote in his journal: *28 May — chapel. Boxes of earth. Fifty or more. One open.*

He did not write what he had seen in the open box. He could not write it. He had looked at it for some time and then had looked away and then had looked at it again and then had turned and gone back up the stairs and locked the door he had entered through, though it had not been locked when he arrived.

## Tether Note

*The legal void* — The chapel entry is the point at which Jonathan's analytical register reaches something it cannot categorize. Fifty sealed boxes of earth (shipping containers, essentially, for an English property transfer — the Count's documented acquisition of Carfax is now visible in its operational form). But the central box contains something outside all property law, outside all professional vocabulary. Jonathan's response is characteristic: he documents the countable facts (fifty boxes, one open) and locks the door. The thing he saw is recorded by its absence in the entry — the blank space after "one open."

The fifty boxes: the precise number is not documented. Only that he counted to fifty and stopped. The remainder is an undocumented excess.
