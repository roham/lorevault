## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He did not know, on waking, whether it was morning or afternoon.

He had been awake for most of the night — or what he believed was the night; the soft intervals had stopped being reliably distinguishable from waking, and he was not certain that the long unlit period had been night and not something else. He woke to light. The light suggested afternoon.

He stayed in the chair — he had not been in the bed; he was not certain when he had moved from the bed to the chair — and looked at the light for some time. He thought: I can confirm the approximate time from the angle of light. He noted the angle. The angle said three in the afternoon.

He wrote in his journal: *16 June — morning uncertain. Writing this at what the light says is three in the afternoon.*

He thought: tomorrow I will write the date and see what follows.

He did not know what he meant by this. He wrote it down anyway.

## Tether Note

*The uncertain morning* — Jonathan can no longer distinguish on waking between morning and afternoon without consulting external evidence (the light angle). The soft intervals have extended across what should be sleep and waking. "Morning uncertain" is the final failure of basic temporal orientation before June 17 — he cannot locate himself in the day without evidence. The closing thought ("tomorrow I will write the date and see what follows") is the most uncharacteristic sentence in the journal: Jonathan writing prospectively about the journal itself, not knowing what the entry will contain. It is the threshold sentence.
