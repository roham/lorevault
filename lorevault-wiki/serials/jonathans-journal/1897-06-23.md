## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He watched from his window.

He had seen the Count in his rooms, at his desk, in the corridors. He knew the Count's proportions, his manner of dress, the set of his shoulders. He would have said, before that moment, that he knew what the Count looked like when he moved.

What he saw from the window was the Count descending the outside face of the castle wall. The Count moved head-first, downward, his cloak spreading around him, his hands finding purchase on the stone courses Jonathan had measured three days before.

Jonathan wrote down what he saw. He did not attempt to explain it. He noted: the speed was considerable. He noted: the hand-placement was on the stone courses he had estimated at eighteen-inch spacing. He noted: the angle of the body was consistent with something that did not move in the way a human being moves when climbing.

He reviewed his notes and could find no error in them.

He wrote: *23 June — I have now seen the Count descend the face of the castle. I have no explanation for the method. I am recording the fact.*

The professional register: record the fact. Account for it later, if an account becomes possible. He had been doing this since May 4. The ledger was becoming very long.

*The lizard descent* — Jonathan sees the Count move down the castle's exterior face and responds in the only register available to him: he records the fact, notes the absence of explanation, and files it. His passive measurement practice had been accumulating data for an event he could not have predicted.
