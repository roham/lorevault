## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He thought, during the morning, about writing to Mina and suggesting she come home.

He thought through the argument for this. Lucy is unwell; the situation at Whitby is uncertain; Mina's presence there may or may not be helping. Against: Mina is there because her friend is ill and her friend may need her; Mina has not asked to come home; he has no information indicating she is in any difficulty herself. He does not have grounds to make this decision on her behalf.

He did not write to Mina about coming home.

He worked through the afternoon on a correspondence matter. After work he noted in his journal: *4 September — considered writing to Mina about returning. Did not. Her call to make, not mine.*

He looked at the notation. He had told himself this — *her call to make, not mine* — and he had believed it when he said it, and he still believed it. He had been, for some months, a person for whom decisions about leaving and staying had been made entirely by others. He had some experience now of what it felt like when the decision was not yours to make. He thought Mina should be the one to decide when to come home.

He did not write this in the journal. He noted only: *Did not write.* This was the accurate amount.
