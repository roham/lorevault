## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

A letter from Dr. Seward.

Written on the nineteenth: Lucy is weaker; Van Helsing remains; the situation is concerning but not without hope. Seward's phrasing is careful, which is the phrasing of a man who is choosing what to put in a letter to a person who is not a medical colleague. Mina read it once and set it down. He read it after her.

He noted in his journal: *20 September — letter from Seward, written 19th. Lucy weaker. "Not without hope": Seward's phrase. Van Helsing remains in attendance.*

He thought about Seward's phrasing. *Not without hope* is not the same as *hopeful.* It is the double negative — the thing that remains when the positive has become unavailable. He is a solicitor; he reads documents. He has been reading the letters from Whitby with the same care he applies to professional documents. The letters have been getting more careful.

He did not say this to Mina.

He added to the entry: *Mina sent a reply.* He noted the time: early afternoon. He noted that the afternoon was otherwise quiet, which it was.
