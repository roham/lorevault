## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He wrote the first ordinary entry.

He had been writing extraordinary entries since May 3: entries about the Count's property, the locks, the women, the descent, the fever, the nun, the decision. August 12 was the first day where the entry he needed to write was: we walked along the river. We ate. We are in Budapest. Mina is well. I am better.

He wrote this.

He reviewed it. It was true. It was accurate within its scope. It did not contain anything that required the specific documentation practices he had developed over ninety-eight Castle days. It was an ordinary entry.

He wrote at the bottom: *12 August — the first ordinary entry since May 3. I am noting this. The ledger continues; its content has changed.*

He noted: noting the first ordinary entry was itself a slightly extraordinary act. He had not been able to simply write the ordinary entry without noting that it was ordinary. He might get better at this. He would note that too.
