## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

In the evening he opened the first journal.

Past the flyleaf.

He had been to the flyleaf on September 19 — *March–May 1897* in the pre-Castle hand. He had not turned the page. Today he turned the page.

March 3, 1897. He reads: a client matter, a note about correspondence, a professional observation about a lease that had unusual covenants. He reads: the weather was poor for three days and then improved. He reads: Mr. Hawkins mentioned the Transylvania commission in a preliminary way; Jonathan noted it as a potential future instruction.

He stopped there and closed the journal.

He noted in the current journal: *25 September — opened the first notebook past the flyleaf. Read March 3. Client matter, weather, first mention of the Transylvania commission in passing.* He paused. He wrote: *The Transylvania commission is mentioned on March 3 as a "potential future instruction." I did not know, when I wrote that, what it would be.*

He looked at what he had written. He thought: the March entry is written by a man who does not know. He would need to read the whole March section before reaching the first Transylvania-specific entries in April. He was not ready for that yet. He had read one page. He closed both journals.
