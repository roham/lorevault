## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

A woman came to look after him.

He had been ill in the room at the Golden Krone and the hotel had sent for help — he gathered this from what happened, not from being told; he had been feverish through the night and had slept through the arrangement. When he woke a nun was present, seated at a small table with what appeared to be sewing. She looked up when he stirred.

She said something in Romanian that he did not catch. She brought him water.

He wrote, when he was able: *29 July — a nun. She brought water. I thanked her. She said something. She did not leave.*

He noted: this was the first woman in the journal — the first woman since his departure from Exeter — who had brought him something without having come for something. He had written many women into the ledger: Mina (in letters, in imagination, in the direct address of July 7); the proprietress of this hotel (May 4); the three women. They had all been part of the Castle's register, or part of the longing that the Castle registered. The nun brought water and sat with her sewing.

He found this very difficult to add to the ledger. He added it.
