## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He wrote to Mina inside the journal.

He had written to her formally — on letter paper, in professional script — in the letters that the Count had dispatched. Those letters said he was well and had returned to Exeter. He had also kept the unsendable letters from May and early June, the private letters in his coat, written in the same hand but to a different effect.

On July 7 he did something different. He wrote to Mina directly in the journal entry, in the second person, as though she were reading over his shoulder.

He wrote: *7 July — Mina, if you are reading this: I am in the Castle. I am well. I have not left. The letters that said I left were not mine in any sense that matters. This journal is the accurate record. The date is July 7. The month is July. I do not know when this will reach you, or whether it will.*

He reviewed this. He noted: the journal was not a letter. It had no delivery mechanism. He was writing to Mina in a document that had no route to her.

He kept writing anyway. The journal was the last accurate document. He would make it do everything.
