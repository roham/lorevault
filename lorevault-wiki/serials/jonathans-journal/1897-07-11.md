## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He studied the mountain road from the east window.

He had been at the window before — the measurement window, the window from which he had observed the crate-loading, the lizard descent. On July 11 he looked at it differently: not as the observation point but as the departure point. He was looking for the route to the mountain road.

The drop to the courtyard: seventy feet (June 20, amended to sixty-eight by later measurement). The courtyard to the castle gate: approximately forty metres, depending on route. The castle gate: mortice-locked, bolts engaged (July 3). The gate to the mountain road: through the approach forest, estimated thirty minutes on foot at the pace he maintained when surveying properties in the Exe valley.

He wrote: *11 July — the mountain route. Courtyard-to-gate: blocked. Gate: blocked. If the gate were open: approximately 30 minutes to the road.*

He noted: the route was blocked at two points, both of which he had already documented. He had spent two days documenting blockages without examining the route beyond them. He did not know why.

He thought: perhaps because naming the full route meant acknowledging that the full route was what he wanted. He had not wanted to want it.

## Tether Note

*The mountain route* — Jonathan maps the full escape route for the first time: window to courtyard (70ft/June 20), courtyard to gate (locked/July 3), gate to mountain road (estimated 30 min on foot). He has documented every blockage but not the complete route. His note — "perhaps naming the full route meant acknowledging that the full route was what he wanted; he had not wanted to want it" — is the journal's most explicit statement of self-suppression. Echo E-GH1-065: "the mountain route" — the first complete escape map; the route that was always available to name and was not named until wanting it could no longer be avoided.
