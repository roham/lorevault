## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He completed the Hawkins leasehold recommendation.

He had been working on this since early September — the comparables research, the analysis of the current rate against market, the summary of the client's options. Today he drafted the final recommendation. He read it once. He made two changes of phrasing for precision. He read it again.

He noted in his journal: *29 September — Hawkins leasehold recommendation complete. Read twice.* He paused. He had read it twice. He considered this. He had read it twice not from anxiety but from the usual professional care — this was a recommendation that would carry weight; it should be read twice. He had read the Devon abstract once in September because it was a routine matter; he read a major recommendation twice because it merited it.

He found this distinction useful. The question was not whether he was reading twice compulsively, but whether the number of reviews was proportionate to the weight of the document. Twice for a recommendation was proportionate. Once for a routine abstract was proportionate. He had been calibrating this without knowing he was calibrating it.

He noted: *Proportionate. Not compulsive. This is the right calibration.*

He closed the journal and signed the recommendation.
