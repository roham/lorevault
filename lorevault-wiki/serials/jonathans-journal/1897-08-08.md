## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Mina arrived.

She came through the door of the hotel room — Sister Agatha had been there, and then Sister Agatha was not there, and then Mina was. He had not written what this looked like in advance because he had not known what it would look like.

She was smaller than he remembered. He did not know whether she had changed or his memory had; he had been working from an imprecise internal document for ninety-eight days.

She said his name.

He wrote: *8 August — Mina arrived. She said my name. I said hers. We sat.*

He noted: he had been writing this journal since May 3, partly toward this moment. He had addressed her directly in it (July 7). He had decided she would receive it (August 3). He had known she was coming (August 5). None of these preparations had prepared him for her arriving and saying his name.

The ledger, he noted, did not have a field for this.
