## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

An ordinary Sunday.

He went to church. He read in the morning. He and Mina had lunch and then he went to the office — it had become a Sunday habit, he noted, without quite having decided to make it one — and worked for two hours on the Hawkins correspondence that had arrived Saturday.

He came home at four. He noted in his journal: *3 October — Sunday. Office two hours. Hawkins correspondence. Will read more of the old journal this evening.*

He found that his relationship to the phrase *the old journal* had changed. In August it had been a thing he looked at from the outside — the spine, the cover, the weight of it. In September he had reached the flyleaf and then the first page. Now he was reading through it, incrementally, evening by evening, and it had become a thing he was in the middle of rather than a thing he was approaching.

He had read March and April. He was approaching May.

He noted: *Two months read out of five. The reading is going.* He found this accurate. He closed the current journal and put it beside the others on the shelf, where it belonged.
