## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He read April in the old journal.

April 1897. He had left March at the Transylvania commission's first mention on March 3 and had not returned until now. Today he read the April entries: the preparation. He had studied the history of Transylvania, the geography of the Carpathian region, the relevant property law of the Austro-Hungarian empire. He had learned phrases of Romanian and some German for the journey. He had organized the client file — the Carfax documents, the title searches, the count's requirements — with the systematic efficiency he applies to major foreign instructions.

He read his own preparation notes. They are excellent notes. He had been, in April 1897, an efficient and capable solicitor doing thorough preparation for a complex commission. His handwriting is clean. His organization is logical. His analysis of the client's requirements is accurate.

He wrote in the current journal: *2 October — read April in the old notebook. Preparation entries. History, language, file organization. The work was well done.*

He paused and added: *The man who wrote April knew exactly what he was preparing for professionally. He did not know anything else.*

He closed both journals.
