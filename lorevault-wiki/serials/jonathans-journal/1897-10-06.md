## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He read May 1 again.

He had closed the journal at May 1 on Sunday evening; he had thought about it at the office on Monday; now he opened the old journal to May 1 and read it in full, slowly, as a document.

May 1, 1897. Full entry. He reads: the exchange rate notation; a reminder about the Munich hotel address; a note about the documents he was carrying; a reflection on the commission itself — that it was unusual to travel so far to meet a client, but the client's requirements had made this necessary, and unusual was not the same as difficult. His exact words: *Unusual but not difficult. This kind of commission is what good preparation is for.*

He read this sentence several times.

He noted in the current journal: *6 October — read May 1 in full. "Unusual but not difficult. This kind of commission is what good preparation is for."* He wrote these words from memory, and then checked them against the old journal. They matched.

He thought: the sentence was correct about what the commission would be professionally. It was a sentence that did not have the information to be incorrect in any other way.

He closed the old journal. He found he was not distressed. He was reading accurately.
