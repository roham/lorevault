## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He noticed the light first.

He had been in the Castle for eighty-one days. The light inside had been mediated — through small windows, through the Castle's particular way of holding or deflecting illumination, through the quality of its stone. He had adapted to it without knowing he was adapting. On July 23, in the open pass road, the light was direct. He had to limit his exposure to it; he looked at the road ahead and not at the sky.

He walked. He kept the east road — the same road from Bistritz that the Count's carriage had brought him up on May 4. He would know the landmarks when he saw them.

He wrote, in the evening, having found a sheltered place off the road: *23 July — the light is different outside. I had forgotten how it was. The road is the right road. I have the coat. I have the journal. I am moving.*

He noted: he had the coat. He had the journal. These were the things he named. He had two hands, two feet, a body in functional condition. He had not named these. The coat and the journal were what he was carrying; they seemed more essential to name than the body carrying them.
