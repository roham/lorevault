## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He worked through the day's post in order.

He does this by a method he developed some years ago: largest matter first, then correspondence requiring response, then correspondence requiring filing, then correspondence requiring no action. He had not worked through post by this method for some months. He worked through it by this method today, and found the method intact.

At the bottom of the pile — not because he had left it there deliberately, but because it had been filed in a separate stack before he began — was the Carfax matter.

He opened the file. He had looked at it two days ago when he found it; now he looked at it again. The title abstracts. The conveyance documents. His professional summary on the last page: *Transaction complete. Property transferred. File closed.*

He read the summary. It was accurate. He had written it before his departure in May, anticipating that the transaction would complete correctly, as it had. The file was closed in the sense that no further professional action was required. The summary was correct.

He closed the file and returned it to its drawer.

He noted in his journal: *20 August — reviewed the Carfax file. Transaction complete, correctly filed. No further action required.*

He did not write what he thought about afterward. Some notations are accurate within their professional scope, and within that scope they are complete.
