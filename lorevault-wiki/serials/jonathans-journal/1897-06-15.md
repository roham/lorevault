## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He watched the courtyard from his window at the late hour.

He had done this before — May 19, the eleven-minute gathering he had timed. He had timed every event he observed from windows: seven minutes, eleven minutes, three minutes, seven minutes. The timing had been the frame that made observation possible: a number gave the event a category even when the event had no other category.

He saw a shape cross the courtyard below. He reached for his watch.

He put the watch away without opening it.

The shape was not something he was prepared to measure. He watched until it had gone. He did not know how long this took.

He wrote in his journal: *15 June — courtyard, late. Shape. Duration: not timed.*

## Tether Note

*Duration: not timed* — The timing practice has now been explicitly broken. Jonathan reaches for the watch, which has been his instrument for managing the unmanageable, and puts it away. The shape below is outside the category that timing could hold. The previous four timed events (7/11/3/7 minutes) established the practice as a method for creating documentation of the undocumentable; the cessation of the practice marks the point at which the method itself fails. "Duration: not timed" is the most precise thing Jonathan can write — and it is a record of not-measuring.
