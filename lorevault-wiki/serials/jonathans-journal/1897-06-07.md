## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

There was a window on the south side he had not previously opened.

It was in the small corridor between the second library room and the locked staircase — a narrow window, high in the wall, that required standing on the bench below it to reach the latch. He opened it.

The drop below was sheer: the cliff face went straight down several hundred feet to the river. He could see the bend in the river to the west. The gorge was narrow here; the far wall was close but the distance to the water was very great.

He stood at the window for some time. He was aware that he was calculating — not jump distances, which would have been meaningless, but the geometry of the gorge: the width at the waterline, the angle of the cliff below the window, whether a signal from this height might be visible from any inhabited point on the far wall.

He concluded that the far wall showed no signs of habitation. He closed the window.

He wrote in his journal: *7 June — south window. Not applicable.*
