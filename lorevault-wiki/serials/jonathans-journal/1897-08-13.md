## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The train left Budapest at quarter past eight.

He noted the time in his journal. He noted it because he had been noting times — the instinct had not yet left him — and then he reviewed what he had written and saw that the time did not need to be noted. The train's departure time was available on the timetable. It was not information he needed to preserve.

He left the notation. It was accurate. He would get better at knowing what did not need to be written.

Through the window: the city receding. Rooftops, then the river, then the wider margin of the plain. He had been in Budapest long enough to know certain streets, certain distances from certain corners to the hospital. He would not know these things much longer; the city would reduce, in memory, to a hospital ward and a church and a municipal office where documents were witnessed. This seemed like the correct proportion of a city one had moved through rather than lived in.

He is traveling west. He knows what is west of him. He has not known this with confidence for some months — east had been his direction, and east had become complex. West is England. He can name every major city between here and Exeter without consulting a timetable. He consulted one anyway. He noted which trains they would take. He noted the connections.

Mina was beside him. He did not note Mina's presence in any documentary sense. She was simply present, as a person is present when one does not require documentation of the exits.

He closed the journal. The city was gone. Austria was next.
