## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He prepared.

The coat with the three letters: he would wear it. He had the journal. He would carry the journal. He had reviewed the June 25 descent and noted the correction to the stone course spacing; the third course from the bottom had been wet in late June. He checked it from the window: it appeared dry. This was the dry part of the summer.

He had, also, the Count's letter (filed in the journal cover since July 6). He had the second brief. He had the journal itself.

He thought: if he reached Bistritz with this coat and this journal, someone who was not the Count's agent could read what had happened. The coat and the journal together constituted a complete record. He had been keeping it for 79 days without knowing this was the purpose.

He wrote: *20 July — prepared. Coat with letters. Journal. Count's letter filed inside. The third course appears dry. I will go tomorrow night or the night after, depending on conditions.*

He noted: *depending on conditions* was his phrase now, not the Count's. The conditions were his to read.
