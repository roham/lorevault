## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Jonathan went to the window again — not the east-wing window he had decided not to use, but the library window that overlooked the castle entrance. He sat at it for a long time. He was not doing this deliberately. He had gone to the library to find something to read. He found that the books were all in English and all about England — history, geography, directories, legal texts. He understood that they had been assembled for him, or for someone like him, as a preparation. The Count was preparing himself for England. Jonathan was the last lesson.

He chose a gazetteer. He sat with it, open to the Exeter entry, for one hour. He did not read it. He was listening. The castle made no sound during daylight hours. He had begun to count on this.

## Tether Note

The library assembled for a visitor is the Castle's second offer — after the unlocked door. This is the difference between knowing a thing and acting on it. The iceberg here is deep: the library implies all the previous visitors Dracula has prepared for. Jonathan is not the first.
