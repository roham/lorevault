## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He counted his money.

He had not looked at it since arriving — there had been no occasion. The count at Exeter had been four pounds, eleven shillings, and eight pence. He counted it now: the same. He had been here thirty-three days. He had paid for nothing.

He had paid for nothing because there was nothing to pay for. Meals appeared. The room was maintained. The fire was laid. He had not been presented with a bill.

He thought: money assumes a system that can receive it. A price assumes a seller; a payment assumes a transaction. There had been no transaction. He had spent thirty-three days in a place where money was not a unit of anything.

He put the coins back in his pocket. He wrote in his journal: *5 June — four pounds, eleven shillings, eight pence. Unchanged.*

He thought: I have not spent a single thing that was mine.

---

## Tether Note

*The untouched coin* — Jonathan's inventory of the money is an inventory of the limits of exchange: every instrument he has (legal knowledge, consular rights, English currency) functions only inside a system that can receive it. The Castle is not that system. The money is perfectly preserved because it has never been accepted. The last sentence — "I have not spent a single thing that was mine" — reads at first as relief and then, on reflection, as a different kind of record.
