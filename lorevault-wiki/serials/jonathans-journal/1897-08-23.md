## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He drafted the matter summary for the Hawkins/Dracula file.

He had been meaning to do this since his return. The Carfax transaction was complete, the file closed, but the matter summary — the brief professional record of the engagement, written for the firm's files — had not been completed. This was a formality, but it was the kind of formality he maintained.

He wrote it as he would write any matter summary. *Client: Count Dracula, of Transylvania.* He gave the address he had for the Count: the Castle, Borgo Pass region. *Nature of matter: real property acquisition, Carfax estate, Purfleet, Essex. Principal documents: title abstract, conveyance deed, requisitions on title (Mr Hawkins, senior). Outcome: property transferred, freehold. File status: closed.* He noted the date range: *Instructions received January 1897. Travel undertaken May–August 1897 in connection with client consultation.* Under *Solicitor's health during matter*: *A period of illness contracted during travel, now fully resolved. No impact on matter outcome.*

He read it through. It was accurate within its professional scope. It described a foreign-client transaction that had completed correctly, in which the solicitor had traveled to meet the client, contracted a period of illness, and returned. The matter had concluded successfully.

He signed the summary and filed it with the Carfax documents.

He noted in his journal: *23 August — matter summary drafted and filed. The professional record is now complete.*
