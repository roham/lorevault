## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Mina was reading a letter and laughed at something in it.

He asked what it said. She told him: Lucy is at Whitby for the summer, with her mother. The town has a harbor and an old abbey on the cliff — Mina has described the abbey before; Lucy says the ruins are spectacular at sunset, that the churchyard above the harbor is the sort of place where one feels appropriately small. Lucy says the fishing boats come in at dawn and the smell is not what a letter from a friend usually contains, but this is Whitby and the harbor is the harbor.

He listened to this. He found it pleasant. Lucy sounds like a person who reads landscapes.

He wrote in his journal: *21 August — Mina had a letter from Miss Westenra, who is at Whitby. Whitby: coastal town, North Yorkshire, about six hours from London via York.* He paused. He had been to Whitby once, as a boy of eleven, with his parents. He remembered it as gray and clean and very loud near the harbor.

He does not have any professional document concerning Whitby.

He added to the notation: *old abbey on the cliff. Churchyard above the harbor.* He reviewed this and recognized that he had just documented a postcard. He noted that he had done this. He would get better at knowing what did not need to be written.
