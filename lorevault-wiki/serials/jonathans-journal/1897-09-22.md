## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He did not go to the office.

He made tea in the morning. He and Mina sat in the parlor. He read; she was sometimes reading and sometimes not. In the afternoon Mrs. Hawkins came and was informed and was sorry. In the evening he made dinner, because Mrs. Hawkins had offered and they had declined.

He noted in his journal: *22 September — at home. Tea. Parlor. Mrs. Hawkins informed. Dinner made.*

He looked at this entry. He had not written anything about Mina. He has written about Mina throughout the journal — her arrival in Budapest, her journals on the train, the station departure on August 27, the reunion on the seventeenth. He had not written about her today.

He thought about this and found that it was correct. Mina's grief is not his to document. He has a journal; she has a journal; what she is carrying today belongs to her account, not his. He can note the tea and the parlor and the dinner. He can be present in the house. These are the accurate contents of his record.

He added a single line: *Mina is here.*

He found that this was accurate and complete.
