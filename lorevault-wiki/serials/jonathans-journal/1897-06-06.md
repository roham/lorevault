## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He wrote a letter to Mina in his head.

Not on paper. Paper became evidence; he had understood this. But he thought through the letter carefully, in the order he would have written it, with the words he would have chosen.

He had sent her a letter through the Count's postal arrangement in early May. He had written her a letter that stayed in his coat pocket. He had written a second coat-pocket letter on May 20. Both were still in his coat.

This letter was not in his coat. It was nowhere. He thought it through and then he did not write it.

He could not have said, afterward, exactly what the letter contained. He had thought it through precisely and then it had become unavailable — not forgotten, but not accessible in the way that written things are accessible. It had existed for the duration of the thinking and then had closed.

He wrote in his journal: *6 June — letter, unwritten.*

## Tether Note

*The third Mina letter* — the series of Mina letters has now produced a third form: the May letter through the Count (official); the May 13 and May 20 letters in the coat pocket (unsent, physical, retrievable); the June 6 letter (composed mentally, not written, no longer fully accessible). Three forms of a letter: sent but not real, real but not sent, neither. The unwritten letter is the most complete erasure — it leaves no artifact.
