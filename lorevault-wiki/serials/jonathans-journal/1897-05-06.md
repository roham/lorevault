## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

On the night of 5 May, Jonathan slept for what he believed was eight hours. He woke to find the candle at his bedside burned to a stub, though he had lit it fresh before eleven. A candle of that size should have lasted past midnight. The room was fully dark when he woke; it was not yet dawn. He had no clock. He dressed, sat at the writing desk, and wrote the date at the top of a fresh page: *6 May.* He wrote nothing else. He could not have said afterward what he had been looking at in the dark, or for how long, or whether he had been asleep again between sitting down and hearing the Count move somewhere in the castle below. He sat there until the light came, and then he closed the journal without filling the page.

Thirty years later, when the journal was copied out for the proceedings, the blank page was noted as deliberate. The archivist wrote *intentional omission* in the margin. She did not ask Jonathan what had kept him at the desk, dressed, in the dark, for whatever time had passed.
