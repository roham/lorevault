## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He heard something below.

He could not be more specific than this. He was in his room, not asleep — sleep had become unreliable since June; the soft intervals from earlier in the Castle stay had resolved into a pattern in which he slept in the afternoon and stayed awake through much of the night. He heard a sound from below: not the wolves, not the wind, not the castle's ordinary structural sounds which he had catalogued by now. Something else.

He waited. He heard it again.

He went down to the corridor below his room and looked. Nothing. He went to the chapel passage. The chapel: nothing. The crates were as he had left them — twenty-two, approximately, minus however many had been removed in the week since his count.

He returned to his room and wrote: *5 July — a sound, unidentified, twice. Below. I checked the corridor and the chapel passage. Nothing present that I could observe.*

He noted: nothing present that I could observe. This had become his standard qualifier. He had been noting, for weeks, the limits of his observational frame — the soft intervals, the things he had heard and not found, the spaces between entries. The nothing was getting very precise.
