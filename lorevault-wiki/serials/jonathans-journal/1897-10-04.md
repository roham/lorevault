## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He read to May 1.

He had said he would read more of the old journal on Sunday evening and he had; he had read late April — the final preparations, the packing, a note about the exchange rate for Austrian currency. Then he had continued, because the reading had been going well, and he had read May 1.

May 1, 1897: *Leave tomorrow for Munich. Commission should complete by end of June, possibly earlier. Mina has my address for correspondence. Looking forward to the commission — interesting country, competent preparation.* He had written this on May 1. He had been looking forward to it. His handwriting was easy and large — the hand of a man with no reason to write small.

He read this entry and closed the journal.

He noted in the current journal: *4 October — read to May 1. May 1 ends: "looking forward to the commission." The last entry before departure. I have read up to here.*

He thought: May 3 is the next entry. In May 3 he arrives in Munich and begins the journey east. He was not ready to read May 3 yet. He knew where May 3 led.

He put the old journal back on the shelf. He found that he was not distressed by this. He was simply not ready yet, and not-yet remained accurate.
