## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The entry was not written.

Jonathan took out the journal, wrote the date — *18 June* — and did not continue. He could give no account of why. He could give an account of the previous night's entry (three facts: the room, the women, the phrase), but he could not, on June 18, produce additional text.

He held the pen. The words that had previously organized his experience — *arrived, documented, noted, arranged, confirmed* — were not, at that moment, organizing anything. He had been a professional. He was, in the Count's formulation, a belonging. These two states did not coexist easily in the solicitor's register.

He wrote nothing further.

The entry for June 18 is the date and the blank space after it.

He noted, when he came back to the journal on June 19, that he had skipped a day. He added, in the margin: *[18 June — nothing written. Entry absent. I do not know why.]*

This marginal note is itself a document. It is Jonathan noting the gap in his documentation. He had not previously noted gaps. There had been no gaps.

## Tether Note

*The first written absence* — Jonathan's documentation practice has been unbroken since May 3. June 18 is the first day he opened the journal and closed it without writing. The marginal note added the following day — acknowledging the gap — is the journal acknowledging its own failure. The blank space is Echo E-GH1-042 (named: "first written absence"): a structural hole in the serial that readers will recognise retrospectively as the hinge point between the documenting Jonathan and the documented one.
