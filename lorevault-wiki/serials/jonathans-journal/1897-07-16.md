## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He walked the castle.

He had not done this before — walked it as a complete circuit, room by room, from his own chambers to the Count's empty rooms to the chapel corridor to the lower passage and back. He had moved through it in specific, purposeful journeys: the descent on June 25, the chapel on June 26, the gate on July 3. Today he simply walked it.

The castle was his, now, in the sense that he was its only occupant. He had been its only occupant for some time, but the Count's presence — even when the Count was not visible — had organised the space. The rooms had been the Count's rooms. Now they were rooms.

He found three things he had not previously documented: a small window off the north corridor, unglazed, about eight inches wide; a locked iron door in the lower passage that he had assumed led to a cellar; and a broken stone in the east staircase that moved when he stepped on it.

He wrote: *16 July — walked the full circuit. Three previously undocumented features. I have been here 75 days. There are still things I have not seen.*

He noted: the castle had more to offer the surveyor than he had extracted from it. He had spent 75 days in it and had only documented what his captivity had forced him to see.

## Tether Note

*The full circuit* — Jonathan walks the entire castle on the first day of his sole occupancy and finds three undocumented features after 75 days. The professional irony: he was sent here to survey the Count's property; he is still finding new features. The observation that "the Count's presence had organised the space" — that occupied rooms are different from mere rooms — is the journal's most direct statement of what the Count's departure has changed.
