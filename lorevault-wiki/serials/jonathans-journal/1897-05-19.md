## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He heard voices at two in the morning.

He had not been sleeping. He had been at the window that faced the inner courtyard — a smaller window than the others, set into the south wall, which he had found opened on a latch. He was watching, without particular intent, because there was nothing else to do at two in the morning in a castle where he could not get out.

Below, at the base of the courtyard, he saw the Count. He was not alone. There were three other figures. Jonathan could not make out features or voices clearly; it was dark, and the courtyard had an odd acoustic quality that made sounds arrive at the wrong time, slightly after the movement that produced them. He watched for eleven minutes.

In the morning his breakfast tray was on the table when he woke. He had not heard the door.

He wrote in his journal: *19 May — courtyard, 2AM. Eleven minutes.*

He did not write: who the other figures were. He did not write: that someone entered his room while he slept.
