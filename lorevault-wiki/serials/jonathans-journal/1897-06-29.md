## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The carriage came.

He heard it at approximately three in the afternoon: wheels on the stone approach, the horses. He went to the window. A carriage was in the courtyard below — two horses, a driver he did not recognise, a vehicle that looked as though it had made the pass from Bistritz. He thought: this is the carriage.

Then the wolves came.

They came from the treeline to the east and arranged themselves between the carriage and the castle gate through which he would have needed to walk to reach it. They did not attack the horses. They did not advance. They stood in the gap.

The carriage remained in the courtyard. The driver did not move. The wolves did not move.

After approximately twenty minutes, the carriage turned and left by the route it had come.

Jonathan wrote: *29 June — the carriage came. The wolves prevented. The carriage left. The departure date was not the departure.*

He noted: the Count had named the date. The Count had also arranged the wolves, or the wolves had arranged themselves, or the arrangement was coincidence. He could not determine which. He wrote all three possibilities in the margin. He could not weight them differently from one another.

The legal paradox from June 24 had resolved: a departure named by a person who holds the keys is not the same as a departure. He had the answer now.
