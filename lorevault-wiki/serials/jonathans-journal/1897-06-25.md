## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He used the route he had measured.

He had spent five days calculating the drop, estimating the stone courses, recording the spacing. He had told himself this was the measurement habit, the professional observer's automatic accumulation. On June 25 he understood it had been preparation — not decided, but conducted — and the preparation was ready whether or not the decision had been made.

He went out the window.

The stone courses held. They were approximately at the spacing he had estimated (eighteen inches; the actual interval was perhaps sixteen, perhaps nineteen — he could not be precise while moving). He descended without difficulty until the third course from the bottom, where the stone was wet. He paused, found a dry grip two courses above, and continued.

He reached the courtyard and found a door into the lower section of the castle he had not previously been able to access. It was unlocked.

He wrote, later: *25 June — descended the east face. Third course from the bottom: wet. Correction to earlier estimate: 16–19 inches, not a uniform 18. The door on the north side was unlocked. I went in.*

He had noted the possibility on June 20. He had not decided to use it. The body had apparently made a separate arrangement.
