## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He wrote a letter to Mina on the ninth.

Not in the journal — the July 7 entry had been a letter inside the journal, addressed to Mina within the text, understood to be undeliverable. This was different. He took letter paper from the writing desk, the same paper he had used for the Count's legal correspondence, and wrote a letter to Mina Harker, Exeter, in the ordinary social hand he used for personal correspondence.

He did not seal it. He folded it and put it in the lining of his coat.

The coat already contained the May 13 letter and the May 20 letter — the two personal letters he had written in the first weeks, before the soft intervals, when he had still believed in the possibility of delivery. The lining now held three letters. He had not sent one.

He wrote in the journal: *9 July — a third letter in the coat lining. Written on Castle writing paper. The hand is mine. The address is Exeter. I do not know how it will travel.*

He noted: he did not know how it would travel. He had written it anyway. The coat was becoming a document.
