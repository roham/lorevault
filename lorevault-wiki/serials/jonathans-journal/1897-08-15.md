## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Mina was writing in her journal.

He had not known, until she mentioned it in Budapest, that she had kept a journal through the summer. She had maintained it since before he left — since before May 3. She had written through his absence: the waiting, the letters, the silence in June and July, the telegram, the journey east to collect him.

He was the one who wrote in journals. He had understood himself to be the one who wrote in journals. It had seemed a personal characteristic — the documentation instinct, the solicitor's habit carried into private life.

Mina had been writing in a journal too. He considered this. Her journal contained the summer as she had experienced it, from a position he had no access to. He had been documenting the Castle; she had been documenting the not-knowing. Two records of the same period, kept separately, written toward each other across the distance.

He would not ask to read it. It was her record. He had the same instinct about his own.

He wrote in his journal: *15 August — into Germany. Mina is writing beside me. Two journals being kept at the same table. I think this is correct: the documentation of a shared journey requires two accounts. Neither is complete without the other.*

He reviewed this. He closed the journal. Mina was still writing. He looked out the window at Germany and found, without surprise, that Germany was accurately Germany.
