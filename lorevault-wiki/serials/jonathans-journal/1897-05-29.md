## Tether Note

*The third blank* — The journal has now failed to document a day for the third time, on three different grounds. May 16: the day in which everything had already been said. May 26: twenty-three entries, documented count of a compression. May 29: a date, and the pen put down.

May 29's failure is not absence of material; it is the presence of material that the record cannot hold. Jonathan wrote the date because writing the date was something he could do. He put the pen down because the next word was not available to him.

The distinction from May 16: on May 16, Jonathan could not write because there was nothing new to say. On May 29, he cannot write because what is new is outside the categories the journal was built to receive.
