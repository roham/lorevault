## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He walked with Mina to the station.

Her train left at half past nine. He carried her bag to the platform. The northbound express: she would change at Bristol and again at York; Whitby was the terminus. He knew this route because he had looked it up when she first mentioned the journey; it was the kind of thing he still looked up.

They stood on the platform until the train came. He noticed he was watching the clock — measuring the interval between arrival and departure, a habit that had become very precise. He watched himself watching the clock and thought: there is nothing to measure here; the train will come when it comes.

The train came. She boarded. She leaned from the window and said something ordinary — she would write when she arrived, she would be back by the twelfth, he should eat properly. He said he would. He watched the train leave.

He stood on the platform for a moment after it was gone. He had stood on platforms in this way before the Castle — it is what one does when a train departs. He was not doing anything unusual. He walked back to the office.

He wrote in his journal: *27 August — Mina departed for Whitby, 9:32. She will write when she arrives.*
