## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He did not leave his room until midday.

He stayed in the chair until the light was fully established — not just the pale pre-dawn grey but the strong direct light of late morning. He had learned to distrust the early light. When it was unambiguously noon, he rose and moved about.

He assessed his condition professionally. He was physically well — his body's condition had not deteriorated in the conventional sense. He was tired but not ill. He could reason and record. He ate the bread and cold meat that had been left outside his door (this continued; the food appeared daily without a visible mechanism; he had noted this and moved on). He was functioning.

He wrote: *18 July — condition assessed: physically well. Tired. Reasoning intact. Recording intact. The door held and I am recording.*

He added: *I believe the door held because I needed it to. I am not certain this is a sufficient explanation.*

He reviewed this. It was not an explanation in any sense the solicitor's register recognised. He left it in.
