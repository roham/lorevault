## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He counted the completed matters since his return.

He had a habit of doing this at the end of the month. August was not yet ended, but he was close enough; he counted. Eight completed matters: the Tiverton conveyance, two mortgage completions, a lease renewal, a client estate matter that had required only correspondence, two searches and a brief in connection with a dispute that had settled before it required further work. Three matters in progress.

This is approximately his usual rate for the corresponding period. He checked against his records from August of previous years: 1896, nine completed and two in progress; 1895, seven completed and four in progress. His current rate was within the normal range.

He had not worked for May, June, July, or most of August. Hawkins the elder had carried the caseload. He had been, for those months, not working. Now he was working. The caseload had absorbed his return without difficulty — Hawkins had managed the continuity well, nothing had been lost, no client had been poorly served.

He wrote: *25 August — eight completed matters since return. Three in progress. Within normal rate. The firm did not lose ground in my absence. I am current.*

He read this back. He noticed he had written *I am current* as a separate sentence, with emphasis, as though it required stating. He left it. It had required stating.
