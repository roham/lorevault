## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He woke at the usual time and the breakfast tray was there.

He had written *I am a prisoner* the night before, and he had meant it, and now there was a tray. Bread. Cold meat. A small pot of honey. The same as every morning.

He ate.

He thought: the fact that breakfast still comes is information. He did not yet know what kind.

He opened the journal to a new page. He wrote the date: 16 May. He held the pen above the line. He thought: if I write nothing after the date, the blank space is also a record. It records the day on which I could find nothing to say that I had not already said.

He put the pen down. He did not write anything else.

The Count came in the evening and they spoke about the legal documents for the Carfax property. Jonathan answered correctly. He answered as a clerk. It seemed the right thing to do.

He thought, afterward: that is what I am now. I am still a clerk. A prisoner who is still a clerk. He did not know if this was a comfort or a further piece of information he did not yet have a category for.

## Tether Note

*The day after naming* — the installment tracks the gap between analysis and action. Jonathan has categorized his situation ("I am a prisoner") but has not yet moved to escape or resistance. He enters a phase of managed attendance: continuing to function as a clerk, continuing to be useful, while the journal holds open a blank page. The blank page is the Lampblack: the dated entry with no entry is itself an entry.

The "still a clerk" observation seeds the next phase of Jonathan's arc: what does a person do when their professional identity is the only frame left to organize their behavior? The solicitor's faculty of category-making (naming things correctly) applied to an uncategorizeable situation.
