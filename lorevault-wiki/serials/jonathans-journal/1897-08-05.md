## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Sister Agatha brought him a telegram.

It was from Mina. She had received his letter — the August 1 letter. She was coming. She gave a date of arrival. She said: *I am coming. Do not be afraid. I am coming.*

He read it three times.

He wrote: *5 August — a telegram from Mina. She is coming. She gave a date. She said: do not be afraid.*

He noted: *do not be afraid* was not a phrase he had used in his letter. He had said *I will explain everything* and *I need to see you*. He had not said he was afraid. She had received the letter and had understood something that was not in the words. She had replied to what was in the letter, not what the letter said.

He had been keeping a record for ninety-four days. The record was accurate within its scope. She had just read something outside its scope.

He held the telegram.

## Tether Note

*"Do not be afraid"* — Mina's telegram replies to what is in Jonathan's letter rather than what the letter says. He had not written that he was afraid; she understood it and replied to it. The journal has been the record of what Jonathan could write; Mina reads what he could not. Echo E-GH1-090: "'Do not be afraid'" — Mina reading outside the record's scope; the telegram that responds to the unwritten; the limit of the journal acknowledged by the person who will read it.
