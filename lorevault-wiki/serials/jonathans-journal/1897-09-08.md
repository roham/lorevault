## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He went to the office.

There was a new instruction from Hawkins — a straightforward leasehold query, the kind of matter he has handled many times. He read the brief, made his notes, set the timetable. He did not look at the brief three times to confirm he had understood it correctly; he read it once. He has been looking at briefs twice and sometimes three times since returning, which is not his usual practice. Today he read the brief once and his notes were complete.

He filed the notes and moved to the next matter.

He noted in his journal that evening: *8 September — new instruction from Hawkins. Read the brief once. Notes complete first review.* He paused and then added: *This is better than the first week.*

He reviewed this. It was accurate. He had read the September briefs more confidently than the August briefs; the interval between receiving a matter and performing well on it was shortening. He had not been tracking this explicitly, but now that he noticed it, it seemed worth noting: the professional performance was settling back into its usual form, and he was aware it was doing so, and he found he was glad.

He did not think this was a small thing.
