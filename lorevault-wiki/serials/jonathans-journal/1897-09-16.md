## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

A letter from Mina.

She is coming home. Lucy is now in Dr. Seward's care at his asylum — a private arrangement; Van Helsing is still attending; there is nothing more Mina can do for now, and remaining felt, after this week, like remaining on ceremony rather than remaining for Lucy. She has said her goodbyes. She will take the morning train on the seventeenth and arrive in Exeter by early evening.

He read this and was, simply, relieved.

He wrote in his journal: *16 September — Mina coming home. Arrives tomorrow evening.* He thought about what to write to meet her. He would meet her at the station. He noted: *Will meet her at the station.* He thought: I will not time the interval on the platform.

He noted this too: *I will not time the interval.* He found this accurate — he meant it; he did not think he would time it. He was glad to note that he did not think he would.

He wrote a reply to Mina: he would be on the platform. She should not hurry; the train was long enough. He looked forward to seeing her.

He closed the journal. Tomorrow he would meet Mina at the station and not time anything.
