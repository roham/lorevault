## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He tallied the third week.

Four completed matters. Two new instructions received and begun. Correspondence current. He reviewed these numbers against the first and second weeks back: week one, three completed, correspondence slow; week two, four completed, one new instruction; week three, four completed, two new instructions, correspondence current.

The numbers were improving and then had plateaued, which was also an improvement — plateau at a good level is a form of arriving. He had been, in the Castle, acutely aware of any plateau as a sign that time was stalling, that the arc was not moving. He thought now: a professional plateau at a normal rate is not a stall. It is normal.

He wrote in his journal: *14 September — third week tally: 4 completed, 2 new, correspondence current. This is approximately my usual rate. The plateau has been reached. I am not worried about the plateau.*

He read this back. He found it accurate. He noted that it was the first tally entry where he had explicitly stated that he was not worried about something, as distinct from simply not recording worry. Noting the absence of worry seemed, on reflection, like a sign that the worry had recently been present. He did not write this.

He closed the journal and went home.
