## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He wrote to Mina in the afternoon.

He wrote about the office — two matters filed, a new instruction expected from Hawkins. He wrote about the house — Mrs. Hawkins had aired the study, which she does when he is away; it was a good day for it. He wrote that he hoped the sea air was helping.

He wrote a paragraph about the Lucy entries — that he had noticed himself tracking her condition, that he thought this was probably useful, that he hoped the tracking had a positive terminus. He read this paragraph back. It was accurate. It was also, he thought, a paragraph about his own patterns more than it was a letter to Mina about her friend's illness. He removed it.

He sent the letter without the paragraph.

He noted in his journal: *6 September — wrote to Mina. Removed one paragraph before sending. The paragraph said: I have been tracking Lucy's condition the way I tracked things in the Castle, and I do not know what I am tracking toward, and I hope it resolves well.* He wrote this in the journal in full, because the journal was the correct place for it.

He found it useful that the journal was the correct place for things the letters were not.
