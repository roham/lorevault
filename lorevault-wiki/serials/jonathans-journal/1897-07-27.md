## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He was ill.

He woke feverish — he could tell by the way his handwriting felt unreliable before he had written anything; the hand's feedback had changed. He had been unwell before in his life and recognised the quality of the recognition.

He remained in the room. He ate the bread that was brought to him. He drank water.

He tried to write and found the entry was shorter than he intended. He wrote: *27 July —*

And then he wrote: *ill. Feverish. In the village. Coat: under pillow. Journal: present.*

He had put the coat under his pillow. He had not decided to do this; he had done it and noted it afterward. The documents in the coat — the three letters, the second brief, the Count's letter — were under his head.

He slept.
