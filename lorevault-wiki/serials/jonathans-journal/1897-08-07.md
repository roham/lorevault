## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He counted the days.

He had kept the count as he went — each entry dated, the dates sequential — but he had not sat down and counted the total until August 7. He counted from May 3 (the first entry, Munich) to August 7. Ninety-seven days.

He had been writing this journal for ninety-seven days. He had arrived at the Castle on May 5 and left on July 22. He had been in the Castle for seventy-eight days.

He wrote: *7 August — 97 days since May 3. 78 days in the Castle. 16 days since leaving. The counts are: those.*

He noted: he had always counted things. Crates, courses, dates, intervals, distances. He had counted his way through the Castle and out of it. The counts were: those. He had arrived at them correctly and they held.

He had no immediate use for the counts. He wrote them anyway. The counting habit had got him through the Castle; he would not abandon it on August 7 simply because the need was less obvious.
