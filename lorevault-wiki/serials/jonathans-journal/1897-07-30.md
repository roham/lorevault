## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He was better.

He could confirm this because the fever had the quality of something in retreat — still present, but moving backward. He had experience of illness as a child; he had the sense of which direction the fever was going. It was going.

The nun — Sister Agatha, he had learned her name — read to him in the afternoon. She read from a book he did not recognise, in Romanian; he could not follow the content but he could follow the quality of it: slow, rhythmic, not demanding. He did not need to understand it. He needed not to be silent.

He wrote: *30 July — better. Sister Agatha read. I did not follow the words. I followed the sound. It was sufficient.*

He noted: for eighty-seven days (May 3 to July 28) he had been the only source of text in his life. He had written and read and re-read his own entries. A voice reading something he could not follow was the first new text he had encountered. He had not known he needed it until he was receiving it.
