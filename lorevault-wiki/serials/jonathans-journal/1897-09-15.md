## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

In the evening he picked up one of the old journals.

Not the current one — one of the first two, the ones from May through August 11. He picked up the first one: the notebook begun in March 1897, the one with the Castle entries starting May 3. He looked at the cover. He had used a brown cover — a practical notebook, the kind he uses for professional work; he had not expected to need a different kind of notebook for what he was going to record.

He held it for approximately three minutes. He did not open it.

He put it back on the shelf.

He wrote in his journal: *15 September — picked up the first notebook. Held it. Did not open it. Three minutes, approximately.*

He looked at this notation. He had counted the time again — automatically, the reflex — and in this case the time was accurate information. Three minutes holding a journal he has not been able to open since August 26. He had been closer to opening it than he had been on August 26, when he had only looked at the spine. This was a distinguishable step.

He thought: I am tracking this too. He found he did not mind.
