## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He passed a man on the road.

A farmer, from his dress — moving in the opposite direction, toward the pass. Jonathan had not spoken to another person since the July 10 courtyard approach (the worker who would not enter the register of his offer). Before that: the Count, whose conversation had been of a specific kind. Before that: he could not say precisely. The last conversation that resembled an ordinary exchange had been with the Count's coachman in May, and even that had had its particular character.

He said, in German: Good day.

The man looked at him. He said: Good day.

They passed each other and continued in their respective directions.

He wrote: *24 July — a man on the road. Good day. Good day. The exchange was complete.*

He noted: *the exchange was complete*. He had managed, in the course of a morning, a complete social transaction. Subject, verb, response. He had not lost the capacity for ordinary exchange. He had not been certain he had retained it.

He had retained it. He wrote this down.
