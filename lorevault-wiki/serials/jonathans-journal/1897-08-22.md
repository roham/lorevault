## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He spent the morning on a Devon conveyance.

A property near Tiverton: the client wished to transfer a parcel to a family member. Title abstract, three generations. He checked each generation in order, working backward: the current holder, the 1871 transfer, the 1849 original. Clear at each point. No encumbrances, no disputes in the registry, no annotations that required explanation. A clean title.

He drafted the abstract. He drafted the requisitions. He drafted the transfer summary. He reviewed each document in order. Each was accurate. He initialed the file.

He has done this hundreds of times. He has done this since his second year as an article clerk, when Hawkins the elder first assigned him a conveyancing matter on his own and he had understood that this was the work — the careful reading of documents to find the gap between what a property was supposed to be and what it actually was. The work was finding the gap and, if no gap existed, saying so.

No gap existed in the Tiverton property. He said so in the abstract, accurately.

He wrote in his journal: *22 August — Tiverton conveyance complete. Clean title. Filed.*

He looked at the notation. Then he added: *The work I did before May 3 and the work I am doing now are the same work. The interval between does not appear in any professional document.* He reviewed this. It was accurate. He initialed the journal entry.
