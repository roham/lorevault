## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He went to the office.

There was work waiting: correspondence from before May 3, routine matters held through the summer, entries in the ledger he maintains that had been annotated in his absence by Hawkins the elder in a careful and adequate hand. He sat at his desk. His desk was as he left it, mostly — the papers had been straightened; everything was in the correct place.

In the bottom drawer he found a small stack of notes in his own handwriting.

He recognized the hand immediately. It is clean, even, the hand of a person who writes specifications for a living and has been writing specifications for a living for some years. The hand before the Castle. He developed, in the Castle, a different hand — smaller, compressed, pressed harder into the paper, the product of writing in circumstances where each notation might be final. He has been watching this hand moderate, since Budapest, toward something between the two.

He read the notes. They were professional in the ordinary sense: a client query, a land search, a letter to a colleague about a matter of procedure. They were written by a Jonathan Harker who was, at the time of writing, completely informed about where he was going and had no information about what would happen there.

He is reading his own notes from before he knew.

He filed the notes in the correct place. He wrote in his journal: *18 August — found notes in the pre-Castle hand. Recognizable. The hand I was before. Not quite mine now, but I expect it will be again.*

He was not certain of this. He wrote it anyway, as a reasonable professional projection.
