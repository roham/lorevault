## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

A letter arrived from Mina.

She writes from Whitby: the weather has been variable; the town is beautiful in the rain; she and Lucy have been walking along the cliff and sitting in the churchyard above the harbor, which Lucy likes particularly. She says the churchyard is a good place to sit and think. She says Lucy has not been sleeping well for the past week or two — she looks pale, she tires easily; Dr. Seward, who is Lucy's friend as well as her physician, has been attending; he is unable to find anything specifically wrong but recommends fresh air and rest. Mina says Whitby has plenty of both; the sea air must help.

He read this through once. He read it again.

He noted in his journal: *2 September — letter from Mina. Lucy Westenra is unwell. Sleeping badly, pale. Dr. Seward attending. Mina says the sea air will help.* He paused. He wrote: *I will reply today.*

He did not write anything further in the journal about Lucy. He had never met Lucy Westenra. He knew her through Mina's letters — a cheerful and lively person, a good correspondent. He did not know what it meant that she was pale and sleeping badly. He thought: Mina will write more when there is more to write.

He set the letter beside the postcard and wrote his reply to Mina.
