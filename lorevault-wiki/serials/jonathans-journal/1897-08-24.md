## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

Mina mentioned at supper that she was thinking of going to Whitby.

Lucy had written that she would like company before the summer ended; the Westenras were staying through early September; Mina had always meant to visit. She looked at him as she said this, which he understood to mean she was asking whether he had objections.

He had no objections. He said: of course, go.

He wrote in his journal: *24 August — Mina is considering a visit to Miss Westenra in Whitby. Whitby: approximately six hours from London via York.* He noted the rail route he knew. He noted that he was noting the train journey rather than the purpose of the visit and recognized this as a persisting reflex — the logistics before the people.

He considered this. The purpose of the visit was: two friends who had not seen each other in some months, during which period one of the friends' husbands had been in a castle in Transylvania. The logistics were: a train. He had written the train.

He added: *I will be working. She should go.* He found this accurate and left it.

He would be in Exeter. The caseload was manageable. Mina would be in Whitby with Lucy. He noted the dates she was considering. He made no additional notation.
