## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

End of September.

He tallied: eleven completed professional matters. Three in progress. Correspondence current. This was his best month since returning — better than August's ten, better than the weeks of adjustment in the first week.

He noted in his journal: *30 September — September tally: 11 completed matters, 3 in progress, correspondence current. Best month since return.*

He sat for a moment before closing the journal.

He thought about September. He thought about what September contained: Mina's departure and return; Lucy's death; the question with no letter; the Hawkins recommendation; the walk to the cathedral close; the first page of March 1897 in the Castle journal. And eleven completed professional matters.

He wrote: *Lucy Westenra died in September 1897. I completed eleven professional matters in September 1897.*

He looked at these two sentences. They are true simultaneously. They do not explain each other. They are both in September and both in the ledger and the ledger has no field that connects them — not because the connection is absent, but because a ledger is not the instrument for it.

He wrote: *The ledger continues. Its scope has not changed. September is in it.*

He closed the journal on September.
