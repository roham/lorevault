## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

At half past one in the morning he heard it: a sound from below, then the sound of horses, then the particular quiet of a vehicle moving over the courtyard stones, fading. He had the watch already in his hand — he did not know when he had reached for it. He listened. From the first sound to silence: three minutes.

He wrote in his journal: *21 May — three minutes.*

He looked at the line. He thought: I am becoming the person who writes the times. He did not know when this had started. The wall descent: seven minutes. The courtyard gathering: eleven minutes. The departure: three minutes. He had a series. He did not yet have a reason for the series, only the habit of recording it.

He put down the watch. He thought: if the Count has departed, I am alone in the castle. He tested this thought for alarm and found none — or none that was new. He had been alone in the castle, functionally, since the first night. The Count's presence had always been a condition imposed on him, not a protection.

He did not attempt the doors.
