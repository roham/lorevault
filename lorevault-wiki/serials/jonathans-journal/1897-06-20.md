## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He estimated the drop from his window to the courtyard below.

He did this methodically, as he would have measured the dimensions of a room in a property transaction: using the height of a known reference (the large entrance doors, which he had walked through on arrival), scaling upward to the window. He arrived at approximately seventy feet.

Seventy feet onto stone courtyard flagging. He held this figure for some time.

He was not certain whether this was a calculation toward escape or simply an exercise in the professional habit of measurement — he had spent forty-eight days measuring and documenting the Count's property, and measurement was now something he did automatically.

He wrote: *20 June — approx. 70 ft to courtyard. Eastern face. The stones of the wall extend outward at intervals — decorative courses, perhaps structural — spaced roughly 18 inches apart.*

He then wrote: *I do not know how I know the spacing of the stones.*

He had not been counting them deliberately. The count was simply there when he looked for it. The professional observer's habit of accumulating incidental data had been running without his directing it. He had been cataloguing the Castle since May 4 without deciding to. He had the measurements of his own captivity noted in a document he had been maintaining on behalf of his captor.
