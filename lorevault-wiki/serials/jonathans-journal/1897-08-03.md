## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He considered what to do with the journal.

The question had been forming since July 31 (not ready) and August 2 (the memory not in the record). He had options:

He could destroy it. The brief on June 12 had been written to be burned; he had not burned it. The journal was a more complete document. Destroying it would resolve the question of whether he could explain everything to Mina: it would remove the explaining.

He could keep it closed and carry it until he knew whether it was needed.

He could give it to someone else to read first — to Mina, or to Mr. Hawkins, or to someone with the professional standing to evaluate it.

He wrote: *3 August — three options: destroy, keep closed, give to Mina. I will keep it closed. I will give it to Mina when she arrives. She will read it if she chooses. I will not read it first.*

He noted: this was a decision. He had made one declaration of intent (July 19) and it had led to an action (the descent). He was making a second.

## Tether Note

*The journal's disposition* — Jonathan applies the decision framework (three options, weighted) to the question of the journal's future. The decision: keep it closed, give it to Mina, let her read it if she chooses. He explicitly decides not to read it first. This echoes the June 21 advance letters (E-GH1-045) — documents sent without Jonathan fully controlling their reception — but inverted: he is choosing to give the journal to the person he trusts most, not to the person who holds him. Echo E-GH1-088: "the journal's disposition" — three options considered; the decision to give rather than destroy; Mina named as the journal's intended reader.
