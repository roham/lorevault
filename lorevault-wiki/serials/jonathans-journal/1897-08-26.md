## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He looked at the shelf where he keeps his journals.

There were three. This is more than he usually has — he fills one journal in a year, sometimes two if a year has been eventful. Three in a single year was new.

The first: begun March 1897, the record of the spring before the journey. Pages of professional notes, ordinary life, a few entries about the Transylvania commission. The Castle entries begin on May 3. He had not looked at these since returning.

The second: a second notebook he had begun in the Castle, when the first was nearly full. He had continued it through the Bistritz recovery and into Budapest. It ended on August 11.

The third: the current journal, begun August 12.

He did not open the first two. He looked at them on the shelf. He noted the dates on the spines — he had written the dates in the front of each notebook when he began it — and he calculated: May 3 through August 11 in the first two notebooks combined. August 12 to present in the third.

He wrote: *26 August — looked at the journals. Three notebooks. May 3 to August 11 in the first two. August 12 to present in the third.* He added the total: *176 days covered across three notebooks.*

He counted the days again to verify the arithmetic. The arithmetic was correct.

He did not read what was in the first two notebooks. He was not ready to do this yet. He thought he would probably know when he was ready. He wrote this, and then filed the journal on the shelf beside the others.
