## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

A letter from Lord Godalming.

Arthur Holmwood — Lucy's fiancé; he inherited the title from his father in late September. He wrote to thank Mina for her letter and her friendship with Lucy; he wrote that Lucy had spoken warmly of Mina; he wrote in the correct form of condolence acknowledgment, which is a recognized professional form. His phrasing was precise.

He read the letter after Mina and set it down.

He noted in his journal: *28 September — letter from Lord Godalming (Arthur Holmwood). Acknowledged Mina's condolence. Lucy had spoken warmly of Mina.*

He thought about Arthur Holmwood — whom he had never met, any more than he had met Lucy. He knew Holmwood through Lucy's letters as described by Mina: a decent man, devoted. He had lost Lucy. The letter's precise phrasing was the form a decent man uses when his grief is not yet available for ordinary expression.

He recognized this form. He had used it, or something like it, in certain entries since May. He did not write this in the journal.
