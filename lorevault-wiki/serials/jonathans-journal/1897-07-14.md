## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The Count visited after midnight.

He did not explain why he came after midnight. His hours had never been the hours of daylight — Jonathan had noted this in May, documented it, filed it in the ledger of things observed without explanation. The Count visited at the hours he visited.

He said: his preparations were complete. He would be leaving the Castle, probably tomorrow, perhaps the day after. He wished Jonathan to remain at the Castle until further transportation arrangements could be made on his behalf. This would not be long.

Jonathan thanked him.

He wrote: *14 July — the Count's departure is tomorrow or the day after. I am to remain. Transportation will be arranged. He said "not long."*

He noted: *not long* was another phrase without a terminus. He had catalogued these since June 19. An arrangement without a terminus. As far as is practicable. Not long.

The Count had a vocabulary for bounded-but-unspecified durations that Jonathan was collecting against his will.
