## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He tried to remember when they had last met.

He and the Count had had several professional dinners in the first weeks — not formal meetings, but the kind of sustained conversation in which the Count had asked his questions and Jonathan had answered them. These had been useful for the Count; Jonathan had understood their function clearly enough. He had been glad of them because they had been scheduled and he had known when they would occur.

He went back through the journal. The last entry that mentioned the Count by name was May 17 — the letter-copying session. There had been the courtyard observation on June 3, but the Count had not been aware of Jonathan's presence. There had been no direct meeting, no dinner, no professional conversation, since May 17.

That was twenty-two days.

He thought: the Count no longer needs the pretense of a professional relationship. He thought: I cannot tell exactly when this became true because it was not a decision; it was an absence.

He wrote in his journal: *8 June — last meeting: May 17. Twenty-two days.*

## Tether Note

*The dissolving relationship* — Jonathan attempts to locate the end of the professional relationship and finds he cannot mark it precisely: it ended not with a decision but with an absence. The last meeting is datable only in retrospect, by searching backward through the journal. The relationship between the Count and Jonathan has reached its functional end — the Count has what he needed — and Jonathan can identify the last interaction but not the moment of ending.

The twenty-two-day gap is the invisible second phase of the captivity: the first phase was active management (professional dinners, letter-copying, the Count's questions); the second phase is simply presence without purpose.
