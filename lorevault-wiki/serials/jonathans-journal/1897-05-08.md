## Source — Bram Stoker, *Dracula* (1897), Jonathan Harker's Journal, Chapter 2

> *I have been unable to sleep. I used to keep a journal of my work and thoughts because I did not want to forget, and also because I thought it would be pleasant for Mina to read what I had been doing when I was away from her. Now I find myself writing because I do not know what else to do. I keep this journal that I may not forget; and I am not sure that I will be able to tell Mina what I have felt. There is something I cannot put into words — not because the words do not exist, but because writing them would make them real in a way I am not ready for.*

## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

What Jonathan could not write: there was a portrait in the long corridor between the library and the east wing. The portrait was of a woman, unnamed, in a dress that was not contemporary to the Castle. He had passed it three times before he realized that she was looking at the door behind him — not the painter, not the viewer, but something specific at a specific location in the room where she had sat. He turned twice to see what she was looking at. The third time he did not turn.

That night, Jonathan wrote Mina a letter that did not mention the portrait, the east-wing window, or the blank page of May 6. The letter said that he was well, that the work was nearly done, and that he would be home before the 20th. He folded it and addressed it. He did not look for the post box.

## Tether Note

The portrait is the first named-but-unseen figure in GH-1 *The Castle* — she appears before Dracula is ever explicitly introduced, and she is looking at something Jonathan cannot face. Per MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md: a MIRROR-layer reading of this moment would ask whether she is a past Harker; the DREAM-layer would ask whether she is a future Mina. The PRIME layer simply records that she is looking at a door, and Jonathan stops turning around. Spine-event: Jonathan decides what not to write. The journal ceases to be a complete record.
