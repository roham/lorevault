## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He found a letter under his door.

It was from the Count. He had not previously received written communication from the Count — their entire engagement had been conducted in person, in the Count's rooms or his own. The letter was brief, formal, and in a hand he recognised from the June 21 drafts he had been asked to copy.

It said: preparations were complete. He wished Jonathan to be ready to depart on short notice — perhaps as early as the following week, perhaps somewhat later. He would be informed at the appropriate time. He trusted Jonathan remained well and comfortable.

Jonathan read it twice.

He wrote: *6 July — a letter from the Count. First written communication since I arrived. He is ready. I am to be ready. The date is not specified. I do not know why he chose to write rather than visit.*

He held the letter. He noted that it was, for the first time in the engagement, a document from the Count in the Count's own hand addressed to Jonathan. He had the letter; the Count had had his three letters since June 21. The documentary exchange was now bilateral, at least formally.

He filed it in the journal cover, with the second brief.
