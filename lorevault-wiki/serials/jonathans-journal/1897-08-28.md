## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The house was quiet in the evening.

He noticed this after supper — the quality of the quiet. He has been noting qualities of silences since May; he had not thought this would apply to the parlor in Exeter. But it did apply, because there are different kinds of quiet and the skill, if it is a skill, is distinguishing them.

This quiet: chosen. He is alone in the house because Mina is traveling. The house is not quiet because someone has locked the doors or because the alternatives to quiet are dangerous. It is quiet because it is an ordinary evening and he is in it alone, by ordinary circumstance. There are sounds from the street. He can hear Mrs. Hawkins in the kitchen. The quality is domestic solitude, which is different from the Castle's solitude in every way he can name and a few ways he cannot name yet.

He sat with this for a while.

He wrote in his journal: *28 August — quiet evening. The different kind. The kind that is not the Castle's kind.* He paused and then added: *I can name the difference. This is something.*

He made tea himself because Mrs. Hawkins had gone home. The kettle was familiar. He drank the tea in the chair by the window, which has a view of the street, and looked at the street, and found the street accurately itself.
