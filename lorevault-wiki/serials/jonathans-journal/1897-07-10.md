## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He went down to the courtyard.

There was a worker there — one of the men he had seen on the earlier crate-loading days, compact and quick in his movements, not one he had spoken to. Jonathan had not spoken to any of the workers since May. He went down and approached this man in the courtyard and took out the letter from his coat.

He said, in German: please take this letter to Bistritz. He would pay.

The man looked at him. He looked at the letter. He looked away.

Jonathan waited. He held the letter out.

The man continued working without acknowledging the letter or Jonathan's presence. After a time Jonathan put the letter back in his coat and returned to his rooms.

He wrote: *10 July — tried to pass a letter to a worker in the courtyard. German. The worker did not take it. He did not refuse; he did not acknowledge. He simply continued.*

He noted the distinction. The worker had not refused, precisely. He had simply not engaged with the transaction. Jonathan had offered something in a register the worker had decided not to enter. He understood, walking back to his room, that this was not the worker's decision to make.

## Tether Note

*The worker who would not* — Jonathan attempts delivery and encounters a refusal that is not stated as a refusal: the worker does not engage with the transaction. Jonathan's analysis is precise — the worker had not refused; he had simply not entered the register of the offer. This is the same pattern as June 19's "arrangement without terminus" (E-GH1-043): a situation with the form of an exchange and none of its substance.
