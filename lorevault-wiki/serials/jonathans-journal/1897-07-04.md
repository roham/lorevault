## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The Count visited in the evening.

It was their first conversation since June 24, when the Count had named the departure date. Jonathan noted this interval: ten days without direct address. In a professional engagement, ten days without contact from the client would be unremarkable. In these circumstances it had felt like something else, though Jonathan could not name what.

The Count inquired after his health. Jonathan confirmed he was well.

The Count said: matters were proceeding. The property arrangements were advancing. He expected to complete his departure from the Castle within the fortnight. Jonathan would, naturally, accompany the arrangements as far as was practicable.

Jonathan thanked him.

He wrote: *4 July — the Count. First conversation in ten days. He is leaving within the fortnight. I will accompany the arrangements "as far as is practicable." I do not know what this means.*

He noted: *as far as is practicable* was a phrase he had used in legal correspondence himself. It was a phrase that acknowledged a limit without specifying the limit. He had always found it a useful formulation. He now found it less useful.
