## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He did not see the Count leave.

He had expected to. He had been at the east window through most of the morning. No carriage came. No unusual movement in the courtyard. The castle was quiet in the way it had been quiet since July 8 — the acoustic emptying that had not reversed.

He checked the Count's rooms in the afternoon. The doors were unlocked — this was new; they had been locked since May. The rooms were empty. The writing desk was bare. The personal objects he had seen when he first arrived (the candlestick, the particular quality of the inkwell) were gone.

He wrote: *15 July — the Count's rooms are unlocked and empty. He has left. I did not observe the departure.*

He noted: he had been waiting to observe it. He had been at the window. He had not seen anything. The Count had left in the interval between observations, or in the interval between something and something else that Jonathan could no longer specify.

He wrote: *I am now waiting. I do not know for what. The journal is open.*

## Tether Note

*The unobserved departure* — Jonathan was at the window and did not see the Count leave; the rooms are empty when he checks. The Count has left in an interval — between observations, or between something and something else that Jonathan "can no longer specify." The unobserved departure closes the Count's arc in the journal: Jonathan arrived, documented the Count's property, was claimed, watched the preparations, and missed the actual moment of departure. The journal closes the entry with "The journal is open" — the record is running; there is nothing yet to record.
