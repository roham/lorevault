## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

End of the month.

He does a professional tally at the end of each month — matters completed, matters in progress, correspondence outstanding. He did this: ten completed matters in August, three in progress, correspondence current.

He then sat for a while before closing the journal.

He had been doing this differently since May — since May the journal had been more than professional tally; it had been the record, the evidence, the instrument. August had been the first month since April where the journal was not doing this additional work. He reviewed August's entries: the Budapest departure, Austria through a train window, the channel, Exeter, the chair, the notes in the pre-Castle hand, the Carfax file, the Devon abstract, the three notebooks. These were ordinary entries. They did not require the Castle's documentation practices.

He wrote: *31 August — tally complete. Ten completed matters. First full calendar month since April without an extraordinary entry. I am noting this.*

He considered whether noting the first ordinary month was itself a slightly extraordinary act. He had written something similar before, about the first ordinary entry. He let it stand. He thought: I am getting better at this, but slowly, and the notations are part of getting better.

He closed the journal on August. September tomorrow.
