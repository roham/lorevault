## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

They left Bistritz by carriage.

He had arrived in Bistritz by carriage on May 4, on the way to the Castle. He was leaving Bistritz by carriage on August 10, on the way somewhere else. The road was the same road, westward; the direction was reversed. He noted this in the professional register and did not produce any further analysis of it.

He had said goodbye to Sister Agatha. She had given him a small cross on a cord; he had accepted it without hesitation. On May 4 the proprietress of this same hotel had offered a cross and he had initially been uncertain (he had written about his uncertainty then; it was in the journal, somewhere before August; it was in the part he had not read). He accepted it this time without uncertainty.

He wrote, from the carriage: *10 August — leaving Bistritz. Westward. Sister Agatha gave a cross. I took it.*

He noted: he had not written why he took it without hesitation. He did not try to explain it. He took it. He wrote it. The explaining could wait.
