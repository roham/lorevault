## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

October.

He wrote the date at the top of the new entry — *1 October 1897* — and did the first-of-the-month recalibration. Three in-progress matters from September carrying forward. Two new instructions expected from Hawkins before the end of the week. The leasehold recommendation he had submitted on September 29 was likely to generate a response and further instruction.

He projected: if the matters proceeding normally, October should be comparable to September's eleven. He did not commit this projection to the record; projections are not evidence.

He noted: *1 October 1897. October. Three in progress. Timetable set.*

He turned the journal page. He had been keeping the current journal — the post-August-12 journal — in the same notebook for nearly seven weeks. He was approaching the halfway point of the notebook. He noted this without particular concern.

He thought: September is completed; it is in the ledger; October has its own ledger page. He found this accurate. He closed the journal and went to the office.
