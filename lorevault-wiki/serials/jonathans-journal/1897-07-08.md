## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The castle was quiet.

Not the quiet of late at night — he knew that quiet, had catalogued it since May. This was a different quality: the quiet of a house from which most of the contents had been removed. An empty room has a different acoustic character than a furnished one; he had observed this in property work, surveying houses in advance of sale. The sounds of the Castle — the structural creaks, the wind in the corridors, the sound of the Count moving in the lower rooms — had changed. They had the quality of sound in an empty space.

He went to the chapel to recount. Eleven crates remained.

He wrote: *8 July — the castle is quieter than it was. Chapel: 11 crates remaining. The removal has continued since July 2 without my observing it. I do not know how they are moving them.*

He noted: he had been in the castle for sixty-six days. For sixty-six days the sounds had been one set of sounds. On July 8 they were different. He had spent sixty-six days learning a house, and the house was now becoming something else.

He could not say what it was becoming. He wrote down the eleven crates and left it there.
