## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He had been sleeping more than usual.

Not from exhaustion — he was not doing enough to be exhausted. But in the afternoons particularly he found himself in the chair with no memory of deciding to sleep, and when he woke the light had changed in ways that implied more time had passed than felt like the correct amount.

He was not afraid of sleep itself. He was aware, precisely, of what he knew before going to sleep and what he knew on waking. Those two states were continuous and reliable. It was the interval between that had become unreliable — not blank, not empty, but soft in the way that a memory seen through water is soft. There but not fully present.

He had noted this beginning, perhaps, in the middle of May. He had not written it then. He wrote it now.

He wrote in his journal: *9 June — sleep: intervals soft. Duration uncertain.*

## Tether Note

*The soft interval* — Jonathan's description of the changed sleep register applies the same vocabulary as the May 16 blank page and May 29 date-only entry, but locates the problem in a different dimension: not the record but the experience itself. The intervals between sleep and waking are not inaccessible; they are accessible as "soft" — present but not fully retrievable. This is the first entry in which Jonathan identifies a change in his own experience rather than in the documents or instruments available to him. Something is happening to the experiencer, not only the record.
