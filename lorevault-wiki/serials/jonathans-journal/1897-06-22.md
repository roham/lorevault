## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

From the east window, in the late afternoon, he watched men working in the courtyard below.

They were loading large wooden crates onto a wagon. The crates were heavy — he could tell from the labour of moving them — and they were not secured with the care he would have expected for goods of commercial value. The men worked quickly and without ceremony. He counted the crates: seventeen. He watched until the wagon moved out of sight through the castle gate.

He wrote: *22 June — 17 crates. Wooden. Heavy. Eastward through the gate.*

He did not know what the crates contained. He had not been told and had not asked. He had the instinct, from his professional training, that the number might matter later — that a count taken now was worth something even if the relevance was not yet visible.

He added: *The soil here has a quality I noticed on arrival. I noted it then without recording why. I am noting it now again. The crates smelled of it.*

He had not previously written down the smell. He did not know why he wrote it now. He added it to the ledger of observations whose purpose he could not yet account for. He had been keeping that ledger since May 4 without deciding to. The entries were accumulating their meaning at a rate he could not control.
