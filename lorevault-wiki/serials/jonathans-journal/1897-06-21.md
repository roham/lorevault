## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

The Count visited his rooms in the morning.

He produced three sheets of paper, written upon in a hand Jonathan did not recognise. He explained that these were letters Jonathan was to copy in his own hand. The contents of the first: he was well, his work at the Castle was nearly concluded, he expected to leave within the week. The second: he was departing the following morning. The third: he had arrived safely in Bistritz and was in good health.

The Count said: they were to be sent at intervals. To reassure those at home.

Jonathan copied them.

He noted, afterward, that he had done this without discussion. He had read the letters, understood their purpose — they were instruments for establishing, at a distance, a version of events that had not occurred — and he had copied them in his professional hand. He had written himself out of captivity on three separate occasions, in his own handwriting, without leaving the room.

He wrote in his journal: *21 June — three letters. Copied. Contents noted. The handwriting is mine. The dates are not specified.*

The dates were not specified. When they were to be sent, and in what sequence, and what interval should elapse between them: none of this had been arranged. He was to trust that they would be sent correctly. The professional matter of his own safe return home had been delegated to his captor.
