## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He checked the second brief.

He had written it on June 12 (the summary: May 3 through June 11, every material fact, folded into the back cover of the journal). He had not burned it. He had kept it as the only document he controlled. He took it out on June 28 and read through it.

The facts were correct. The dates were correct. The material sequence — arrival, professional engagement, progressive loss of egress, soft intervals, ownership claim, advance letters, chapel discovery — was accurate. It was a solicitor's brief for a situation in which the solicitor was the subject.

He folded it back into the journal cover.

He wrote: *28 June — tomorrow. The brief is in order. The facts are correct.*

He had spent four days on the question of what to do with what he had found in the chapel. He had not arrived at an answer. He had verified the facts on June 26, confirmed the central fact on June 27, and had no next professional step available. The brief documented everything. There was no one to submit it to.

Tomorrow was June 29. The carriage would come, or it would not. He would be on it, or he would not. He had the brief in the journal cover.

That was what he had.
