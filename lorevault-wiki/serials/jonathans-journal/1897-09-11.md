## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He thought about the word *provisional*.

He had written it on September 9 without particular deliberation — accuracy requires marking uncertainty, and he had been uncertain. He had not known if the improvement would hold. It had not held. The word had been right.

He thought about this during the morning, not anxiously, but as a professional matter. He had been, in the Castle, developing documentation practices under extreme uncertainty — everything he wrote there was provisional in some sense, because everything he observed might have been wrong, might have been staged, might not have continued to be true by the time anyone read it. He had written with that uncertainty embedded. He had not always marked it explicitly, but it had been there.

He is marking it explicitly now.

He noted in his journal: *11 September — the word "provisional" was accurate. I wrote it because accuracy requires marking uncertainty. The uncertainty was correct.* He paused, then added: *This is a documentation practice I would like to keep.*

He found this useful to say. A solicitor who marks uncertainty accurately is a more reliable solicitor than one who does not. The Castle had taught him the cost of encountering facts that the record had not marked as uncertain. He would note this.

He made tea and went back to work.
