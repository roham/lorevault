## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

There were four doors on the east corridor. Jonathan tried them in sequence, the way a methodical man would, left to right. Three were locked. The fourth opened so readily that he stepped back — the latch was loose; it had not been locked but merely rested shut. The room beyond was empty and cold. Its window was the only one he had found at a climbable height. He stood at it for some time, looking down. The drop was not impossible. He did not write this in the journal.

Later, at dinner, the Count said he hoped Jonathan had enjoyed his walk in the castle. Jonathan said the library was very fine. The Count said yes, the library was very fine. He said this in a tone that made Jonathan understand, without any further exchange, that the Count knew which rooms Jonathan had been in, and in what order, and how long he had spent at the window.
