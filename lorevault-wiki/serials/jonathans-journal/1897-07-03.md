## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He went down to the gate.

He had tried the main entrance on May 15 — locked, no key offered. He had not returned to it since. On July 3 he went again, this time with the purpose of examination rather than the purpose of leaving. The professional habit: when you cannot solve a problem, study it.

The gate was a double door of heavy timber banded with iron. There were two locks, both mortice. He examined them closely. The escutcheons were old — seventeenth century, he estimated, though he could not be certain. The bolts, from the visible edge of the door, were of a newer installation than the locks: perhaps Victorian, perhaps earlier. The wood around the lower hinge was sound.

He wrote: *3 July — the gate. Two mortice locks, both engaged. Bolts: Victorian-era or earlier, well-set. The hinges are sound. The door is solid. No key visible in the lock from this side. I could not open it.*

He noted: the gate had been in the same condition since May 15. He had spent forty-nine days in the Castle without returning to study the primary egress point. He did not know what to make of this. He wrote it down anyway.
