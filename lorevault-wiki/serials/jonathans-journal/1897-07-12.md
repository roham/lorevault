## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He went to the chapel and found it empty.

No crates. The floor was marked with the impressions of where they had stood — rectangular depressions in the dust and grime, arranged in the five-by-ten grid he had documented on June 26. The smell of soil was faint, receding. The chapel itself, without the crates, was simply a disused chapel: cold, stone, empty of everything it had held.

He counted the floor impressions: fifty-two. He had counted fifty in June. The discrepancy was in the floor, not the count.

He wrote: *12 July — the chapel is empty. 52 floor impressions (previous count: 50; the floor is the authoritative document). All crates departed at unknown date between July 8 and today. The count is the count.*

He stood in the empty chapel for some time. He had first come here on June 26. He had counted the crates. He had found the Count in one of them. He had double-checked.

The floor was what remained of that. Fifty-two impressions in dust and grime.

He found that this was harder to leave than he had expected.
