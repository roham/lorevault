## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

His coat pockets were empty.

He reached in on his way to the library — a habit, not a deliberate check — and found the left pocket empty. He stopped. He checked the right pocket. Empty also.

The two letters he had kept there since May — the letter of May 13, which he had not been able to send; the letter of May 20, which he had written knowing it could not be sent — were gone. He had carried them for three and a half weeks. He had not thought about them every day. He had known they were there.

He searched his room carefully. He opened every drawer, every case, every jacket. He had not put them elsewhere; he knew he had not put them elsewhere.

He sat on the edge of the bed and thought about what it meant that they were gone. Someone had been in his room while he slept and had taken two folded letters from the left pocket of his coat. He thought: I have no record of when this happened. The soft intervals are not blank enough to provide an alibi.

He wrote in his journal: *11 June — pockets empty. May 13, May 20. Gone.*

## Tether Note

*The emptied pockets* — The two unsent Mina letters — the most private documents in Jonathan's possession, carried because they were real and unsendable — have been taken. The violation is precise: someone entered the room during one of the soft sleep intervals and removed the letters specifically. Jonathan notes he has no record of when this happened; the soft intervals cannot serve as an alibi for the room or for himself. The last private space has been accessed.
