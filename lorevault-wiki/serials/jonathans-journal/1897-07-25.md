## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

He was more tired than he had known.

He had been moving through the pass for three days on what he had believed was functional energy. On July 25 the functional energy ran out. He sat down beside the road in the early afternoon and found he could not immediately rise.

He took out the journal.

He did not write anything. He held it. He had been writing in it since May 3 and the habit of holding it was independent of the act of writing; it was simply the thing he held when he needed to hold something.

He sat for some time. He rose and continued.

He wrote, later: *25 July — tired. Sat beside the road. Held the journal. Rose. Continued.*

He noted: the entry was the shortest since June 18 (the blank entry with the marginal note). Five declarative sentences. He had held the journal without writing in it — a new category of use. The journal as physical object, independent of its documentary function. He had needed it for its weight.
