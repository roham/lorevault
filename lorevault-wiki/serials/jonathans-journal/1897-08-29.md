## Lampblack Detail — daemon annotation (not in Stoker)

A postcard arrived in the morning post.

Mina: arrived safely, Whitby is gray and very beautiful, the sea is enormous, Lucy met her at the station and looked very well. The postcard had a picture of the harbor on the front — the boats and the cliff beyond, and at the top of the cliff, the ruins of the abbey. He looked at the abbey in the picture. He thought: that is the abbey Lucy described in her letter. He turned the postcard over and read it again.

He put it on the desk.

He noted in his journal: *29 August — postcard from Mina. Arrived safely. Lucy met her at the station. Lucy looked well.* He wrote the date and the time of delivery: nine fifteen. He reviewed this notation. He recognized he had written the postcard delivery time and considered whether he needed to write the delivery time of a postcard from his wife saying she had arrived safely at the seaside. He decided to leave the delivery time. The postcard was information. Information had a time of delivery.

He set the postcard where he could see it from his desk. He found he liked having it there. This was not information; he noted it anyway.
