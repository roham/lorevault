# Research Synthesis — Three Visions

## Key Insight Per Vision

### Vision A: "Discover the Story"
**Insight**: Desire is created by the glimpsed-but-unreachable, not by the unknown. The locked door with a visible title creates more pull than a mystery. Cards should be named keys to named chapters — not abstract collectibles.

**Reference products**: Genshin Impact (literal padlocks on story quests), Florence (90-second emotional chapters), Infinity Blade III (gear-gated story branches).

### Vision B: "Chase the Set"
**Insight**: Named gaps create identity. "I'm the person who doesn't have Charizard yet" is an obsession loop that generic collecting can never match. The empty slot must be the CORRECT SIZE — the binder holds the shape of the missing card, making absence physical.

**Reference products**: Pokemon TCG Pocket (silhouette-with-frost treatment), Panini sticker albums (printed borders on empty slots), Pokemon GO (regional exclusives create named hunts).

### Vision C: "Play to Collect"
**Insight**: Found money asymmetry — items you find feel more valuable than items you buy. The "wait, this is worth money?" moment is the most powerful conversion trigger in gaming. But it requires 7-14 days of pure gameplay before marketplace exposure. You can't shortcut the emotional runway.

**Reference products**: CS:GO (free case drops worth hundreds), Marvel Snap (marketplace at Collection Level 500), Clash Royale (upgrade wall reveals scarcity).

---

## Biggest Risk Per Vision

| Vision | Risk | Severity |
|--------|------|----------|
| A: Story | "Story is slow." If pack opening doesn't trigger immediate story progress — if the user has to go somewhere else to read — the loop breaks. The reveal must happen AT the moment of collection. | Medium |
| B: Chase | "Everyone does this." The visible-gap-plus-packs mechanic is well-understood and well-replicated. Execution (the FEEL of the gap, binder quality, near-miss moments) must be exceptional to differentiate. | High |
| C: Play | "Digital items are worthless." Overcoming this assumption requires sustained gameplay, social proof, and zero expiry mechanics. The game itself must be genuinely fun — thin gameplay kills the whole chain. | High |

---

## Which Vision Has the Strongest Evidence Base?

**Vision A has the strongest UNIQUE position** — no major digital collectible app has made story progression the core collecting mechanic. The lore-graph data already exists. The "locked door" pattern is proven by Genshin but not yet applied to collectible cards.

**Vision B has the strongest PROVEN pattern** — the Panini/Pokemon binder mechanic has worked for 50 years. The risk is differentiation, not feasibility.

**Vision C has the strongest CEILING** — "found money asymmetry" is the most powerful psychological hook, and a game-first funnel could capture audiences who'd never download a "collectible" app. But it requires the longest runway and the highest execution bar on the game itself.

---

## Recommendation for Build Order

1. **Build Vision A first** (Cycles 7-12) — strongest unique position, existing data foundation, most novel approach. If it works, it's defensible.
2. **Build Vision B second** (Cycles 13-18) — proven pattern, execution-dependent. The binder quality will determine whether it's "another collectible app" or something special.
3. **Build Vision C third** (Cycles 19-24) — highest ceiling but highest risk. The battle system needs to be genuinely fun, which is the hardest thing to prototype.

---

## Cross-Vision Observation

All three visions share one pattern: **delay complexity, front-load the emotional hook.**
- Vision A: delay navigation, front-load the story reveal
- Vision B: delay marketplace, front-load the visible gap
- Vision C: delay value awareness, front-load the game fun

This is the universal anti-pattern to the current product: LoreVault shows EVERYTHING on day one and NOTHING is emotionally front-loaded.
