## Cycle 29: Hybrid Exploration

**Question**: Can the best elements of all three visions combine? Or does combining them recreate the "everything" problem?

### Finding: C+B works. Adding A to the game loop would break it.

The C+B hybrid succeeds because set-completion is a **passive scoreboard** — it adds meaning to winning without adding a second verb. The binder fills in the background. The player never has to stop battling to "manage their collection."

Story chapters (Vision A) are different. They require active engagement — reading, choosing, progressing through narrative. If embedded in the battle loop, they'd create a second mode that competes for attention. The player would face a fork: "Do I battle or do I read?"

**Resolution**: Story is a Season 2 retention layer, gated by binder completion. The bridge between seasons: one story chapter released as a battle event (narrative context, but the verb is still battle). This tests retention lift without redefining the product identity.

### Frigga Research: Hybrid Evidence

Key findings from final convergence research:

1. **Pokemon TCG Pocket launched hybrid** but collection (not battle) carried Season 1. Our inverse: battle carries Season 1, binder is passive.

2. **Identity split doesn't fracture users when the identity is "winner."** Hearthstone's "competitive deck-builder" absorbed both pack-opening and gameplay. The C+B hybrid's one-way valve (battle → cards → binder) prevents identity fracture. Valve's Artifact failed because winning at draft felt disconnected from owning valuable cards — two success signals.

3. **Game IS acquisition in**: Slay the Spire (combat = add card to deck), Fortnite Ch1 (eliminations = materials = victory), MTG Arena (draft wins = packs = collection). Players self-identify as players. Collecting is invisible infrastructure.

4. **Season 1 → Season 2**: Anchor identity in S1 so S2 enriches rather than redefines. Ship story as "depth unlocked by your collection," not "the real product you were missing." The Destiny 1 → Taken King arc in miniature.
