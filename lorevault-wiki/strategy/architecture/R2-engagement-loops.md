## 1. Daily Loop — *Jonathan's Journal* and the Footnoter's Dawn Note

The daily loop's job is to make a non-spending user open the app or an email *every single day* for a year, because that is the substrate Dracula Daily proved can grow from zero to 240K subs (A4 §2). Free Sample pack alone is anemic (G1 §3.6 and GAME-ECONOMY §9 "Daily moment reveal" flag). We stack three concrete beats into a single 2-minute morning ritual, one of which is a binding commitment the user opts into voluntarily.

**Beat A — *Jonathan's Journal* (the anchor).** A free daily email/push/in-app panel that serialises a public-domain novel in real time against its canonical in-novel calendar. Launch title: Stoker's *Dracula*, May 3 – November 7, 2026 (A4 §7 opp-1). One journal entry per day, on the day Jonathan Harker wrote it, illustrated with a single Lampblack-rich detail ripped from that day's text (the wolf at the pass, the blue flames over the earth). The daily panel is **one image, one quote (30–80 words), one one-line Footnoter marginalia**, and one "mark as read" tap. No pack, no offer, no chart. The promise is literary; the monetisation is downstream. After Dracula closes Nov 7, the serial rotates: *Watson's Casebook* (a rolling Conan Doyle reading pact, one case per fortnight, mid-case cliffhangers daily), *The Persephone Almanac* (seasonal myth fragments timed to equinoxes and lunar cycles), and in Topsy weeks, *The Footnoter's Dispatch*. This is the diasporic funnel from A4 §5 archetype 3 and the specific gap-closure G1 §3.6 lines 176 call for.

**Beat B — *Today on the Lattice*** (the surface tease). A single card of the day, drawn from the live Series catalog, surfaced on Home with one sentence of iceberg lore and *one visible Echo element* (an object in frame that doesn't belong, a dangling lore-note clause, a named-but-unseen second figure — per G1 §3.4 remediation). The user can tap to read the lore note; they cannot buy the card from this surface. The non-commerce of this surface is load-bearing: it is where the *Lattice itself* gets seen daily, independent of spend. Rotation is Council-curated weekly, not algorithmic — the same card can resurface across two different weeks with two different Echo pulls, which is how iceberg ratio is enforced at the *daily surface layer* and not just at the catalog layer.

**Beat C — Free Sample pack, re-framed.** The weekly free Sample pack (GAME-ECONOMY §6) does not become daily. Instead, the user gets a daily **"Sample ticket"** — a ledger entry, a stamp on a page, a single pomegranate seed — and seven collected tickets over seven days **become** the free Sample pack. This fixes the gap G1 flagged ("Free Sample alone is anemic") by making the pack itself feel *earned*, while giving us a daily return hook that is visibly progressing. Breaking the streak re-zeroes the ticket count; a reading pact (Beat A completion) restores a missed day once per month. Streak is the Pokémon Pocket daily-return primitive (A4 §3) minus the gacha nausea.

**Closing the loop.** The 2-minute ritual is: open → read today's Journal entry → see today's Lattice card → collect today's Sample ticket → leave. Any of the three can lead deeper — a Journal entry may link to a companion illustrated card; *Today on the Lattice* may show a Deep element that pays off only in a Set shipping three months later; the ticket page surfaces the user's full-year streak calendar as a Spine-of-return artifact. None of the three sells anything directly. That is the rule. The daily loop is the *brand*, not the funnel.

## 2. Weekly Loop — Sunday Lampblack-Tally and Topsy Installments

The weekly loop is the Sunday-night ritual that turns the Dracula Daily diaspora into a *community*. The daily loop hooks the individual; the weekly loop binds the cohort. Two anchors do the work.

**Beat A — *The Lampblack Tally* (Sunday 6pm UTC).** A weekly newsletter/in-app digest, Footnoter-voiced, three sections long, shipping every Sunday from rebirth week.
1. *The Week That Was* — a 200-word editorial recap of the week's Jonathan's Journal entries, written as if the Footnoter is reading alongside the audience, naming one Echo the community surfaced this week ("readers have begun to notice that the Demeter's log dates align suspiciously with a case Watson did not publish"). We *never* name a Deep element. We *often* confirm an Echo one week after it was spotted, which is the drip that builds the theorising engine.
2. *This Week's Residue* — a five-card strip showing the week's five *Today on the Lattice* surfaces with one newly-revealed-to-be-related detail linking two of them (Watson's cane shadow ↔ the gallows shadow in an Odysseus background). This is where the 1:2:4 iceberg doctrine (cosmology §1) becomes *visible* at a cadence the audience can anticipate.
3. *The Drop Horizon* — a one-line, un-hyped forecast of what ships in the next two weeks, framed as "The Pane of Inheritance is brightening" rather than "BUY PACKS NOW." Per the A4 §2 tired list and G1 §3.3 voice remediation, we strip trader register entirely.

**Beat B — Topsy Wednesdays (every fourth week).** Roughly once a month, Wednesday becomes **Mirror Wednesday** — a Topsy Pratchett-grade installment lands in the daily Journal slot. The voice shifts to the Footnoter (§7), the source fiction is mock-epistolary ("Received Tuesday by Lattice post, addressed to No One In Particular"), and the accompanying card is always MIRROR or DREAM shell (per Topsy Decisions 16–18). Mirror Wednesday is an internal label; fans will name it themselves. A/B we assume it becomes the most-shared day of the month. The 20% Topsy ratio (Topsy Decision 16) lands cleanly as ~1 of every 4–5 weekly arcs.

**Beat C — The Reading Pact (optional, self-selected).** A weekly commitment module users opt into: "I will read this week's Journal entries on the day they land, and open pack X at the week's close." Marvel Snap's Snap/Retreat tension primitive (A4 §3) reengineered for reading: commit, and your Sunday pack gets an odds bump (no new cards minted — the bump comes from *pack construction rules*, not supply inflation). Break the pact, lose the bump for two weeks. This gives the Dracula-Daily archetype and the Canon-Gamer archetype (A4 §5 archetypes 3 and 8) a tension-mechanic that matches their motivation; the gambler-collector ignores it and buys the Apex pack instead.

**Beat D — Sunday Showcase.** The week closes with a community Showcase spotlight — one user-built Showcase room (§6 retention driver) featured in the Tally newsletter, Council-picked, with a short Footnoter caption. The featured user receives an illustrated Footnoter marginalia card minted 1/1 for them. This is the *social surfacing* mechanism the Dracula-Daily Tumblr layer (A4 §2) produced organically and that we are choosing to scaffold deliberately at week-close rather than leave to algorithm.

The weekly loop is where the brand becomes *habitual*. Sunday night, Lampblack Tally, Mirror Wednesday — those three phrases should enter the vocabulary of the community by end of Series 1 quarter 1.

## 3. Monthly Loop — The Drop, Staged

Per SET-ROADMAP §5, most months ship two Sets; M1, M4, M5, M7, M10, M13, M16, M19, M22, M25, M27, M30, M33, M34 ship one. The monthly loop is **the drop itself**, staged over five days so the event lasts longer than the transaction.

**Day –3 — *The Tease*.** A single Surface image lands in the daily *Today on the Lattice* slot and the Lampblack Tally. It is not labeled as a drop teaser. It is a lore post: an object, a line of text, a compositional fragment. No title. No price. No pack tier. The Footnoter (on Topsy drops) or the set's chosen in-world narrator (Watson, Mycroft, the Wolf, the Hatter, the Phantom, the Pythia — per SET-ROADMAP voice-tethers) drops a single quoted sentence beneath it. Community theorises in Discord / Tumblr / Reddit for 72 hours.

**Day –1 — *The Cold-Open*.** 18 hours before drop, a two-minute animated / scrolling-web lore drop ships. Narrator-voiced (Sir Ian McKellen for Watson, Tilda Swinton for the Pythia, the Topsy Footnoter always an un-attributed British-Isles voice to preserve Decision 17's never-give-the-Footnoter-a-face rule). Shows the *Set's Spine* being walked — the canon events enacted in narrative sequence. Ends on the Ultimate Moment's silhouette with the Set name revealed. No price. No count. No CTA. This is the Dracula Daily aesthetic of *canon as the offer*, not pack as the offer.

**Day 0 — *Drop Day Live Event* (60 minutes, scheduled to avoid June 20 – July 15, 2026 — A4 §6 threat-1).** A 60-minute live stream opens pack sales. Universe-narrator commentary throughout — the narrator, in-world, opens packs alongside the community (*"Dr. Watson here; I am not certain why the universe has asked me to witness the opening of these boxes, but here we are"*). Live-revealed Legendary and Ultimate pulls get an in-stream shout-out and a 5-second silent pause — the Courtyard unbox-reveal emotional architecture (A4 §3). The Council joins at mid-stream for a live Iceberg Echo confirmation ("Yes, that smudge on card #7 is what you think it is"). Drop goes live mid-stream and remains open for 72 hours (not "until sold out" — FOMO chrome is banned per G1 remediation).

**Day +3 — *The Continuance*.** Three days after drop, a follow-up lore post ships in the Lampblack Tally: one Echo element from the new Set that connects back to a previous Set's Deep reference. "The cane Watson described in his BS-1 casebook has just appeared in the background of a GM-3 card; it is not captioned." This is the payoff moment for patient collectors; it is also the proof the iceberg is *working* at the monthly cadence, not just at the Series cadence. It is also the surface where we let the community name the new Lampblack residue (Cosmology §1 — *we let those names propagate. We do not publish them*).

**Day +7 — *The Primary-Close*.** One week after drop, pack sales close. All remaining unsold supply burns publicly (if economy spec authorises; if not, it retires to Council vault with publicly-visible mint count). Secondary opens or continues. The Set is now *out of print* — the SET-ROADMAP's Set-level staggered cadence (§5) means at any given time, several Sets are in-print and several are collector-complete. This creates the *retrograde hunt*: a new user in M14 can still chase Series 1 Sets on secondary, which is the gambler-collector's favorite game (A4 §5 archetype 7).

The monthly loop is the *event rhythm*; the daily and weekly loops make the event feel *earned*.

## 4. Seasonal Loop — Series-Long Arc, Where the Iceberg Surfaces

Each Series runs ~12 months (SET-ROADMAP §1). A Series is not a season of a TV show — it is a single set of openings cut into already-existing glass (Cosmology §1). The seasonal loop is how we make that timeframe *feel* like one story.

**The Series thesis drop (Month 1 of each Series).** At the opening of each Series, the Council publishes a thesis statement — not the arc, but its *one-line mood*. Series 1: *"The glass catches light."* Series 2: *"Other rooms in the same house."* Series 3: *"What holds the glass together"* (SET-ROADMAP §1). This one sentence is all the roadmap the audience gets. Everything else is felt through drops. Archetype 6 (Multiverse Lorehead, A4 §5) lives on this — they build their own Series-map in Notion, we never publish ours.

**The quarterly arc beats.** Per SET-ROADMAP §5, the drop calendar paces every quarter around a narrative high point.
- *Q1 of each Series*: Universe openings. Series 1 Q1 Surface-reveals BS, EK, GH; Series 2 Q1 opens shell expansion with CYBER and MODERN; Series 3 Q1 begins recognition arcs. This is the "Pane of Argument gains its first figure" quarter.
- *Q2*: Deepening. Iconic Sets land — Reichenbach in Series 1 M10, the Lampblacker ships in Series 2 M20, the Watson Variations in Series 3 M29.
- *Q3*: Tethering. The cross-Universe Echo elements that were seeded in Q1 become *visible* (Watson's limp echoing on Odysseus's gait, the Apple appearing on Olympus — Cosmology Iceberg audit §6).
- *Q4 (Series close)*: the Ultimate moment of the Series. Series 1 closes with a first tether audit and the first Echo-to-Surface promotion (the Lampblack residue becomes Lampblack *the named force*). Series 3 closes with the Council confirming exactly one of the Three Laws (Cosmology §1, Decision 8).

**Iceberg surfacing cadence.** The 1:2:4 ratio (Cosmology §4) is enforced *per Series* in the roadmap's §6 audit. In engagement terms: at the close of each Series, one Deep element from the *previous* Series gets promoted to Echo, and one Echo from the *previous* Series gets promoted to Surface. This is the promise the brand keeps: patience pays. A Deep reference planted in Series 1 M3 pays off in Series 2 M15; the collector who spotted it is named (by handle, with permission) in the Lampblack Tally that week.

**Seasonal commerce beats.** Two Apex-tier drops per Series, one mid-Series, one at Series close. One ONE-OFF auction per Universe per Series (5 per year, timed to cultural preloads: Dracula season for GH; Halloween for EK-6 Snow Black; Public Domain Day for BS-1). One Council-curated *Reading Room* physical/phygital event per Series — London for Series 1, Edinburgh or NYC for Series 2, tethered to an adapted cultural moment. A4 §3 Bored Ape Miami HQ lesson ported to our lane on a $200K budget.

**Year-round continuity for off-season archetypes.** The Sherlockian Completist (archetype 1) and the Multiverse Lorehead (6) need the seasonal loop more than the gambler. The Gambler-Collector (7) needs the monthly drop more than the seasonal. Both are served by the same calendar; the seasonal surfacing is what keeps the Completist engaged through a bad market quarter.

A Series ends not with a finale but with a *silence* — a two-week publication pause where only the Jonathan's Journal daily keeps running. Then the next Series's thesis drops, and the cycle reopens.

## 5. Hook Ladder — A User's First Month, Day by Day

A new user's first 30 days are the conversion ladder. Each step names a surface and a conversion ask. No step asks for money before Day 8.

**Day 0 — Arrival.** User hits the home page from a BookTok clip, a Dracula Daily link, or a Young Sherlock post. Surface: `/` (rebuilt per G1 Move 1, lore-first, one hero Moment, one Footnoter line). Ask: *read one quote*. Conversion: tap into `/lattice` (Worlds index). No signup. No pack.

**Day 1 — The first card encounter.** User taps a Universe tile from `/lattice` and lands on a Set teaser. Surface: `/card/[id]` in the promoted `/prototype/exemplars` design system (G1 Move 1). Ask: *read the iceberg lore-note*. Conversion: sees one Echo element highlighted; page offers "subscribe to Jonathan's Journal, free, daily." ~40% take it. This is our only Day-1 funnel target.

**Day 2 — The daily anchor begins.** User receives their first Journal entry by email/push. Surface: email / PWA. Ask: *tap "mark as read."* Conversion: becomes a streak-eligible user. Sample ticket counter shows 1/7.

**Day 3 — First free pack previewed.** Ticket counter hits 3/7; app shows a partial Sample pack card-back shape with four more positions to fill. Surface: Home / `/open`. Ask: *come back tomorrow*. No pack given yet. Behavioural debt accumulates — this is the Pokémon Pocket Pack Points lesson (A4 §3) recast as *earn the free thing* instead of *grind the paid thing*.

**Day 4–7 — The Footnoter discovery.** During this window, at least one Mirror Wednesday Topsy entry will land *or* a Lampblack Tally will drop (Sunday). The user sees the Footnoter voice for the first time. Surface: Jonathan's Journal / Lampblack Tally. Ask: *share one quote you liked*. Conversion: share gets an optional "get the card this quote is from" CTA (not pushed, not free — link to pack). ~15% convert from here.

**Day 7 — First free Sample pack opens.** Ticket counter hits 7/7. Surface: `/open`, full ritual animation (G1 §4 KEEP). Ask: *open the pack*. Conversion: user owns 1 Common card. They land on their first `/card/[id]`-in-exemplar-design for a card they *own*. First ownership moment.

**Day 8–14 — The first paid pack.** User has read the Journal for a week, owns one Common, and has seen approximately five Echo elements they don't fully understand. Surface: `/open` Standard pack tier ($9). Ask: *buy one Standard pack to deepen your set*. Conversion: copy is not "limited time" or "FOMO" — it is "you currently hold 1 of 12 Commons in this Set; this pack offers 3 more Moments." Target: 8–12% of Day-7 activated users convert to first $9 pack in this window. This is the A4 §1 "depth of spend $10 → $10K" ladder's first rung.

**Day 15–21 — Set-completion awakening.** User now owns 4–5 cards. The app surfaces their *Pokémon-badge-case view* of the Set (§6 retention driver) — which Moments they own, which they don't, without price chrome. Surface: `/collection` binder. Ask: *keep reading*. Conversion: ~30% of first-paid users buy a second pack in this window to fill a specific Moment gap. The Sherlockian Completist archetype activates here.

**Day 22–30 — First Curated pack ($49) or first parallel-hunt signal.** The first Rare Parallel (ARCANA, per GAME-ECONOMY §4) is likely sitting in the user's binder by now. The Lampblack Tally on day ~28 names the ARCANA parallel by visual signature; the user recognises one. Surface: `/collection` + Lampblack Tally. Ask: *consider a Curated pack, or join the Reading Pact for next week's drop*. Conversion: ~5–8% of activated users reach a $49 spend in the first 30 days; ~40% of activated users are still opening Journal entries daily at Day 30. Day-30 retention of Journal subscribers is the number we optimise against; 40% matches the Dracula Daily re-open rate at an order of magnitude smaller audience.

At Day 30, the user has been taught one loop per level of intensity: daily (Journal), weekly (Tally), monthly (first paid pack). If the first monthly drop of their account lifetime falls in their second month, the drop feels *anticipated*, not *assaulted*.

## 6. Retention Drivers — Beyond Loops

Loops pull users back; drivers make them *stay*. Six mechanics, each resolves a specific G1 or A4 finding.

**Driver 1 — Showcase / Identity.** Each user gets a `/showcase` page (G1 §3.7 "Lore Identity as first-class primitive" — A4 §6 threat-5 counter to Moca). Their Showcase is a 4–12 card arrangement with a room-aesthetic they choose (gaslight study, olive grove, looking-glass hallway, Baker Street pavement). The arrangement is public by URL and OpenGraph-card-ready. Social-shareability is not a feature; it is the default. The Sunday Showcase spotlight (§2 Beat D) reinforces this weekly. Archetype 5 (the Taste-Collector) lives here.

**Driver 2 — Set-completion badge case.** Per GAME-ECONOMY §9, every Set has a 20-Moment badge grid; missing Moments are silhouettes with one Echo element visible (*"a figure facing away at the edge of the frame"*), not empty slots. Completing a Set grants a 1/1 Footnoter-written commemorative lore-note, minted only for the completer. No fungible reward. The badge is the reward. Pokémon's Trainer Badge metaphor, ported to literary canon. Archetypes 1 (Sherlockian Completist) and 8 (Canon-Gamer) live here.

**Driver 3 — Parallel-hunt status.** Per GAME-ECONOMY §4, a collector hunting ARCANA across all Series-1 Sets earns a visible *"ARCANA Pursuer"* status at 25% coverage, *"ARCANA Chronicler"* at 50%, *"ARCANA Master"* at 100% with a bespoke ONE-OFF drop right. The status is a single line under the user's Showcase name; no XP bar, no percentage progress chrome (A4 §5 archetype 7 and 8; G1 voice-gap remediation). Gambler-collector retention.

**Driver 4 — Canon Theory Wall (community).** A moderated in-app surface where users post Lampblack-residue theories. Council "pins" one per week in the Lampblack Tally; pinned theorists get a small commemorative card (5-mint total per pin). This is the Dracula Daily Tumblr layer (A4 §2) made first-party. Archetypes 3 and 6 live here.

**Driver 5 — Reading Room (phygital anchor).** One Council-curated physical reading room per Series — London for Series 1 (Baker Street), Edinburgh for Series 2 (Gothic Horror / Robert Louis Stevenson), Athens or the British Museum for Series 3 (Greek Myth). Drop holders get in; non-holders can visit for ticketed public hours. The A4 §3 Bored Ape Miami HQ lesson: physical permanence kills the jpeg objection. $200K envelope for Series 1. Archetypes 1, 4, 5, 7.

**Driver 6 — Re-emission subscriptions beyond Dracula.** Once *Jonathan's Journal* completes Nov 7, 2026, the next re-emission rotates in (*Watson's Casebook*, then *The Persephone Almanac*, then *The Wolf's Year* — a year-long Grimm serial). Users can subscribe to individual re-emissions; superfans subscribe to all. This is the Dracula Daily playbook run in perpetuity (A4 §7 opp-1, opp-2, opp-3). Retention of the Dracula-Daily diaspora past their novel's close. Archetype 3 primary; archetype 2 (Pratchett-Discoverer) activates during Topsy re-emissions.

**Driver 7 — The Trust Contract, surfaced.** Per Cosmology §4, every Pane sold stays canon to its own Pane forever. Users should *see* this in product — every card page carries a one-line Trust Contract line at the bottom: *"This Pane is canon. The Lattice may shift; this card does not."* Per A4 §4 lesson 1, "lore ≠ roadmap." The counter to post-FTX and post-Bored-Ape gambler skepticism is a *visible* non-retcon commitment. Retention across all archetypes.

Drivers 1–3 are individual; 4–5 are social; 6–7 are structural. Together they answer archetype needs 1 through 8 directly.

## 7. Topsy Interlude — How 20% Satire Manifests in the Loops

Topsy is doctrinally 20% of Sets (TOPSY §"How Much, and Where"). In engagement terms, that is roughly one Topsy flavour beat every four to five weekly arcs — it cannot feel like a label, it has to feel like a seasonal *weather pattern*.

**Topsy in the daily loop.** On Mirror Wednesdays (§2 Beat B), the Jonathan's Journal slot hands off to *The Footnoter's Dispatch*. The entry is self-contained (never a continuing arc, per Footnoter's never-first-person rule) — a single 150-word Footnoter bulletin on an adjacent absurdity. Example for a May 2026 Mirror Wednesday (Dracula season): *"The Count had, by modern accounting, been in breach of quite a number of licensing agreements for several centuries; the paperwork had not caught up because the paperwork had not, strictly speaking, learned to travel by night."* The accompanying *Today on the Lattice* card rotates to a MIRROR or DREAM shell pulled from the catalog. Users who subscribe to Jonathan's Journal get Mirror Wednesday automatically; they can opt out. Conversion (H): 75%+ stay opted in, because the delight of a Pratchett-register interruption inside a Stoker run is exactly the "fell into a different world for one card" experience Topsy Decision 20 promised.

**Topsy in the weekly loop.** Every fourth Lampblack Tally becomes a *Topsy Tally* — same three sections, Footnoter-voiced throughout, with the week's Echo element being a Topsy inversion ("readers have noticed that the wolf from EK-3 appears, briefly, as a character reference in a Mycroft dispatch"). Archetype 2 (Pratchett-Discoverer) converts here.

**Topsy in the monthly drop.** Roughly 2–3 drops per year are Topsy Sets. Their staging (§3) differs: the Day –3 tease is always an absurdity-forward image (a bureaucratic-form compositional Lampblack pull); the Day –1 cold-open is narrated by the Footnoter, not the in-world serious narrator; the Day 0 live stream has the Footnoter as heard-not-seen MC (Decision 17: never a face). The drop remains in MIRROR or DREAM shells only (Topsy Decisions 16, 18). Topsy drops are timed to cultural tension moments — an EK-6 *Snow Black* Topsy drop lands Halloween week 2026 against the Del Toro *Frankenstein* / Gyllenhaal *The Bride!* cultural peak (A4 §7 opp-4), where the *serious* Gothic Horror registers are at maximum and a sincere-joke counter-drop reads as relief.

**Topsy in the seasonal loop.** One Topsy Set per Universe per Series (≈5 per year, matching TOPSY §"How Much, and Where" ratio). The Series thesis statement (§4) does not name Topsy; it is felt as a register-shift during the Series, not telegraphed. Archetype 2's activation is entirely dependent on this register's *unexpectedness*.

**The Footnoter's never-face rule, operationally.** Across all surfaces — daily, weekly, monthly, seasonal — the Footnoter is voice only. Their name may appear in attribution, never in an image. The Council enforces this; any art brief that depicts the Footnoter fails Council review.

Topsy is our competitive moat against MTG's bible (Cosmology §1; TOPSY §"The Pitch"). In the loops, it is our proof of *register discipline* — the one voice that shows up 20% of the time and is still recognisably *of the Lattice*.
