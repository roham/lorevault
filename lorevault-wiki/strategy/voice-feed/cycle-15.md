## Card: bs1-r05 "The Moriarty Letter" (Rare / PRIME / REASON)

**Art:** Holmes standing at the desk, holding a single letter in both hands with deliberate lightness, cold midday light.
**Tier word limit:** ≤20 words

### Candidate 1 — Mode-1 (Watson attributed, A1 Deduction)
> "He read it twice. The second reading was slower, which meant the first had been correct." — Watson, casebook

Word count: 16. Two-clause deduction about a deduction: Watson reads Holmes reading. The logic is: slower second reading = the first reading was the one he hoped he'd misread. This is Watson's finest register — observation of a man who cannot lie to himself.

### Candidate 2 — Mode-2 (unattributed, A1 variant)
> "The letter was precise in its hand and imprecise in its intent. This, Holmes said, was the threat."

Word count: 18. Two-clause, with Holmes quoted on the second. Pane-identifiable as Baker Street (deduction language, Holmesian phrasing). PASS.

### Candidate 3 — Mode-3 (Post Office document artifact)
> "Delivered to 221B Baker Street, W. Addressee declined to return it. — Post Office record, December 1891"

Word count: 12 + attribution. A6 Logbook. The "declined to return it" carries the threat: Holmes kept the letter deliberately, against protocol. PASS.

### Mosaic Scorer Proxy
- C1: Baker Street ✓ (Watson attribution, deduction-about-deduction cadence) — **PASS**
- C2: Baker Street ✓ (Holmes quoted, deduction language) — **PASS**
- C3: Baker Street ✓ (221B address, period Post Office register, December 1891) — **PASS**

All three pass. 

**Selected:** Candidate 1 — most psychologically precise. The "second reading was slower" observation earns the deduction it reaches.

### Lore-Note (Echo-only, 1–2 sentences)
The letter was never described in Watson's published casebook. A fragment matching its handwriting appears, attributed to "a correspondent who did not survive," in Watson's unsorted papers.

## Card: bs1-l02 "The Seven-Per-Cent" (Legendary / PRIME / SHADOW)

**Art:** Holmes seated, Morocco case open at armrest with hypodermic needle, looking directly at the viewer.
**Tier word limit:** ≤30 words, attribution mandatory

### Candidate 1 — Mode-1 (Watson attributed, A11 Anonymous Witness variant / three sentences)
> "The case was open when I came in. I pretended not to see it. He noticed that I pretended, and was grateful." — Watson, casebook, suppressed

Word count: 24 + attribution. Three sentences. Sentence 1: observation. Sentence 2: Watson's active choice. Sentence 3: Holmes's perception of the choice + its emotional consequence (gratitude = he did not want to have to explain). Each sentence earns the next.

### Candidate 2 — Mode-2 (unattributed narrator)
> "A man who knows everything accumulates the particular boredom of knowing. The needle was not boredom. It was what boredom felt like from the inside."

Word count: 28. Three sentences, philosophical register. Pane-identifiable: BORDERLINE (could read as Greek Myth, as an oracle on Cassandra's knowledge). FAIL without art.

### Candidate 3 — Mode-3 (Watson's 221B inventory, A6 Logbook)
> "Morocco case, contents: one syringe; one solution at seven per cent; one argument Watson had already lost. — Watson, inventory of 221B, 1888"

Word count: 17 + attribution. Logbook format. The third item ("one argument Watson had already lost") breaks the inventory cadence and reveals Watson as the subject of his own accounting. PASS.

### Mosaic Scorer Proxy
- C1: Baker Street ✓ (Watson attribution, "suppressed" provenance, mutual-pretense register) — **PASS**
- C2: Pane-ambiguous (philosophical register without markers) — **FAIL**
- C3: Baker Street ✓ (Morocco case + seven per cent + Watson self-attribution in third person) — **PASS**

**Selected:** Candidate 1 — the three-sentence structure earns each word; the "gratitude" in the final clause is the third dimension the Legendary tier requires.

### Lore-Note (Echo-only, 1–2 sentences)
The Morocco case appears in Watson's private inventory of 221B without a quantity listed. The inventory was written in 1888 and was not revised.
