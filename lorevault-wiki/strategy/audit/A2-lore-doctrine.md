## 1. Load-Bearing Concepts — what the rebirth would fail without

The corpus rests on roughly a dozen concepts that every downstream surface (art, voice, set design, drop cadence, terms-of-service) depends on. Remove any one and the stack collapses.

**The Lattice and the Lampblack** (`MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §1). The meta-frame compressed to exactly two proper nouns. The Lattice is the geometry of Panes; Lampblack is the only diegetic physics permitting cross-Pane recognition. Per Decision 1, this is the single cause and no second physics is ever invented. Without this compression the rebirth has no handle — every other artifact in the corpus opens with a reference to it.

**The Three Laws of the Lattice** (`MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §1, Laws One-Three). Spine, Lampblack, No-Merge. These are the meta-rules that foreclose the `Once Upon a Time` bloat failure (R3 §1) and the Crisis-on-Infinite-Earths reboot failure (R1 §2). The corpus is explicitly structured around Laws that are felt, never stated (Decision 8). Without the Laws, Spine adherence is a stylistic preference instead of a binding constraint.

**The Spine** (`MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §1, Law One; `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §3 layer 4; Decision 2). Every figure's non-negotiable canonical-event list. Sherlock: *deduces, falls, returns, refuses domesticity*. Alice: *crosses a threshold, distrusts size, accepts nonsense as law, walks back*. V2 explicitly flagged Decision 2 as "the operationally cleanest move in the whole document" (`REVIEW-quality.md` §1). Without the Spine there is no way to say Sherlock-CYBER and Sherlock-PRIME are the same figure rather than a property and its skin.

**The 4-layer Lampblack recognition stack** (`MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §3). SILHOUETTE, PROPS, GESTURE, SPINE. Conjunctive, not disjunctive — drop any layer and the card fails Council review. This was V2's "prop-is-not-enough" rewrite and is the first of V2's five rewrites to actually land in doctrine (`REVIEW-quality.md` §1 weak-point 1 + the Canon Council rejection examples in `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §3a). Without it, an octopus with a blue ribbon is Alice.

**The Five Universes as cosmological roles** (`MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §2; `economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` §2). Baker Street = Pane of Argument, Enchanted Kingdom = Inheritance, Wonderland = Categorical Failure, Gothic Horror = Transformation, Greek Myth = Fate. V2 called this "genuinely Pratchett-shaped thinking" (`REVIEW-quality.md` §1 strong-point 3) because the Universes *operate on* each other rather than being five flavors of the same juice. Without the role-framing, the five Universes are aesthetic buckets and cross-Universe tethers have nothing to bind.

**The 8-Shell taxonomy (locked)** (`MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §2; Decision 4). PRIME, CYBER, MODERN, AETHER, HOLLOW, MIRROR, DREAM, SAINT. Locked for Series 1-6; expansion requires unanimous Canon Council approval. This is the operating-constraint matrix for every art brief, plus the 20×8 matrix in `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §2 that tells Set leads which shells are Strong / Okay / No per character. Without the lock, shell proliferation produces the Marvel Phase 4 bloat R6 §3 catalogued.

**Shells × Parallels as orthogonal axes** (Decision 3; `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §1). Shell = *who the figure is right now*; Parallel = *how the painting reads*. Every card carries both. Collapse this distinction and both collapse — art treatment and narrative chassis fuse into an incoherent hybrid.

**The Elemental third axis** (`MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §5). Six classes (REASON, WONDER, SHADOW, RAPTURE, FATE, WILD) mapped to figures rather than shells or universes. Ships Day 1, locked through Series 6. Without it, the cosmology has no temperamental/values register; MTG-grade mechanical depth is unreachable.

**The five Parallels as a cross-cutting collector hunt** (`economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` §4). ARCANA, AETHER, WITNESS, NEON, ONE-OFF. Tighter mint counts than base; only on Rare+. The core innovation vs Top Shot is that Parallels are distinct art variants hunted across every Set. Without Parallels, the 1,200-piece Series 1 catalog is 400 base pieces and a much weaker depth-of-spend ladder.

**The Iceberg Doctrine 1:2:4 ratio** (`MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §4; Decision 7). Surface : Echo : Deep for every quarter. The rate-limiter that prevents Lattice-soup. Without it, shell-expansion in Series 2 collapses into press-release-grade Pottermore expansion (R6 §5).

**The Canon Council with override authority over the founder** (Decision 10 + Decision 14). A 3-person body empowered from drop one. Gatekeeps Spines, the 1:2:4 ratio, and the 8-shell taxonomy. V2 called Decision 9 (no Pane is decanonized) "the single strongest collector-protection move in the deliverable" (`REVIEW-quality.md` §1 strong-point 2) — and Decision 10 is what operationally enforces it. Without the Council, every other constraint is founder-vetoable and therefore non-binding.

**The five universe voice registers** (`FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.md` §1; Decision 6). Watson-chronicling / bone-rhyme / Liddell-logic / operatic-exhaustion / bronze-tongue. The voice-lead's Mosaic Test (blind universe recognition ≥18 of 20 cards) is how a collector can identify the Pane from prose alone. The voice is *how the Pane recognizes itself across shells* (`FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.md` §2). Without the registers, the shell-expansion of Series 2 fractures into five unrelated franchises wearing the same names.

**The Topsy Register** (`TOPSY-REGISTER.md` passim; Decisions 16-20). 20% of Sets ship in the Pratchett-grade satirical mode. MIRROR/DREAM-only. Voiced by The Footnoter, never depicted. This is described as "LoreVault's competitive moat against MTG" (`TOPSY-REGISTER.md` The Pitch) because MTG's bible disallows Pratchett mode. Without Topsy the corpus has banned its own most distinctive voice possibility in pursuit of MTG-grade flavor — V2 explicitly called this out (`REVIEW-quality.md` §6 distinctness verdict).

## 2. Contradictions and Gaps Between Docs

**Voice count: 5 vs 6.** `FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.md` §1 names five voices — one per Universe. `TOPSY-REGISTER.md` Topsy Voice Rules states Topsy "extends FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE §1 with a sixth voice." The flavor doctrine was not amended; the two docs now disagree on whether there are five or six registers. V2 also recommended a sixth carve-out (a Wonderland mode-2 philosophical register — `REVIEW-quality.md` Rewrite 5) which is a *different* sixth voice than the Footnoter. The corpus now implies three candidate sixth voices: Footnoter (Topsy), Wonderland-philosophical (V2), and neither (FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE unchanged). This must be reconciled — the candidates are not the same register and cannot collapse into one.

**Decision numbering: gap at 16.** `MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §6 is titled "The Decisions That Bind" and says "the following thirteen numbered decisions" — but then numbers 1 through 15 (so there are actually 15, not 13 — the prose is stale). `TOPSY-REGISTER.md` adds Decisions 16-20. `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §3a/§3b references Decision 15 correctly but `REVIEW-quality.md` Rewrite 4 proposes its own "Decision 16" for quarterly iceberg audit at card-level granularity. That Rewrite-4 Decision-16 and the Topsy Decision-16 now conflict numerically. The decision registry is not authoritative — there is no single file that lists all binding decisions in order.

**"The Lampblack thickens" usage frequency.** `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §3b and §4 say the phrase is the audience-facing flavor-text reference for shell-contact, used in compositions where the multi-layer alignment is visually present; it is "not a generic Easter-egg label." `EXEMPLAR-CARDS.md` uses the phrase twice in five cards (Card 4 GH-5 Legendary and Card 5 GM-10 Ultimate). V2 warned it is "on track to become a tic — by Series 1 close, if it appears on more than 3 cards, it crosses from leitmotif to brand-tag" and recommended capping at one per Series (`REVIEW-quality.md` §3 voice-tic check; §6 tic check). Doctrine is silent on the cap; the shells spec is permissive; V2 wants a hard cap. No resolution shipped.

**Set BS-2 tier pull vs roadmap.** `economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` §5 gives Baker Street's Set-by-Set table as BS-1 221B / BS-2 The Cases / BS-3 Moriarty / BS-4 Reichenbach. `SET-ROADMAP.md` §2.1 has BS-1 221B / BS-2 The Hounds of Baskerville / BS-3 The Napoleon of Crime / BS-4 Reichenbach. The economy spec's BS-2 ("The Cases") has silently been replaced with BS-2 ("Hounds of Baskerville"). Minor but the two docs now disagree on one-fifth of Baker Street's Series-1 arc — and the economy spec's "mid-canon iconic cases" arc has no home in the current roadmap.

**The sixth parallel, deferred.** `economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` §8 notes `dream-psychedelic` "could be absorbed into ARCANA. Or promoted to a 6th parallel later." No later doc names a sixth parallel. Either commit to five (update the economy prose) or carve the sixth (and specify mint counts at Rare/Legendary/Ultimate). Currently a dangling possibility.

**Series 1 scope — 5 Universes × 4 Sets vs staggered launch.** `economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` §5 says each Universe gets 4 Sets in Series 1, dropped monthly. `MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §4 launch policy says Surface-reveal only Baker Street and Enchanted Kingdom in Q1 (M1-M4), then Greek Myth and Gothic Horror in Q2. `SET-ROADMAP.md` §1 and §5 have all five Universes with cards shipping from M2-M3. The economy spec's "dropped monthly in staggered cadence so at any time some sets are in-print and some are collectible-complete" is not operationalized anywhere — when does each set go out of print?

**Open Questions 1-5 in the economy spec are unanswered.** `economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` §11 enumerates five CEO questions (Series 1 scope, mint-count economics, Parallels at Common tier, ONE-OFF distribution, Series 2 new-Universe-or-deepen). None are answered anywhere in the later docs. These need decisions before art catalog commissioning.

## 3. Underdeveloped Areas

**The Lampblacker as original anchor** (Decision 11; `SET-ROADMAP.md` GH-7 / GH-10; `REVIEW-quality.md` §2 GH-7 stress test). The figure is named, scheduled (S2.M20), assigned five outbound tethers, and committed to as Gothic Horror's Penny-Dreadful-style anchor — but has no published Spine. V2 flagged this directly: "the figure is unspecified... a figure built to do narrative work without a defined desire is a function, not a character" (`REVIEW-quality.md` §2 GH-7 Ultimate Moment landing). V2's Rewrite 3 offers a draft Spine ("bears witness, leaves residue, is recognized but not named, refuses to be the figure others ask them to be") but this is a *proposal* by the reviewer, not doctrine. Until the Council publishes a Spine paragraph internally, GH-7 is a doctrinal promise without craft underneath.

**The Footnoter.** `TOPSY-REGISTER.md` names the Footnoter as Topsy's canonical narrator, gives three sample quotes, and binds Decision 17 (never depicted, never given a face). But the Footnoter has no Spine either, no stated relationship to the Lampblacker, and no clear rule for whether Footnoter appears on flavor attributions across Topsy Sets or only some of them. Is the Footnoter a *person*-shaped entity like Granny Weatherwax's narrator-voice, or a diffuse authorial conceit? The Pratchett analog would resolve this — Pratchett's own narrator has persistence. LoreVault's does not yet.

**The Vertigo Moment / Dream Hand-Off / Saint's Glimpse mechanics** (`MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §4). Three sanctioned shell-crossing event types, each Ultimate- or Legendary+-tier only. `EXEMPLAR-CARDS.md` Card 5 demonstrates the Saint's Glimpse mechanic (Persephone-SAINT with PRIME and HOLLOW shell-silhouettes flanking the halo as iconographic attributes). Vertigo Moment and Dream Hand-Off are *described* but never exemplified. Vertigo in particular — "the figure perceives themselves in another shell as a flicker, doubled silhouette, mismatched reflection" — needs at least one worked card spec or it is art-direction that will be invented on the fly by the first Ultimate commission that tries to use it.

**The Three Laws of the Lattice, text-of.** The Laws are stated in `MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §1 as internal doctrine but Decision 8 says fans will not see them stated until Year 3 at earliest, one per year, never all three. The internal statements ("The Spine", "The Lampblack", "Panes Touch, They Do Not Merge") are the names; the operational Law *text* is given in the surrounding paragraphs. There is no one-sentence canonical form of each Law that the Council could release if the community converges on one. If and when the Council confirms a Law, what exactly do they publish? This needs drafting internally even if never published.

**Mode-3 in-world artifact saturation** (`FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.md` §1 mode-3 rules; V2 `REVIEW-quality.md` §6 recommendation). V2 identified mode-3 artifacts (case files, newspaper cuttings, Hesiod-fragments, ship's logs) as LoreVault's single most distinct voice register vs MTG — "the register Magic cannot match because Magic's worlds are not citing real documents" — and recommended raising saturation to ~40% across the catalog. Current doctrine has per-Universe mode-3 targets (Greek Myth ~50%, Gothic Horror ~35%, Baker Street ~25%, Enchanted Kingdom ~50%, Wonderland ~15%). The 25-sample table in `FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.md` §6 has only ~30% mode-3. V2's recommendation is not yet reflected in the per-Universe targets or in the sample.

**Watson's interpretive lens** (`REVIEW-quality.md` §6 Watson voice). The voice rules ("a watch is gold-cased, not gleaming") cannot manufacture the interpretive thesis R3 §2 demands. V2 recommended committing Watson to "the doctor who treats Holmes as a patient" — the single Watson lens every Set runs against. Not yet in doctrine. Without it, Watson will drift into ACD pastiche over 12 Sets.

**The Borrowed Pane.** Named in cosmology §4 (Months 15-24) and iceberg Series-2 Deep elements (`SET-ROADMAP.md` §6), referenced as "the Panes the Lattice has not yet returned to us" — but no spec exists. Is it one Pane, a cluster, a non-canon sandbox, a seasonal wildcard? The R6 §1 Universes-Beyond analogy gives shape but no specifics. A Series 2 gesture that doesn't ship a Set is fine; a Series 4 commitment that still has no spec is not.

**Pack-mechanics validation.** `economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` §6 specifies Pack tiers ($0 / $9 / $49 / $199 / $999), §10 specifies the 48-hour prototype test (Reichenbach with 20 moments). Neither has been run. The economy is designed but not validated against any real purchase behavior.

## 4. Obsolete Artifacts

**`taste/ART-DIRECTION-V3.md`** is the explicit call-out. The doc is dated 2026-04-24 like the others, but it predates the Lattice/Lampblack cosmology — it does not reference the Lattice, the Lampblack, the 8 shells, Parallels as distinct from modes, the Canon Council, the Spine system, or any Decision. Its `CHARACTERS` gain `iconography` + `narrativeBeat` + `compositionHint` schema was useful pre-cosmology scaffolding but has been superseded by the Shell × Parallel × Elemental metadata triple (`MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` Appendix) and the per-character 4-layer Lampblack stacks (`MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §3). The `PROMPT_VERSION = 3` template in §"What Changes in Code" is also superseded by the v6+ structure referenced in `EXEMPLAR-CARDS.md` art prompts. The Canon-Specific Visual DNA section (Paget / Tenniel / Nielsen / Coppola / Waterhouse) contains reference material that is still useful — it should be lifted into a living `taste/` successor and this file marked obsolete, not simply deleted.

**The v6 MODES mapping as standalone design** (`economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` §8). The section itself is not obsolete — it explicitly reconciles v6 MODES to the Parallel system — but the underlying `scripts/seed-moodboard.mjs` v6 MODE semantics (painterly-epic / ornate-ritual / mythic-cosmic / witness-photoreal / modern-reimagined / dream-psychedelic as *modes*) are superseded by Parallels. The §8 reconciliation IS the obsoleting note; any remaining code that treats MODES as authoritative art-direction rather than as legacy seed-naming should be updated.

**The V1 economy's "Fandom tier" comment** (`economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` §3). "Drop 'Fandom' — we're not gated by a players' association, we own the canon" is correctly obsolete (we've dropped to 4 tiers) but the comment reads as pre-cosmology framing. This is minor prose-cleanup, not a doctrinal problem.

**V2's named "one-rewrite-done" artifact.** V2 (`REVIEW-quality.md`) was written before the 4-layer Lampblack stack landed in `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §3. Its §1 weak-point 1 ("Lampblack is named but never demonstrated *doing* anything in-frame") has been *partially* addressed by §3a Canon Council Rejection Examples (which demonstrate Lampblack's rigor) but V2's ask was narrower: show Lampblack as a force acting on a figure against the figure's will. The 4-layer stack landed the recognition-rigor half; the active-force half did not.

No other doctrine file is obsolete. The cosmology doctrine, shell spec, set roadmap, flavor doctrine, exemplar cards, Topsy register, economy spec, and V2 review are all current as of 2026-04-24.

## 5. Decisions Waiting to Be Made

Downstream stages (art commissioning, voice-lead hiring, launch calendar, ToS drafting) need these resolved before execution:

1. **Sixth voice register reconciliation.** Is the sixth voice (a) the Footnoter only, (b) a Wonderland-philosophical carve-out, (c) both (making seven total), or (d) neither (FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE stays at five and Topsy is not a voice but a *register-modulation* within MIRROR/DREAM)? Requires Council vote.
2. **"The Lampblack thickens" cap.** Per-Series cap, per-Set cap, or per-Ultimate-only? V2 recommends one per Series, reserved for Ultimates/Legendaries. Bind or reject.
3. **Decision-registry authority.** Decisions 1-15 (cosmology) + 16-20 (Topsy) + V2's proposed 16 (card-level iceberg audit) have collided numerically. A single canonical decision registry needs to be established and the numbering rationalized.
4. **BS-2 tier consolidation.** Does Baker Street Series 1 ship `The Cases` (economy spec) or `Hounds of Baskerville` (roadmap) as BS-2? Roadmap is newer and more likely authoritative; economy spec should be updated.
5. **Sixth Parallel.** Promote `dream-psychedelic` to a 6th Parallel with its own mint ladder, or absorb into ARCANA permanently?
6. **Economy spec Open Questions 1-5** (`economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` §11). Series 1 scope (1 Set × 5 Universes vs 2 Sets × 2 Universes), mint-count calibration, Parallels at Common tier, ONE-OFF distribution mechanism, Series 2 new-Universes-vs-deepen. All five are CEO-waits.
7. **Lampblacker Spine.** Publish internal Spine paragraph before GH-7 art commissioning. V2 Rewrite 3 offers a draft; Council must ratify or replace.
8. **Watson interpretive lens.** One-sentence commitment to prevent ACD pastiche drift. V2 proposes "doctor who treats Holmes as a patient." Ratify or replace.
9. **Borrowed Pane spec.** One or many? Named figures now, or stay fully gestured through Series 3?
10. **Mode-3 saturation.** Accept V2's ~40% across-catalog recommendation or keep the per-Universe targets?
11. **Staggered-cadence print lifecycles.** When does each Set actually go out of print? The economy spec asserts staggered cadence; roadmap does not operationalize it.
12. **ToS language for Decision 9** (no Pane decanonized). Doctrine commits to it; legal language does not yet exist.
13. **Which of the Three Laws does the community get first** (Decision 8 — Year 3)? The doctrine defers this until the community has converged but Council needs to be ready to confirm whichever wins. Draft text for all three Laws needs to exist internally.
14. **Canon Council membership.** Decision 10 commits to a 3-person body with override authority from drop one. Who? The doctrine is silent on the identification mechanism.
15. **HOLLOW Topsy experiment** (`TOPSY-REGISTER.md` Shell binding). "Reserved for one experimental set and then evaluated" — which set, evaluated against what bar?

## 6. Doctrine Self-Test — V2's Five Rewrites, Applied Status

V2's STRENGTHEN-AND-SHIP verdict (`REVIEW-quality.md` Verdict + The 5 Highest-Impact Rewrites) identified five specific rewrites that close most of the gap between current state and "Pratchett-caliber, MTG-grade" bar. The task description notes one is done (the 4-layer Lampblack stack); this audit confirms that count and identifies the four remaining.

**Rewrite 1 — Cosmology §1, Lampblack as active force. NOT APPLIED.** V2 proposed adding a paragraph showing Lampblack acting on a figure ("A Sherlock-PRIME, in 1891, dreams of a visor where his cap should be..."). Current `MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §1 still describes Lampblack as residue/accumulation — noun-passive. The 4-layer stack (Rewrite 1's partial sibling) addresses rigor-of-recognition but not force-of-action. Outstanding.

**Rewrite 2 — FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE §6 Row 1 (and opening-sample principle). NOT APPLIED.** V2 proposed replacing the current Row 1 ("The watch was wound for an owner with a longer right arm...") with "The watch was gold-cased and wound clockwise. The body in the river had been left-handed since Afghanistan." `FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.md` §6 Row 1 is unchanged as of 2026-04-24. V2 flagged this as pedagogically high-leverage — the first sample anyone reads. Outstanding.

**Rewrite 3 — Lampblacker Spine paragraph. NOT APPLIED.** V2 proposed the Council publish an internal Spine paragraph for the Lampblacker before any Set commissioning. `SET-ROADMAP.md` GH-7 row is unchanged; no Spine has landed in `MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` or `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md`. Outstanding. This is the highest-risk outstanding rewrite because GH-7 Council-voicing is load-bearing for Series 2.

**Rewrite 4 — Iceberg quarterly audit at card-level granularity. NOT APPLIED.** V2 proposed making the Council's quarterly audit enforce named Echo/Deep elements at card-level granularity (min 12 Echo + 24 Deep per Series with screenshot proof). `SET-ROADMAP.md` §6 Iceberg Compliance Audit names 3 Echo + 6 Deep per Series — below V2's ask and V2 itself flagged as 7.5% specificity against claimed unit count. Outstanding.

**Rewrite 5 — Sixth register carve-out for Wonderland philosophical-wit. NOT APPLIED (and now complicated).** V2 proposed adding a §1.6 to FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE for unattributed Wonderland-philosophical (Pratchett-grade serious-funny) as A9 Wisecrack archetype, one per Set, Wonderland-only. `TOPSY-REGISTER.md` ships a *different* sixth voice (the Footnoter, MIRROR/DREAM-only, Pratchett-borrowed-truth mode) — not the Wonderland-philosophical register V2 named. The V2 rewrite is outstanding AND has been structurally pre-empted by Topsy without replacement. This one needs a Council decision per §5 item 1 above before it can be "applied" cleanly.

**Rewrite already applied — 4-layer Lampblack stack.** `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §3 + §3a (Canon Council Rejection Examples: Octopus-Alice, Bodyless-AI-Sherlock, Apple-only Snow White, Hattered-Watson, Casual-Forward-Stance Holmes) + §3b ("Lampblack thickens" phrasing rule restated for multi-layer alignment) lands V2's weak-point-1 rigor ask. This is the one rewrite fully closed.

**Count: 1 applied, 4 outstanding.** The outstanding rewrites are Rewrite 1 (Lampblack as force), Rewrite 2 (flavor sample #1 replacement), Rewrite 3 (Lampblacker Spine publication), and Rewrite 4 (card-level iceberg audit). Rewrite 5 (Wonderland philosophical-sixth-voice) is outstanding but now entangled with the Topsy sixth-voice collision and cannot be cleanly applied until §5 item 1 is decided.

## Closing Note — Overall Posture

The corpus is **structurally complete and internally consistent at the 85-90% level.** The three highest-leverage fixes for the rebirth are: (a) rationalize the decision registry and sixth-voice question so downstream work has one canonical list to obey, (b) publish the Lampblacker Spine internally so Series 2 has a figure under its operating commitment, and (c) apply the four outstanding V2 rewrites before the first Series 1 Set is commissioned rather than after. The doctrine has earned the right to commission art against; it has not yet earned the right to commission *eighty Sets* worth of art against, because four known craft gaps would propagate at scale if left unclosed. The Council and the founder's restraint (Decision 14) are the correct safeguards; they are load-bearing precisely because the outstanding rewrites are not yet bound.
