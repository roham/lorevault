## 1. Buyer state of digital-collectibles, 2026 Q2

The market is bimodal. One pole — **Pokémon TCG Pocket** — is a generational hit: $35M in January 2026 (down from $37M December 2025), $43M October 2025 after the Mega Rising set, and $1.49B cumulative revenue with 100M+ downloads since October 2024 global launch ([Sensor Tower via Spyyd3r](https://x.com/Spyyd3r/status/2017985038785171530); [Sensor Tower news feed](https://app.sensortower.com/news-feed/pokemon-tcg-pocket-hits-245m-all-time-ios-revenue-less-than-a-month-after-launch/67315042ef93f311e1c28732)). The other pole — **tokenised Pokémon / sports card trading on Courtyard** — posted **$7.82M** in the week ending April 20, 2026 and **$8.6M / 101,767 transactions / 15,744 buyers** the week before, leading all NFT collections globally and outpacing CryptoPunks by a full order of magnitude ([NFT Plazas, April 2026](https://nftplazas.com/courtyard-nft-platform-beats-cryptopunks-weekly-sales/); [Robotics & Automation News, Feb 2026](https://roboticsandautomationnews.com/2026/02/04/marketplaces-for-tokenized-pokemon-card-nfts/98607/)).

What's **lapsed**: the 2021-flavoured sports-video-moment model. **NFL ALL DAY** fumbled from a September 2022 peak of $18.4M to **$1.3M / -87% YoY** and, critically, failed to acquire net-new crypto-curious users — the buyer overlap with NBA Top Shot was nearly total ([Decrypt](https://decrypt.co/155347/nfl-all-day-has-fumbled-nft-sales-down-87-from-a-year-ago); [The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/228033/nfl-crypto-millions-of-dollars-dapper-labs-digital-collectibles)). **Disney Pinnacle** sits small but intact: ~480K pins sold, 67K traded, $400K+ trading volume and a $1,000 top individual sale as of mid-2025 reports ([DappRadar](https://dappradar.com/blog/disney-pinnacle-all-about-the-new-nft-platform-on-flow)). **Marvel Snap** — the Snap economy's closest analogue in the card-battler register — accumulated $102.9M in year two, down **40% YoY** from ~$173M year one ([Naavik](https://naavik.co/digest/is-marvel-snap-monetization-too-modest/); [MobileGamer.biz](https://mobilegamer.biz/marvel-snap-is-about-to-break-100m-in-earnings-for-developer-second-dinner/)). **Sorare** is a cautionary tale: 13% layoffs in 2024, infrastructure migration to Solana, and publicly reported difficulty raising new capital ([Data Insights Market](https://www.datainsightsmarket.com/news/article/sorares-financial-trouble-is-the-nft-fantasy-sports-giant-failing-49001); [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/sorare-moves-solana-upgrade-chain-185250610.html)).

**Gambler-collector money is moving in three directions:**
1. **Graded-physical-on-chain** (Courtyard's Pokémon + sports slabs) — authenticated scarcity with tangible exit.
2. **Gacha card packs** (Pokémon Pocket) — tuned pull drama, daily re-entry, no secondary-market friction.
3. **Magic: The Gathering's Universes Beyond** — $1.7B revenue for MTG in 2025 (+60% YoY, Final Fantasy became highest-selling set ever in a single day), making up 60%+ of Hasbro gaming revenue ([Wargamer](https://www.wargamer.com/magic-the-gathering/mtg-release-schedule); [AllKeyShop](https://www.allkeyshop.com/blog/mtg-historic-1-7-billion-revenue-hasbro-2025-news-d/); [GameSpot](https://www.gamespot.com/articles/magic-the-gathering-revenue-increased-by-an-astonishing-amount-last-year/1100-6538123/)).

The common thread: **authenticated scarcity + pull drama + IP gravity**. The gambler-collector did not leave — they left anything that was only one of the three.

## 2. Current taste signal in our lane — public-domain narrative + illustrated collectibles + multiverse

The cultural tailwind is real and datable.

- **Public-domain literary canon** is in open commercial bloom. Luc Besson's *Dracula*, Guillermo del Toro's *Frankenstein* (9 Oscar nominations; 85% Rotten Tomatoes on 374 reviews; streaming on Netflix), Maggie Gyllenhaal's *The Bride!*, and Guy Ritchie's eight-episode *Young Sherlock* on Prime Video (March 4, 2026 launch) are all live simultaneously ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frankenstein_(2025_film)); [Netflix Tudum](https://www.netflix.com/tudum/articles/frankenstein-guillermo-del-toro-release-date-cast-photos); [ScreenRant](https://screenrant.com/young-sherlock-season-1-prime-video-series-better/)).
- **Sherlock Holmes fandom** is the oldest modern fandom, runs on "meta" (in-world essay-level analysis), and hosts **110,000+ Sherlock tagged works on AO3** as of November 2025 ([Wikipedia — Sherlock Holmes fandom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_fandom)). Young Sherlock has refreshed the top of that funnel.
- **Dracula Daily** — the gold-standard exemplar — grew from 1,600 subscribers (2021) to 200,000 (May 2022) to **240,000+** (October 2023) off pure epistolary re-emission of an 1897 novel, with a Tumblr-driven memetic layer ([Wikipedia — Dracula Daily](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dracula_Daily); [WBUR Endless Thread](https://www.wbur.org/endlessthread/2023/11/03/introducing-icymi-how-a-substack-revived-the-dracula-fandom)). This is direct proof-of-concept for serialised public-domain narrative as audience-building primitive.
- **Pratchett-grade demand** is now surging: **Thief of Time graphic novel** ships April 2, 2026 from Puffin; **Monstrous Regiment** graphic novel arrives fall 2026; **The Wee Free Men** follows early 2027 (adapted by Rhianna Pratchett); *The Discworld Bestiary* released March 2026; BBC Studios has struck a fresh multi-novel Discworld TV deal with Motive Pictures and Endeavor Content ([ScreenRant](https://screenrant.com/new-discworld-adaptations-terry-pratchett-shared-universe/); [Den of Geek](https://www.denofgeek.com/tv/discworld-terry-pratchett-new-tv-series/)).
- **Romantasy / BookTok collector editions** — sprayed edges, foil stamps, illustrated endpapers, clothbound hardcovers — are now a default expectation of first printings. Hand-painted, fine-art-textured cover illustration work is the winning visual vocabulary ([Beautiful Books](https://beautifulbooks.info/special-editions/2026-01/); [TropeHunt](https://tropehunt.com/blog/most-gorgeous-romantasy-book-covers/)).
- **TikTok** has an active Peter Pan dark-retelling / "Peter Pan was the villain" micro-genre with creators like `@marymecham_author` and `@tskinley` surfacing multi-million-view hits ([TikTok Peter Pan retelling tag](https://www.tiktok.com/discover/peter-pan-retelling-with-romance)). Dark retellings of canon characters are load-bearing cultural currency right now.
- **Multiverse** as a narrative primitive is being normalised at theatrical scale: *Avengers: Doomsday* (December 18, 2026) + *Avengers: Secret Wars* (December 2027), both scripted by Loki showrunner Michael Waldron ([FandomWire](https://fandomwire.com/tom-hiddlestons-loki-is-bringing-the-x-men-and-fantastic-four-to-save-earth-from-doctor-doom-mcu-theory/); [ScreenRant](https://screenrant.com/avengers-doomsday-loki-thor-reunion-mcu-fix/)). Lattice/Lampblack lands into a culturally pre-taught frame.

**Tired**: generic Greek-myth retellings, "the villain's story" without a specific canonical anchor, Alice-in-Wonderland visuals as generic psychedelia, and the "public domain + sexy" reflex that skips lore depth.

## 4. What competitors are doing that's failing — DO NOT inherit

1. **Generic PFP drops with roadmap theatre.** [H] 96% of NFT collections are effectively dead; Bored Ape floor fell from ~$350K peak (2022) to ~$30K–$40K by April 2026 ([Analysis Group](https://www.analysisgroup.com/news-and-events/news/settlement-ends-bored-ape-yacht-club-nft-trademark-case-following-court-decision-supported-by-analysis-group-expert-testimony/); [Financial News](https://www.financial-news.co.uk/the-death-of-the-nft-inside-the-abandoned-digital-wallets-of-the-bored-ape-elite/); [BlockNuggets](https://blocknuggets.com/news/nft-decline-analysis-2026/)). **Lesson**: lore ≠ roadmap; ship canon, not Q3 deliverables.
2. **Acquiring only existing NFT wallets** (NFL ALL DAY problem). 100% overlap with NBA Top Shot buyers = zero TAM expansion ([The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/228033/nfl-crypto-millions-of-dollars-dapper-labs-digital-collectibles)). **Lesson**: LoreVault's onramp must be legible to a BookTok buyer who has never heard of Flow.
3. **Pure-RNG packs with no deterministic fallback** (Pokémon Pocket community revolt over siloed Pack Points between expansions; $300+ spenders reporting zero grails) ([GameRant](https://gamerant.com/pokemon-tcg-pocket-pack-point-exclusivity/)). **Lesson**: unified Topsy Points across all sets and all time.
4. **Over-generous monetisation without depth** (Marvel Snap's -40% YoY collapse; Second Dinner publicly said to need course-correction) ([Naavik](https://naavik.co/digest/is-marvel-snap-monetization-too-modest/)). **Lesson**: whales need legitimate spend ceilings; the ladder must go high, not just the floor stay low.
5. **Sports-moment video collectibles** as primary format ([Decrypt](https://decrypt.co/155347/nfl-all-day-has-fumbled-nft-sales-down-87-from-a-year-ago)). Video moments don't accrue lore; illustrated canon does. **Lesson**: favour illustration + text artefact over video.
6. **Over-rotating to chain-migration narratives** (Sorare → Solana; Animoca's Moca Chain mainnet) as the product story ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/sorare-moves-solana-upgrade-chain-185250610.html)). Consumers don't buy chain; they buy feelings about a story. **Lesson**: Flow is infrastructure, not the pitch.
7. **Pin-trading-only loyalty layer** (Disney Pinnacle at $400K trading volume after 18 months on-chain). The audience is too casual; no speculative upside ⇒ no gambler engagement ([DappRadar](https://dappradar.com/blog/disney-pinnacle-all-about-the-new-nft-platform-on-flow)). **Lesson**: free monthly pin layer is fine as onramp; cannot be the economy.

## 5. Audience archetypes refined — LoreVault-specific sub-segments

The generic collector/gamer/speculator/connector model is too coarse. Here are eight sub-archetypes grounded in the public-domain literary lane:

1. **The Sherlockian Completist** — 45–65, runs a canonical chronology spreadsheet, active on /r/Sherlock and 221B Con circles, already owns a Granada DVD box set. Will spend $400 on an illustrated *Valley of Fear* edition without blinking. Gambler posture: low; completionist posture: extreme. Priority: full-set guarantees, annotation metadata, vault-grade framing.
2. **The Pratchett-Discoverer** — 18–28, came to Discworld through the 2026 Puffin graphic novels or the forthcoming BBC series. Does not know the order of the novels. Needs onboarding-as-canon-tour. Priority: guided reading pacts, "Rincewind-starter" bundles, footnote-heavy editions.
3. **The Dracula-Daily Diaspora** — 22–35, Tumblr/BookTok native, joined a 240K-subscriber epistolary Dracula group-read for the memes and stayed for the community. Will engage *daily* with serial canon. Priority: scheduled re-emission, meme-ready quote cards, shareable milestone artefacts ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dracula_Daily)).
4. **The Romantasy Aesthete** — 20–34, collects ACOTAR and Fourth Wing sprayed-edge editions, wants public-domain canon refracted through hand-painted art + spice subtext. Buys for the shelf, reads after. Priority: art-first drops, limited illustrated sub-editions, fan-fic-adjacent paratext ([Beautiful Books](https://beautifulbooks.info/special-editions/2026-01/)).
5. **The Taste-Collector** — 28–45, designer / gallerist / architecture-Twitter. Doesn't read the lore; buys for the object. Overlaps with the post-PFP refugee looking for "lore without jpg-shame". Priority: museum-grade typography, artist-first drops, physical tie-ins.
6. **The Multiverse Lorehead** — 20–32, came up on MCU Phase 4–6, reads *House of Leaves* and watches *Everything Everywhere All At Once* annually. Wants Lattice/Lampblack to be *hard* — diagrammatic cosmology, not vibes. Priority: connectable card text, cross-set references, community-editable canon wiki. Pre-sold on the multiverse primitive by Avengers: Doomsday (Dec 18, 2026).
7. **The Gambler-Collector (core Dapper audience)** — 30–50, owns NBA Top Shot, at least one Courtyard vault card, trades weekly. Will spend $5K+ per drop if scarcity is credible and secondary has a bid. **Read: the single highest-LTV cohort in the deck**; non-negotiable economics: burns, serials, leaderboards, challenges.
8. **The Canon-Gamer** — 25–40, plays Magic Arena Standard *and* Marvel Snap; post-meta-fatigued by Universes Beyond becoming the whole game. Wants a TCG whose **lore is the game**, not its skin. Priority: gameplay integration at Year 2, Pratchett-register flavour text, drafting events with narrative stakes.

**Weighting (H):** archetypes 7 (gambler-collector) and 3 (Dracula-Daily) are the first year's economics and funnel respectively. 1, 2, 6 are the heart of the brand. 4, 5 are the aesthetic Trojan horse that opens up the BookTok lane. 8 is Year 2+ when gameplay layers ship.
