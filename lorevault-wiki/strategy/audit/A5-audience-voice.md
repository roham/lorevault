## 1. The Stated Bar

Twelve CEO-sourced criteria. Each is a lift from the cited source file, not a paraphrase.

**Bar 1 — Pratchett caliber.** *"There are authors like Terry Pratchett who take modern tropes and turn them into fantasy. We need to be at that caliber."* — CEO seed brief.

**Bar 2 — MTG flavor-text quality.** *"Cards must be like Magic: The Gathering — art, description, rarity, set, elemental qualities, and most importantly a quote that's directly apt and inspiring and relevant to the image, in as poetic and precise and meaningful and heart-hitting a way as Magic: The Gathering."* — CEO seed brief. Canonized in `FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.md` line 8: *"Each line must sit alongside the best Magic: The Gathering quotes and not flinch — as poetic, as precise, as meaningful, as heart-hitting."*

**Bar 3 — The iceberg.** *"The launch should tease the full vision but there's a massive iceberg of stuff under the hood."* — CEO seed brief. Canonized as the 1:2:4 Iceberg Doctrine (Cosmology §4): for every unit of Surface, two units of Echo and four units of gestured-at Deep.

**Bar 4 — Three-reference fusion.** *"Something between Pokémon that lives in our real world and Terry Pratchett that is almost like a topsy-turvy version of our world, and Harry Potter that is basically like a world within our world."* — CEO seed brief.

**Bar 5 — Multiverse, not anthology.** *"There might be a Sherlock Holmes that is a retro-futuristic cypherpunk from a different galaxy, or it's a Sherlock Holmes that is a robot, or it's a modern day Sherlock Holmes."* — CEO seed brief.

**Bar 6 — Infinitely explorable PD universe.** *"how best to approach the narrative and the storytelling of a public domain universe that can be engaging, exciting, and infinitely explorable."* — CEO seed brief.

**Bar 7 — Cohesive-collectible discipline.** *"Cards should feel like cohesive collectibles telling a story that crosses space and time and different possibilities through sets, drops, and individual content."* — CEO seed brief.

**Bar 8 — More epic / more detailed / more wild.** Vote-side:

## 3. The Unsaid Criteria

Five criteria the CEO has not stated but every iteration reveals:

**Unsaid 1 — Collector-grade, not illustration-grade.** The `victorian-engraving` (0/11) and `silent-film-still` (1/6) rejections aren't about intrinsic craft. An engraving reads as book-plate illustration. The CEO wants cards that read as objects at 2.5×3.5 inches with mythic-rare density. Period-illustration's minimalism is a collectibility disqualifier regardless of quality.

**Unsaid 2 — Win the flick, reward the hold.** The CEO is a gambler-collector. At thumb-scroll speed the 200ms recognition test is everything. `mythic-cosmic` wins at 90% because nebula + divine particulate + god-scale grabs the flick. Ghibli-painted Alice lost because quiet does not win a thumb-scroll. Rule: every card must pass the 200ms flick test AND reward the 5-second hold.

**Unsaid 3 — Recognition-plus-transgression, not recognition alone.** `tarot-baroque` on Irene Adler died ("Way too old school") because it is *just* recognition — no surprise. `rumpelstiltskin × tarot-baroque` survived because the character is obscure enough that the tarot-iconography treatment *is* the transgression. Rule: every card needs Lampblack (you recognize the figure) AND a second charge (shell-flip, parallel-treatment, moral-inversion).

**Unsaid 4 — Epic means grandeur, not action.** Nothing in the 17 comments asks for more motion. Every "more epic" is asking for more scale, density, cosmic-weight. Not Marvel fight-scenes. Caspar David Friedrich with a three-hundred-year-old character standing in the wanderer's place.

**Unsaid 5 — Single-artifact iconicity.** Every approved card contains one Lampblack-strong object or gesture — the pomegranate seeds, the deerstalker on the hat-stand, the half-bitten apple, the seven-and-a-half teeth. Cards pass when the collector can describe the icon in one sentence. Cards fail when they are atmospherically nice but iconographically empty — which is where Mad Hatter's failures cluster.

## 4. The Topsy Moment — Why Snow Black Landed

The CEO's unprompted *"Did you make Snow Black up? I think that's a really clever"* (per `strategy/REBIRTH-ORCHESTRATION.md` §3.5 context) was the seed for the Topsy Register. The mechanism is documented in `TOPSY-REGISTER.md` line 10: *"a Pratchett-grade satirical reading of the same Spine."* Four components produced the delight:

1. **Artifact, not illustration.** Snow Black is a new IP derived by inverting one axis of Snow White's Spine (see `TOPSY-REGISTER.md` Topsy Spine table). The CEO's "did you make this up?" is the audience experiencing *originated-by-inversion-over-preserved-Lampblack* — the exact mechanism Wicked used at billion-dollar scale (per `research/R3-pd-remix-cases.md`).

2. **One axis flipped, not many.** Lampblack (apple, tricolor, glass-coffin-as-cryotube — see `EXEMPLAR-CARDS.md` Card 2) is preserved identically. Only the moral charge on the central figure rotates. The CEO sees the original AND the inversion in the same glance — hence "clever," not "confusing."

3. **The joke holds, never punches.** Topsy line 10: *"a Topsy set is serious about its joke. It is never a punchline."* Snow Black is Snow White with one moral axis rotated and the rest of the grammar treated with full reverence. The delight is *sincerity inside the joke*.

4. **Name telegraphs without explaining.** *White → Black* is a three-character swap. Topsy Decision 20 canonized the rule: *"'Snow Black' passes. 'Snow White But Funny' fails."*

**Rebirth replication rules:**
- Every Topsy candidate must pass the *"did you make this up?"* test. First response = "is that a thing?" If yes-unambiguously-real, it's not a Topsy. If "oh, it's the recognizable thing with one axis rotated," it is.
- The Topsy Spine table already names six further candidates (*The Society of Deductive Practitioners*, *The Hound of Bakerloo Street*, *The Witches of the Three-Star Inn*, *Atalanta's Marathon*, *Dr. Jekyll's Subscription*, *The 8:14 to Hades*). Seed all six into Series 2/3.
- Decision 16 binds at 20% of Sets per Series. That ratio is a load-bearing audience-delight *rate*; below 15% the register undersaturates, above 25% the joke overruns the sincere register and the corpus reads as parody.

## 5. Audience Contradictions

Four places the CEO's taste self-contradicts. The rebirth must resolve each explicitly.

**Contradiction 1 — "More epic / wilder / more detailed" vs "stillness over spectacle."** `taste/ART-DIRECTION-V3.md` line 34: *"Stillness over yelling (Rebecca Guay, John Avon, Terese Nielsen are the most-loved MTG artists — all quiet)."* Yet the 17 comments unambiguously demand "more epic / wilder / more detailed." This is not a contradiction but a misreading the rebirth must correct: *stillness in gesture, epic in scale, wild in rendering density.* Mythic-cosmic at 90% is the proof — both still AND epic. Prompts that land one side while missing the other fail.

**Contradiction 2 — "Pratchett caliber" (comic) vs "heart-hitting MTG-grade" (mythic).** Yawgmoth and the Discworld footnote are voice-antipodes; the CEO wants both. Resolution = the Topsy/non-Topsy split: ~80% of Sets ship universe-native serious (MTG register), ~20% ship Topsy (Pratchett register). Never on the same card (Topsy Decision 18: Topsy + PRIME is illegal).

**Contradiction 3 — *Once Upon a Time*-grade PD recasting vs OUAT collapsed from bloat.** The seed brief names OUAT as aspiration; `research/R3-pd-remix-cases.md` §1 names it as the archetypal failure. The CEO is asking for OUAT's ambition with its collapse-modes structurally prevented. The Lattice's lockbox — eight-shell taxonomy, Spine discipline, Decision 9 ("no Pane is ever decanonized") — is the answer. Ship OUAT ambition visibly while the rate-limiter operates invisibly.

**Contradiction 4 — "Infinitely explorable" vs "massive iceberg under the hood."** Infinite and iceberg pull opposite ways. Iceberg Doctrine (Decision 7, 1:2:4) resolves: the *Surface* expands visibly and infinitely (new Sets, Shells, Parallels), but the *Deep* — metaphysics of Lampblack, number of Panes, origin of the Lattice — stays permanently withheld (Cosmology §4). Infinite exploration at the surface; bounded reticence at the core. A "Lattice origin story" Set would break the bar.

**Secondary contradiction — 15% baseline approval vs the need to ship at volume.** The CEO rejects 85%. At rebirth cadence (10+ candidates/cycle) that's 8-9 rejections per cycle minimum. The loop must be designed for the CEO's taste as gate, not generator; agents must not fear rejection or they will regress toward the mean. A sudden spike to 50%+ approval would be a regression, not a win.
