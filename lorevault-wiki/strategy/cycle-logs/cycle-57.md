## WHY

The September 3–9 arc develops Jonathan in Exeter as the illness unfolds at Whitby — each entry a response to incoming information he can't verify or act on.

The arc has two internal threads:

**Thread 1: Lucy's condition as record.** Jonathan receives four letters about Lucy across the cycle. By September 5 he has recognized that the entries constitute a case record he didn't intend to build — tracking her condition as he tracked things in the Castle. The September 6 "unsent paragraph" makes this explicit: he removes a paragraph from the letter to Mina because it's about his own patterns rather than Lucy, and writes it in the journal instead. By September 9 he names the arc across four entries with an explicit notation: "well → unwell → worse → better (provisional)." The word "provisional" is a key solicitor's term — he cannot claim certainty; accuracy requires marking the uncertainty.

**Thread 2: Professional recovery continuing.** September 8 marks the first entry where Jonathan notices the interval between receiving a matter and performing well on it is shortening. "I read the brief once" — a small notation with a lot of weight. By contrast, earlier in the return arc he was reading briefs twice or three times to confirm understanding. The recovery is measurable at the level of professional practice.

The "not his call" entry (September 4) carries the cycle's deepest moment: Jonathan considers writing to Mina to suggest she come home, decides against it, and notes briefly that he has experience of what it costs when that decision is removed from someone. He doesn't write this in the letter. He notes it in the journal and moves on. The Castle is never named; the implication is precise.
