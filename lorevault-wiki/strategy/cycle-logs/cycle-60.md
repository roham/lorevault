## WHY

The September 24–30 arc closes September. Two entries carry particular weight:

**September 25 ("March 1897"):** The Castle journal opened for the first time past the flyleaf. Jonathan reads March 3, 1897 — the date the Transylvania commission is mentioned as a "potential future instruction." He writes: "I did not know, when I wrote that, what it would be." This is the first direct encounter with his pre-Castle self in the context of the Castle assignment. One page read. The approach to May 3 is now not just a matter of holding the journal but of reading it.

**September 30 ("eleven matters"):** The September tally entry holds the cycle's most compressed lore-doctrine statement. Two sentences: "Lucy Westenra died in September 1897. I completed eleven professional matters in September 1897." Both true. The ledger has no field connecting them — not because the connection is absent, but because a ledger is not the instrument for it. "The ledger continues. Its scope has not changed. September is in it." This is the clearest articulation of the journal's doctrine since the August 12 "first ordinary entry": the ledger is not a record of everything, but it accurately records what it is a record of. September — all of September — is in it.

Also notable: September 29 ("proportionate") is the first entry where Jonathan distinguishes the checking habit as calibrated rather than merely reduced. He reads the Hawkins recommendation twice — from professional care, not anxiety — and notes: "proportionate, not compulsive." The recovery is now measurable at the level of professional judgment about when to apply the habits that the Castle developed.
