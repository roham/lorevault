## WHY

The September 17–23 arc contains the cycle's most concentrated lore-doctrine entries. Four of the seven entries address the journal's limits directly:

**September 17 ("did not time it"):** The timing reflex declined, confirmed twice. The August 27 station reflex — caught and named — is here resolved: he was on the platform and did not measure. He writes it twice to be sure it is in the record.

**September 18 ("hers to keep"):** Mina tells him about Whitby. He does not write everything she says. "Not everything belongs to the journal. Some of it is hers to keep." This extends the journal's doctrine to recognize what it cannot contain — and names Van Helsing as someone who knew more than he said.

**September 19 ("not yet"):** Jonathan opens the Castle journal to the flyleaf. He does not turn past it. He writes "not yet" rather than "not ready" and distinguishes the two: not-ready describes an insufficient state; not-yet names a time that has not arrived. This is a step forward from August 26 (looking at the spine) and September 15 (holding it).

**September 20 ("not without hope"):** Seward's letter read as a document. "Not without hope is not the same as hopeful. The positive form has become unavailable." The solicitor reading a letter as a solicitor — and not saying what he observes to Mina.

**September 21 ("Lucy died on 20 September"):** The death written with the full date because dates of death should be written completely. "I have noted this." The case record that accumulated across seven entries (August 29 – September 21) is closed.

**September 22 ("not mine to document"):** The journal's limit applied to the person closest to him. Her grief belongs to her account. He notes the tea, the parlor, the dinner. He adds: "Mina is here." Accurate and complete.

**September 23 ("no letter for that question"):** He wants to ask Seward what was wrong with Lucy. He does not ask. He writes in his journal: "This was not the letter for that question. There may not be a letter for that question." The thing that belongs to no professional form — and therefore to the journal, or to no record at all.
