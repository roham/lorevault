## WHY

The August 13–19 arc reverses the outward journey and returns Jonathan to Exeter. Each entry introduces a "first" of the return:
- First departure — the westward train, Budapest receding (Aug 13)
- First recognition of an unnecessary notation — documentation habit caught in the act (Aug 14)
- Discovery of Mina's parallel journal — two accounts of the same period (Aug 15)
- The channel — last border; England visible (Aug 16)
- Exeter — the city unchanged; the returning self changed (Aug 17)
- Pre-Castle handwriting — reading own notes from before he knew (Aug 18)
- Carfax papers — the correct work that enabled the Count's arrival; Whitby named (Aug 19)

August 19 is the pivot entry: Jonathan finds his own correct professional work (the Carfax file) and records alongside it the name "Whitby" — Mina's letter from Miss Westenra. The two halves of the novel's horror are present in the same journal entry: the Carfax acquisition (already done) and Whitby (where the Count will arrive by ship). Jonathan writes both notations without recognizing they belong together. He writes: "I do not know why this seemed relevant to record alongside the first."
