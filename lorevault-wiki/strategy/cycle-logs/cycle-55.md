## WHY

The August 20–26 arc is the Whitby preparation arc — Jonathan in Exeter while the horror has already begun elsewhere. The dramatic irony of the arc: in Stoker's timeline, the Demeter ran aground at Whitby on August 8 (bringing the Count to England), and Lucy's first vampiric encounter was around August 11. By the time Jonathan is calmly reviewing Devon leases and filing the Carfax matter summary, the Count is already at Carfax — at the address Jonathan transferred.

The arc never names this. Jonathan has no access to it. Each entry is an ordinary professional entry:
- Aug 20: The Carfax file closed correctly — nothing further required
- Aug 21: Lucy's letter describes Whitby; Jonathan notes it as travel geography
- Aug 22: A clean Devon conveyance; the solicitor's competence intact
- Aug 23: The professional matter summary filed — the Castle absent from professional documentation
- Aug 24: Mina plans Whitby travel; Jonathan notes the logistics reflex
- Aug 25: Eight completed matters; he is current
- Aug 26: Three notebooks counted, the old ones not opened

The August 23 entry ("accurate within professional scope") is a key line: it names the doctrine of the whole post-Castle arc. Jonathan is not suppressing; he is accurately documenting within the scope his professional register provides. The Castle is not part of a matter summary. This is not concealment — it is the structural limitation of professional documentation.

The August 26 entry closes the arc: 176 days documented, the journals counted but not read. He will know when he is ready.
