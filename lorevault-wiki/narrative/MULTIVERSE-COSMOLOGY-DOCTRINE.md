**Status:** Foundational. Downstream design (sets, cards, voice, art briefs, drop scripts) must conform.
**Date:** 2026-04-24
**Companion:** `/economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` (5 Universes / 4 Tiers / 5 Parallels — the economy this cosmology dresses).

## 1. The Meta-Frame — *The Lattice and the Lampblack*

LoreVault's multiverse is called **The Lattice**. Every story humans have ever told that survived the public domain is a **Pane** in the Lattice — a single, fragile, finite reality cut from older glass. The character you know is a *figure caught mid-gesture* inside one Pane. The same figure exists in other Panes, frozen in different gestures, wearing different chassis, but always recognizably *itself*. The thing that links Panes is **Lampblack** — the residue burned off when one Pane brushes against another. A drop of Lampblack on a Pane is what lets a Victorian Sherlock recognize a robot Sherlock as kin and not as costume.

The cosmological mechanic is borrowed from R1's two cleanest case studies and welded together. From Marvel's *Spider-Verse*, we take **canon events** (per R1 §1) — the fixed beats every shell of a character must hit, or stop being that character. Sherlock must deduce the impossible. Alice must fall. Dracula must be found out. The Wolf must be at the door. Persephone must descend. From Pokémon's regional-variant doctrine (R1 §7, R2 §5), we take **species-as-shell**: a Hisuian Vulpix is not a Kanto Vulpix in costume; it has its own type, stat block, and mythos, but the *species spine* — Vulpix — is preserved. The Lattice fuses these. A Pane is a *region*; Lampblack is the *species-memory* that survives the trip across regions; the canon events are the *moves the figure must make to count as that figure*.

This solves the failure modes R1 catalogued. DC's reboot exhaustion (§2): we never erase a Pane, we add Lampblack to it. Marvel's Phase-4 bloat (§1, R6 §3): canon events are the rate limiter — a Pane only ships if its figures hit their fixed beats, otherwise it's sandbox. *Once Upon a Time*'s collapse (R3 §1): we lock the meta-rule before the third Universe collides. The Witcher's Conjunction of the Spheres (R1 §8) is our model for the *single in-world cause* — Lampblack is that cause, and it is the only cause. We will not invent a second.

The Lattice is governed by three load-bearing laws. These are inviolable; per R2's master rule, the most common failure of expanding fictional universes is meta-rule violation, and the rules must state in one sentence each.

**Law One — The Spine.** Every figure has a Spine: a non-negotiable list of canon events they must enact, regardless of which Pane they appear in. Sherlock's Spine: *deduces, falls, returns, refuses domesticity*. Alice's Spine: *crosses a threshold, distrusts size, accepts nonsense as law, walks back into the world unchanged in form but changed in seeing*. The Spine is the load-bearing meta-rule per Universe; downstream design names a figure's Spine before designing a single card.

**Law Two — The Lampblack.** Cross-Pane recognition is communicated through visible residue — a recurring symbol, prop, line, or scar that survives translation. Watson's Afghanistan limp. Alice's blue hair-ribbon. Dracula's count of nights. The Wolf's seven-and-a-half teeth. Lampblack is what tells the collector "these two cards are the same figure refracted," without an exposition card explaining it.

Lampblack acts. It is residue in origin but force in effect — a pressure one Pane exerts on its nearest counterparts through accumulated contact. Sherlock-PRIME, reading a case file in 1895, pauses at a word he does not know and feels, for less than a second, that he has reached for something in a darkened room — a visor, perhaps, equipment with no 1895 analogue — and found it not there. He writes *proceed* and continues. He will not remember it. What pressed through was Lampblack: not his, but Sherlock-CYBER's, bleeding faintly across the Pane-boundary and touching the figure closest to the gestural signature of that reach. Lampblack does not speak; it does not redirect. It exerts — a gravity, a habitual weight, a flicker in the peripheral register of a mind entirely inside its own Pane. This is the distinction that matters for downstream design: Lampblack is not only the *evidence* that two shells share a figure; it is the *mechanism* by which they remain aware of each other at the threshold of perception. A card that carries Lampblack correctly will make the collector feel they have seen this figure before. That feeling is not a metaphor. It is the physics working.

**Law Three — Panes Touch, They Do Not Merge.** Two Panes can leave Lampblack on each other; they cannot be unified into a single Pane. There is no "Earth-Prime." There is no canonical Sherlock. Each shell is *equally real*. This forecloses the *Crisis on Infinite Earths* failure (R1 §2) where the multiverse periodically collapses into one timeline — that move is structurally banned in the Lattice.

The handle: **The Lattice and the Lampblack.** The Lattice is the geometry; Lampblack is the only physics it has. Everything downstream — sets, voices, drops, art-briefs, fan theory — descends from those two nouns and three laws. A character is a *figure* (the eternal pattern). A Pane is a *Universe-shell* (where we find them). A Spine is a *fixed beat-list* (what they must do to be themselves). Lampblack is *the residue that proves they are*.

Per R2, the world precedes the story. The Lattice was here before Series 1; it will be here after Series 12. Series 1 is a single set of openings cut into already-existing glass.

## 2. The Five Universes as Cosmological Anchors

Per the existing economy spec, five Universes are permanent. Each is now redefined as a *Pane-cluster* in the Lattice — a region where many of the same figure's shells coexist with shared tonal signature. Per R2 §1 (Pratchett), each Universe is a *lens*, not a continuity; per R5 §7 Rosewater Rule 5, each carries a color-philosophy register that downstream voice must obey.

### 2.1 Baker Street — *The Pane of Argument*

**Tonal signature:** Gaslight, fog, the click of a pocket-watch, paper turning in lamplight. Cold deduction with a warmth at the hearth. Per R5, this is the **Blue/White** register — curiosity sharpened to condescension, moral certainty held by Watson, doubt held by Holmes.
**Cosmological role:** The Pane of Argument. Baker Street is the Lattice's *prosecuting attorney* — the Pane where reality is interrogated and forced to confess. When a card from another Universe needs to be tested against logic, a Baker Street figure does the testing. Holmes is the Lattice's standing scepticism.
**Canon translation across shells:** The Spine — *deduces, falls, returns, refuses domesticity* — translates with no friction across PRIME, CYBER, MODERN, HOLLOW, and SAINT (Holmes-as-mythologized-detective-god). It does NOT translate to DREAM (Holmes refuses dream-logic by design — that's Wonderland's job). Per R3 §2, BBC Sherlock's load-bearing IP was *Holmes-as-sociopath + mind-palace visual*; LoreVault's load-bearing IP for Baker Street is *Holmes-as-deduction-engine + Watson-as-witness-conscience*. The Watson role is non-optional; a Sherlock card without a Watson echo (in Lampblack, in voice, in implied audience) is a portrait, not a Moment.

### 2.2 Enchanted Kingdom — *The Pane of Inheritance*

**Tonal signature:** Forest at dusk, woodsmoke, iron-bound chests, the specific cruelty of stepmothers and wolves. Pre-Disney darkness, per the economy spec. Per R5, this is the **Black/Green** register — survival logic and natural law braided together; the morality of "what gets eaten."
**Cosmological role:** The Pane of Inheritance. Enchanted Kingdom is where the Lattice keeps its *bloodlines and curses* — the Pane through which generational consequence flows. If a figure in another Universe is paying a debt their grandmother incurred, Enchanted Kingdom is where that ledger is kept. Snow White's apple, Cinderella's ash, Hansel's crumbs — all are Lampblack-rich because they survive across centuries.
**Canon translation across shells:** Per R3 §6 (Wicked) and R3 §1 (*Once Upon a Time*), this Universe's most monetisable move is **sympathetic-villain reframing**. Stepmothers, wolves, witches all have full Spines; their canon events include *acts justified by their own logic*. The Wolf of Red Riding Hood is not a costume villain — his Spine includes *hunger*, *misread as evil*, *eaten at the end*. Translates cleanly to PRIME, CYBER, MODERN, MIRROR, SAINT. Tightest-fitting shell across the five: this Universe's figures *want* to be re-skinned, because the source material is short and the shells expand it.

### 2.3 Wonderland — *The Pane of Categorical Failure*

**Tonal signature:** Vivid impossible color, garden geometry that doesn't add up, sentences that loop. Carroll's logic-puzzle sub-stratum under nonsense surface. Per R5, this is the **Blue** register — pure curiosity with the moral compass spinning.
**Cosmological role:** The Pane of Categorical Failure. Wonderland is the Lattice's *fault zone* — the place where the categories of other Universes (size, time, cause, identity) stop holding. Per R1 §10 (Borges/Chiang), Wonderland is the Pane where every choice is taken simultaneously and the moral weight comes from grief over the road not taken. When the Lattice needs to surface that any figure could have been someone else, Wonderland holds the proof.
**Canon translation across shells:** Alice's Spine — *crosses a threshold, distrusts size, accepts nonsense as law, walks back* — is universal. The Cheshire Cat, Hatter, and Queen of Hearts all carry Spines that survive translation, but Wonderland is the Universe most resistant to HOLLOW (post-apocalyptic) — apocalypse implies a coherent before-and-after, and Wonderland denies coherent time. CYBER, DREAM, MIRROR translate beautifully; HOLLOW does not. Per R3 §1 lesson, lock cross-pollination physics: **Wonderland figures cannot appear in HOLLOW shells, full stop.**

### 2.4 Gothic Horror — *The Pane of Transformation*

**Tonal signature:** Wet stone, candle-wax, breath in cold rooms, the specific anatomy of fear. Stoker, Shelley, Leroux, Stevenson — the literature of the body as betrayer. Per R5, this is the **Black** register — self-interest and dark humor, with R/B borderline for the operatic figures (Phantom, Hyde).
**Cosmological role:** The Pane of Transformation. Gothic Horror holds the Lattice's *body horror archive* — every figure who is not what they appear to be, every body that is also a confession. When Lampblack carries the residue of "this figure was once something else," it travelled through Gothic Horror.
**Canon translation across shells:** Per R3 §7 (Castlevania) lesson, Dracula's Spine *includes grief* — Lisa Tepes's burning kicks the entire arc; without grief, Dracula is a costume. LoreVault enforces emotional Spines: Frankenstein's creature must *be witnessed and rejected*; Hyde must *be released by Jekyll's choice*; the Phantom must *be unmasked*. Translates to PRIME, CYBER, NEON, HOLLOW (Gothic Horror is the Universe most fluent in HOLLOW because apocalypse is its native register). Per R3 §4 (*Penny Dreadful*) lesson, this Universe needs an **original anchor** — see §6 Decision 11 below — to keep collectors invested across the borrowed names.

### 2.5 Greek Myth — *The Pane of Fate*

**Tonal signature:** Bronze, wine-dark sea, stone polished by feet, the specific weight of inevitability. Hesiod, Homer, Ovid. Per R5, this is the **White/Red** register — moral certainty enforced by impulsive divine emotion; Olympian gods are *certain* and *furious*.
**Cosmological role:** The Pane of Fate. Greek Myth is where the Lattice keeps its *long-running consequences and the gods who enforce them*. Where Enchanted Kingdom holds three-generation debt, Greek Myth holds *cosmic-scale debt* — Atlas, Sisyphus, Prometheus, the Furies. When a Lampblack residue carries the weight of "this is forever," it passed through Greek Myth.
**Canon translation across shells:** Greek figures carry the heaviest Spines in the Lattice — they were canonized 2,500 years ago and are over-determined. Persephone descends. Odysseus returns home and is unrecognized. Achilles chooses glory over length-of-life. These Spines translate to AETHER (their native register), PRIME, CYBER (cleanly — Achilles in CYBER is recognizably Achilles), and SAINT (Greek figures *invented* SAINT). They translate *poorly* to MIRROR — the gender architecture of Greek myth is so canonized that flipping it removes Lampblack rather than adding it; per R3 §2 lesson, escalation pressure breaks the IP. **Greek Myth figures shipped in MIRROR must be the rare exception, voice-justified case by case.**

## 3. The Multiverse Shell Taxonomy

Per R1 §1 (Spider-Verse) and §2 (DC), numbered/named taxonomy increases collector value. Per R4, certain shells are culturally legible NOW; others are nostalgia. Per R3 §8 (Bridgerton), the genre is the IP. The Lattice ships with **eight shells**, locked. Future shells require Council approval (per R6 §2 — small canon body, distributed production).

The economy spec already names five **Parallels** (ARCANA / AETHER / WITNESS / NEON / 1/1 ONE-OFF) which are *art treatments* — visual signatures that cut across all sets. **Shells are different from Parallels.** Shells are *narrative-cosmological treatments* — the version of the figure being depicted. A Sherlock-CYBER Moment can be rendered in ARCANA, AETHER, WITNESS, or NEON Parallels. Shell is the *who*; Parallel is the *how the painting reads*. (See §6 Decision 6.)

**SHELL-01: PRIME.** The figure in their canonical period, faithfully rendered. Victorian Sherlock; medieval Wolf; Bram Stoker's 1897 Dracula. Spine fully present, no relocation. **Who fits:** every figure in the Lattice. **Who doesn't:** none — PRIME is mandatory baseline. Per R3 §2 (BBC Sherlock failure), PRIME must not be reverent museum-piece work; it must have an interpretive thesis. Per R4 Thread 3, the WITNESS Parallel pairs especially well with PRIME — sepia "evidence" of a fictional figure as if historically real.

**SHELL-02: CYBER.** Cyberpunk relocation — the figure in a near-future high-tech low-life chassis. Sherlock as cyberdeck-running consultant; Alice in a server room; Frankenstein's creature as biotech runaway. Per R4 Thread 5 (steampunk/dieselpunk fatigue), CYBER works *because* it is the inheritor of what steampunk used to be — texture in a flat-design world. **Who fits:** Sherlock (cleanly), Frankenstein (perfect — Mary Shelley wrote a CYBER figure in 1818), Hyde, Phantom, Wolf. **Who doesn't:** Alice (Wonderland already breaks tech-logic; CYBER becomes redundant), Persephone (her descent is geological/divine, not infrastructural). Use sparingly in Wonderland.

**SHELL-03: MODERN.** Today's urban world. The figure in 2026 with phones, Ubers, Slack, jetlag. Per R3 §2, this is BBC Sherlock's terrain — and the failure mode is escalation-to-parody. **Who fits:** Sherlock, Watson, Moriarty, Dorian Gray, Snow White (as a queer teen in Brooklyn), Hades (as a Manhattan estate-lawyer). **Who doesn't:** the Wolf (the literal physical animal collapses to mascot in MODERN — the *figure* Wolf must remain feral), Cyclops, the Furies. MODERN demands a working analog for the figure's threat-register; if the threat doesn't translate, the shell fails.

**SHELL-04: AETHER.** Mythic-cosmic register. The figure raised to god-tier scale, depicted against nebulae and divine particulate. This is the parallel-rendered art register the economy spec already names; as a *shell*, AETHER means the figure *is* operating at cosmic scale, not merely depicted there. Hades-AETHER is Hades-as-cosmic-judgment, not Hades-with-glowing-background. **Who fits:** Greek figures (native), Dracula (cosmic-scale antagonism is in the source), Sherlock-as-Reason-itself, the Wolf-as-Hunger-itself. **Who doesn't:** Watson (his power is being un-cosmic), the Hatter (his power is local nonsense). AETHER is for figures whose Spine *can* support cosmic scale; forcing it on figures whose power is intimate kills them.

**SHELL-05: HOLLOW.** Post-apocalyptic. The figure operating in a Lattice-Pane after a catastrophe. Ash, ruin, scarcity, the specific exhaustion of survivors. **Who fits:** Frankenstein's creature (perfect — already a survivor), Dracula (cleanly — vampires inherit the world), Wolf (the apocalypse is his element), Persephone (winter eternalised). **Who doesn't:** Alice (per §2.3 — the Pane of Categorical Failure rejects HOLLOW's coherent before/after), Watson (HOLLOW erodes the social register Watson needs to witness from), the Hatter (same reason as Alice). HOLLOW is the most powerful shell for Gothic Horror; deploy heavily there, sparingly elsewhere.

**SHELL-06: MIRROR.** Identity-inverted — gender-flipped, role-swapped, or canonical-relationship-reversed. Per R4 Thread 2 (post-colonial counterfactuals) and Thread 6 (recognition + transgression), MIRROR is one of the most resonant registers in current culture, but R3 §6 (Wicked) and R3 §1 (Once Upon a Time) prove it works only when the inversion is *answered* by a Spine-event, not just a costume change. A female-Sherlock Spine still includes *deduces, falls, returns* — but the *refuses domesticity* canon event now reads against different cultural pressure. **Who fits:** Sherlock, Watson, Hatter, Wolf, Hyde, Snow White (as Snow Black, gender-and-role inverted), Phantom (as woman behind the mask in a male-fronted opera). **Who doesn't:** Greek figures with rare exceptions (per §2.5), Dracula's count-as-aristocrat-of-self-denial (the gender architecture is foundational to the figure).

**SHELL-07: DREAM.** Psychedelic-spiritual. The figure depicted in waking-dream register — Ghibli-adjacent (per R2 §6), Sandman-adjacent (per R1 §4). Saturated impossible color, interior logic, the figure as glimpsed-from-the-side-of-sleep. **Who fits:** Alice (native — Wonderland is the Pane of DREAM by design), the Hatter, Hades (the underworld as dreamspace), Phantom, Dorian Gray. **Who doesn't:** Sherlock (he refuses dream-logic by Spine), Watson, Moriarty (his power is rationalist-corrupt; dream blunts him). DREAM is Wonderland's home shell; export it sparingly.

**SHELL-08: SAINT.** The figure mythologized to god-tier within their own canon — the version after fans have prayed to them for a century. Sherlock-SAINT is the Sherlock who has become a *concept* — an icon on a wall, invoked. Holmes the patron of investigators; Alice the patron saint of those who fell down holes; Persephone the patron of return. Per R4 Thread 1 (lost civilizations) and Thread 4 (cosmic vertigo), SAINT borrows the *aesthetic of religious veneration* without borrowing its targets. **Who fits:** Sherlock, Alice, Dracula (his anti-saint shadow), Persephone, Achilles, Frankenstein's creature (the patron saint of the made-and-rejected). **Who doesn't:** Watson (sainthood requires solitude; Watson's power is partnership), Moriarty (he must remain antagonist, never icon), Hyde (he is the *failure* of sainthood — his Spine forecloses it).

These eight shells are the locked taxonomy for Series 1 through Series 6. New shells require unanimous Canon Council approval (per R6 lesson) and a shipped justification doc.

## 4. The Iceberg Doctrine

Per R4 Thread 7, the iceberg-reveal structure — surface tease, deeper layer, "forbidden" lore — is the single most valuable mechanic in current attention culture, and LoreVault should own it outright. Per R3 §1 (*Once Upon a Time*), bloat is the dominant failure mode of cross-IP collision; per R6 §3 (Marvel Phase 4), volume collapse of a single canon happens when too much is revealed too fast. The iceberg is therefore not a marketing flourish — it is the rate-limiter that prevents the Lattice from collapsing into soup.

**The principle:** at any moment in LoreVault's life, the audience can perceive three layers of the cosmology — the *Surface* (what is shipped and explained), the *Echo* (what is shipped and unexplained), and the *Deep* (what is referenced but never shown). The ratio is fixed: **for every unit of Surface, ship two units of Echo and gesture toward four units of Deep.** This is the 1:2:4 rule. Per R2 §2 (Tolkien's appendices, Le Guin's anthropological reticence, Miyazaki's no-exposition entry), withholding is the primary tool of vastness. Every Surface fact narrows the Lattice; every visible-edge-of-the-unsaid enlarges it.

**Launch (Series 1, Months 1–4):** ship Surface only for two Universes — Baker Street and Enchanted Kingdom. Ship Echo across all five. Deep references the *Lampblack* as a mechanic without ever naming the metaphysics. The audience is told: *Sherlock and Holmes are figures in the Lattice. The Lattice is bigger than what we are showing you.* The phrase "the Lattice" is used. The phrase "Lampblack" is used. The Three Laws are not stated. Per R1 §3 (Dark Tower), tether don't gate — every Set is enjoyable cold; the cosmology rewards return-visit.

**Months 5–9 (Series 1.5):** Surface-reveal Wonderland and Greek Myth. The Three Laws begin to be felt without being stated. The Spine of Sherlock (deduces, falls, returns, refuses domesticity) is now visible across multiple shells; collectors begin to identify it themselves. **The biggest mistake we can make here is to publish a "Spine reference document."** Per R5 §6, MTG's worst sin was over-explanation; per R6 §5 (Pottermore failure), expansion-by-decree feels like a press release. The Spine is to be *inferred*, not posted.

**Months 10–14 (Series 1 close → Series 2 open):** Surface-reveal Gothic Horror with an *original anchor character* (per R3 §4 lesson; see §6 Decision 11). The Three Laws become statable by community fans, who post their own theories on which is which. We confirm or deny obliquely, never definitively. Lampblack residues across shells are *named* by collectors — Watson's limp, Alice's ribbon — and we let those names propagate. We do not publish them.

**Months 15–24:** Series 2 introduces a sixth provisional Universe — call it the **Borrowed Pane**, a concept borrowed from R6 §1 (WotC's "Universes Beyond"). The Borrowed Pane is *adjacent to canon*, holding figures from public-domain works not yet promoted to permanent Universe status (1001 Nights, Arthurian, Norse). It functions as a sandbox. Its non-canon status is *the feature*. Per R6 §1, Universes Beyond was a $200M+ success precisely because it didn't pretend to be canon — LoreVault inherits this lesson.

**Months 25–36:** the first **Council-approved doctrinal reveal**. One law, stated. We pick the most fan-derived law (whichever the community has converged on most cleanly) and confirm it. The other two stay submerged. Per R3 §2, the lesson of BBC Sherlock is that escalation pressure breaks the IP — the Iceberg Doctrine builds *escalation ceilings* into lore. We never reveal more than one Law per year. We never reveal all three.

**What gets withheld, permanently:**
- The origin of the Lattice. There is no creation myth. No *first* Pane. (Per R2 §6, *My Neighbor Totoro* — Totoro is never explained, and the absence is the asset.)
- The *number* of Panes. The Lattice is finite but its census is unknown. (Per R1 §10, Borges — branching has moral weight only when the road not taken can talk back, but we never count the roads.)
- The metaphysics of Lampblack itself. We say it is the residue of Pane-contact. We never say *why* contact produces residue.
- The fate of figures whose source authors are still in copyright. (Legal hygiene per R3 footer; per R6 §4 Lucasfilm lesson, never reveal what you don't have authority to ship.)

**The trust contract:** per R1 §9 (Star Wars Legends), if we split, we never erase. Every card sold remains canon to *its own Pane*, forever. Even if the Lattice retcons, a Series 1 Reichenbach Ultimate stays the Ultimate of *that* Pane. The collector's investment is structurally protected against future doctrinal change. This is non-negotiable and must be in the terms of service. (See §6 Decision 9.)

The iceberg cadence is enforced by the Canon Council per R6's TPC-model recommendation. The Council's only continuous job is gatekeeping the 1:2:4 ratio. Any quarter that breaches it is failed.

## 5. Voice Doctrine

Per R5, MTG flavor text operates in three modes — Attributed first-person quote, Unattributed narrator, In-world textual artifact — at roughly 55/25/20 in modern sets. LoreVault inherits this triad and adapts it. Per R5 §4, the compression rule is non-negotiable: art shows, mechanic does, flavor *means*. Per R5 Rule 1, a big idea in as few words as possible — operating bound 8–30 words, median 18.

**The house voice — five registers, one per Universe:**

**Baker Street voice — *forensic-confessional*.** Per R5 §3, this is **Yawgmoth-adjacent**: declarative present tense, abstract noun, moral inversion. *"It is not enough to live. One must matter."* Holmes's voice is Yawgmoth's syntactic structure with detective content. Watson's voice is **Karn-adjacent**: third-person fable opening, moral implied, never stated. *"I am reminded of the case of the missing yellow waistcoat. We never recovered it. We recovered the man."* Mode-1 (attributed) at ~70%, mode-3 (in-world artifact: case files, telegrams, Watson's notebook) at ~25%, mode-2 (unattributed) at ~5%.

**Enchanted Kingdom voice — *bone-rhyme*.** Per R5 §3, this is **Greven il-Vec-adjacent** (the Curse archetype) and **the Song Fragment archetype** (R5 §7 archetype 6). Two short lines, internal rhyme or assonance, ends mid-thought. *"Steel for the hand, blood for the field / Neither the holder, neither shall yield."* Wolves speak in imperatives. Witches speak in conditionals — every line is a contract being offered. Stepmothers speak in third-person passive ("a girl was given an apple"). Mode-3 (artifact) at ~50% (woodcut inscriptions, marginalia, recipes), mode-1 at ~30%, mode-2 at ~20%. Per R3 §6 lesson (Wicked), the antagonist register inside Enchanted Kingdom is the highest-leverage voice work — give Wolves and Stepmothers thesis-statement quotes, not exposition.

**Wonderland voice — *Liddell logic*.** Per R5 §3, this is **Niv-Mizzet-adjacent** for the Hatter (rhetorical question, never explains, mathematics-as-flex) and **Liliana Vess-adjacent** for the Queen of Hearts (second-person address, double meaning, sexualized power). Alice's voice is **the Reversal Joke archetype** (R5 §7 archetype 10): three beats — expectation, expectation, snap. *"I came looking for a rabbit. I found a court. I hate courts."* Mode-1 at ~60%, mode-2 at ~25%, mode-3 at ~15% (caterpillar smoke-rings as inscription, tea-stained letters).

**Gothic Horror voice — *operatic-exhaustion*.** Per R5 §3, this is **Sorin Markov-adjacent**: clinical detachment, century-scale time markers. *"For three hundred years I have been the answer to a question no one knew to ask."* Dracula speaks in centuries. Frankenstein's creature speaks in negations (he was made of *not-this* and *not-that*). Hyde speaks in present-tense imperatives. The Phantom speaks in incomplete sentences — music interrupts him. Mode-1 at ~50%, mode-3 at ~35% (letters, journals, opera-libretti fragments — the source texts of these figures *are* mode-3, so we honor that), mode-2 at ~15%.

**Greek Myth voice — *bronze-tongue*.** Per R5 §3, this is **the Philosophical Aphorism archetype** (R5 §7 archetype 7): two sentences, second collapses the first. *"You always have a choice. That doesn't always mean you have a good choice."* Greek figures speak as if quoted by Hesiod three centuries after they died — the voice is *already in citation*. Mode-3 dominates at ~50% (epic-fragment inscriptions, oracle pronouncements, prayer-tablet text), mode-1 at ~30%, mode-2 at ~20%. Per R5 §7 archetype 5 (Artifact-Reading), use ellipsis-opening and ordinal time markers heavily.

**Cross-Universe voice rules (binding on all five):**
1. **No puns** (R5 Rule 3). Reserved for explicit comedy drops, never canon.
2. **No paraphrasing the mechanic** (R5 Rule 4). A Reichenbach Ultimate's flavor text never says "Holmes falls."
3. **One narrator per Set** (per R5 Rule 8 + R6 §1 WotC discipline). A Set is voiced by ONE in-world narrator — Watson voices BS-1, Mycroft voices BS-3, Moriarty's posthumous letters voice BS-4. Cross-Set narrator-rotation is the chief tool against R5 §6's "recurring-character overuse trap."
4. **Color-philosophy alignment per R5 Rule 5** is enforced per-Universe (see §2 above): Baker Street Blue/White, Enchanted Kingdom Black/Green, Wonderland Blue, Gothic Horror Black, Greek Myth White/Red.
5. **Per R5 §6 modern-snark drift warning:** no Marvel-quip voice. No card flavor reads as a Joss Whedon rewrite. Per R3 §3 (Cynicism failure mode), wink-density must stay under sincerity budget. Specifically: no fourth-wall jokes. The Lattice does not know it is being read.
6. **Pratchett borrowed-truth (R2 §1)** is reserved for the in-world artifact mode-3 only. We can satirize Victorian newspapers in Watson's notebook clippings; we cannot have a card flavor wink at a 2026 referent.

**The Mosaic Test (R5 Rule 10):** at every Set lock, voice-lead reads all 20 cards' flavor text in sequence. Do they form a world or a noise floor? If two voices sound identical, one is rewritten. If three voices sound the same, the Set is held.

## 6. The Decisions That Bind

The following thirteen numbered decisions are the load-bearing constraints downstream design must respect. Each cites the research it draws on. Each is *decided*, not deliberated.

**Decision 1 — The Lattice is the meta-frame; Lampblack is its only physics.** Per R1 §8 (Witcher's Conjunction of the Spheres), a multiverse needs exactly one diegetic cause for cross-shell collision; we will not invent a second mechanic. Every cross-shell phenomenon is Lampblack residue or it is sandbox.

**Decision 2 — Every figure has a Spine; no card ships without its figure's Spine on file.** Per R1 §1 (Spider-Verse canon events) and R2 §3 (Le Guin "one true thing per thing"). The Spine is the meta-rule per figure. Cards that violate Spine are rejected. Set leads must publish (internal-only, never to fans) a one-paragraph Spine for every figure on the docket before art is briefed.

**Decision 3 — Shells and Parallels are orthogonal axes.** A Sherlock-CYBER Moment can render in any of the five Parallels (ARCANA / AETHER / WITNESS / NEON / 1/1). Per R1 §2 (DC's house-style identities) and R5 §4 (compression rule), conflating them collapses both. Shell = *who the figure is*; Parallel = *how the painting reads*. Both must be named on every Moment's metadata.

**Decision 4 — The eight shells are locked for Series 1–6.** PRIME, CYBER, MODERN, AETHER, HOLLOW, MIRROR, DREAM, SAINT. Per R6 §1–2 (TPC + WotC discipline), proliferating shells without a canon body is the dominant failure mode. New shells require unanimous Canon Council approval *and* a shipped justification doc — no exceptions, including for the founder.

**Decision 5 — Sherlock-PRIME and Sherlock-CYBER share core deductive cognition (the Spine) but never appear on the same card.** Per R1 §1 (Marvel incursion rule: when two universes' boundaries collide, both die) and per R3 §1 (*Once Upon a Time* bloat). Two shells of the same figure together is a multi-shell *Set theme*, not a multi-shell *card*. Cross-shell cards are explicitly banned for Series 1–4.

**Decision 6 — Each Set is voiced by ONE in-world narrator.** Per R5 Rule 8 + R6 §1 (WotC's per-set Worldbuilding Lead). Narrator rotation is the chief tool against the R5 §6 "recurring-character overuse trap" that killed Jaya Ballard's voice by reprint #20.

**Decision 7 — The 1:2:4 Iceberg Ratio is binding per quarter.** Per R4 Thread 7 (iceberg-reveal structure) and R2 §2 (withholding as the primary tool of vastness). Surface : Echo : Deep = 1 : 2 : 4. Any quarter that breaches the ratio is failed by the Canon Council.

**Decision 8 — The Three Laws of the Lattice (Spine, Lampblack, No-Merge) are revealed at most one per year, starting Year 3.** Per R3 §2 (BBC Sherlock escalation collapse) and R6 §3 (Marvel Phase 4 volume collapse). Year-1 and Year-2: Laws are *felt*, not *stated*. The community must converge on each Law before we confirm it. We never publish all three.

**Decision 9 — No Pane is ever decanonized.** Per R1 §9 (Star Wars Legends lesson). Every card sold remains canon to its own Pane forever. Future doctrinal change cannot retroactively reduce a collector's holdings. This is in the Terms of Service.

**Decision 10 — The Canon Council is a 3-person body empowered to override the founder on canon questions, in writing, from drop one.** Per R6 §5 (Wizarding World single-author bottleneck) and R6 §6 (DC editor-in-chief authority). The single highest-leverage operational decision is decoupling property from founder. The Council's only continuous job is gatekeeping Spines, the 1:2:4 ratio, and the eight-shell taxonomy.

**Decision 11 — Gothic Horror requires an original anchor character by Series 2.** Per R3 §4 (*Penny Dreadful* lesson — Eva Green's Vanessa Ives). PD figures attract; an original keeps. Without the anchor, Gothic Horror is a museum gift shop. The anchor is created in-house, voiced by the Canon Council, and holds full Spine status.

**Decision 12 — Greek Myth figures shipped in MIRROR shell are the rare exception.** Per §2.5 above and R3 §2 (escalation breaks IP). The gender architecture of Greek myth is foundational; flipping it removes Lampblack rather than adding it. Each MIRROR-Greek card must be voice-justified, Council-approved, and shipped no more than once per Series.

**Decision 13 — Wonderland figures cannot appear in HOLLOW shell.** Per §2.3 above. Apocalypse implies coherent before-and-after; Wonderland denies coherent time. The two are structurally incompatible. This is the first locked cross-pollination prohibition; per R3 §1 (*Once Upon a Time* lesson), lock cross-pollination physics before the third Universe collides. Future prohibitions are added by Council decision.

**Decision 14 — No founder voice in canon, ever.** Per R3 §7 (Castlevania/Ellis) and R3 §9 (Sandman/Gaiman). One creator's reputation can void a property overnight. The Canon Council voices the Lattice; the founder voices the company. The two never cross.

**Decision 15 — The phrase "the Lattice" and the phrase "Lampblack" are the only metaphysical proper nouns shipped to fans in Series 1.** Per R5 Rule 1 (a big idea in as few words as possible) and R2 §2 (withholding). Every additional metaphysical noun shipped is a narrowing. Series 2 may add at most one (and only with Council approval).
