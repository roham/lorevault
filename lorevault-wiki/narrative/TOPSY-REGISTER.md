# The Topsy Register — LoreVault's Mirror Mode

**Status**: Doctrine extension to `FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.md` and `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md`. CEO directive 2026-04-24.
**Position in the canon**: 20% of total Sets ship in the Topsy register. The other 80% remain in their universe-native serious voice.

## The Pitch

The serious lattice and the comic lattice are the same lattice — but a third of the visible universe ships **topsy**. These are MIRROR-shell sets written in the lineage of Terry Pratchett: borrowed-truth-as-comedy, named absurdities, footnote-grade narrator, and the central rule that **a Topsy set is serious about its joke**. It is never a punchline.

Snow Black was the seed. The conceit: a public-domain figure recast through a structural inversion that turns the canonical moral charge inside out — *and the inversion is itself the new IP*. Snow White is a fairytale; Snow Black is a Pratchett-grade satirical reading of the same Spine. You can collect both. Collectors who own EK-1 (Snow White, PRIME) and EK-6 (Snow Black, MIRROR) own a *conversation*.

This register is LoreVault's competitive moat against MTG. Magic cannot ship a Pratchett set — its world-building bible disallows it. LoreVault can, because every public-domain figure has a recognized comic shadow already (Pratchett's *Witches Abroad* is exactly this trick on Cinderella). We claim the lane.

## How Much, and Where

- **Volume**: ~20% of Sets ship Topsy. In the 60-Set Series 1-3 catalog: ~12 Topsy Sets, distributed roughly: Series 1 = 1 (canary in coal mine), Series 2 = 5, Series 3 = 6.
- **Shell binding**: Topsy is **only** legal in MIRROR and DREAM shells. Never PRIME, never SAINT, never WITNESS, never AETHER (the mythic register collapses under comic mode). HOLLOW Topsy is reserved for one experimental set and then evaluated.
- **Parallel binding**: Topsy + ARCANA parallel = highest-comedy compositional density (the sacred-geometry mandala becomes a *bureaucratic* mandala — civil-service forms in alchemical layout). Topsy + WITNESS = the documentary register played for satire. Topsy + NEON = synthwave Pratchett (controversial; one Set max per Series).
- **Tier rule**: Topsy can occupy any tier, but the Set's Ultimate Moment must still be *moving*, not just funny. The joke must hold a feeling at the bottom.

## The Footnoter — LoreVault's Topsy Narrator

Every Topsy set is voiced (in flavor text and lore notes) by a recurring in-world narrator: **The Footnoter**. The Footnoter is omniscient, dry, and intermittently sentimental in ways that catch the reader off guard. Their voice is the Pratchett dry-omniscient register imported wholesale: "*It is a peculiarity of the universe that gravity treats poets and accountants exactly the same; this has not stopped either profession from filing complaints.*"

The Footnoter:
- Speaks in third person about figures, never in first
- Drops in unsignaled footnote-asides (often parenthetical, sometimes with em-dashes)
- Names absurdities directly ("the third quietest Sherlock currently on the Lattice")
- Is allowed *one* sincere sentence per set, and the sincere sentence must land harder than the jokes
- Is the only narrator allowed to use the word "the Lampblack" outside of liturgical contexts

The Footnoter is a meta-figure like the Lampblacker — referenced across Sets, never depicted. (Decision 16, Council vote: never give the Footnoter a face.)

## The Topsy Spine — what gets inverted

A Topsy Set's narrative arc is built by taking the source-canon Spine and inverting **one and only one** of its moral or structural axes. Inversions LoreVault has authority to ship:

| Source canon | Topsy axis-flip | Set name (in roadmap) |
|---|---|---|
| Snow White (innocent victim of vain queen) | Vanity inverted onto innocent | EK-6 "Snow Black" |
| Sherlock (unique deductive genius) | Deduction is so common it's a trade union | TBA "The Society of Deductive Practitioners" |
| Hound of the Baskervilles (terror unknowable) | The Hound is a mascot; the terror is HR | TBA "The Hound of Bakerloo Street" |
| Macbeth's witches (fate-bringers) | Fate-bringers running a B&B | TBA "The Witches of the Three-Star Inn" |
| Atalanta (footrace as fate) | The footrace is just a footrace | TBA "Atalanta's Marathon" |
| Frankenstein (creator vs creation) | Wellness-brand memoir | TBA "Dr. Jekyll's Subscription" |
| Persephone (cosmic ritual descent) | The descent is a commute | TBA "The 8:14 to Hades" |

Each row is a license to print a Set. Each must obey: invert ONE axis only, keep the Lampblack 4-layer stack intact, ship a sincere Ultimate Moment underneath the joke.

## Topsy Lampblack Rule

The 4-layer Lampblack stack still applies. **The inversion happens above the Lampblack, not by replacing it.** A Topsy-Sherlock still has the silhouette, the pipe, the temple-fingertip gesture, the deduction-Spine. What's flipped is the *meaning* of those layers — Sherlock's deductive uniqueness becomes deductive ubiquity. The recognition holds; the moral charge flips.

This is the doctrinal load-bearing rule for Topsy: **the joke is the inversion of meaning over an unchanged Lampblack.** If you change the Lampblack, you've made a different character. If you change the meaning while keeping the Lampblack, you've made a Topsy.

## Why this works (per the research)

- **R1 (multiverse conventions)**: Discworld is a single universe that supports infinite stories because Pratchett never broke his world's physics; the comedy comes from applying ordinary physics to mythic situations. LoreVault Topsy works the same way — the Lattice's physics holds; comedy emerges from inversion of moral charge.
- **R2 (world-builders)**: Pratchett's "borrowed truth" technique — every Discworld element is a real human folly refracted through fantasy. Topsy applies it: every Topsy Set takes a real social fixture (HR, subscriptions, civil service, B&Bs) and refracts a public-domain canon through it.
- **R3 (PD remix cases)**: Wicked is a billion-dollar property because it inverted ONE moral axis (vanity → courage) on Snow White's witch and held the rest. Snow Black does this exactly. The Topsy slate is twelve Wickeds, distributed across the catalog.
- **V2 quality review**: explicitly named "Wonderland mode-2 philosophical register" as a gap closure that costs nothing structurally and unlocks the Pratchett bar. Topsy carves out that and wider.

## Decisions That Bind (extending the original 15)

**Decision 20**: Topsy Set names must telegraph the joke without explaining it. "Snow Black" passes. "Snow White But Funny" fails.

## What the customer sees

Topsy Sets are not labeled "Topsy" on the customer-facing card. The label is internal. The customer encounters them as **named MIRROR Sets with a different writing voice**. The discovery is the joy — a collector buys EK-6 expecting more EK content, opens the pack, reads "*Snow Black asked the apothecary whether the apple was free-range*", and falls into a different world for one card. That delight is the iceberg pull.
