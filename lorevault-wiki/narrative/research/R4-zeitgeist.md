# R4 — TikTok / Instagram Alt-Universe Zeitgeist Map (2024–2026)

**Brief:** Map the AI-generated alt-universe / alt-history content space currently driving Gen-Z and Millennial attention. For each thread: dominant creators, recurring tropes, the *sticky* emotional payload, and the *gimmick* failure mode. Then: what LoreVault can authentically claim and what is toxic to touch.

**Data caveat:** TikTok view/follower numbers are imprecise and move daily. Counts cited are observed ranges from public profile pages and reporting at time of compile (April 2026). Treat as orders of magnitude, not point estimates.

---

## 1. The "Lost Civilization" Thread

**Dominant accounts.** `@anaxandrov` ("This is Ancient") sits at the center of the Graham-Hancock-adjacent TikTok lane — Younger Dryas, 70-ton granite beams at Giza, "how did they build it" framing. View counts cluster in the 500K–5M range per video, with the highest-performing posts (Cholula, Saqqara) breaking 10M. `@my.ai.gallery` runs the same thesis through pure AI imagery — Atlantis-as-Santorini, "lost city" carousels — typically 100K–1M views. The wider lane is fed by Joe Rogan / Lex Fridman podcast clips of Hancock himself, which TikTok creators repackage with AI b-roll. Younger sibling format: AI-generated "300,000 years ago" speculative reconstructions, often Veo-3 footage of cyclopean masonry under jungle canopy with a low, slow voiceover.

**Recurring tropes.** Aerial drone-style flyovers of impossibly precise stonework. The phrase "they don't teach you this in school." Cuts to a single archaeologist on a podcast saying "we don't actually know." Maps with question marks over the Sahara, the Amazon, the Black Sea floor.

**Sticky.** Wonder fused with grievance — *the world is older and stranger than the institution told you, and you're the one who noticed.* That's a powerful emotional package: it flatters the viewer's intelligence while promising hidden depth.

**Gimmick.** Repetition kills it. Once you've seen ten "scientists are baffled" intros, the eleventh registers as content sludge. The pseudoscience tax also compounds: serious viewers churn out as the format gets sloppier.

## 2. "AI Imagines [Event] as [Alternate Setting]"

**Dominant accounts.** `@what.if_ai` is the canonical reference — grew from ~27K followers (2023, *Rest of World*) to roughly 88K by 2025, with breakout posts like "What if India ruled Great Britain?" (1.7M+ views) and "What if the Philippines was never conquered by Spain?" (250K+). `@impossibleaicinema` (~250K followers) runs the adjacent format: "AI interviews the Vikings," "AI reports the Black Death as a news broadcast," "AI interviews the Tudors" — Veo-3 character footage with synthetic voiceover. `@anaxandrov` cross-pollinates from thread #1.

**Structural pattern.** Three beats: (1) a counterfactual hook stated as a question, (2) a 5–10 image carousel or 15-second video reveal of the alt-aesthetic, (3) a closing line that lands somewhere between melancholy and triumph ("imagine the world we lost"). Frequent payload: post-colonial rewrites where Global South nations are the imperial power.

**Sticky.** Two emotions stacked. First: *aesthetic delight* — "Mayan skyscrapers" or "Roman steam-trains" hit the same pleasure center as concept art. Second: *political wish-fulfillment* — entire ex-colonial diaspora audiences are engaging with these as identity content, not novelty.

**Gimmick.** Visual Midjourney drift — every "what if" eventually looks like the same brass-and-marble fusion. Also: the format is intrinsically thin. There's no second act, only the reveal.

## 3. AI-Generated Vintage-Photo Storytelling

**Dominant accounts.** No single name owns this; it's a distributed style executed by mid-tier accounts (10K–500K followers) across `#aigallery`, `#midjourney`, and `#aihistory`. Daguerreotype is the dominant Midjourney style preset (catalogued at midlibrary.io); sepia + ambrotype + tintype prompts are the secondary register. The "fake historical photographs" subgenre — covered by *Vice* and *Digital Camera World* in their reporting on the photographer who fooled the internet with AI-generated "ancient cameras" — is the reference template.

**Pattern.** A sepia carousel of 6–10 images "documenting" something that never happened: the 1894 Patagonian airship expedition, the Boston giants of 1888, a forgotten Black cowboy regiment. Voiceover is intentionally archival — soft, slightly hissy, BBC-historical register. The unmarked-AI aspect is load-bearing: viewers either believe it briefly or enjoy the brief belief.

**Sticky.** *Plausibility-pleasure.* The brain registers a sepia photo as evidence before it registers it as image. That micro-credulity, even when consciously corrected, is the dopamine hit. Also: a quiet melancholy — "look at what was almost real."

**Gimmick.** When the AI tells (warps fingers, garbles letterforms), the spell breaks instantly and the viewer feels *had*. The format only works at the current generation of model fidelity; one bad hand kills the post.

## 4. Ancient Aliens / Lost-Tech / Atlantean Crossover

**Dominant accounts.** `@my.ai.gallery` runs a clean Atlantis-ancient-mystery lane. The wider Ancient-Aliens TikTok ecosystem is heavily ex-History-Channel content remixed with AI b-roll — Giorgio Tsoukalos clips, Hancock interview snippets, Erich von Däniken images now rendered live in Midjourney. Hashtags `#ancientaliens` and `#lostcity` aggregate hundreds of mid-tier creators with no single dominant voice.

**Pattern.** AI-rendered "Atlantean crystal-tech" imagery + voiceover quoting Plato + cut to real megalithic site (Puma Punku, Göbekli Tepe). The crossover with Thread 1 is total — ancient-aliens content increasingly *is* lost-civilization content with one extra speculative leap (the builders weren't pre-Younger-Dryas humans, they were *visitors*).

**Sticky.** *Cosmic vertigo.* The implied scale — millennia, galaxies, intentional erasure — produces a sublimity-adjacent feeling that low-stakes content can't match. It also offers belonging: a community of people who "see it."

**Gimmick.** The aesthetic ceiling is low — every Atlantean render eventually looks like a videogame loading screen. And the conceptual ceiling is lower: once you've posited aliens, there's nowhere to escalate.

## 5. Steampunk and Dieselpunk Revival

**Dominant accounts.** `#aisteampunk` and `#dieselpunkaesthetic` are the discovery surfaces; no dominant single creator. The lane is fed by AI-image-prompt accounts (5K–100K followers) plus fashion/cosplay creators using AI as moodboard. Robot Builders' "Prompting for AI-Generated Steampunk & Retro-Futuristic Videos: The 2025 Creator's Guide" is a standard workflow reference. Runway Gen-3, Kaiber, and Pika Labs are the core video tools; Suno generates the score.

**Visual register.** Steampunk: brass, gears, oil lamps, Victorian silhouettes — warm palette, candle-light. Dieselpunk: chromed steel, art-deco geometry, zeppelins, interwar military uniforms — colder palette, fluorescent and sodium-vapor light. Audiences treat these as distinct subgenres; mixing them reads as creator inexperience.

**Sticky.** *Texture-craving.* In a flat-design, glass-and-Helvetica visual culture, ornament functions as luxury. Steampunk and dieselpunk are surface-rich — viewers stop scrolling because the screen suddenly has stuff in it.

**Gimmick.** Both aesthetics are old enough (steampunk peaked ~2012, dieselpunk has been recycling since *Sky Captain*) that a pure visual play feels nostalgic rather than fresh. The format works only if there's narrative on top of the texture.

## 6. Magical Realism + My City

**Dominant accounts.** Smaller and more dispersed than the other threads — no dominant handle has consolidated the lane yet. Pockets exist around Tokyo (kami / liminal spaces, bridging into Izumi Kato fine-art reference and `#liminalfantasy` TikTok), New York (dragons-over-Manhattan AI carousels), Mexico City and Lagos as emerging variants. `#liminalspaces` is the adjacent tag with broader reach. Most posts are creator-of-the-week phenomena rather than franchise accounts.

**Pattern.** A photographically-realistic plate of a familiar city, AI-composited with one impossible element — a kitsune at a Shibuya crossing, a leviathan curled around the Brooklyn Bridge, a dragon perched on Torre Latinoamericana. Voiceover, when present, is first-person and quiet: "in my city, this is normal."

**Sticky.** *Recognition + transgression.* The viewer sees their commute, then sees it broken. The emotional payload is belonging — *my city has secret depth* — combined with the small thrill of surface fracture. This is the thread closest to traditional fantasy fandom's "what if magic returned" hunger.

**Gimmick.** Tonally fragile. One pixel too cartoonish and it reads as Pixar-pitch; one pixel too gritty and it reads as Doctor Strange concept art. Also genuinely hard to series — the second dragon-over-NYC post is already a sequel.

## 7. Conspiracy-Aesthetic Crossover

**Dominant accounts.** This is the radioactive lane. Tartaria / Mud Flood content — the "QAnon of architecture" per Bloomberg — has substantial TikTok reach (slideshow carousels of Beaux-Arts buildings as "Tartarian survivors," buried-doorway photos as "mud flood evidence"). It nests inside `#oldworld` and `#hiddenhistory`. QAnon-adjacent "they don't want you to know" framing has been formally banned-by-hashtag on TikTok since 2020 but persists via spelling variants and rhetorical structure rather than explicit naming.

**What gets borrowed.** The *iceberg reveal* structure (surface layer → deeper layer → forbidden layer). The *suppressed truth* framing ("the books we burned"). The *visual decoder ring* — annotated maps, circled details in old photos. The *initiate voice* — "if you know, you know."

**Where the line is.** The aesthetic moves are reusable. The *targets* are not. The instant the iceberg implicates a real ethnic group, a real living person, or a real political faction, the content has crossed from world-building into propaganda. Tartaria is already over the line because it has functional overlap with white-nationalist Hyperborean narratives (per GNET research).

**Sticky.** *Decoder-ring belonging.* The most addictive emotion in this entire space — you are not consuming, you are *seeing through.*

**Gimmick.** The format demands constantly higher stakes; today's hidden truth must be wilder than yesterday's. That's an audience-radicalization treadmill, not a content strategy.

## What LoreVault Can Authentically Claim From This Space

**Reusable threads.**

The *iceberg-reveal structure* (Thread 7's form, decoupled from its targets) is the single most valuable mechanic in this entire landscape. Surface tease → deeper layer → "forbidden" lore — that is exactly the launch arc the brief calls for, and the audience is conditioned to engage with it. LoreVault should own this structure outright.

The *AI-imagines counterfactual* (Thread 2) is a clean fit: public-domain characters in alternate-setting renders (Sherlock in dieselpunk Cairo, Dracula in a flooded Atlantis, Frankenstein in a Mayan-skyscraper Tenochtitlán) is on-brief and carries no IP risk. This is the most directly reusable execution lane.

The *vintage-photo register* (Thread 3) is the highest-leverage visual voice — sepia "evidence" of LoreVault characters as if they were historical figures gives every collectible a documentary frame. Cheap to produce, high credibility per dollar.

The *magical-realism + my city* angle (Thread 6) is underclaimed and ideal for community UGC: "your city, with our characters." That's a real distribution wedge no one has consolidated yet.

The *texture/ornament* of steampunk and dieselpunk (Thread 5) is reusable as a visual register for specific arcs, not as the whole brand.

**Toxic threads.**

Anything that touches *Tartaria, Mud Flood, or Hyperborean* content — even aesthetically — is downstream of white-nationalist source material and will be flagged by journalists within one news cycle. Avoid.

Anything that adopts QAnon's *they-don't-want-you-to-know* targeting voice, where the hidden truth implicates a real group, is brand-ending. Borrow the *form* of revelation, never the *target* structure.

Hancock-style Younger-Dryas / lost-civilization framing is *adjacent-toxic*: the audience is huge but the discourse is contested, and aligning too closely makes LoreVault legible as pseudoscience-adjacent rather than as fiction. Reference the aesthetic, not the thesis.

**Clean translation:** LoreVault is a *fictional* multiverse using *public-domain* characters with the *iceberg-reveal* delivery cadence. That sentence is on-brief and entirely defensible.
