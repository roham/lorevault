## 2. Summary

### Coverage tally
- **Asks fully addressed: 12 / 14**
- **Asks partially addressed: 1 / 14** (Ask 6: virtual world creation — interactive/metaverse register not directly treated)
- **Asks not addressed: 0 / 14**
- **Asks with cosmetic gaps: 2 / 14** (Ask 11: "robot Sherlock" specifically is folded into CYBER rather than being its own shell; Ask 14: exemplar sample exercises 5/8 shells and 5/12 archetypes — system is complete, sample is intentionally partial)

### Critical gaps that must be closed before SHIP
**None.** No critical completeness gaps. Every ask in the brief has a deliverable that addresses it; the spine of the work (cosmology, shells, voice, roadmap, exemplar cards) is structurally complete.

### Medium gaps that should be closed
1. **Ask 6 — virtual world creation (game-engine / metaverse register).** The brief says "how virtual worlds are created; how narrative worlds are created" as two distinct items. R2 covers the latter exhaustively but not the former in its game-engine / metaverse / interactive-platform sense. If LoreVault is purely a card-collectible product the gap is null. If the CEO has any future intent for a connected virtual-world layer (a Roblox-style metaverse, an MMO, an in-engine 3D environment), the research base is missing. Recommended addition: a mini-R7 covering Roblox UGC platform dynamics, Fortnite-as-platform, VRChat, Decentraland/Sandbox, and Minecraft world-craft — each with technique, what scales, and what fails. Effort: one focused research agent, ~2K words.
2. **Borrowed Pane is gestured at but never fleshed out.** `MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §4 (Months 15–24) and `SET-ROADMAP.md` §4 reference the Borrowed Pane as the Universes-Beyond-style sandbox for Norse, Arthurian, 1001 Nights, but no Set ships in it through Series 3 and no doctrinal expansion exists. This is intentional per Iceberg Doctrine — but a Series 4+ sketch (even one paragraph) would close the loop on what the Borrowed Pane *promises* without committing to ship dates.

### Cosmetic gaps that can wait
1. **Ask 11 — "robot Sherlock" specifically.** The brief calls out three Sherlock shells: cypherpunk-from-different-galaxy (= CYBER + AETHER combo, doctrinally legal but not exemplified as a single card), robot, modern-day (= MODERN, addressed in BS-3-equivalent and per matrix). Robot Sherlock as a literal android-chassis is folded into CYBER ("visor where the deerstalker was, inhaler where the pipe was" per `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §1). If the CEO meant a literal robotics-Sherlock distinct from cyberpunk-Sherlock, that distinction is not preserved. Not a structural gap — an exemplar card or a doctrinal note ("CYBER includes android chassis") would fully close it.
2. **Exemplar coverage (Ask 14).** Five cards do not exercise CYBER-shell, MODERN-shell, AETHER-as-shell (only as parallel), the 1/1 ONE-OFF parallel, the Vertigo-Moment shell-crossing mechanism, or the Dream Hand-Off mechanism. `EXEMPLAR-CARDS.md` closing notes acknowledge this transparently and frame it as deliberate proportionality. To fully demonstrate the system in samples, three additional exemplars would close it: (a) Sherlock-CYBER × WITNESS card showing the cyberdeck consultant; (b) Hades-MODERN × NEON card showing the Manhattan-estate-lawyer threat-translation; (c) a Vertigo-Moment Ultimate (per `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md` §4.1) showing PRIME-figure glimpsing CYBER-self as Lampblack residue. Effort: ~3 additional card specs.
3. **No "Brand Bible" deliverable as a single artifact.** `R6-brand-mgmt.md` recommends a TPC-style Canon Council and a written bible "before the second drop." The doctrine docs collectively *are* the bible, but there is no single consolidated "LoreVault Brand Bible v1.0" document that an external partner (artist, writer, drop designer) could read in 30 minutes. Each existing doc is excellent in isolation; consolidation would help operationalization. Effort: ~1 day of editorial work.
4. **Pratchett-caliber test.** The brief explicitly calls out Pratchett ("authors like Terry Pratchett who take modern tropes and turn them into fantasy") as a quality bar. `R2-worldbuilders.md` §1 covers Pratchett's technique. `MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` §5 voice rules acknowledge Pratchett's borrowed-truth move and reserve it for mode-3 in-world artifacts only. This is a defensible discipline choice but means the *Pratchett satirical voice itself* is mostly off-table for fan-facing cards. If the CEO wanted Pratchett-grade satire to be a primary register (not just a constrained one), the doctrine forecloses it. Worth flagging because it is a deliberate scope-narrowing of the brief's tonal aspiration. Cosmetic because the doctrine cites the rationale (R5 §6 modern-snark drift; R3 §3 cynicism failure mode), not an oversight.
