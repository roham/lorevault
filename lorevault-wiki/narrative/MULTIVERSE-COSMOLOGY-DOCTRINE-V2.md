# MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE V2
## The Cosmological-Variant Pane Model

*Master architecture document for LoreVault. Series 1 onward. Supersedes MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE V1 (the "5 IP-bucket Universes" model) on all points where the two conflict. Where V1 specified parallels, rarities, or art-treatment doctrine, V2 preserves V1 unchanged.*

---

## §1 The New Frame

### 1.1 What was wrong with V1

V1 organized LoreVault as five Universes, each functioning as an IP-bucket: Baker Street (Holmesian), EK (East Kingdom / Asian myth), Wonderland (Carrollian), Gothic (horror-folk), Greek (classical). Universes were lore-isolated. A Holmes card could not meet a Zeus card except through ad-hoc crossover packs, which the team kept producing because the audience kept asking for them.

The crossover demand was diagnostic. The audience was not asking us to break the model. The audience was telling us the model was wrong. Five sealed IP-buckets cannot host the kind of story the founder has been pointing at since the beginning.

### 1.2 The founder's load-bearing instruction

> *"Zeus and Shiva and Odin should inhabit the same universes — of course there would be many universes with different Zeuses and Odins each with their own flavors, kind of like how DC/Marvel multiverses work. But there would also be universes where Zeus and Odin were killed as babies and Titans rule, and yet others where the Titans were killed and never emerged so the Old Gods rule — El, Anu, Nun. This is what Pratchett would do."*

Read this carefully. The founder is not describing five universes with different IPs. The founder is describing **one shared metafiction** containing many universes, each universe internally fully populated by figures from many pantheons, with each universe distinguished from its neighbors not by which IP-bucket it represents but by **a single branching point in cosmological history**.

V1 sliced the multiverse by IP. V2 slices it by counterfactual.

### 1.3 The new model

A **Pane** is a cosmological variant — a universe defined by a single what-if axiom. Each Pane contains figures drawn from any and all pantheons. The Pane's identity is its axiom, not its cast.

- The Lattice-Standard Pane has Zeus and Odin and Vishnu and Holmes-variant all walking around in the same 1880s register. No divergence; the grounding case.
- The Pane Where the Titans Held branches at the Titanomachy. Cronus was never overthrown. Zeus was swallowed and stayed swallowed. Ymir was never killed (because Odin was never born to kill him, because Zeus was never born to be the template for Odin in this Pane's mythography). Tiamat was never killed. The world is older, rawer, bronze-age, ruled by what came before.
- The Pane Where the Old Ones Persist branches further back. The younger pantheons never displaced their elders at all. El never ceded to Baal. Anu never faded to Marduk. Nun is still the dark water at the edge of everything.

These are not three separate IP-buckets. These are three counterfactual answers to the same cosmological question. The figures are shared across all of them; what differs is which figures rose, which were swallowed, which never emerged. The cast is comprehensive everywhere. The axiom decides who's on top.

### 1.4 Creative lineage

This model is not invented from scratch. It descends from four traditions, each contributing one structural rule.

**DC/Marvel multiverse.** The "Earth-X" convention: variants of the same character across parallel Earths. Earth-616 Spider-Man and Earth-1610 Spider-Man are both Spider-Man in a way that survives the differences. We adopt this for the figure-level grammar: Pane-2 Zeus and Pane-7 Zeus are both Zeus, and the audience must feel that.

**Terry Pratchett's Discworld.** The "sounds right but isn't exactly the same" rule. Pratchett's Death is recognizably Death-the-archetype but is also specifically and only Pratchett's Death. The reader hesitates half a second, recognizes the source, then notices the tweak. We adopt this for every figure-name treatment in V2: the Pratchett-grade tweak is the texture rule (see §3).

**Borges, *The Garden of Forking Paths*.** All branches coexist. The garden is the totality of branches; no branch is the "real" one. We adopt this for the Pane-status rule: no Pane is canonical. The Lattice-Standard Pane is the grounding case for the audience's sense of normal, but it has no metaphysical priority. The Pane Where the Titans Held is just as real, just as full of finished history.

**Michael Moorcock's Eternal Champion.** The shape repeats across realities; the axioms diverge. Elric, Corum, Hawkmoon, Erekosë are the same recurring figure under different cosmological constraints. We adopt this for the Lampblack Residue (see 1.5): the figure persists across Panes by its essential shape, even as the specifics change.

### 1.5 The Lampblack Residue

The **Lampblack Residue** is the trace of a figure's essential nature that persists across Pane-variation. It is the recognition handle.

- Holmes-variant has the deerstalker (or its functional equivalent) in every Pane that contains him. In the Holmes-as-Sadhu Pane the deerstalker becomes the ascetic's cloth; the residue is preserved by translation, not by literal carryover.
- Odin-variant has the missing eye in every Pane that contains him. In Pane-13 (Ragnarok-Already-Was) Odin is dead, but the residue persists by absence — the empty seat, the eye-patch hung on a peg, the ravens still circling.
- Loki-variant has the trickster's smile, regardless of which pantheon he's currently embedded in.

The Lampblack Residue is not a costume rule. It is a recognition contract with the audience: *this figure is still that figure, just differently axiomed.* When a card depicts a Pane-variant of a known figure, the Residue is the answer to the audience's instinctive question — "wait, is that…?" — and the answer is yes, with a tweak.

### 1.6 The Pratchett-grade tweak

The fundamental creative rule for every figure, name, scene, and detail in V2 is the **Pratchett-grade tweak**. Sounds right; isn't exactly the same.

Casual reader hesitates 0.5 seconds, recognizes the source, notices the difference. Too obvious is untwisted (Zeus-as-Zeus is V1, and V1 is what we are leaving). Too obscure is incomprehensible (a deep-cut Hurrian variant the audience cannot place is a research-fail, not a tweak).

The 0.5-second test is the operational gate. Every figure-name in V2 must pass it. Hesphaestos passes. The Houri-Cat passes. Sintereclaus passes. A name nobody has any handle on does not pass; a name that is just the source unchanged does not pass either.

§3 documents the tweak doctrine in full.

### 1.7 What this changes

V2 changes the cosmological architecture entirely. It does not change:
- The parallel system (ARCANA / AETHER / WITNESS / NEON / 1/1) — preserved unchanged.
- The card-mint pipeline — preserved unchanged.
- The voice registers from V1 — preserved, with five additional registers added (see §5).
- The cultural-sensitivity discipline — strengthened (see §6).

V2 does change:
- The top-level structural unit ("Universe" → "Pane").
- The way figures are scoped (Pane-bound, with cross-Pane Residue tethers).
- The way Sets are organized (thematic across Panes, not bound to one Universe).
- The Bible files (5 Universe Bibles → 5 Pane Bibles for Series 1, with more to follow).

## §2 The What-If Axiom Catalog — 30 Named Panes

The following thirty Panes constitute the Series 1 cosmological catalog. Panes 1–15 are documented in full; Panes 16–30 are documented in short form for Series 2 expansion.

---

### 1. The Lattice-Standard Pane

**Axiom.** No divergence. All pantheons coexist in a modern 1880s–1920s register. The grounding Pane.

**Pantheon-state.** Zeus on Olympus. Odin in Asgard. Vishnu and Shiva both present in their full Hindu cosmology. Holmes-variant walks Victorian streets where Hermes delivers telegrams. Athena curates the British Museum's antiquities wing. A Valkyrie is a battlefield nurse. Krishna teaches in Calcutta.

**Tonal signature.** Gas-lit amber; Victorian city; high contrast; the familiar made mythic. The palette is sepia and lamp-yellow with sudden saturated breakthroughs (a Krishna-blue, a Valkyrie-silver) where the mythic punches through the modern.

**Anchor figures.** Holmes-variant; Hermes-as-telegraph-messenger; Athena-as-museum-curator; the Valkyrie-Nurse; Krishna-the-Calcutta-philosopher; Hephaestos-as-Birmingham-engineer; Iris-the-rainbow-postwoman; a Norn working as a railway switchwoman.

**Notable absences.** Nobody. The Lattice-Standard Pane's whole point is that everyone is present.

**Lampblack residue.** The detective's deerstalker. The one-eyed wanderer. The lyre. The spear. The blue-skinned cowherd. The hammer. The caduceus, here a brass telegraph-key.

### 2. The Pane Where the Titans Held

**Axiom.** The Titanomachy reversed. Cronus was never overthrown; his children were swallowed and stayed swallowed. Ymir's body still constructs the Norse world (Odin was never born to slay him). Tiamat was never killed (Marduk never emerged because Ea made peace rather than fighting).

**Pantheon-state.** Cronus is sky-king at Othrys. Typhon walks freely. Atlas holds the celestial sphere voluntarily, not as punishment. Apsu and Tiamat hold the Near East. Ymir's body is the Norse landscape; mountains are his ribs, rivers his blood. The world is in an arrested, pre-Olympian state.

**Tonal signature.** Bronze-age amber and basalt. Older, rawer. Gods without human faces. The world still in the act of forming. Scale is enormous; intimacy is rare.

**Anchor figures.** Cronus-Archigenes (the king who never fell); Typhon-Tuphaon (the hundred-headed storm, ambulatory); Tiamat-Tihamat (the salt-sea mother, undivided); Ymir-Aurgelmir (the world-body, breathing); Atlas (still willing).

**Notable absences.** All Olympians (swallowed, still swallowed). Thor (Ymir was never killed, so Odin was never born, so Thor was never born). Marduk. Zeus's children — Athena, Apollo, Artemis — never emerge.

**Lampblack residue.** The sickle at the belt (Cronus's, the one he never had to use again after Othrys). The swallowed potential — characters refer to "the ones inside" in low voices. The enormous scale — every figure is too big for the frame.

### 3. The Pane Where the Old Ones Persist

**Axiom.** The younger pantheons never displaced their elders. El never ceded to Baal. Anu never faded. Nun remains.

**Pantheon-state.** El and Asherah at the source of two rivers, governing a divine council of seventy. Anum at the apex of the Mesopotamian heavens, unmoved across millennia. Nunu (the primordial dark water) lapping at the edges of creation. Dyaus Pita (not Indra) rules the Indo-European sky as a sky-bull, enormous and slow. The world is older, less humanized, less narrativized.

**Tonal signature.** Deep lapis and ochre. Levantine limestone. The geometry of ancient temples. Vast scale, unhurried time. Silence is more common than speech.

**Anchor figures.** El-Elyon (white-bearded, riverine, the old priest-king); Athirat-of-the-Sea (Asherah, walking on tide-foam); Anum (Sumerian sky, absolute stillness); Nunu (primordial dark water, half a presence); Dyaus-Pita (sky-bull, enormous).

**Notable absences.** Zeus (never rose; the Titanomachy was unnecessary because the elder generation was never threatened). Odin (never rose; Vili and Vé and the giants persist instead). Indra (never rose; Dyaus Pita holds his place). Marduk (never rose; Anum holds). Baal (present but minor, never displaced El).

**Lampblack residue.** The white beard at the river-source. The still center. The dark water that predates light. The throne nobody dares fill because it's already filled.

### 4. The Sinterklaas-Reigns Pane

**Axiom.** The Victorian sentimentalization and Coca-Cola commercialization of Christmas never happened. The old winter-feast figures hold real power at the dark of the year.

**Pantheon-state.** Sintereclaus the bishop arrives by canal-boat on December 5. Schmutzlein is his co-equal shadow, not his servant — they are two halves of the same office. Krampen owns Krampusnacht with full authority. The Yule Goat is Thor's animal, present at the feast. Berchta walks January 5. Hulda shakes her featherbed and snow falls. The Long Night (December 21) is a real threshold-event, not a metaphor.

**Tonal signature.** Dutch master chiaroscuro. Canal-black and lantern-gold. Ice and fog. The dark of the year as genuinely dark. Interiors are warm in a way that emphasizes the cold outside. Faces half-lit.

**Anchor figures.** Sintereclaus (the bishop, with staff and book of names); Schmutzlein (the shadow, equal authority, soot-faced); Krampen (Krampus, with chains and switches); Berchta (Perchta, with the goose-foot); Hulda (Frau Holle, with the featherbed).

**Notable absences.** The Coca-Cola Santa (never invented). The American department-store Christmas. Father Christmas in his English folk-variant is present but in diminished form, having lost ground to the Dutch-Germanic complex.

**Lampblack residue.** The bishop's staff. The accompanying shadow. The bundle of switches. The book that lists every name.

### 5. The Wild Hunt Eternal Pane

**Axiom.** The Hunt rides every night, not just on Old Year's Day. The leader changes by season and moon-phase.

**Pantheon-state.** Jölvir (Odin, name shifted) leads the dark-moon hunt. Gwynn-mab-Nudd leads at the equinoxes. Herne leads in the oak-month. Cailleach Daoraidh leads in deep winter. Diana Nemorensis leads at the full moon. The Erlking leads when fog comes from the east. The hounds — Wisht-hounds, Cwn Annwn, Hellhounds, the Gabriel Ratchets — are always running, regardless of who leads.

**Tonal signature.** Motion-blur. Slate and bone. The permanent sound of a horn somewhere just over the next hill. Moors and mist. Speed above stillness. No frame holds still.

**Anchor figures.** Jölvir-on-Slaipnir (the dark-moon leader); Gwynn-mab-Nudd (the equinox leader); Cailleach-Daoraidh (the winter leader); Diana-Nemorensis (the full-moon leader); Rex Catus, the King of the Cats (a leader for one specific liminal night, by treaty).

**Notable absences.** No single permanent leader. The absence of stability is the axiom itself. There is no off-season.

**Lampblack residue.** The horn-note. The white animal being chased. The sound that precedes the Hunt — the audience always hears it before they see anything.

### 6. The Pre-Vedic Pane

**Axiom.** The Aryan synthesis never imposed. The pre-Aryan traditions hold. Asuras are the sky-gods, not demons.

**Pantheon-state.** Varuna and Mitra govern the sky-covenant as equal Asura kings. Rudra is a prairie-storm archer at the wilderness-edge, not yet the cosmic dancer. The Matrikas — the seven terrible mothers — govern birth and death without yet having been synthesized into Durga. Ravana is a learned Brahmin king, not a demon. Indra exists only as an unanchored storm-potential, a weather pattern with no name.

**Tonal signature.** Indus Valley terracotta and lapis. Older India before the Sanskrit synthesis. The steppe behind the monsoon. Architecture is geometric and pre-iconic.

**Anchor figures.** Varuna-the-Sky-Keeper; Mitra-the-Covenant; Rudra-the-Archer (wilderness-edge, not yet cosmic); Ravana-the-Scholar; the Matrikas (seven, distinct, un-synthesized).

**Notable absences.** Indra (storm-potential only, not yet a god). Shiva (Rudra hasn't become him). Durga (the Matrikas are not yet synthesized). The Buddhist overlay is also absent (see Pane 11 for the doubled effect).

**Lampblack residue.** The storm that has no name. The covenant between equal sky-powers. The wilderness-edge archer with the bow that will eventually be cosmic.

### 7. The Yggdrasil-Karnak Pane

**Axiom.** Norse and Egyptian cosmologies braid. The World Tree grew from Nun's primordial waters.

**Pantheon-state.** Yggdrasil's roots descend into the Nun. Ra's solar barge sails Norse underground rivers, passing Nidhogg in the dark. Anubis weighs hearts at the foot of the World Tree, not at the Hall of Two Truths. The Norns sit by the Well of Urd, but the Well is in an Egyptian oasis with date-palms. Osiris and Baldur share an underworld and a death-and-return narrative — they are twin figures, not the same figure but recognizing each other.

**Tonal signature.** Papyrus-gold and ash-silver. The geometry of hieroglyphics inscribed on runestones, and runes inscribed on temple-walls. The Nile delta meets the ash-tree at a horizon that should not exist.

**Anchor figures.** Anubis-at-the-Well (jackal-headed, with the scales, at Yggdrasil's root); Ra's-Barge-on-the-Nidhogg-River; the Norns-in-the-Oasis (three weavers under date-palms); Osiris-Baldur (the dual death-and-return figure, two faces of one rite); a Valkyrie with ibis-wings.

**Notable absences.** No clean separation of the traditions. The absence of borders is the axiom.

**Lampblack residue.** The tree-root descending into dark water. The feather weighed against the heart. The barge passing through darkness. The eye-patch and the ankh in one hand.

### 8. The Wonderland-Safavid Pane

**Axiom.** The Carroll/Wonderland axis shifts. Alice is a Safavid courtesan-poet who falls into a chess-court of viziers.

**Pantheon-state.** The Cheshire Cat is a houri. The Red Queen is a Safavid Shah's daughter. The White Rabbit is a court messenger delivering impossible dispatches across the Isfahan rooftops. The Mad Hatter is a Vizier with an impossible counsel. The logic of Wonderland persists fully — riddles that change their own rules, scale-shifts, forced trials — but the aesthetic is Persian court poetry.

**Tonal signature.** Persian miniature palette. Gold and turquoise. The impossible made mathematically beautiful. Flat-perspective tile-work where a cat can vanish into a pattern.

**Anchor figures.** Alice-Shahrazad (the poet who falls); the Houri-Cat (Cheshire-as-paradise-being, with a smile that lingers in the calligraphy); the Shah's-Daughter (Red Queen, with a chess-court); the Vizier-Hatter (the impossible counselor, with a hat that contains another hat).

**Notable absences.** Victorian England. Lewis Carroll's specific imagery (replaced one-for-one by Safavid equivalents — the croquet flamingos become fighting-quail, the playing cards become chess-pieces).

**Lampblack residue.** The impossible logic. The conversation that changes the rules of conversation. The vanishing smile.

### 9. The Holmes-as-Sadhu Pane

**Axiom.** Holmes was born into a Brahmin family in Calcutta.

**Pantheon-state.** Holmes-Sharma is a wandering Hindu ascetic-detective, applying the methods of jnana yoga to crime. Watson-Whitfield is a British East India Company surgeon, the chronicler. Moriarty is a Theosophist who has weaponized a corrupted Kali worship. Baker Street is a Calcutta lane. Mycroft is an Indian Civil Service bureaucrat with the same impossible memory.

**Tonal signature.** Calcutta sepia and temple-red. The overlay of Empire and mythology. Dust and deduction. The heat shimmers; the deduction does not.

**Anchor figures.** Holmes-Sharma (the sadhu-detective); Watson-Whitfield (the colonial surgeon); Moriarty-Theosophist (the corrupted-Kali adversary); Irene-Adler-as-Devadasi (the temple dancer who is the only one to outwit him); Mycroft-as-ICS-Bureaucrat.

**Notable absences.** Victorian London. The English cultural assumptions of the source material. Baker Street as a London address.

**Lampblack residue.** The deerstalker replaced by the ascetic's head-cloth. The pipe replaced by sandalwood incense. The two-man friendship — surgeon and observer — unchanged.

### 10. The Christ-Was-Mithras Pane

**Axiom.** The Mithraic mysteries won the third-century theological competition.

**Pantheon-state.** Mithras Sol Invictus is the dying-and-rising god of the Roman world. The Eucharist is bull's blood and bread. The seven Mithraic grades — Corax, Nymphus, Miles, Leo, Perses, Heliodromus, Pater — are the social hierarchy. The tauroctony (the bull-slaying) replaces the cross as the central image. Cathedrals are underground caves. The Pope is a Pater.

**Tonal signature.** Roman stone and bronze. The underground cave-temple as sacred space. Firelight and bull-shadow. Closed and warm and permanent.

**Anchor figures.** Mithras-Tauroctone (the bull-slayer, eternally caught in the act); Sol Invictus (his twin, the unconquered sun); Cautes and Cautopates (the torch-bearers, one torch up at equinox, one down); the Father-grade (the Mithraic Pope).

**Notable absences.** The cross. The specifically Christian narrative (though the dying-and-rising pattern is fully present, transposed). The empty tomb is replaced by the empty cave.

**Lampblack residue.** The bull. The cave. The torch that points up and the torch that points down. The blood-and-bread meal.

### 11. The Buddha-Did-Not-Renounce Pane

**Axiom.** Siddhartha became a king. The Dharma never spread.

**Pantheon-state.** The Vedic-Hindu pantheon dominates South Asia unmodified by Buddhist synthesis. Shinto is unmodified by the Buddhist overlay. Bon holds Tibet. The Chinese folk pantheon has no Buddhist additions. Siddhartha Gautama governs a kingdom and is remembered as a wise but not enlightened king. The Bodhi tree is just a tree.

**Tonal signature.** Vedic bronze and saffron. Pre-Buddhist South Asia. The world before the great pacification. Sharper, more martial, less merciful.

**Anchor figures.** Indra-War-King (fully present here, since Vedic synthesis persisted); Rudra-Fully-Realized (here he becomes Shiva, but without the Buddhist overlay that softened him in our world); Shiva-Without-Buddhist-Overlay; an unmodified Shinto pantheon (kami unmediated by Bodhisattvas); Tibetan-Bon figures uncontested.

**Notable absences.** The Buddha (still a man, just a king). The bodhisattvas. Buddhist iconography throughout Asia. The doctrine of non-violence as a major civilizational current.

**Lampblack residue.** The Bodhi Tree (still just a tree, unidentified by anyone). The seat where he sat (empty, unmarked). The path he didn't take.

### 12. The Aztec-Sun-Held Pane

**Axiom.** The Spanish never landed. The Triple Alliance reigns. Quetzalcoatl returns as herald of the Sixth Sun.

**Pantheon-state.** Huitzilopochtli's face is fed nightly with the proper rites. Tonatiuh rolls across the sky on schedule. The Fifth Sun is sustained, day by day, with ongoing maintenance. Quetzalcoatl-Ehecatl is returning from the east as wind, not as Cortés. The gods are present, specific, fed, and answerable. The cosmology functions.

**Tonal signature.** Obsidian and jade. Mexica geometry. The urgency of a sun that must be fed to continue. Architecture is precise; ritual is precise; consequences are precise.

**Anchor figures.** Huitzilopochtli-Fed (sated, watching); Tonatiuh-Rolling (on schedule); Quetzalcoatl-Returning (wind from the east, herald not conqueror); Tezcatlipoca-Watching-from-the-Mirror; Mictlantecuhtli-Patient.

**Notable absences.** The Spanish. The colonial synthesis. The Virgin-of-Guadalupe overlay. The smallpox. The destruction of the codices.

**Lampblack residue.** The feathered serpent. The sun that could stop. The obsidian mirror that shows only truth.

*This Pane requires Mesoamerican-register voice review and consultation with Nahua cultural advisors before any Set mints from it. See §6.*

### 13. The Ragnarok-Already-Was Pane

**Axiom.** Ragnarok has already occurred. The world was destroyed and rebuilt. We are in the post-Ragnarok age.

**Pantheon-state.** The survivors — Baldur returned, Vidar, Vali, Modi, Magni — rebuild. Surtr's fire scorched Yggdrasil's roots but did not end everything. Some prophesied returns did not occur. The world is newer and smaller and less crowded.

**Tonal signature.** Arctic light after catastrophe. Pale green shoots in black ash. The exhaustion and relief of survival. Color is muted but light is clear.

**Anchor figures.** Baldur-Returned (different than before; death changed him, made him quieter); Vidar-the-Silent-Now-Speaking (the silence broke when his father died); Yggdrasil-Regrowing (a sapling, not a tree); Nidhogg-Diminished-not-Dead (the worm survived, smaller); an Einherjar Who Survived (the warrior who fought at Ragnarok and lived, an unprecedented condition).

**Notable absences.** Odin (dead). Thor (dead). Loki (dead). The old order. The ravens (Hugin and Munin both lost).

**Lampblack residue.** The new green shoot. The silence after the horn. The gods who are not their parents.

### 14. The Orphic-Gold Pane

**Axiom.** The Orphic mystery tradition is literally true. Souls transmigrate according to the gold-tablet instructions. The material world is a fallen Pleroma.

**Pantheon-state.** Phanes-Protogonos is the first god, the one who hatched from the cosmic egg. Zagreus-Dionysus has been dismembered by the Titans but will reconstitute. The underworld contains the Spring of Memory and the Spring of Forgetting; the soul who drinks correctly escapes. The Demiurge-Yaldabaoth rules the visible world, mistakenly believing himself supreme. Sophia is incomplete.

**Tonal signature.** Gold leaf and vine-black. The geometry of initiation. The underground as map of the cosmos. Light is precious; most things are dark.

**Anchor figures.** Phanes-Hatching-from-the-Egg; Zagreus-Dismembered (in pieces, awaiting); Sophia-Incomplete; the Gold-Tablet Bearer (the soul who remembered the instructions); Ananke-the-Necessity (the constraint above all gods).

**Notable absences.** The Olympians as rulers (they're present but subordinate to the deeper Orphic order; Zeus exists but Phanes is older and more real).

**Lampblack residue.** The gold tablet. The spring of Memory. The vine. The egg.

### 15. The El-Asherah Pane

*A focused Levantine subset of the Old-Ones-Persist axiom.*

**Axiom.** Canaanite polytheism persisted. Yahweh-monotheism never won.

**Pantheon-state.** El at the source of two rivers, presiding over a divine council of seventy children, each governing one of the seventy peoples of the earth. Asherah-poles in every grove, tended without controversy. Baal is present as storm-champion but is not sovereign — he answers to El. Mot and Yam are in their annual contest, a seasonal rhythm rather than a cosmic crisis.

**Tonal signature.** Levantine ochre and sea-blue. Cedar groves. The architecture of Ugarit. Polytheist peace — no anxiety, no exclusivity, no jealous god.

**Anchor figures.** El-at-the-Rivers; Athirat-of-the-Sea (Asherah, walking on tide-foam, El's consort); Baal-the-Champion (storm-rider, subordinate); Anat-the-War-Daughter (Baal's sister, fierce); Mot-and-Yam-in-Season (death and sea, alternating).

**Notable absences.** The Yahweh-singular. The prophets. The covenant-with-one-people. The Decalogue as cosmic centerpiece.

**Lampblack residue.** The white beard at the water-source. The seventy children. The sacred grove that was never cut down.

### Panes 16–30 (short form for Series 2 expansion)

**16. The Kongo-Norse Pane.** Nzambi's life-force animates Yggdrasil. The Simbi water-spirits are the Nine Worlds' rivers. The Egunguns ride alongside the Wild Hunt. Requires Kongo-tradition advisor panel before mint.

**17. The Pangu-Ymir Pane.** Both world-body giants coexist. The world is doubly constructed from two dismembered cosmic beings; their bones are mountain-ranges pointing in different directions, intersecting at impossible angles.

**18. The Apkallu-Athenian Pane.** The Mesopotamian fish-sages arrived in Athens before Socrates. Philosophy and haruspicy are the same practice. The Agora is also a ziggurat; Plato's Academy reads omens in sheep-livers.

**19. The Manichaean-Bleeding-World Pane.** The realm of darkness has partially won. Light-particles are imprisoned in matter. The world is a battlefield of extraction. Every lit candle is an act of cosmic rescue; every meal eaten correctly releases trapped light.

**20. The Sleeping-Kings Pane.** Charlemagne, Arthur, Barbarossa, and Frederick II are all sleeping in the same mountain simultaneously. The mountain is all four mountains at once, in superposition. Their waking is overdue and the ravens are restless.

**21. The Sun-Wukong-Loki Pane.** Both heaven-storming rebels broke free simultaneously. The Jade Emperor and Odin are both under siege. The two tricksters have not yet met but the collision is imminent and cosmically scheduled.

**22. The Persephone-Inanna Pane.** Both descent goddesses are in the underworld at the same time. The underworld has two queens now. Ereshkigal and Hades are managing a diplomatic incident with Persephone and Inanna both refusing to leave.

**23. The Holmes-Mythos Pane.** Holmes-variant and the Cthulhu-Mythos coexist. The detective investigates the cosmic horror. The horror is genuinely cosmic and the detective is genuinely equal to it. Watson keeps better notes than Lovecraft's narrators.

**24. The African-First Pane.** /Kaggen created the world first. The other creation myths are his stories told wrong. Anansi stole the stories of all the other gods before any of them knew they had stories. Requires San/Akan advisor panel before mint.

**25. The Zoroastrian-Victory Pane.** Ahura Mazda has won. Angra Mainyu is imprisoned. The Frashokereti — the renovation — has occurred. The world is renewed. But something unexpected happened: goodness is boring, and the renovated world is very, very quiet.

**26. The Dionysian-Permanent Pane.** The Bacchanalia was never suppressed. The Dionysian mysteries are the state religion of Rome. The Senate meets in a state of controlled ecstasy. The empire expanded on wine and vision.

**27. The Ghost-Month Pane.** The Chinese Ghost Month is permanent. The hungry ghosts are always out. The living and the dead share the city on equal terms. Ghost-etiquette is the primary social skill.

**28. The Three-Norns-Wrong Pane.** Urd, Verdandi, and Skuld made an error in the weaving of fate. One thread is in the wrong place. No one knows which thread. Every figure in this Pane might be living someone else's fate.

**29. The Shiva-Dances Pane.** Shiva's cosmic dance (Tandava) never stopped. The universe is continuously destroyed and recreated, ongoing. Nothing is permanent. Impermanence is the operating condition. Nataraja's foot is always raised. *Requires Brahmin council and Dalit/Bahujan sign-off before mint. See §6.*

**30. The Last Oracle Pane.** The Oracle at Delphi is the last functioning connection to divine communication. All other oracles are closed. Everyone in the world comes to Delphi. The line is very long. The Pythia is exhausted.

## §3 Pratchett-Grade Tweak Doctrine

The Pratchett-grade tweak is the texture rule that operates at every level of LoreVault output: figure-name, scene-construction, attribute selection, voice register. It is the difference between V1's earnest IP-bucket presentation and V2's recognized-but-not-quite cosmological play.

### 3.1 The 0.5-second test

The casual reader hesitates 0.5 seconds, recognizes the source, then notices the difference. This is the operational gate. Apply it to every name, every scene, every detail.

- Hesphaestos. Hesitate. Recognize Hephaestos. Notice the shifted vowel. *Pass.*
- Hephaestos. No hesitation. *Fail (untwisted).*
- Wbqxr the Anvil-God. Hesitate, fail to recognize, give up. *Fail (incomprehensible).*

### 3.2 Name-shift rules

**Shift one syllable.** Hephaestos becomes Hesphaestos. Krampus becomes Krampen. Cailleach becomes Cailleach Daoraidh (extension rather than mutation, but operating on the same 0.5-second principle). Frau Holle becomes Hulda. Sinterklaas becomes Sintereclaus.

**Shift one consonant.** Tiamat becomes Tihamat (insertion of an aspirate). Anu becomes Anum (the Akkadian inflection adopted as the form). Typhon becomes Tuphaon.

**Shift the inflection language.** A Greek figure rendered with Latin or Akkadian endings; a Norse figure rendered with Old English; a Hindu figure rendered with Pali or Tamil. The figure remains recognizable; the linguistic register tilts.

**The hesitate-and-recognize test gates all of these.** A name that no longer triggers recognition is a research-fail. A name that triggers recognition without any hesitation is V1.

### 3.3 Combined figures

Combination is a stronger move than name-shift. It explicitly fuses two source-figures into a single Pane-figure, preserving the Lampblack of both.

- **Loki-Hermes.** The trickster-messenger serving both worlds. Caduceus and trickster-smile. One figure, two residues.
- **Athena-Sarasvati.** Wisdom-warrior-goddess and learning-river-goddess. Spear and lyre. The owl alongside the swan.
- **Anubis-Hermes.** The Graeco-Egyptian psychopomp Hermanubis from late antiquity, present in V2 not as a syncretism-metaphor but as a real Pane-figure with full divine office.
- **Cailleach-Hecate.** Winter-hag meets three-faced goddess at the December crossroads. Two old-woman figures with separate liturgies fused into one figure with two liturgies.

Combined figures are powerful and must be used sparingly. Three to five per Pane is the working ceiling.

### 3.4 Shifted-axis archetypes

The shifted-axis move takes an archetype and gives it to an unexpected figure, preserving the archetype's shape while changing its incumbent.

- **Wild Hunt led by the Cheshire Cat.** The Hunt's archetype (riders, hounds, horn, terror) is preserved. The leader is wrong in a way that is also right — the Cat's vanishing-smile fits the Hunt's never-quite-seen quality better than expected.
- **La Befana as Hecate in disguise.** The Italian Epiphany witch is revealed as the three-faced crossroads goddess in retirement.
- **The Tomte as a god who made himself small.** A Norse house-spirit is reframed as a major figure who voluntarily reduced his own scale to escape a cosmic obligation.
- **The Erlking as the Hunt's abandoned advance scout.** Goethe's Erlking is given a place inside the Wild Hunt structure — he was supposed to come back; he didn't.

### 3.5 Cross-pantheon scene mash-ups

The strongest Pratchett moves are scenes, not just names. A scene mash-up places figures from different pantheons in a shared situation that reveals something about both.

- **The Norns at Delphi.** Two fate-weaving systems in the same room. The Norns weave; the Pythia speaks. They disagree about the standard for prophecy.
- **The Apkallu in Athens.** Mesopotamian fish-sages dispensing cosmogony in the agora. The Athenians want logos; the Apkallu offer omens. Productive friction.
- **Anubis and Hel judging the dead together.** Two underworld officers, two scales of judgment, an actual interjurisdictional dispute.

### 3.6 The "doesn't quite fit" test

The full operational gate, restated:

- **Recognize source + notice difference = Pratchett-grade.** This is what we want.
- **Recognize source, notice no difference = untwisted.** This is V1, and we are leaving V1.
- **Don't recognize source = incomprehensible.** This is research-fail.

Every figure-card, every scene-card, every Moment in V2 must pass this gate at the design-review stage. The gate is the difference between the V2 product and any other "myth-mashup" product on the market.

## §4 Set / Pack / Pane Re-Architecture

V2 changes the structural relationship between cosmological location, thematic collection, and curated drop. The new model:

### 4.1 Definitions

- **Pane.** A cosmological variant, defined by a named what-if axiom. The 30 Panes documented in §2 constitute the Series 1 catalog. Panes are stable; new Panes are added by formal axiom-proposal review.

- **Set.** A thematic collection that ranges *across* Panes. A Set is bound by theme, not by Pane. "The Long Night" is a Set; it contains Moments drawn from Panes 1, 4, 5, 13, and 27 — five different cosmological variants treating the same theme (winter, dark, dread, threshold).

- **Pack.** A curated drop. A Pack pulls cards from multiple Sets and multiple Panes. The Standard Pack is required to pull from at least three Panes per drop, ensuring the audience encounters cosmological variation as the default.

- **Parallel.** ARCANA / AETHER / WITNESS / NEON / 1/1. Preserved unchanged from V1. Parallels are art-treatment overlays that operate orthogonally to Pane and Set.

### 4.2 Card attributes

Every card in V2 carries three orthogonal axes:

1. **Pane** — which cosmological variant the card is set in.
2. **Set** — which thematic collection the card belongs to.
3. **Parallel** — which art treatment the card carries.

A card's identity is the intersection: *(The Long Night) × (Sinterklaas-Reigns Pane) × (NEON parallel)* is a different card from *(The Long Night) × (Wild Hunt Eternal Pane) × (NEON parallel)*, even though they share the Set and Parallel.

### 4.3 Card-detail surface

The card detail page shows:

- **Pane chip.** A small cosmological-context tag identifying the Pane (e.g., "Pane 04 — Sinterklaas-Reigns").
- **Set tag.** The thematic collection (e.g., "Set: The Long Night, Moment 12 of 20").
- **Lampblack Residue tether.** A line identifying cross-Pane variants of the same figure ("This figure also appears in Pane 01 as Hermes-the-Telegraph-Messenger, and in Pane 05 as Jölvir's Forerunner"). This tether is the audience's entry point into the multiverse-grammar.
- **Surface / Echo / Deep iceberg.** Three depth-tiers of lore — Surface (the immediate scene), Echo (the cross-Pane resonances), Deep (the cosmological-axiom context). Preserved from V1.

### 4.4 Series 1 Set catalog

Five Sets ship with Series 1, each consisting of 20 Moments distributed across multiple Panes:

- **The Long Night** — 20 Moments across 5 Panes (Lattice-Standard, Sinterklaas-Reigns, Wild-Hunt-Eternal, Ragnarok-Already-Was, Ghost-Month). Theme: winter, dread, threshold, the dark of the year.
- **The Wild Hunt** — 20 Moments across 5 Panes (Lattice-Standard, Wild-Hunt-Eternal, Sinterklaas-Reigns, Sleeping-Kings, Sun-Wukong-Loki). Theme: pursuit, riders, the horn, the chase that does not stop.
- **The Old Ones Persist** — 20 Moments across 2 Panes (Old-Ones-Persist, El-Asherah). Theme: the elder generation that never ceded; the throne nobody dares fill.
- **The Dying and the Rising** — 20 Moments, cross-pantheon dying-god complex. Draws from Lattice-Standard, Christ-Was-Mithras, Yggdrasil-Karnak, Orphic-Gold, Ragnarok-Already-Was. Theme: the god who dies and returns, in all its variants.
- **The Trickster Roads** — 20 Moments, cross-pantheon trickster complex. Draws from Lattice-Standard, Sun-Wukong-Loki, African-First, Sinterklaas-Reigns (Schmutzlein as trickster), Wonderland-Safavid (the Houri-Cat). Theme: the figure who breaks rules and sometimes makes the world.

Each Set's distribution across Panes is fixed at the Set-design stage and audited at mint.

## §6 Cultural-Sensitivity Discipline

V2 retains and strengthens the cultural-sensitivity discipline. The expanded Pane catalog brings in figures and traditions that were not in V1's scope. The discipline operates as a three-tier classification with a per-Pane review certificate.

### 6.1 Tier 1 — EXCLUDED until formal community partnership

The following traditions are fully excluded from Series 1 and will not be minted under any condition until a formal community partnership exists.

- **Indigenous American traditions.** Tribal sovereignty applies. These are living law, not mythology. No Series 1 mint. Engagement with any specific nation requires that nation's sovereign tribal government, not an academic advisor.
- **Aboriginal Australian traditions.** AIATSIS Code of Ethics for Research with First Nations Peoples applies. Sacred knowledge is restricted by traditional protocol. All Series excluded until partnership; partnership is unlikely on the timescale of the project.
- **African diaspora living religions** (Vodou, Santería, Candomblé, Lucumí, Palo, others). These are living faith traditions with practicing communities. Twelve-month consultation minimum before any mint.

A figure or scene that originates in any Tier 1 tradition is excluded from V2 output. If a card's design draft incorporates Tier 1 material, the card is killed at design review.

### 6.2 Tier 2 — REQUIRES advisor panel before mint

The following traditions may appear in V2 output but only with prior advisor-panel review and sign-off.

- **Hindu major figures** (Lord Ganesha, Vishnu, Shiva, Durga, Kali, others of comparable status). Brahmin council review *and* Dalit/Bahujan sign-off are both required; one without the other is insufficient. Pane 29 (Shiva-Dances) and any treatment of Pane 06, Pane 11, or Pane 09 figures involving these deities falls under this requirement.
- **Yazidi tradition.** Post-genocide community. NEVER use the "Peacock Angel = Satan" framing under any circumstance. Tawûsî Melek treatments require Yazidi community advisor sign-off.
- **Hawaiian tradition.** Consult the Office of Hawaiian Affairs and relevant cultural practitioners before any depiction of Pele, Kāne, Lono, Kū, or related figures.
- **Maori tradition.** Consult relevant iwi under the principles of the Treaty of Waitangi. Te ao Māori cosmology is not free for use.
- **Parsi/Zoroastrian tradition.** Small living community. Pane 25 (Zoroastrian-Victory) requires community advisor review before mint.

Tier 2 figures may be designed and drafted; they may not be minted without the documented sign-off.

### 6.3 Tier 3 — PROCEED with care

The following traditions have either no living community or a living community whose stance on respectful adaptation is well-documented and broadly permissive.

- **Greek/Roman Hellenismos revival.** A small living revival movement exists; respectful treatment is the standard, and the movement is generally welcoming of artistic adaptation.
- **Heathenry.** Living community; explicitly multi-cultural framing required; carefully avoid white-nationalist signal in iconography, naming, and scene-construction. Pane 02, Pane 05, Pane 13, Pane 16, Pane 17 all require Heathenry-aware design review.
- **Mithraic, Gnostic, Orphic mysteries.** No surviving practicing communities. Relatively free for adaptation. Pane 10 and Pane 14 fall here.

### 6.4 Per-Pane Cultural Review Certificate

Each Pane carries a Cultural Review Certificate, generated through a three-stage gate:

1. **Internal cultural review.** The LoreVault editorial team's cultural-sensitivity reviewer reads every figure draft and every scene draft for the Pane. Issues flagged at this stage are resolved before stage 2.
2. **External advisor review.** Domain advisors — the Brahmin council, the Yazidi community advisor, the Heathenry-aware reviewer, others as specified — read the post-stage-1 drafts and provide written feedback. Stage 2 is non-optional for any Tier 2 material.
3. **Community sign-off.** Where required (all Tier 2 figures, all Tier 1 partnership negotiations), formal community sign-off is documented in writing and archived. Sign-off is per-figure or per-scene, not per-Pane in aggregate; one figure being approved does not approve any other.

A Set cannot mint figures from a living tradition until the relevant Pane's Certificate is complete for the figures involved. The Certificate is the artifact that gates mint authorization.
