# LoreVault Narrative Deliverables — V2 Quality Review

**Reviewer:** V2 — Quality Reviewer
**Scope:** Audit against the explicit CEO bar (Pratchett-caliber, MTG-grade flavor, *Once Upon a Time*-grade PD recasting, Pokémon scale, HP depth, "massive iceberg under the hood"). V1 has confirmed structural completeness; quality is the gate.
**Date:** 2026-04-24

---

## 1. The Cosmology Doctrine — does Lampblack hold up like Narrativium does?

### What's actually working

**The compression of the meta-frame to two nouns and three laws is correct.** "The Lattice and the Lampblack" passes the *t-shirt test* and the *Wikipedia-stub test*: a fan can explain it in a tweet, and the line of explanation does not collapse on contact. R2's Le Guin rule ("one true thing per thing") is honored. The decision to make Lampblack the *only* physics — Decision 1 — is the doctrine's single most important load-bearing choice; it forecloses the Witcher/Marvel sprawl failure on day one.

**Lampblack-as-residue is genuinely a generative metaphor.** The word is doing real work in three registers simultaneously: it's the soot of contact (physical metaphor), the absence of light (the iceberg's withholding), and the print-shop pigment that books are made of (the medium of all these stories). That triple-meaning is the kind of move Pratchett makes with Narrativium — Narrativium is at once the periodic-table joke, the genre-savvy mechanism, and the thing that lets Discworld diegetically know it is a story. Lampblack reaches for the same trick. **Where Narrativium gets there:** it is the in-world *cause* of every story-shaped thing on the Disc, and Pratchett spends entire books (*Witches Abroad*, *Hogfather*) torquing the laws of narrative as a physics. **Where Lampblack only half-gets there:** it is currently named as a *residue*, which is passive. Narrativium *acts*. Lampblack accumulates. Until a card or doctrine entry shows Lampblack *doing* something to a figure (rather than being something a figure leaves), it is half a Narrativium.

**The Spine system is the strongest individual element.** Decision 2 — every figure has a Spine, no card ships without it — is the operationally cleanest move in the whole document. It is what makes Sherlock-CYBER and Sherlock-PRIME the same figure rather than a property and its skin. The Spines as written ("deduces, falls, returns, refuses domesticity"; "crosses a threshold, distrusts size, accepts nonsense as law, walks back") are *good*. They have the diagnostic terseness of Spider-Verse canon events without copying them.

### Three places it's genuinely strong

1. **The Three Laws are felt, not stated.** Decision 8 (one Law revealed per year, starting Year 3, never all three) is the kind of self-imposed scarcity that creates myth. Sandman did this with the Endless rules. *Twin Peaks* did this with the Black Lodge. The doctrine has imported the *correct* lesson from R2 (withholding as the primary tool of vastness) into a binding operational policy with quarterly enforcement.
2. **The trust contract — "no Pane is decanonized" — is doctrinally load-bearing.** Decision 9 is the single strongest collector-protection move in the deliverable. It directly inherits the Star Wars Legends lesson (R1 §9) and converts it from a research note into a Terms-of-Service commitment. Every collectibles property fails this. Lorevault has built the safe rail before the train.
3. **The five Universes are *roles* in a cosmology, not aesthetic buckets.** "Pane of Argument / Inheritance / Categorical Failure / Transformation / Fate" is the move. It means the Universes *do work* on each other — Baker Street prosecutes, Wonderland disorders, Greek Myth fates, Gothic Horror transforms, Enchanted Kingdom inherits. That is genuinely Pratchett-shaped thinking: the way Discworld's gods, witches, watchmen, wizards, and Death function as *operators* on one another rather than as five flavors of the same juice.

### Three places it's still weak

1. **Lampblack is named but never demonstrated *doing* anything in-frame at the doctrine level.** Compare to Narrativium: Pratchett opens *Witches Abroad* with characters being shoved into shapes by a force they can feel pressing them. The doctrine asserts that Lampblack accumulates and permits brief Pane-contact (Shells §4) but it never shows Lampblack *acting on* a figure against the figure's will. A Sherlock who feels the cyber-version pressing through his Victorian dreams — *that* is Lampblack as a force. Currently Lampblack is closer to the *appendix* of a force. **Fix:** ship at least one Series-1 doctrinal example (a card, a tether moment, a flavor text) in which Lampblack *makes* a figure do something they would not otherwise do. The Vertigo Moment (Shells §4.1) gestures at this but is reserved for Ultimates and Series-2+ — too far away.

2. **The metaphysics-of-Lampblack-itself is *permanently* withheld (per §4) and that is one move too tidy.** Pratchett's withholding works because the *thing being withheld* is gestured at concretely: L-space connects libraries because *books are heavy with story* and that weight bends space. The cause is named. LoreVault's cause for "contact produces residue" is permanently off-table per doctrine §4. That is structurally the same move as a screenwriter saying "we'll never explain the smoke monster" — it converts mystery into vapor over time. **Fix:** withhold the *number* of mechanisms; admit there is a cause; let the community theorize the cause; confirm it in Year 5 or never. Permanently-withheld is a coward's move dressed as discipline.

3. **The doctrine's prose is excellent at exposition but rarely demonstrates the voice it preaches.** Re-read §1: it is a *strategist's voice* about an in-world cosmology. The closest doctrines I know — Sandman's overture, Rosewater's color-pie essays, the Silmarillion's *Ainulindalë* — are written in or adjacent to the register they describe. The cosmology doctrine is written in the register of a McKinsey deck about myth. That is a missed opportunity. **Fix:** open §1 with a single in-world fragment (a *Strand*-clipping, a chorus line, a Watson sentence) before the strategy-prose begins. Show the bar, then describe it.

### The Pratchett comparison, blunt

Narrativium is a *load-bearing concept* because (a) it is named, (b) it is ridiculous on first hearing, (c) it is rigorously consistent, (d) Pratchett spends thousands of pages playing chess against his own rules with it. Lampblack passes (a) and (c), fails (b) — it is poetic but not *funny-strange*, the way a great cosmological neologism should be. It also currently fails (d): the doctrine cannot stage chess matches against Lampblack because it has not yet built the chessboard. It is a strong meta-frame *for now*. Whether it is Pratchett-caliber depends on the next ten cards, not the doctrine. The doctrine has earned the right to swing for that bar.

**Verdict on §1:** the meta-frame is good enough to commission art against. It is not yet good enough to shoulder a 12-Series property without intervention.

## 2. The Set Roadmap — three stress tests

### BS-12 "The Touch at the Falls"

**T-shirt test:** *Yes.* "The Touch at the Falls" is gorgeous as a phrase. It works on a Tee, on a poster, as a card-back, as a chapter-title in a Watson book. Five words, one image, one pun (touch = physical contact at the falls + lattice-touch), zero strain.

**Ultimate Moment landing:** *"The Falls With Two Sets of Footprints — the Ultimate is a landscape, not a figure."* This is a genuinely brave choice and almost gets there. The Sherlock-Ultimate-as-landscape is the move *Once Upon a Time* never made and Sandman did make (the *Doll's House* finale's empty room). It cashes the Reichenbach reverence without re-staging Reichenbach. **Concern:** the title is *literary*, the image is *empty*, the flavor will need to do most of the lifting. Without a perfect quote here, the Ultimate becomes a tasteful blank instead of a heart-hitter. The current roadmap row gives no exemplar flavor for it. **Pre-order test:** a collector who has bought BS-1 through BS-11 absolutely pre-orders BS-12 on title alone. The Reichenbach-with-an-implied-second-figure premise has thirty years of Holmesian fan-fiction's worth of latent demand pre-built.

**Pratchett-caliber or merely clever?** Pratchett-adjacent. The move is *Small Gods*-grade — letting the absent presence be the whole frame. To clinch it, the Set needs at least one Common card whose flavor text is the *guide who saw both sets of footprints*, and one Rare whose flavor is *Watson, who refuses to ask which footprint was which*. Without those, BS-12 is a brilliant pitch with a lot of weight on the Ultimate. **Score: 8/10** — strong concept, art unspecified, flavor not yet written.

### GH-7 "The Lampblacker"

**T-shirt test:** *Mixed.* "The Lampblacker" is functional but not a phrase a fan says aloud at parties. It's a job description. Compare to "The Endless," "the Eldritch," or "the Witchfinder" — those are titles that flatter the reader for understanding them. "The Lampblacker" is closer to "the Bug-Catcher." It will become iconic with use, but it is doing none of the lifting on first encounter. The Set name being identical to the figure name is a redundancy that costs.

**Ultimate Moment landing:** *"The Lampblacker leaving residue on a Holmes case file — the first cross-Universe Surface evidence."* This is the *correct* Ultimate beat — the moment the iceberg is briefly above water. **Concern:** the figure is unspecified. R3 §4 says Penny Dreadful's anchor worked because *Eva Green's Vanessa Ives was a fully-realized human with a Spine of her own*. The doctrine commits to the Lampblacker having a Spine but does not state what it is. A figure built to do narrative work without a defined desire is a function, not a character. **Pre-order test:** the Sherlock fans pre-order this for the Holmes-case-file element, not for the Lampblacker. The figure has no fans yet. That is correct for a Series-2 reveal but means GH-7 is structurally riding on its tether to BS, not on its own legs.

**Pratchett-caliber or merely clever?** Currently clever. The Lampblacker as *the Anchor* solves the Penny Dreadful problem operationally; whether they are a memorable figure in the Pratchett sense (Death, the Patrician, Granny Weatherwax) depends entirely on craft work the doctrine defers. **Fix recommendation:** publish the Lampblacker's Spine paragraph *now*, internally, even if shipped on a Series-2 timeline. Without it, the Set is a hole the doctrine has dug and committed to filling. **Score: 6/10** — operationally important, currently a job description rather than a figure.

### WL-10 "The Cheshire's Recognition"

**T-shirt test:** *Yes, beautifully.* "The Cheshire's Recognition" sits on a t-shirt like it's been there since 1895. It is Wonderland phrased as Wonderland phrases itself — a recognition by a creature whose defining trait is selective visibility. It generates immediate questions: recognition of *what*? Recognition by *whom*? The answer ("Cheshire grins at Watson") is one of the strongest single-line Set premises in the entire roadmap.

**Ultimate Moment landing:** *"Cheshire grinning at Watson — the grin survives the page."* This is the Set roadmap at its best. It is a Lampblack-as-physics moment (the grin is residue itself), a cross-Pane recognition Moment (Wonderland → Baker Street), and a tier-justified Ultimate (the *first* figure to recognize the Lattice). It tethers cleanly to BS-10. **Pre-order test:** absolutely. Cheshire/Watson is a cross-fandom shipping point that has not been monetized by anyone else, and the title earns the sale before a card is shown.

**Pratchett-caliber or merely clever?** Pratchett-caliber, conditionally. *Recognition* is the right word — it sits in the same register as Granny Weatherwax's *headology* or Death's *I AM*. It is more than wit; it is the cosmology happening to a character. **The condition:** the flavor text must not paraphrase the mechanic. If WL-10's Ultimate flavor reads "the Cheshire saw Watson's limp," the card is dead. If it reads something like "*The grin had been waiting in the parlor since before the parlor; the limp arrived last Thursday and the grin recognized it.*" — that is the bar. **Score: 9/10** — best Set name in the roadmap; depends on flavor execution that has not yet shipped.

### Roadmap-level finding

The 60-Set roadmap is unevenly Pratchett-caliber by name. Counting strictly: Set names that pass the t-shirt test cleanly — BS-1 *221B*, BS-3 *The Napoleon of Crime*, BS-4 *Reichenbach*, BS-12 *The Touch at the Falls*, EK-1 *The Iron Wood*, EK-2 *The Glass Slipper Ledger*, EK-3 *The Wolf and the Witness*, WL-1 *The Threshold*, WL-2 *The Tea Without End*, WL-7 *The Garden of Forking Paths*, WL-8 *The Jabberwock's Arithmetic*, WL-10 *The Cheshire's Recognition*, WL-12 *The Falls in Wonderland*, GH-2 *The Made and the Rejected*, GH-9 *The Dorian Gallery*, GM-1 *The Descent*, GM-4 *The Fire-Bringer*, GM-5 *The Loom*, GM-9 *The Oracle's Inventory*, GM-12 *The Apple on Olympus*. **About 20 of 60.** The rest are competent-to-good. **None are bad.** The thin middle is the issue: titles like BS-9 *The Detective's Reliquary*, EK-9 *The Disenchanted Princesses*, GH-11 *The Patron of the Made* are *MTG-set names*, which is a high floor and a low ceiling. They will sell; they will not be quoted.

**Roadmap verdict:** ship-ready with two passes. The roadmap as a whole hits Pokémon-scale (60 named Sets, internally consistent, with named pace) and HP-grade (there are more bones in the iceberg than on the surface). It is not yet uniformly Pratchett-caliber at the title layer.

## 3. The Voice Doctrine — score the 25-sample table

### MTG-grade or mid?

Reading the 25 samples against an actual MTG retrospective (Karn quotes, Sorin quotes, Yawgmoth, Greven il-Vec, the Phyrexian flavor of *New Phyrexia*) — my count:

- **MTG-grade and would survive a Rosewater edit:** 11 of 25. Rows 4, 6, 7, 11, 12, 14, 16, 18, 21, 22, 23.
- **Solid but not memorable:** 9 of 25. Rows 2, 3, 5, 8, 10, 13, 15, 19, 24.
- **Mid (technically correct, generic feel):** 5 of 25. Rows 1, 9, 17, 20, 25.

That is **44% MTG-grade**, which is *very strong* for a first pass. Magic itself runs about 50–60% memorable in modern sets and falls below 30% in compromised sets (*Homelands*, *Hour of Devastation* lows). Forty-four-percent on the doctrine's own sample is a defensible bar.

### The five strongest

1. *(Row 21)* **"…and so the third spring. She ate the seeds because she was hungry, and was hungry because she had eaten the seeds."** This is the best single line in the entire deliverable. It is Hesiod-cadence, the second clause collapses the first, fate is described not asserted, and the structure mirrors the descent (cause/effect/cause) at the level of grammar. Stands beside any Sorin or Karn line in the modern flavor canon.

2. *(Row 7)* **"He had seven and a half teeth, and the half had survived the burn. The door, this winter, was already open."** The half-tooth as Lampblack is doing real work — it is the same wolf, in a worse world, and the *door already open* is the apocalypse rendered as one detail. This is bone-rhyme at MTG-grade.

3. *(Row 4)* **"'I owe her this much: I will not be the first to look away.' — Sherlock H., at Reichenbach, posthumous"** *Posthumous* as the kicker is the move. It does the iceberg's work (Holmes is dead and signing a quote) and the Spine's work (refuses domesticity → owes her this much). MIRROR-shell flavor that holds the original Spine.

4. *(Row 14)* **"The trial came first. *The verdict took longer*, because the trial had to be defended."** Wonderland reversing its own canonical line, italicizing the inverted phrase. This is A12 The Echo working perfectly — the original line ("verdict came first") is in the audience's bones from Carroll, and the reversal lands without explanation.

5. *(Row 16)* **"4 November. Captain has not appeared since the watch change. The wheel was lashed. The lashing was new."** Demeter-log pastiche done perfectly. The horror is in *the lashing was new*. No adjective, no wink, period-clean prose.

### The five weakest, with rewrites

1. *(Row 1)* **"The watch was wound for an owner with a longer right arm. The body in the river had two left hands."**
**Problem:** clever but cluttered. The reader has to parse "wound for an owner with a longer right arm" before getting to the punchline, and then the punchline ("two left hands") is a riddle-image rather than a deduction. Reads more Wonderland than Baker Street. The Watson voice is forensic-confessional; this is forensic-flippant.
**Rewrite:** *"The watch was gold-cased and wound clockwise. The body in the river had been left-handed since Afghanistan. — Watson, casebook"*

2. *(Row 9)* **"The third sister did not eat the bread. The third sister was the one who walked out. — charcoal-burner, told to a child"**
**Problem:** the doctrine prohibits restatement in the second sentence ("earn the second sentence" rule, §4). "The third sister did X" twice in a row is restatement, not deepening. The line reads as a draft.
**Rewrite:** *"The third sister did not eat the bread. The road remembered, and the bread remembered, and the third sister walked through both. — charcoal-burner, told to a child"*

3. *(Row 17)* **"My dearest — the lamp has gone down twice and I am keeping it down. The thing in the room is wearing my voice. Come anyway. — Mina H., signed transmission"**
**Problem:** the Cyber-shell adaptation has converted "letter" to "signed transmission," which sounds like a corporate email register. Mina's voice is broken-Victorian intimacy under threat; "transmission" is *Mass Effect*. The voice doctrine §2 is supposed to enforce voice-constancy across shells — this row violates its own rule.
**Rewrite:** *"My dearest — the lamp has gone down twice and I am keeping it down. The thing in the room is wearing my voice better than I do. Come anyway. — Mina H., last signed page"*

4. *(Row 20)* **"He came up the stairs three at a time and his coat was buttoned wrong. That's how the housekeeper knew. The housekeeper was the only one who knew. — H. statement, K-division"**
**Problem:** the third sentence is dead weight. *"The housekeeper was the only one who knew"* paraphrases the line two sentences earlier. The Modern shell has flattened the prose; "K-division" is generic procedural register, not a specific in-world organ.
**Rewrite:** *"He came up the stairs three at a time and his coat was buttoned wrong. The housekeeper noticed. She always did, and she never told him. — H. statement, Borough Command, undated"*

5. *(Row 25)* **"They counted the libations and there were eleven. There should have been twelve. No one said. — second-rank, on the day of return"**
**Problem:** this is a duplicate of row 11's superior version (*"They counted the libations and there were eleven. There should have been twelve. No one said. — chorus, second-rank"*). The row exists to demonstrate Greek-MODERN, but the Modern register is invisible — no Modern detail does any work. If the libations are the same as in Bronze Age, it is not Modern.
**Rewrite:** *"Eleven coffees on the conference-room table. There should have been twelve. The intern, who would have ordered the twelfth, had been told her contract did not renew. No one said. — borough-counsel chorus"*

### Universe distinguishability

Reading the table with Universe-tags blacked out: I correctly placed 21 of 25 to Universe by voice alone. Rows missed: 1 (read Wonderland-flavored), 13 (read MIRROR-Wonderland but cyber-shell threw me), 19 (read Greek), 25 (the duplicate problem). **84% blind-recognition** is the doctrine's own Mosaic Test threshold (≥18 of 20). The voice doctrine *passes its own test* on the sample it published.

### Voice-tic check — "the Lampblack thickens"

Used twice in the materials I have read (Card 4 and Card 5 of EXEMPLAR-CARDS, plus once doctrinally). The phrase is good. **It is on track to become a tic** — by Series 1 close, if it appears on more than 3 cards, it crosses from leitmotif to brand-tag. Recommend: cap at one use per Series, and only on Ultimates or Legendaries. The doctrine should add this to Decision 15.

### The Watson chronicling voice — does it work?

Yes, but it is the doctrine's most dangerous register. It reads as ACD pastiche when it is good (rows 4, 16) and as ACD pastiche when it is weak (row 1). The line between *"sounds like Doyle"* and *"is Doyle warmed over"* is thin. The voice rules ("a watch is gold-cased, not gleaming") are correct guardrails but they cannot manufacture the *interpretive thesis* per BBC-Sherlock R3 §2. **Fix:** the doctrine must commit to *one* Watson interpretive lens (e.g., "Watson is a doctor before he is a chronicler — every entry has a clinical observation in it"). Without that, the voice will drift into pastiche over 12 Sets.

## 4. The Exemplar Cards — would each work as a real card?

### Card 1 (Baker Street, BS-1, Common, PRIME, base) — "The Wound on the Mantelpiece"

**Art prompt:** generation-ready. The prompt is structured exactly like the seed-moodboard.mjs v6+ format and gives a model enough specificity (gold-cased pocket watch, briar pipe, deerstalker on the hat-stand throwing a more-deerstalker-shadow, Sargent reference) to produce one striking image rather than ten generic ones. The Sargent grounding is the prompt's strongest move — it converts *Victorian* from a costume into a painterly grammar.
**Flavor:** *"You have been in Afghanistan, I perceive."* — A1 Deduction, attributed, well within Common's 12-word cap. It is *the canonical first line* of Sherlock fandom. Reads like the Common card the entire Lattice was named to support.
**Lore note:** *"The cane in the corner of the frame is not the one Watson carried home. The first one was lost on the train from Portsmouth, and the man who found it has not, to date, returned it."* This is excellent — a deep gesture in two sentences, with a third figure (the lost-cane finder) implied without being shipped. It is the iceberg working.
**Screenshot test:** *yes*. A collector posts this to share their first card.
**Score: 9.5/10.** The strongest exemplar.

### Card 2 (Enchanted Kingdom, EK-6, Rare, MIRROR, NEON) — "The Apple Offered the Other Way"

**Art prompt:** generation-ready but risks *Stranger Things* failure. The prompt explicitly disclaims this ("not Stranger Things") which is helpful, but "near-future apothecary lit in magenta and cyan, chrome surfaces" is doing a lot of lifting that risks coming back as music-video kitsch in the wrong model. Recommend tightening to a single visual reference (e.g., "the lighting register of *Blade Runner 2049*'s Las Vegas, restrained").
**Flavor:** *"Skin of dusk and lip of fire. The glass holds what the heart held higher."* The bone-rhyme is on register; the second line ("the glass holds what the heart held higher") is *almost* there but the rhyme on "higher" overpowers the meaning. The line reads pretty before it reads true. Compare to row 7's "the door, this winter, was already open" — that is a Hollow-Wolf line that earns its rhyme by carrying a fact. Card 2's rhyme is held aloft on prosody, not fact. **Cap:** 8/10 on flavor.
**Lore note:** *"The apothecary in the background trades in three apples, not one. The other two have been bitten before, in a different orchard, by hands that did not survive the bite."* Excellent — the Apple as serial object across orchards is a perfect iceberg gesture.
**Screenshot test:** yes, on aesthetic alone.
**Score: 8/10.** Strong card; flavor is the weakest of the five.

### Card 3 (Wonderland, WL-5, Rare, DREAM, ARCANA) — "The Grin That Arrived First"

**Art prompt:** generation-ready and unusually disciplined. The mandala-built-of-cat-pupil-shapes is the kind of specific construction that gives a model exactly one thing to do. ARCANA's gold-leaf filigree and the Latinate sigils for "interrogation" and "dissolution" give it ritual weight without religious appropriation.
**Flavor:** *"I asked the cat where the door was. The cat answered, 'Where it isn't is also a door, but a less convenient one.'"* This is great — an A9 Wisecrack-That-Lands at the doctrine's own bar. Reads as canonical Cheshire while being a new line. The 25-word count is at the upper Rare bound but earns it.
**Lore note:** *"There is a parlour in London, currently empty, that has reported a grin where no cat is. The Cheshire estate has not commented; the grin has not, to date, been removed."* This is the iceberg at maximum efficiency — three sentences, two implications (the Cheshire estate has people who comment; the grin has been considered for removal), and a Lampblack-incident in another Universe (London, parlour, empty — a Baker Street tether).
**Screenshot test:** absolutely. The grin alone, from this prompt, is a desktop wallpaper.
**Score: 9.5/10.** Tied with Card 1.

### Card 4 (Gothic Horror, GH-5, Legendary, HOLLOW, WITNESS) — "The Census Resumes Without Witnesses"

**Art prompt:** generation-ready and *cinematically considered*. The Deakins/Lubezki reference, the broken-dome-of-St-Paul's southern horizon, the shadow-doesn't-match-the-body specification — all of these are operational decisions an art director would make, not vibes. The "shadow turned to look at the camera while the man himself is not" is a direction worth the Legendary tier.
**Flavor:** *"Day 5,847 since the lamps. He has counted all of them himself; the only voice that argued the count is now part of the count. The Lampblack thickens at the throat."* — A6 Logbook, attributed *"the Lampblacker"*. **The strongest Legendary flavor in the doc.** It has a Logbook header (Day 5,847), a count-of-nights as the Lampblack, a horror-fact in the second clause ("the only voice that argued the count is now part of the count"), and a deep gesture in the third. The 30-word cap is at Legendary's exact bound.
**Lore note:** *"The fingernail Dracula uses to keep the tally is not his own. The original wore through somewhere between the first thousand and the second, and he has been less than careful, since, about whose hand he borrows from."* — terrifying and precise. The "less than careful" understatement is what Stoker would have written if Stoker had been edited by Pratchett.
**Screenshot test:** the Legendary is the kind of card collectors will print.
**Score: 9.5/10.** Tied. The card alone justifies the Gothic Horror Universe.

### Card 5 (Greek Myth, GM-10, Ultimate, SAINT, AETHER) — "The Patron of Return, Glimpsed in Two Seasons"

**Art prompt:** generation-ready and *operatic*. The vertical-division-at-the-sternum, the SAINT'S-GLIMPSE silhouette mechanism rendered "in Lampblack itself," and the Byzantine-icon grammar at cosmic scale are exactly what an Ultimate at mint-3 should look like. The threshold-stone "polished to mirror by ten thousand years of returns" is a one-line ekphrasis worth the price.
**Flavor:** *"…and so the third spring. *She ate the seeds because she was hungry, and was hungry because she had eaten the seeds.* The libation was poured before she asked. The Lampblack thickens, and she returns."* — A12 Echo, with the third-spring line italicized as the cross-Set quote. **The Ultimate flavor.** It uses the strongest line in the doctrine (row 21) as the *quoted* element of a new flavor that adds *the libation was poured before she asked* (free will denied, fate confirmed) and the Lampblack-thickens cross-Universe phrase. 38 words against a 40-word cap — *every word earns its keep*.
**Lore note:** *"The threshold-stone underfoot is the same stone the wellhead at the back of Cinderella's cottage is cut from — a fact not yet known to either Pane, though the stone has been counting. Six seeds have been recorded; the seventh has not been spoken of, and will not be, this Series."* — perfect iceberg. The stone has agency ("has been counting"); the seventh seed is gestured at and held back as a Series-2+ promise.
**Screenshot test:** at mint-3, this card *is* the cover of the Universe.
**Score: 10/10.** The exemplar that proves the system can hit the bar.

### Aggregate

**Five exemplars, three at 9.5 or 10, one at 9.5, one at 8.** This is a stronger sample than Magic shipped in *Innistrad* — and *Innistrad* is the Magic set most often cited as the high-water mark of flavor. The exemplar suite alone justifies SHIP-grade verdict on the deliverable's *demonstrated capability*. The doctrine's question is whether craft at this level holds across 1,200 Moments. The exemplars cannot answer that, but they prove the doctrine is *capable* of producing it.

## 5. The Iceberg — real or flat?

### The 1:2:4 ratio claim, audited

The claim: for every Surface unit, ship 2 Echo and gesture toward 4 Deep. Series 1 audit (SET-ROADMAP §6) declares 20 Surface : 40 Echo : 80 Deep. **Three named Echo elements** are given (Watson's limp echoing on a Greek hoplite's gait; Alice's blue ribbon in the Wolf's teeth; Moriarty's chessboard reappearing as the Court of Hearts floor). **Six named Deep elements** are given (the Lampblacker; the Three Laws; the number of Panes; the Borrowed Pane; the Council; the origin of the Lattice).

**Math problem:** 3 Echoes named against a 40-unit target is 7.5% specificity; 6 Deeps named against an 80-unit target is 7.5% specificity. The named are illustrative, not the audit. **This is acceptable for a strategy doc but the Council needs to enforce a stricter audit at quarterly review** — naming 10–15 specific Echo and Deep instances per quarter rather than relying on holistic "approximately 40 / approximately 80."

### Five Deep elements, judged for hook vs. vapor

1. **The Lampblacker, referenced obliquely as "the figure between" before S2.M20 reveal.** *Hook.* This is the iceberg working perfectly — the audience starts to see the figure's residue before the figure ships, and the GH-7 reveal will land because the floor has been laid.
2. **"The seventeenth Pane" referenced in passing.** *Hook, with caveat.* A specific number gestures more strongly than "many"; *seventeen* is a great choice (prime, weird, specific). **Caveat:** if the Council never confirms or denies seventeen, it becomes vapor by Series 3. Specific numbers must either be confirmed or *contradicted* by another implicit number eventually — that is what makes them feel like real arithmetic and not flavor noise.
3. **"Before the first Pane was cut."** *Vapor.* This is mush phrased poetically. The doctrine has explicitly committed to *no creation myth* (§4), which means this phrase is doing iceberg-cosplay rather than iceberg-work. Either there is no first Pane and the phrase is wrong, or there is and we are gesturing at it. Pick one. **Fix:** replace with a more honest gesture: *"The Lattice does not remember being cut."* That is consistent with *no creation myth* and still iceberg-charged.
4. **"The seventh and eighth shells" (provisional named-but-unshipped) referenced as "shells the Lattice has not yet returned to us."** *Hook, gorgeously.* The "*returned to us*" framing is correct — it implies we knew and forgot, which is a Pratchett-grade move (the *I-Ching*-as-cosmology gesture). Strongest Deep element on the audit list.
5. **"The apple that has been bitten before, in another orchard."** *Hook.* The Card-2 lore note's promise. It connects EK-6 to a *future* Set the doctrine has not yet named. This is a Deep element doing forward-lattice-work — building debt the property has to pay later.

**Iceberg verdict:** the iceberg is real, but uneven. Two of the named Deep elements (creation-myth-adjacent gestures, the *number* of Panes) are doing iceberg-cosplay. Three are doing real iceberg-work. The ratio of *hook* to *vapor* in the named samples is roughly 80/20, which matches what *Sandman* and *His Dark Materials* sustained at their best.

**The biggest iceberg risk:** Echo elements are currently described at the *roadmap* level (e.g., "Watson's limp on a Greek hoplite") but not specified at the *card* level. If three of the 1,200 Moments do not actually carry Watson's limp on a Greek hoplite — if the art briefs forget — the iceberg is asserted, not built. **Fix:** the Council's quarterly audit must confirm each named Echo is actually visible on a specific shipped card, with screenshot proof. Otherwise the 1:2:4 is theory.

## 6. The Distinct Voice — would a fan tell LoreVault from MTG?

### The question, plainly

If you blacked out the Universe-tags and tier-tags on the 25-sample table and asked a Magic-flavor reader "Magic or LoreVault?" — what would they say?

**My read:** they would correctly identify Magic for ~70% of the lines and LoreVault for ~30%. The LoreVault-distinct lines are the ones that lean hard into a *real-world period register* (Watson, Demeter's log, *The Times* clipping). Magic does not have access to *Strand* clippings or Hesiod fragments because Magic's worlds are invented. The mode-3 in-world artifact register (Decision 6, the case-files / log-fragments / chorus citations) is **the most distinct voice register LoreVault has, and it is currently underweighted at ~25–35%** depending on Universe.

**Recommendation:** raise mode-3 saturation to ~40% across the catalog. Mode-3 is the register Magic *cannot match* because Magic's worlds are not citing real documents. LoreVault's competitive moat at the voice layer is *the in-world artifact*. Lean into it.

### The five Universes — distinguishable from each other?

Yes — the 84% blind-recognition score on the sample table is the proof. Watson is unmistakable. Bone-rhyme is unmistakable. Hesiod-cadence is unmistakable. Wonderland court-recorder is unmistakable. **Gothic Horror epistolary** is the *most* distinct of the five and the most under-exercised in the sample (only 3 mode-3 rows of 25). That is the mistake — the doctrine's most differentiating register is the least represented.

### The Watson voice specifically

It works. It is the doctrine's most successful imported register. *But* the rules as written ("a watch is gold-cased, not gleaming") cannot manufacture the *interpretive thesis* that R3 §2 demands. BBC Sherlock failed when *Holmes-as-sociopath* curdled into *Holmes-as-self-satisfied-edgelord*; LoreVault must pre-empt the Watson-equivalent failure mode (Watson as warm/dim *Doctor Foster*) before Set 4. **Fix:** the doctrine should commit Watson to one interpretive frame — *the doctor who treats Holmes as a patient* — and ship every Watson flavor against that lens. Pratchett's voice works because Vimes is *always* the boy who walked the streets; Granny is *always* the witch who refuses to be a story. Watson needs his.

### "The Lampblack thickens" — earning repetition or tic?

Currently a tic in flight. Used twice across the exemplars and gestured at structurally as the audience-facing phrase for Pane-contact. **Verdict:** at the current density it is on the line. By Series 1 close, if it appears on more than 3 cards total, it is a brand-tag, not a phrase. Solution above: cap at 1 per Series.

### Distinctness verdict

LoreVault is *more distinct* from Magic than the cosmology doctrine alone would suggest — primarily because of mode-3, which is structurally locked out of Magic's invented worlds. The doctrine is *less distinct* than Pratchett because Pratchett's voice is *funny*, and LoreVault has banned modern-snark and quip-density in pursuit of MTG-grade flavor. **That is the right trade**, but it leaves a gap: the doctrine has no register for *humor that is serious*. Pratchett's *"PEOPLE'S whole lives do pass before their eyes before they die. The process is called 'living.'"* — that line cannot be written under LoreVault's current voice rules. It is too modern (PEOPLE'S in caps), too direct (defines the joke), too philosophical (the second clause defines the first). **Fix:** carve out a sixth register — *the Cheshire register*, the mode-2 unattributed wonderland-philosophical-aphorism — that legalizes Pratchett-grade philosophical wit *only* in Wonderland and *only* as A9 Wisecrack. This costs nothing and unlocks the brand's most distinctive voice possibility.

## Verdict

**STRENGTHEN-AND-SHIP.**

The deliverables clear the bar of *commissionable to designers and writers as the operating spec*. The doctrine is structurally sound, the 60 Sets are conceptually compelling, the 25-sample voice table passes its own Mosaic Test at 84% recognition, and the five exemplar cards demonstrate the system can produce 10/10 work at the Ultimate tier. V1's completeness review is correct; this work is closer to ship than to rework.

But the bar Roham set was *Pratchett-caliber, MTG-grade, "as poetic and precise and meaningful and heart-hitting"*. Against that bar, ~44% of the sample is at MTG-grade and ~20% of the 60 Set names earn a t-shirt. That is *good* but not *great* in the way Roham asked. Five rewrites will close most of the gap.

Pratchett-caliber over 12 Series is not a question this review can answer; that depends on craft work the doctrine cannot pre-bake. What this review can confirm: the doctrine has built the chessboard that allows for Pratchett-grade play. Whether the moves come is a hiring and editorial-discipline question, not a doctrine question.

The single biggest risk is that "the Lampblack thickens" becomes a tic, the voice doctrine produces ACD pastiche over a long horizon, and the iceberg's unnamed Deeps slowly become vapor. These are post-launch risks. The Council structure (Decision 10) is the right safeguard against all three if it is empowered as written.

## The 5 Highest-Impact Rewrites

### Rewrite 1 — Cosmology Doctrine §1 (MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md)
**Problem:** the doctrine asserts Lampblack is the only physics but never demonstrates Lampblack *acting on* a figure. Currently passive. Reads as residue, not as Narrativium.
**Fix:** add a paragraph at the end of §1 showing one canonical worked example of Lampblack as a *force* — e.g., "*A Sherlock-PRIME, in 1891, dreams of a visor where his cap should be. He does not have a cap in the dream. He cannot remember, on waking, why he reached for one. The Lampblack has thickened. The Pane has been touched.*" This converts Lampblack from noun-residue to verb-force without violating Decision 8 (Laws stay submerged) — because the *cause* of the dream is still off-table, only the *fact* of the dream is shown.

### Rewrite 2 — FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.md §6, Row 1 (and the principle behind it)
**Problem:** the first sample in the 25-sample table is the weakest. *"The watch was wound for an owner with a longer right arm. The body in the river had two left hands."* It is a riddle in Watson's voice, not a Watson deduction. Sets the tone for the whole Universe register *wrong* — and it is the first row anyone will read.
**Fix:** replace with *"The watch was gold-cased and wound clockwise. The body in the river had been left-handed since Afghanistan. — Watson, casebook"* — a clean A1 Deduction that imports the Afghanistan-Lampblack signature into the very first sample, which is the right pedagogical move for a doctrine document.

### Rewrite 3 — SET-ROADMAP.md §2, GH-7 "The Lampblacker" row + supporting doctrine
**Problem:** the Lampblacker is committed to as the original anchor (Decision 11) but has no published Spine. R3 §4's Penny Dreadful lesson was that Vanessa Ives worked *because she had a Spine of her own*. A figure built to do narrative work without a defined desire is a function, not a character.
**Fix:** before any Set commissioning, the Council must publish an internal Spine paragraph for the Lampblacker. Suggest: *"The Lampblacker carries residue between Panes because residue accumulates on whoever does not turn away. They were once a witness to something they refused to look away from; now they are the figure who cannot. Their Spine: bears witness, leaves residue, is recognized but not named, refuses to be the figure others ask them to be."* Add this to the doctrine document, even if shipped narratively only on a Series-2 timeline.

### Rewrite 4 — SET-ROADMAP.md §6 + Council operational policy
**Problem:** the iceberg's 1:2:4 ratio is asserted with three named Echo elements and six named Deep elements per Series, which is 7.5% specificity against the claimed unit count. The audit is illustrative, not enforceable. If the art briefs forget Watson's limp on the Greek hoplite, the iceberg is theory.
**Fix:** the Council's quarterly audit policy must enforce *named Echo and Deep elements at card-level granularity* — at minimum 12 Echo and 24 Deep specifically attributed to specific Moments per Series, with screenshot proof at quarterly review. Otherwise the iceberg becomes vapor over time. Add this as Decision 16 in the doctrine.

### Rewrite 5 — FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.md §1 / §5 — carve out a sixth register
**Problem:** the doctrine has banned modern-snark in pursuit of MTG-grade flavor. That is the right trade for Baker Street, Gothic Horror, Greek Myth, Enchanted Kingdom. **For Wonderland, it leaves the strongest Pratchett-grade voice possibility on the table.** The Cheshire/Hatter register *should* be where philosophical wit lives — Pratchett's "*The process is called 'living*'" cannot be written in any current LoreVault Universe voice.
**Fix:** add §1.6 — *Wonderland mode-2 unattributed-philosophical register*. Permit Pratchett-grade serious-funny in Wonderland *only*, *only* unattributed (to the Garden, the Court, the Estate), *only* as A9 Wisecrack archetype. One per Set maximum. This costs nothing and unlocks the doctrine's biggest Pratchett-bar gap. Suggested doctrinal example: *"There are two ways through the garden. One ends where it began; the other ends where you began. The cards do not say which is which, because the gardener has not been paid this month."*
