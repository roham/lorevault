**Status:** Strategist-locked. Supersedes SET-ROADMAP.md (IP-bucket model). Downstream art-briefs, voice-leads, drop scripts, and cultural-consultation trackers must conform to this document.
**Date:** 2026-04-27

> **Scope.** ~30 Sets across 3 Series × 10 Sets per Series. Each Set is cross-Pane, collecting 20 Moments from figures across multiple cosmological variants. Each figure carries three attributes: **Pane** (cosmological-variant axiom), **Set** (thematic collection), and **Parallel** (rarity register: ARCANA / AETHER / WITNESS / NEON / 1/1). Sets are organized by mythological complex, not by IP-bucket. Iceberg Doctrine 1:2:4 is enforced per Series.

## 1. Architecture — The Pane Model

### What a Pane Is

A **Pane** is a cosmological-variant — a world defined by a single what-if axiom applied to the deep structure of mythology. Panes are not "universes" in the sense of licensed IP buckets. They are divergent answers to the question: *what if the metaphysical substrate had settled differently?* Every figure in the catalog lives in a Pane. Every Set draws figures from multiple Panes to illuminate the same mythological complex from divergent angles.

### What a Set Is

A **Set** is a thematic collection of 20 Moments organized around a cross-pantheon mythological complex: the dying-and-rising pattern, the trickster function, the world-flood, the descent into the underworld. A Set does not belong to any one Pane. Figures from Lattice-Standard and Titans-Held and Old-Ones-Persist can all appear in the same Set because they are all enacting variants of the same mythic structure. The Pane attribute on each card is what distinguishes them.

### Parallel Attribute Registers

Every Moment has a Parallel attribute drawn from the following registers:

- **ARCANA** — deep-mystery variant; pre-classical iconographic mode
- **AETHER** — cosmic-register variant; figures at the boundary of the divine and the elemental
- **WITNESS** — mortal or semi-mortal observer variant; the human inside the myth
- **NEON** — contemporary-register variant; the complex translated into a living cultural surface
- **1/1** — unique Ultimate Moment; one per Set

## 2. The Pane Catalog

## 3. Series-Level Meta-Arcs

### Series 1 — *"The Complexes Beneath the Names"* (Months 1–12)

**One-line thesis:** *The same pattern — the dying god, the hunt, the long night — runs across all five Panes; attentive collectors will notice it before it is named.*

Series 1 establishes the five launch Panes and the five committed Sets. Three Sets ship in the first half of the year (Long Night, Wild Hunt, Old Ones Persist — already written). Two new Sets (Dying and the Rising, Trickster Roads) complete the Series in the second half. The overarching movement is from *winter and darkness* (Long Night, Wild Hunt) through *elder persistence* (Old Ones) into *death-and-return* (Dying and the Rising) and *boundary-crossing* (Trickster Roads). By Month 12, the five mythological complexes have been demonstrated across five Panes; collectors are mapping the pattern — the same figure, differently named, in every Pane.

Cultural-consultation pre-loads required in Series 1 (see Section 6).

### Series 2 — *"The Deep Axioms"* (Months 13–24)

**One-line thesis:** *Three new Panes each represent a different answer to the question of how the cosmos is structured — mystery-initiatory, dualist, solar-sacrificial — and their figures change the reading of everything already shipped.*

Series 2 introduces the Orphic-Gold, Manichaean-Bleeding-World, and Aztec-Sun-Held Panes, expanding the catalog to 8 Panes. The five new Sets in Series 2 are organized around structural relationships between the divine and the material: the flood, the craftsman, the sleeping king, the mystery initiate, the world-building combat. By Month 24, the expanded Pane set has introduced three incompatible cosmological answers to the same questions; collectors are beginning to understand that the Pane attribute is not flavor but doctrine.

### Series 3 — *"The Full Catalog, The Shared Floor"* (Months 25–36)

**One-line thesis:** *With 20 Panes active, the Sets in Series 3 are the places where all of them touch the same floor — descent, celestial combat, sacred marriage, apocalypse — and the community can finally see the complete shape.*

Series 3 expands to the full 20-Pane catalog and completes the roadmap with four Sets organized around the deepest cross-cultural structures. These Sets are not about a single pantheon or a single mythology; they are about the pattern that every mythology in the catalog reaches toward. By Month 36, the full Pane census is visible, and the shape of the mythological substrate — the "floor beneath all the floors" — has been demonstrated without being explained.

*(Already written — reference only)*

#### S1-4 | THE DYING AND THE RISING

Cross-pantheon dying-god complex across all five launch Panes. The Titans-Held Pane presents a world where no dying-and-rising cycle has been authorized by the new Olympians; the cycle runs underground, in mystery traditions, as a forbidden truth. The OP Pane shows the pattern as the oldest operating law. The SR Pane's winter-lord variant (the year-king who dies at midwinter) provides the seasonal register.

**Pane spread:** LS, TH, OP, SR, WH

#### S1-5 | THE TRICKSTER ROADS

Cross-pantheon trickster complex across all five launch Panes. Figures: Loki (Norse), Hermes/Mercury (Greek/Roman), Anansi (Akan/West African diaspora), Eshu-Elegba (Yoruba), Maui (Polynesian), Sun Wukong (Chinese), Hanuman (Hindu), Coyote (North American Indigenous). Each trickster is shown on a road — in transit, between registers, neither fully in nor out of any category.

The LS Pane's 1880s–1920s register allows Hermes as a telegraph messenger and Anansi as a traveling salesman, without reducing them; the divine function rides the contemporary surface. The WH Pane's eternal-motion register suits the trickster's restlessness. Loki in the TH Pane — where the Titanomachy was reversed and Cronus holds — is a figure with no clear lord, which is the most precisely Loki situation possible.

**Pane spread:** LS, TH, OP, SR, WH
**Moments:** 20 (4 per Pane; Pane spread selects most resonant figures for each Pane's axiom)
**Notes:** Anansi and Eshu-Elegba require cultural pre-load before West African diaspora figures are finalized; see Section 6. Coyote (North American Indigenous) requires tribal-nation consultation before North American trickster figures are included in any card; see Section 6. Sun Wukong is PD via *Journey to the West* (Ming Dynasty; no active IP). Hanuman is PD via Ramayana tradition.

#### S2-3 | THE SLEEPING KINGS

### 4.3 Series 3 — 10 Sets

Series 3 activates all 12 remaining Panes, bringing the full catalog to 20 Panes. Sets draw from the widest Pane spreads yet — some Sets draw from 10 or more Panes — because by Month 36 the full cosmological vocabulary is available. The four committed Series 3 Sets address the deepest mythological structures; six additional Sets complete the year.

---

#### S3-1 | THE DESCENT

Underworld journeys across the full Pane catalog. Inanna's descent (Sumerian), Orpheus (Greek), Izanagi pursuing Izanami (Japanese), Odysseus in Book XI (Greek), Gilgamesh's search after Enkidu's death, Psyche's descent for Persephone's box, Heracles' katabasis. The PI Pane — Persephone and Inanna both descended simultaneously and neither returned — defines the extreme register of this Set. Every figure encounters the fact that the underworld has rules and the living do not understand them until they are already broken.

**Pane spread:** LS, TH, OP, OG, MB, AS, PI, RA, GU (9 Panes)
**Moments:** 20
**Notes:** In the GU Pane, Enkidu did not die; Gilgamesh's descent-driven-by-grief did not occur; the GU-Pane Moment in this Set is a Gilgamesh who has heard of the descent from others and does not understand it — the WITNESS Parallel register.

#### S3-2 | THE SKY-BATTLE

Thunder-gods defeating cosmic monsters — the combat cosmogony complex across the full catalog. Zeus/Typhon (Greek), Marduk/Tiamat (Babylonian), Indra/Vritra (Vedic), Thor/Jormungandr (Norse), Baal/Yam (Ugaritic), Tlaloc's storm-wars (Mesoamerican). Unlike S2-6 (Dragon-Slayer, a ground-level hero complex), the Sky-Battle is a *cosmological* event — the outcome determines the structure of the world. In the TH Pane, the sky-battle was lost by the Olympians; Zeus/Typhon ended differently. In the RA Pane, Thor/Jormungandr happened at Ragnarok, which already occurred; the Moment is archaeological.

**Pane spread:** LS, TH, OP, OG, MB, AS, YK, RA, SD (9 Panes)
**Moments:** 20
**Notes:** YK Pane's Norse/Egyptian braid means the sky-battle in YK blends Marduk's cosmological role with Thor's; art-brief must establish a visual grammar for YK that distinguishes it from both pure-Norse and pure-Egyptian figures.

#### S3-3 | THE SACRED MARRIAGE

Divine couples and their unions as cosmological events: Zeus/Hera (Greek), Osiris/Isis (Egyptian), Shiva/Parvati (Hindu), El/Asherah (Canaanite), Enlil/Ninlil (Sumerian), the hieros gamos pattern in agricultural religion generally, Freyr/Gerdr (Norse, as sacred-marriage archetype). This Set activates the EA Pane fully for the first time. In the PI Pane, the sacred marriage is suspended — Persephone is still in the underworld and will not return; the marriage waits. In the SD Pane, the sacred marriage is danced continuously; Shiva and Parvati are in perpetual union-and-dissolution.

**Pane spread:** LS, TH, OP, OG, MB, EA, YK, PI, SD (9 Panes)
**Moments:** 20
**Notes:** EA Pane's first full activation. All figures PD through ancient textual record. El/Asherah material uses Ugaritic textual sources (Ras Shamra tablets, 14th c. BCE); PD by any measure.

#### S3-4 | THE APOCALYPSE OF X

End-times figures from multiple traditions: Ragnarok (Norse), Frashokereti (Zoroastrian cosmic renovation), Kalki (Hindu, the tenth avatar at the end of the Kali Yuga), the Last Judgment figure-complex (via PD pre-1923 literary sources only — Dante, Milton, the Revelation visual tradition in medieval iconography), Pralaya (Hindu dissolution), the Aztec fifth-sun ending-scenario, the Buddhist world-cycle ending. Each tradition's apocalypse has a different *structure*: Ragnarok is inevitable and known; Frashokereti is a renovation, not an ending; Kalki is a new beginning's violent birth. The Set's intellectual project is the comparison.

In the RA Pane, the apocalypse is *over*; these figures are survivors or fossils. In the MB Pane, the apocalypse is *in progress*. In the TH Pane, Ragnarok cannot happen in its canonical form — the preconditions (Baldur's death, Loki's punishment) never obtained because the Olympians never held sway; the Moment is a Ragnarok that approaches from a different direction.

**Pane spread:** LS, TH, OG, MB, AS, YK, CM, RA, BD, AF (10 Panes)
**Moments:** 20
**Notes:** CM Pane's Apocalypse-of-X Moment must use PD sources only (Revelation via its pre-1923 literary treatment, not any contemporary theological reading). BD Pane's Buddhist apocalypse is drawn from canonical Pali texts and Mahayana sources — PD through ancient record. Kalki avatar material requires tonal review per R3 §6 to avoid any register that reads as commentary on living religious practice.

#### S3-5 | THE FIRST LANGUAGES

Divine language-giving, naming, and the word as creative act: the Egyptian Thoth as scribe and word-giver, the Hebrew/Canaanite concept of divine speech as creation, Brahma's Sanskrit, the Sumerian Me as divine words-as-laws, the Norse runes as Odin's self-sacrifice-gained gift, the Yoruba Ifa divination as divine language. The AF Pane is this Set's deepest register — the African-First axiom holds that the generative linguistic act originated in the African cosmological traditions; the other figures' language-gifts are derivatives.

**Pane spread:** LS, OP, OG, EA, YK, AF, HS (7 Panes)
**Moments:** 20
**Notes:** HS Pane (Holmes-as-Sadhu): the detective's ratiocination is shown as a form of divine language in this Pane — deduction as mantra. AF Pane requires cultural pre-load at its most rigorous; see Section 6. All other figures PD.

#### S3-6 | THE UNDERWORLD CARTOGRAPHERS

Figures who mapped, described, or administered the underworld: Hades/Pluto (Greek), Osiris as judge of the dead (Egyptian), Yama (Vedic/Buddhist), Emma-O (Japanese Buddhist), Ereshkigal (Sumerian), Mictlantecuhtli (Aztec), the Tibetan Bardo figures. Unlike S3-1 (The Descent, about *travelers* through the underworld), this Set is about the *infrastructure* of the underworld — the judges, the cartographers, the record-keepers. In the GP Pane, these figures work in a world where the gates never closed; the administration is overwhelmed and improvising. In the OG Pane, the underworld is also an initiatory space; the cartographers are initiatory guides.

**Pane spread:** LS, TH, OG, AS, GP, PI, YK (7 Panes)
**Moments:** 20
**Notes:** Tibetan Bardo figures require cultural pre-load; see Section 6. AS Pane underworld figures (Mictlantecuhtli and the nine levels of Mictlan) require same Mesoamerican cultural consultation as S1-4, S2-6.

#### S3-7 | THE HEALER GODS

Divine healing, medicine, and restoration: Asclepius (Greek), Dhanvantari (Hindu, churning-of-the-ocean physician), Thoth as physician of Horus (Egyptian), Airmed and the Tuatha Dé Danann healing tradition (Irish), Brigid's healing well (Irish), Bao Gu (Chinese), the Yoruba healing orisha Osain. In the RA Pane, healing is the primary divine function of the post-Ragnarok world — the battle is over; now comes the restoration. In the MB Pane, the Healer Gods are the front line of resistance; light as therapeutic. In the OG Pane, healing is initiatory: the body is healed because the soul's itinerary requires a vessel.

**Pane spread:** LS, TH, OG, MB, AF, RA, GP (7 Panes)
**Moments:** 20
**Notes:** Osain (Yoruba) requires cultural pre-load via same West African consultation track. Airmed and Brigid figures are PD via Irish literary tradition (medieval manuscripts, PD by centuries).

#### S3-8 | THE ANIMAL LORDS

Master-of-animals figures and their divine relationships with the non-human: Artemis as Lady of the Beasts (Greek), Cernunnos as animal lord (Celtic), Pashupati (Indus Valley, pre-Vedic, carried into the PV Pane), the Siberian Bear-Lord shamanic complex, the Inuit Sedna as mistress of sea animals, Anansi's relationship with the animal kingdom (Akan). The WH Pane's eternal-motion register makes every figure in this Set a variant of the Hunt — the question of whether the Animal Lord controls the animals or serves them is the WH Pane's version of the complex.

**Pane spread:** LS, OP, PV, AF, WH, YK (6 Panes)
**Moments:** 20
**Notes:** Sedna (Inuit) requires Indigenous consultation; see Section 6. PV Pane's Pashupati material uses Indus Valley seal iconography (2600–1900 BCE); no text survives, so Moments in PV Pane for this Set are necessarily visual/inferential — art-brief must note the absence of text and how that affects flavor-text voice.

#### S3-9 | THE CHILDREN OF THE GODS

Second-generation divine figures and the specific heroism of being born into cosmological consequence: Hercules/Heracles (Greek), Rama (Hindu), Horus (Egyptian), Baldr (Norse), Cú Chulainn (Irish), the Twins of the Popol Vuh (Mayan). Each is a figure who did not choose their divine inheritance but whose existence becomes the point at which the cosmological structure is tested. In the GU Pane, Gilgamesh's semi-divine status is the framing — he is 2/3 divine and the fractions matter. In the TH Pane, Heracles operates in a world where the Olympians are not the ruling power; his labors were set by a different authority structure.

**Pane spread:** LS, TH, OG, MB, AS, YK, GU (7 Panes)
**Moments:** 20
**Notes:** Popol Vuh Twin Hunters require Mayan cultural consultation (same track as S2-7). Rama figure in the SD Pane requires tonal review; the SD Pane's ongoing-dance axiom interacts with Rama's dharmic register in ways that require theological consultation; see Section 6.

#### S3-10 | THE LONG MEMORY

Figures associated with divine memory, record-keeping, and the preservation of cosmological history across catastrophe: Thoth as divine scribe, Saraswati as guardian of knowledge (Hindu), the Muses collectively (Greek), Odin's sacrifice for the runes as a memory-technology, the Mesopotamian antediluvian sages (Apkallu), the Irish filid as keepers of genealogy and myth, Anansi's preservation of stories (Akan). This Set is the Series 3 close — the final Set of the full roadmap — and its implicit argument is that the Pane model itself is an act of memory: all 20 Panes are held simultaneously, none suppressed, none made canonical over another.

In the RA Pane, the Long Memory is what survives Ragnarok: not power, but record. In the MB Pane, the record-keepers are the most threatened figures; the darkness wants the memory destroyed. In the AF Pane, the Long Memory is the original act — before any other tradition, the African cosmological record was kept.

**Pane spread:** LS, TH, OP, OG, MB, AF, YK, RA, EA (9 Panes)
**Moments:** 20
**Notes:** This Set should coordinate with the cultural pre-load teams for West African and Canaanite/Levantine traditions, both of which are active by Series 3. Saraswati Moments require tonal review for the SD Pane (same ongoing-dance register concern as S3-9 Rama). All other figures PD.

## 7. PD-Cliff Notes

All figures in the LoreVault Pane catalog are drawn from sources in the public domain. The following notes flag proximity concerns and confirm PD status for the figures most likely to require verification.

**Pre-1923 textual sources (US PD, unqualified):** Tammuz (Sumerian clay tablets), Adonis (Ovid), Attis (Catullus, Pausanias), Osiris (Egyptian funerary texts), Dionysus (Euripides, Hesiod), Baldur (Prose Edda, ca. 1220 CE), Inanna (Sumerian), Quetzalcoatl (Aztec codices), Loki (Prose Edda), Hermes (Hesiod, Homeric Hymns), Maui (oral tradition compiled in PD ethnographic record), Sun Wukong (*Journey to the West*, Ming Dynasty), Hanuman (Ramayana), Charlemagne (medieval chronicle), Arthur (Malory 1485), Prometheus (Aeschylus, Hesiod).

**Living religious traditions (PD as mythological figures; tonal review required):** Shiva, Parvati, Vishvakarman, Dhanvantari, Saraswati, Rama, Kalki, Brahma, Yama, Osain, Ogun, Eshu-Elegba, the Yoruba orishas generally. These figures are PD as ancient mythological material; they are also active objects of living religious practice. The PD status covers the right to use; the tonal-review requirement covers the *way* of use.

**Copyright-proximity flags:**
- **Sun Wukong**: 2014 animated features and 2023–2025 game properties are under active copyright; LoreVault Moments must draw from the Ming Dynasty literary source, not from any contemporary adaptation's visual design.
- **Maui**: the Disney *Moana* version (2016) is under active copyright; LoreVault's Polynesian Maui draws from pre-20th-century ethnographic compilation, not from the Disney character design or personality register. Art-brief must specify source material explicitly.
- **Anansi**: the Neil Gaiman *American Gods* (2001) and *Anansi Boys* (2005) versions are under active copyright; LoreVault's Anansi draws from oral tradition and the Rattray/Christaller 19th-century ethnographic compilations only.
- **St. George**: public domain via hagiographic tradition; no contemporary media adaptation is close enough to the ancient figure to create confusion, but art-brief should specify early iconographic sources (13th–15th century panel painting tradition).

## Appendix — One-Page Summary for Downstream Agents

LoreVault V2 ships 30 named Sets across 34 drop months (36 calendar months including two production-gap pairs). The organizing principle is the **Pane** — a cosmological variant defined by a single what-if axiom — not the IP-bucket. Every Set is cross-Pane, collecting figures from multiple cosmological variants who are all enacting the same mythological complex (dying-and-rising, trickster, descent, etc.). Series 1 launches 5 Panes and 10 Sets. Series 2 adds 3 Panes and 10 Sets. Series 3 adds 12 Panes and 10 Sets, reaching a full catalog of 20 Panes.

Each figure carries three attributes: Pane (cosmological variant), Set (mythological complex), and Parallel (rarity register: ARCANA / AETHER / WITNESS / NEON / 1/1). Cultural pre-loads are required before eight consultation tracks are satisfied; the pre-load schedule is production-gate-locked against the relevant Set drop months. All figures are drawn from PD sources; proximity flags for Sun Wukong (contemporary game IP), Maui (Disney), Anansi (Gaiman), and others are noted in Section 7. Total Moments: 600. Total catalog with Parallels: approximately 3,000 distinct art pieces across three years.

*— LoreVault Canon Council, Set Roadmap V2, 2026-04-27*
