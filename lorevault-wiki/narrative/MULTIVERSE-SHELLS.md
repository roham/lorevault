# LoreVault Multiverse Shell System — Operational Spec

**Status:** Operating spec. Conforms to `/narrative/MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md`. Downstream art briefs, card metadata, and Set leads must obey the per-character Lampblack table and the Shell × Character matrix below.
**Date:** 2026-04-24
**Companion:** `MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md` (Decisions 1–15) and `/economy/GAME-ECONOMY-DESIGN-V1.md` (5 Universes / 4 Tiers / 5 Parallels).

## 1. Shell vs Parallel — The Orthogonal Axes, Made Concrete

Per Decision 3, **Shell** and **Parallel** are orthogonal. Shell answers *who is this figure right now* — which Pane of the Lattice we are standing in. Parallel answers *how the painting reads* — the artistic register. Shell is the figure's chassis; Parallel is the museum lighting. A Moment's metadata carries both, plus Universe, Set, Tier, Serial.

**Worked combinations — Sherlock Holmes:**

- **Sherlock-CYBER × ARCANA.** Cyberpunk Holmes — visor where the deerstalker was, inhaler where the pipe was — rendered with an alchemical sigil mandala behind his head. Reads: *the deduction-engine is a cosmic constant; the chassis is just this century's housing.* Strong combo; ARCANA elevates the chassis to ritual rather than tech-fetish.
- **Sherlock-CYBER × WITNESS.** Same cyberpunk chassis, rendered as documentary-cinema photoreal — Deakins register, as if a 2050 journalist photographed him at a crime scene. Reads: *this Holmes lives in the viewer's world; the chase is mundane; the deduction is what's strange.* Strong combo, opposite emotional register from ARCANA.
- **Sherlock-PRIME × NEON.** Victorian Holmes — cap, pipe, gaslight — in synthwave palette. Magenta fog, cyan lamplight, face lit as if from a CRT. Legal but rare; the tension between canonical figure and forward-dreaming paint must be the point. Reserved for Legendary+.
- **Sherlock-SAINT × AETHER.** Holmes-as-concept, patron of investigators, against nebulae and god-rays. Native combo — SAINT and AETHER amplify each other.

**The collapsing-redundancy rule.** A (Shell × Parallel) is redundant when the Parallel re-says the Shell. Two banned cases for Series 1:

1. **CYBER × NEON = banned.** NEON's signature *is* modern/cyber relocation. Stacking on CYBER doubles the word.
2. **AETHER × AETHER = banned.** AETHER-shell figures need a Parallel that *contrasts* — WITNESS, ARCANA, or 1/1. Never the AETHER Parallel.

All other combinations (40 cells minus the two banned, minus the four impossible Wonderland-HOLLOW per Decision 13) are legal. Set leads choose the Parallel that contrasts or complicates the Shell, never doubles it.

**Reasoning by column:**

**PRIME** — mandatory baseline for every figure (Decision 4 + §3 doctrine). No "No" cells.

**CYBER** fits figures whose Spine is infrastructural, scientific, or self-engineered — Sherlock (cognition), Frankenstein (creation), Hyde (chemical self-alteration), Phantom (acoustic/architectural), Dracula (count-as-biotech-aristocrat), Rumpelstiltskin (transactional engineering), Athena (strategy as code), Cheshire (probability cat). Fails on Alice (Wonderland already breaks tech-logic — redundant per §1), the Hound (the dog is the dog), Watson (his power is human witness).

**MODERN** fails when the figure's threat-register doesn't translate to 2026. The Hound becomes mascot. Prometheus becomes podcast guest. Greek gods mostly lose Spine in MODERN — Athena (recasts as strategist) is the exception. Hyde, Phantom, Jekyll translate cleanly because they were already urban.

**AETHER** fits figures whose Spine can support cosmic scale. Greek figures native. Dracula scales (count-of-nights = cosmic ledger). Sherlock can be raised to Reason-Itself but Legendary+ only. Watson can't be raised — his power is being un-cosmic. The Hatter's power is local nonsense; AETHER kills him.

**HOLLOW** is Gothic Horror's home. Native to Frankenstein, Dracula, Phantom, Hyde. Banned from Wonderland (Decision 13). Banned from Watson (erodes his witness register). Strong on Wolf/Red Riding Hood — the apocalypse is the wolf's element. Greek figures mostly Okay; Medusa-as-petrified-survivor works strongly.

**MIRROR** is identity-inversion. Strong on figures whose Spine survives inversion because it isn't gender-locked — Sherlock, Watson, Hatter, Wolf, Hyde, Snow White, Phantom. Banned on Greek figures (Decision 12, rare exception only). Banned on Dracula (gender architecture foundational). Banned on the Hound. Banned on Zeus (patriarchal-divine Spine; inversion removes Lampblack).

**DREAM** is Wonderland's home — Alice, Hatter, Cheshire native. Banned from Sherlock (his Spine refuses dream-logic). Banned from Watson (the awake one). Banned from the Hound (he is the *thing dreamed of*, not the dreamer). Strong on Phantom, Hyde.

**SAINT** is figures who can be venerated as concept. Banned on Watson (sainthood requires solitude; his Spine is partnership). Banned on Hyde (his Spine *is* the failure of sainthood). Banned on Moriarty (must remain antagonist). Strong on Greek gods (Greek figures invented SAINT).

The matrix is the operating constraint. Set leads who want to ship a "No" cell write a Council justification doc per Decision 4.

## 3. The Lampblack Mechanics — Per-Character Cross-Shell Continuity

Per Law Two (Lampblack) and Decision 1 (Lampblack is the Lattice's only physics), the figure's recognizable residue must survive every shell translation. The Spine is the *content* preserved; the Lampblack is the *visible mark* that signals preservation. A single Lampblack residue must be expressible in at least two media (silhouette/prop, line/voice, scar/symbol).

Below is the locked Lampblack list for the 20 starter figures. These are binding on art briefs and voice work for Series 1–6.

- **Sherlock Holmes.** The deerstalker silhouette (cap, visor, hood, halo of brimmed shadow — whatever century it is). The pipe (briar, inhaler, vape, smoke-ring artifact). The phrase *"You have been in—"* opening any deduction. The fingertips touched at the temple.
- **Watson.** The Afghanistan limp (left leg, weight shifted; in CYBER it's a servo hitch; in SAINT it's a stigmata). The notebook (paper, glass, datalog — always being written *in*). His voice opening every story in third-person about Holmes.
- **Moriarty.** The chessboard (in CYBER it's a strategy lattice; in MODERN it's a market terminal). The phrase *"We are bound, you and I."* The asymmetric oscillation of the head — a tic from the source.
- **Irene Adler.** The photograph (the one she will not surrender; in CYBER it's an encrypted file; in SAINT it's a relic). The masculine garment worn over feminine (a frock-coat, a tactical jacket, a bishop's stole). Her name spoken once and remembered forever.
- **The Hound (Baskerville).** Phosphorescent jaw (gas, paint, bioluminescence, halo). The howl as *off-frame sound* — Lampblack lives in audio cues for this figure. The single paw-print larger than expected.
- **Snow White.** The apple (red, half-bitten, geometric, glowing — never absent). Skin-blood-ebony tricolor (the skin white as snow, lips red as blood, hair black as ebony — color triad survives every shell). The glass coffin or its analog (vitrine, cryotube, reliquary).
- **Evil Queen.** The mirror (any reflective surface that talks back — handheld, room-scale, screen). The crown, slightly crooked. The phrase *"who is the fairest"* in any register.
- **Red Riding Hood.** The red garment (cloak, hoodie, signal flare — red on red on red). The basket (or its load-bearing analog: a bag, a payload, a delivery). The grandmother's house as destination.
- **Rumpelstiltskin.** The wheel (spinning wheel, gear, centrifuge, hard-drive platter). The unspoken name as the Lampblack itself — every shell foregrounds *he has a name no one says*. The straw-to-gold transmutation gesture.
- **Alice.** The blue ribbon (hair, wrist, neck, data-cable — always blue, always tied). The size-discordance moment (a too-big door, a too-small chair). The pinafore silhouette (apron, lab coat, surgical gown — the white over the blue).
- **Cheshire Cat.** The grin that arrives before the body and stays after. The stripe (purple-pink, neon, scar-tissue). The tail as punctuation in the composition.
- **Mad Hatter.** The hat with the price-tag still on it (10/6, $10.60, a barcode). Tea (cup, vial, IV bag) at improbable angle. The clock stuck at six.
- **Queen of Hearts.** The heart (suit-card, organ, pulse trace, neon sign). The phrase *"Off with—"* always cut off mid-line. The painted rose (red-over-white, blood-over-snow).
- **Dracula.** The count of nights (a tally — wall-scratches, calendar marks, journal entries; in CYBER it's a counter at the corner of the frame). The shadow that doesn't follow the body's geometry. The collar-line at the throat.
- **Frankenstein's Monster.** The seam (forehead, neck, wrist — at least one stitch visible in every shell). The asymmetric eye-line (one eye older than the other). The hands disproportionately large.
- **Jekyll / Hyde.** The cabinet (medicine cabinet, locker, server cage — where the second self is kept). The gloved hand vs ungloved hand split-frame. The mirror that shows the other.
- **Phantom of the Opera.** The mask (half-face — left side covered, right side visible, every shell). The chandelier (or its analog: a hanging weight that will fall). Music-staff fragments in the negative space.
- **Prometheus.** The chain (iron, neural, contractual). The fire (literal, electrical, informational — the gift in his hand). The liver-eagle silhouette in the background or on the body.
- **Medusa.** The serpents (hair, cables, tendrils, fiber). The petrified figure in the foreground or at her feet (a witness turned to stone; in CYBER it's a frozen avatar). The shield-as-mirror.
- **Zeus.** The thunderbolt silhouette (held, holstered, scar-shaped). The eagle off-frame. The beard that storms.
- **Athena.** The owl (perched, in flight, holographic). The aegis (shield, badge, security clearance). Helmet at the temple — never on the head, always set down.

These 20 Lampblack signatures are the cross-shell continuity contract. A card that drops one without Council approval fails the Mosaic Test.

## 4. Shell-Crossing Events — The Lattice Lets Two Panes Touch

Per Decision 5, two shells of the same figure never appear together on a card in Series 1–4. Per Law Three, Panes touch but do not merge. Reconciling operationally: shell-crossing is **rare, ritualized, reserved**, at three event types only:

1. **The Vertigo Moment — Ultimate tier only.** Once per Series, one Ultimate per Universe may depict shell-crossing as *vertigo*: the figure perceives themselves in another shell as a flicker, doubled silhouette, mismatched reflection. Two shells are never both fully present — one is the figure, one is residue. Lampblack carries the contact. *Example: Sherlock-PRIME at Reichenbach glimpses Sherlock-CYBER as the spray turns to digital noise — for half a second the deerstalker is a visor.* The other shell is rendered as Lampblack-only.

2. **The Dream Hand-Off — DREAM shell exclusive.** A DREAM-shell figure may dream of themselves in another shell; the dreamed-of figure is half-erased, sketched, never frontal. Legendary+ only. Composition rule: dreamed-of shell occupies upper-third, never the foreground.

3. **The Saint's Glimpse — SAINT shell exclusive.** SAINT cards may include silhouettes of the figure-in-other-shells as the saint's iconographic attributes — a Sherlock-SAINT halo with both deerstalker and visor among its seven symbols. Both as *symbols*, neither as live figure. SAINT Ultimate only. Maximum two shell-silhouettes per saint.

**The in-fiction mechanism, named.** Per Decision 15, no new metaphysical proper nouns. The audience-facing phrase is *"the Lampblack thickens"* — used in flavor text. The mechanism is never explained; the audience is invited to infer that Lampblack accumulates at certain moments (death, dream, veneration) and accumulation permits brief Pane-contact.

**Permanently banned per Decision 5 and Decision 1:**
- Two shells of a figure both fully present, both interacting as live agents.
- Any in-fiction *travel* between shells. Figures do not move between Panes; they leave Lampblack on each other. Load-bearing distinction.
- Any flavor-text explanation of shell-crossing metaphysics. Per Decision 8, Laws stay submerged; shell-crossing is felt, never explained.

Vertigo, Dream Hand-Off, and Saint's Glimpse are the only sanctioned mechanisms. New mechanisms require Council approval (Decision 4).

## 5. The Elemental Question — A Third Axis, Decided

**Decision: Elemental is a third axis, standalone. Six classes. Mapped to figures, not shells or universes.**

Reasoning. Shells encode *who the figure is right now*; Universes encode *which Pane-cluster they belong to*; Parallels encode *how the painting reads*. None carries the *temperamental signature* that MTG's color pie captures — the question of "what does this figure value, and what will they sacrifice to keep it?" Per R5 §7 Rosewater Rule 5, the color pie is a values-and-philosophy register, not aesthetic. Mapping elemental to shell would conflate identity with temperament: Sherlock-PRIME and Sherlock-CYBER share elemental class; temperament does not change with chassis. Mapping to universe would forfeit cross-Universe figure-comparison utility.

**The six elemental classes, locked:**

1. **REASON.** Interrogates. Holds methodology, evidence, deduction; sacrifices warmth and certainty about non-falsifiable things. *MTG: Blue/White.* Figures: Sherlock, Athena, Watson, Mycroft.
2. **WONDER.** Says *what if*. Holds openness and the legitimate question; sacrifices stability. *MTG: Blue/Green.* Figures: Alice, Cheshire, Hatter (Wonder-corrupted).
3. **SHADOW.** Survives by hiding what it is. Holds secrecy, transformation, the second self; sacrifices being-known. *MTG: Black.* Figures: Dracula, Hyde, Moriarty, Phantom, Irene Adler.
4. **RAPTURE.** Converts feeling into kinetic act. Holds ecstasy, vengeance, the broken seal; sacrifices durability. *MTG: Red.* Figures: Queen of Hearts, the Hound, Wolf, Hyde-as-rage.
5. **FATE.** Submits to a longer time. Holds inheritance and the closing ledger; sacrifices agency-in-the-moment. *MTG: White/Black.* Figures: Snow White, Persephone, Prometheus, Frankenstein's creature, Medusa, Red Riding Hood.
6. **WILD.** Refuses category — neither protector nor antagonist, outside the moral architecture. *MTG: colorless.* Figures: Cheshire (alternate read), Rumpelstiltskin. Rarest class; use sparingly.

**Interactions.** *Mechanic:* Battle/Trivia (economy §9) uses elemental as the matchup axis. REASON beats RAPTURE; RAPTURE beats FATE; FATE beats WONDER; WONDER beats SHADOW; SHADOW beats REASON; WILD is meta-neutral, doubled by Parallel/Ultimate combos. *Flavor:* elemental gates voice register — REASON Yawgmoth-adjacent, WONDER Niv-Mizzet-adjacent, SHADOW Sorin-adjacent, RAPTURE imperative/exclamatory, FATE ordinal-time and ellipsis-openings, WILD fragmentary. *Visual:* a single colored serif at the card's lower-right — REASON cyan, WONDER violet, SHADOW black, RAPTURE crimson, FATE gold, WILD silver. The serif is the only visual element of elemental; it does not bleed into art treatment (Parallel's job).

Elemental ships from Day 1, name-locked, no new classes for Series 1–6.

## 6. Worked Examples — Six Figures, Full Synthesis

Each example shows the figure's full shell distribution + Lampblack + elemental class + one card concept showing the shell × parallel × elemental synthesis at work.

### Sherlock Holmes
**Shells:** Strong in PRIME, CYBER, MODERN, MIRROR, SAINT. Okay in AETHER, HOLLOW. No in DREAM.
**Lampblack:** Deerstalker silhouette + pipe + temple-fingertip + *"You have been in—"* opener.
**Elemental:** REASON.
**Card concept:** *Sherlock-CYBER × ARCANA × REASON.* "The Synth-Reichenbach," Legendary in BS-4. CYBER Sherlock at a server-stack waterfall, visor where the cap was, ARCANA mandala behind his head. Cyan REASON serif. Flavor (Yawgmoth-adjacent): *"It is not the fall that is interesting. It is the arithmetic of the fall."*

### Alice
**Shells:** Strong in PRIME, MODERN, MIRROR, DREAM, SAINT. Okay in AETHER. No in CYBER (Wonderland already breaks tech-logic), HOLLOW (Decision 13).
**Lampblack:** Blue ribbon + size-discordance moment + pinafore silhouette.
**Elemental:** WONDER.
**Card concept:** *Alice-DREAM × ARCANA × WONDER.* "The Threshold Refused," Ultimate Wonderland Set. DREAM Alice with the world bending into a sigil-mandala built from oversized keys and undersized doors; blue ribbon the only solid line. Violet WONDER serif. Flavor (Reversal Joke): *"I came for a rabbit. I found a question. I refused to answer."*

### Dracula
**Shells:** Strong in every shell except MIRROR (gender architecture foundational).
**Lampblack:** Count-of-nights tally + non-Euclidean shadow + collar-line at throat.
**Elemental:** SHADOW.
**Card concept:** *Dracula-HOLLOW × WITNESS × SHADOW.* "The Last Census," Legendary Gothic Horror Set 2. HOLLOW Dracula in ruined city, tally on a collapsed clocktower wall. WITNESS photoreal as if drone-captured at dusk; collar-line catches the only warm light. Black SHADOW serif. Flavor (Sorin-adjacent): *"I have outlived the candle. I have outlived the man who lit it. There is no one left to count me."*

### Medusa
**Shells:** Strong in PRIME, AETHER, HOLLOW, MIRROR, DREAM, SAINT. Okay in CYBER, MODERN.
**Lampblack:** Serpents + petrified witness + shield-as-mirror.
**Elemental:** FATE.
**Card concept:** *Medusa-AETHER × ARCANA × FATE.* "The Patron of Returned Looks," Ultimate Greek Myth. Cosmic-scale Medusa, nebulae-serpents in her hair, the shield-mirror reflecting the viewer's own face turned to stone. ARCANA mandala — the medieval-grimoire sigil for the petrifying gaze. Gold FATE serif. Flavor (bronze-tongue, mode-3): *"…and they who sought her face found their own, returned in granite. So Hesiod, lost ninth hymn, fragment 412."*

### Snow White
**Shells:** Strong in PRIME, MODERN, MIRROR, DREAM, SAINT. Okay in CYBER, AETHER, HOLLOW.
**Lampblack:** Apple (always half-bitten) + skin-blood-ebony tricolor + glass coffin or analog.
**Elemental:** FATE.
**Card concept:** *Snow White-MIRROR × NEON × FATE.* "Snow Black, in the Glass Locker," Rare in an Enchanted Kingdom MIRROR Set. Gender-and-role inverted — a young man, ebony skin, snow-white hair, blood-red lips, half-bitten apple in hand. NEON synthwave palette; coffin is now a hospital cryotube. Gold FATE serif. Flavor (bone-rhyme, marginalia): *"Skin of dusk and lip of fire / The glass holds what the heart held higher."*

### Prometheus
**Shells:** Strong in PRIME, CYBER, AETHER, HOLLOW, SAINT. Okay in MODERN, MIRROR, DREAM.
**Lampblack:** Chain + fire (gift in hand) + liver-eagle silhouette.
**Elemental:** FATE.
**Card concept:** *Prometheus-CYBER × WITNESS × FATE.* "The Patch That Couldn't Be Reverted," Legendary. CYBER Prometheus chained to a server rack — chain literal and data-binding; the fire is a glowing data-payload; the liver-eagle a recurrent process loop in the rack's cooling-light pattern. WITNESS as if leaked black-site footage. Gold FATE serif. Flavor (bronze-tongue, log-fragment): *"Day 4,212. The fire is still loose. They cannot put it back. The eagle returns at the same hour."*

## Appendix — Operating Constraints for Set Leads

Per Decisions 2, 4, 6 and the Mosaic Test, every Set lead must, before art is briefed:

1. List figures on the docket.
2. Choose each figure's Shell from §2's matrix. "No" cells require Council justification.
3. Choose each Moment's Parallel; verify it is not redundant per §1.
4. Confirm the figure's Lampblack from §3 will be in the composition.
5. Tag elemental class per §5.
6. Verify any shell-crossing obeys §4 (Vertigo / Dream Hand-Off / Saint's Glimpse only).
7. Submit to the Canon Council with the Spine paragraph attached.

Each card's metadata carries: Series, Universe, Set, Moment, Tier, **Shell**, **Parallel**, **Elemental**, Serial, Lampblack-tags. The Shell × Parallel × Elemental triple makes Sherlock-CYBER × ARCANA × REASON distinct from Sherlock-CYBER × WITNESS × REASON distinct from Sherlock-PRIME × ARCANA × REASON. Collector hunts along any of three axes; the cosmology supports all three without collapsing.
