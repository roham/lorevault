## 1. Series-Level Meta-Arcs

The Lattice is here before Series 1; it will be here after Series 12. Each Series is a single set of openings cut into already-existing glass — not a "season" of a TV show. The arcs below describe what is *felt* across each year, not what is *stated*.

### Series 1 — *"The Glass Catches Light"* (Months 1–12)
**One-line thesis:** *Each Universe is shown to be real on its own terms; the audience first notices that two Universes share the same residue.*

Series 1 ships PRIME-shell Sets that establish each Universe's foundational figures and Spines in their canonical period — Victorian Sherlock, medieval Wolf, 1865 Alice, 1897 Dracula, Bronze-Age Persephone. The Lattice and Lampblack are *named* but never explained. Three Universes (Baker Street, Enchanted Kingdom, Wonderland) Surface-reveal in Q1; Greek Myth and Gothic Horror Surface-reveal in Q2. The first cross-Universe Lampblack residue (Watson's Afghanistan limp echoing in a Greek hoplite's gait — see GM-3) appears in Q3 without commentary. By the close of Series 1, attentive collectors are *theorizing* the Three Laws on their own; we never confirm them. Per R3 §2 (BBC Sherlock escalation collapse), Series 1's discipline is restraint — every Universe ships its most recognizable figures first because Lampblack only resonates against canon people knew before they got here.

### Series 2 — *"Other Rooms in the Same House"* (Months 13–24)
**One-line thesis:** *The shells multiply. Figures the audience knows in PRIME now appear in CYBER, MIRROR, HOLLOW, DREAM, and SAINT — each shell justified by a Spine event the figure must still hit.*

Series 2 is the shell-expansion Series. The eight-shell taxonomy (Decision 4) becomes legible: every Universe ships at least one non-PRIME Set, and Gothic Horror ships its Decision-11-mandated **original anchor** — the Lampblacker (see GH-7) — a Council-voiced figure who appears in HOLLOW and binds the Universe's other figures. Series 2 introduces the **Borrowed Pane** (per R6 §1 Universes Beyond lesson) as a sandbox concept gestured at in Set tethers but never given its own Set. Cross-Universe Lampblack residues are now visible to collectors who pay attention — Alice's blue ribbon turns up on a Greek shrine; Dracula's count of nights echoes in a Sherlock case file — and we let the community name the residues without confirming them. The 1:2:4 ratio holds because for every Surface shell shipped, two Echo shells are gestured at on cards (mentioned, drawn into a panel corner) and four Deep shells are referenced in flavor text without art.

### Series 3 — *"What Holds the Glass Together"* (Months 25–36)
**One-line thesis:** *The audience meets the lattice-touchpoints — places where two Panes have left visible Lampblack on each other — and one of the Three Laws is, for the first time, stated aloud.*

Series 3 turns toward the *physics of the Lattice*. Sets in Series 3 are organized around **lattice-touchpoints** — locations or moments where two Panes have brushed against each other long enough to leave residue. The Reichenbach Falls shows up in Wonderland (BS↔WL, see WL-12); the Apple appears on Olympus (EK↔GM, see GM-12); the Phantom's mirror reflects something it shouldn't (GH↔WL, see GH-12). These are not crossover gimmicks — they are the *first visible evidence of the Lattice itself*. By Month 36, the Canon Council confirms whichever of the Three Laws the community has converged on most cleanly (per Decision 8). Per the trust contract (Decision 9), no Series 1 or 2 Pane is decanonized; Series 3's revelations are additive only. The Ultimate Moments of Series 3 are *recognition events* — the figure realizing they are not alone in the glass.

### Pacing of Ultimates Across the Three Series
Per R3 §1 (Once Upon a Time bloat) and R6 §3 (Marvel Phase 4 volume collapse), the cardinal sin is front-loading apex Moments. The roadmap distributes the genuine apex beats across all three Series:

- **Series 1 apex Ultimates** are *canonical-period definitive* (Reichenbach, the Apple's bite, Persephone's descent). High recognition, no doctrinal stretch.
- **Series 2 apex Ultimates** are *shell-recontextualization* (Sherlock-CYBER's Reichenbach, Persephone-HOLLOW's winter, the Lampblacker's reveal). Medium recognition, high collector gratification.
- **Series 3 apex Ultimates** are *lattice-touchpoints* (Cheshire Recognizing Watson, the Apple on Olympus, the Phantom's Other Mirror). Lower instant recognition, highest mythic weight.

This distribution ensures the Lattice has more story to tell at Year 4 than at Year 1.

## 3. Cross-Set Tethering Map

Every Set tethers to at least one other; lattice-touchpoint Sets in Series 3 tether to two or more. Tethers are **Lampblack-only** — a figure cards from Set A do not appear on cards from Set B; instead, residues (props, scars, lines, postures) recur across the two Sets, visible only to attentive collectors. Per Decision 5, no cross-shell card is shipped Series 1–4.

### Tether classes
1. **Spine continuation tethers** — same figure across shells/sets. Example: BS-1 → BS-5 → BS-7 (PRIME → CYBER → HOLLOW Holmes).
2. **Lampblack-residue tethers** — a residue (Watson's limp, Alice's ribbon, Dracula's count of nights, the Apple) recurs across two Universes' Sets. Example: BS-1 ↔ GM-3 (the limp).
3. **Anchor tethers** — Series 2's GH-7 (the Lampblacker) tethers to one Set per Universe in S2.
4. **Touchpoint tethers** — Series 3's S3.M34–M36 close-Sets are explicit lattice-touchpoints; each tethers to multiple others.
5. **Voice tethers** — same in-world narrator voicing two Sets. Watson voices BS-1 and BS-4. Mycroft voices BS-3 and BS-8. The Cheshire voices WL-1 and WL-5. The Wolf voices EK-1 and EK-7. The Lampblacker voices GH-7 and GH-10.

## 4. Cross-Universe Events (Crossover Policy)

**The default policy is no crossovers.** Per Decision 5 and R3 §1 (Once Upon a Time bloat), shared-card cross-Universe Sets are explicitly banned for Series 1–4. The Lattice expresses cross-Universe relationship through *Lampblack residue*, never through *figures appearing together*.

**The single exception class — lattice-touchpoint Sets — is reserved for Series 3.** A lattice-touchpoint is a *place* (the Reichenbach Falls, the Apple, the Wellhead, the Phantom's Mirror) where two Panes have brushed long enough to leave residue. Lattice-touchpoint cards are *landscapes*, *objects*, or *traces* — never two figures from different Universes on the same card. The four touchpoint Sets shipped in Series 3 are: BS-12, EK-12, GM-12, GH-12, WL-12 (paired one-to-one as Reichenbach↔Falls, Wellhead↔Olympus, and Phantom's Mirror↔Looking-Glass).

**Why no full crossovers in Years 1–3.** Per R3 §1, *Once Upon a Time* failed because every fairy-tale figure ended up in the same town and the meta-rule "magic is a curse" was overrun within two seasons. Per R3 §5 (League of Extraordinary Gentlemen film), pulling multiple PD figures into a team caper breaks every Spine in the team. Per R6 §3 (Marvel Phase 4), unscoped multiverse access destroys narrative weight.

**The Lampblacker (GH-7, Series 2) is not a crossover figure.** They are an in-Universe original (Decision 11) who *carries* Lampblack between Panes. The audience sees them in Gothic Horror Sets only; their residue appears in other Universes' Sets without their figure appearing. This is the design that R3 §4 (Penny Dreadful's Vanessa Ives) validated: an original anchor that holds the borrowed names.

**The Borrowed Pane** (per R6 §1 Universes Beyond, and Iceberg Doctrine §4 Months 15–24) is gestured at in Series 2 but does not ship a Set in Series 1–3. Norse, Arthurian, and 1001 Nights residues appear in flavor text and background detail; their full Universe-promotion is a Series 4+ decision. The non-canon-yet status is the feature.

**Frequency:** lattice-touchpoint Sets only. Five of them, all in Series 3 close (M34–M36). After Series 3, the Council reviews touchpoint policy. Until then: zero cross-Universe figure-shared cards.

## 6. Iceberg Compliance Audit (Surface : Echo : Deep — 1 : 2 : 4)

The doctrine binds every quarter; the audit below holds across each Series. Surface = shipped + explained. Echo = shipped + unexplained. Deep = referenced (in flavor text, background art, voice) + never shown.

### Series 1 (Months 1–12)
**Surface:** the five Universes' canonical figures in PRIME shell. The phrase "the Lattice." The phrase "Lampblack." Total Surface units: roughly 20 (4 Sets × 5 Universes; counted as conceptually-explained units of cosmology, not card count).
**Echo (target ~40 units, 2× Surface):** approximately 40 items in the catalog — visible but unexplained. **Three named Echo elements:**
1. **Watson's Afghanistan limp recurring on a Greek hoplite's gait** (BS-1 ↔ GM-3, M9). Drawn into the corner of an Odysseus card; never captioned.
2. **Alice's blue ribbon in the Wolf's teeth** (WL-1 ↔ EK-3, M8). Visible in the margin of a single Wolf card; flavor text doesn't acknowledge it.
3. **The chessboard pattern of Moriarty's table reappearing as the floor of the Court of Hearts** (BS-3 ↔ WL-3, M9). Same geometry, different scale. Never named.

**Deep (target ~80 units, 4× Surface):** referenced, never shown. **Six named Deep elements:**
1. **The Lampblacker** — referenced obliquely in three Series 1 flavor texts as "the figure between"; not shipped as a card until S2.M20.
2. **The Three Laws of the Lattice** — implicit in Spine adherence; never stated.
3. **The number of Panes** — alluded to ("more glass than light," "the seventeenth Pane") but never confirmed.
4. **The Borrowed Pane** — Norse and Arthurian residues appear in Watson's notebook clippings; no Norse Set ships.
5. **The Council** — referenced as in-world keepers of the Spine; never depicted.
6. **The origin of the Lattice** — explicitly withheld per doctrine §4. References take the form "before the first Pane was cut," never resolved.

**Ratio audit:** 20 : 40 : 80 = 1 : 2 : 4. ✅ Compliant.

### Series 2 (Months 13–24)
**Surface:** seven non-PRIME shells (CY, MD, AE, HO, MI, DR, SA) demonstrated across all Universes. The original anchor (Lampblacker) shipped. Total Surface units: roughly 35.
**Echo (~70 units):** **three named Echo elements:**
1. **The Lampblacker's residue on cards in BS-7, EK-7, WL-5, GM-7** — visible (a smudge, an inscription, a fingerprint) without naming.
2. **Persephone's pomegranate seeds appearing on Snow White's apple** (GM-1 ↔ EK-6, M16). Same fruit, different ledgers.
3. **The Cheshire's grin appearing in the empty Baker Street parlor** (WL-5 ↔ BS-7, M20). The grin without the cat is the defining Wonderland Lampblack; it surfaces in HOLLOW London once.

**Deep (~140 units):** **six named Deep elements:**
1. **The Council's identities** — referenced in mode-3 artifact flavor on three Sets.
2. **The Borrowed Pane is named** — the words "the Borrowed Pane" appear in two flavor texts (no Norse Set yet).
3. **The Lampblacker's origin** — withheld until S3.
4. **The seventh and eighth shells** (provisional named-but-unshipped shells) referenced as "shells the Lattice has not yet returned to us."
5. **The Reichenbach-Wonderland connection** — flavor text in WL-5 hints at "a fall on a fall."
6. **The Apple's true count** — referenced as the apple "that has been bitten before, in another orchard."

**Ratio audit:** 35 : 70 : 140 = 1 : 2 : 4. ✅ Compliant.

### Series 3 (Months 25–36)
**Surface:** SAINT shell across all five Universes. Five lattice-touchpoint Sets. The Lampblacker's origin Set (GH-10). One of the Three Laws confirmed by the Council in M36. Total Surface units: roughly 50.
**Echo (~100 units):** **three named Echo elements:**
1. **The Apple of Discord and Snow White's apple are the same Apple** (GM-12 ↔ EK-4 ↔ EK-12, M34–36) — shown on the same table but never *captioned* as identity.
2. **The Wellhead at the back of Cinderella's cottage and the Wellhead at the gate of Hades** are visibly the same wellhead in two Sets (EK-12 ↔ GM-1, M34) — same stone, different inscriptions.
3. **The Phantom's Other Mirror** reflects something from Looking-Glass House without the Phantom shown reaching for it (GH-12 ↔ WL-4, M35).

**Deep (~200 units):** **six named Deep elements:**
1. **The other two Laws** — explicitly *not* confirmed in M36; the Council confirms one. The other two stay submerged.
2. **The total census of Panes** — still withheld.
3. **The Lampblacker's purpose** — origin is shipped; *purpose* is referenced only as "the residue must go somewhere."
4. **The Series 4 Universes** — referenced as "the Panes the Lattice has not yet returned to us." No Series 4 Sets named.
5. **The Council members** — referenced in mode-3 voice but never named or depicted.
6. **The metaphysics of Lampblack** — confirmed as "the residue of contact"; the *why of contact-producing-residue* permanently withheld per doctrine §4.

**Ratio audit:** 50 : 100 : 200 = 1 : 2 : 4. ✅ Compliant.

### Audit conclusion
The roadmap is structurally Iceberg-compliant across all three Series. Per Decision 7, the Canon Council reviews the ratio quarterly; any quarter that breaches it is failed and the Set held until correction.
