# LoreVault Universe Research — Slavic / Russian Folktale (Series 2+ Candidate)

**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Status:** PD-deep at the literary AND visual layer (rare). Two non-trivial overhangs: (1) Witcher / Sapkowski / CD Projekt RED is the largest derivative claimant on this folkloric substrate and lives in litigated territory; (2) the Russian-vs-Ukrainian-vs-Polish framing is geopolitically charged in 2026 and must be handled with care.
**Headline:** Slavic folktale is the **best PD-illustrated set in the entire candidate bench** — Bilibin, Vasnetsov, Vrubel, and Zworykin together provide a uniquely complete pre-1925 Russian-folk visual vocabulary that no other Universe enjoys. The literary canon (Pushkin, Afanasyev, Zhukovsky) is rock-solid PD. The danger is *contemporary* — Witcher visual language has colonized half of fantasy, and a Russian-coded Universe shipped in 2026 needs deliberate Slavic-broad framing (Polish, Ukrainian, Czech, Belarusian) rather than Russian-Federation-coded chauvinism.

## 1. Canon Enumeration

**Top 25+ Figures (PD-deep, Pushkin / Afanasyev / byliny / Slavic-pagan attested):**

- **Witches & antagonists:** Baba Yaga (iron-toothed, bony-leg, mortar-and-pestle, hut-on-chicken-legs); Koschei the Deathless / Koshchei Bessmertny (soul hidden in a needle in an egg in a duck in a hare in an iron chest beneath a green oak on Buyan Island); Likho (one-eyed misfortune); Kikimora (female house-spirit gone bad).
- **Heroines:** Vasilisa the Beautiful (with her dying-mother's blessing-doll); Marya Morevna (warrior-princess who imprisons Koschei); Snegurochka / The Snow Maiden (born of snow, melts in spring); Tsarevna Lebed' / The Swan-Princess of *Tsar Saltan*; Yelena the Wise; Alyonushka.
- **Heroes:** Tsarevitch Ivan / Ivan Tsarevich (the recurring tale-prince); Ivan-Durak (Ivan the Fool, who wins because he is foolish-pure); Ruslan (of Pushkin's *Ruslan & Lyudmila*); Prince Gvidon (of *Tsar Saltan*); Finist the Bright Falcon.
- **Bogatyri (Russian epic-knights of the byliny cycle):** Ilya Muromets (peasant-paralytic-turned-strongman, defender of Kyivan Rus'); Dobrynya Nikitich (dragon-slayer of Zmey); Alyosha Popovich (priest's-son trickster); Svyatogor (mountain-giant elder); Mikula Selyaninovich (the plowman whose plow no bogatyr can lift).
- **Magic creatures & familiars:** Firebird / Zhar-Ptitsa (incandescent feather, plumage of fire); Sirin & Alkonost (paradise-bird-women); Gamayun (prophetic bird); Sivka-Burka (the magic horse-of-three-colors); the Grey Wolf of *Ivan-Tsarevich and the Firebird*.
- **Spirits of place (Slavic-pagan substrate, pre-Christian):** Domovoi (house-spirit, hearth-protector, small bearded figure behind the stove); Leshy (forest-spirit, green-haired, can shape-shift tree-tall or grass-tiny, leads travelers astray); Rusalka (water-maiden, soul of a drowned or unbaptized woman); Vodyanoy (male water-spirit, fish-bearded, lives by mills); Bannik (bathhouse-spirit, dangerous on the third wash); Polevik (field-spirit, noon-sun-stroke); Poludnitsa (Lady Midday, sun-stroke-bringer).
- **Monsters:** Zmey Gorynych (three-headed fire-breathing dragon, lives in mountains); Solovei the Robber / Nightingale-Brigand (defeated by Ilya Muromets); Chudo-Yudo (multi-headed sea-monster).
- **Polish/Pan-Slavic figures:** Pan Twardowski (Polish Faust, sold soul to Devil, escaped to the Moon on a rooster); the Wawel Dragon (Kraków, defeated by shoemaker Skuba's sulphur-sheepskin trick); Boruta (Polish lord-demon of Łęczyca); Strzyga (Polish vampiric undead-bird).
- **Pushkin-canonical:** Ruslan, Lyudmila, Chernomor (the wicked dwarf-sorcerer); Tsar Saltan, Tsarina, Prince Gvidon, the Swan-Princess; the Golden Cockerel; King Dadon; the Old Fisherman & his Wife and the Golden Fish.

**Top 10–15 Moments:**

- **Vasilisa & Baba Yaga:** Vasilisa is sent into the forest by her stepmother to fetch fire from Baba Yaga; the magic doll given by her dying mother does the impossible chores; Baba Yaga sets her free with a skull-on-a-stick lantern that burns the wicked stepmother to ash. ([Vasilisa the Beautiful — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasilisa_the_Beautiful))
- **Koschei's Death:** Marya Morevna captures Koschei and chains him; Ivan unwittingly frees him; Koschei abducts Marya; Ivan must travel beyond the world to find the egg containing Koschei's soul-needle; in the Afanasyev variant, the horse from Baba Yaga's stable kicks Koschei's skull open. ([Koshchei — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koshchei)) ([Death of Koschei — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Death_of_Koschei_the_Deathless))
- **The Firebird:** Tsar's apple-tree is robbed of golden apples nightly; the youngest son catches a feather of the Firebird; the search takes him through the wolf-grey-wolf cycle, the Tsar-Maiden, and the resurrection-by-living-water moment.
- **Ilya Muromets origin:** paralyzed peasant for 33 years, healed by passing kaliki (wandering pilgrims), inherits Svyatogor's strength, defeats Solovei the Robber-Nightingale, defends Kyivan Rus'. ([Ilya Muromets — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilya_Muromets))
- **Dobrynya vs. Zmey Gorynych:** three-day-three-night fight; Dobrynya breaks pact-truce when dragon kidnaps Zabava Putyatishna; final triumph. ([Zmey Gorynych — godsandmonsters.info](https://godsandmonsters.info/zmey-gorynych/))
- **Pushkin's *Ruslan & Lyudmila* (1820):** Lyudmila stolen at the wedding by Chernomor the dwarf-sorcerer; Ruslan's quest; the giant living-head; reunion through a battle of magic. ([Ruslan and Ludmila — Poetry in Translation](https://www.poetryintranslation.com/PITBR/Russian/RuslanAndLudmilaI.php))
- **Pushkin's *Tale of Tsar Saltan* (1831):** queen and infant son sealed in a barrel and cast to sea; Prince Gvidon grows in days; the Swan-Princess transforms him to insect to spy on his father; the three wonders: the squirrel that cracks emerald nuts, the 33 sea-knights led by Chernomor, the Swan-Princess herself.
- **Pushkin's *Golden Cockerel* (1834):** astrologer-eunuch gives Tsar Dadon a golden cockerel that warns of attacks; the prophecy turns; the cockerel kills the Tsar.
- **Snegurochka:** old couple build a daughter from snow; she lives a winter; falls in love; melts on the first warm day. ([The Snow Maiden — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Snow_Maiden))
- **Pan Twardowski (Polish):** sells soul to Devil, names "Rome" as the only place of collection, Devil tricks him into an inn called Rome, salt-in-the-eyes escape, lifted to the Moon on a flying rooster, hangs there still. ([Pan Twardowski — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sir_Twardowski))
- **Wawel Dragon (Polish):** dragon under Wawel Hill terrorizes Kraków; shoemaker Skuba feeds it a sulphur-stuffed sheepskin; dragon drinks the Vistula and explodes. ([Wawel Dragon — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wawel_Dragon))
- **The Twelve Months (Czech / Russian variant):** stepdaughter sent to gather violets / strawberries / apples in deep winter; the twelve Months around their fire help her; the cruel sister freezes.

**Props:** mortar-and-pestle (Baba Yaga's mount); the chicken-legged hut; the skull-lantern of Vasilisa; the magical doll-from-mother; Koschei's needle / egg / chest / oak-on-Buyan; the Firebird's feather; the apple of youth (Yablochki Molodye); the living-water and dead-water (one heals wounds, one resurrects); the magic mirror; the tablecloth-self-laying (samobranka); the seven-league-boots (sapogi-skorokhody); Ilya's mace; Dobrynya's whip; the magic comb-becomes-forest, towel-becomes-river.

**Places:** the dark forest (les), the bathhouse (banya), the village stove (pech') as portal/hearth, the bridge-of-judgement at the river of fire (Smorodina), Buyan Island, Kitezh (the city that sank to escape Mongol invaders, visible to the pure of heart), Kyivan Rus' as the bogatyr-frame, the bog and the rye-field at noon, Wawel Hill, the Carpathian crossings.

## 2. PD Status

**Deep PD, top-tier safe at the source layer:**

- **Alexander Pushkin (1799–1837)** — verses, fairy tales, *Ruslan & Lyudmila* (1820), *Tale of Tsar Saltan* (1831), *Tale of the Golden Cockerel* (1834), *Tale of the Fisherman and the Fish* (1833), *Tale of the Dead Princess and the Seven Knights* (1833). Pushkin died 1837 — deep PD globally. ([Pushkin — Wikisource](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Author:Alexander_Pushkin))
- **Vasily Zhukovsky (1783–1852)** — Pushkin's mentor, ballads, *The Sleeping Tsarevna*, translator-into-Russian-of-Western-folktale; PD.
- **Alexander Afanasyev (1826–1871)** — *Narodnye russkie skazki* / *Russian Folk Tales* (1855–1864), the foundational corpus, ~600 tales. PD. ([Russian Folktales — RussianFolktales.com](https://www.russianfolktales.com/characters/baba-yaga))
- **Andrew Lang's *Red Fairy Book* (1890)** — includes "The Death of Koschei the Deathless" in English; PD.
- **Byliny (oral epic-poems)** — Kirsha Danilov's collection (compiled mid-1700s, first published 1804), Kireevsky's collections (mid-1800s), Rybnikov & Hilferding 1860s–70s field-recordings; all PD. ([Bylina — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bylina))
- **Polish folklore corpora:** Oskar Kolberg (1814–1890) ethnographic *Lud* multi-volume; Adam Mickiewicz (1798–1855) *Dziady* / *Pan Tadeusz* with folkloric strands; PD.
- **Czech:** Karel Jaromír Erben (1811–1870) *Kytice / A Bouquet*; Božena Němcová (1820–1862) *Národní báchorky a pověsti*; PD.
- **Ukrainian:** Mykhailo Drahomanov, Pavlo Chubynsky 19th-c collections; PD. *Lesya Ukrainka* (1871–1913) — *Forest Song / Lisova Pisnya* (1911) PD as the author died 1913.

**Operatic/literary derivatives also PD:** Rimsky-Korsakov *Snow Maiden* (1881–82), *Tale of Tsar Saltan* opera (1900), *Golden Cockerel* (1909); Mussorgsky *Night on Bald Mountain* (1867 — Slavic witches'-sabbath); Tchaikovsky's incidental music to Ostrovsky's *Snegurochka* (1873). All composer/playwright deaths pre-1925.

The literary substrate is as PD-clean as Norse or Greek. **The unique advantage is the visual layer (Section 4).**

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield — Specific Locks

**ODIN ESCALATION on Witcher above all. CD Projekt RED owns audiovisual rights to Sapkowski's universe (acquired 2008, settled 2019 royalty dispute), and Netflix-Witcher costuming/casting/design is heavily protected. Sapkowski's novels are in copyright since 1986+. The risk is that CD Projekt RED has effectively trained a generation of artists to draw "Slavic dark fantasy" in Witcher's visual language — every leshy, striga, kikimora illustration on ArtStation since 2015 borrows Witcher silhouettes. We must visibly differentiate.** ([Variety — Sapkowski $16M demand](https://variety.com/2018/gaming/news/the-witcher-andrzej-sapkowski-royalty-dispute-1202964998/))

1. **Disney's *Fantasia 2000* "Firebird Suite"** — the volcanic-spirit reimagining of the Firebird (Pixote-style red-and-black ash phoenix), the Sprite, the Elk. The Stravinsky 1910 score itself is PD in US (composed >115 years ago) but Boosey & Hawkes-Disney litigated home-video use. The *visual* design is owned by Disney. Avoid the volcanic-Firebird interpretation entirely. ([Fantasy/Animation — Firebird Suite analysis](https://www.fantasy-animation.org/current-posts/analysis-of-fantasia-2000s-the-firebird-suite))
2. **Disney 1940 *Fantasia* "Sorcerer's Apprentice"** — Mickey-as-Goethe/Dukas-Apprentice with marching brooms. Goethe's 1797 *Der Zauberlehrling* is PD; the Mickey/broom imagery is Disney-locked. (Tangential to Slavic but the apprentice-archetype trap is similar.)
3. **CD Projekt RED *Witcher 3* Slavic-creature designs** — the specific kikimora-as-arachnid silhouette, the leshy-as-elk-skull-tree-spirit (the antlered fungal-tree forest god), the noonwraith / poludnica-as-billowing-white-veil-with-curved-blade, the striga-as-claw-and-howl. These are CD Projekt's *interpretation*, copyrighted. Avoid. ([Slavic mythology behind The Witcher — Slavorum](https://www.slavorum.org/slavic-mythology-behind-the-popular-witcher-series/))
4. **Witcher Netflix (2019+)** — Henry Cavill / Liam Hemsworth-as-Geralt look (white-haired, two-sword-back-harness), Yennefer's specific design, Jaskier's costume. Avoid all on-show casting/styling.
5. **Sapkowski's specific non-folkloric inventions** — Geralt as a "witcher" (mutant-hunter caste), Ciri's elder-blood lineage, the Lodge of Sorceresses, Nilfgaardian iconography. These are post-1986 and copyrighted to Sapkowski/heirs.
6. **Hellboy 2 (2008) Baba Yaga design** — del Toro/Mignola's specific Baba Yaga (one-eye-traded, gaunt-claw, white-veil) is Dark Horse / Mignola IP. The chained-coffin Baba Yaga from Mignola's *Chained Coffin* (1998+) is in copyright. Folkloric Baba Yaga is PD; *Mignola's* is not. ([Hellboy II Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellboy_II:_The_Golden_Army))
7. **Don Bluth / Fox *Anastasia* (1997)** — Rasputin-as-undead-sorcerer with green-glowing reliquary, the bat sidekick Bartok. The *historical* Anastasia Romanov is not folkloric (and not appropriate for LoreVault). The Bluth Rasputin look is locked. (Not a folkloric overlap, but worth flagging since *Anastasia* is the first thing US audiences associate with "Russian fairy-tale movie.")
8. **Katherine Arden *Bear and the Nightingale* (2017+) Winternight Trilogy** — Vasya Petrovna's specific characterization (Vasilisa-coded but Arden's invention), Morozko-as-frost-demon as written, Medved-the-Bear as written. Folkloric Vasilisa, Morozko, the Bear-the-Eater are PD; *Arden's specific* portraits are not. ([Bear and the Nightingale — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Bear_and_the_Nightingale))
9. **Naomi Novik *Spinning Silver* (2018) and *Uprooted* (2015)** — specific Polish-coded characterization (the Wood, the Staryk king); in copyright.
10. **Marvel *Black Widow* / Red Room** — Russian-coded but spy-genre, low overlap; flag if any Slavic-folk-creature character ever introduced.
11. **John Wick "Baba Yaga" reference** — purely metaphorical naming; not a design lock, but the cultural association ("Baba Yaga" = "boogeyman / unkillable assassin") is now strong in US audiences and may need disambiguation.
12. **Recent Russian fairy-tale tentpole films (*Posledniy Bogatyr* / *The Last Knight*, 2017+; sequels 2020, 2021)** — Russian-Disney's reimagining; specific cast/costume locked. Russian-domestic IP overhang though limited US-distribution.
13. **Stravinsky *Firebird* (1910)** — score itself: PD in US (now >115 years old, well past US 95-year-from-publication for foreign works pre-1928). Specific Diaghilev/Ballets Russes 1910 stage designs by Golovin and Bakst — designers died 1930 / 1924, so designs are PD by death+95 in most jurisdictions, **UNCLEAR for restored productions.**
14. **Tarkovsky / Soviet animation aesthetics** — Yuri Norshteyn's *Tale of Tales* (1979) and *Hedgehog in the Fog* (1975), Soyuzmultfilm's *Snegurochka* (1952), Roman Kachanov works — Soviet-era IP, complicated post-1991 reassignment. Avoid direct visual quotation. Soyuzmultfilm catalog is held by Russian Federation entity. **UNCLEAR** post-2022 sanctions environment for any licensing path; assume blocked.
15. **Bajka Polish animation, Czech surrealist (Švankmajer) folk aesthetics** — in-copyright living artists' work; do not visually quote.

**ODIN ESCALATION priority order:** **(1) Witcher creature-design vocabulary** (highest commercial-collision risk because it has *defined* Slavic dark-fantasy for a generation), **(2) Hellboy/Mignola Baba Yaga**, **(3) Disney Fantasia 2000 Firebird**, **(4) Arden / Novik Slavic-fantasy literary register**.

## 4. Pre-Modern PD Visual Register — The Bilibin Stack

**This is Slavic folktale's structural advantage over every other Universe in the bench.** Russian-folk fairy tale enjoys a **uniquely complete pre-1925 illustrated canon** by named, world-class artists who illustrated the *exact* tales LoreVault would draw from. No other Universe (not Norse, not Greek, not Egyptian, not Arthurian) has this density of one-illustrator-to-one-canon match.

- **Ivan Bilibin (1876–1942)** — *the* canonical Russian fairy-tale illustrator. His 1899–1902 series of folio-format colored illustrations for *Vasilisa the Beautiful*, *Tsarevitch Ivan, the Firebird, and the Grey Wolf*, *The Frog Princess*, *The Feather of Finist the Falcon*, *Marya Morevna*, and *The White Duck*, plus his Pushkin illustrations (*Tale of Tsar Saltan* 1905, *Tale of the Golden Cockerel* 1906, *Tale of the Fisherman* 1908), define what "Russian fairy-tale visual" *means* globally. Bilibin's style: art-nouveau line + Russian-lubok flatness + Japanese-print compositional borders + Byzantine-icon palette. Bilibin died 1942; works are PD in countries with life+82 or less, which now includes the US (life+70 EU rule + US restoration of foreign works = PD by 2013 in Russia, PD in EU by 2013, PD in US for works published pre-1928 unconditionally). Bilibin's 1899–1902 Russian fairy-tale folios are firmly PD-US for the LoreVault use case. The Met holds open-access scans. ([Bilibin — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivan_Bilibin)) ([Met Museum — Bilibin Vasilisa](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/346087)) ([Met Museum — Bilibin Firebird](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/349538)) ([Wikimedia Commons — Russian copyright rules](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Copyright_rules_by_territory/Russia)) ([Public Domain Image Library — Bilibin](https://publicdomainimagelibrary.com/collections/bilibin-ivan))
- **Viktor Vasnetsov (1848–1926)** — the heroic-Romantic painter of byliny and skazka. *Bogatyrs* (1898, Tretyakov Gallery — Ilya, Dobrynya, Alyosha on horseback), *Ivan Tsarevich Riding the Grey Wolf* (1889), *Vasnetsov's Snow Maiden* (1899), *Alyonushka* (1881), *The Knight at the Crossroads* (1882), *Sirin and Alkonost* (1896), *Baba Yaga* (1917). Public domain — Vasnetsov died 1926, works pre-1928 PD-US; EU PD as of 1996+. ([Russia Beyond — Vasnetsov folklore paintings](https://www.rbth.com/arts/331649-russian-folklore-paintings-vasnetsov))
- **Mikhail Vrubel (1856–1910)** — the symbolist-hallucinatory register. *Demon Seated* (1890), *Demon Downcast* (1902), *The Swan Princess* (1900, the iconic *Tsarevna-Lebed* portrait of his wife Nadezhda Zabela in costume), *Pan* (1899, the Slavic-leshy-coded forest god), *Snegurochka* designs for the 1885 Mamontov private opera. Vrubel died 1910 — deep PD. The Vrubel register adds the **fever-dream / madness / Lampblack-edge** pole that complements Bilibin's clean line. ([TheArtStory — Vrubel](https://www.theartstory.org/artist/vrubel-mikhail/))
- **Boris Zworykin / Zvorykin (1872–1942)** — Bilibin's stylistic heir. Worked in Paris exile from 1921; illustrated *The Firebird and Other Russian Fairy Tales* and *Maria Morevna* (Moscow 1904) and Pushkin tales. Heavily gold-leafed, art-nouveau Byzantine. Met Museum holdings open-access. ([Met Museum — Zworykin Pushkin](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/382780)) ([Shapero Rare Books — Zworykin Maria Morevna](https://shapero.com/en-us/products/zworykin-maria-morevna-1904-first-edition-113049))
- **Elena Polenova (1850–1898)** — earlier Abramtsevo-circle illustrator, *War of the Mushrooms* and other Russian fairy-tale folios; PD.
- **Ivan Shishkin (1832–1898)** — landscape master whose deep-Russian-forest paintings (*Morning in a Pine Forest* 1889) provide the Leshy-realm backdrop register; PD.
- **Mikhail Nesterov (1862–1942)** — religious-folkloric, *Vision to the Youth Bartholomew* (1890); pairs well as the bogatyr-saint pole.
- **Polish: Jacek Malczewski (1854–1929)** — symbolist with rusałka and folkloric subjects; PD.
- **Polish: Stanisław Wyspiański (1869–1907)** — *Wesele* / "The Wedding" 1901 with folkloric apparitions; PD.
- **Czech: Mikoláš Aleš (1852–1913)** — Czech-folk illustrator; PD.
- **Czech: Josef Mánes (1820–1871)** — folk-costume-and-tale; PD.
- **Pre-modern source register: Russian *lubok* (popular woodblock prints, 17th–early 19th c.)** — the flat, captioned, color-blocked register of Ilya Muromets, Bova Korolevich, Yeruslan Lazarevich. PD by age. The lubok is the *folk* visual substrate that Bilibin elevated.
- **Old Russian icon tradition (pre-Petrine)** — Theophanes the Greek, Andrei Rublev (~1360–1430). Andrei Rublev's *Trinity* (~1411) and *Holy Trinity* register provide the Byzantine-Russian-iconographic flatness that grounds the whole stack. PD by centuries.

**Recommendation: Bilibin-primary lead register (line + flat color + folio-border framing) + Vasnetsov heroic-pole for bogatyr/landscape scenes + Vrubel symbolist-pole for Lampblack-coded madness/dream sequences + lubok-folk-print as low-rarity background register.** This stack is *visibly Slavic-fairy-tale* and *visibly not Witcher* — Witcher's grimdark-photoreal Northern-European look has zero overlap with Bilibin's flat-jewel-tone-Byzantine-art-nouveau. The pre-modern PD register is the cleanest defense we have against CD Projekt RED visual collision.

## 5. Top 5 Figures — Spine + Lampblack

**Baba Yaga — the Iron-Toothed Grandmother, the Bone-Fence Witch**
- *Spine:* Ambivalent forest-witch (not pure-evil): tests heroes, sometimes helps, sometimes eats them. Lives in a hut on chicken legs (which turns to face you when addressed correctly), inside a fence of bones with skull-lanterns. Travels by mortar, steered with pestle, sweeping her tracks behind her with a broom of birch. Has iron teeth; sometimes appears as three sisters (all named Baba Yaga); sometimes one-leg-bone, one-leg-flesh.
- *Lampblack:* The grandmother who is also the grave. Hospitality and devourment in the same gesture. The Lampblack-residue of *the question that judges*. Cross-tether to **Enchanted Kingdom Witch** (the EK shadow-witch is Baba Yaga's clean cousin) and **Greek Hecate** (the threshold-crone, witch-of-the-crossroads).

**Koschei the Deathless — the Hidden-Soul Tyrant**
- *Spine:* Skeletal sorcerer-lich, immortal because his death is *outside his body*: a needle inside an egg inside a duck inside a hare inside an iron chest beneath the green oak on the island of Buyan. Steals brides, especially Marya Morevna. Defeated only by hero-quest to find and break the needle.
- *Lampblack:* The soul kept somewhere it cannot be hurt — and the price is the soul cannot be lived in. Koschei is the Lattice's **most concentrated Lampblack figure in any Universe** because his entire being is a nested-vessel residue. The egg-in-duck-in-hare-in-chest is *literally* the Lattice-and-Lampblack metaphor: each shell a degree of separation from the lit core. **Koschei should anchor LoreVault's soul-displacement / "kept-separate" mechanics across Universes.** Cross-tether to Egyptian *ba/ka* split-soul, Norse Odin's traded eye, EK's "true-name" mechanics.

**Vasilisa the Beautiful — the Doll-Bearer**
- *Spine:* Orphaned, blessed by her dying mother with a small wooden doll that helps her if fed. Sent through the dark forest to fetch fire from Baba Yaga; performs impossible tasks; returns with a skull-lantern that incinerates her wicked stepfamily. In some variants, marries the Tsar; in others, becomes Vasilisa the Wise (Marya Morevna's near-twin).
- *Lampblack:* The mother-given object that carries her forward after the mother is gone. The doll *is* the Lampblack — a spirit-residue of the dead held in a wooden form that *eats and helps*. Cross-tether to Enchanted Kingdom Cinderella (the dead-mother helper-from-beyond), and to Egyptian shabti-figurine-as-soul-helper.

**The Firebird (Zhar-Ptitsa) — the Feather That Cannot Be Returned**
- *Spine:* Bird of incandescent plumage; one feather illuminates a hall; possessing a feather summons the Tsar's quest (he must have the bird itself). The Firebird-quest is the engine of multiple skazki: *Ivan-Tsarevich and the Grey Wolf*, *Tsar Vyslav*, *The Firebird and Princess Vasilisa*. Linked in opera tradition to Stravinsky 1910 (PD score; locked Diaghilev visuals).
- *Lampblack:* Light-as-curse. To touch a Firebird-feather is to be conscripted into a quest you didn't choose. Cross-tether to **Greek/Egyptian Phoenix** (rebirth-from-ash) — but the Slavic Firebird is *not* a rebirth bird, it is a *seizure-of-attention* bird. Important to differentiate from Phoenix in mechanics: Firebird's feather is a **summons-card**, Phoenix's ash is a **resurrection-card**.

**Ilya Muromets — the Peasant Strongman, Defender of the Land**
- *Spine:* Paralyzed for 33 years until kaliki pilgrims heal him; receives the dying strength of the ancient giant Svyatogor; defeats Solovei the Robber-Nightingale (whose whistle topples forests); serves Prince Vladimir of Kyivan Rus'; defends the southern steppe-frontier from Tatar/Kalin-tsar incursions. Eventually venerated as Saint Ilya of Murom (Orthodox sainthood, relics in Kyiv-Pechersk Lavra). ([Ilya Muromets — RBTH](https://www.rbth.com/arts/336894-ilya-muromets-bogatyr-saint))
- *Lampblack:* The strength that comes only after the long stillness. The bogatyr-as-late-bloomer. Counter-archetype to Greek Achilles (early-doom) — Ilya is the *delayed* hero. Cross-tether to **Arthurian Galahad/Percival** (chosen-by-grace) and **Norse Beowulf** (frontier-defender).

## 6. Cross-Tethers to Other Universes

The Slavic Universe is uniquely **highly cross-tethered** because Slavic folk substrate sits at the crossroads of European, Indo-Aryan, Byzantine, and steppe traditions:

- **Baba Yaga ↔ EK Witch shadow ↔ Greek Hecate ↔ Gothic Horror crone-archetype.** The threshold-crone is one of LoreVault's most universal figures. Baba Yaga is the *most ambivalent* version: not-pure-evil, judges-and-helps. Powerful for cross-Universe tournament mechanics (the witch who appears in three Universes wearing three faces).
- **Koschei's soul-in-egg ↔ Lampblack-vessel mechanic across Universes.** This is the **single best mechanic-export** from this Universe. Koschei's nested-vessel-soul = pure Lampblack architecture. Mirror in: Greek Pandora's box (residue-trapped-in-vessel), Egyptian canopic jars (organs-as-displaced-soul), Arabian Nights jinn-in-bottle, EK Snow White's apple-as-cursed-vessel. **Recommendation: Koschei is the hero-figure of any Lampblack-themed cross-Universe set.**
- **Firebird ↔ Greek Phoenix ↔ Egyptian Bennu ↔ Arabian Anqa.** Four PD-clean fire-birds across four Universes. The Slavic Firebird is the *quest-engine* variant, distinct from rebirth-birds of Greek/Egyptian.
- **Rusalka ↔ Gothic Horror drowned-bride ↔ Arthurian Lady-of-the-Lake ↔ Greek nymph/Naiad.** Slavic Rusalka is *unquiet-dead* (suicide / drowned / unbaptized), heavier Lampblack than the Greek Naiad. Direct cross-tether to Gothic Horror's Carmilla/Lenore-archetype and to Arthurian's lake-magic. Cross-tether also to *Lord of the Rings*-style folkloric-borrowed dead-marsh figures (LotR is in copyright; tether is to Tennyson/Arthurian PD layer).
- **Leshy ↔ Greek Pan ↔ Norse Skogsrå ↔ Arthurian Green Man.** The forest-spirit archetype is universal. Vrubel's *Pan* (1899) is literally a Slavic-Greek cross-tether painted by Vrubel himself.
- **Domovoi ↔ Lares (Roman) ↔ Greek hearth-spirits.** Household-spirit archetype.
- **Ilya Muromets ↔ Norse Beowulf ↔ Arthurian Knight-of-the-Realm ↔ Greek Heracles.** Frontier-defender bogatyr archetype.
- **Snegurochka ↔ HC Andersen Snow Queen / Ice Maiden** (HCA died 1875, PD) — winter-girl-who-melts mechanic.
- **Pan Twardowski ↔ Goethe Faust (PD) ↔ Christopher Marlowe Faustus (PD) ↔ Arabian-Nights pact-with-djinn.** The Slavic Faust variant is uniquely *escape-by-trickster*-toned (he gets to the moon, doesn't get dragged to hell), distinguishing it from the Germanic damnation-Faust.
- **Wawel Dragon ↔ Arthurian dragon ↔ Greek Hydra ↔ Norse Fafnir.** Standard dragon-slayer cross-tether but distinguished by the *trickster-shoemaker* defeat (not heroic combat).

## 7. Mechanic / Set-Design Hooks

- **Soul-in-Egg / Nested-Vessel mechanic (Koschei):** ultra-rare cards stored *inside* other cards. A Koschei card cannot be defeated until you collect the chest, then the hare, then the duck, then the egg, then the needle — a 5-deep nested-set chase. Highest-tier collector-flex mechanic in the entire bench.
- **Hut-on-Chicken-Legs (Baba Yaga):** location-card that *re-orients* on owner-command, exposes different doors / abilities depending on which face it shows. Card with rotating states.
- **Skull-Lantern (Vasilisa):** trophy-card that *destroys-on-trigger*, can be played once devastatingly. Burns wicked-step-family-cards in opponent's deck.
- **Firebird-Feather summons-card:** acquiring it auto-triggers a multi-stage quest sequence; owner cannot decline.
- **Bogatyr-Trinity (Ilya / Dobrynya / Alyosha):** triptych-set where collecting all three unlocks a fourth meta-card (Vasnetsov's 1898 painting *Bogatyrs* as the canonical visual reference).
- **Bathhouse / Banya threshold-card:** in folk-tradition the third-wash is when Bannik appears; deck-mechanic where the *third-play* of a banya-card flips effect.
- **Twelve-Months wheel:** a rotating-twelve-card seasonal set where each Month-card has a counter-Month, giving a self-contained mini-meta.
- **Pact-with-Devil / Pan Twardowski mechanic:** signing-a-card-in-blood gives huge short-term boost but creates a *Roman-trigger* delayed liability the opponent can activate.
- **Living-Water / Dead-Water pair:** dead-water heals wounds; living-water resurrects; must be applied in correct order — signature mechanic separating *healing* from *revival* into a two-card combo.
- **Buyan Island vault-zone:** a special play-zone where Koschei-related cards must be staged in the correct order (oak → chest → hare → duck → egg → needle).
- **Lubok-tier visual register for low-rarity cards:** flat folk-print register for commons; Bilibin folio register for rares; Vrubel register for ultra-rare Lampblack-coded.

## 8. Live-Tradition Tensions

This is the single most fraught area of the Slavic Universe and **must be navigated with care for a 2026 launch.**

- **Russia-Ukraine war (ongoing 2022–present)** — A LoreVault Universe shipped in 2026 that codes as "Russian fairy tales" reads as a politically loaded gesture in much of Western Europe and especially Eastern Europe. Ukrainians have spent the past four years actively differentiating Ukrainian folklore from Russian folklore as part of a broader cultural decolonization.
- **The Kyivan Rus' framing problem.** The bogatyrs (Ilya, Dobrynya, Alyosha) explicitly serve Prince Vladimir of *Kyivan Rus'* — a polity claimed by both Russian and Ukrainian (and Belarusian) national-historical narratives. Russian-Federation chauvinism reads Kyivan Rus' as "ur-Russia"; Ukrainian historiography reads it as "ur-Ukraine"; the historical reality is it was a multi-ethnic medieval Slavic principality centered on Kyiv. **LoreVault should adopt the Kyivan-Rus'-as-shared-heritage frame**, not the Russian-imperial frame. Ilya is "Ilya of Murom" (a town in modern Russia) but serves "Vladimir of Kyiv" (in modern Ukraine); honor both and collapse neither.
- **Recommendation: brand the Universe as "Slavic Folktale," not "Russian Folktale."** This is non-negotiable for 2026. Within the Universe, draw deliberately and visibly from Polish (Pan Twardowski, Wawel, Mickiewicz), Czech (Erben, Němcová, Twelve Months), Ukrainian (Lesya Ukrainka *Forest Song*, Carpathian mountain tales, the rusalka being especially Ukrainian-coded), Belarusian (Polesia-region forest spirits) and Russian sources in *visible* balance. Don't ship Series 1 Slavic with only Pushkin/Afanasyev/Bilibin and call it "Slavic" when the inside-baseball reads as straight Russian.
- **Rodnovery / Slavic Native Faith** — modern Slavic-pagan revival, tens of thousands (possibly hundreds of thousands) of practitioners across Russia, Ukraine, Poland, Czechia, Slovenia, Serbia. Living religion. Some Rodnovery groups have fought on both sides of the Donbas war (Svarozich Battalion pro-Russian; Azov regiments include Rodnovery-adjacent ideology). Some Rodnovery factions overlap with ethno-nationalist far-right movements; others are explicitly inclusive and pluralist. **LoreVault must engage source material as folk-narrative-PD, not as live-religion practice**, and avoid any iconography that could read as endorsing specific Rodnovery factions. Domovoi/Leshy/Vodyanoy as folk-tale figures is fine; Perun-as-thunder-god-icon is more fraught (Perun is actively venerated by living Rodnovers and politically loaded). ([Slavic Native Faith — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavic_Native_Faith))
- **Russian Orthodox sensitivities** — Ilya Muromets is canonized (Russian Orthodox saint, relics at Kyiv-Pechersk Lavra). Treating him purely as a folk-figure is fine; iconographic-style portrayal in a card game is fine because Vasnetsov 1898 is the canonical PD register; but avoid any portrayal that reads as deliberately blasphemous of the saint-aspect. ([Saint Ilya Muromets — Orthodox Gifts and Crafts](https://orthodoxgiftsandcrafts.com/blogs/orthodox-saints-iconography/saint-ilya-muromets-the-warrior-monk-and-protector-of-the-russian-land-life-miracles-and-prayers))
- **Polish-Russian relations** — historically fraught, currently very fraught (Poland is heavily supportive of Ukraine, hosts millions of refugees). A "Slavic folktale" Universe that visibly includes Polish material (Pan Twardowski, Wawel Dragon, Mickiewicz) reads better in Poland than a Russian-only frame. Polish folkloric material is also less cinematically saturated than Russian, giving LoreVault visual breathing room.
- **Diaspora considerations** — large Russian, Ukrainian, Polish, Czech, Serbian diaspora communities in the US/Canada are *the* most likely early enthusiasts of this Universe. Most diaspora experience of these tales is mediated through the Bilibin/Zworykin folio register. Honoring that visual heritage is also marketing.

## 9. Risk Synthesis

**PD source layer:** A+. Pushkin, Afanasyev, Zhukovsky, byliny, Polish/Czech/Ukrainian collectors all 19th-century. Universally PD-clean.

**PD visual register:** A+. **Highest-quality, named-illustrator-to-named-tale match in the entire candidate bench.** Bilibin alone gives us a complete Russian-fairy-tale visual canon. Add Vasnetsov + Vrubel + Zworykin and the visual stack is uniquely defensible — visibly *not* Witcher, *not* Disney, *not* Mignola. This is Slavic's structural advantage.

**Derivative-rights minefield:** B-. Witcher is the largest claimant, and Witcher's grip on "Slavic dark fantasy" visual language is the principal competitive risk. Hellboy-Mignola, Disney *Fantasia 2000*, Don Bluth *Anastasia*, Arden *Bear and the Nightingale*, Naomi Novik are smaller-but-real claims. The Bilibin-led visual stack is the cleanest defense.

**Live-tradition / geopolitical:** C+, requires deliberate handling. The Russia/Ukraine war, Rodnovery's political entanglement, and the bogatyr-Kyivan-Rus' shared-heritage problem all argue for **"Slavic Folktale" framing, not "Russian Folktale"**, and visible Polish/Ukrainian/Czech/Belarusian inclusion in Series 1.

**Mechanic-export potential:** A+. Koschei's nested-vessel soul is the single best Lampblack-mechanic in the entire candidate bench. Baba Yaga's hut-orientation, Vasilisa's mother-doll, the Firebird-feather-summons, the bogatyr-trinity all yield distinctive set-design hooks.

**Cross-tether density:** A. Slavic substrate sits at the crossroads of Indo-European, Byzantine, and steppe traditions; cross-tethers to Greek (Hecate, Pan, Phoenix), Norse (Beowulf-Ilya, Fafnir-Zmey), Egyptian (Phoenix-Firebird, ba/ka-Koschei), Arthurian (Lady of the Lake-Rusalka, Green Man-Leshy), Gothic Horror (drowned-bride-Rusalka), and EK (Witch-Baba Yaga, Cinderella-Vasilisa) are abundant.

**Headline recommendation:** **GREEN-LIGHT for Series 2 or Series 3, brand as "Slavic Folktale" not "Russian Folktale," lead visual register Bilibin-Vasnetsov-Vrubel, anchor mechanic on Koschei's nested-vessel soul, deliberately balance Russian/Ukrainian/Polish/Czech material in Set 1, avoid all Witcher/Hellboy/Fantasia 2000/Arden/Novik visual or character collisions, and in particular do not under any circumstances render a leshy as the antlered-fungal-skull-tree-thing — that silhouette is CD Projekt RED-coded for a generation and we must visibly choose Bilibin's green-bearded-bear-clad-grandfather-of-the-forest instead.**

## 10. ODIN ESCALATION SUMMARY

- **Witcher / CD Projekt RED / Sapkowski** — the largest derivative claimant on Slavic folklore in 2026; Sapkowski's specific characterizations (1986+) are in copyright; CD Projekt RED's audiovisual designs (2007+ games, 2019+ Netflix collaboration) are heavily protected; Sapkowski settled a royalty dispute with CD Projekt for an undisclosed amount on December 20, 2019. The risk vector is *visual vocabulary capture* — a generation of artists has been trained to draw Slavic dark fantasy in Witcher's grimdark-photoreal register. **LoreVault's Bilibin-art-nouveau register is the structural defense.** Run Odin against any leshy/striga/kikimora/noonwraith art produced by LoreVault contractors to detect Witcher-coded silhouettes before mint. ([Variety — Sapkowski $16M demand](https://variety.com/2018/gaming/news/the-witcher-andrzej-sapkowski-royalty-dispute-1202964998/))
- **Bilibin attribution** — Bilibin died December 7, 1942 (in besieged Leningrad). Russian copyright law extends life+70 years (since 2004 amendment). Bilibin's works entered Russian PD on January 1, 2013. Russia withdrew its Berne Convention Article 18 reservation in January 2013, meaning Bilibin's works are now PD-treated under Berne worldwide. US-specific: Bilibin's pre-1928 publications (the entire 1899–1908 fairy-tale and Pushkin folio series) are PD-US unconditionally; later works restored under URAA but now expired by life+70+restoration math. **Verdict: Bilibin 1899–1908 series is comprehensively PD-clean for US/EU/Russia LoreVault use.** Source attributions should still credit Bilibin by name, the holding institution (Met Museum, State Russian Museum, Tretyakov Gallery), and the publication date — partly as best-practice, partly as a hedge against any claim of moral rights (Russian moral rights are perpetual under domestic law, but limited to attribution and integrity, not commercial exclusivity). ([Bilibin — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivan_Bilibin)) ([Wikimedia Commons — Russian copyright rules](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Copyright_rules_by_territory/Russia)) ([International copyright relations of Russia — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_copyright_relations_of_Russia))
- **Soyuzmultfilm Soviet-animation aesthetics** — UNCLEAR licensing path post-2022 sanctions; assume blocked; do not borrow.
- **Geopolitical** — frame as "Slavic Folktale" not "Russian Folktale"; visibly balance four-plus national source-streams; do not foreground any iconography (Perun-thunderbolt, swastika-derivative kolovrat, etc.) that signals Rodnovery-far-right factions.
