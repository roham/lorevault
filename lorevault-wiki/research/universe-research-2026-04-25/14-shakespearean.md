## 2. PD Status

**The texts: deep PD, ~400 years.** First Folio 1623; quartos earlier; sonnets 1609. Out of any conceivable copyright window in every jurisdiction.

**The plots, characters, dialogue, names, settings:** all PD. Anyone may stage, film, remix, mash up.

**What is NOT PD:** every specific staging, film, costume design, score, choreography, and translation done within the last ~95 years (US) or life+70 (most of world). This is the dense layer LoreVault must navigate (Section 3).

**Bottom line:** the substrate is the freest material on the planet. The *specific visual register* of any 20th–21st-century adaptation is locked.

## 4. Pre-Modern Canonical PD Register

This is where Shakespeare becomes a gift to LoreVault. The Romantic era spent forty years building a **canonical visual lexicon for Shakespeare** — and almost all of it is deep PD.

- **Boydell's Shakespeare Gallery (1786–1804)** — John Boydell commissioned scores of leading British painters (Reynolds, West, Romney, Northcote, Smirke, Westall, Fuseli, etc.) to produce paintings for every major scene of every major play, then engraved them. ~170 paintings total. The gallery went bankrupt in 1804 and its prints became the **canonical 19th-century visual reference for staging Shakespeare**. Internet Archive, Folger, Met, Huntington all have full sets. **Deep PD; LoreVault should treat Boydell as the primary visual substrate.**
- **Henry Fuseli** (Swiss-Br., 1741–1825) — "The Three Witches" (1783), "Macbeth Consulting the Vision of the Armed Head" (1793), "Macbeth, Banquo and the Witches" (1793–4). Fuseli's gothic-sublime register is the canonical look for the *uncanny* Shakespeare — witches, ghosts, dream-encounters. **Deep PD.** This is the Lampblack register for our purposes.
- **William Blake** — "Pity" (c.1795, from Macbeth I.vii); "The Night of Enitharmon's Joy" / "Hecate" (c.1795). **Deep PD via Tate, Met, PD Image Archive.** Blake's visionary register pairs with Fuseli for the supernatural strand.
- **John Everett Millais — "Ophelia" (1851–2)** — Pre-Raphaelite; Tate Britain's most popular work; Elizabeth Siddal as model. **Deep PD; Tate explicitly hosts as PD.** This is the single most-recognized Shakespeare painting in existence and the canonical Ophelia visual.
- **Pre-Raphaelite Shakespeare more broadly** — Dante Gabriel Rossetti, Holman Hunt, Arthur Hughes ("Ophelia," 1852), J. W. Waterhouse ("Miranda," "Ophelia"), Edmund Blair Leighton. **All PD.** Pre-Raphaelite-jewel palette is the canonical *romantic* register.
- **George Cruikshank, Robert Smirke, John Gilbert** — Victorian Shakespeare illustrators; **PD.**
- **Howard Pyle** (1853–1911) — American illustrator; some Shakespeare; **PD.**
- **First Folio woodcuts and early-quarto title-page art** — **PD.**

**Recommendation:** The LoreVault Shakespeare visual register should be a layered Boydell + Fuseli + Blake + Pre-Raphaelite stack. This gives us a unified, coherent, ~1780–1900 painted visual language that is:
- **Deep PD** (no clearance work)
- **Already canonical** (every Shakespeare reader has seen these images even if they don't know it)
- **Distinguishable** from every modern film adaptation (we look like a museum, not a movie)
- **Lampblack-rich** (Fuseli + Blake supply the unhuman / supernatural register that the other Universes need)

## 6. LoreVault Original — The META-UNIVERSE Proposal

**The key claim:** Shakespeare is not a Universe in the same shape as Greek Myth, Wonderland, EK, or Baker Street. **Shakespeare is the cross-Universe template that the other four Universes already echo.**

Evidence — every Series-1 Universe already has a Shakespeare-shadow:

1. **Hamlet → Baker Street** — "the prince who cannot act until he has *deduced* the murder." Holmes is the Hamlet who solved the procrastination by professionalizing the deduction. Lampblack: skull on the desk (Hamlet's Yorick → Holmes's bookshelf).
2. **Lady Macbeth → Greek Myth** — Klytemnestra's shadow. Both: queen who arms her husband for murder, then unravels. The Argive-bath scene and the sleepwalking scene are the same dramaturgical beat 2,000 years apart.
3. **Prospero → Wonderland** — the white-bearded reality-bender on his island/garden, who controls the weather and the creatures and finally lets the dream end. Prospero's "we are such stuff as dreams are made on" is the Alice-frame two centuries early.
4. **The Three Witches → Eastern-Kingdom (EK) Stepmother-shadow** — the three-faced female prophecy-figure / hag-trio is the same archetype as the wicked-stepmother / Baba-Yaga node. Lampblack: cauldron is portable.
5. **Falstaff → (any tavern Universe)** — the drinker-mentor banished by the heir. This is the universal "letting-the-old-friend-down" Spine.
6. **Romeo & Juliet → any feud-Universe** — the lovers-across-the-line is the most cross-portable Spine in literature.
7. **Caliban → colonial-other archetype across all Universes** — the "this island's mine" claim haunts every settler/explorer Pane.

**The Lattice reading:** Shakespeare is the **diagonal thread** in the Lattice. Every other Pane has Shakespeare-shadow figures already; LoreVault didn't put them there, the canon did. We surface them.

**Proposed mechanic:** "Shakespearean tether" — a special card/relation type. A Holmes card with a Shakespearean tether reveals the Hamlet-shadow underneath. A Klytemnestra card tethered to Lady Macbeth shows the lineage. The tether is the gameplay manifestation of the Lattice.

This is the LoreVault original contribution: **Shakespeare as the canon's own Rosetta Stone, rendered as a gameplay layer.**

## 8. Audience Legibility

- **Mass:** extreme. Shakespeare is the single most-recognized author in any language. Hamlet, Romeo, Juliet, Macbeth, the witches, Ophelia, "to be or not to be," the skull — all global. There is no demographic floor under which awareness drops below ~80% in literate populations.
- **Specialist:** very deep. PhD-level Shakespeare scholarship is a planet-scale industry; the RSC, Folger, Globe, Shakespeare's Birthplace Trust, Bardolatry circuits provide a high-spend, high-engagement specialist tier.
- **Cultural pre-loads:**
  - **2024: Shakespeare's 460th birthday** — global anniversary content cycle.
  - **2023: First Folio 400th anniversary** — Folger, British Library, RSC all ran year-long programs.
  - **Ongoing film cycle** — Coen *Tragedy of Macbeth* (2021), Spielberg *West Side Story* (2021), revolving RSC/NT Live captures.
  - **Theatre-reopening narrative post-COVID** — Globe, Broadway, RSC all framed reopening as Shakespeare-led.
  - **K-12 / undergrad curriculum** — Shakespeare is mandatory in nearly every English-language school system, providing recurrent generational inflow.
- **Risk:** "Shakespeare fatigue" / Bardolatry-skepticism among younger audiences who associate the author with school-coercion. Counter: the META-UNIVERSE framing means we never *brand* Shakespeare; we surface him as the connective tissue under figures the audience already loves.

## 9. Recommendation — FORK (ODIN ESCALATION)

Three options. CEO/Council must pick before Series-1 Lattice locks.

**(a) META-UNIVERSE — Shakespeare as cross-Universe tether layer (RECOMMENDED).**
- Shakespeare figures appear as *shadow-lines* under figures in the existing five Universes (Hamlet under Holmes, Klytemnestra under Lady Macbeth, etc.). Special "Shakespearean tether" card type makes the cross-link visible and tradable.
- *Pros:* matches LoreVault's Lattice frame perfectly; Shakespeare's defining quality across 400 years has been *figure-portability*; gives us a unique gameplay primitive no competitor has; uses Shakespeare's universal recognition as glue, not as a separate SKU.
- *Cons:* requires the Lattice to be designed *with* the tether layer from day one; can't bolt on later cleanly.

**(b) Own Universe — ship as full Pane in Series 4+.**
- *Pros:* simplest IP story; massive figure inventory; Boydell + Pre-Raphaelite register gives us a beautiful coherent look.
- *Cons:* Shakespeare-as-own-Pane fights against what makes Shakespeare *Shakespeare* — figure-portability. We'd be the seventh-millionth product to ship "the Shakespeare collection." Nothing distinctive.

**(c) Cross-cutting Shell — "STAGE" shell.**
- Any figure from any Pane can be re-skinned in costumed-theatrical Shakespeare register. Holmes-as-Hamlet, Alice-as-Miranda, Achilles-as-Hotspur cards.
- *Pros:* highest gameplay novelty; fan-favorite mashup format.
- *Cons:* legibility risk — audience may not understand whether they're collecting Holmes or Hamlet; brand-confusion; harder to price.

**Strong recommendation: (a) META-UNIVERSE.** Shakespeare's 400-year survival mechanism is precisely figure-portability; the META-UNIVERSE reading turns that into a LoreVault-native game mechanic. (c) is a downstream feature that can sit on top of (a). (b) is the safe-but-undistinctive default.

**ODIN ESCALATION:** This decision changes the Series-1 Lattice topology and the card schema (tether card type, shadow-line metadata). Cannot be deferred past Series-1 lock. Needs CEO + Council sign-off.

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

Shakespeare is PD but not neutral. Any LoreVault execution must navigate:

- **Antisemitism in Merchant of Venice (Shylock).** "Hath not a Jew eyes" is the most-anthologized humanizing speech in literature; the trial scene is the most-cited example of Christian-supremacist legal violence in literature. Both readings are live and the play is regularly de-listed in school districts. Recommendation: Shylock can ship as a Spine figure with a Lampblack of *forced conversion / "the pound of flesh extracted from the merchant himself."* Avoid the hat-and-gabardine cartoon register; use the dignified Boydell-period painted register.
- **Race in Othello.** Othello's Blackness is structural to the play's tragedy. Centuries of blackface performance (Olivier 1965 included) are the *locked* IP nightmare and the cultural-violence record. Recommendation: ship Othello cards using painted-register figures painted by Black artists where possible, or use period-painted register with explicit acknowledgment. Iago is the figure to lean into for the villain-Spine; Othello himself should be the dignified-tragic-Spine, not a costume.
- **Caliban + The Tempest's colonial frame.** "This island's mine, by Sycorax my mother" is one of the most-cited anti-colonial speeches in English. Caliban must ship as the *speaker of the curse,* not as the monster of Prospero's gaze. The Lampblack here is *the island's prior owner, dispossessed.*
- **Gender violence.** Taming of the Shrew, the smothering of Desdemona, Lavinia in Titus Andronicus, the Ophelia narrative-arc, Lady Macbeth's "unsex me here," the marriage-coercion in MND. Many of these are flatly unstageable in modern register without re-framing. Recommendation: lead with the *agency* readings (Rosalind, Beatrice, Portia, Viola, Paulina) and treat the Lavinia/Desdemona/Ophelia register as tragic-Spine with explicit compassion-context, not as collectible-violence.
- **Globe-as-imperial-export.** The British-Council-Shakespeare global-soft-power story is real and has produced legitimate decolonization critique. Recommendation: in geographic packs, include non-Anglo Shakespeare register (Kurosawa-shadowed, Vishal Bhardwaj-shadowed, *Hamlet*-in-Arabic-translation tradition, etc.) as visible nodes — *but only via the painted-PD register or via our own commissioned art,* never via the locked films.
