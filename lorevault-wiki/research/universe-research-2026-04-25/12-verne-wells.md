# LoreVault Universe Research — Verne / Wells Classic SF

**Candidate Universe:** Verne–Wells Proto-SF (Voyages Extraordinaires + Scientific Romances)
**Researcher slot:** VERNE/WELLS CLASSIC SF
**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Recommendation (TL;DR):** **STRONG candidate, Series 3+ slot.** This is the single cleanest, deepest PD canvas LoreVault has access to for the retro-future / Steampunk register — Verne is globally PD, Wells is fully US-PD for everything that matters, and there is a parallel **canonical PD illustration register** (Riou, Benett, Goble, Alvim Corrêa, Frank R. Paul) that gives the Universe a visual signature no licensee owns. The minefield is narrow and well-mapped: Disney's 1954 Nautilus, Pal's 1953 Tripod and 1960 Time Machine, the 2003 LXG film, and the soft "Steampunk-as-genre" silhouette. Avoid those specifics; use the 19th-century engraved register; ship clean.

## 2. PD Status

**Verne (1828–1905):** All major works deep PD globally. Every novel cited above was published 1864–1905 — out of US copyright by 1981 at the latest under the 95-years-from-publication rule, and PD in life-plus-70 jurisdictions since 1976.

**Wells (1866–1946):** Fully PD in US for every pre-1930 work. Specifically:
- *The Time Machine* (1895) — US PD since 1990s; life+70 PD since 2017.
- *The Island of Doctor Moreau* (1896) — same.
- *The Invisible Man* (1897) — same.
- *The War of the Worlds* (1898) — same; explicitly cited by Public Domain Review and confirmed by 2025 Amazon adaptation.
- *When the Sleeper Wakes* (1899) and revised *The Sleeper Awakes* (1910) — both US PD; Project Gutenberg hosts both.
- *The First Men in the Moon* (1901) — US PD (pre-1931 publication).

**UNCLEAR / NARROW:** *The Shape of Things to Come* (1933 novel) — under the URAA-restored 95-year rule, US copyright potentially runs through **2028**. **Avoid the 1933 novel and its specific terms (Wings Over the World, Modern State, Everytown-as-named) until 2029.** The *concepts* (post-war reconstruction, scientific dictatorship) are not protectable, but the named entities are. The 1936 Korda **film** is a separate copyright we treat as locked (see §3).

In life+70 jurisdictions (EU/UK/Canada-now-life+70), all Wells works PD since **1 January 2017**. In life+50 holdouts (Canada pre-2022, NZ): cleared earlier.

Treat **everything Verne** and **everything Wells published before 1 January 1931** as fully clear LoreVault canvas.

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield

The PD source is clean; the **specific 20th-century film/comic re-imaginings are not.** Avoid:

1. **Disney's 1954 *20,000 Leagues Under the Sea* — Harper Goff's Nautilus design.** This is the most consequential lock in the Universe. Goff's Victorian-iron-rivet, cigar-with-ridged-spine, viewing-saloon-with-grille-iris **silhouette is the world's recognised "Nautilus"** and remains under Disney copyright/registered industrial design through Disneyland's *Submarine Voyage* and merchandising. **Do not** draw a Nautilus that reads as Goff's. Use Verne's textual description (cigar-shape, double-hull, electric, length 70m) rendered through the **Riou/Neuville 1870 Hetzel engravings** instead — those are the canonical PD silhouette and predate Disney by 84 years.
2. **James Mason's Captain Nemo (1954)** — specific look (white tunic, peaked cap, long beard, organ-playing). Avoid the Mason-Nemo specifically; render Nemo as Verne wrote him (Indian / Sikh prince, dark-skinned per Verne's *Mysterious Island* reveal — see §10 for the right way to handle).
3. **Disney's *Atlantis: The Lost Empire* (2001)** — Mike Mignola-styled Nautilus-adjacent submersibles ("Ulysses"); avoid that visual register.
4. **Pal's 1953 *War of the Worlds* Tripod** — the manta-ray-with-cyclops-eye-on-cobra-neck design. Iconic but specific to George Pal Productions / Paramount.
5. **Spielberg's 2005 *War of the Worlds* Tripod** — the slimmer organic-tentacled design with the howling foghorn. Paramount/DreamWorks.
6. **Pal's 1960 *Time Machine* — the velvet-saddle, brass-dial, spinning-disc-behind-the-seat machine; the green Morlock with white hair and red eyes.** William Tuttle's Morlock mask design is MGM-owned. *The text describes Morlocks as "ape-like, white-furred, large pale eyes."* Use the text + the Pal-machine-shaped-but-not-identical principle.
7. **2002 Wachowski/Wells *Time Machine*** — Jeremy Irons' Über-Morlock; specific.
8. **1956 Cantinflas *Around the World in 80 Days*** — the balloon (which is **not in Verne's novel** — Verne's Fogg never travels by balloon; the balloon was added by the 1956 film and has been the visual default ever since). **Do not put Fogg in a balloon.** This is the most common derivative-trap in the entire Verne canon.
9. **2004 Jackie Chan *Around the World in 80 Days*** — specific cast.
10. **Alan Moore / Kevin O'Neill *League of Extraordinary Gentlemen* (1999–)** — Captain Nemo as turbaned Sikh prince with scimitar, the *specific* re-framing as "Prince Dakkar" given a hard ethnic/aesthetic identity. The PD source supports a Sikh/Indian Nemo (Verne reveals it in *Mysterious Island*); avoid Moore's *specific* silhouette and naming patterns ("Janni Dakkar," etc.).
11. **2003 *LXG* film** — Sean Connery-Allan-Quatermain-led ensemble; specific cast.
12. **Things to Come (1936, Korda)** — William Cameron Menzies's Modernist Everytown architecture, the white-cone-helmet "Wings Over the World" pilots. Korda film copyright + URAA-restored. Do not reference the film silhouettes; the 1933 *novel* is itself URAA-trapped through 2028.
13. **Steampunk-as-genre cosplay aesthetic** — broadly inspired by Verne/Wells, **no single rightsholder**, but the *brass-goggles-on-leather-aviator-hat* clichés are the visual exhaust of decades of derivative work and read as 1990s/2000s Burning Man rather than as 1870s Hetzel-Verne. Steer the LoreVault register **older and primmer** than Steampunk-cosplay — toward the actual 19th-century engraved source.
14. **Doctor Who's Time Lord-as-Time-Traveller-shadow** — the BBC-owned specific iconography (TARDIS, sonic screwdriver, regeneration). Wells's Time Traveller is the *shadow* the Doctor casts; we get the original.

**ODIN ESCALATION (see §11).**

## 4. Pre-Modern Canonical PD Illustration Register

This section is the single most valuable finding for Series 3+. There is a **parallel register of PD illustration** that *is* the canonical visual identity — predating every locked film. Use it as the LoreVault Universe's primary visual source.

1. **Édouard Riou (1833–1900)** — first and most recognised illustrator of the Voyages Extraordinaires. Illustrated *Five Weeks in a Balloon*, *Journey to the Centre of the Earth* (the canonical Lidenbrock-and-Hans engravings), *Captain Hatteras*, *Children of Captain Grant*, **and the first eleven chapters of *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea*** before Alphonse de Neuville completed the rest. Riou died 1900 — **deeply PD globally.** The Riou *Journey* engravings of the underground sea, the prehistoric beasts, and the descent are *the* image.
2. **Alphonse de Neuville (1835–1885)** — completed *20,000 Leagues* with Riou; gave us the canonical Nautilus saloon, Captain Nemo at the organ, the Atlantis ruins, the giant squid attack. **Deep PD.**
3. **Léon Benett (1839–1917)** — *Around the World in 80 Days* (51 engravings — the canonical Fogg/Passepartout/Aouda/Fix imagery), *The Begum's Fortune*, *The Steam House*, *Robur the Conqueror*. **Deep PD.**
4. **Émile Bayard, Jules Férat, A. de Bar, L. Dumont** — secondary Hetzel illustrators across the *Voyages Extraordinaires* corpus. The Hetzel publishing house commissioned ~4,000 illustrations across the 54-volume series, **all PD.**
5. **Warwick Goble** — *Pearson's Magazine* serial illustrations for *War of the Worlds* (1897, predating book publication). The first visual Tripod ever rendered. **PD (Goble died 1943, life+70 since 2014; US-PD as pre-1929 publication).**
6. **Henrique Alvim Corrêa (1876–1910)** — 132 illustrations for the 1906 Belgian luxury edition (French translation by Davray) of *La Guerre des Mondes*. Wells himself praised them: *"Alvim Corrêa did more for my work with his brush than I with my pen."* **The most cinematically powerful Tripod imagery ever made — predates Pal by 47 years and Spielberg by 99.** Deeply PD (Corrêa died 1910). **This is the LoreVault Tripod register.**
7. **Frank R. Paul (1884–1963)** — 38 *Amazing Stories* covers Apr 1926 – Jun 1929, including the **August 1927 *War of the Worlds* cover** (Tripods over a city, the foundational pulp-SF image). All Paul covers from **1926–1929 are US-PD** (pre-1930 publication, no copyright renewal on most pulp work). Paul's later 1930s/40s work is **partially** PD (renewal-dependent) — UNCLEAR per cover; default to the 1926–1929 set.
8. **A.C. Michael, J. Allen St. John, and other 1900s–1920s pulp illustrators of Wells/Verne adaptations** — case-by-case, but the dominant body is PD.

**Recommendation: the Riou + Neuville + Benett + Goble + Alvim Corrêa + Paul-1926-29 set is the LoreVault Universe's canonical visual register.** No existing rightsholder owns this combined register. It pre-dates Disney by 60+ years. It reads as *actually Victorian SF* rather than *cosplay-Steampunk*. **This is the unfair advantage of the Universe.**

## 5. Top 5 Figures with Spine + Lampblack

Lampblack = the single graphic mark that signs the figure across the lattice.

1. **Captain Nemo** — *Spine:* the lost prince whose hatred-of-empire is so total he has built an extension of himself (the Nautilus) and lives outside nations; speaks every language, plays Bach, weeps at sunken ships. *Lampblack:* **the Nautilus N monogram** carved on every door, plate, napkin, and the captain's signet — Verne explicit; canonical engraving register.
2. **The Time Traveller** — *Spine:* the unnamed scientific gentleman who *returned and left again* — the only figure in the canon who is mostly absence. *Lampblack:* **the spinning brass time-dial** (Verne-precursor lab-instrument, not the Pal-velvet-saddle); or the white-quartz lever.
3. **Phileas Fogg** — *Spine:* the wager, the watch, the precise crossing-of-the-globe; the Englishman who defines Englishness *by leaving England* and proving it travels. *Lampblack:* **the open pocket-watch** at 8:45 PM Saturday at the Reform Club; or the round-the-world ticket-stub register.
4. **Doctor Moreau** — *Spine:* the vivisectionist who tried to make beasts into men and made priests instead; the Sayer-of-the-Law is his real artwork. *Lampblack:* **the surgical scalpel + the Beast-Mark** (a stitched cross-shape on a furred shoulder).
5. **Griffin (the Invisible Man)** — *Spine:* the discovery cost him everything but visibility; rage-driven, freezing, alone, hated. *Lampblack:* **the bandage-wrap with one dark goggle-lens** showing through; or the empty footprint-in-snow.

Universe-set glyph candidates: **the Nautilus N**, the Riou-engraved spinning brass dial, the cracked Cavorite sphere.

## 6. LoreVault Original — Unique Expressions + Cross-Tethers

LoreVault's job is not to re-render the canon; it is to **cross-tether** the canon to other Universes such that the lattice tightens and the lampblack reads. Five originals:

1. **The Lincoln Island Convergence** (Mysterious Island as universe-junction) — Verne himself made the Mysterious Island the convergence point for *In Search of the Castaways*, *20,000 Leagues*, and the original castaway plot. **LoreVault uses Lincoln Island as the cross-tether anchor for the Verne-Wells Universe** — the place where Nemo dies and where Eloi-future-Earth could be glimpsed. This is *PD-source-justified* world-mechanic and has no derivative-rights cost.
2. **Time Traveller ↔ Wonderland Alice** as **portal-fall figures** — both pass through a threshold (the time-lever / the rabbit-hole), both meet a strange caste-society that looks innocent but eats its own (Eloi/Morlocks; Queen's croquet/tarts), both *return changed and leave again.* Cross-tether: the Time Traveller's second departure is into Wonderland.
3. **Captain Nemo ↔ Gothic-Horror rebel-king** — Nemo as the **scientific** echo of the Byronic / Frankensteinian outcast; the Nautilus as a *horizontal* castle-on-the-sea; cross-tether to Frankenstein: both create monsters (the Creature; the Nautilus itself), both flee Europe to the polar/oceanic margins.
4. **Doctor Moreau in CYBER shell** — Moreau is the bio-tech monster-maker; cross-tether forward to any future cyberpunk Universe as the *grandfather of the splice*; the Sayer-of-the-Law becomes the prototype "AI repeating human values it doesn't understand."
5. **Cavor & Bedford on the Moon as proto-AETHER** — the Selenite hive-society is the original "post-human optimised civilisation" trope. Cross-tether to whatever AETHER/HIVE Universe ships in Series 4+; Cavor's recorded radio-messages from inside the Moon are the *first AI-as-distant-voice* artefact in literature.
6. (Bonus) **Eloi / Morlock as civilisational-scale Wolf-prey** — cross-tether to EK's predator/prey dynamic at scale of millennia rather than scale of forest.
7. (Bonus) **The Invisible Man as Wonderland-categorical-failure** — Griffin is what happens when a person breaks the *visibility* category Wonderland plays with; cross-tether as the *scientific* analogue to Cheshire-Cat-fade.

## 9. Recommendation

**STRONG Universe candidate. Recommend Series 3 slot.**

Open with **either** *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea* **or** *The Year 802,701* (Time Machine future-Earth) as the inaugural set-anchor. Argue for *20,000 Leagues* opening:

1. Captain Nemo is the single strongest figure in the entire Verne-Wells canon — equal to Holmes, Dracula in name-recognition.
2. The Nautilus is a self-contained world (a cross-tether-friendly mobile location).
3. The Mysterious Island convergence gives natural lattice-bridges into the rest of the Wells side.
4. The PD illustration register (Riou + Neuville) is at peak strength for this novel.
5. The derivative-rights minefield is *narrow and well-mapped* — avoid Goff's Disney silhouette and James Mason's specific look; use the 1870 Hetzel engravings as the visual anchor.

**Series 3 expansion path:** open with *20,000 Leagues* (Common-to-Legendary set mostly Nemo + Nautilus + the voyage), then drop *The Year 802,701* (Time Machine + Eloi + Morlocks + Weena) as the second set, then *War of the Worlds* (Tripods + Heat-Ray + Martians) as the **Legendary-tier-anchor** third set leaning hard on the Alvim Corrêa register (which no rightsholder has used) for visual differentiation.

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

Two distinct loads:

**Verne — colonial-era racial framings.** *Around the World in 80 Days* contains a sati-rescue plot (Aouda) that participates in the British-colonial-saviour narrative; Passepartout's racial-comic register is dated; *The Mysterious Island* names Pencroff's Black servant Neb in a way that requires care. *Robur the Conqueror* has a Black servant Frycollin played for cowardly comic relief — **avoid Frycollin entirely.** Aouda is the trickiest figure: in the novel she becomes Fogg's wife and is treated with dignity but the rescue framing is extractive. **Recommendation:** include Aouda only if rendered as a full agent-of-her-own-story (Verne's text supports this — she is Parsi, Cambridge-educated in the novel, and the marriage is her initiative). Do not render the sati pyre scene.

**Verne — Captain Nemo as PoC.** Verne reveals in *The Mysterious Island* (1874) that Nemo is **Prince Dakkar, son of an Indian raja, son of the deposed Rao of Bundelkhand, anti-British rebel of the 1857 uprising.** This is *the* PD-source canonical Nemo. Disney/Mason whitewashed him in 1954. **LoreVault should render Nemo as Verne wrote him — South Asian, anti-imperial, dark-skinned, turbaned — using the 1874 Hetzel engravings (which depict him so) as the primary visual reference.** This is one of the clearest cases in the canon of PD-source recovery being *more* representative than the 20th-century film register. (Be explicit but careful: avoid the Alan Moore *LXG* specific re-framing.)

**Wells — paternalism toward "savages."** *Time Machine*'s Eloi-as-childlike framing reads as soft-eugenics-adjacent at scale; *Doctor Moreau*'s Beast-People + the Sayer-of-the-Law have a colonial-mission undercurrent ("teach the savage the Law"). *The War of the Worlds* opens with Wells comparing the Martian invasion to British colonisation of Tasmania ("are we such apostles of mercy as to complain if the Martians warred in the same spirit?") — **this is one of the most morally sophisticated passages in the canon and should be quoted, not avoided.**

**Wells — antisemitism / eugenics in later work.** Avoid *Anticipations* (1901) — contains explicit eugenic and antisemitic passages. Don't use it as a source, even though it is technically PD.

**General rule:** lean into the PD-source-canonical rendering of Nemo (Sikh/Indian prince) — that is *more representative* than any film. Avoid the Aouda sati scene, the Frycollin character, and *Anticipations*.

## 11. ODIN ESCALATION

Two ongoing brand-exploitation flags requiring Odin desk review:

1. **Disney's continuing 20,000 Leagues IP exploitation.** Disney has continuously extended the 1954 Goff-Nautilus into Disneyland's *Submarine Voyage* (1959–1998), Disneyland Paris's *Les Mystères du Nautilus* (1994–present), the Tokyo DisneySea park (2001–present, with a Nemo's Lair attraction featuring the Goff Nautilus), Disney+ *Nautilus* TV series (announced 2022, premiered AMC 2024 after Disney shelved), and the *Atlantis: The Lost Empire* franchise (2001–2003) which uses Nautilus-adjacent design language. **Risk:** Disney's lawyers monitor the Nautilus visual space aggressively. **Mitigation:** the Riou/Neuville 1870 Hetzel engravings are 84 years older than Goff and are visibly distinct (no viewing-saloon iris-grille; different hull articulation; the engraved register is line-art not photographic). **Use the 1870 source. Do not under any circumstances use Goff's silhouette as visual reference.**

2. **Universal's Wells holdings — "Dark Universe" residual claim.** Universal owns the 1933 Claude Rains *Invisible Man* film and the Whale-era horror-monster IP and has repeatedly tried to relaunch a "Dark Universe" franchise (2017 Tom Cruise *Mummy*, 2020 Leigh Whannell *Invisible Man*). Universal does **not** own Wells — Wells is PD — but Universal owns the *specific* Claude-Rains-era and Elisabeth-Moss-era visual treatments. **Risk:** any modernised LoreVault Griffin that reads as 1933-Whale or 2020-Whannell will draw a polite letter. **Mitigation:** render Griffin from Wells's 1897 text directly — albino chemist, dark goggles, false nose, bandages, Iping village setting. The text is older than Universal's first Griffin film by 36 years.

3. **Paramount's *War of the Worlds* film rights** are continuing — 1953 Pal, 2005 Spielberg, 2025 Amazon (Paramount/MGM). Paramount's *War of the Worlds* claims attach to specific Tripod designs and to specific film-setting transpositions (Pal: 1950s LA; Spielberg: 2000s New Jersey; 2025: screenlife). They do **not** attach to Wells's text or to pre-1953 illustration. **Mitigation:** the Alvim Corrêa 1906 + Goble 1897 + Frank R. Paul 1927 register predates every Paramount Tripod and is fully PD. **Use the Alvim Corrêa Tripod as the canonical LoreVault Tripod.**

**Net Odin posture:** moderate caution, well-mapped. The 19th-century PD illustration register is a *complete* end-run around all three rightsholder claims and gives the LoreVault Universe its visual differentiation. The minefield is real but it is narrow and named.
