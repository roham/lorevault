## 2. Public-Domain Source Status

- **Grimm Brothers** (Jacob d.1863, Wilhelm d.1859). All seven editions of *Kinder- und Hausmärchen* (1812 1st ed → 1857 7th "Großen Ausgabe") are in the **public domain worldwide**; copyright lapsed many decades ago because the authors died well over 100 years ago. ([Public Domain Review on Grimm](https://publicdomainreview.org/essay/the-forgotten-tales-of-the-brothers-grimm/); [Project Gutenberg #2591](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/2591))
- **Charles Perrault** (d.1703). *Histoires ou contes du temps passé* (1697) — 8 stories incl. Cinderella, Sleeping Beauty, Red Riding Hood, Puss in Boots, Bluebeard, Tom Thumb, Diamonds and Toads, The Fairies. Deep PD in every jurisdiction.
- **Hans Christian Andersen** (d.1875). All 150+ tales published 1835–1874. **Worldwide PD** — author dead more than 100 years; the original Danish texts and standard 19th-c. translations are unencumbered. Note: any *new* English translation (e.g. a 1990 Tiina Nunnally rendering) carries its own copyright on the translation, not the underlying tale. We must source either the Danish original or pre-1929 English translations.

**LoreVault rule**: when adapting an Andersen text, cite either the Danish original or a translation published before 1930 (Andrew Lang, H.W. Dulcken, Mrs. H.B. Paull). For Grimm, use Margaret Hunt 1884 or Edgar Taylor 1823 — both deep PD.

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield (Disney + adjacent)

This is the section that earns its keep. Disney's expression of public-domain tales is **separately copyrighted** and Disney is one of the most aggressive enforcers in IP history. We avoid every named element below. Trademark on character names persists indefinitely; copyright on each film extends ~95 years from publication under current US law.

### Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs (Disney, 1937)
- Enters US PD **January 1, 2033** ([Catalyst Legal](https://catalystlegal.org/snow-white-public-domain-still-protected-copyright/); [Trademarkia](https://www.trademarkia.com/news/copyrights/is-snow-white-public-domain)). Until then, **everything Disney added is locked**.
- **The signature dress** — yellow skirt, blue puff-sleeve bodice, red collar, red bow in dark hair. CEO's canonical example. **DO NOT REPLICATE.**
- **The seven dwarfs by name**: Doc, Grumpy, Happy, Sleepy, Bashful, Sneezy, Dopey. **Disney-original 1937**; Grimm's 1812 dwarves are **unnamed**. We may have seven dwarves; we may not name them anything close.
- The specific dwarf visual designs (round-bellied, white-bearded, color-coded caps).
- The specific apple shape (deep red, single leaf, purple cauldron drip, hag-witch hands).
- The specific magic mirror frame and the masked smoke-face that speaks from it.
- "Heigh-Ho" / "Whistle While You Work" / "Someday My Prince Will Come" (music PD until 2033 in US).
- Trademark continues past 2033 on Disney logos and packaging.

### Cinderella (Disney, 1950)
- Enters US PD **2046**.
- Specific powder-blue ballgown silhouette and tiara.
- **Fairy Godmother** as Disney drew her — plump, gray-bunned, "Bibbidi-Bobbidi-Boo" (1948 Mack David / Al Hoffman / Jerry Livingston, separately copyrighted).
- **Named mice**: Jaq, Gus (Octavius), Suzy, Perla, Bert, Mert. Mice exist in Perrault — they are turned into horses for the carriage — but **named, anthropomorphic, personality-coded mice are Disney**. ([Disney Cinderella character list](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Disney's_Cinderella_characters))
- **Lucifer** the calico cat — Disney-original 1950, modeled on Ward Kimball's house cat. ([Walt Disney Family Museum](https://www.waltdisney.org/blog/going-back-fairy-tale-five-animators-cinderella))
- The pumpkin coach with the specific Disney sculpt.
- Stepsister names Anastasia and Drizella in their Disney designs.

### Sleeping Beauty (Disney, 1959)
- Enters US PD **2055**.
- **Aurora** — Disney named her after the Tchaikovsky ballet princess; Perrault leaves her unnamed; Grimm calls her *Dornröschen* / Briar Rose. Use **Briar Rose** (Grimm-PD) or the unnamed **Princess** (Perrault-PD), never "Aurora."
- **Maleficent** — Disney-original name and design (horned headdress, black-and-purple robe, raven Diablo, green flame). The fairy is **unnamed** in Perrault and Grimm. We can have a witch-fairy antagonist; we cannot call her Maleficent or draw her with horns + green fire. ([Sleeping Beauty Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sleeping_Beauty_(1959_film)))
- **Three good fairies named Flora, Fauna, Merryweather** — Disney-original. Perrault's fairies are unnamed and number seven; Grimm has thirteen wise women. We use thirteen wise women.
- Disney's specific Aurora design (gold gown OR pink gown, golden flowing hair).
- 2014 *Maleficent* live-action: Angelina Jolie's specific cheekbone-prosthetic design and the "true love's kiss is the mother's, not the prince's" plot inversion are protected as a separate Disney work.

### The Little Mermaid (Disney, 1989)
- Enters US PD **2085**. Andersen 1837 is fully PD, but Disney's adds are locked. ([Thorntons on Little Mermaid copyright](https://www.thorntons-law.co.uk/knowledge/copyright-extension-and-the-little-mermaid))
- **Ariel** — Disney-original name (Andersen's mermaid is unnamed); red hair, green tail, purple shell bra. Avoid the entire silhouette.
- **Sebastian** the Jamaican-accented crab, **Flounder** the yellow-blue fish, **Scuttle** the seagull — all Disney-original characters not in Andersen.
- **Ursula** — Disney-original name and design (tentacled lavender sea-witch with white pageboy, modeled on drag performer Divine). Andersen's sea-witch is gaunt, lives in a coral house, and is morally ambiguous, not villainous.
- The "happy ending" — Andersen's mermaid dissolves into sea-foam and ascends as a daughter of the air; Disney gives her marriage and legs. We **must use the Andersen ending** to stay clear and to honor the doctrine of melancholy.

### Beauty and the Beast (Disney, 1991)
- Enters US PD **2087**.
- **Lumière** (candelabra), **Cogsworth** (mantel clock), **Mrs. Potts** (teapot), **Chip** (teacup), **Wardrobe**, **Featherduster** — *all Disney-original sentient-object characters*. Villeneuve 1740 / Beaumont 1756 has **no enchanted-object servants**. ([Disney B&B characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Disney's_Beauty_and_the_Beast_characters))
- **Gaston** — Disney-original villain. The 1740 source has no rival suitor.
- **Belle** as a name is Disney's; the original is *La Belle* ("Beauty," generic). In French this is borderline-generic, but courts have ruled Disney's specific Belle (yellow ballgown, brown bun, blue village dress) is protected. ([Courthouse News on B&B copyright case](https://www.courthousenews.com/jurors-deliberate-in-beauty-and-the-beast-copyright-case/))
- **The Beast's specific design** — buffalo horns, lion's mane, wolf's head, gorilla body, blue cape. 1740 Beast is "elephantine" with a trunk in the source description; closer to a tapir. Lean into the source description, away from Disney.

### Tangled (Disney, 2010)
- Enters US PD **~2106**.
- **Pascal** the chameleon, **Maximus** the horse, **Flynn Rider** — all Disney-original (screenwriter Dan Fogelman). ([Pascal and Maximus Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pascal_and_Maximus))
- **Mother Gothel** — Disney's name for the witch (Grimm calls her *Frau Gothel* in some translations — UNCLEAR if Disney's name is original or adopted; safer to use **Dame Gothel** from older Edgar Taylor translation, or simply **the Enchantress**).
- **Glowing healing hair** — Disney-added. Grimm's hair is just hair-as-rope.

### Frozen (Disney, 2013) — applied to Andersen's Snow Queen
- Enters US PD **~2109**.
- **Elsa**, **Anna**, **Olaf**, **Sven**, **Kristoff**, **Hans** — Disney-original names and designs.
- **Sister-protagonist framing** — Disney-original. Andersen's pair is **Gerda and Kai**, friends-as-siblings, not blood sisters. Use Gerda + Kai.
- **The Snow Queen as protagonist** — Andersen's Snow Queen is the antagonist. We restore her to villain.
- **"Let It Go," reindeer-as-comic-relief, snowman-buddy** — all locked.

### Wicked (Universal/Maguire, 1995 novel; 2003 musical; 2024 film)
- Maguire's *Wicked* and the Schwartz/Holzman musical are **separately copyrighted** derivatives of Baum's PD *Oz*. ([UBC IP Law on Wicked](https://iplaw.allard.ubc.ca/2024/12/04/wicked/))
- This belongs to the Greek-Myth-adjacent *Oz*-PD world rather than to Enchanted Kingdom, but worth a crossover note: if we ever do an Oz-derived Pane, we use Baum 1900–1920 (PD) and avoid Maguire/Schwartz.
- No known specific Snow-White-Wicked crossover film, but: the live-action *Snow White* (Disney 2025, Rachel Zegler) carries fresh derivative copyright on its specific staging — avoid.

### Mirror Mirror (Relativity Media, 2012) and Snow White and the Huntsman (Universal, 2012)
- Both are separately copyrighted Snow White takes. Avoid Eiko Ishioka's specific costume designs (Mirror Mirror) and Charlize Theron's golden-spike Ravenna gown (Huntsman).

### The Witcher / Once Upon a Time / Shrek
- *Shrek* (DreamWorks 2001) — Lord Farquaad's "fairy-tale internment camp" composite is a parody but the specific gags are protected.
- *Once Upon a Time* (ABC 2011–2018) — its specific recombinant casting is locked but heavily Disney-adjacent (it's a Disney property), so doubly avoid.

## 5. Top 5 Spine + Lampblack Candidates

Per the Lattice/Lampblack doctrine, these five carry the Pane:

1. **The Wolf** — already in current Lampblack roster. Oldest archetype here. Tethers Pane to Gothic Horror (werewolf shadow), Wonderland (Cheshire-grin shadow), Greek Myth (Lycaon shadow). Spine glyph: **bared-teeth crescent in forest negative space.**
2. **Snow White** — per Topsy doctrine deploy as **Snow-Black variant** (the corrupted reflection in a smoked mirror). The PD register is the Jüttner 1905 black-bowed dress *not* the Disney yellow-blue-red. Spine: **the bitten apple + the closed eye behind glass.**
3. **The Witch (composite)** — Hexe / Bruja / Hag / Baba — composite of the Hansel & Gretel hut-witch, Snow White's Queen-as-hag transformation, the Sea Witch, and the thirteenth wise woman. Spine: **the iron hearth-hook over fire.** Tethers to Wonderland's Queen of Hearts as a related archetype.
4. **The Stepmother** — the household tyrant; composite of Cinderella's stepmother, the Queen-mother in Snow White, the witch in Rapunzel as adoptive maternal. Tethers to Greek Myth's Furies. Spine: **the looking-glass turned inward.**
5. **The Snow Queen** — chosen over Cinderella because (a) Andersen-only, no Perrault overlap, (b) ice biome opens visual range Cinderella cannot, (c) the *kiss that freezes the heart* is one of the strongest Lampblack-permeable mechanics in canon — direct connective tissue to Greek Myth (Persephone/winter), to Gothic Horror (the cold revenant), to Baker Street (the unsolved disappearance of Kai). Spine: **shard of mirror in the eye + crystal star.**

(Cinderella sits sixth — kept on the bench. Her ash-hearth seals are Lampblack-rich, but her PD-only silhouette is harder to differentiate from Disney without active labor.)

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

**Excluded outright** from any LoreVault expression of this Pane:

- **"The Jew Among Thorns" (KHM 110)**, *Der Jude im Dorn*. Antisemitic by design — Wilhelm Grimm edited successive editions to make the Jewish figure progressively more demeaning. Banned in some elementary-school districts on this basis. ([Wikipedia on The Jew Among Thorns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Jew_Among_Thorns); [De Gruyter chapter "On the Demonising of Jews and Gypsies in Fairy Tales"](https://www.degruyterbrill.com/document/doi/10.1515/9781782381877-010/html?lang=en)). **Permanently excluded.**
- **"The Good Bargain" (KHM 7)** — also contains antisemitic content; **excluded**.
- **Roma/Sinti depictions**: several Grimm tales (and the 1812 footnotes) contain "Zigeuner" stock-villain framing. Where we adapt tales with itinerant figures, we use neutral terms ("travellers," "the wandering musician") and never use the slur. The Bremen Town Musicians is fine; what we avoid is the framing.
- **Cinderella stepsister mutilation** (toes/heels cut to fit the slipper, eyes pecked out by doves at the wedding) — present in Grimm 1812; we keep the *darkness* but soften the eye-pecking specifically because it reads as antisemitic-tinged retribution in some scholarly readings, and because it's gratuitously gore-comic in a way that doesn't earn its keep in collectible form.
- **"Frau Trude"** — the kindling-witch transformation of a curious child is fine; the original framing as a punishment for the child's rebellion against parental authority should be inverted toward agency.

We make this an explicit editorial filter in the Pane's brief sheet.

## ODIN ESCALATION — Disney Overreach Watchlist

Disney has historically asserted broad rights even in genuinely unprotectable territory. Where we anticipate friction, in priority order:

1. **"Dwarf" generic vs "Doc/Grumpy/Happy" specific.** Disney owns the seven *named* dwarves and their specific designs; they do **not** own dwarves-around-a-cottage as a concept. **We use seven unnamed (or numbered) dwarves with Jüttner 1905 silhouettes.** Any cease-and-desist on the generic concept is unwinnable for them — but they may try.
2. **"Princess" trade dress.** Disney has trademarked "Disney Princess" as a brand but **not** "princess" generically. Our princesses must never be marketed as "princesses" in a Disney-tier-style lineup. Use "noblewomen," "the daughters of the Pane," or proper names from PD source.
3. **The fairy godmother concept.** Perrault's *marraine fée* is PD; Disney's specific portly gray-bunned design is not. **Use Doré or Dulac references.** Avoid "Bibbidi-Bobbidi" anywhere near any vocal flourish.
4. **Talking household objects.** This is **Disney 1991 invented expression** — original to *Beauty and the Beast*. Villeneuve 1740 has servants who are *invisible*, not enchanted-into-objects. Any LoreVault Beast Pane keeps the servants invisible (or animal-form), **never enchanted candelabras and clocks.**
5. **Glowing-healing-hair.** *Tangled*-original. Grimm Rapunzel's hair is just hair; her tears restore the prince's sight, not her hair's glow. **Hair = rope/blindfold/tether only.**
6. **Sister-bond Snow Queen.** *Frozen*-original. Use Gerda+Kai (friends-as-near-siblings-but-not-blood).
7. **Specific apple-shape, mirror-frame, spindle-design, glass-slipper-design.** These are tiny but Disney has prevailed in lower-court rulings on cumulative trade-dress similarity. **Brief artists with PD references (Jüttner apple, Doré mirror, Vogel spindle, Crane slipper) explicitly.**
8. **Names that *sound* like Disney's** — Auralea (Aurora), Ariella (Ariel), Bell (Belle). **Banned**, even though arguably defensible. Not a hill to die on.
9. **Live-action 2025 *Snow White*** introduced new specific staging — we have **not** seen it referenced and should not. Treat as locked.
10. **Disney's enforcement appetite is asymmetric vs our budget.** Even a winnable fight is expensive. **Discipline at brief stage is 100x cheaper than litigation.** This document is the brief.
