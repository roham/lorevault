# Cross-Universe Storyline Patterns for LoreVault

**Brief #21 — LoreVault MULTIVERSE-CROSSOVER research**
**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Author:** Multiverse-Crossover Researcher (Claude, on behalf of CEO)
**Doctrine anchored to:** "The Lattice and the Lampblack" (`lorevault-wiki/HANDOVER.md`, `MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md`, `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md`, `SET-ROADMAP.md`)
**Core CEO insight quoted in scope:** *"the most interesting thing is also crossovers between universes — that's where we can create really interesting storylines, similar to what Disney did with Once Upon a Time on Netflix."*

This dossier synthesizes 24 precedents — every meaningful cross-canon property of the last 70 years — into 12-15 implementable patterns, an explicit failure-mode catalog, 18 concrete LoreVault Moments, and an ODIN escalation list. It is opinionated. The single biggest finding: **crossovers must be rare, load-bearing, and disciplined by an Iceberg ratio — never the default mode of card production**. Every property that treated crossovers as the headline collapsed; every property that treated them as the *secret beneath the headline* endured.

## 1. Pattern Catalog — 14 Cross-Universe Storyline Patterns

These are the structural shapes that recur across the 24 precedents. Each pattern names a specific cross-canon move that worked at least once, and identifies the precedent that demonstrates it.

1. **Fugitive Figure** — A figure flees their home Pane and surfaces in another. *Precedent:* Hyde fleeing Soho into Wonderland's underbrush; *Penny Dreadful*'s Dorian Gray hosting Ethan Chandler. Reads as: "Someone you know, somewhere they shouldn't be." Structurally low-cost: only one figure crosses, only one Pane is "polluted."

2. **Shared Villain (Web-Spider)** — A villain is the literal mechanism of cross-Pane convergence. *Precedent:* Dracula in *Anno Dracula* — every other Victorian figure is now defined by their relationship to him. The Dread Pirate Roberts of Lattice-aware antagonists. Moriarty as Lampblack-bankroller across Panes is the LoreVault analog.

3. **Same Object Across Panes (Echo-Object)** — A single artifact appears, transformed, in multiple Universes. *Precedent:* The One Ring of Ash and Power across Tolkien-derived sets; the Sandman's pouch of dream-sand. The deerstalker becomes the Pythia's veil becomes the Cheshire Cat's grin. The object carries Lampblack residue; the figures don't have to meet.

4. **Twin-Soul Recognition (Spine-Mirror)** — Two figures from different Universes recognize each other as the same Spine. *Precedent:* Borges' *Book of Imaginary Beings* explicitly pairs Chinese vs European dragons as the same archetype expressed through different cultural lenses. Persephone meets Snow White; both have died-and-returned; both Spines are *Underworld-Bride*.

5. **Crossover Investigation (Detective-Across-Pane)** — A detective from one Universe investigates a mystery in another, treating the unfamiliar Pane as a foreign jurisdiction. *Precedent:* Holmes vs Cthulhu in pastiche; *Sleepy Hollow* TV's Ichabod-in-modernity inverted. Holmes investigates a Wonderland disappearance: the genre never breaks because the *detective frame* is preserved.

6. **Inverted Encounter (Antagonist-Becomes-Protagonist)** — A canonically-villain figure becomes the POV; the canonical hero becomes adversary. *Precedent:* *Wide Sargasso Sea* (Bertha = protagonist, Rochester = oppressor); *Mary Reilly* (Hyde's housemaid POV); *Foe* (Crusoe's silent companion). The Wolf as protagonist, Red as the threatening huntress is this exact shape.

7. **Ensemble Reveal (Always-In-The-Same-Story)** — Multiple figures from different Universes are revealed to have always been adjacent in the same event. *Precedent:* *League of Extraordinary Gentlemen* Vol. 1 — Mina, Quatermain, Nemo, Jekyll, Griffin always-already-at-the-same-table. The reveal isn't crossover — the *non-revelation that they had been apart* was the lie.

8. **Cross-Pane Letter (Epistolary Bridge)** — A figure writes to a figure in another Pane; the letter passes through Lampblack. *Precedent:* *Anno Dracula*'s Mina-to-Lucy letters across the alternate-timeline gap; *Sandman*'s Death-corresponds-with-mortals trope. Watson writing to Mina compares casebooks across the Doyle/Stoker boundary — both are *epistolary novels in canon*, so the form is native.

9. **Lampblack Trade (Residue-Bleed)** — A *substance* — soot, fog, ash, dust — visibly bleeds from one Pane into another. *Precedent:* *Hellboy*'s "everything seeps through the same crust of folklore"; *Sandman*'s Dreaming as a substance that passes between sleepers regardless of which mythology they belong to. Mechanically the cleanest crossover: no figures cross, only *physics*.

10. **Shell-Shift (Same Figure, Different Era)** — A figure appears wholesale-transposed into a different historical or genre context. *Precedent:* *Marvel 1602* (the X-Men in the Elizabethan era), Marvel's What If, Gaiman's habit. This is a *Parallel* axis move (per `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md`), not a Universe-crossover. Important to distinguish — many naive "crossovers" are actually shell-shifts and should be treated as Parallels, not cross-Universe Moments.

11. **Convergence-Cathedral (Climax-As-Crossover)** — A multi-Universe climactic event where figures from every Universe converge for one fight, one meeting, one ritual. *Precedent:* *Crisis on Infinite Earths* (the apex *and* the warning); *Across the Spider-Verse*'s society scene; *Avengers: Endgame*. The most dangerous pattern — the one that made *Crisis* destroy DC continuity for a generation. **Use sparingly, never as a Set-anchor.**

12. **Visiting-Universe Tour (Disney-Hop)** — A protagonist *tours* other Universes one Pane at a time, returning home between visits. *Precedent:* *Kingdom Hearts*' Sora hopping Disney films; *Sandman*'s Dream visiting Hell, Faerie, Asgard. The tourist structure protects each visited Pane from full integration — the visitor leaves, the Pane closes behind them. Cleaner than ensemble.

13. **Genre-Native Crossover (Same-Form-Different-Canon)** — Crossovers respect the *form* of both source canons (epistolary, court-record, journal-with-marginalia). *Precedent:* *Jonathan Strange & Mr Norrell* using Austen-prose for an alternate-history-of-magic England — the formal register made the magical content *feel canonical to the era*. Watson's casebook + Mina's journal cross because they're both Victorian first-person epistolary forms. Genre-mismatched crossovers (Wonderland nonsense + Greek-myth lyric register) read as parody unless explicitly marked.

14. **Compendium Crossover (Lattice-as-Index)** — The crossover lives in *commentary*, not in narrative — a bestiary, a guidebook, a wiki, an annotated edition. *Precedent:* *Book of Imaginary Beings*; *League of Extraordinary Gentlemen*'s *Black Dossier*; the Lovecraft mythos as compiled by August Derleth. The crossover is implied by the *cataloguer's* ordering, not enacted by figures meeting. Powerful for LoreVault — the wiki itself can be a cross-Pane artifact.

## 2. Card-Mechanical Implementation

For each pattern, the TCG-card or Moment-card expression. These integrate with FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE A12 (Echo flavor texts) and the Iceberg surface/echo/deep tiers from existing LoreVault doctrine.

| Pattern | Card-mechanical expression |
|---|---|
| Fugitive Figure | **Dual-Origin Moment** — primary Universe is the Pane the figure currently inhabits; "Origin" tag names the Pane they fled. Card art shows the figure against the *new* Pane's background; their silhouette retains a residue color from the home Pane. |
| Shared Villain | **Bankroller-tag** — a Legendary villain card has a "Sponsors:" line listing names of figures across other Universes. No mechanical effect required; it generates Echo-text density. |
| Echo-Object | **Cross-Pane Object Moment** — single card shows two image-halves (split-illustration), each side an Echo of the same artifact. Front face: Sherlock's deerstalker. Back face: the Pythia's veil. One card, two Panes, no figure-crossing. |
| Spine-Mirror | **Twin-Soul card** — two Legendary figures from different Universes share a "Spine" tag. A Set sub-mechanic (e.g., a "Mirror Pair" badge) lets collectors identify Spine-pairs across the Set. *Underworld-Bride* badge on Persephone and Snow White. |
| Detective-Across-Pane | **Crossover Investigation Moment** — a 3-card mini-arc within a Set. Card 1: detective declares jurisdiction. Card 2: encounter with foreign-Pane figure. Card 3: solution that cites both Panes' canon. The arc is the storyline; each card is collectable independently. |
| Inverted Encounter | **POV-Flip Moment** — two cards: one labeled "Canon" (e.g., Red Riding Hood), one labeled "Inverse" (the Wolf as protagonist). Same illustration, opposed flavor texts. Mechanical ratio: 1:1 in box, both required for the "Spine" reveal. |
| Ensemble Reveal | **Convergence Set** — a dedicated Set whose entire 20-card composition is cross-Universe. *Carnaval of Echoes* — see Section 9. |
| Cross-Pane Letter | **Epistolary Echo card** — card flavor text is the body of a letter from Pane A; the recipient is in Pane B. The card itself is in *neither* Universe — it's a "Lampblack" classification. Players collect these as a tertiary axis. |
| Lampblack Trade | **Substance card** — no figures, only environment. A card showing London fog drifting into Wonderland; a card showing Greek olive smoke curling into Baker Street's chimneys. These are the **lowest-stakes, highest-density** crossover Moments — should make up the bulk of crossover production. |
| Shell-Shift | **Parallel card, not crossover** — explicitly mark these as the Parallel axis (per existing `MULTIVERSE-SHELLS.md`); do NOT count toward the cross-Universe quota. Crucial discipline: prevents Parallel-bloat masquerading as crossover-richness. |
| Convergence-Cathedral | **Set-Climax Ultimate** — one card per Series, Ultimate-tier, gated behind multi-Universe collection completion. The card depicts a crossover climax. Quantity-controlled: ONE per Series. The scarcity *is* the discipline. |
| Visiting-Universe Tour | **Touring-Figure subset** — a single Lampblacker figure (e.g., the Lattice-walker from `MULTIVERSE-COSMOLOGY-DOCTRINE.md`) appears across 5 Sets as a recurring Echo. The same figure, different Pane each appearance. Reads like a recurring Easter egg. |
| Genre-Native Crossover | **Form-Tag crossover** — crossover Moments require BOTH source canons to share an implicit form (both epistolary, both gothic, both lyric, both detective-procedural). Form-mismatch crossovers (Wonderland-nonsense × Greek-tragedy-meter) are explicitly rejected at design. |
| Compendium Crossover | **Wiki-only crossover** — the LoreVault wiki itself stages cross-Pane discoveries that NEVER appear as cards. Wiki readers find the crossover; collectors don't pay for it. This is free narrative density. |

## 3. Failure-Mode Catalog

Eight specific ways crossover storytelling has BLOATED or FAILED in precedents, paired with the LoreVault discipline that prevents each.

### F1. Once-Upon-a-Time Bloat (Too-Many-Universes-Flatten-Stakes)
*Precedent:* OUaT Seasons 4-7. Adding Frozen, Oz, Wonderland, Camelot, Land of Untold Stories, the Wish Realm — each new Universe diluted the established Enchanted Forest. Season 7 averaged 0.55 in 18-49, vs. Season 1's 4.0. ([Cinemablend](https://www.cinemablend.com/television/2307742/why-once-upon-a-time-is-ending-after-season-7))
*LoreVault discipline:* **Five Universes are LOCKED for Series 1 per `SET-ROADMAP.md`**. Universe-additions only at full Series boundaries, never mid-Series. Net new Universes capped at 1-per-Series after Series 2.

### F2. Phase-4 Marvel Volume-Collapse (Every-Multiverse-Cameo-Is-Empty)
*Precedent:* MCU Phase 4-5. Cameos in *Multiverse of Madness*, *No Way Home*, *Loki* became "Easter eggs without weight." Critical thesis: oversaturation drained specialness from each variant. ([CBR](https://www.cbr.com/marvel-phase-4-too-much/), [Ringer](https://www.theringer.com/marvel-cinematic-universe/2023/2/23/23610103/marvel-studios-phase-5-quantumania-multiverse-saga-superhero-movie-fatigue))
*LoreVault discipline:* **Iceberg ratio applied to crossover**: 1 surface crossover Moment per Set (explained), 2 Echo crossovers (unexplained recognitions), 4 Deep crossovers (gestured-at). Never more than 7 cross-Universe Moments per 100-card Set.

### F3. DC Reboot-Exhaustion (Erase-Canon-To-Fix-Bloat-Loses-Trust)
*Precedent:* *Crisis on Infinite Earths* (1985), *Zero Hour* (1994), *Infinite Crisis* (2005), *Flashpoint*/New 52 (2011), *Doomsday Clock*/*Rebirth* (2016+). DC has *re-collapsed* its multiverse five times in 40 years. Each reset wipes collector confidence in card-permanence. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crisis_on_Infinite_Earths))
*LoreVault discipline:* **No retcons. Ever.** A Moment minted in Series 1 is canonical forever. The Lattice does not reboot. Lampblack residue accumulates; it does not erase. This is a chain-permanence promise (NFT-native discipline DC could not honor).

### F4. Kingdom-Hearts Narrative-Incoherence (Cross-Canon-Without-A-Spine)
*Precedent:* Kingdom Hearts series. By KH3, the metaplot was so saturated with original lore + Disney-cameos + Final Fantasy callbacks that Nomura himself disowned the "crossover" framing — claiming the original characters were always the heart. KH3 cut almost all FF characters because they no longer fit. ([CBR](https://www.cbr.com/kingdom-hearts-3-final-fantasy-characters/))
*LoreVault discipline:* **The Lattice is the Spine, not the figures.** Decision 11 (per `HANDOVER.md`) — Gothic Horror needs its Lampblacker-Spine original anchor by Series 2. Crossover figures are not the load-bearing scaffold. *See Section 6.*

### F5. OUaT-In-Wonderland Empty-Shell-Spinoff
*Precedent:* *Once Upon a Time in Wonderland* (ABC 2013-14) averaged 3.76M viewers / 0.9 demo vs. parent show's 7.08M / 2.20. Cancelled after 13 episodes. The single-Pane spinoff lacked the cross-Pane *discovery* that made the parent show work. ([SlashFilm](https://www.slashfilm.com/1751786/why-abc-canceled-once-upon-a-time-in-wonderland/), [TVSeriesFinale](https://tvseriesfinale.com/tv-show/once-upon-a-time-in-wonderland-cancelled-abc-confirms-no-season-two-31959/))
*LoreVault discipline:* **No single-Universe Series.** Every Series must include at least 3 Universes. Wonderland-only is not a viable Series structure.

### F6. Sleepy-Hollow Premise-Erosion (Genre-Drift-Once-Crossover-Becomes-The-Show)
*Precedent:* *Sleepy Hollow* (Fox 2013-17) started with a tight Ichabod-in-modernity premise + Headless Horseman as Apocalypse herald. Seasons 3-4 added Egyptian myth, biblical prophecy, witches, demons, *Bones* crossover — the show "found the rubicon and crossed it from fun-and-smart-silly to bad-silly." Cancelled at 0.5 demo. ([Looper](https://www.looper.com/214538/the-real-reason-sleepy-hollow-was-canceled/), [Variety](https://variety.com/2017/tv/news/sleepy-hollow-cancelled-fox-season-5-1202419243/))
*LoreVault discipline:* **Crossovers serve the host Pane's genre, never override it.** Pattern 13 (Genre-Native Crossover) is mandatory: form-mismatch crossovers are rejected at design.

### F7. MTG Universes-Beyond Identity-Dilution
*Precedent:* WotC announced 50/50 split between in-universe and Universes-Beyond sets. Lord of the Rings hit, Final Fantasy did $200M Day 1 — but criticism mounted that Dominaria/Zendikar/Ravnica are being eclipsed. Players cite "card frame is distinct" as the *only* protection. ([Wargamer](https://www.wargamer.com/magic-the-gathering/group-therapy-universes-beyond), [MTG Wiki](https://mtg.fandom.com/wiki/Universes_Beyond))
*LoreVault discipline:* **LoreVault has no "Universes Beyond" — every Universe IS in-universe**. Public-domain canon IS the LoreVault world; there is no separate primary canon to dilute. This is a structural advantage MTG cannot replicate. Make it explicit in marketing.

### F8. Penny-Dreadful Spine-Loss (Anchor-Death-Kills-Show)
*Precedent:* *Penny Dreadful* ended with Vanessa Ives (only original character among the public-domain ensemble). Showtime + Logan agreed there was no show without her. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanessa_Ives), [ScreenRant](https://screenrant.com/penny-dreadful-series-finale-ending-controversial-vanessa-death/))
*LoreVault discipline:* **Each Universe has a Lampblacker-Spine** — original-anchor figure designed by LoreVault, loadbearing for that Universe's emotional gravity. Decision 11 needs to stand up FIVE Spines, not one. *See Section 6.*

### F9. Convergence-Climax Inflation
*Precedent:* OUaT had 4 separate "all the realms unite" climaxes by Season 6. Marvel had Endgame, then *another* convergence in *Multiverse of Madness*, then *another* teased for *Secret Wars*. Each diminishes the next.
*LoreVault discipline:* **One Convergence-Cathedral per Series, max.** Pattern 11. Scarcity preserved by design quota.

### F10. Hercules-Xena Crossover-As-Default-Drift
*Precedent:* By Hercules S3 / Xena S2, the shows had crossed over so often that Hercules began appearing in Xena's solo arcs as a recurring guest. The shows became *one show split across two slots*, which made each individually less valuable. ([Looper](https://www.looper.com/636823/what-you-need-to-know-about-the-hercules-and-xena-crossovers/))
*LoreVault discipline:* **Crossover frequency caps per figure** — no Legendary figure appears in more than 2 cross-Universe Moments per Series. Prevents the "this figure is now everyone's figure" dilution.

## 5. The Once Upon a Time Failure Mode (Detail)

OUaT Seasons 1-3 worked because: (a) every cross-Pane reveal *resolved* a known Storybrooke mystery; (b) figures crossed under explicit narrative cost (curses, portals, prices paid); (c) the show had a single load-bearing Spine — Emma Swan, the Savior — which was both original and recurring.

Seasons 4-7 broke because:

- **Frozen integration was a marketing acquisition, not a narrative one.** Critics flagged the "audience-grab" framing immediately. Ratings spiked then declined faster — the textbook bait-and-bleed pattern. ([Rotten Tomatoes consensus](https://www.rottentomatoes.com/tv/once_upon_a_time/s04))
- **Universe-additions accelerated.** Oz, Camelot, Land of Untold Stories, Wish Realm, Hyperion Heights — by Season 7, viewers could not name what Universe a given character was *from*. Stakes flatten when origin becomes interchangeable.
- **The Spine moved.** Season 7 soft-rebooted with a new Savior (Henry's daughter), a new town (Hyperion Heights), and most of the original cast departing. Ratings collapsed to 0.55 demo. ([Cinemablend](https://www.cinemablend.com/television/2307742/why-once-upon-a-time-is-ending-after-season-7))
- **Convergence-Cathedrals inflated.** Each season ended with a "the realms unite" climax. By S6 the climax was rote.

**LoreVault disciplines that prevent this:**

1. Universe roster locked at Series boundary — no mid-Series additions (`SET-ROADMAP.md`).
2. Iceberg ratio (1:2:4) caps crossover density per Set; crossover scarcity is scheduled.
3. Lampblacker-Spine per Universe (Decision 11) — not a single Spine for the whole product. Multiple Spines distribute load.
4. Convergence-Cathedral budget: 1 per Series.
5. No retcons — chain-permanence is the LoreVault equivalent of OUaT's "we promised Storybrooke was forever."

## 6. The Penny Dreadful Lampblacker-Spine Pattern (and Decision 11)

**The diagnostic:** Vanessa Ives was original. Frankenstein, Dracula, Dorian Gray were public-domain. The show's emotional center was the original; the canonical figures orbited her. When Vanessa died, the show ended — not because plot ran out, but because there was no other Spine. ([ScreenRant](https://screenrant.com/penny-dreadful-series-finale-ending-controversial-vanessa-death/))

**The implication for LoreVault:** Decision 11 in `HANDOVER.md` says Gothic Horror needs an original anchor by Series 2. This is correct, but *all five Universes* should have a Lampblacker-Spine. Public-domain figures alone are not enough for emotional gravity — they're too well-known, too pre-loaded, too distributed across other media.

**Five Lampblacker-Spine candidates (one per Universe):**

1. **Baker Street: "The Footnote"** — an unnamed clerk whose name appears in the margins of every Holmes case. Original. Anchors the *epistolary witness* role.
2. **Enchanted Kingdom: "The Cartographer"** — the woman who draws the Forest's maps. Maps don't survive in canon fairy tales; she's why. Original.
3. **Wonderland: "The Glass-Polisher"** — the figure who cleans the Looking-Glass and is the only one who knows it's two-way. Original. (Penny Dreadful's exact mechanic — a maintenance-of-reality character.)
4. **Gothic Horror: "The Letter-Carrier"** (per epistolary discipline) — the postal-clerk who delivered Mina's letters, Frankenstein's correspondence, Jonathan Harker's diary excerpts. Knows everything; says nothing. Original. **This is the Vanessa-Ives slot for Gothic Horror.**
5. **Greek Myth: "The Oracle's Translator"** — the woman who carried the Pythia's words from the temple to the supplicants. Anonymous in canon. Becomes named in LoreVault. Original.

Each Spine is the figure that ties their Universe's public-domain figures together emotionally. They are the *load-bearing originals*. Each can die in their final Set arc, the way Vanessa did, without ending LoreVault — because the *Lattice* is the meta-Spine, and the Universe-Spines are nested.

## 7. The League of Extraordinary Gentlemen LoreVault-Precedent

Moore's mechanic (per Wikipedia + EBSCO + Sequart sources):

- **All Victorian fiction is one universe.** The Black Dossier appendix-and-sidebar mode catalogues hundreds of cameos from Verne, Doyle, Stoker, Wells, Stevenson, Carroll, Dickens, Hardy.
- **Public-domain status is the fuel.** Moore explicitly chose 1898 because he could legally use everyone.
- **Sidebar density > main-plot density.** The headline plot is small (six characters, one mission); the *world* is encyclopedic — generations of readers have annotated *LoEG* for cameos most readers miss on first pass.
- **Each character keeps their canonical voice.** Mina speaks like Stoker's Mina; Quatermain speaks like Haggard's Quatermain. Moore did not flatten dialect.

**LoreVault inherits:**

1. The "all public-domain canon is one shared world" thesis. This *is* the Lattice.
2. The sidebar-density model: cards are sparse, the *wiki* is encyclopedic. Pattern 14 (Compendium Crossover).
3. Voice-preservation — flavor text matches each Universe's canonical register. (Already in FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE.)

**LoreVault diverges from:**

1. **Moore's all-on-one-team frame.** *LoEG*'s six-figure team is dramatically efficient but too dense for a TCG — every Moment should not be an ensemble. LoreVault should stay closer to *Anno Dracula*'s structure (one figure-of-attention per scene, the rest are background) than *LoEG*'s ensemble structure.
2. **Moore's contemporary-jab tendency.** *LoEG* later books got increasingly bitter and contemporary-political. LoreVault stays anchored to its source eras.
3. **Moore's escalating ensemble.** By Vol. 4 (*The Tempest*), Moore had every public-domain figure ever imagined on the page. This is the OUaT failure mode in comic form. LoreVault Iceberg ratios prevent it.

## 8. The 1:2:4 Iceberg Doctrine + Crossover

The existing FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE A12 establishes Iceberg ratios. Apply to crossover specifically:

- **Surface (1)**: One *explained* cross-Universe Moment per 100-card Set. Reader gets it on first pass. Example: "The Cottingley Investigation" (Holmes investigates EK fairies). The flavor text names both Universes.
- **Echo (2)**: Two *unexplained recognitions* per Set. Reader catches it on second pass. Example: "The Pythia's Veil / The Deerstalker" — the same object across two Panes. Flavor text doesn't say "this is the same hat from BS"; the Echo-tag does.
- **Deep (4)**: Four *gestured-at convergences* per Set. Reader catches it only by tracing wiki + multi-Set holdings. Example: "London Fog Reaches the Wood" — environment-only, no figures, no callouts. Lampblack is doing the work.

**Set-level discipline:** 7 cross-Universe Moments per 100-card Set. The other 93 Moments are intra-Universe or single-Universe. **Crossover density never exceeds 7%.**

**Worked example — Set 3 hypothetical "The London-Wood Crossing":**
- Surface (1): *The Wolf at the Demeter*.
- Echoes (2): *The Cheshire Cat in the Reichenbach Mist*, *Watson and Mina Compare Casebooks*.
- Deeps (4): *London Fog Reaches the Wood*, *Carroll's Letter to Stoker*, *Moriarty's Loan to Moreau*, *The Lampblacker's Index*.

That is the full crossover content of a 100-card Set. The other 93 cards are pure-Universe Moments. **The crossover is the seasoning, not the meal.**

## 9. Crossover-Set Strategy: "Carnaval of Echoes"

A dedicated Set for Series 2 or 3 in which every Moment is a cross-Universe encounter. **Proposed name: *Carnaval of Echoes*.** *Set Code:* CDE.

**Narrative arc:** A single night when Lampblack residue thickens enough that the Panes brush against each other across the entire Lattice. Figures encounter their cross-Pane counterparts for one night only. Dawn ends it; some figures remember, some don't.

**Why a Set, not a Series:** Constrains crossover-saturation to one container. After Carnaval, normal Iceberg discipline returns. **This is exactly the discipline Marvel violated by extending the Multiverse Saga across Phases 4-6 with no exit.**

**20-Moment composition (illustrative):**

1-3. *Three Surface Moments* (Holmes-meets-Pythia, Persephone-and-Snow-White-tea, Frankenstein-meets-the-Hatter)
4-9. *Six Echo pair-Moments* (three pairs of Spine-Mirror cards)
10-17. *Eight Lampblack-trade environment cards* (no figures; pure residue)
18-19. *Two POV-Inversion Moments* (Wolf-as-protagonist, Mina-as-investigator)
20. *One Convergence-Cathedral Ultimate*: "The Long Brush" — every Universe meeting at once. One per Series. Mythic/Ultimate tier.

**Set-internal discipline:** Carnaval of Echoes is a *capped* Set — 20 Moments only, lower than standard 100. This signals the constraint is intentional. Players know Carnaval ends. The scarcity is the marketing.

Cross-Universe questions that need Council / CEO / external reasoning before Series 1 ships:

1. **Crossover density in Series 1.** Recommendation here is **near-zero** (1-2 Echoes total across the whole Series, no Surface, no Convergence). Series 1 must establish each Universe's solidity before crossing them. **CEO confirm: defer crossover-richness to Series 2+?**

2. **Crossover rarity tier policy.** Should Surface-tier crossovers be Legendary-only? (Recommendation: yes for Surface, mixed for Echoes/Deeps to maintain density.) **Council policy needed.**

3. **Crossovers in Set quotas.** Do cross-Universe Moments count toward each Universe's per-Set Moment quota, or are they additive? (Recommendation: count toward *both* Universes' quotas at 0.5 each — economical, prevents quota-gaming.) **Council policy needed.**

## Closing Thesis

Every precedent that survived 10+ years did three things:

1. **Treated crossover as the seasoning, not the meal.** *Discworld*, *Hellboy*, *Sandman*, *Anno Dracula* — all had vast cross-canon density, all kept it sub-7% of any single book/issue. ([Discworld Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discworld))
2. **Anchored to a Spine.** Vanessa Ives, Sora's KH-original-trio, Mignola's Hellboy. Without an original load-bearing center, public-domain figures alone don't sustain a property.
3. **Refused convergence-as-default.** *LoEG*, *Spider-Verse*'s canon-events, *Pratchett*'s sub-series-stay-mostly-separate — restraint *is* the brand promise. ([Spider-Verse Canon Events](https://intothespiderverse.fandom.com/wiki/Canon_Events))

LoreVault's existing Lattice doctrine + Iceberg ratios + 5-Universe lock + Lampblacker-Spine plan already anticipate every failure mode in this dossier. The remaining work is *quantitative discipline*: cap crossover density at 7% per Set, commission five Spines for Series 2, defer Convergence to Series 3, ship Carnaval of Echoes as a one-time 20-Moment capped Set, and never — under any condition — retcon.

The CEO insight is correct: crossovers are where the most interesting storylines live. The discipline is also correct: that's exactly why you ration them.
