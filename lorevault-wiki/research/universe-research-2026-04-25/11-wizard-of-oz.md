# LoreVault PD-Canon Research: WIZARD OF OZ / Baum's Oz Canon

**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Universe Candidate:** Wizard of Oz / Baum's Oz Canon (1900-1919) + Thompson continuations + Denslow & Neill visual register
**Status:** STRONG candidate for Series 2/3 — but with the most aggressively-defended derivative-rights minefield of any Series 1-vicinity Universe (MGM/WBD + Wicked franchise both peaking in 2025-2026)
**ODIN Escalation Flag:** Ruby slippers are an absolute redline. NEVER touch them. The Baum text says **silver shoes** — that is both legally clean and aesthetically distinctive positioning.

## 1. Canon Enumeration

### Top Figures (Baum + Thompson, drawn from PD layer only)

**Tier 1 (universally legible):**
- Dorothy Gale — orphaned Kansas farm girl, deuteragonist of 1900 + ongoing presence; canonical PD design = Denslow's pinafore + dark braids, NOT Garland's gingham specifically
- Toto — Cairn-terrier-style small black dog (Baum text says "little black dog"; Denslow draws him scruffy/terrier; the *specific* MGM Cairn cast is film-side but the breed is Baum-canonical via Denslow's drawings)
- Scarecrow — straw-stuffed brain-seeker; Denslow drew him in conical hat + blue Munchkin coat
- Tin Woodman / Tin Woodsman of Oz — heart-seeker; Baum's backstory is the *Nick Chopper flesh-man transformed-by-cursed-axe* tale (much darker than MGM)
- Cowardly Lion — courage-seeker
- The Wizard / Oscar Diggs — humbug from Omaha; full name Oscar Zoroaster Phadrig Isaac Norman Henkel Emmannuel Ambroise Diggs
- Glinda the Good — **Witch of the SOUTH** in Baum (Quadling Country); MGM moved her to North and merged her with Witch-of-North
- Wicked Witch of the West — Baum describes her as one-eyed, old, shrunken, wearing a golden cap, fearing water; **NOT green-skinned in Baum** (green is MGM/Wicked)
- Wicked Witch of the East — killed offstage by Dorothy's house; owner of the Silver Shoes
- Princess Ozma — rightful ruler of Oz, central from Book 2 onward; transformed-as-boy backstory ("Tip")
- Tik-Tok — clockwork copper man, three wind-up springs (thoughts/speech/action); proto-cyborg; Book 3+

**Tier 2 (specialist-legible, deep-canon):**
- Patchwork Girl ("Scraps") — sentient quilt-construct, electric personality
- Jack Pumpkinhead — wooden-bodied figure with a carved-pumpkin head (replaceable as it rots)
- The Sawhorse — wooden hobby-horse animated by the Powder of Life
- Polychrome — the Rainbow's Daughter, ethereal sky-being
- Mombi — old witch of the North/Gillikin who hid Ozma
- Nome King Roquat — ruler of the underground Nomes; Magic Belt holder; **fears eggs**
- Billina — yellow hen, Dorothy's companion in *Ozma of Oz*
- The Hungry Tiger — companion to Cowardly Lion; perpetually hungry but morally restrained
- The Shaggy Man — wandering hobo-figure with the Love Magnet
- Aunt Em / Uncle Henry — Dorothy's Kansas guardians
- Royal Historian of Oz — Baum's self-insert framing device
- The Glass Cat ("Bungle") — transparent feline animated with Powder of Life
- Captain Bill / Cap'n Bill — wooden-legged sailor, recurring companion

**Top Moments (Baum-canon, pre-MGM framing):**
1. The cyclone arrival — Kansas farmhouse lifted, deposited in Munchkin Country, lands on Witch of the East
2. Dorothy receives the **Silver Shoes** from the Witch of the North's kiss-mark on her forehead
3. Meeting the Scarecrow on the cornpole
4. Oiling the rusted Tin Woodman (frozen mid-axe-stroke in the forest)
5. The Cowardly Lion's roar-and-tears encounter
6. The deadly Poppy Field (Lion saved by field mice — pre-MGM detail)
7. Emerald City revealed — but only because everyone wears **green-tinted spectacles** locked on with gold buckles (the city isn't actually green; this is Baum's central reveal)
8. Three audiences with the Wizard (each pilgrim sees a different form)
9. Capture by Winged Monkeys (controlled by the Golden Cap's three wishes)
10. Melting the Wicked Witch of the West with a bucket of water
11. The Wizard exposed as humbug — gives Scarecrow bran-brains, Tin Man silk-heart, Lion liquid-courage
12. Wizard's balloon departure (accidental — Dorothy left behind)
13. Journey to Glinda in the South through the Hammerheads / Fighting Trees / China Country
14. Glinda explains the Silver Shoes — Dorothy clicks heels three times saying "Take me home to Aunt Em"
15. The Shoes fall off mid-flight over the Deadly Desert and are lost forever

**Props (PD-canonical, Baum-text):**
- **Silver Shoes** (NOT ruby slippers — silver, pointed-toe, Mary-Jane-style with bow per Denslow)
- Wicked Witch's broom (Baum: ordinary broom, bestowed by the Witch as a "chore" before her death)
- Golden Cap — three-wish controller of the Winged Monkeys (passed Witch → Dorothy → Scarecrow)
- Silver Whistle — also Winkie-related
- Magic Belt — Nome King's, later Ozma's
- Powder of Life — animates inanimate matter (Mombi → Sawhorse, Jack Pumpkinhead, Glass Cat, Scraps)
- Eggs — Nome King's existential terror
- Green Spectacles — the lens that makes Emerald City "green"
- Oilcan — Tin Woodman's life-support
- Love Magnet — Shaggy Man's affection-compeller
- Wishing Pills — recurring magical macguffin

**Places (Baum-canonical geography):**
- Munchkin Country (East, BLUE) — farmland, blue-coated peoples
- Quadling Country (South, RED) — Glinda's domain, rose-colored
- Winkie Country (West, YELLOW) — where the Wicked Witch ruled, yellow palette
- Gillikin Country (North, PURPLE) — Mombi's domain, witches & magic
- Emerald City (Center, GREEN — but only via spectacles)
- Deadly Desert — surrounds Oz; turns living things to sand
- Land of Ev — coastal kingdom across the desert (*Ozma of Oz*)
- Land of Mo — neighboring fairy land
- Yellow Brick Road — runs from Munchkin East to Emerald City; Baum does NOT spiral it

## 2. PD Status — Confirmed

**Baum's 14 novels (1900-1920):** All deeply public domain in US.
- *The Wonderful Wizard of Oz* (1900) → PD 1956
- *The Marvelous Land of Oz* (1904) → PD 1960
- *Ozma of Oz* (1907) → PD 1983
- Through *Glinda of Oz* (1920, posthumous) → PD 1996
[Sources: Wikipedia "Copyright status of The Wonderful Wizard of Oz and related works in the United States"; New Media Rights]

**Ruth Plumly Thompson (1921-1939, 19 books):**
- *The Royal Book of Oz* (1921) through *The Yellow Knight of Oz* (1930) — all PD as of 2026
- *The Pirates in Oz* (1931) — PD 2027
- *The Purple Prince of Oz* (1932) — PD 2028
- *Ojo in Oz* (1933) — PD 2029
- *Speedy in Oz* (1934) — PD 2030
- Last 5 (1935-1939: *Wishing Horse, Captain Salt, Handy Mandy, Silver Princess, Ozoplaning*) — PD since 1963-1967 (copyright not renewed)
- Two deutero-canonical Thompson books (*Yankee in Oz* 1972, *Enchanted Island of Oz* 1976) — STILL UNDER COPYRIGHT until 2068/2072
[Source: Wikipedia copyright status page; Kiddle summary]

**John R. Neill illustrations (1904-1939):** PD per US copyright term — same dates as the books they appeared in. Neill's three self-authored Oz books (1940-1942) enter PD 2036-2038. *The Runaway in Oz* (posthumous 1995, edited by Eric Shanower) is ENCUMBERED until ~2094.

**W.W. Denslow's 1900 illustrations:** Co-copyrighted with Baum, fully PD since 1956. Denslow died 1915.

**Frank Kramer / Dirk Gringhuis / Dick Martin** illustrations of later non-canonical Oz works — most under copyright; AVOID.

**Net:** The full Baum + Denslow + Neill (1900-1929) corpus is rock-solid PD. Thompson 1930-1934 will fully clear by 2030.

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield (the LOCKED layer — DO NOT TOUCH)

This Universe has the **most aggressively-defended derivative-rights stack** of any candidate. Warner Bros. has won lawsuits specifically about Wizard of Oz character merchandise and is currently mid-blockbuster cycle on Wicked.

**MGM 1939 film locks (Warner Bros owns; copyright runs to 2035):**
1. **Ruby slippers** — both as design AND as registered trademark. Absolute redline. Use silver.
2. **Specific green Wicked Witch skin** (Margaret Hamilton makeup) + her specific pointed-chin black-hat-tilted-back silhouette
3. **Specific Tin Woodman costume** — silver-grey with funnel hat (Baum's text says tin-suit but doesn't specify funnel-shape)
4. **Specific Scarecrow look** — Ray Bolger's black-button-eyes, blue-tunic, slack-jaw face — protected as a *visual character depiction* under WB v. AVELA (8th Cir. 2011)
5. **Specific Cowardly Lion face** — Bert Lahr's makeup design, mane shape, bow-in-mane
6. **"Over the Rainbow"** — Arlen/Harburg, copyrighted 1939, PD 2035
7. Other 1939 songs ("If I Only Had a Brain," "Ding Dong the Witch is Dead," "We're Off to See the Wizard") — same expiry
8. **Specific yellow-brick-road spiral arrangement** — Wicked-2024 had to use a "circle that continues" instead
9. **Specific Munchkin costuming and choreography** (the Lollipop Guild specifically)
10. **Tornado-house-sepia-to-Technicolor reveal** sequence

**Wicked franchise (Schwartz musical 2003 + Maguire novel 1995 + 2024/2025 films):**
11. **Elphaba** as a name and character reframing — Maguire-original. PD-clean Witch-of-West is fine; the name "Elphaba" is not.
12. **Galinda → Glinda** name-arc and "Popular"/"Defying Gravity"/"For Good" songs — full Schwartz copyright
13. **The Erivo / Grande specific designs** from Wicked (2024) and Wicked: For Good (Nov 21, 2025) — current cultural saturation; Wicked: For Good grossed $540M+ on $150M, opened $147M domestic, biggest musical opening behind Lion King 2019
14. **Wicked-specific scrolling map / Shiz University / Madame Morrible** specifics

**Other locked derivatives:**
15. *The Wiz* (1978 film, Diana Ross / Michael Jackson) — Black urban Oz reframe, specific costumes
16. *Return to Oz* (1985 Disney) — specific Wheelers, Mombi-with-changeable-heads design
17. *Oz the Great and Powerful* (2013 Disney/Sam Raimi) — James Franco specific, Mila Kunis / Rachel Weisz specifics
18. *The Wonderful Galaxy of Oz* (1992 anime) — specific designs
19. *Once Upon a Time* (ABC) Wicked-arc — Rebecca Mader's specific Zelena

**Legal test (WB v. AVELA, 8th Cir. 2011):** Even though Baum's text is PD, *visual character depictions* developed solely for the 1939 film are independently copyrighted. Quote: *"the scope of the film copyrights covers all visual depictions of the film characters."* This is the precise reason LoreVault must build entirely from the **Denslow + Neill register** — not a single design beat from MGM.

## 4. Pre-MGM Canonical PD Visual Register (THE PLAY)

This is LoreVault's single biggest differentiation move for Oz: **lean HARD into Denslow + Neill**. They predate MGM by 35-39 years. They're more visually distinctive. They're *what Baum approved*.

**W.W. Denslow (1900, *Wonderful Wizard of Oz* only):**
- Art Nouveau / late-Victorian poster-illustrator register
- Heavy black ink outlines + flat color fields (turn-of-century chromolithography)
- Palette: olive-green, rust-orange, mustard-yellow, brick-red, slate-blue — earthy, NOT MGM-Technicolor
- Quirky, almost cartoony figures — Tin Woodman is shorter and more comical; Scarecrow has a wide flat face; Toto is unmistakably a small scruffy terrier-mutt
- Signature: tiny seahorse hidden in many panels (his personal mark)
- Co-held copyright with Baum on the original 1900 book
- DIED 1915, fully PD

**John R. Neill (1904-1939, books 2-14 + Thompson run + 3 own books):**
- THE long-term canonical illustrator — "The Royal Illustrator of Oz" / "JNO."
- More Art Deco / Beaux-Arts than Denslow
- Dynamic figures, pen-and-ink with selective watercolor / spot color
- Created the canonical visual designs for: **Ozma** (slim regal girl, poppies in hair), **Tik-Tok** (round copper barrel-body, brass clockwork visible), **Patchwork Girl** (rag-quilt construction, wild yarn hair), **Jack Pumpkinhead** (lanky wooden body, jack-o'-lantern head), **Scraps**, **the Sawhorse**, **the Hungry Tiger**, **Polychrome**
- Iconic Edwardian-children's-book layout — text-bordered illustrations, drop-caps, decorative margins
- Neill's three self-authored Oz books (*The Wonder City of Oz* 1940, *The Scalawagons of Oz* 1941, *Lucky Bucky in Oz* 1942) enter PD 2036-2038 — UNCLEAR on these for now; stick to 1904-1929 Neill

**Recommendation:** LoreVault's Oz visual identity = Denslow's olive-and-rust 1900 register for *Book 1 figures* (Dorothy, Toto, Scarecrow, Tin Man, Lion, Wizard) + Neill's jewel-toned Edwardian register for *Book 2+ figures* (Ozma, Tik-Tok, Patchwork Girl, Jack). This single decision delivers (a) full PD clearance, (b) maximum visual distance from MGM/Wicked, and (c) genuine visual distinction in a market where every Oz adaptation defaults to MGM.

## 5. Top 5 Figures with Spine + Lampblack

**Dorothy Gale**
- *Spine:* The girl from elsewhere who walks into a wonderland, completes a quest, and returns home — but the home she returns to is the *same* and she is *changed* (Baum's quiet inversion of the bildungsroman)
- *Lampblack:* The Silver Shoes; the heel-click-three-times mechanism (Baum: she clicks them and says "Take me home to Aunt Em"); the lost-shoes-over-the-Deadly-Desert ending
- *Cross-tether:* Sister-shadow of Alice (both girls who fall through portals; Dorothy returns, Alice wakes)

**Scarecrow**
- *Spine:* The brain-seeker who already had the brain — Baum's central joke is that the Scarecrow is the smartest of the four pilgrims throughout the journey, but doesn't know it. Becomes ruler of the Emerald City after the Wizard departs.
- *Lampblack:* The cornpole; the bran-and-pin "brains"; the conical pointed hat (Denslow); the no-spine made king
- *Cross-tether:* Shadow of Sherlock-as-deduction-machine-that-doubts-itself; the imposter-syndrome archetype

**Tin Woodman (Nick Chopper)**
- *Spine:* The heart-seeker with the *darkest* backstory in the canon — was a flesh-man Munchkin woodcutter who fell in love with Nimmie Amee; Wicked Witch of the East cursed his axe so each swing severed his own limb; tinsmith Ku-Klip replaced each piece with tin until no flesh remained, including no heart. The silk-heart Wizard gives him is technically the working organ.
- *Lampblack:* The oilcan; the funnel-hat (Baum text); the rust-paralysis; Nimmie Amee
- *Cross-tether:* Proto-cyborg / Frankenstein-shadow; the body-mod-becomes-self question

**Wicked Witch of the West**
- *Spine:* One-eyed (a single eye "as powerful as a telescope"), old and shrunken, fears water; controls Winged Monkeys via the Golden Cap (3 wishes); rules Winkie Country West. Her death by water is canonical from page one of Chapter 12.
- *Lampblack:* Golden Cap; bucket of water; the scream "I'm melting!" (this specific phrasing is MGM — Baum's text is "Look out — here I go!" → UNCLEAR if "melting" line is locked, treat as MGM-only); broom; Winged Monkeys
- *Cross-tether:* EK Stepmother shadow; the Greek Furies / one-eyed-Cyclops register

**Glinda the Good**
- *Spine:* **Witch of the SOUTH** in Baum (NOT the North/Witch-of-North — that's MGM's merger). Rules Quadling Country. The wise gatekeeper who explains the Silver Shoes mechanism at the end. Long red hair, white robe, Great Book of Records.
- *Lampblack:* The Great Book of Records (auto-records every event in Oz); rose-and-Quadling palette; the Bubble (UNCLEAR — bubble-arrival is partly MGM)
- *Cross-tether:* The wise-woman / Athena / fairy-godmother register

## 6. LoreVault Original — 3-5 Unique Expressions

**1. The Spectacle Lens.** Every PD Oz reading anchors the reveal that *Emerald City isn't actually green — everyone wears locked-on green spectacles*. LoreVault Oz can build an entire mechanic around this: the **Lens of Oz** as a collectible that re-tints other cards in your collection (cross-tether to Wonderland's "drink-me/eat-me" scale-shifters; cross-tether to Holmes's magnifying glass as the seeing-instrument).

**2. The Four Coloured Lands.** Use the four-colour quadrant geography (blue Munchkin / red Quadling / yellow Winkie / purple Gillikin + green Emerald) as a **set-collection mechanic**: every Oz card belongs to one quadrant; a complete N-S-E-W set unlocks an Emerald City card. This is Baum-canonical, MGM-free, and self-evidently game-shaped.

**3. The Powder of Life.** Mombi's animation-powder (introduced *The Marvelous Land of Oz*, 1904) is the canonical mechanic that brings Sawhorse, Jack Pumpkinhead, Scraps, and the Glass Cat into being. Perfect LoreVault primitive: a card-modifier that animates static collectibles. Cross-tether to the Lampblack itself.

**4. The Magic Belt.** Nome King → Ozma transfer. Wish-granting object with a ledger of past uses. Natural rarity-tier mechanic.

**5. The Royal Historian framing.** Baum's self-insert as "Royal Historian of Oz" who *receives transmissions from Ozma via wireless* (in *Tik-Tok of Oz*, 1914) is a perfect LoreVault meta-frame: every card is a transmission from the Historian, dated and serialized.

**Cross-tethers:**
- Dorothy ↔ Alice (portal-girls; one returns, one wakes)
- Scarecrow ↔ Holmes (deduction-machine that doubts itself; the imposter)
- The Wizard-as-humbug ↔ Holmes/Watson (the man-behind-the-curtain inversion)
- Wicked Witch West ↔ EK Stepmother + Greek Furies (the female monstrous)
- Tik-Tok ↔ proto-cyber-shell (1914 — predates "robot" by 6 years; Karel Čapek's R.U.R. is 1920)
- Nome King's egg-fear ↔ Wonderland's Humpty-Dumpty (egg-as-existential-object)

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

**Baum's 1890 Aberdeen Saturday Pioneer editorials** — Baum published two editorials in December 1890 and January 1891 explicitly advocating "total annihilation" of remaining Native Americans (in response to Sitting Bull's killing and the Wounded Knee massacre). His descendants formally apologized to the Sioux nation in 2006. This is *real*, *documented*, and *not sarcasm* per the historical-record reading (Robert Venables, Cornell). [Source: Wikipedia "The Aberdeen Saturday Pioneer"; Project MUSE "Sympathy or Racism?: L. Frank Baum on Native Americans"]

**LoreVault stance:**
- The Oz *novels themselves* contain no Native American imagery. The editorials are separate from the fiction.
- LoreVault should *acknowledge* the Baum editorials in any "About the Royal Historian" framing — short, direct, modern; no sanitization. The Oz canon is the canon; Baum the man had a documented record, and the descendants apologized.
- **Avoid:** the Winged Monkeys' enslavement-backstory if drawn in a way that maps to racial enslavement imagery; the Hammerheads of Quadling Country if drawn caricaturally; any "savage tribes" framing from Thompson's books (UNCLEAR — Thompson has some racial caricature in *Captain Salt in Oz* etc., flag for review)
- The Wicked-musical reframing of the Wicked Witch as victim-of-bigotry is a precedent — LoreVault doesn't have to take that frame, but if asked "is the Wicked Witch evil," the modern answer is more nuanced than Baum's 1900 framing

**The Wicked Witch's "blackness" in Baum vs MGM:** Baum's WW is described as old, shrunken, one-eyed, with "a body so thin you could almost see through it." She's not racialized in the text. MGM's specific *green skin* is the costume design choice that became iconic. Neither is "black-coded" in any meaningful sense — but be aware that some early-20th-century criticism reads colorism into "Wicked = dark = bad" framing. LoreVault should treat the Witch as *the female monstrous* archetype (cross-tether to EK Stepmother + Greek Furies), NOT as a racial signifier.

## ODIN ESCALATION SUMMARY

1. **NEVER ruby slippers.** WBD owns trademark + design copyright + the 2024-2025 Wicked films pointedly use silver/crystal slippers because of this. Single absolute redline.
2. **Wicked: For Good is the dominant cultural Oz of 2025-2026.** $540M+ box office; Erivo + Grande are the public faces. LoreVault's "original PD canon" positioning must be deliberate counter-programming, not accidental coincidence. Marketing discipline required.
3. **WB v. AVELA (2011)** is the controlling case — visual character depictions developed solely for the 1939 film are independently copyrighted even though Baum's text is PD. We must build *exclusively* from Denslow + Neill registers, not MGM. Every figure should pass a "would Denslow or Neill recognize this?" test.
4. **MGM 1939 film enters PD in 2035** — 9-year horizon. By Series 4-5 we may be able to incorporate film-side designs, but not before. Plan for the post-2035 expansion.
5. **Thompson is not fully clear until 2030** — stick to Baum (14 books) + Thompson 1921-1934 + Neill 1904-1929 for Series 2/3. Avoid Thompson 1935-1939 (technically PD via non-renewal but messier) and Neill's three self-authored books (PD 2036-2038).
