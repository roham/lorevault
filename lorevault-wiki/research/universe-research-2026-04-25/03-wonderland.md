## 1. Canon Enumeration

The Snark is a structural goldmine — bellman, butcher, beaver, baker, banker — and rarely strip-mined by adaptors, which means it's clean PD with no contemporary derivative locks.

**Top 20 figures (sourced to text, not adaptation):**

## 2. Public-Domain Status

**Deep PD across all jurisdictions.** Carroll died 1898; *Wonderland* (1865), *Looking-Glass* (1871), *Snark* (1876), and *Sylvie and Bruno* (1889/93) cleared UK life+70 by 1968 and US 95-from-publication by the early 1970s at latest. Tenniel died 1914; the original 1865/1871 wood-engraved illustrations cleared global PD by 1985 at the absolute latest, and were already in the US PD as pre-1928 publications. Multiple sources confirm the Tenniel art "came into the public domain in 1964" in core jurisdictions [copyrightaid.co.uk; alice-in-wonderland.net FAQ].

**No reversionary claim is plausible.** Walt Disney attempted to enjoin Lou Bunin's 1949 stop-motion *Alice* film and lost on PD grounds — the leading historical precedent that Disney cannot lock the underlying figures [cartoonresearch.com].

**Trademark — the only live concern:** Disney holds USPTO trademark registrations for "Alice in Wonderland" as a brand-mark for specific goods classes (toys, apparel, theme-park experiences, certain entertainment services). Trademark protects source-identification, not the figure. Use the descriptive title, the Carroll text, the Tenniel illustrations, or new derivative artwork referring to the underlying PD figures and Disney's TM registrations are not infringed. The risk surface is naming-collision (a product line literally branded "Alice in Wonderland" in a Disney TM class) and trade-dress confusion (artwork that mimics Disney 1951 closely enough that consumers attribute it to Disney). LoreVault avoids both by branding "Wonderland" as a Pane of the LoreVault Lattice and visually anchoring to Tenniel.

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield (the interdiction list)

This is the heaviest derivative-lock surface of any Series 1 Universe — Carroll has been adapted more than any other 19th-century English author. Treat the following as **untouchable**:

**Disney 1951 *Alice in Wonderland* (animated):**
- The **Doorknob** is an entirely original Disney character (created to replace exposition) — DO NOT USE [disney.fandom.com; alice-in-wonderland.net].
- Cheshire Cat's specific **magenta-and-fuchsia stripe pattern** with rounded blobby shape is Disney's interpretive design — Tenniel's Cheshire is a tabby. Use Tenniel's tabby register.
- Mad Hatter's specific **chartreuse-green hat with orange hair and a yellow bow-tie** is a Disney palette lock. Tenniel: top-hat with "10/6" price-card, plain ink-line — use this.
- Queen of Hearts' specific **black-bobbed hair and stout silhouette with red-and-black valentine collar** is Disney design. Tenniel: tall, severe, playing-card-flat. Use Tenniel.
- The Caterpillar's specific **smoke-letter exhalations spelling out his words** is Disney choreography.
- The "Talking Flowers" appear in Carroll's *Looking-Glass* (Garden of Live Flowers) but Disney's **"All in the Golden Afternoon" choreography, song, and pansy/rose/daisy-as-pretty-girls character designs** are Disney inventions [disney.fandom.com Flowers of Wonderland]. Use Tenniel's Tiger-Lily and the underlying Carroll text.
- The **Tweedles' specific propeller-beanie + striped-shirt** look is Disney; Tenniel drew them in formal schoolboy collars.

**Tim Burton's *Alice in Wonderland* (2010) and *Alice Through the Looking-Glass* (2016):**
- **Underland** as the world's name (Burton retcons "Wonderland" as Alice's mishearing) — proprietary; LoreVault must use "Wonderland."
- **Bayard the Bloodhound** as a named, speaking character is original to Burton (no Bayard in Carroll) — DO NOT USE.
- **Helena Bonham Carter's Red Queen** with the giant CGI bulbous forehead and tiny body, plus the specific blue-veined makeup pattern — locked.
- The **specific Underland aesthetic**: desaturated mossy palette, the "Marmoreal" White Queen castle architecture, the chess-piece animation rigs, the specific Bandersnatch design (boar-bear-rat hybrid Burton invented; Carroll's text gives no description).
- Mia Wasikowska's **white-armor-over-blue-gown Jabberwock-slaying costume**.
- The **named Vorpal Sword as a glowing magical artifact of state**, rather than just the poem-prop in Tenniel's illustration.
- Mad Hatter Tarrant Hightopp's **named character with a personal back-story and a giant orange-pink Depp-aesthetic** — locked.

**American McGee's Alice (2000) and Alice: Madness Returns (2011):**
- **EA owns the IP outright; American McGee himself cannot make a sequel** — the franchise is the most aggressively contested adaptation in the Carroll derivative chain [thegamer.com; gamedeveloper.com].
- The **gothic Alice**: lank black hair, sunken eyes, blood-splatter on the apron, the kitchen-knife "Vorpal Blade" weapon — locked.
- The specific **asylum/madness framing** with Wonderland as a mental-illness manifestation — locked as McGee/EA narrative property.
- LoreVault may do *its own* gothic Alice in the Gothic-Horror Universe Pane (see §6) but must not echo McGee's specific dress, weapon-rig, or asylum framing.

**Other locks to note in passing:**
- **The Matrix**'s "down the rabbit-hole / red pill / white rabbit tattoo" is allusion, not derivative-lock — but Wachowski-style cyberpunk-Alice imagery should be avoided.
- **Madonna's "Drink Me" / "Bedtime Story" video aesthetic** — locked, but specific enough to be a non-issue.
- **SyFy's "Alice" (2009)** miniseries by Nick Willing — original characters (Hatter as smuggler-rebel) locked.
- **Once Upon a Time in Wonderland** (ABC 2013) — Disney property, locked.
- **"Alice in Borderland"** (Manga/Netflix) — Haro Aso's original derivative; locked, but distant enough from canonical figures to barely matter.

## 4. Pre-Derivative Canonical Illustrators (the safe-harbor PD register)

**Sir John Tenniel (1820–1914)** is THE canon. The 92 wood-engraved illustrations across *Wonderland* (1865, 42 plates) and *Looking-Glass* (1871, 50 plates) are the visual register Carroll personally supervised — Carroll dictated dress, expression, and composition. Disney 1951's silhouette inheritances (the blue-pinafore + white-apron + headband Alice; the tall-card Queen; the top-hatted Hatter; the bored elongated Cheshire) are **Tenniel-derived, not Disney-original**, which is the IP basis for LoreVault using these silhouettes without infringing Disney. Status: deep PD globally.

**Arthur Rackham (1867–1939)** illustrated *Wonderland* in 1907 the moment Tenniel's UK copyright expired (1906) — commissioned by Heinemann [themarginalian.org]. His sepia-tinted, art-nouveau, eldritch-tree-line register is a totally different aesthetic from Tenniel and is **fully PD globally**. Status: PD; LoreVault may sample.

**Mabel Lucie Attwell (1879–1964)** illustrated *Wonderland* in 1910/1911 (Raphael Tuck & Sons) in a sentimental round-faced style. Status: PD in the US (pre-1928 publication); UK life+70 cleared in 2034 — UNCLEAR for fresh UK commercial use, but US-safe and usable for reference [encyclopedia.com; nocloo.com].

**Charles Robinson (1870–1937)** illustrated *Wonderland* in 1907. Status: PD globally (life+70 cleared 2007).

**Harry Furniss (1854–1925)** illustrated *Sylvie and Bruno* (1889/1893) under Carroll's direct supervision. Status: PD globally. These are the closest thing to Carroll-supervised art outside Tenniel and are wildly underused by every adaptor — a clean exclusivity opportunity for LoreVault.

**Peter Newell (1862–1924)** illustrated *Wonderland* (1901), *Looking-Glass* (1902), *Hunting of the Snark* (1903) — PD globally.

**Salvador Dalí (1904–1989) — 1969 illustrations:** 12 heliogravures + 1 four-color etching frontispiece commissioned by Random House [themarginalian.org; openculture.com]. **STATUS: STILL IN COPYRIGHT** — the Dalí estate (Fundació Gala-Salvador Dalí) controls the rights through at least 2059 (life+70 in EU/UK) and through 2064-ish in the US (95-from-publication for a 1969 work). DO NOT USE as a visual register without licensing. Marked: **FLAG FOR LICENSING REVIEW IF WANTED — otherwise interdict.**

**Ralph Steadman (1936– )**, **Helen Oxenbury (1938– )**, **Anthony Browne (1946– )**, **Yayoi Kusama (1929– ) 2012 edition**, **Camille Rose Garcia (2010 Disney edition)** — all in copyright, all locked. Do not sample.

**Leonard Weisgard (1949)**, **Marie Laurencin (1930)**, **Willy Pogany (1929)** — UNCLEAR, depends on jurisdiction; reference but don't lift.

**Best-available LoreVault visual register:** Tenniel (primary canon), with Rackham + Robinson + Newell + Furniss as orthogonal PD alternates for cross-Pane variation across the 8 Shells × 5 Parallels grid.

## 5. Top 5 Figures — Spine + Lampblack

**Alice — Spine:** the unflappable child-logician who walks through nonsense answering on its own terms. **Lampblack:** the categorical-failure shimmer; she is Lampblack-bright because every Pane is a place where her categories should not work but do. Her dress (blue pinafore, white apron, hair-band) is Tenniel-canonical, NOT Disney — Disney inherited it from Tenniel via Mary Blair color-keys, but the silhouette is 1865.

**Cheshire Cat — Spine:** the grin-without-cat; vanishment-as-mode; commentary without commitment. **Lampblack:** highest in the deck. The grin without the cat *is* a Lampblack-residue figure literalized — "what's left when the cat goes elsewhere." Cheshire is the universe's own footnote about itself.

**Mad Hatter — Spine:** time-broken host of an infinite tea. **Lampblack:** time-locked at six o'clock for offending Time-as-a-person; he is a figure pinned to one moment by another figure's punishment. Native to Gothic-Horror cross-tether.

**Queen of Hearts — Spine:** sovereign of capricious, theatrical executioner-justice; the "Off with their heads!" reflex-arc that is never carried out. **Lampblack:** the gap between her command and its execution — courts-of-cards, paint-the-roses-red panic — is a structural Lampblack feature; she rules a kingdom that is held together by bluff.

**White Rabbit — Spine:** pocket-watch anxiety; the late-functionary; the threshold-crosser who pulls Alice into the Lattice. **Lampblack:** he is a *summoner* — a figure whose primary effect is to make the protagonist appear in a different Pane. Lampblack-rich because of the threshold function, not because of his own inner depth.

## 6. LoreVault Original Derivative Proposals (cross-tether engine)

LoreVault's North-Star is "every figure across many Panes." Wonderland's strongest cross-tether figures:

1. **Cheshire-as-Lampblack-Incarnate** — The grin-without-cat is the literalization of "residue between Panes." Make Cheshire the only canonical figure who appears (always partially) in *every* Universe Pane simultaneously — a meta-figure who serves as the in-world avatar of the Lattice itself. Cross-tether: Cheshire grins out of every Universe's Snark Shell.

2. **Alice-in-Greek-Myth (Underworld Descent)** — Alice falls down the rabbit-hole and lands in Hades. Her categorical-logic ("but if I am dead, why do I have feet?") is the only mortal logic that has ever made Persephone laugh. Tenniel-line Alice in chiton + asphodel against carbon-black Greek-vase ground. Pane: Greek-Myth × Wonderland-Parallel.

3. **Mad-Hatter-in-Gothic-Horror (Time-Broken)** — Tea-table at six o'clock forever, but the candles are mourning-candles, the teacups are funeral-cups, and the March Hare is a hare-skull. The Hatter's "broken watch" is no longer comic but the actual instrument by which a Gothic timeline froze. Cross-tether: pairs with Frankenstein (broken-time creator) and Dracula (un-aging predator).

4. **Queen-of-Hearts-in-Enchanted-Kingdom (Stepmother-Shadow)** — She is the Stepmother in every Grimm tale told inside her court. The painted-roses-red ritual is the Snow-White poison-apple ritual viewed sideways. Cross-tether: Snow-White's stepmother queen, Cinderella's stepmother, Hansel-and-Gretel's witch — all the same figure, always carrying playing-card residue.

5. **White-Rabbit-in-Baker-Street (Sherlock's Watch-Witness)** — The pocket-watch anxiety reframed as forensic precision; the white-rabbit becomes the witness who saw the time-of-death. Tenniel-line rabbit in 221B parlour. Cross-tether: Doyle Universe × Wonderland-Parallel.

6. **The Snark-as-Boojum (Soft-Apocalypse)** — *The Hunting of the Snark* gives LoreVault its single underused canonical apocalypse-figure: encounter the Snark and "softly and suddenly vanish away, and never be met with again." The Boojum is a Lampblack-source of last resort — the figure whose touch deletes a figure from the Lattice entirely. Use sparingly; it is the rarest Universe-event.

## 7. Aesthetic Totalism

**Palette:** Tenniel ink-line on cream rag-paper as the base; impossible-color saturation overlay (Wonderland's color-logic does not obey local lighting). Looking-Glass register adds checker-black and checker-white at high contrast. Signature accent triad: **pinafore-blue (Pantone-ish 2945C navy-leaning) + apron-white + rose-red (the painted rose, the heart-suit, the queen's lips).**

**Biome:** the impossible garden (with the painted roses), the infinite tea-table (a table that extends past the visual horizon — Carroll's text gives "they had a great deal of room at the table" implying the table is huge), the courtroom-of-cards (a stadium where the audience and jury are themselves cards), the chessboard-landscape (rivers as black squares; brooks Alice must jump as rank-divisions), the rabbit-hole (vertical descent through bookshelves and orange-marmalade jars).

**Iconography (the LoreVault Wonderland glyph set):** pocket-watch, Drink-Me bottle, Eat-Me cake, hookah, mushroom, chess piece (King/Queen/Pawn — the chess piece is the strongest single-symbol because it carries Looking-Glass plus the structural fact that Alice IS a pawn becoming queen), flamingo-mallet, hedgehog-ball, painted rose, key (the small golden key on the glass table), looking-glass frame, white-rabbit silhouette, top-hat with "10/6" card, courtroom-card-fan, vorpal-sword, Bellman's bell.

**Typography:** **Liddell-logic court-record + rhetorical-question.** Card titles read like courtroom transcript fragments ("Q: But the table was bare. A: Yes. Q: Then where did the cake come from?"). Caption voice is Carrollian: posing impossible questions and answering them with technical precision. Avoid whimsy-cute typography; the Tenniel register is dry, exact, Victorian-engraver.

**Set-glyph (the single Universe-mark):** an **impossible chess piece** — a Tenniel-line White Knight whose horse is also a fish, drawn in single-line wood-engraver style. Alternate: a **perspective-broken pocket watch** where the 12-hour face shows the four trial-suits (heart, diamond, club, spade) at the cardinal positions and "6 o'clock — Tea" at the bottom.

**Sample Moment — Alice across rarity tiers:**
- **Common:** "Alice meets the White Rabbit — the rabbit checks his pocket-watch and runs." Tenniel close-cropped; pinafore-blue + cream.
- **Rare:** "Alice drinks Drink Me — the room expands as she contracts." Three-frame shrink-sequence; Tenniel-line.
- **Legendary:** "Alice tells the Queen 'You're nothing but a pack of cards' — the courtroom dissolves." Tenniel courtroom-of-cards composition; cards mid-air, frozen at the apex.
- **Universe (1-of-1):** "Alice steps through the Looking-Glass." Single panel, Alice mid-passage, mirror surface a Lampblack-silver Pane through which other Universe Shells can be glimpsed (the only canonical Wonderland Moment that explicitly stages cross-Pane traversal).

## 8. Audience Legibility

**Mass score: ~10/10** — Wonderland is arguably the single most legible English-language fantasy property worldwide. Almost every adult anglophone, plus most Western-educated non-anglophones, can name Alice, the White Rabbit, the Cheshire Cat, the Hatter, and the Queen of Hearts unprompted. The "down the rabbit-hole" idiom is in active everyday use.

**Specialist score: ~9/10** — Carroll scholarship is one of the densest single-author bibliographies in literature. Annotated Alice editions (Martin Gardner's *The Annotated Alice*) are a benchmark. The Lewis Carroll Society of North America is an active scholarly community. Specialist-collectors will know Tenniel-vs-Rackham-vs-Newell instantly.

**Cultural pre-loads (positive):** "down the rabbit-hole" as journey metaphor; "we're all mad here" as reassurance-meme; "off with their heads" as comic-tyrannical. Wonderland has a uniquely high *participatory* legibility — costume parties, tea-party events, escape-rooms.

**Risk pre-loads (see §10):** Carroll-Liddell biographical questions; psychedelic-readings of the mushroom and Drink-Me sequences (1960s overlay).

## 9. Recommendation

**KEEP as a Series 1 locked Universe.** Wonderland is non-negotiable for LoreVault — it is the highest legibility-per-PD-cleanliness ratio of any candidate Universe.

**Refinements:**
1. Anchor 100% of visual production to **Tenniel 1865/1871** as primary register. Rackham + Newell + Furniss as approved alternates for cross-Pane variation.
2. Maintain a written **interdiction list** (this document, §3) for visual reviewers; every artwork passes a "Disney/Burton/McGee echo check" before mint.
3. Mine the **Snark and Sylvie & Bruno** aggressively — these are clean PD with near-zero adaptation pressure, which gives LoreVault canonical figures (Bellman, Boojum, Outlandish-Watch) that no competitor has.
4. Use Cheshire-as-Lampblack-incarnate as the doctrinal seed for the Lattice north-star across all five Universes.
5. Treat the **looking-glass-passage Moment** as the canonical cross-Pane traversal artwork — the in-fiction explanation for how Lattice-traversal works.
6. **Brand naming:** never use bare "Alice in Wonderland" as a product line name (Disney TM collision risk in some Nice classes). Use "Wonderland" as the Pane name, with figure-level naming ("Alice," "the White Rabbit," "the Cheshire Cat") which are all PD personal-name-style descriptors and not TM-protected for the underlying figures.

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

**Carroll-Liddell biographical concern:** Charles Lutwidge Dodgson's relationship with the Liddell family — particularly Alice Pleasance Liddell (1852–1934), the model for the protagonist — is the most-debated biographical issue around the Wonderland canon. The historical record is genuinely UNCLEAR: contemporary scholarship is divided between "perfectly normal Victorian friendship that has been misread by modern lenses" and "concerning levels of preoccupation with a child by an unmarried adult man, regardless of whether anything was ever physical." Modern sensitivity around adult-male/young-girl dynamics means LoreVault should:
- Never reference Carroll-as-narrator directly
- Never reference Alice Liddell as the model
- Treat Alice as a fictional figure, not a historical girl
- Avoid any photographic-style or "real-Alice"-coded artwork (Carroll's actual photographs of Alice Liddell exist and are in the PD; **DO NOT use them** under any circumstance)
- Stick to Tenniel-line drawn register, which is a stylized child-form

**Caucus-Race / Dodo (chapter 3):** Minor concern. The Caucus-Race is a Carroll satire on then-contemporary politics; the Dodo is widely read as a self-portrait (Dodgson stuttered "Do-do-Dodgson"). Some critics have noted very mild colonial-era racial coding in the broader Victorian children's-literature register; in Wonderland specifically the issue is minor and well below, e.g., Kipling-tier concern. Approach: render the Dodo straight from Tenniel; do not editorialize.

**Psychedelic / drug-coded readings:** The hookah-smoking Caterpillar and the size-changing mushrooms have been retroactively read as drug-allegory since the 1960s (Jefferson Airplane "White Rabbit," etc.). LoreVault should not lean into this register — it is a 1960s overlay, not a Carroll original — but should not pretend it doesn't exist either. Render mushroom and hookah straight from Tenniel; let viewers project as they will.

**Madness and the Hatter:** Carroll's Hatter is "mad as a hatter" — the period idiom referencing mercury-poisoning among 19th-century felt-hat workers. Modern mental-health sensitivity means LoreVault should treat the Hatter as **time-broken**, not "mentally ill" — the cross-tether to Gothic-Horror frames the Hatter's condition as a temporal injury inflicted by Time-as-a-person, which is Carroll-canonical and avoids the "comic-mental-illness" pattern.

**Net assessment:** Cultural-sensitivity concerns are real but manageable. Wonderland is significantly LESS exposed than Greek-Myth (rape myths, slavery context) or Gothic-Horror (colonial racial coding in Dracula, Frankenstein's monster framings). The primary discipline is biographical (no Liddell, no Carroll-as-narrator) and aesthetic (Tenniel-line, not photographic).

## ODIN ESCALATION — The Disney Trademark Line

**The single hard legal line for LoreVault Wonderland:**

> Disney does not own Alice. Disney owns *Disney's Alice* — specific 1951 design choices (Doorknob character; Cheshire's magenta-fuchsia stripe; Hatter's chartreuse-and-orange palette; the Talking Flowers' pansy-girl designs; specific song-and-choreography sequences) and *Burton's Alice* (Underland naming, Bayard, specific Red-Queen-Bonham-Carter makeup, named-character Tarrant Hightopp, the named-and-glowing Vorpal Sword as artifact-of-state). Disney also owns USPTO trademark registrations for "Alice in Wonderland" as a brand-mark in specified Nice classes — which protects source-identification, not the underlying figure.

**The PD-safe register:**
- Carroll text (1865/1871/1876/1889) — fully PD
- Tenniel illustrations (1865/1871) — fully PD globally since 1985, US-PD pre-1928
- Rackham 1907, Newell 1901–1903, Robinson 1907, Furniss 1889–1893 — fully PD
- All canonical figures (Alice, White Rabbit, Cheshire Cat, Hatter, Queen of Hearts, Jabberwock, Bandersnatch, Tweedles, Walrus & Carpenter, Humpty Dumpty, Red/White Queens, Lion & Unicorn, Mock Turtle, Gryphon, Dormouse, Bill the Lizard, Caterpillar, Knave, Bellman, Baker, Snark, Boojum) — fully PD as figures

**The hard interdictions (Disney/Burton/EA-McGee/Dalí):**
1. No Disney 1951 specific designs (Doorknob, Cheshire stripe palette, Hatter palette, Talking Flowers character designs)
2. No Burton 2010/2016 specific designs (Underland naming, Bayard, Bonham-Carter Red Queen makeup, Marmoreal castle, named-glowing Vorpal Sword, Mia-Wasikowska armor, Tarrant Hightopp)
3. No American McGee / EA gothic Alice (lank-black-hair, blood-apron, Vorpal-knife, asylum framing — EA holds the IP exclusive)
4. No Dalí 1969 illustrations without licensing the Fundació Gala-Salvador Dalí (in copyright through ~2059 EU / ~2064 US)
5. No reference to Alice Liddell as the historical model; no Carroll-photograph use
6. No bare "Alice in Wonderland" product-line branding in classes where Disney holds USPTO trademarks (use "Wonderland" Pane naming)

**The visual-review rule:** every Wonderland artwork passes through a "Disney/Burton/McGee echo check" before mint. If a Cheshire reads as Disney-Cheshire rather than Tenniel-Cheshire, kick back. If a Hatter reads as Depp-Hatter rather than Tenniel-Hatter, kick back. The only canonical visual register is Tenniel 1865/1871.

This is the universe with the highest derivative-lock surface in the Series 1 deck — it requires the strictest visual discipline, but the payoff is the highest mass-legibility of any Universe.
