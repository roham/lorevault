# LoreVault Universe Research — Celtic / Irish Myth (Series 2+ Candidate)

**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Status:** PD source layer is *spectacular*. Derivative-rights minefield is dominated by one specific living-artist hazard (Jim Fitzpatrick) plus a band of modern blockbuster locks (Disney *Brave*, Hellblade, *American Gods*, *Outlander*). Walkable — but the differentiation move is **not** painting-illustrator register; it is **stone, vellum, and metal**: John Duncan + illuminated-manuscript + High-Cross-knotwork-frame. That choice is what makes the Universe ours.
**Headline:** Celtic/Irish myth is the **most "obviously already done" Universe in the bench** and yet the **least visually-codified by any single 21st-c blockbuster**. There is no MCU here, no Game of Thrones here, no Riordan-Hollywood-tentpole here. The slot is open. The risk is collapsing into Riverdance / Outlander / Fitzpatrick-poster pastiche. The move is to win it on the vellum register, not the watercolor register.

## 2. PD Status

**Source layer — pristine PD:**
- **Lebor Gabála Érenn** ("Book of Invasions") — 11th c. Public domain.
- **Táin Bó Cúailnge** — surviving recensions in Lebor na hUidre (~1100), Book of Leinster (~1160), Yellow Book of Lecan (14th c.). Public domain.
- **Lebor na hUidre** ("Book of the Dun Cow") — manuscript ~1100 CE. Public domain.
- **Acallam na Senórach** ("Colloquy of the Ancients" — Fenian frame-tale) — late 12th c. Public domain.
- **Mabinogion** (Four Branches + tales) — preserved in **White Book of Rhydderch** (~1350) and **Red Book of Hergest** (~1382). Public domain.
- **Black Book of Carmarthen** (~1250, Welsh) — Public domain.
- **Yellow Book of Lecan**, **Book of Leinster**, **Book of Ballymote**, **Book of the Dean of Lismore** — all medieval Irish/Scottish manuscript collections. Public domain.

**English translations — robustly PD:**
- **Lady Augusta Gregory** — *Cuchulain of Muirthemne* (1902), *Gods and Fighting Men* (1904). She died 1932. Both volumes published well before 1929 — **deep PD in the US** (Project Gutenberg hosts both). ([Project Gutenberg — Gods and Fighting Men](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/14465)) ([Wikipedia — Cuchulain of Muirthemne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuchulain_of_Muirthemne))
- **Lady Charlotte Guest** — first major English Mabinogion translation (1838–1849). Deep PD.
- **James Stephens** — *Irish Fairy Tales* (1920), *In the Land of Youth* (1924), *Deirdre* (1923). Stephens died 1950; works pre-1929 are deep US PD. Pre-1929 Irish-myth retellings safe to use; later works UNCLEAR.
- **Standish O'Grady** — *History of Ireland: The Heroic Period* (1878), *Cuculain: An Epic* (1882). Deep PD.
- **Eleanor Hull** — *The Cuchullin Saga in Irish Literature* (1898), *Cuchulain: The Hound of Ulster* (1909, illustrated by Stephen Reid). Deep PD.
- **Patrick W. Joyce** — *Old Celtic Romances* (1879). Deep PD.
- **Kuno Meyer** — *Voyage of Bran* (1895), *Ancient Irish Poetry* (1911). Deep PD.

**W.B. Yeats — UNCLEAR / stratified — flagging for ODIN ESCALATION:**
- Yeats died **1939**. UK / Ireland / EU: PD as of 2010 (life + 70).
- US: rule-of-thumb is *publication-pre-1930* = PD as of 2026 (the rolling 95-year wall). So **everything Yeats published 1889–1929 is PD in the US**, including: *The Wanderings of Oisin* (1889), *The Celtic Twilight* (1893/1902), *The Wind Among the Reeds* (1899), *In the Seven Woods* (1903), *The Green Helmet and Other Poems* (1910), *Responsibilities* (1914), *The Wild Swans at Coole* (1919), *Michael Robartes and the Dancer* (1921 — contains "Easter, 1916"), *The Tower* (1928 — entered US PD on 2024-01-01). ([Project Gutenberg — The Celtic Twilight](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/10459)) ([Duke CSPD — Public Domain Day 2024](https://web.law.duke.edu/cspd/publicdomainday/2024/))
- *The Winding Stair* (1929 → US PD 2025-01-01), *Words for Music Perhaps* and beyond *may* still be in US copyright — UNCLEAR per work without an estate-by-estate audit. **Flag: do not use any Yeats poem published 1930–1939 without per-poem clearance.** Some 1930s works had renewals; some did not. The Yeats estate (now operating through his children's literary executors) has historically been active in licensing, particularly in the UK/Ireland.
- **ODIN ESCALATION (Yeats):** before quoting *any* Yeats poem in card flavor-text, run a per-poem US-publication-date check against 1929 cutoff. For anything 1930–1939, get IP-counsel sign-off. Default: lean on pre-1929 Yeats only — that body alone is enormous.

The substrate is unbeatable. Multiple deep-PD English translations exist by world-class stylists.

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield — Specific Locks

**1. Jim Fitzpatrick (b. 1944, alive 2026)** — *the* canonical modern Celtic-myth illustrator. Thin Lizzy *Jailbreak* (1976), *Vagabonds of the Western World* (1973), *Black Rose* (1979) album covers; the Che Guevara red-and-black portrait (which he later asserted copyright on, 2011); decades of Tuatha-Dé-Danann / Cú-Chulainn / Morrigan / Lugh poster-art with a *very specific* visual signature: stylized knotwork-frame, jewel-toned color, art-nouveau-meets-Celtic-revival figure work, distinctive faces with elongated features and beaded hair. ([Wikipedia — Jim Fitzpatrick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jim_Fitzpatrick_(artist))) ([JimFitzpatrick.com](https://jimfitzpatrick.com/)) ([Irish Examiner — Fitzpatrick 10 greatest hits](https://www.irishexaminer.com/lifestyle/artsandculture/arid-40282277.html))

**ODIN ESCALATION (Fitzpatrick):** Fitzpatrick is **alive and actively litigious about commercial appropriation** (the 2011 Che reversal proves the pattern). His poster-Celtic-myth visual register has so dominated 1970s–2020s Irish-myth imagery that any LoreVault Celtic art that *looks like Fitzpatrick* is a direct collision risk — even though the underlying *figures* (Cú Chulainn, Morrigan, Lugh) are PD. Concretely avoid: (a) the specific Fitzpatrick face-style (elongated, art-nouveau, jewel-eyed), (b) his specific knotwork-frame compositions, (c) his specific palette (deep reds, golds, violets in saturated stained-glass register), (d) his specific Cú Chulainn standing-bound-to-the-stone-with-raven composition (the *most famous* modern depiction; a Fitzpatrick original), (e) his specific Morrigan-as-raven-woman compositions. **The differentiation move is to skip late-Celtic-Revival illustrator register entirely and go pre-modern (illuminated manuscript + High Cross + John Duncan tempera) instead.** This is non-negotiable.

**2. Pixar/Disney *Brave* (2012)** — Specific locks: Merida's hair (1500+ individually sculpted curls), the Clan DunBroch tartan (Disney registered it in the Scottish Register of Tartans), the will-o'-the-wisp specific blue-glow design, the bear-mother arc, the specific Highland-stylized castle/forest. ([Wikipedia — Brave 2012 film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brave_(2012_film))) ([Wikipedia — Merida (Brave)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merida_(Brave))) Avoid: red-curly-haired warrior-princess archetype, will-o'-the-wisp specific Brave styling, Pixar Highland register.

**3. Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice (Ninja Theory, 2017) and Hellblade II (2024)** — Senua specifically as a **Pict** warrior with face-paint, lime-bleached braid-clumps, Norse-Celtic fusion in Helheim, photogrammetry-realism register. ([Wikipedia — Hellblade Senua's Sacrifice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellblade:_Senua%27s_Sacrifice)) Avoid the specific Senua face-paint pattern, the specific lime-and-blood Pict register; this is the closest-existing-game to our space and the differentiation must be visible.

**4. American Gods (Gaiman novel 2001 + Starz 2017–2021) — Mad Sweeney** — Pablo Schreiber's specific portrayal of the leprechaun Mad Sweeney (red-bearded, modern-bar-fight rage, suspenders-and-greatcoat). The leprechaun *type* is PD; the Schreiber portrayal is locked. Avoid that visual.

## 4. Pre-modern PD Register — The Vellum-and-Stone Vocabulary

Recommended visual stack — every layer pre-1925 and PD-clean. **The thesis: skip the watercolor-and-illustrator era entirely; go straight to manuscript + stone + tempera-symbolist.**

- **John Duncan (1866–1945, Scottish Symbolist)** — *The Riders of the Sidhe* (1911, tempera, McManus Galleries Dundee), *St Bride* (1913), *Tristan and Isolde* (1912). Iconic Celtic-Revival paintings. He died 1945 — **PD in EU/UK as of 2016 (life + 70); US: pre-1929 publications are PD under the 95-year rule, and *Riders of the Sidhe* was exhibited in 1911 — so deep PD in US.** ([Wikipedia — Riders of the Sidhe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Riders_of_the_Sidhe)) ([Art UK — John Duncan's Riders of the Sidhe](https://artuk.org/discover/stories/john-duncans-the-riders-of-the-sidhe))
  - *The Riders of the Sidhe* specifically: Tree of Life, the Grail, the sword (Bronze-Age-modeled, museum-studied), the stone-of-calm — Duncan literally researched the Battersea shield (British Museum) and the Iron Age Torrs horse-helmet (National Museum of Scotland) for the imagery. **This painting alone could be the visual North Star for the Universe.**
- **Stephen Reid (1873–1948, Irish illustrator)** — 16 chromolithographs for Eleanor Hull's *Cuchulain: The Hound of Ulster* (1909). All PD as of 2026 (publication 1909 + 95 = 2004 US PD). ([Internet Archive — Hull, Cuchulain Hound of Ulster](https://archive.org/details/cuchulainhoundof00hull))
- **Henry Justice Ford (1860–1941)** — illustrations for Andrew Lang's color fairy books and various Yeats / Celtic-Twilight publications. Pre-1929 work deep PD.
- **Arthur Rackham (1867–1939)** — illustrated some Celtic / Cornish material (Comus, Irish fairy tales). Pre-1929 PD.
- **Daniel Maclise (1806–1870)** — *The Marriage of Strongbow and Aoife* (1854), *The Origin of the Harp*, *The Spirit of Justice*, Irish-historical / Celtic-romantic register. Deep PD.
- **The Pre-Raphaelites (Rossetti, Burne-Jones, Waterhouse)** — Celtic-revival-adjacent imagery (Waterhouse's *The Lady of Shalott* 1888, Burne-Jones's Briar Rose). Deep PD.
- **George Russell ("AE", 1867–1935)** — Irish mystical-painter and poet, friend of Yeats. Theosophical-Celtic visions of the Sidhe in pastel and oil. Pre-1929 PD.
- **The Book of Kells (~800 CE)** — *the* canonical illuminated manuscript. The manuscript itself is deep PD; **but Trinity College Dublin asserts publication-rights over its specific photographic reproductions** ("Images are available for single-use academic application only"). ([Wikipedia — Book of Kells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_of_Kells)) ([Trinity College Dublin — Book of Kells](https://www.tcd.ie/library/research-collections/book-of-kells.php)) **Workaround: use the imagery as visual *inspiration* for an original LoreVault knotwork-frame system — render our own knotwork from scratch in the same vocabulary; do not copy-paste TCD's photographs.** The vocabulary (insular knotwork, zoomorphic interlace, Carpet pages, the Chi-Rho monogram-page motif, beast-and-man interlace) is fully PD and reproducible from publicly-available study sketches and 19th-c facsimiles.
- **Book of Durrow (~700 CE)** — earlier insular-illuminated, simpler register. PD.
- **Lindisfarne Gospels (~700 CE, British Library)** — the third great insular-illuminated manuscript. PD; British Library has digitized.
- **High Cross stone carvings** (8th–12th c.) — Cross of the Scriptures (Clonmacnoise), Muiredach's High Cross (Monasterboice), Cross of Moone, Cross of Ahenny. **Deep PD across the board.** The interlace-knotwork-Christ-in-Glory carved register is uniquely Irish-Insular and visually unmistakable.
- **Pictish symbol stones** (~6th–9th c.) — the Aberlemno stones, the Pictish Beast, double-disc-and-Z-rod, the comb-and-mirror, salmon and serpent symbols. PD.
- **Iron Age / La Tène metalwork** — the Battersea Shield, the Torrs horse-helmet (used by Duncan), the Tara Brooch (8th c., NMI Dublin), the Ardagh Chalice, the Petrie Crown. Object-photography rights vary by museum, but the *forms* and *patterns* are PD.

**Recommendation: lean hard into the manuscript-and-stone register.** Specifically:
1. **Card-frame system based on insular-knotwork** — original LoreVault knotwork-borders inspired by but not copied from Kells/Durrow/Lindisfarne. Each Universe in the bench gets a frame-language; this is ours.
2. **High-Cross compositional grid for Legendary cards** — the four-arm-quadrants-with-central-roundel from Muiredach's Cross is a perfect rarity-tier visual signature.
3. **John Duncan's tempera register for figure-cards** — the *Riders of the Sidhe* palette (jewel-toned but matte, tempera-flat) is the right answer to "we want lush but not Fitzpatrick-saturated."
4. **Pictish-stone-carving register for Common cards** — the spare, schematic, single-symbol-on-stone aesthetic for low-rarity Moments.

The result is **unmistakably not Disney-Brave, not Hellblade, not Cartoon-Saloon, not Fitzpatrick.** It is *vellum and stone*. It is what Celtic visual culture *actually is* before the modern illustrator era flattened it.

## 5. Top 5 Figures — Spine + Lampblack

**Cú Chulainn — the Hound, the Boy at the Ford**
- *Spine:* Born Setanta to Lugh and a mortal woman; renamed when as a boy he killed Culann's hound and offered to be the hound until a replacement matured. Trained on Skye by the warrior-woman Scáthach. Wields the Gáe Bulg (barbed spear, opens 30 wounds inside the body). Subject to *ríastrad* — the warp-spasm / battle-frenzy that contorts him into a one-eyed, hair-aflame monster. Defends Ulster single-handed at the ford against Medb's army. Killed Ferdiad his foster-brother. Dies bound upright to a standing stone, raven landing on his shoulder.
- *Lampblack:* The boy who *names himself by what he kills*. The cost of every saved homestead is the friend at the next ford. Cross-tether to Achilles (single warrior with one weakness, raised in a parallel education-by-monster, dies young).

**Morrigan — Raven at the Ford, Washer-of-Shrouds**
- *Spine:* Phantom-queen of war and fate. Triple-aspect (Badb, Macha, Nemain in some readings; Morrígan + Badb + Macha in others). Appears as raven, eel, wolf, beautiful woman, hag at the ford washing the bloody clothes of the warrior who will die that day. Cú Chulainn refuses her; she becomes his enemy. Foretells Ragnarök-equivalent battle endings.
- *Lampblack:* The washing-shroud-of-the-future. A war-goddess who is also a *prophet of grief*. Cross-tether to Greek's Furies (Erinyes) — both are female, plural-singular, blood-stained, and tied to vengeance/justice. The Lampblack residue here is the *foreseen but unprevented* — the raven who knew and could not warn.

**Lugh Lámhfhada — the Long-Armed, Master of Every Art**
- *Spine:* Son of Cian (Tuatha Dé Danann) and Eithne (Fomorian, Balor's daughter). The "samildánach" — skilled in all arts. Slays his grandfather Balor of the Evil Eye at the Second Battle of Mag Tuired with a sling-stone (or spear) through the lid. One of the Four Treasures' wielders (Spear of Lugh).
- *Lampblack:* The bridge-figure between two warring orders (Tuatha Dé Danann + Fomorian by blood). The Lattice's hybrid — proves the seam between order and chaos is *biological*, not abstract.

**Fionn mac Cumhaill — Thumb-of-Knowledge, Captain of the Fianna**
- *Spine:* Leader of the Fianna warband. As a boy, cooks the Salmon of Knowledge for the poet Finegas; burns his thumb on the hot fish-flesh; sucks it; gains all wisdom forever. Calls on it the rest of his life. Hunts boar, stag, and rival in his great-trees-and-moor cycle. Ends the cycle bitter, having lost Diarmuid to Gráinne.
- *Lampblack:* Wisdom-by-burned-thumb — knowledge as accidental and partial, never volitional. Cross-tether to Norse Sigurd (taste dragon-blood, gain bird-tongue) — same archetype: hero gains supernatural understanding through accidental tasting of the magical body.

**Manannán mac Lir — Sea-God, Pane-Walker**
- *Spine:* Sea-god; gatekeeper of Tír na nÓg; mantle-of-mist that hides islands; the boat *Wave-Sweeper* that needs no sail; cross-piece to Welsh Manawydan. Travels between worlds at will. Gives Fionn the Crane Bag (made from the skin of a transformed woman, contains the treasures of the sea).
- *Lampblack:* The traveler-between-Universes par excellence. **Manannán is the canonical cross-tether interface for the Celtic Universe.** His mantle-of-mist is the Lattice's veil; his Wave-Sweeper crosses Panes the way Loki's shape-shifting crosses identities.

## 6. LoreVault Original Derivative Proposals

1. **"Manannán the Pane-Walker"** — Manannán becomes the canonical *cross-Universe ferryman* in the LoreVault Lattice. Where Norse-Loki is the *transformation* interface, Manannán is the *transit* interface. Mechanic: cards can be moved between Universe-decks via Manannán's mantle-of-mist. The Wave-Sweeper is a cross-Pane vehicle.

2. **"The Morrigan's Washing"** — Morrigan as the **prophetic Lampblack agent**: when she appears in play, she *reveals* (washes clean) the top card of an opponent's deck and marks it as fated-to-die. Direct from canonical washer-at-the-ford imagery; no MCU or game-industry collision.

3. **"The Children of Lir as Lampblack-across-Universes"** — the four swan-children of Lir cursed for 900 years across three lakes are the **paradigmatic Lampblack residue in the Celtic Universe**. They are *creatures unable to die, unable to fully live, voicing the canon in song across centuries*. Mechanic: Lir's swans appear in *any* Universe across the Series as a recurring drift-residue — they are the first cross-Universe Lampblack-card-set, rendering the concept legible. The Lir-swans are the visible proof that Lampblack is a *thing that travels*.

4. **"Cú Chulainn's Ríastrad as state-flip"** — the warp-spasm becomes a card-state mechanic: a normally-controlled Cú Chulainn card can flip into Ríastrad-Cú Chulainn at a cost, gaining huge power but becoming ungovernable (ally-targeting, friendly-fire). Pure source-text faithful.

5. **"Salmon of Knowledge as universal Lattice-glyph"** — the Bradán Feasa is a deck-search/library card available in cross-Universe play. Whoever burns their thumb on it draws three. The Lampblack residue: each draw also reveals what they will lose to know it.

6. **"Bran the Blessed's Severed Head as oracle"** — the Welsh giant-king's head-that-sings-prophecy-for-80-years becomes a **persistent oracle artifact** that survives across plays/games. Cross-tether to Greek's oracular figures.

## 7. Aesthetic Totalism — The Vellum-and-Stone Register

**Palette:** Vellum-cream + iron-grey + bog-amber + blood-red + moss-green + insular-blue (Lindisfarne-blue, ground from lapis). The dominant hue is *aged parchment*, not Fitzpatrick-jewel-saturation. Counter-color is *stone-grey* (high-cross granite), accent is *fire-gold* (illuminated-letter gold-leaf). The card should *read* as a torn vellum page bordered in carved stone.

**Biome stack:**
- **Sídhe-mound** rendered as Newgrange/Brú-na-Bóinne entry-stone with triple-spiral (kerbstone K1) and the inner chamber lit by winter-solstice dawn light
- **Bog and standing-stone** plain (Connemara, the Burren, Achill)
- **Atlantic cliff and salt-spray** (Skellig Michael, the Cliffs of Moher, Aran)
- **Oak-grove** (the canonical druidic grove)
- **Ford** (the canonical Cú Chulainn battle-site; Morrigan-washing site)
- **Mead-hall** (Irish Iron-Age timber roundhouse with thatch and central hearth — *not* the Norse longhall)
- **Tír na nÓg** rendered as a soft-light gold-and-mist coastal otherworld, swans on water
- **Ben Bulben** (table-mountain silhouette, the Diarmuid-and-Gráinne site)

**Iconography:**
- **Triple-spiral / triskelion** (Newgrange kerbstone, deep PD)
- **Insular knotwork frame** (original LoreVault rendering)
- **Raven** (Morrigan)
- **Salmon** (Bradán Feasa)
- **Swan** (Lir's children, Caer Ibormeith)
- **Stag** (Fionn's hunt; Cernunnos-adjacent)
- **Hound** (Cú Chulainn)
- **Standing stone with ogham inscription**
- **Cauldron** (Dagda's; Welsh Cauldron of Rebirth)
- **Spear** (Lugh's)
- **High-Cross silhouette** (Muiredach's Cross compositional grid)
- **Ogham letter strokes** as set-glyph candidate

**Typography:** Insular-uncial-adjacent display face (Insular half-uncial style as used in the Book of Kells; many digital revivals exist) + ogham-stroke ornamental marks. Body text in a clean modern serif. Card flavor-text in **alliterative / kenning-rich Irish-bardic register** ("the hound at the ford," "the raven over Mag Tuired," "the swan-throated child of Lir") — with occasional glossed Old-Irish phrases for authenticity.

**Set glyph candidates:** (a) **triskelion / triple-spiral** (uniquely insular, deep PD, instantly readable as Celtic), (b) **stylized salmon** (Fionn-resonant), (c) **knotwork-bordered ring** (Kells-inspired). **Recommend triple-spiral** as primary — it predates the Christian era, it is on the Newgrange kerbstone (~3200 BCE), it is unmistakable, and it has no MCU/blockbuster collision.

**Sample Moments by rarity:**
- **Common (C):** *Salmon Leaping the Falls* — a single salmon mid-leap in a stone-cut Pictish-symbol-stone register, vellum-cream background, ogham-stroke margin.
- **Rare (R):** *Morrigan at the Ford* — the washer at the bloody linen, raven at her shoulder, ford-water in stone-grey, Duncan-tempera palette. No knotwork-frame; instead, a torn-vellum edge.
- **Legendary (L):** *Cú Chulainn at the Ford* — the boy single-against-the-army, rendered in Stephen-Reid-1909 chromolithograph register but updated, knotwork-frame around the panel, ríastrad-distortion visible in his face. The Diarmuid-Gráinne-flight equivalent for love-card.
- **Ultimate (U):** *The Riders of the Sidhe* — direct homage to Duncan 1911, four mounted Sídhe carrying the Tree of Life, the Grail, the Sword, the Stone-of-Calm, in matte tempera on vellum, full High-Cross-quadrant compositional grid. **The Universe's defining ship.**

## 8. Audience Legibility

**Mass-market score: MEDIUM-HIGH.** Most literate Western consumers know "Celtic" as an aesthetic signal (knotwork, green, fairy-folk) and can name *one or two* figures (Cú Chulainn or Morrigan if they game; leprechauns/Sídhe if they don't). What they don't know is that the Four Cycles are *as deep as Greek myth* — this is a teach-as-you-collect Universe. Pre-loads:
- St. Patrick's Day cultural visibility annually (low-quality but ubiquitous)
- Riverdance/Lord of the Dance cultural memory (1990s+)
- *Brave* (2012, ~$540M global) — primed Highland aesthetic to a generation
- *Outlander* (2014–) — primed adult-romance Highland aesthetic
- *Hellblade* I/II — primed dark-Celtic-warrior register
- *American Gods* — primed Mad Sweeney
- *Smite* — primed Cú Chulainn / Morrigan as game-characters
- *The Secret of Kells* / *Song of the Sea* — primed Insular-Celtic visual register (small but devoted audience)
- *Ghost of Tsushima*-like ambient interest in non-Greek/Norse ancient warrior cultures

**Risk: pattern-match to "leprechauns and shamrocks" or to "Outlander Highland romance."** Mitigation = the vellum-and-stone register kills both pattern-matches at first glance.

**Specialist score: HIGH.** Genuine sub-cohorts:
- (a) Irish/Irish-diaspora cultural-pride collectors (US: ~32M Irish-American, ~9% of population);
- (b) Celtic-pagan / Druidic-revival practitioners (recognized religion in UK/Ireland; tens of thousands US/UK practitioners);
- (c) Welsh / Cornish / Manx / Breton language-revival communities;
- (d) Celtic-music fans (The Pogues, Clannad, Loreena McKennitt, traditional sessions);
- (e) Irish-literature enthusiasts (Yeats, Joyce, Heaney, Stephens);
- (f) Celtic-history / archaeology enthusiasts (Newgrange, Tara, Hill of Uisneach pilgrims);
- (g) Reenactor / LARP / SCA Celtic-warrior cohort;
- (h) Tattoo culture (knotwork, triskelion, claddagh — massive demand).

**Key tailwind: the Irish-American collector cohort is large, affluent, identity-engaged, and underserved by mainstream IP.** No one else has built a *prestige* Celtic-collectible product. This Universe could become *the* Irish-diaspora cultural artifact in the LoreVault collection.

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

Celtic / Irish myth is *living tradition*. Treat as such.

- **Irish vs Scottish vs Welsh vs Cornish vs Manx vs Breton are distinct cultures.** They share an ancestral language family (Goidelic = Irish/Scottish/Manx; Brythonic = Welsh/Cornish/Breton) and overlapping mythic substrate but **are not interchangeable**. The Mabinogion is *Welsh*, not "Celtic" generically. The Táin is *Irish*. Avoid generic-Celtic-blending that flattens the distinctions. Tag every figure to its specific cultural origin in card-text and set-organization.

- **Modern Druidic / Celtic-Pagan revival is real religious practice.** Tens of thousands of practitioners in UK/Ireland/US/Canada/Australia (e.g., the Order of Bards, Ovates and Druids; ADF — Ár nDraíocht Féin). Treat the Tuatha Dé Danann (especially Brigid, Lugh, the Dagda, the Morrigan) with the same respect granted to any living religious figures. The tone is reverent fan-of-the-myth, not Saturday-morning cartoon. Brigid in particular is doubly-active: living-pagan goddess + living-Catholic-saint (St. Brigid of Kildare, sacred flame relit at Kildare 1993, ongoing) — *both* communities are sensitive about her depiction.

- **Irish nationalism and the Easter Rising (1916) layer.** The Celtic-Revival of 1880–1920 was inseparable from Irish independence politics. Yeats, Lady Gregory, Standish O'Grady, Maud Gonne — *all* nationalist-aligned. The figure of Cú Chulainn is itself politically loaded: Oliver Sheppard's bronze *Death of Cú Chulainn* (1911, installed in Dublin GPO 1935 as Easter Rising memorial) cemented the warrior-bound-to-the-stone as a *nationalist-republican* icon. **Be aware**: Cú Chulainn imagery has been deployed by Loyalist-Unionist murals in Northern Ireland *and* by Republican murals — both sides claim him. For LoreVault, stay carefully neutral; treat him as the Ulster Cycle hero, not a 20th-c political symbol. ([Wikipedia — Death of Cú Chulainn statue]) UNCLEAR specific risks of mural-iconography-in-card-art; recommend a Northern-Ireland-specific cultural reviewer pass on Set 1 art.

- **Pictish heritage (Hellblade-adjacent space) is contested.** The Picts are a real archaeological people of pre-Gaelic Scotland; their symbol-stones are PD and visually rich; but their direct cultural descendants (Lowland Scots, Highlanders, modern Scots-identifying communities) hold the inheritance with care. Lean lightly on Pictish symbolism; do not appropriate it as generic-Celtic decoration.

- **No leprechauns-as-comedy.** The Aos Sí (Daoine Sídhe) tradition includes the leprechaun (*leipreachán*) as a *minor sídhe craftsman-figure*. Cartoon-leprechaun-with-pot-of-gold is a 19th-c American-Irish-immigrant-stereotype caricature with anti-Irish-racism roots. **Do not deploy comic-leprechaun imagery.** If we want a craftsman-sídhe card, use the canonical *fer dorcha* / *gruagach* / *clurichaun* register from the source folklore, with Duncan-tempera dignity.

- **No "Celtic spiritual wisdom" New-Age mysticism.** A lot of modern self-help / wellness / yoga-adjacent product co-opts Celtic imagery as generic spiritual-decoration. LoreVault's card-text register should be the source-canonical Bardic-poetic register (alliteration, kenning, geis-and-honor-language), not the Hallmark-mystic register.

- **Diaspora-aware casting.** The Irish diaspora (US, Canada, Australia, Argentina) is a real, large, identity-engaged community. Render Irish/Celtic figures with the dignity that diaspora communities actively defend. Consult Irish-cultural-organization reviewers (e.g., the Irish American Cultural Institute, the Celtic Studies Association of North America) for Set 1 review.

## Summary Verdict

**Celtic / Irish Myth = Tier 1 Series 2 candidate, conditional on the vellum-and-stone differentiation move.**

The PD source layer is unbeatable (Lebor Gabála Érenn, the Táin, the Mabinogion, plus Lady Gregory + James Stephens + Stephen Reid + John Duncan + Yeats-pre-1929 in deep US PD). The audience demand is real and underserved. The single-largest-living-IP-hazard is **Jim Fitzpatrick** — solved by skipping the late-Celtic-Revival illustrator register entirely and going pre-modern (manuscript + stone + Duncan-tempera). The single-largest-blockbuster-hazard is **Disney *Brave*** — solved by NOT going Highland-fantasy-cartoon and instead going Insular-monastic-archaeological. Cross-tether-design is *natural* (Manannán = Pane-walker ferryman; Lir's swans = canonical Lampblack-across-Universes drift; Sídhe = literal parallel-Pane people). The Ulster Cycle (Cú Chulainn / Morrigan / Medb / Ferdiad) is the right Set 1 spine.

The Universe's defining-ship Moment is **John Duncan's *Riders of the Sidhe* (1911)**, redrawn as the Universe's Ultimate-rarity card. Get that one image right — vellum cream, jewel-tempera figures, High-Cross compositional grid, original LoreVault knotwork frame — and the Universe announces itself.

**ODIN ESCALATION items:**
1. **Jim Fitzpatrick** is alive, litigious, and visually dominant in modern Celtic-myth illustration; any LoreVault art that *resembles* his style is collision risk even on PD source figures. Ban Fitzpatrick-style at the visual-direction level; require an explicit Fitzpatrick-comparison audit on every Set 1 hero card.
2. **Yeats post-1929 US PD status is per-poem.** Anything Yeats published 1930–1939 needs per-poem clearance against US copyright-renewal records before quotation. Default policy: pre-1929 Yeats only.
3. **Trinity College Dublin asserts publication-rights on Book of Kells photographs.** The manuscript itself is PD; their photos are not. Recreate knotwork-vocabulary in original LoreVault renderings; do not paste their digitized leaves.
4. **Northern-Ireland-political iconography of Cú Chulainn** is contested (Loyalist + Republican murals both claim him). Recommend a Northern-Ireland cultural reviewer pass on Set 1 art before ship.

**UNCLEAR / open-loop items for follow-up:**
- Specific status of Cartoon Saloon (*The Secret of Kells*, *Song of the Sea*, *Wolfwalkers*) visual-trade-dress claims — UNCLEAR exact overlap risk for our flat-illuminated work; their style is distinctive but adjacent.
- Specific Marvel comics Celtic-pantheon design locks (Marvel's Morrigan, Dagda, Cú Chulainn one-shot designs) — UNCLEAR full extent; recommend a Marvel-comics-specific visual diff audit on Set 1 figure cards.
- Status of the bronze Oliver Sheppard *Death of Cú Chulainn* (1911) image as a model for our Cú Chulainn-bound-to-the-stone card — Sheppard died 1941, so UK PD 2012; US PD if pre-1929 publication of a photograph (the bronze itself was created 1911–1912 and exhibited 1914) — UNCLEAR exact reproduction-rights for the GPO-installed bronze, recommend per-image clearance.
- Living James Stephens estate status (he died 1950) for any post-1929 work — UNCLEAR per-work, default to pre-1929 only.
