# LoreVault Universe Research — Arthurian Legend
**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Candidate:** ARTHURIAN LEGEND (Geoffrey of Monmouth → Chrétien → Mabinogion → Malory → Tennyson → Twain)
**Researcher:** Claude (Opus 4.7)
**Verdict (TL;DR):** STRONG candidate for Series 2 or 3. PD-clean source canon spans 900 years. ## 2. PD Status

**Deep-PD foundational canon** — all in clear public domain worldwide:
- **Geoffrey of Monmouth, *Historia Regum Britanniae*** (c. 1136) — the first connected Arthur narrative; Latin original PD; multiple PD English translations (Sebastian Evans 1903, J.A. Giles 1842).
- **Chrétien de Troyes** (c. 1170s–90s) — *Erec et Enide*, *Cligès*, *Lancelot (Knight of the Cart)*, *Yvain*, *Perceval (the Conte du Graal)*. Old French original and 19th/early-20th-century translations PD.
- **Mabinogion** — Welsh medieval (manuscripts: White Book of Rhydderch c. 1350, Red Book of Hergest c. 1382–1410). **Lady Charlotte Guest's translation (1838–45)** is solidly PD; multiple modern PD-clean translations also exist (Sioned Davies 2007 is in copyright — avoid).
- **Sir Thomas Malory, *Le Morte d'Arthur*** (Caxton 1485) — the master compendium; deep PD.
- **Alfred, Lord Tennyson, *Idylls of the King*** (1859–85) — fully PD.
- **Mark Twain, *A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur's Court*** (1889) — PD.
- **Wace's *Roman de Brut*** (1155, introduces Round Table) and **Layamon's *Brut*** (c. 1200) — PD.
- **Sir Gawain and the Green Knight** (Pearl-poet, c. 1400) — PD original; Tolkien's 1925 translation in copyright; W.A. Neilson 1917 PD.
- **The Vulgate / Lancelot-Grail Cycle** (c. 1210–35) — PD original; H. Oskar Sommer 1908–16 PD.
- **Tristan en prose** (13th c.) — PD.

(Sources: [Wikipedia — Mabinogion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mabinogion); [Lady Charlotte Guest — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lady_Charlotte_Guest); [Project Gutenberg — Story of King Arthur and his Knights](https://www.gutenberg.org/files/60184/60184-h/60184-h.htm))

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield (LOCKED — DO NOT TOUCH)

The Arthur space is *the* most dense modern-derivative minefield of any candidate Universe. Itemized:

1. **T.H. White, *The Once and Future King*** (1958, individual volumes 1938–1958) — under U.S. copyright through ~2053. White's Wart-as-childhood-Arthur, the talking-animal pedagogy (Merlin transforms Wart into a fish, ant, hawk, badger, etc.), the specific *boy-becomes-king* tutelage arc — **all locked**. ([Once and Future King — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Once_and_Future_King); [Online Books — NO US ACCESS](https://onlinebooks.library.upenn.edu/webbin/nonus?id=olbp77805))
2. **Disney *The Sword in the Stone*** (1963, copyright renewed 1991) — Madame Mim, Archimedes the speaking-comic owl, the Wart-Merlin wizard's-duel sequence, Merlin's pink-and-blue robes-and-pointy-hat-with-stars **as Disney rendered them**, the bumbling-old-wizard archetype as Disney drew it. ([Sword in the Stone (1963) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Sword_in_the_Stone_(1963_film))).
3. **Lerner-Loewe *Camelot*** (Broadway 1960, film 1967) — the specific songs, "If Ever I Would Leave You," and the Vanessa Redgrave / Richard Harris staging.
4. **John Boorman, *Excalibur*** (1981) — the specific *mirror-polish armor in green-emerald-light* aesthetic, Nicol Williamson's specific Merlin (skull-cap, silver-flash chain mail), Helen Mirren's Morgana, the specific armored-sex scene, Boorman's specific Pre-Raphaelite-via-cinema look. ([Excalibur — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Excalibur_(film))). **This one looms large** — it IS the cinematic Arthurian baseline for any viewer over 30 (see ODIN ESCALATION).
5. **Monty Python and the Holy Grail** (1975) — the Killer Rabbit of Caerbannog, the Black Knight ("'tis but a scratch"), the Knights Who Say Ni, swallow-coconuts, French taunters, the Trojan Rabbit, the Bridge of Death. The whole comedic vocabulary.
6. **Spamalot** (Idle/Du Prez musical, 2004) — Python derivative, double-locked.
7. **Mary Stewart's Merlin Trilogy** (*Crystal Cave* 1970, *Hollow Hills* 1973, *Last Enchantment* 1979) — Stewart's specific Merlin-as-narrator and her Antonia-Fraser-style historical-British rendering.
8. **Marion Zimmer Bradley, *The Mists of Avalon*** (1983) — Morgaine-as-protagonist, the Goddess-religion-versus-Christianity framing, the specific "summoning the mists" mechanic, the priestess-of-Avalon order as Bradley constructed it. ([Mists of Avalon — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Mists_of_Avalon)). **Additionally avoid Bradley association generally** given her documented complicity in serial child sexual abuse — a LoreVault citation, even passing, would be a brand-trust catastrophe.
9. **Bernard Cornwell, *Warlord Trilogy*** (1995–97) — Cornwell's Derfel-narrator, his post-Roman / sub-Roman Britain reconstruction.
10. **BBC *Merlin*** (2008–12) — Colin Morgan's young-Merlin, Bradley James's young-Arthur, the specific reverse-aging / contemporaries-as-young-men premise.
11. **Cursed** (Netflix 2020) — Katherine Langford's Nimue-as-protagonist; the *Wheel-of-Time*-style fey-revolution recasting.
12. **King Arthur** (Bruckheimer/Fuqua 2004) — Keira Knightley's woad-painted Guinevere, the specific "historical Arthur as Roman cavalry officer" rendering.
13. **King Arthur: Legend of the Sword** (Guy Ritchie 2017) — Ritchie's frenetic-cut Arthur.
14. **Steinbeck, *The Acts of King Arthur and His Noble Knights*** (1976, posthumous, incomplete) — in copyright.
15. **The full pop-culture Arthur shorthand** — the Disney-Boorman-Camelot composite that *most* viewers actually picture when they hear "King Arthur." This is the gravity well to escape.

## 4. Pre-Modern Canonical PD Visual Register

**This is the LoreVault edge.** Arthurian art history before 1928 is exceptionally rich and exceptionally underused in modern adaptations.

### Tier-1 Recommendation: Beardsley + Burne-Jones + Medieval Illumination

- **Aubrey Beardsley, *Le Morte d'Arthur*** (J.M. Dent 1893–94) — ~500 illustrations including ~93 full images, all PD worldwide (US, UK, EU, Canada, Australia under life+70). Beardsley's black-line-on-white, peacock-feather ornament, sinuous Art Nouveau silhouettes, peacock-blacks-and-whites — **THE canonical PD register.** Available high-resolution at Internet Archive and Public Domain Image Library. ([Public Domain Image Library — Beardsley Morte d'Arthur](https://publicdomainimagelibrary.com/products/morte-darthur-1893-aubrey-beardsley-1872-1898-93-images); [Internet Archive — Beardsley facsimile](https://archive.org/details/beardsleysillust0000bear); [Public Domain Review — Beardsley](https://publicdomainreview.org/collection/aubrey-beardsley/))
- **Edward Burne-Jones** (d. 1898, deeply PD) — *The Last Sleep of Arthur in Avalon* (1881–1898, his unfinished masterwork); the Holy Grail / San Graal Tapestries (Stanmore Hall, 1891–94, Morris & Co., designed by Burne-Jones) — six tapestries depicting the Grail Quest; *The Beguiling of Merlin* (1872–77); Briar Rose series. ([Burne-Jones — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Burne-Jones); [Holy Grail Tapestries — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Grail_tapestries))
- **John William Waterhouse** — *The Lady of Shalott* (1888), *I Am Half-Sick of Shadows* (1916), *The Lady of Shalott Looking at Lancelot* (1894) — PD; gifted to Tate 1894. ([Tate — Lady of Shalott](https://www.tate.org.uk/art/artworks/waterhouse-the-lady-of-shalott-n01543))
- **Dante Gabriel Rossetti** (d. 1882) — *Arthur's Tomb*, *La Belle Dame Sans Merci*, multiple Pre-Raphaelite Arthurian watercolors; PD.
- **William Morris** — Arthurian poetry + tapestry-design heraldry; PD.
- **Frederic Sandys, Eleanor Fortescue-Brickdale, W.H. Margetson** — late-Pre-Raphaelite Arthurian painters; PD.

### Tier-2: American Golden-Age Illustration
- **Howard Pyle, *The Story of King Arthur and His Knights*** (Charles Scribner's Sons, 1903) — pen-and-ink, deep PD; Pyle died 1911. Often called among the finest pen-and-ink illustrations of all time. ([Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/files/60184/60184-h/60184-h.htm)).
- **N.C. Wyeth** — early-career Arthurian work (some pre-1928 PD; later work locked under URAA / 95-year rules — verify per-piece).

### Tier-3: 19th-Century French / British Illustration
- **Gustave Doré, *Idylls of the King*** (1867–69, 36 plates, large-folio steel engravings) — Doré died 1883; PD worldwide. Definitive Tennyson visual interpretation of its era. ([Fisher Library — Doré Idylls](https://fisher.library.utoronto.ca/fisher-blog/making-tennysons-epic-more-muscular-gustave-dores-illustrations-idylls-king-0))
- **W.H. Margetson, Daniel Maclise, Julia Margaret Cameron** (photographs of Tennyson's Idylls, 1874) — PD.

### Tier-4: Medieval Illuminated Manuscripts
- **Lancelot-Grail / Vulgate Cycle illuminations** (BnF, BL, Bodleian holdings) — PD as 13th–14th-century works; reproductions from public archives PD.
- **Tristan en prose illuminations** (13th c.) — PD.
- **Roman de Brut manuscript illuminations** — PD.

### Recommended LoreVault Visual DNA
**Beardsley line + Burne-Jones twilight + medieval-illumination border-and-initial** = a Pre-Raphaelite jewel-box shrunken to card scale. Distinct from every modern Arthurian property. *No film viewer will mistake it for Boorman; no kid will mistake it for Disney.*

## 5. Top 5 Figures — Spine + Lampblack

**Arthur** — Spine: *the king who pulled the sword from the stone, founded the Round Table, was betrayed by his sister's son, and was carried to Avalon — not dead, just sleeping.* Lampblack: the Pendragon dragon-banner; the empty Siege Perilous; the once-and-future flag.

**Merlin** — Spine: *the prophet who counsels kings, sees the future, and is finally trapped under stone (or in a hawthorn, or a cave) by the woman he loved.* Lampblack: a prophecy-stone, a half-buried oak, a cave-mouth no one can find twice.

**Morgan le Fay** — Spine: *Arthur's half-sister, healer of Avalon and his shadow at once — the hand that wounds is also the hand that bears him to the isle.* Lampblack: a black mirror; nine queens in a barge; mistletoe.

**Lancelot** — Spine: *the best knight in the world, undone by the love that destroys his king's kingdom and his own claim on the Grail.* Lampblack: a broken white shield; a queen's chamber-window; the locked-out chapel of Corbenic.

**Galahad** — Spine: *the perfect knight, son of the imperfect father, who achieves the Grail and dies of too much grace.* Lampblack: an empty white shield with a red cross; a chalice rising in silent light; an empty seat at the Round Table.

**Universe Lampblack candidates** (residue across Moments): *the hilt of Excalibur*, *the scabbard's healing weight*, *the dragon-banner snapping in wind*, *the white hart bounding through Logres*, *the mist over the Lake*, *the Wasteland's blighted grain*, *the third throw of the sword into water*.

## 6. LoreVault Original — Cross-Tethers

**3-5 unique expressions:**
1. **"The Hand and the Throw"** — a Lampblack-only set: every card in this set shows only Bedivere's hand, the sword in mid-arc, the Lake's hand emerging — never a face. Distills the ending into pure gesture.
2. **"The Wasteland Inventory"** — the Fisher King's blighted realm rendered as still-life: a dry well, a barren furrow, a king bleeding into a cup. Lampblack of *failure-to-heal*.
3. **"Nine Queens in a Barge"** — the Avalon-departure as nine specific queens (Morgan, the Queen of Northgalis, the Queen of the Wasted Lands, etc. per Malory Book XXI), each with her own glyph.
4. **"The Round Table Empty"** — every Round Table card shows only the table and one empty seat — Siege Perilous if Galahad is yet to come, the king's seat if Camlann has happened. The set is read by *which seat is empty*.
5. **"The Mabinogi Branches"** — a Welsh-substrate-only set drawing on Bran's severed head, Rhiannon's birds, Arawn's hounds. Older-than-Malory primal stratum, visually distinct from the rest of the Universe.

**Cross-tethers to other Universes (Series 1 lock):**
- **Arthur ↔ Holmes** — both deduce-truth-from-clue; Arthur reads the sword-in-stone as Holmes reads a footprint. Cross-card moment: "The Last Deduction" — Bedivere watches the throw; Holmes watches the Reichenbach mist.
- **Morgan le Fay ↔ Wonderland Queen of Hearts** — shadow-sister archetype, the woman whose *no* unmakes a court. Lampblack: a black mirror that opens onto a chessboard.
- **Avalon ↔ Wonderland's Pane-of-Sleep / DREAM shell** — Arthur's "not dead, just sleeping" cross-tethers to Alice's dream-frame. The sleeping king is the dreaming child.
- **The Wasteland ↔ Gothic Horror's HOLLOW shell** — the Fisher King's blighted realm as the original *empty inside* trope; cross-tether to Frankenstein's hollow creation, Dracula's hollow castle, Jekyll's hollow self.
- **The Holy Grail as Lampblack-residue across all Panes** — every Universe has a thing-sought-and-not-fully-found. The Grail is the canonical exemplar; in Wonderland it's *home*, in Holmes it's *the truth*, in Gothic it's *the cure*, in Arthurian it's the Grail itself. **The Grail is the LoreVault cross-Pane meta-Lampblack.**

## 7. Aesthetic Totalism

**Palette:** verdigris-bronze (oxidized armor) + iron-rust (wound-color) + Pre-Raphaelite jewel (crimson-cinnabar, lapis-blue, peridot-green) + mist-grey + bone-white (Galahad) + dragon-red (Pendragon banner) + peacock-black (Beardsley line).

**Biome:** old-growth oak forest, mistal (foggy moor), mirror-still lake, isle-in-mist, ruined stone circle, mead-hall with fire-pit, jousting field with pavilions, ruined tower, the Wasteland (dry furrow, dead orchard), Tintagel cliff, Glastonbury tor.

**Iconography:** sword-in-stone, Excalibur, Holy Grail (chalice + bleeding lance pair), Round Table, scabbard, white hart, white stag, raven (Bran-substrate), the Lady's hand emerging from water, the Pendragon dragon-banner, the Siege Perilous, hawthorn (Merlin-trap), nine queens in a barge.

**Typography:** medieval illuminated initial-capital (think Lindisfarne / Book of Kells but secular Arthurian); alliterative-stress body type echoing the Pearl-poet and the *Brut*; Gothic blackletter for top-tier rarities; Beardsley-style ornamental flourishes.

**Set-glyph:** **a single drawn-broken sword crossing a chalice, both half-submerged in water-line.** Reads as Excalibur + Grail + Lake in one mark.

**Sample Moments by rarity:**
- **C (Common):** *Kay grumbling at the practice yard.* Beardsley-line sketch, peacock-black ink. Tag: "He calls every squire a fool. He is also the one who taught the king to ride."
- **R (Rare):** *Pellinore on the Questing-Beast's trail.* Howard Pyle pen-and-ink register, Logres forest. Tag: "Forty-one years on one quarry. The beast still breathes. So does he."
- **L (Legendary):** *The third throw — Bedivere at the Lake's edge, hand in air, sword in arc.* Burne-Jones twilight palette, mist-low. Tag: "Twice he hid the sword. Once he threw it. The hand was always there."
- **U (Unique / 1-of-1):** *The Last Sleep of Arthur in Avalon.* Direct visual citation of Burne-Jones' 1898 unfinished canvas. Nine queens, the king's stone-bier, the apple boughs. Tag: "Not dead. Sleeping. Until."

## 8. Audience Legibility

**Mass legibility:** *exceptionally high.* Arthur is the most universal English-language mythic register after the Bible. "King Arthur," "Excalibur," "Round Table," "Holy Grail," "Camelot," "Merlin," "sword in the stone" — every one is a single-word concept that doesn't require explanation in any anglophone market and most non-anglophone ones.

**Specialist depth:**
- **Medievalists** — Malory studies, Vulgate scholarship, Mabinogion philology, Chrétien manuscript tradition.
- **Pre-Raphaelite art collectors / Burne-Jones / Beardsley collectors** — a real audience that buys art books and visits Tate Britain for these works.
- **Welsh / Cornish / Breton cultural-identity audiences** — Arthurian legend as Brythonic-Celtic ancestral myth, distinct from the Norman-French Malory overlay.
- **Glastonbury / pagan / Goddess-revival audiences** — must-handle-carefully (see §10).

**Cultural pre-loads (continuously refreshed):**
- *The Green Knight* (A24, 2021, David Lowery) — recent prestige Arthurian, verbatim PD source (Pearl-poet) but distinctive directorial vision.
- *Cursed* (Netflix 2020), *King Arthur: Legend of the Sword* (Ritchie 2017), *Merlin* (BBC 2008–12), *King Arthur* (2004) — recurring TV/film cycle.
- Glastonbury Festival ongoing cultural moment (cumulative Glastonbury-as-Avalon syncretism).
- Welsh language and cultural revival, S4C, Eisteddfod.
- "King Arthur of the Britons" still romanticized broadly across the anglosphere; Tintagel as English-Heritage tourist site.

## 9. Recommendation

**STRONG candidate for Series 2 or Series 3.**

**Why strong:**
- Source canon spans 900 years, all PD; impossible to exhaust.
- Visual register through Beardsley + Burne-Jones + medieval illumination is *uniquely* PD-clean AND distinct from every modern Arthurian property — gives LoreVault a look no one else owns.
- Mass legibility is best-in-class; "King Arthur" needs zero translation.
- Welsh substrate (Mabinogion) gives a genuine novelty layer that 99% of Arthur-aware audiences have never seen rendered.
- Cross-tether logic (Avalon ↔ Wonderland's Pane-of-Sleep; Wasteland ↔ Gothic HOLLOW; Grail-as-meta-Lampblack) integrates cleanly into the Lattice.

**Why NOT Series 1:**
- The Disney/Boorman/White gravity well requires *more* aesthetic discipline than a Series-1 launch can afford. Better to ship Series 1 with the locked five (which presumably already navigate cleaner-PD waters), then bring Arthur in with a fully resolved visual identity.

**Recommended opening Moment:** **"The Sword and the Lake."** A single Legendary card, dual-rendered: one half is the boy's hand on the stone-set hilt (sword *in*); the other is Bedivere's third-throw hand and the Lady's hand from the Lake (sword *out*). The arc of the entire Universe in one card. Use Beardsley line + Burne-Jones palette + medieval-illuminated border-frame.

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

- **Welsh, Cornish, Breton ancestral mythology vs. Norman-French overlay.** Arthur, Myrddin, Gwenhwyfar, Bran, Rhiannon, Pwyll are *Brythonic Celtic* before they are Malory's. The Mabinogi predates the chivalric romance tradition that Chrétien grafted on. **Never present Arthur as exclusively English.** Welsh audiences are vocal and correct on this; Cornish and Breton audiences less numerous but equally specific. Use Welsh-language name-renderings (Gwenhwyfar, Myrddin, Cei) at least on rarer cards as a sign of respect.
- **Modern Glastonbury syncretism.** Glastonbury-as-Avalon is a *modern* identification (Glastonbury Abbey monks claimed the grave in 1191 — likely a publicity ploy after a fire). Real Glastonbury today is layered: Christian pilgrimage site + pagan/Goddess-revival site + festival-culture site + tourist economy. Treat with neutrality; do not editorialize.
- **Welsh-language and cultural revival.** S4C, Eisteddfod, and the rising prominence of Welsh-language media. A LoreVault Arthurian Universe that uses *only* English names will read as tone-deaf; one that uses Welsh names *only* will read as performative. Recommend **bilingual cards on the Welsh-substrate set** ("Bran the Blessed / Bendigeidfran"), English-primary on the Malory-substrate set.
- **Bradley association.** As noted in §3 — never cite *Mists of Avalon* even tangentially. Bradley's documented complicity in serial child sexual abuse is a brand-trust catastrophe waiting to happen. The Goddess-Avalon priestess framing she popularized is *not* in the PD canon; if LoreVault wants priestesses-of-Avalon, build them from Geoffrey's *Vita Merlini* (which has nine sisters of Avalon led by Morgen) rather than from Bradley.
- **Christian iconography of the Grail.** The Grail is unambiguously a Christian relic in Malory and Chrétien (Holy Blood / Last Supper cup). Some adaptations secularize or paganize it; LoreVault should default to the *PD-canonical Christian* framing and let the meta-Lampblack reading (Grail-as-thing-sought) carry the cross-Universe resonance — don't force a paganization that the source doesn't support.

## ODIN ESCALATION

1. **T.H. White / Disney 1963 mind-share** is so culturally dominant that *some* readers — including possibly some internal LoreVault reviewers — will reflexively conflate Wart-as-young-Arthur, Madame Mim, the wizard's-duel sequence, and the bumbling-pointy-hat Merlin with "the Arthurian canon." **They are not.** They are 1958/1963 derivative-locked work. Flag this in onboarding for any LoreVault writer/illustrator working in the Universe: *if it feels familiar from childhood, it's probably locked.* The PD-canonical Merlin is Geoffrey's prophet-engineer who designs Stonehenge and Chrétien's wild-man-of-the-woods, not Disney's fishbowl-tutor.

2. **Boorman's *Excalibur* (1981) aesthetic looms enormously large** — for many adult viewers, the silver-mirror armor, the green-emerald forest light, and Nicol Williamson's specific Merlin *are* the visual baseline of "Arthur." LoreVault must escape this gravity. The Beardsley + Burne-Jones + illumination register does so cleanly *if rigorously held* — but any drift toward "knight in mirror-polish full-plate in foggy green forest" will read as Boorman-derivative even if no specific element is borrowed. Visual-direction discipline matters here more than in any other candidate Universe.

3. **Mists of Avalon mind-share** is the third gravity well — among readers under ~50 with a feminist-Arthuriana lean, Bradley's Morgaine-centric framing is what "Avalon" means. LoreVault cannot use Bradley's framing AND should not ship anything that *reads as* Bradley-adjacent. Reframe Avalon through Geoffrey's *Vita Merlini* (PD, c. 1150 — nine sisters of Avalon led by Morgen the healer) and Burne-Jones' *Last Sleep* visual.
