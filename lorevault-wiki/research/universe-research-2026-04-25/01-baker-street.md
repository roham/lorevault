## 2. Public-Domain Legal Status (US + UK + ODIN flag)

### United States (current as of 2026)
- **All 60 Doyle Holmes works are now US public domain.**
- The last copyrights expired **January 1, 2023**, when the 1927-published *Case-Book of Sherlock Holmes* fell out of US copyright (95-year term post-1998 Sonny Bono extension).
- Pre-2023 the Conan Doyle Estate had asserted copyright over the **10 stories first published 1923–1927** ("the Ten"): *The Mazarin Stone*, *Thor Bridge*, *The Creeping Man*, *The Sussex Vampire*, *The Three Garridebs*, *The Illustrious Client*, *The Three Gables*, *The Blanched Soldier*, *The Lion's Mane*, *The Retired Colourman*, *The Veiled Lodger*, *Shoscombe Old Place*. (Note: the public count varied as terms staggered — the final twelve were the legal cliff and as of 1/1/2023 all are PD in the US.)
- *His Last Bow* (collection 1917) entered US PD in 2013.

### United Kingdom / EU
- UK is **life + 70**. Doyle died **July 7, 1930**, so all canon entered UK PD on **January 1, 2001**.
- Anomaly worth flagging: under the 1911 Copyright Act it was life+50, so canon was briefly UK-PD from 1981 → re-protected 1996 → re-PD 2001 (per CITMA / IPKat). Not relevant to our 2026 use but explains why old Granada 1980s adaptations sometimes look like they had freer hands.

### Trademarks (the live minefield)
The Conan Doyle Estate Ltd **does still hold registered trademarks** on certain phrases/marks (incl. SHERLOCK HOLMES word-marks in classes covering merch, games, entertainment) in multiple jurisdictions. The 2014 *Klinger v. Conan Doyle Estate* (7th Circuit, Posner, J.) and the 2020 *Enola Holmes* case both pushed back at the Estate's strategy of substituting trademark for expired copyright — but **registered marks remain enforceable for source-identification**, so we cannot title a release "SHERLOCK HOLMES™" or trade-dress it as the Estate's licensed merchandising line.

### Litigation history (cite-able)
- **Klinger v. Conan Doyle Estate, 755 F.3d 496 (7th Cir. 2014)** — Posner ruled Holmes/Watson and the pre-1923 character traits are PD; estate cannot demand licensing for new derivative works that draw only from PD canon. Awarded fees against the Estate for "quasi-extortionate" conduct.
- **Conan Doyle Estate v. Springer / Netflix / Legendary, 2020 (Enola Holmes)** — Estate sued Netflix claiming the empathetic, emotionally-warmer Holmes depicted in Enola Holmes derived specifically from the **Ten** still-copyrighted stories (Doyle's post-WWI, post-bereavement softer Holmes). Settled December 2020 on undisclosed terms; **no merits ruling**. As of 2023 the entire canon is PD, so the "empathetic-Holmes" theory is now moot for new derivatives.

## 4. Pre-Derivative Canonical Illustrators (PD — these are our visual DNA)

The single biggest IP-clean superpower for Baker Street: the **first-publication illustrators are themselves all out of US copyright** (pre-1931 publication = PD; further, all the relevant artists died well before 1955 so EU PD too).

1. **Sidney Paget (1860–1908)** — *Strand Magazine*, 1891–1904. **38 stories illustrated.** Invented the deerstalker cap and Inverness cape (Doyle never specified them — Paget read "ear-flapped travelling cap" / "close-fitting cloth cap" in *Silver Blaze* and *Boscombe Valley* and translated it). Tall, lean, hawkish-profile Holmes. **THIS IS THE LOREVAULT VISUAL ANCHOR.**
2. **Frederic Dorr Steele (1873–1944)** — *Collier's Weekly*, US, 1903–1905 (*The Return of Sherlock Holmes*) and onward through 26 of the last 33 stories. Painted Holmes after the actor **William Gillette** (the stage Holmes who introduced "Elementary, my dear Watson" and the curved calabash pipe to popular imagination). Steele's covers are richly chromatic — gas-amber, blood-red, fog-blue. Steele canonized the **curved calabash pipe** in the American imagination.
3. **Frank Wiles (1881–1963)** — *Strand* illustrations for *The Valley of Fear* (1914–15) and several *His Last Bow* stories. Slightly more cinematic, scenic.
4. **George Hutchinson** — illustrator of the 1891 Ward Lock edition of *A Study in Scarlet*. Pre-Paget Holmes (less iconic, useful for "first impression" renderings).
5. **Howard K. Elcock**, **Arthur Twidle**, **Walter Paget** (Sidney's brother — sometimes confused with him), **Gilbert Holiday** — secondary *Strand* illustrators for *His Last Bow* and *Case-Book*.

**LoreVault visual-DNA recommendation:** PRIME shell follows **Paget linework + Steele color + Wiles cinematic framing**. Cross-hatched ink line + warm gas-amber + cool fog-blue. Use Paget's facial geometry as the locked Holmes/Watson reference — this is the "Holmes-est Holmes" without crossing into any actor's likeness.

## 6. LoreVault Original Derivative Proposals (cross-tethers to other 4 Universes)

The Lattice doctrine wants every figure to braid across Universes. Five cross-tether proposals — IP-clean originals built only on PD foundations:

1. **Holmes vs. Wonderland-logic (× CARROLL).** The "Diogenes Riddle" — an inverse-Diogenes Club where silence is forbidden and only contradictions are spoken; Holmes meets the Cheshire Cat of testimony, where every witness statement is simultaneously true and false. *(Mechanic: a Moment-card with mutable text — "deduction in a non-Boolean room.")*
2. **Holmes investigates the Greek-Myth oracle (× GREEK).** The Delphic Pythia case: the prophecy is the murder weapon. Holmes' first encounter with a system whose premise (gods exist; oracles speak truth) breaks his epistemology. Lampblack residue: *the lens fogs before it cracks.*
3. **Holmes vs. the Grimm-Wood (× ENCHANTED KINGDOM).** A child disappears into a forest "no map admits"; the Brothers Grimm appear as competing investigators (folklore-method vs. deduction-method). Frame: which is more powerful — the pattern of pattern, or the pattern of story?
4. **Holmes at Castle Dracula (× GOTHIC).** Doyle wrote *The Sussex Vampire* (1924) explicitly debunking vampires — perfect canonical hook. Send Holmes east of the Carpathians where his rationalist instrument fails for the first time. Watson ends as the chronicler of *both* Holmes' logic and Stoker's Mina. Lampblack: the Reichenbach motif transposed into the Borgo Pass.
5. **The Mycroft Lattice (× all four).** Mycroft as the meta-investigator who silently catalogues every Pane — a Universe-crossing figure who appears at the edge of the frame in Wonderland, Olympus, the Black Forest, and Castle Dracula, never speaking, always present. Mycroft becomes LoreVault's in-canon avatar of the Lattice itself.

Each cross-tether ships as a 3–5-card mini-arc; not a separate Universe, but a thread that braids two locked Universes through a shared Moment.

## 9. Recommendation — Keep as Universe (Already Locked); Refinements

**Keep.** Baker Street is a mandatory Series-1 Universe. The PD foundation is now total (post-2023), the IP-clean illustrative inheritance (Paget + Steele) is the best of any of the 5 Universes, and the cultural pre-load through 2028 is unbeatable thanks to Amazon's Young Sherlock.

**Refinements to current spec (in priority order):**
1. **Lock the visual register to "Paget linework + Steele color + Wiles cinematic framing"** as the in-house style guide. Forbid art-team reference to *any* post-1930 adaptation visual element. Have IP counsel pre-approve a moodboard of only PD images.
2. **Drop "deerstalker" from any text claim about "Doyle canon."** Always credit Paget (1891) as the deerstalker's origin in PRIME-shell flavor copy. This both is true and pre-empts any "you don't know your canon" specialist attack.
3. **Curved-calabash pipe stays as iconography**, but flavor copy notes it is Steele/Gillette inheritance, not Doyle text. Same defensive logic.
4. **Avoid Enola Holmes entirely.** No "younger sister" character. If we want a young female detective, build one fresh from PD characters (Violet Hunter from *Copper Beeches*, or from Mary Morstan's pre-marriage life).
5. **Cross-tether Mycroft as the Lattice avatar across all 5 Universes** (proposal #5 in §6) — this is unique to LoreVault and is a brand-defining mechanic.
6. **Reichenbach as the Universe's signature Legendary Moment** — every collector should want one Reichenbach pull.
7. **Sussex Bee (His Last Bow) as the Unique-tier "retirement" Moment** — narrative closure card; supply-capped at 1.
8. **PRIME shell only for Series 1.** Hold CYBER (cyberpunk Holmes), MIRROR (gender-flipped), DREAM (dream-logic) for later expansions. Resist the urge to ship Shells we can't visually differentiate from the Cumberbatch/RDJ adaptations.

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

**Minimal but real.** Three flags for editorial / counsel:

1. **Tonga and the Andaman Islanders (*The Sign of the Four*, 1890).** The novel deploys Tonga as a literal racial caricature drawing on imperial-anthropological "savage type" tropes that scholars (McBratney; the *Shima* journal piece by Chatterjee) have documented as foundational to British colonial-racial discourse. The Andamanese are a real Indigenous people (still, today, on North Sentinel and Strait Islands; protected by Indian law). **Do not include Tonga as a card.** If we do *Sign of the Four* as a Moment, anchor on the Agra-treasure / Jonathan Small / Thames-chase axis and exclude the Andamanese caricature.
2. **Mormon-revenge backstory (*A Study in Scarlet*, 1887).** Doyle's Utah flashback portrays the LDS Church as a kidnapping cult. Modern Mormon readers find this offensive; the LDS Church publicly disavowed the depiction long ago. If we ship a *Study in Scarlet* arc, anchor on the London-half (the meet-cute, the Brixton lodging-house, "RACHE" written in blood) and skip the Utah flashback.
3. **Antisemitic / xenophobic asides.** Several stories carry casual period anti-Jewish or anti-Italian / anti-Eastern-European descriptors. Standard practice: paraphrase Doyle's plot beats; do not transcribe slurs into card flavor copy.

We are not making a "definitive Doyle text" product. We are making collectible Moments. Curate the canon's best 60–80 beats, exclude the colonial/sectarian ones, and we ship clean.

## ODIN ESCALATION — Trademark-vs-Copyright Overreach Watchlist

The following are cases where the **Conan Doyle Estate Ltd has historically asserted rights that exceed actual copyright**. IP counsel needs an opinion before we ship:

1. **The Estate has registered SHERLOCK HOLMES word-marks** in entertainment, games, and merch classes in multiple jurisdictions. These survive copyright expiration and are enforceable for *source-identification*. We should ensure our product naming and trade-dress make clear LoreVault is the source — not the Estate.
2. **The Estate's "empathetic / emotionally-warmer Holmes" theory** (asserted in *Enola Holmes*, 2020) is now MOOT — all the post-1923 stories the theory rested on are PD as of 2023. If the Estate threatens us on this theory, the answer is "Klinger + Case-Book PD = no claim."
3. **The Estate has historically sent demand letters** to authors/publishers using Holmes/Watson, even when the use was clearly drawn from PD canon. Posner in *Klinger* called this "quasi-extortionate" and awarded fees against the Estate. Our posture: do not respond to demand letters informally; route through counsel; cite *Klinger* and the 2023 PD cliff.
4. **"Mrs. Hudson" + "Mycroft" + "Moriarty" + "Irene Adler"** — all PD characters; no licensing required. The Estate has at times implied otherwise. They are wrong.
5. **The Sherlockian Society / Baker Street Irregulars (BSI)** — *not* a rights-holder; a fan organization. No license required, but we should engage them as cultural partners (specialist-channel marketing).
6. **The Sherlock Holmes Museum (239 Baker Street, London)** — operates trademarks on its own brand; not a rights-holder for the character. Do not visually replicate the museum's specific 221B-set props as photographed.
7. **"221B Baker Street" itself** — the address is fictional (Baker Street historically only ran to 100 in Doyle's time), in PD, no trademark. Free to use.

**Counsel ask:** Run a clearance search on (a) any LoreVault product/Moment titles using "SHERLOCK HOLMES" as a literal phrase vs. "BAKER STREET" or "THE GREAT DETECTIVE"; (b) opinion letter on Klinger + Case-Book PD posture for the demand-letter playbook.
