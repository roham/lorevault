## 2. PD Status — Itemized

**CRITICAL FINDING:** Lovecraft's late works are far more PD-available than the rolling 95-year clock suggests. Per Wikisource and the H.P. Lovecraft Wiki, **most Lovecraft stories' copyrights were never renewed at the 28-year mark**, dropping them into US PD on January 1 of the year following the renewal lapse. Library of Congress searches have produced no evidence of Lovecraft renewals (per S.T. Joshi, foremost Lovecraft scholar). Even Arkham House's own attorney admitted this in court during *Wandrei v. Derleth Estate* (1973–1986).

### Confirmed PD via 95-year term (deep safety)
- **Herbert West–Reanimator** (1922) — PD 2018
- **The Festival** (1925) — PD 2021
- **Pickman's Model** (1927) — PD 2023
- **The Colour Out of Space** (1927) — PD 2023
- **The Call of Cthulhu** (1928) — PD 2024
- **The Dunwich Horror** (1929) — PD 2025
- **Nyarlathotep** (1920), **Hypnos** (1923), **Statement of Randolph Carter** (1920), **The Rats in the Walls** (1924) — all PD on 95-year term

### PD via non-renewal (per Wikisource declarations and Standard Ebooks editions)
- **The Whisperer in Darkness** (1931) — PD via non-renewal; available as Standard Ebook
- **The Shadow over Innsmouth** (1936 book publication — only book during Lovecraft's life) — entered PD January 1, 1965 due to non-renewal; on Project Gutenberg
- **At the Mountains of Madness** (1936) — PD via non-renewal; Standard Ebooks edition
- **The Shadow Out of Time** (1936) — PD via non-renewal
- **The Haunter of the Dark** (1936) — PD via non-renewal
- **The Thing on the Doorstep** (1937) — PD via non-renewal
- **The Dreams in the Witch House** (1933) — PD via non-renewal
- **The Dream-Quest of Unknown Kadath** (written 1927, published posthumously 1943) — PD via non-renewal
- **The Case of Charles Dexter Ward** (written 1927, published posthumously 1941) — PD via non-renewal

### UNCLEAR / mark cautiously
- **Through the Gates of the Silver Key** (1934, co-written with E. Hoffmann Price) — Price's contribution may complicate; Price died 1988
- **The Mound** (Lovecraft ghost-wrote for Zealia Bishop, 1940 publication) — collaborative, ghost-write status
- Stories ghost-written or revised for clients (de Castro, Heald, etc.) — case-by-case

### Lovecraft Circle adjacent
- **Robert E. Howard** (d. 1936) — works published before his death enter PD on 95-year term, with most pre-1932 stories already PD; Conan stories begin 1932
- **Clark Ashton Smith** (d. 1961) — pre-1929 stories PD; later stories variable; his Tsathoggua and Hyperborea Cycle elements are mixed
- **Frank Belknap Long** (d. 1994) — most works still in copyright

### EU status
- All Lovecraft works PD across EU since January 1, 2008 (life + 70). Howard PD in EU since 2007.

**Sources:** Library of Congress non-renewal evidence per S.T. Joshi (*H.P. Lovecraft: A Life*); Wikisource per-story copyright templates; Standard Ebooks publication confirms producer-side legal review.

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield

**Locked — DO NOT INHERIT:**

- **Chaosium *Call of Cthulhu* RPG (1981, Sandy Petersen)** — Chaosium trademarks "Call of Cthulhu" for game products and protects specific stat blocks, monster descriptions, and the iconic 1981+ Cthulhu illustration silhouette. Per Chaosium's own Q&A: they "make no claim that [they] 'own' the Mythos" but DO protect "storylines, names, creatures, characters, descriptions, and depictions" they originated. The five-pointed-star Elder Sign with eye is **Derleth/Chaosium**, not Lovecraft. Lovecraft's Elder Sign was a tree-branch shape.

- **August Derleth additions (post-1937, Arkham House)** — Cthulhu/Hastur/Cthugha/Ithaqua "elemental" classification; the Elder Gods vs. Outer Gods moral binary (Lovecraft was morally indifferent — Derleth Christianized the cosmology); Cthugha as fire-elemental; the systematized "Cthulhu Mythos" naming itself. Derleth (d. 1971) — works still in copyright until 2042+. **Avoid the Derleth taxonomic frame entirely.**

- **Mike Mignola Hellboy / B.P.R.D.** — specific tentacle-and-rune aesthetic; Ogdru Jahad design; Lovecraft-adjacent imagery in Mignola's bold-shadow ink style. Dark Horse Comics IP.

- **Bloodborne (FromSoftware, 2015)** — specific Great One designs (Rom, Ebrietas, Kos, Mergo, Moon Presence); the "eldritch truth" UI/poetry register; Yharnam architecture. FromSoftware/Sony IP.

- **Lovecraft Country (HBO, 2020, Misha Green / Jordan Peele / J.J. Abrams)** — adaptation-specific monster designs (the shoggoths, the Topsy/Bopsy ghouls); specific cinematic Innsmouth depictions. Warner/HBO IP.

- **Stuart Gordon film canon (1985–2001)** — *Re-Animator* (1985), *From Beyond* (1986), *Dagon* (2001) — specific film designs (Jeffrey Combs as West, the syringe-and-reagent visual) are Empire Pictures / Brian Yuzna / Gordon estate.

- **Guillermo del Toro** — *Hellboy* film designs (with Mignola); his unfilmed *At the Mountains of Madness* concept art. Del Toro's specific shoggoth-pitch art is locked even though the film never shipped.

- **H.P. Lovecraft Historical Society** — specific silent-film and radio-drama aesthetics for *Call of Cthulhu* (2005) and *Whisperer in Darkness* (2011); their period-pulp visual register is theirs.

- **Cthulhu by Gaslight** (Chaosium, William A. Barton, 1986) — Victorian-era Mythos setting specific to Chaosium.

- **Specific cosplay/internet-canonized imagery** — the "plush Cthulhu" green-octopus-on-throne meme image (Toy Vault, late 1990s) has its own design copyright; the "Cthulhu for President" specific illustrations are individually owned.

**ODIN ESCALATION FLAG (a):** The most important practical finding is that the Derleth-Chaosium Elder Sign (star + eye) is the version everyone "knows" and it is NOT PD. LoreVault must use the original Lovecraft tree-branch Elder Sign or design fresh. Do NOT reproduce the star-eye sigil.

**ODIN ESCALATION FLAG (c):** Chaosium's "Cthulhu Mythos" trademark applies only to *game products* — not to collectible/literary-canon products. LoreVault should avoid the literal phrase "Cthulhu Mythos" in product taxonomy out of caution but is otherwise free to depict Lovecraft-original entities. Recommend legal review before naming a Set "Mythos."

## 4. Pre-Modern Canonical PD

Lovecraft commissioned almost no illustrations himself. The pulp magazines (*Weird Tales*, *Astounding Stories*, *Amazing Stories*) carried cover art by Margaret Brundage, Hugh Rankin, Frank Utpatel, Howard V. Brown, and Virgil Finlay — covers from 1923–1937 are mostly PD via non-renewal but require per-issue verification. **Result: there is no canonical, locked "Cthulhu look" predating Petersen 1981.** This is the rare PD Universe with an open visual canvas.

**Recommended pre-modern visual register (all deep PD):**
- **Francisco Goya, *Saturn Devouring His Son*** (1819–23) — the foundational cosmic-horror image; the indifferent gnashing maw
- **Hieronymus Bosch, *Garden of Earthly Delights*** (c. 1500), *Last Judgment*, *Temptation of St. Anthony* — the catalog of impossible biological hybrids; the bestiary register
- **Gustave Doré** — *Inferno* (1857), *Paradise Lost* (1866) illustrations; the cosmic-scale-with-tiny-human composition
- **Odilon Redon** — *The Eye, Like a Strange Balloon, Mounts Toward Infinity* (1882), *The Smiling Spider* (1881); the floating-sentient-organ image
- **Arnold Böcklin** — *Isle of the Dead* (1880–86); the ocean-cult-architecture register
- **Gustave Moreau** — Symbolist mythological tableaux; the jeweled-decadent-monster register
- **Caspar David Friedrich** — *Wanderer above the Sea of Fog* (1818), *The Sea of Ice* (1823–24); the antarctic / sublime-landscape register applicable to *Mountains of Madness*
- **Robert Hooke, *Micrographia*** (1665) — engraved microscopic creatures; the "Outer Gods as microbiology rendered cosmic" register; Mi-go and Shoggoths read as Hooke's flea/louse blown up
- **Medieval bestiaries** (Aberdeen Bestiary, c. 1200) — composite-creature heritage; the marginalia monsters
- **Athanasius Kircher's *Mundus Subterraneus*** (1665) and alchemical-Hermetic illustrations
- **Ernst Haeckel, *Kunstformen der Natur*** (1899–1904) — radiolarians, jellyfish, deep-sea biology rendered as ornate plates; **this is the single best PD register for Deep Ones, Shoggoths, and the Mi-go**
- **Piranesi, *Carceri d'Invenzione*** (1745–61) — impossible architecture; non-Euclidean staircase register

**Recommendation:** Layer Goya/Bosch (cosmic-bestiary horror) with Haeckel (deep-sea biology) and Piranesi (impossible architecture). This is a cohesive, fully-PD, literary-collectible-grade visual system that owes nothing to Petersen, Bloodborne, or Mignola.

## 6. LoreVault Original — Doctrine + Cross-Tethers

**Five Universal Expressions specific to LoreVault:**

1. **Cthulhu as the Pane-of-Sleep** — the dreaming god whose unconsciousness leaks into ours. Cross-tethers to Wonderland's DREAM register and to Greek-Hades (Hypnos = Hades's brother in classical myth, and Lovecraft already made Hypnos a god). LoreVault treats sleep as the universal porous Pane.

2. **The Necronomicon as cross-Pane occult artifact** — the book exists in every Universe. The Sherlock-Holmes Universe has a Necronomicon at the British Museum. The Wonderland Necronomicon is a children's primer. The Enchanted-Kingdom Necronomicon is a cookbook. The Lovecraft Necronomicon is the original. Cross-Pane lattice element.

3. **Innsmouth families as Lampblack-residue of body-horror across Gothic** — the hybrid-breeding curse cross-tethers to Frankenstein-corpus body-horror, to Jekyll-Hyde's split, to Wonderland's transformations. Innsmouth is the saltwater node of a body-horror lattice.

4. **The Antarctic + cosmic shells as AETHER mapping** — Lovecraft's *Mountains of Madness* and the Mi-go's Yuggoth provide LoreVault's deepest AETHER map: the geographic frontier where physics breaks. Cross-tethers to Verne, to *Frankenstein*'s arctic frame, to Poe's *Pym*.

5. **The Elder Sign (Lovecraft-original tree-branch form) as warding glyph** — LoreVault's anti-Lampblack, the ward that closes a Pane. Cross-Pane mechanic.

**The Doctrine of Reinterpretation (REQUIRED — non-negotiable):**

> *LoreVault inherits Lovecraft's cosmic-horror as a universal-existential register: the dread of the indifferent universe, the unbearable bigness of time and space, the sleep that drowns the world. We explicitly REJECT and disinherit Lovecraft's specific bigotries — his anti-Black racism, his anti-Semitism, his xenophobia toward immigrants, his contempt for Pacific Islanders and Indigenous peoples. The cosmic horror is the universe's indifference to ALL of us, not the racist's terror at the Other. Where Lovecraft coded fear-of-the-foreign-body into his monsters, LoreVault explicitly recodes those monsters as fear-of-the-indifferent-cosmos. Innsmouth's Deep Ones are not "racial degeneration" — they are the deep history of all earthly life. The Dunwich Horror is not "miscegenation" — it is the porousness of every Pane. Cthulhu is not the dark Other — he is what waits for all of us when the lattice fails.*

This rejection MUST be named explicitly in the Universe doctrine, in the opening Set's flavor text, and in the credits/back matter of every Lovecraft-Pane product. See §10.

## 9. Recommendation

**STRONG Universe candidate. Recommend as a Series 3+ slot with these specific characteristics:**

1. **Uniquely open visual canvas.** Unlike Disney/Marvel/Star Wars territory, there is NO dominant locked imagery. Petersen's 1981 Cthulhu and Mignola's tentacle-aesthetic and Bloodborne's Great Ones are all derivative — and we explicitly avoid all three. We can build the canonical LoreVault Cthulhu look from Goya + Haeckel + Piranesi and own it. **This is rare and valuable.**

2. **Deep PD inventory.** Per the non-renewal finding, ~95% of Lovecraft's mature-period catalog is already PD — including *Mountains of Madness*, *Innsmouth*, *Whisperer*, *Dream-Quest*, and *Charles Dexter Ward*. We are NOT waiting until 2031. We can ship the full Lovecraft canon now.

3. **Strong cross-Pane lattice anchor.** The Necronomicon, Cthulhu-as-dreaming-god, and the indifferent-cosmos doctrine are unusually portable across the other four Universes. Lovecraft is the AETHER node of the LoreVault lattice.

4. **Required: explicit racism reckoning in doctrine.** Non-negotiable. See §10. Frame the Universe as "cosmic horror reinvented with Lovecraft's specific bigotry interrogated and rejected." Use LaValle and Ruff as the published precedent.

**Opening Set candidates:**
- **"The Sign on the Lintel"** — the Elder Sign, the warding, the Necronomicon, Miskatonic University. A scholarly-discovery opening, light on monsters, heavy on lore, low on race-coded content. Safe entry point.

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity — The Lovecraft Racism Reckoning

**This is foundational, not optional.**

Lovecraft's racism is among the most-documented in 20th-century American letters. S.T. Joshi (foremost Lovecraft scholar, 30+ books): *"There is no denying the reality of Lovecraft's racism, nor can it merely be passed off as 'typical of his time'... It is also foolish to deny that racism enters into his fiction."* Even racists in Lovecraft's own circle considered his views extreme. His 1912 poem "On the Creation of Niggers" is unprintable. His letters are saturated with anti-Black, anti-Semitic, anti-immigrant, and anti-Polynesian content.

**Race-coded source material to handle with explicit care:**
- **"The Shadow over Innsmouth"** — the Deep Ones' Polynesian origin via Captain Marsh's South Pacific pact is read by scholars as a coded miscegenation panic. LoreVault recodes the Deep Ones as deep-time biological inheritance (we are all from the sea), not racial admixture.
- **"The Horror at Red Hook"** (1925) — Lovecraft's most explicitly anti-immigrant story, set among Brooklyn's Syrian/Kurdish/Italian communities. **DO NOT include in any LoreVault Set.** Cite as the story LaValle rewrote into *The Ballad of Black Tom*.
- **"Medusa's Coil"** (Zealia Bishop ghost-write, 1939) — has a notorious last-line racial reveal. **DO NOT include.**
- **"The Rats in the Walls"** (1924) — contains a black cat with an unprintable name. Use the story; rename the cat in any LoreVault depiction.
- **"The Call of Cthulhu"** — the Louisiana cult is described in race-coded terms (the swamp-cultists as "mostly mulattoes" etc.). LoreVault depicts Inspector Legrasse's raid as a multiethnic American sheriff's-posse encounter with a universal cosmic threat — not racial panic.
- **"The Charles Dexter Ward"** — contains anti-Black slurs in mob scenes; sanitize in adaptation.

**Templates we explicitly cite as our literary precedent:**
- **Victor LaValle, *The Ballad of Black Tom*** (Tor.com, 2016) — rewrites "Red Hook" from a Black protagonist's perspective; argues white supremacy is the deeper horror. Hugo and Locus nominee.
- **Matt Ruff, *Lovecraft Country*** (Harper, 2016) — Black 1950s family navigates Lovecraftian horror and Jim Crow simultaneously; HBO adapted it 2020.
- **Ruthanna Emrys, *Innsmouth Legacy* trilogy** (2017–2020) — rewrites Innsmouth from Deep One descendant Aphra Marsh's perspective; explicitly recodes the federal raid as ethnic-cleansing trauma.
- **Caitlín R. Kiernan** — long career rewriting Lovecraft from queer / non-cis perspectives.
- **Stephen Graham Jones** — Indigenous horror writer engaging Lovecraftian register.
- **Brown University's "Racial Imaginaries of H.P. Lovecraft"** (academic resource) — for editorial reference.

**LoreVault's editorial commitment:**
- No race-coded language in any Moment-card flavor text
- Diverse cast across human-figure cards (Innsmouth descendants, Miskatonic faculty, Legrasse's deputies all reflect the actual ethnic composition of their settings)
- Acknowledgments page on every Lovecraft-Universe product naming the racism reckoning explicitly
- Editorial board includes at least one scholar of weird-fiction race-reckoning (LaValle, Emrys, or Kiernan as named consultant if available)

## ODIN ESCALATION SUMMARY

**(a) PD status of late-1930s stories — RESOLVED IN OUR FAVOR.** The original brief assumed 95-year term (Mountains of Madness PD 2031, Innsmouth PD 2031). The actual finding: per Wikisource, Standard Ebooks, Project Gutenberg, and S.T. Joshi's scholarship, **most Lovecraft stories are already US PD via copyright non-renewal**, including all the late-period work. Even Arkham House's own attorney admitted this in court (*Wandrei v. Derleth Estate*, 1973–86). Set composition is not constrained by the rolling 95-year clock — we can ship Innsmouth, Mountains of Madness, Whisperer in Darkness, Dream-Quest, and Charles Dexter Ward immediately. Recommend conservative legal review of each specific story before commitment, but the Library of Congress searches by S.T. Joshi found no renewals. This unlocks the Lovecraft Universe far earlier than projected.

**(b) Lovecraft racism reckoning — REQUIRED IN DOCTRINE.** Non-negotiable. See §6 (Doctrine of Reinterpretation) and §10 (Cultural Sensitivity). Frame the Universe explicitly as "cosmic horror reinvented with Lovecraft's specific bigotry interrogated and disinherited." Cite LaValle and Ruff as the published precedent. Do not include "Red Hook" or "Medusa's Coil." Sanitize race-coded content in retained stories. This is the single largest brand-risk variable for the Universe.

**(c) Chaosium / Arkham House overreach claims — LARGELY DEFLATED.** Chaosium itself admits they don't own the Mythos and only protect specific Chaosium-originated game elements. Arkham House's historical "we own everything" claim was demolished in court 1973–86; their own lawyer argued the works were PD. Practical caution: avoid the literal "Cthulhu Mythos" phrase in product taxonomy (Chaosium has it as a game-product trademark); avoid the Derleth/Chaosium star-eye Elder Sign (use Lovecraft's original tree-branch form); avoid Derleth's Cthugha/elemental/Outer-vs-Elder-Gods Christianized framework (post-1937 Derleth is in copyright until 2042). Otherwise the Lovecraft-original mythos elements are clear.
