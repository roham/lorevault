## 4. Pre-Modern Canonical PD Visual Register

This is the LoreVault visual key for Pulp Heroes — pre-Frazetta, pre-Disney:

- **J. Allen St. John** (1872–1957) — definitive Burroughs illustrator from 1916 (*Beasts of Tarzan*) through ~1936. **Pre-1930 St. John work is unambiguously US PD;** later work rolling. 36 ERB books, 14 Tarzan dust jackets, 9 *Weird Tales* covers. ERBzine archives most of this in clean scans. This is the **canonical "godfather of fantasy art"** register and the *true* Tarzan/Carter look that pre-dates and out-classes the post-1965 Frazetta-Vallejo register. See [ERBzine 2118](https://www.erbzine.com/mag21/2118.html).
- **Frank Schoonover** (1877–1972) — illustrated the first *Tarzan of the Apes* book edition (1914) and early Burroughs for *All-Story*. Pre-1929 work PD. Howard Pyle student; Brandywine school polish.
- **Roy Krenkel** (1918–1983) — Frazetta's mentor on the early Ace paperback Burroughs covers (1962–63). NOT PD; modern. *But* Krenkel's pen-and-ink style intentionally evoked St. John, so the *aesthetic ancestry* is locatable in PD St. John.
- **Hugh Rankin, Margaret Brundage, Hannes Bok** — *Weird Tales* covers; pre-1929 Brundage and pre-1929 Rankin work PD; later rolling. Brundage's pastels did Conan / Solomon Kane covers.
- **Frank R. Paul** (1884–1963) — *Amazing Stories* SF-pulp covers (1926–1939). Pre-1929 PD; defines the early-SF chrome-and-rivet aesthetic. Skylark of Space first edition cover.
- **Original *All-Story* and *Argosy* covers** — Modest Stein, N.C. Wyeth-school anonymous, etc. Pre-1929 PD. Banner typography is a separate research thread.

**LoreVault visual recommendation:** anchor the Pulp Heroes register to **J. Allen St. John (pre-1929) + Frank Schoonover (pre-1929) + Frank R. Paul (pre-1929)**. This is the visual canon BEFORE Frazetta's 1965+ reinvention and is *more historically accurate to the source* than the post-Frazetta look every subsequent licensee has imitated. It also carries automatic "old illustration plate" legibility that signals "literary heritage" rather than "1980s fantasy paperback."

## 5. Top 5 Figures — Spine + Lampblack

**Tarzan** — Spine: human raised by apes; the lord-of-jungle who can pass as English aristocrat but never wholly belongs to either. Lampblack: the bone-handled hunting knife (his dead father's, the only artifact of his original civilization), the rope, the chest-call yell, the "ungawa"-style ape-language fragments. The three-cultures-no-home figure. **Cross-tether:** Mowgli (Kipling, 1894 — also PD); Romulus & Remus.

**John Carter** — Spine: Civil War Confederate cavalry captain (this is in the source — he is explicitly an ex-Confederate, which is a *significant* problem to interrogate, see §10) who astrally projects to a dying Mars and becomes a princess-rescuing warlord-by-skill. Lampblack: the longsword, the radium pistol, the unexpected gravity-leap (lower gravity = higher jumps), the "I still live" mantra, Tharks's tusks and four arms.

**Conan** — Spine: Cimmerian barbarian who passes through every Hyborian-Age role (thief, mercenary, pirate, general, king) by sword and force. Lampblack: the broadsword, the gloomy "Crom" theology (a god who cares nothing for prayer), the laughter-in-the-face-of-doom, the moody Cimmerian fatalism. **Cross-tether:** Heracles, Beowulf, Sigurd.

**Solomon Kane** — Spine: 16th-century English Puritan who wanders the world (Africa, the Americas) hunting evil with saber, flintlocks, and Juju-staff; vengeance-driven beyond reason. Lampblack: Puritan slouch-hat, the saber, the Spanish-style mustache, the staff from N'Longa. **Cross-tether:** Demon-hunter archetype; the wandering paladin; Witchfinder.

**Doc Savage** — Spine: bronze-skinned superman trained from birth by his father's team of scientists; runs a Fortress of Solitude in the Arctic (predates Superman's by five years); five "aides" each a specialist (the Fabulous Five). Lampblack: torn-shirt-revealing-bronze-physique, gear-belt, the supermen's-gym ritual, the "Bronze" name. **Original "Pulp Hero" template — every "team of specialists led by genius" trope (Avengers, Justice League) descends from this.**

## 6. LoreVault Original — 5 Unique Expressions

1. **The Pulp-Adventure Shell as cross-cutting register, not single Universe.** Pulp adventure isn't *a* Universe — it's a *style-shell* that can wrap any of the named figures. Treat it like a Borrowed-Pane that pollinates Greek-Myth (Conan as primal-pre-civilization Heracles), Norse-Myth (Conan as Ragnarok-warrior), Gothic-Horror (Solomon Kane bridges to demon-hunter), and Cyber-Aether shells (John Carter as proto-cyberpunk astral-projection; Lensman as proto-space-opera).
2. **Tarzan ↔ Mowgli sister-fork.** Both 1894–1912, both jungle-raised, both PD. LoreVault's "Feral Heir" sub-arc binds them as TWO answers to the same archetype-question. Mowgli is Hindu-Buddhist mythopoetic-cosmos; Tarzan is colonial-aristocratic-Darwinian. The pair tells a richer story than either alone.
3. **John Carter as Greek-Hero-on-Mars.** Carter cross-tethers Heracles (impossible feats), Odysseus (the warrior on a strange shore), and Norse-Warrior-Shadow (the Confederate-soldier framing is a *problem* but the warrior-on-foreign-soil shape is the eternal one). Barsoom = Mars-as-Hades — a dying, beautiful, exhausted world.
4. **Solomon Kane bridges Gothic-Horror to Norse-Fenrir-binder.** Kane's Puritan-with-saber-against-demons is the proto-Witcher; he interlocks with the Gothic Universe's Van Helsing register and Norse's Fenrir-binding register. Use him as a **connective figure** rather than a standalone.
5. **Doc Savage / Shadow / Lensman as the proto-Cyber/Aether shell.** Doc Savage's Fortress-of-Solitude, his rigorously-trained super-body, and his team-of-specialists is the *direct ancestor* of Iron Man, Batman, and the Avengers. Lensman is the *direct ancestor* of Star Wars, Green Lantern, and every space-cop sub-genre. Treat the 1930s SF-pulp register as an **AETHER+CYBER bridging shell** to a future Series 4/5 Tech-Pulp Universe.

## 7. Aesthetic Totalism

- **Palette:** pulp-cover lurid red, jungle green, Mars rust-orange (Barsoom's iron oxide), sword steel + sword blood, bronze skin (Doc Savage), sun yellow on yellowed-newsprint, Weird Tales chartreuse-and-purple banner, slouch-hat black (Shadow / Kane).
- **Biome:** Africa-of-imagination jungle, Mars dead-sea-bottoms and canals, Hyborian-Age cities (Aquilonian marble, Stygian black-stone pyramids, Cimmerian pine-darkness), Pellucidar perpetual-noon, Cimmerian-mountains, Arctic Fortress, 1930s-Manhattan rain.
- **Iconography:** sword (always — the spinal weapon), knife (Tarzan), the radium-pistol, the four-armed Thark, the calot, the loincloth, the saber-and-Puritan-hat, the Lens (telepathic crystal worn at the wrist), bronze-figure-in-torn-shirt, slouch-hat-and-cloak, pulp-magazine banner, two-fisted-pistols, the princess-in-peril, the rescue-leap.
- **Typography:** pulp-cover banner-headline (heavy block-serif: "WEIRD TALES," "AMAZING STORIES," "ALL-STORY"); adventure-title brushed-italic for figure names; sub-banner kicker for the story title.
- **Set-glyph:** crossed-sword-over-pulp-magazine-banner; alternates: Conan-helm-silhouette, slouch-hat-silhouette, bronze-fist.

**Sample Moment specs:**
- **C (Common):** "Tarzan calls Tantor" — St. John-style ink line, jungle-green palette, base rarity. Lampblack: the bull-elephant's ear, the chest-call.

## 8. Audience Legibility

- **Mass:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Tarzan is one of the 20th-century's most-recognized fictional figures; Conan has had three film waves (1982/2011/animated) and an active gaming franchise (Funcom *Conan Exiles*). John Carter has lower mass-recognition (Disney 2012 film flopped) but high among SF readers. Doc Savage and The Shadow are niche-classic. Lensman is genre-deep only.
- **Specialist:** VERY HIGH. Pulp-fiction collectors are an old, organized, well-capitalized hobby (PulpFest, Windy City Pulp & Paper, the Robert E. Howard Foundation, ERB Inc. fan club). Sword-and-sorcery readers are a distinct buyer-cohort. Mars/Barsoom enthusiasts overlap heavily with classic-SF collectors.
- **Cultural pre-loads:** continued Conan film/game adaptations through 2030; ongoing Mars colonization conversation (SpaceX, NASA Artemis) refreshes Barsoom; ongoing pulp-revival in book-cover design and "weird fiction" subculture; *Stranger Things*-style retro-pulp aesthetic mainstream.

## 9. Recommendation

**SERIES 2: Borrowed-Pane status.** Use a single Pulp-Heroes Borrowed-Pane within Series 2 to test the mechanic — recommend a **John Carter / Tars Tarkas one-Moment pane** within the Greek-Myth Universe (Series 2 anchor), as a "Hero-Across-the-Stars" cross-tether. The text is fully PD; the trademarks are ERB Inc.'s.

**STRATEGIC RISK FOR THIS PANE:** ERB Inc.'s 2012-Dynamite playbook means even a single Borrowed-Pane labeled "John Carter" *invites a trademark suit*. Mitigations:
- Use **"Captain Carter of Mars"** or **"Warlord of Barsoom"** branding (Dynamite's own work-around)
- Hard-anchor visuals to **St. John pre-1929 illustrations** (defensibly PD; nothing of Disney/WETA)
- Get an outside-counsel pre-clearance memo before mint
- Consider EU-launch-first to use the cleaner UK/EU PD posture

**SERIES 3 (target 2028+): Full Pulp Heroes Universe.** When Conan's *Phoenix on the Sword* rolls into US PD on Jan 1 2028, open the full Universe with Conan as anchor figure, Solomon Kane (already PD) as the Gothic-bridge, Tarzan and John Carter as the Burroughs spine, Doc Savage / Shadow on **lazy-rollout** as their copyrights expire (Shadow Jan 1 2027, Doc Savage Jan 1 2029).

**SERIES 4+: Tech-Pulp / Aether-Cyber Universe** anchored on E.E. Smith Lensman (most Magazine-version content believed PD via failure-to-renew) and A. Merritt's lost-world fantastiques. This is a separate research deliverable.

**ALTERNATIVE: Cross-cutting "Adventure" Shell.** Don't make Pulp-Heroes a Universe at all. Make "Pulp-Adventure" a *visual-narrative shell* that overlays existing Universes (Greek-Myth + Pulp-Adventure shell = "Pulp Heracles" line; Norse + Pulp-Adventure = "Pulp Beowulf"). This sidesteps every trademark trap because no figure is named — the shell is style only. **Worth A/B-testing against Borrowed-Pane in Series 2.**

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity — VERY HIGH

This is the highest-friction Universe in the LoreVault inventory. LoreVault must commit to interrogating the legacy in editorial material, Codex pages, and product copy — NOT pretend it doesn't exist.

- **Tarzan's colonial Africa-of-white-imagination.** Burroughs never set foot in Africa. His Africans are uniformly portrayed as savages; "Tarzan" in ape-language means "white skin"; Tarzan's superiority is genetically encoded as the *English aristocrat's birthright* (Lord Greystoke). Critics Edward Said-school have correctly identified this as a foundational text of 20th-century cinematic racism. Burroughs's *African* characters in particular are treated as scenery rather than persons. CNN, *Pacific Standard*, and academic commentary (Academia.edu papers) have catalogued this exhaustively. **LoreVault editorial line:** name this directly in Codex pages. Do not include lampblack from the most racist passages. Lean on St. John's *visual* Africa (jungle, animals, terrain) rather than Burroughs's *peopled* Africa.
- **John Carter's Confederate-soldier protagonist.** This is canonical: Carter is *explicitly* a Confederate cavalry captain, written by a Chicagoan-born-1875 working in a Lost-Cause-saturated era (1912). Disney's 2012 film soft-pedaled this. LoreVault cannot. **Editorial line:** acknowledge in Codex; position Carter as "post-war wanderer who found his moral education on Mars" — Burroughs's text *does* show Carter forming an honor-bond with non-human Tharks across racial lines, which is at least *legible* against the Confederate framing. Do not erase. Do not whitewash.
- **Howard's racism.** Howard's letters (collected in the Foundation archive, also catalogued by Jason Sanford and *Conan Chronology* commentary) include genuinely disturbing views — the "Shemite" coding (read: Jews) is pervasive; he referred to a biracial man as "it." Some scholars (Robert B. Marks, others) argue Howard's racism *softened* late and was sometimes *subverted* in Conan's African Bêlit / Black Coast arcs; others (Sanford) argue his work is not worth preserving. **Editorial line:** acknowledge in Codex; LoreVault does not republish slurs; Conan's Black Coast / Bêlit arc (Bêlit is a Shemite-coded but explicitly heroic pirate-queen and Conan's lover-equal) is a reclaim-able tether and should be foregrounded. The Picts as "savages-noble" framing should be re-interrogated rather than reproduced.
- **Solomon Kane's Puritan violence.** Less a racism issue and more a religious-violence-celebration issue. Kane is a 16th-century Puritan with a saber who hunts demons — the genre is "demon-hunter" and the religious frame is presentable, but it must be presented as character ideology not editorial endorsement.
- **Pulp-era visual register's gendered iconography.** The "princess-in-peril" / "muscled hero rescues nearly-nude woman" register (especially Frazetta-derivative, but also present in Burroughs source) is a legacy. LoreVault editorial line: **lean on the pre-Frazetta St. John register** (which was less skin-emphasizing and more ballet-of-action) rather than the post-1965 Frazetta cheesecake. This is also the IP-safest visual line.

## ODIN ESCALATION — Three Flags

**(a) ERB Inc. and Conan Properties / Heroic Signatures will sue.** The trademark-as-shield strategy is *untested at trial* — every challenger has settled. ERB Inc.'s 2012 *Dynamite* suit is the playbook. Heroic Signatures has the same posture for Conan. Doc Savage / Shadow are still Condé Nast and Condé Nast has sued before (Boing Boing 2006). **Decision required:** is LoreVault willing to *take the trademark fight to trial* if forced, or is settle-and-license the planned posture? This determines whether we engage these figures at all in Series 2/3, or wait for a friendly precedent. Recommend: pre-clear with outside counsel; budget for one trademark-suit settlement at Borrowed-Pane stage as cost-of-test; do not engage Doc Savage or Shadow until Condé Nast posture clarifies or copyrights expire (2027 / 2029).

**(b) Frazetta Estate is the most-aggressive third-party enforcer in the genre.** *Frazetta v. Spurlock* (2022) shows: they catch infringement, they file in M.D. Fla., they win statutory damages and inventory destruction, they have technical forensics for forgery defense. Treat the post-1965 Frazetta visual register as completely off-limits. Treat Boris Vallejo, Ken Kelly, Sanjulian as same-tier risk. **The St. John pre-1929 register is the only safe pulp-visual home.** Train any LoreVault image generation on St. John / Schoonover / Paul *only* and explicitly negative-prompt against Frazetta / Vallejo styles.

**(c) The cultural-imperialism baggage of the entire pulp-adventure tradition is irreducible and must be addressed editorially, not dodged.** Every figure in this Universe has a problem. Tarzan = colonial racism. John Carter = Confederate framing. Conan = Howard's racism. Solomon Kane = religious-violence celebration. Doc Savage = "Mongol Heritage" eugenicist undertones in early issues. The Shadow = "Yellow Peril" antagonists in 1930s issues. Lensman = literal eugenicist cosmology (Arisia breeds humans for the Lens). LoreVault cannot publish this Universe without an explicit editorial framework that names these legacies, refuses to launder them, and uses Codex pages as the surface for that interrogation. **Decision required at CEO / GC level before greenlight:** does LoreVault have the editorial appetite to take this on publicly, with the rigor of (e.g.) the Penguin Classics critical-edition treatment of *Heart of Darkness*? If not, defer the Universe entirely. If yes, this becomes one of LoreVault's strongest demonstrations that PD-canon products can be culturally serious.
