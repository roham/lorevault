# LoreVault Universe Research — Norse Myth (Series 2+ Candidate)

**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Status:** PD-rich, Marvel/Disney IP-saturated. Highest-reward / highest-risk candidate in the bench. Walkable on a thin line — but only if we explicitly forge an Iron-and-Ash register that visually predates and disowns the MCU.
**Headline:** Norse Myth is the **single largest IP-minefield** in the candidate set. Marvel + MCU + Vikings (History) + American Gods + Wagner-Bayreuth have over a century of derivative locks layered onto a deeply public-domain medieval source. The PD is rock-solid. The aesthetic must be surgically chosen to avoid Marvel collision.

## 2. PD Status

**Deep PD, top-tier safe at the source layer:**

- **Codex Regius / Poetic Edda** — manuscript ~1270, but composed material centuries earlier. Public domain.
- **Snorri Sturluson's Prose Edda** — ~1220. Public domain.
- **Heimskringla** (Snorri, sagas of Norwegian kings) — ~1230. Public domain.
- **Sagas of Icelanders** (Njál's, Egil's, Laxdæla, Grettir's) — 13th–14th c. Public domain.
- **Völsunga saga** — late 13th c. Public domain.
- **Saxo Grammaticus, Gesta Danorum** — c. 1200, with the euhemerized short-haft Mjolnir narration. Public domain.
- **Beowulf** — Old English manuscript ~1000 CE. Public domain.

**Public-domain English translations:** Brodeur 1916 (Prose Edda) is confirmed not-in-copyright. Bellows 1923, Hollander 1928, Thorpe 1866 (Poetic Edda) also out of copyright. Project Gutenberg hosts them. ([Project Gutenberg — Prose Edda](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/18947)) ([Internet Sacred Text Archive — Prose Edda](https://sacred-texts.com/neu/pre/index.htm))

The literary substrate could not be more open. The risk lives entirely in the **derivative visual layer.**

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield — 15 Specific Locks

Critical principle (legally confirmed): **Marvel/Disney does not own Norse gods.** They own *their specific expressions* of them. The names and the myths are PD. Every visual signature below is owned by Marvel and must NOT be replicated. ([Snopes — Disney Trademark Norse Gods](https://www.snopes.com/fact-check/disney-trademark-norse-gods/)) ([Washington Post — Disney trademarked Loki](https://www.washingtonpost.com/religion/disney-loki-redbubble-trademark/2021/07/02/d59908ec-d9c0-11eb-8fb8-aea56b785b00_story.html))

1. **Marvel Mjolnir geometry** — the long-hafted, squared-blocky hammerhead Kirby silhouette. Avoid completely. Use the historically attested **short-haft cruciform amulet form** documented from 1,000+ Viking-age finds (Lolland 2014, Sweden 2022 finds, Mammen-style decorated). ([Wikipedia — Mjölnir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mj%C3%B6lnir)) ([Archaeology.org — Sweden Hammer](https://archaeology.org/news/2022/11/03/221104-sweden-thor-hammer/))
2. **"WHOSOEVER HOLDS THIS HAMMER, IF HE BE WORTHY..." inscription** — 100% Marvel-original (Stan Lee / Kirby creation, never in Eddic source). Do not riff on, paraphrase, or echo this inscription. Use authentic runic inscriptions like the Lolland "Hmar x is" / "this is a hammer" instead.
3. **Hemsworth-Thor specific portrayal** — long blond hair styled, beard cut, red cape, bare arms with armor, winged helmet (when worn), specific muscular silhouette. Avoid blond-haired clean-shaven Thor; lean into the **red-bearded, ill-tempered, glove-of-iron-and-belt-of-strength Thor** of the Eddic sources.
4. **Hiddleston-Loki specific portrayal** — tall slim, long black hair slicked back, green-and-gold scaled costume, horned helmet (the curving thin-arc horns are MCU-distinctive). Avoid. Use **gender-fluid, mother-of-monsters, scarred-mouth Loki** with rust/charcoal palette, never green-and-gold.
5. **MCU Asgard "shimmering golden city"** — the crystalline-spire skyline, polished gold-and-bronze towers, dome roofs. Avoid. Render Asgard as **Iron-Age timber longhalls with carved gables, turf roofs, and a great mead-hall (Valhalla) with shield-shingled walls** — Oseberg/Borre carvings, not Marvel CGI.
6. **MCU rainbow Bifrost** — the prismatic crystal-tunnel, the observatory dome, the spinning sword-key mechanism. Avoid. Bifrost in source is a **burning bridge** ("trembling path") partly of fire — render as a smoke-and-fire arc, not a rainbow disco-tube.
7. **Idris Elba Heimdall** — the gold horned helmet, the orange-eyed gaze, the giant key-sword. Avoid that specific look; use the **Gjallarhorn-bearer-at-the-edge** with bone horn and grey wolfskin.
8. **Marvel Sif (Jaimie Alexander)** — black-haired warrior in red leather + steel. Avoid. Sif in source is **golden-haired** (the *whole point* of the Loki-cuts-her-hair myth).
9. **Marvel Frigga (Rene Russo)** — silver-blonde regal queen in green-and-gold robes. Avoid the costume language.
10. **Marvel Asgardian armor patterns** — the layered plate-and-leather with circle-bosses and rune-detail. Avoid the proprietary geometry; use **archaeologically attested Vendel/Valsgärde helms and Viking ring-mail** instead.
11. **Vikings (History 2013–2020) Ragnar Lothbrok** — Travis Fimmel's specific look: shaved-sides + long top, single tight braid, eye kohl, neck tattoos. Avoid this specific visual; if we depict Ragnar (saga-figure, PD), use 19th-c Romantic-painter register or runestone-stylized. ([Wikipedia — Travis Fimmel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travis_Fimmel)) ([TVInsider — Vikings braids](https://www.tvinsider.com/84855/how-the-vikings-braids-mistress-keeps-the-shows-warriors-looking-fierce/))
12. **Vikings Lagertha specific portrayal** (Katheryn Winnick) — blonde shield-maiden braids + leather-and-fur layered look. Avoid that specific costuming.
13. **American Gods Mr. Wednesday** (Gaiman 2001+, Starz 2017+) — old-grifter Odin in three-piece suit, Ian McShane silhouette. Avoid the modern-grifter framing entirely; LoreVault Odin is mythic-temporal, not contemporary-Americana.
14. **Wagner / Bayreuth Brünnhilde** — winged-helmet valkyrie trope (a 19th-c Bayreuth costume invention, not in source). The trope itself is PD-old, but specific Bayreuth productions have stage-design copyrights. Use **Rackham 1911 valkyrie** as the safe canonical PD register, not the corny horned-bra trope.
15. **Marvel-trademarked verbal/visual marks** — "The Mighty Thor" (USPTO 1970), Marvel's specific Loki costume-text "Low Key" (litigated 2021), specific MCU-9-Realms visualization, specific MCU Mjolnir-flight FX. ([Screenrant — Mighty Thor trademark](https://screenrant.com/the-mighty-thor-legal-name-marvel/))

**ODIN ESCALATION** — Disney/Marvel are litigious. The three most dangerous overlaps in priority order: **(1) Mjolnir geometry**, **(2) Loki appearance & costume**, **(3) Asgard architecture & Bifrost design**. We must visibly differentiate on all three from Set 1 of any LoreVault Norse offering.

## 4. Pre-Marvel PD Register — The Iron-and-Ash Vocabulary

Recommended visual stack — every layer pre-1925 and PD-clean:

- **Arthur Rackham, 1910–1912 Wagner Ring illustrations** — 64 watercolor plates for *Siegfried & The Twilight of the Gods* (1911) and *The Rhinegold & The Valkyrie* (1912). The canonical PD Norse-Wagnerian register. Brooding watercolor + line, ethereal forests, gnarled gods. ([Rackham — Wagner Ring](https://www.artpassions.net/rackham/wagner_ring.html)) ([Internet Archive — Rhinegold & Valkyrie](https://archive.org/details/rhinegoldvalkyri00wagn))
- **Peter Nicolai Arbo (1831–1892)** — Norwegian master of Norse history-painting; *Battle of Stamford Bridge* (1870), *Åsgårdsreien* / Wild Hunt (1872), *Valkyrien* (1865). Public domain. ([WikiArt — Arbo, Battle of Stamford Bridge](https://www.wikiart.org/en/peter-nicolai-arbo/battle-of-stamford-bridge-1870)) ([Eclectic Light — Arbo Valkyries](https://eclecticlight.co/2016/05/09/the-story-in-paintings-peter-nicolai-arbo-valkyries-and-mermen/))
- **Constantin Hansen** (Danish, 1804–1880) — Danish Golden-Age, Norse-mythology paintings. PD.
- **Carl Larsson** (Swedish, 1853–1919) — Norse-mythology murals; PD by 2026.
- **W.G. Collingwood** — *Thorstein of the Mere*, 1908–09 Volsung illustrations. PD.
- **Lorenz Frølich** — 19th-c Norse-myth illustrator, prolific. PD.
- **Saxo Grammaticus medieval illuminations** — direct PD.
- **Hylestad stave church portals** (12th c., now in Museum of Cultural History Oslo) — Sigurd-Fafnir cycle in carved-wood relief: Sigurd-piercing-dragon, Sigurd-roasting-heart, Grani-with-birds, Gunnar-in-snake-pit, Regin-decapitated. ([Wikipedia — Hylestad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hylestad_stave_church)) ([Pitt — Sigurd Portal](https://sites.pitt.edu/~dash/sigurddoor.html))
- **Ramsund stone** (~1030 CE, Sweden) — flat-rock carving of full Sigurd-Fafnir cycle. PD. ([Wikipedia — Sigurd stones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sigurd_stones))
- **Iron Age archaeological art:** Oseberg ship-burial carvings (~834 CE) — gripping-beast motifs; Mammen axe (~970 CE) — silver-inlay ceremonial; Borre style (~840–970, gripping beasts with prominent heads); Jellinge, Ringerike, Urnes styles (later sinuous animal-interlace). ([Wikipedia — Viking art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viking_art)) ([Sons of Vikings — Viking art styles](https://sonsofvikings.com/blogs/history/viking-art-styles))
- **Picture stones of Gotland** (Stora Hammars, Tjängvide) — 8th–11th c. PD relief carvings depicting Odin/Sleipnir/Valkyries.
- **Bayeux-Tapestry-adjacent embroidered registers** for narrative panel work.

**Recommendation: Iron-Age-archaeological core + Rackham-Wagner aerial/ethereal layer + Arbo/Frølich Romantic-history register.** The result is unmistakably *not Marvel.* It is bone, iron, smoke, watercolor mist, runestone relief.

## 5. Top 5 Figures — Spine + Lampblack

**Odin — the One-Eyed Pane-Walker**
- *Spine:* All-Father; god of war, wisdom, runes, poetry, the dead. One-eyed (traded at Mimir's well). Self-hanged nine nights on Yggdrasil to win the runes. Flanked by Hugin & Munin (ravens), Geri & Freki (wolves). Rides Sleipnir. Wields Gungnir. Wanders disguised as a grey-cloaked traveler.
- *Lampblack:* The eye he kept sees too much; the eye he lost remembers everything. Wisdom-as-self-mutilation. The Lattice's wandering eye — the residue of every sacrifice he made *to himself*.

**Loki — the Binder/Unbinder, Mother-of-Monsters**
- *Spine:* Æsir-by-oath, jotunn-by-blood; gender-fluid (bore Sleipnir as a mare); father of Fenrir/Jörmungandr/Hel; engineered Baldr's death; chained beneath the venom-serpent until Ragnarök, when he sails Naglfar.
- *Lampblack:* The shape that won't hold. The Lattice's seam where one realm leaks into another. Loki is the Mirror-Shell-residue of the cosmos — the thing that names what is hidden by changing.

**Freyja — Falcon-Cloak, Brísingamen**
- *Spine:* Vanir; goddess of love, war, seid-magic, gold. Owns Brísingamen (the necklace of fire); rides cat-drawn chariot or wears the falcon-cloak. Takes half the war-dead to Folkvangr (Odin takes the other half to Valhalla). Weeps gold tears.
- *Lampblack:* The double-claim — love and the battlefield are the same hand. The Lampblack shimmer of gold over corpses. Series mechanic: Freyja's tears are a deck-currency.

**Fenrir — the Bound Wolf**
- *Spine:* Loki's wolf-son, raised by the gods, bound by Gleipnir (made of impossible things — cat's footfall, woman's beard, mountain's roots, fish's breath, bird's spittle, bear's sinews). Tyr lost his hand swearing the false oath. At Ragnarök, breaks free, swallows Odin, is killed by Vidar.
- *Lampblack:* The promise that will be broken at the appointed hour. Cross-tether to Enchanted Kingdom's Wolf-as-Lampblack-residue: the same predator-archetype, here in the shape of a chained eschatology.

**Sigurd — the Dragon-Slayer**
- *Spine:* Volsung-line; raised by smith Regin; reforges father's broken sword Gram; kills Fafnir-the-dragon (originally Regin's brother, twisted by gold-greed); tastes dragon-blood, gains bird-tongue understanding; takes the cursed Andvaranaut ring; wakes Brynhildr from the ring-of-fire; betrayed; murdered.
- *Lampblack:* Gold *is* a curse-substrate. Every coin is haunted. Sigurd is the Lattice-figure who proves that even a hero's hand cannot un-curse what he picks up. Cross-tether to Greek's Achilles (the doomed-hero archetype) and to Gothic's revenant-of-buried-treasure.

## 6. LoreVault Original Derivative Proposals

Three to five LoreVault-original framings that ride the PD source while clearly diverging from Marvel:

1. **"Odin the Pane-Walker"** — Odin reimagined as the wandering eye of the Lattice itself. His traded eye becomes the *primary lens* of the LoreVault Cosmology — the perspective from which other Universes are seen. Card-mechanic: Odin reveals the top of any Universe's deck because he has already paid the price for that knowledge. The one-eye-traded-for-wisdom motif is *perfectly* aligned to Lampblack-as-residue.
2. **"Loki the Mirror Shell"** — Loki as the cross-Universe interface itself. Where Sherlock has *deduction*, Loki has *transformation*. Mechanic: Loki cards can copy the silhouette of any creature already in play across any Universe. This makes Loki our **canonical cross-tether interface character**, consistent with his Eddic mother-of-monsters / shape-changer essence and dramatically un-Marvel because we strip green-and-gold and replace with rust-and-charcoal.
3. **"Sigurd & the Curse-Coin"** — every gold-token in the Volsung sub-set carries a curse-ledger that propagates to whoever holds it. This makes the Volsung mini-set our economic-game-design playground inside the Universe — direct from PD source (Andvaranaut), zero MCU footprint.
4. **"Fenrir the Promised Wolf"** — Fenrir cross-tethers to Enchanted Kingdom's Big Bad Wolf via shared Lampblack Wolf-residue: same archetype, two iterations. EK's Wolf is the predator-of-the-cottage; Fenrir is the predator-of-the-cosmos. Both speak. Both are bound by language.
5. **"Heimdall at the Pane"** — Heimdall as the watcher-at-the-seam between Universes. His Gjallarhorn signals when a Lampblack-residue threatens to leak from Norse-pane into another. Mechanic: trigger condition for cross-Universe events.

## 7. Aesthetic Totalism — The Iron and Ash Register

**Palette:** Iron-grey + amber + bone-white + blood-red + ice-blue + ember-orange. **The forge-and-fjord register, NOT the Marvel-Asgard-gold-shimmer.** Palette signature: cold grey-blue base, hot red-amber accent, bone-white highlight. The card-frame should *read* as cold metal and old fire, not polished CGI gold.

**Biome stack:**
- Iron-Age timber **longhouse** with turf roof and shield-shingled walls
- frozen **fjord** with grey water and grey sky
- **Yggdrasil** as the cosmic axis-mundi, ash-tree with three roots reaching three wells (Urd, Mimir, Hvergelmir), inhabited by eagle-at-crown, Nidhogg-at-roots, Ratatoskr-running-the-trunk
- **dragon's lair** of black scales and gold-coin floor (Sigurd/Fafnir register)
- **mead-hall** with central long-hearth, smoke-vent, carved gable-beasts
- **ash of Ragnarök** — the post-fire grey-on-grey landscape
- **runestone clearing** under birch
- **stave-church** Christian-Norse hybrid with Sigurd-portals

**Iconography:**
- **Rune** (single rune as set-glyph candidate)
- **raven** (Hugin/Munin)
- **wolf** (Fenrir/Geri/Freki)
- **axe** (Mammen-style silver-inlay)
- **drinking horn / mead-cup**
- **hammer with SHORT haft** per medieval lore — explicitly the Lolland-cruciform shape
- **arm-ring** (Iron-Age oath-ring, not jewelry)
- **longboat-prow** with gripping-beast carving
- **world-tree** silhouette
- **runestone in profile**

**Typography:** runic-adjacent display face (NOT actual runes for body — runes are scarce and we'd misuse them) + scaldic alliterative-stress cadence in card flavor-text. Triple-stress lines. Kennings ("whale-road" = sea, "battle-sweat" = blood).

**Set glyph candidates:** (a) single rune (Algiz / Tiwaz / Eihwaz), (b) Yggdrasil-tree silhouette, (c) Mjolnir-cruciform-Lolland-shape. Recommend **Yggdrasil silhouette** as primary — uniquely Norse, zero MCU collision.

**Sample Moments by rarity:**
- **Common (C):** *Drink at the Long-Hearth* — a man passes a horn to a stranger; smoke; firelight; rune-graffiti on a roof-beam
- **Rare (R):** *The Forging of Gleipnir* — six dwarves at an anvil binding impossible-things into a chain that looks like ribbon; Fenrir watching from the dark
- **Legendary (L):** *Odin's Self-Hanging* — nine nights on Yggdrasil, runes flickering into being beneath his skin; one-eye-bleeding-light, Hugin and Munin circling. Rackham-register watercolor.
- **Ultimate (U):** *Heimdall Lifts the Horn* — at the edge of Bifrost (rendered as smoke-and-fire arc, NOT prismatic crystal), wolf-skin cloak, Gjallarhorn raised, the first note about to break the world. The Ragnarök-ignition Moment.

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

Norse Myth is **not just mythology — it is living religion** to a non-trivial population. Treat as such.

- **Modern Heathenry / Ásatrú is a recognized religious practice** in Iceland (state-recognized 1973), the US, the UK, Scandinavia. Universalist branch is the dominant strain. Treat Odin / Thor / Frigga / Freyja with the same respect we'd grant any living religious tradition. Do not mock; do not gimmick. The tone is *reverent fan-of-the-myth*, not *Saturday-morning-cartoon*. ([Wikipedia — Heathenry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heathenry_(new_religious_movement))) ([Norse Myth Blog — American Heathenry](https://www.norsemyth.org/2021/09/articles-of-faith-american-heathenry.html))

- **White-supremacist appropriation is the genuinely dangerous risk.** Specific symbols co-opted by Nazi/neo-Nazi/folkish movements: Othala rune, Sonnenrad / Black Sun, Tiwaz/Tyr, Algiz, Sig-runes, Valknut (in some contexts), and certain Mjolnir-amulet styling. The Nazi Party explicitly used Viking iconography for Aryan-purity narratives; that legacy is still live in 2026 (Charlottesville 2017, Capitol Riot 2021 Q-Shaman, etc.). **LoreVault must actively reject any signaling that resembles folkish/folkist Heathenry.** ([SPLC — Racist Odinist Religion](https://www.splcenter.org/resources/reports/new-brand-racist-odinist-religion-march/)) ([Inside Edition — Asatru fight](https://www.insideedition.com/inside-asatruisms-fight-against-the-co-opting-of-their-religion-by-white-supremacists-83137)) ([Wild Hunt — Q-Shaman response](https://wildhunt.org/2021/01/heathens-respond-to-q-shaman-and-norse-imagery-in-capitol-riot.html))

  Concrete dos/don'ts:
  - Avoid Othala, Sonnenrad, isolated Sig-runes, Tiwaz alone, Black Sun. Be cautious with Valknut.
  - Use **Yggdrasil, Mjolnir-cruciform-amulet, raven-pair, runestone-in-context, Oseberg-prow** — universalist-Heathen-aligned, not folkish.
  - Visually depict **diverse skin tones** in raid-parties, mead-halls, and pantheon. Historical Vikings traded with North Africa, Constantinople, Central Asia. The "all-blond Viking" image is itself a 19th-c Romantic invention. Diversity is *more historically accurate*.
  - Partner with universalist-Heathen consultants or consult The Troth / Heathens United Against Racism for set-1 review.
  - In any narrative copy, never invoke "blood," "purity," "ancestry-by-bloodline," "folk," or "kindred" in the folkish-Heathen sense.

- **Sami / Finnish indigenous-adjacent material:** Some Northern-Norse material bleeds into Sami (joik, drum, Lapland landscape) and Finnish (Kalevala) traditions. Sami in particular is a living indigenous people with active concerns about cultural appropriation. **Avoid Sami iconography (Lavvu tents, Sami drums, joik motifs, Sami clothing patterns). Avoid Kalevala material (Väinämöinen, Sampo) — adjacent but distinct, and Finland has its own cultural-property sensitivities.** Stay in Norse-Iceland-Denmark-Norway-Sweden visual lane, not the indigenous-circumpolar lane.
