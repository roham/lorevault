## 2. PD status

**Deep PD, no ambiguity.** Hesiod *Theogony* and *Works and Days* c. 700 BCE; Homer *Iliad* and *Odyssey* c. 750 BCE; the 5th-century-BCE tragedies of Aeschylus, Sophocles, Euripides; Apollonius Rhodius *Argonautica* 3rd c. BCE; Virgil *Aeneid* 19 BCE; Ovid *Metamorphoses* 8 CE; Apollodorus *Bibliotheca* 1st-2nd c. CE; Hyginus *Fabulae* 1st-2nd c. CE; Pausanias *Description of Greece* 2nd c. CE — all 1800+ years past life-of-author plus any conceivable term.

US case law confirms it: a California district court has held that *"the immortal gods are free for all mortal beings to use — and none can claim exclusive rights to them under copyright law"* (the "Mere mortals have no exclusive rights in Greek gods" ruling, summarized in Lexology). The Copyright Alliance's reading of the *scènes à faire* doctrine is even broader: *"Greek gods, dialogues among them about mortal affairs, rivalries among the gods, and mythical beasts such as the Hydra or the Nemean Lion are unprotectable elements."*

**Classical art is also deep PD.** Doryphoros, Discobolus, Venus de Milo, Laocoön Group, Belvedere Apollo, Boxer of Quirinal, Charioteer of Delphi, Riace bronzes, Nike of Samothrace, the Parthenon marbles, Athenian red-figure and black-figure pottery, Corinthian aryballoi — all out of copyright by 2,000+ years; museum-photograph claims to "new copyright" via *Bridgeman v. Corel* are unenforceable in the US for faithful 2D reproductions.

**Renaissance through Symbolist painting is mostly PD.** Botticelli (1480s), Caravaggio (1590s), Bernini (1620s) — deep PD. The 19th-century Academic and Pre-Raphaelite painters who built the modern visual register of Greek myth — Bouguereau (Birth of Venus 1879), Cabanel, Waterhouse (Hylas and the Nymphs 1896, Echo and Narcissus 1903, Ulysses and the Sirens 1891), Burne-Jones (Perseus series 1875-1898), Frederic Leighton, Lord Leighton (Flaming June, Captive Andromache), Gustave Moreau (Oedipus and the Sphinx 1864), Lawrence Alma-Tadema, John Singer Sargent — all dead before 1955, all PD globally. **This is the single most important fact for the Greek Myth Universe:** the dominant *visual* register the modern eye reads as "Greek mythology" — academic painting from 1850-1910 — is fully usable.

## 5. Top 5 figures — Spine + Lampblack

### Persephone
- **Spine (PD, Hymn to Demeter):** Maiden in the meadow plucks a narcissus, the earth opens, Hades takes her, Demeter grieves and the world goes barren, Zeus orders return, the pomegranate seed binds her to half a year below.
- **Lampblack:** The split self. The girl who learned the room she was taken to. The seed eaten knowingly, not by trick — the moment of complicity in her own becoming. **Avoid the Lore-Olympus pink-pixie-cut and the Hadestown spring-dispenser. Anchor visually in Bernini's *Rape of Proserpina* + Evelyn De Morgan's *Demeter Mourning Persephone*** for the doubled sorrow and the marble-thigh-finger-imprint.

### Achilles
- **Spine (Iliad):** Wrath. The withdrawal from battle. Patroclus dies in his armor. The return, the dragging of Hector, the pity for Priam.
- **Lampblack:** The man who chose the short bright life over the long dim one and grieved the choice. The heel as the only mortal place in him. **Avoid Pitt-Troy-blonde-warrior and Miller-Song-of-Achilles-Patroclus-narrator.** Anchor visually in red-figure pottery Achilles + the Riace bronzes + Frederic Leighton's *Captive Andromache*-register grief paintings.

### Odysseus
- **Spine (Odyssey):** Cunning. The wandering. Polyphemus, Circe, the Sirens, Hades's threshold, Calypso's seven years, the home he doesn't recognize, Penelope's bow-test, the slaughter of suitors.
- **Lampblack:** The man who is too clever to die and pays for it in years. The lie as a survival tool that becomes a homecoming problem. **Avoid Damon-Nolan-2026-Odyssey once it ships.** Anchor in Waterhouse *Ulysses and the Sirens* + Draper's same-titled work + classical hero-statuary.

### Medusa
- **Spine (Hesiod / Apollodorus / Ovid):** A Gorgon — Ovid adds the Athena-temple-rape-by-Poseidon-as-cause-of-curse (Met. 4), which is the modern feminist anchor. Petrifies by gaze. Perseus uses the polished shield, the helm of Hades, winged sandals, the harpe. Her blood gives birth to Pegasus and Chrysaor.
- **Lampblack:** The victim made monstrous and then killed for the monstrousness she didn't choose. **Avoid Harryhausen's snake-lower-body design (1981/2010 lock).** Anchor in **Caravaggio's *Medusa* shield (1597, deep PD)** + classical-pottery winged Gorgons (Athenian black-figure) + Frederick Sandys' *Medusa* + Burne-Jones's Perseus cycle Gorgon panels.

### Prometheus
- **Spine (Hesiod, Aeschylus *Prometheus Bound*):** Fire-thief. Foreknowledge ("Pro-metheus" = "forethought"). Bound to a Caucasus rock. The eagle eats his liver daily; it regrows nightly. Heracles eventually frees him.
- **Lampblack:** The cost of the gift to humans. The body as the site of recurring punishment for a one-time act. **No big derivative lock here** — Prometheus is the one major figure not heavily repackaged in modern pop. Anchor in classical-statuary muscular-bound-Titan + Rubens's *Prometheus Bound* (1611-12) + Gustave Moreau register.

## 7. Aesthetic totalism

## 10. Cultural sensitivity

Greek myth carries baked-in gender violence (Zeus's serial assaults — Leda, Europa, Io, Callisto, Danae; Apollo/Daphne; Hades/Persephone; Poseidon/Medusa). Modern feminist retellings (Haynes, Saint, Barker, Miller) have made the violence visible; the audience now reads these stories with that lens.

**Navigation:**
- Don't aestheticize assault as romance. The Lore-Olympus / "Hades is sexy" read is *culturally laminated* among 18-30 women but it's also *contested* by classicists and many feminist readers as an aestheticization of abduction. LoreVault's collector base (financial-gambler-collectors per the CEO context) skews male and likely doesn't want either pole — neither romance-Hades nor takedown-Hades. Stay in the *Hymn to Demeter* register: descent as loss, return as conditional, the seed as binding. Tragic, not romantic. Not didactic, not preachy.
- Apollo/Daphne: render as transformation-and-grief, not chase-as-comedy. Bernini's sculpture is the visual anchor — terror in her face, leaves in her fingers.
- Zeus's affairs: depict via the *consequences* (the children, the punishments visited on the women) more than the *acts*.
- Medusa: Ovid's curse-by-Athena-after-Poseidon-rape framing is the modern feminist anchor and is deep PD. Use it.

**Modern Greek diaspora cultural ownership:** very minor concern. Greek myth is universally treated as world-cultural-patrimony, not Greek-national-IP. Modern Greece has not asserted cultural-ownership claims over it (cf. how Norse myth handles cleanly via Iceland). Light caution: don't play the gods as buffoons in a way that reads as Western-orientalist mockery (the Disney-Hercules problem). The classical-statuary register is in fact the *respectful* register.

## ODIN ESCALATION

**The question:** Has the modern romance-Hades visual (Lore Olympus + Hadestown + Supergiant *Hades* + the broader BookTok / AO3 Hades-Persephone-as-true-love wave) become so culturally laminated that LoreVault cannot use a Hades/Persephone romance frame at all without infringement?

**Legal answer (low-confidence; consult counsel):** No, not infringement. The myth is deep PD and the *romance reading itself* is not copyrightable — only specific expressions are. Copyright Alliance and *Mere Mortals* are clear: ideas, archetypes, plot-tropes are unprotectable; specific creative expressions are. LoreVault can do a Hades/Persephone romance pull without infringing as long as it does not borrow Smythe's pink-pixie-Persephone, Mitchell's Depression-mining-town Underworld, Supergiant's specific portrait designs, or Disney's blue-flame-hair Hades.

**Cultural answer (higher-confidence; this is the real risk):** Yes, the romance-Hades visual is *so laminated* that any LoreVault attempt at a modern-romance-Hades frame will be read by audiences as derivative of Lore Olympus regardless of legal cleanness. The cultural lamination is the bigger risk than the legal lock. Two responses possible:

1. **Avoid the romance frame entirely.** Anchor in the *Hymn to Demeter* descent-as-loss register. Renaissance and Pre-Raphaelite oil-painting register. This is the recommended path. It's also more distinctive — a market full of Lore-Olympus-derivative collectibles benefits from the *one* product that goes the other way.
2. **Lean into the romance frame but in an *anti-laminated* visual register.** Bernini sculpture + Caravaggio chiaroscuro + Symbolist gold-leaf — explicitly *not* webtoon-flat-color, explicitly *not* Hadestown-pinstripe-industrial. Rare and Legendary tier only, never Common (where the lamination effect is strongest).

**Recommendation: option 1.** The Greek Myth Universe should be the *un-Lore-Olympus*. Marble, bronze, oil paint. Tragic, oracular, hieratic. Let the rest of the market do romance-Hades; LoreVault is the one that does Bernini's Persephone with Hades's marble fingers indenting her thigh.
