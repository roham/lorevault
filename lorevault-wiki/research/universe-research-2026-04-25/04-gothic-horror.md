# LoreVault Universe Research — Gothic Horror

**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Universe slot:** 04 (Series 1 of 5 locked)
**Researcher mandate:** Stoker (Dracula 1897), Shelley (Frankenstein 1818/1831), Stevenson (Jekyll & Hyde 1886), Leroux (Phantom 1909–10), Polidori (Vampyre 1819), Le Fanu (Carmilla 1872). Lovecraft handled in a separate dossier.
**Headline:** Gothic Horror is a deep public-domain Universe with a uniquely sharp derivative-rights gradient. The minefield is not the texts — it is the **iconic 20th-century film silhouettes** that the public has fused with the figures. **A January 1, 2027 cliff dramatically clears the field**: the 1931 Universal Dracula and Frankenstein films enter U.S. public domain that day. Series 1 ships into the most permissive Gothic-Horror legal environment in the 95-year history of these designs.

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield — 14 Locked Elements

This is the single most important section in the dossier. **The texts are free; the iconic film silhouettes are not.** Each item below is a specific copyrighted/trademarked work that LoreVault must NOT recreate.

1. **Lugosi Dracula 1931 — Universal.** Slicked-back widow's-peak hair, white tie + tails + medallion, full-length opera-cape with stand-up collar, hypnotic close-up eye-light. **Cliff: U.S. PD on January 1, 2027** under the Copyright Term Extension Act (1931 + 95 = 2026, expires end of year). [Wikipedia 2027 in PD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2027_in_public_domain). Until then: avoid the *specific* silhouette.

2. **Karloff / Pierce Frankenstein's monster 1931 — Universal.** Flat-topped square skull, neck electrodes ("bolts"), heavy brow ridge, frock-coat-and-asphalt-spreader's-boots silhouette, signature green skin (added by Universal merch in the 50s). Jack Pierce's 4-hour makeup is the locked work. **Same January 1, 2027 cliff.** Universal has aggressively enforced this design — Sony's Hotel Transylvania was forced to redesign (Frankenstein with no green skin and no neck bolts; Dracula with pink-lined cape). [Plagiarism Today](https://www.plagiarismtoday.com/2011/10/24/how-universal-re-copyrighted-frankensteins-monster/), [Screen Rant](https://screenrant.com/hotel-transylvania-universal-monsters-different-looks/).

3. **Lon Chaney Phantom 1925 — Universal.** Chaney's self-devised skull-face makeup. **Currently U.S. PD** (Universal failed to renew copyright in 1953). [Public Domain Review](https://publicdomainreview.org/collection/the-phantom-of-the-opera-1925/). The 1925 silhouette is a free asset; flag the unusual gift.

4. **Bride of Frankenstein 1935 — Universal.** Elsa Lanchester's vertical-zigzag bouffant with white lightning-streaks, bandage-shroud gown, the hiss. **Cliff: U.S. PD on January 1, 2031.**

5. **Wolf Man 1941 — Universal.** Pierce yak-hair makeup on Lon Chaney Jr. (Lon Chaney's son). **Cliff: 2037.**

6. **Hammer Christopher Lee Dracula 1958+.** Bloodshot-red eyes, fangs visible, tall lean silhouette in monastic black, the Hammer red. Hammer / StudioCanal own. Avoid the *specific* eye-and-fang style and Hammer's signature scarlet blood.

7. **Hammer Frankenstein cycle 1957–74 (Peter Cushing).** The Cushing-as-Victor cold patrician aesthetic.

8. **Coppola / Eiko Ishioka 1992 *Bram Stoker's Dracula*.** The locked silhouettes:
 - Red-armor dragon-knight prologue (segmented blood-armor, the dragon-helm).
 - Red lacquered-silk robe with the long-cuff "Old Dracula" silhouette.
 - Top-hat-and-blue-spectacles Victorian London look (Gary Oldman in Piccadilly).
 - Mina's red dress / wolf-encounter sequence.
 - Ishioka won the Costume Design Oscar; Sony Pictures owns. Powerful design, totally locked.

9. **Branagh / Mary Shelley's Frankenstein 1994.** De Niro's stitched-Creature look, Branagh's wet-leather bare-chested Victor.

10. **Andrew Lloyd Webber / Maria Björnson Phantom 1986 (musical).** **The vertical-half white mask covering the right side of the face is a Björnson original — NOT in Leroux.** Realized by milliner Rodney Gordon. Really Useful Group enforces aggressively. Leroux's Erik wears a *full* mask and is described with a death's-head face beneath. **LoreVault Phantom must use a full mask, a Persian/Venetian style, or a different geometry — NEVER the vertical half-mask.**

11. **Joel Schumacher Phantom 2004 film.** The same half-mask plus added tropes (the rose, etc.).

12. **Penny Dreadful (Showtime, 2014–16, John Logan).** Specifically: **Vanessa Ives** (Eva Green) is Logan's original character — NOT in any source novel. Locked. The ensemble's specific costume signatures are also Showtime's. (Note: a 2023 lawsuit by Anna Biani alleged Logan lifted Vanessa from a forum RP; status of that suit is **UNCLEAR** as of this writing — does not change the fact that LoreVault must treat Vanessa as Showtime IP.)

13. **Netflix / BBC *Dracula* 2020 (Moffat-Gatiss)**. Claes Bang's specific portrayal, Sister Agatha character (Moffat-Gatiss original), the "I am Dracula… and I bid you welcome" pub-scene reframings.

14. **Robert Eggers *Nosferatu* 2024.** Bill Skarsgård's specific Orlok design, Eggers's specific moustache/decay aesthetic. (Note: *Nosferatu* the 1922 Murnau film is itself PD because the Stoker estate's lawsuit caused its prints to be ordered destroyed but copies survived; Murnau-specific Orlok rat-design is **PD in U.S.**)

**Trademark vs. copyright distinction:** Universal holds USPTO **trademark registrations** on FRANKENSTEIN as a brand mark (e.g., USPTO 73271294, 74505230) for entertainment/merchandise. Trademark does NOT block use of a public-domain figure in a creative work, but it DOES block use that suggests Universal source/sponsorship, and it is the lever Universal pulls in cease-and-desist letters. **LoreVault should never use the word "Universal Monsters," should not use the locked makeup designs, and should not market the Universe in a way that implies a Universal Studios tie-in.**

## 4. Pre-Derivative PD Visual Register

These are LoreVault's safe visual reference well — illustrators and painters whose Gothic atmospheres pre-date or run parallel to Universal/Hammer/Coppola, and whose works are unambiguously in the public domain.

- **Theodor von Holst** (1810–1844). The 1831 Frankenstein frontispiece (engraved by William Chevalier) — Victor recoiling from the freshly-awakened Creature on the slab, moonlight through the lab window, skull on the floor. **PD.** This is the *original* visual canon for the Creature, and it shows a beautiful long-haired figure — not the flat-headed Karloff brute. Lampblack source-of-truth.
- **Henry Fuseli, *The Nightmare* (1781).** The succubus on the chest of the sleeping woman, the horse's blank eye through the curtain. PD. Foundational Gothic-painting reference; Fuseli was Holst's mentor.
- **Caspar David Friedrich** (1774–1840). *Wanderer Above the Sea of Fog*, *Abbey in the Oakwood*, *The Sea of Ice* (which directly informs Walton's Arctic). PD. Atmospheric biome.
- **John Martin** (1789–1854). Apocalyptic-sublime engravings; the visual scale of Romantic terror. PD.
- **Aubrey Beardsley** (1872–1898). PD. Decadent black-and-white linework — Yellow Book / *Salomé*. Adjacent to but not directly illustrating the canon; usable for typography and Gothic-decadent border art.
- **Sidney Sime** (1865–1941). Lord Dunsany's illustrator; ink-and-wash phantasmagoria. PD in U.S. for pre-1930 work; UK PD 2012.
- **Arthur Rackham** (1867–1939). Pre-1930 Rackham (e.g., 1909 *Undine*) is U.S. PD. Twisted-tree silhouettes, faerie-grotesque. PD coverage in life+70 jurisdictions varies.
- **Harry Clarke** (1889–1931). His 1919 Poe *Tales of Mystery and Imagination* monochrome plates and the 1923 colour plates are widely treated as PD; Clarke d. 1931, so life+70 jurisdictions cleared **2002**, U.S. clear (pre-1930 publication). Public Domain Review hosts them. **Confirmed PD.** Highest-value visual cousin to LoreVault's Gothic spine — stained-glass linework, fever-dream colour.
- **Gustave Doré** (1832–1883). PD. *Inferno*, *Don Quixote*, *Raven*. Atmospheric crowd-and-architecture reference for asylum, opera, ship.
- **Friston & Fitzgerald** *Dark Blue* magazine illustrations to *Carmilla* (1871–72). PD. The original visual canon for Carmilla.
- **Original 1901 Dracula frontispiece** (Constable abridged edition). PD. Dracula descending the wall lizard-fashion, his name in shouting capitals — the only illustration Stoker himself approved.

**UNCLEAR / verify:** Specific later-life Rackham works (post-1930). Specific Beardsley estate marks. When in doubt, restrict to pre-1930 first-publication.

## 6. LoreVault Original — Lampblacker Spine Candidate

**Decision 11 mandate:** Each Universe needs an original LoreVault anchor character that is unambiguously LoreVault IP — a "Lampblacker Spine" that the Universe orbits without leaning on a public-domain figure as its primary brand carrier.

**Proposed Lampblacker Spine for Gothic Horror: "The Archivist of the Demeter."**

Concept: a survivor pulled from the wreck of the Demeter at Whitby — neither Dracula nor a Harker — who has spent the years since collecting and authenticating the *physical evidence* of the supernatural across the Stoker / Shelley / Stevenson / Leroux / Le Fanu canon. The Archivist is the Universe's narrator-at-the-edge, the figure into whose ledger the Moments are inscribed, and the only character who appears across every set without being from any of the source novels.

Three candidate expressions:

1. **Inkbound Archivist** — a near-silent figure in a long dark coat, leather satchel of evidence, ink-stained fingertips, a small mirror in a velvet pouch (used to test the un-dead). Always seen at the edge of a Moment, never the center.
2. **The Ledger-Keeper of Whitby** — gendered female; rescued from the *Demeter* wreck as a child; raised in the Whitby Abbey ruin; the consciousness behind the typography of the Universe.
3. **The Persian's Successor** — descendant or apprentice of Leroux's Daroga (the Persian); the Archivist crosses Paris-opera-cellar to Carpathian-castle to Soho-lab; the bridge figure.

**Recommended: variant #2, the Ledger-Keeper of Whitby**, gendered female. Reason:
- Fully original (no source-text equivalent).
- Whitby is iconic and PD.
- Pairs naturally with Mina (typewriter/ledger lineage) and gives the Universe a non-monster spine.
- Visually distinct from every Universal/Hammer/Coppola convention.

**Cross-tethers to other Universes:**
- **Enchanted Kingdom Universe** — the Ledger-Keeper trades evidence with a Brothers-Grimm archivist. Witch's-iron crossover: the wafer + the witch's mark.
- **Cosmic Horror (Lovecraft) Universe** — the Demeter shipwreck has a parallel: the *Pickman* / Innsmouth ledger; the Archivist refuses to open Lovecraftian volumes (genre boundary as character trait).
- **Mythic / Arthurian Universe** — Carfax Abbey is built on older ground; the Archivist's "Carfax dig" is a tether.
- **Sleuth / Detective Universe** — Utterson and Seward as forensic-adjacent figures; Archivist exchanges letters with them.

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

Gothic Horror's source texts encode 19th-century prejudices. LoreVault must navigate without sanitising the canon out of existence.

- **Stoker's Romanian / Eastern-European Orientalism.** Slovaks "more barbarian than the rest"; the "pollution of pure Anglo-Saxon blood" passages; the Balkans as superstition-ridden Other; the "draculism" critique (Todorova, Said). Romania has spent decades pushing back on the tourism-and-stereotype overhang. **LoreVault posture:** the Carpathian biome should be rendered with reverence — real Whitby, real Bistritz, real Romanian-folkloric specifics — not as a generic "scary Eastern Europe." Avoid any visual that codes Romani people as villainous.
- **Shelley's "monster" dehumanisation.** Modern readings restore the Creature's articulacy and dignity; LoreVault should follow that restoration. The Creature is not a brute. He is Milton's reader.
- **Le Fanu's Carmilla and the lesbian-vampire trope.** Carmilla pre-codes a century of "predatory queer woman = monster" cinema. **LoreVault posture:** treat Carmilla as a tragic-romantic figure with full interiority, not as Hammer-style erotic spectacle. The Karnstein lineage frames the curse, not her sexuality.
- **Phantom's deformity.** Erik's "death's-head" face is grounded in 19th-century beliefs that physical difference encodes moral horror. Modern collectibles audiences will notice. **LoreVault posture:** lean on the *mask* and the *music* as Erik's iconography; treat the face beneath as sorrow not spectacle.
- **Jekyll/Hyde and the "criminal physiognomy."** Stevenson is closer to a moral parable than a physiognomic claim, but the Hyde-as-deformed-troglodyte trope intersects with a long history of class-and-disability stereotype. **LoreVault posture:** Hyde's wrongness is *behavioural* — the trampled child, the cane — not facial.

## ODIN ESCALATION — Universal-Specific Boundary Flag

**Risk surface:** Universal Studios has been the most aggressive enforcer of derivative-monster designs in the entertainment industry for 70+ years. Their playbook (per documented Hotel Transylvania production notes and the Plagiarism Today / Foley Hoag legal write-ups):

1. They send pre-production cease-and-desist letters with an explicit list of forbidden design elements — neck-bolts, green skin, flat-top, scar pattern, cape silhouette, widow's-peak, wrapped-mummy bandages, wolf-man yak-hair pattern. They do this BEFORE infringement, as a chilling notice.
2. They maintain trademark registrations on "FRANKENSTEIN" and "DRACULA" as entertainment brand marks (USPTO 73271294, 74505230 are confirmed; full portfolio is **UNCLEAR** without a paid search).
3. They have litigated and won settlements at the design level — not just at the brand level.

**The bright line LoreVault must hold:**
- ✅ Use Stoker / Shelley / Stevenson / Leroux text, names, plot, places, props.
- ✅ Use pre-1930 PD illustrators (Holst, Friston, Beardsley, Clarke, Doré, Rackham, Sime).
- ✅ Use original LoreVault designs that are NOT visually fused with Universal-specific silhouettes.
- ❌ NEVER replicate the Karloff flat-head + neck-bolt + scarred-cheek silhouette before January 1, 2027.
- ❌ NEVER replicate the Lugosi widow's-peak + opera-cape + medallion silhouette before January 1, 2027.
- ❌ NEVER replicate the Lloyd-Webber/Björnson vertical-half-white-mask Phantom geometry — this is current copyright and trademark, no PD cliff in sight.
- ❌ NEVER replicate Coppola/Ishioka 1992 dragon-armor, top-hat-with-blue-spectacles, or red-cuff robe.
- ❌ NEVER use the brand string "Universal Monsters."
- ❌ NEVER use Penny Dreadful's Vanessa Ives or other Logan-original characters.

**Pre-launch action:** route the final visual deck through external IP counsel for a clearance memo specifically against the 14-item Section 3 list. This is one Universe where a $5K legal opinion saves a $5M lawsuit.

**Date-watch:** January 1, 2027 is on the calendar. Build the 2027 Liberation drop in Q3–Q4 2026.
