## 4. Pre-modern canonical PD register (the visual lattice)

This is American Gothic's strongest aesthetic-totalism asset — the illustrators predate cinema and are deeply PD.

- **Gustave Doré, *The Raven* (1884, posthumous)** — 26 steel-engraved plates, French edition. **Library of Congress hosts the complete edition; CONFIRMED PD US.** This is the canonical Raven imagery; the chiaroscuro-cathedral-of-grief register defines Poe visually for over 140 years. *(openculture.com / Library of Congress)*
- **Édouard Manet, *Le Corbeau* (1875)** — Mallarmé's French translation, lithographs by Manet. Deep PD.
- **F.O.C. Darley (1822–1888)** — Victorian America's most famous illustrator; **first fully illustrated *Rip Van Winkle* (1848)** + Sleepy Hollow lithographs through American Art Union; signed contract with Poe Jan 31, 1843 to illustrate *The Stylus*; illustrated "The Gold-Bug" first serial. **Darley is the canonical 19th-century Irving + Poe illustrator. Deep PD.** *(Wikipedia / eapoe.org / lookandlearn.com)*
- **Original 1839 *Tales of the Grotesque and Arabesque*** — first edition typography + ornaments PD.
- **Aubrey Beardsley** (1872–1898) — Poe-adjacent decadence; PD.
- **Howard Pyle** (1853–1911) — Sleepy-Hollow-adjacent New England illustration; PD.
- **Harry Clarke, *Tales of Mystery and Imagination* (1919, reissued 1923)** — the most famous 20th-century Poe illustrations; Clarke (1889–1931) died 1931. **US PD CONFIRMED** (1919 publication is pre-1925; works fully PD in US since at least 2015 under URAA / pre-1925 publication rule). *(publicdomainreview.org / publicdomainimagelibrary.com)* UK 70-years-pma rule = PD since Jan 1, 2002.

**Recommendation register:** **Doré (Raven primary) + Darley (Sleepy Hollow + Rip Van Winkle primary) + Clarke (full Poe-tale visual range) + Manet (decadent French-American hybrid)**. This stack gives LoreVault a pre-cinema, pre-Burton, pre-Corman visual identity that no derivative-rights holder can encroach on.

## 5. Top 5 figures with Spine + Lampblack

**1. The Unnamed Poe Narrator (Tell-Tale-Heart / Cask / Black Cat composite).**
- **Spine:** the obsessive-confessor walking us through their crime in first-person present-collapse. The genre's defining voice.
- **Lampblack:** the bricked-up wall (Cask), the heartbeat-under-floorboards (Tell-Tale), the beating eye, the trowel.

**2. Roderick Usher.**
- **Spine:** the cursed-house-master with hyperaesthesia — every sound, every light pains him; his sister is buried alive; his bloodline collapses with his architecture.
- **Lampblack:** the cracked façade of Usher; Madeline's bloodied shroud; the storm-lit tarn.

**3. C. Auguste Dupin — *the proto-Sherlock*.**
- **Spine:** Paris, 1841. The reclusive analytical genius who lives with an unnamed friend-narrator, smokes meerschaum, sees what the Préfecture cannot, decodes the impossible. **Predates Sherlock Holmes by 46 years (Holmes debuts *A Study in Scarlet* 1887). Conan Doyle: "Each [of Poe's detective stories] is a root from which a whole literature has developed."** Holmes himself dismisses Dupin in *Study in Scarlet* — Doyle's own anxiety-of-influence wink.
- **Lampblack:** the locked-room; the orangutan + razor (Rue Morgue); the purloined letter "hidden in plain sight"; the meerschaum pipe.

**4. Ichabod Crane.**
- **Spine:** the lanky New-England schoolmaster, scripture-reader, superstitious, courting the Dutch heiress, pursued through autumn-night woods by a horseman whose head is a blazing pumpkin. Ambiguity preserved: ghost or Brom Bones in costume?
- **Lampblack:** the pumpkin-head; the covered bridge; the abandoned hat-and-pumpkin in morning-light.

**5. Hester Prynne.**
- **Spine:** Boston, 1640s, the scaffold, the embroidered scarlet A she refuses to hide, the silent minister hiding his complicity, the elf-child Pearl, the cuckolded physician renamed Chillingworth. The first great American novel of moral hypocrisy.
- **Lampblack:** the scarlet A (gold-threaded, ostentatious, defiant); the scaffold at noon; Pearl's wildflower-crown.

**Lampblack candidates (Universe-set glyphs):** the Raven on the Pallas-bust, the scarlet A, the pumpkin-head, the bricked wall, the swinging pendulum, the seven-colored masque-rooms.

## 6. LoreVault original — unique expressions + cross-tethers

**Unique expressions (LoreVault-original, fully ours):**
1. **"The Tell-Tale Hour"** — opening Set name; the hour when every American Gothic confessor speaks at once, heartbeats bleed through walls, and the Lampblack pools.
2. **The Pallas-Bust Glyph** — the raven on the marble bust of Pallas Athena = the Universe set-glyph. (Owl-of-Minerva inverted into corvid-of-grief.)
3. **The Bricked-Wall Mechanic** — a card/Moment mechanic where a played card is "walled" face-down; revealed only when an opponent triggers Montresor-vengeance.
4. **The Seven-Rooms Sequence** — Masque-of-Red-Death color-progression as a play-area / rarity-staircase (blue → purple → green → orange → white → violet → black-and-blood-red).
5. **The Hudson Lattice** — Sleepy Hollow + Rip Van Winkle + the Catskill spirits as a single Hudson Valley sub-pane, with the bridge-crossing as a transit/portal mechanic.

**Cross-tethers (Lampblack-explicit, cross-Universe storyline-grade):**

- **Auguste Dupin ↔ Sherlock Holmes (Baker Street).** This is the single most important cross-Universe spine in the entire LoreVault corpus. Dupin (1841) is the literal genetic ancestor of Holmes (1887); Conan Doyle named the influence on record. **Make this Lampblack-explicit.** A "Method-Lineage" arc in which Holmes traces his methodology backward through ratiocination to Dupin = a marquee cross-Universal storyline. Dupin's meerschaum + Holmes's calabash = paired Lampblack props. **ODIN-ESCALATION FLAGGED — see end.**
- **Roderick Usher ↔ Frankenstein's Creature (Gothic Horror).** Both are "cursed-bloodline-as-architecture" figures: Usher's house IS his body; Frankenstein's body IS the Creature's house. Cross-tether: a "cursed-vessel" duology.
- **Ichabod Crane ↔ Cheshire Cat (Wonderland).** Both are portal-figures: Ichabod is *pursued through* a portal-bridge by an impossible horse; the Cheshire Cat *is* the portal itself. Both vanish at dawn leaving only an artifact (pumpkin / grin).
- **The Raven ↔ Odin's Ravens Huginn + Muninn (Norse Myth).** Universe-cross-bird Lampblack. Poe's Raven is a single grief-messenger; Odin's are a paired thought-and-memory courier. A "Three Ravens" sub-story — Nevermore-meets-Huginn-meets-Muninn — is a pre-baked cross-Universe Set.
- **The Tell-Tale Heart narrator ↔ Dr. Jekyll (Gothic Horror).** Both are first-person obsessive confessors of self-divided crime. Paired-narration storyline.

**Cross-Universe storyline-pattern recommendation:** "**The Method and the Madness**" — Dupin × Holmes × Tell-Tale-narrator × Jekyll. Four obsessive minds, two reasoning-toward-truth (Dupin/Holmes), two confessing-toward-doom (Tell-Tale/Jekyll). A canonical cross-pane Set.

## 9. Recommendation

**STRONG OWN-UNIVERSE candidate. American Gothic should be its own Universe (likely Series 2 or 3), NOT folded into Gothic Horror.**

**Rationale:**
- **Distinct lineage.** European Gothic (Walpole / Radcliffe / Lewis / Shelley / Stoker / Polidori / Stevenson / Le Fanu) and American Gothic share register but are separate genealogies. American Gothic is the *response* to European Gothic — Hawthorne, Poe, and Irving consciously americanized it (Puritan-conscience instead of aristocratic-decay; New World wilderness instead of crumbling abbeys; democratic-confessor instead of cursed-noble). Folding them collapses a real literary distinction.
- **Anchor-strength.** The Raven, Sleepy Hollow, and Scarlet Letter are mass-recognized in the US as American — same way Holmes is recognized as British. Putting Poe in "Gothic Horror" alongside Dracula reads as a category error to the American mass audience.
- **Volume.** Top 20 figures + 10–15 Moments is enough for a full Universe Series, not a sub-pane. Three full Sets fit comfortably (Poe / Irving-Hawthorne / Bierce-Gilman-Freeman).
- **Cross-Universe spine.** Dupin → Holmes is a marquee cross-Pane storyline that ONLY works if American Gothic stands as a peer Universe to Baker Street.

**Opening Set: "The Tell-Tale Hour."**
- Centerpiece Moments: Raven-on-Pallas (Ultra), Usher-house-collapse (Legendary), Madeline's return (Legendary), Ichabod-bridge (Rare), Hester's scarlet A (Rare), Tell-Tale-heartbeat (confessor-mechanic intro).
- Centerpiece figures: Roderick Usher, Auguste Dupin, Ichabod Crane, Hester Prynne, the unnamed Tell-Tale narrator.
- Aesthetic register: Doré + Darley primary; Clarke for Poe-tale Moments; Manet for one-off Raven legendary card.

**Series-2 ordering recommendation:** ship American Gothic *concurrently with* a Baker Street second-wave so the **Dupin × Holmes "Method-Lineage" storyline** can land as a paired-Universe marketing beat. Largest cross-Pane storyline in the entire LoreVault catalog.

## 10. Cultural sensitivity

- **Poe's race politics — complex.** Poe wrote in antebellum slaveholding Richmond/Baltimore; some scholarship reads "The Black Cat," "Hop-Frog," and "Murders in the Rue Morgue" (the orangutan-as-racialized-other) as containing racial subtext or active racism. Approach: emphasize the analytical-Dupin and the obsessive-confessor lineages; avoid replicating the orangutan-as-racial-cipher reading; if Hop-Frog is included, frame it as the *jester-vengeance* arc, not the racial-cartoon some 19th-century illustrators made it.
- **Hawthorne's Puritan critique.** Hawthorne descended from Salem-witch-trial judge John Hathorne (added the "w" to dissociate). *Scarlet Letter* + "Young Goodman Brown" are *critiques* of Puritan hypocrisy, not endorsements — frame accordingly. Hester is a feminist proto-icon, not a victim of God; play this honestly.
- **Bierce's complicated colonial legacy.** Bierce was a Union veteran of the Civil War (Owl Creek Bridge depicts a Confederate sympathizer). Frame Owl Creek as anti-war psychological-horror, which is the modern critical consensus — not as Confederate-nostalgia.
- **Indigenous Hudson Valley peoples.** The historical Sleepy Hollow / Tarry Town / Tappan Zee region is **Lenape land** (specifically the **Weckquaesgeek**, an Algonquian-speaking subgroup). Frederick Philipse's manor was built on dispossessed Lenape territory using transatlantic-trafficked African labor. Irving's tale focuses on Dutch-colonial folklore and *erases* both. **LoreVault recommendation: include a Lenape-acknowledgment in any Sleepy Hollow set's lore-text + consider commissioning at least one Moment that surfaces Lenape pre-colonial Hudson Valley as part of the Universe substrate, not its absence.** *(scenichudson.org / westchestermagazine.com)*
- **Gilman's racism.** Gilman herself held documented racist + nativist views (white-supremacist eugenicist writings). "The Yellow Wallpaper" stands as a feminist landmark, but treat the *story* as the canonical asset, not the *author* as a hero-figure — no biographical hagiography of Gilman.
- **Gendered confinement.** "The Yellow Wallpaper" + "Berenice" + "Ligeia" + Madeline-Usher all involve confined / pathologized / buried women. Frame the women as protagonists with agency in their own arcs, not as ornaments to male narrators' madness.

## ODIN-ESCALATION

**Auguste Dupin ↔ Sherlock Holmes is a Tier-1 cross-Universal storyline.**

- Direct genealogy: Dupin (Poe, 1841) → Holmes (Doyle, 1887). 46-year gap. Doyle on record acknowledging influence ("Each of Poe's detective stories is a root from which a whole literature has developed"). Holmes himself dismisses Dupin in *A Study in Scarlet* (1887) — Doyle's anxiety-of-influence wink.
- Shared structural DNA: reclusive analytical genius + first-person friend-narrator + Paris/London locked-room + meerschaum/calabash pipe + Préfet/Lestrade authority-figure-foil + ratiocination/deduction.
- Cross-Universe storyline pitch: **"The Method-Lineage."** Holmes encounters a contemporary case that mirrors Rue Morgue; the trail leads him backward through correspondence and library-archive to a Paris of forty years prior; the Universes briefly bleed; Dupin's meerschaum and Holmes's calabash sit on the same desk for one Moment.
- **Action: flag for inclusion in cross-storyline-patterns dossier.** This is the single richest cross-Pane spine identified in the dossier corpus to date.
