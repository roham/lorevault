# LoreVault Universe Research — Japanese Yokai / Shinto (Series 2+ Candidate)

**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Status:** PD-deepest source layer in the candidate set, but the **single largest derivative-IP minefield in the entire universe-roster** — Ghibli + Pokemon + Yo-kai Watch + Demon Slayer + Inuyasha + Mushishi + Natsume's Book of Friends collectively own most of the modern visual register. The literary source predates them by 200–1,300 years; the woodblock canon predates them by 100–250 years. Walkable on a tightrope — but only with a ruthlessly-policed Sekien/Yoshitoshi/Hokusai woodblock register and a live cultural-advisor engagement. **Plus a Shinto-living-religion sensitivity layer that no other candidate carries.**
**Headline:** Yokai/Shinto is the highest-risk highest-reward Series 2+ candidate. Everyone has seen Totoro and Pikachu. Almost nobody has seen the 1781 Sekien woodcut of Nuppeppō or the 1889 Yoshitoshi print of Shuten-dōji's severed head. The win is *aggressively pre-modern.*

## 1. Canon Enumeration

**Top Yokai (Sekien + Konjaku + Hearn-attested, deep PD):**

- **Predator-yokai:** Oni (horned ogres), Tengu (mountain crow-or-long-nosed goblins), Kappa (river-imp turtle-children), Yamauba (mountain-witch crone), Onibaba (demon-hag), Jorōgumo (whore-spider), Nure-onna (snake-woman), Rokurokubi (long-neck stretcher), Nukekubi (detached-head)
- **Cold/snow:** Yuki-onna (snow-woman; Hearn-canonical 1904)
- **Animal-shapeshifters:** Kitsune (fox, with kyūbi nine-tailed apex), Tanuki (raccoon-dog trickster), Bakeneko / Nekomata (cat-yokai with one or two tails), Inugami (dog-spirit), Mujina (badger)
- **Tsukumogami (object-spirits, 100-year-old artifacts becoming alive — uniquely Shinto-animist):** Karakasa-obake (one-eyed parasol), Chōchin-obake (paper-lantern yokai), Bakezōri (sandal-yokai), Boroboroton (ragged futon), Kasa-obake — the entire Tsukumogami emaki tradition is the heart of the Hyakki Yagyō register
- **Architectural-environmental:** Nurikabe (invisible-wall blocker), Daidarabotchi (mountain-sized giant), Hyakume (hundred-eyes flesh-yokai), Nuppeppō (lump-of-flesh wandering yokai), Konaki-jiji (crying-old-man), Nopperabō (faceless ghost)
- **Ghosts (yūrei):** Onryō (vengeful spirit, Oiwa is the canonical), Funayūrei (drowned-sailor), Goryō (high-rank vengeful), Ubume (mother-ghost with infant)
- **Dragons / serpents:** Yamata-no-Orochi (8-headed serpent from Kojiki), Ryū (dragons broadly), Mizuchi (water-serpent)
- **Apex / "great three":** Ghost of Emperor Sutoku, Tamamo-no-Mae (nine-tailed kitsune in court-woman form), Shuten-dōji (king of oni at Ōeyama)

**Top Shinto Kami (Kojiki/Nihon Shoki-attested, deep PD):**

- **Three Noble Children of Izanagi:** Amaterasu-Ōmikami (sun goddess), Tsukuyomi-no-Mikoto (moon kami), Susanoo-no-Mikoto (storm/sea kami)
- **Primordials:** Izanagi & Izanami (creation pair), Ame-no-Minakanushi
- **Major:** Inari-Ōkami (rice/foxes/business — the most-shrined kami in Japan), Hachiman (war/archery, originally Emperor Ōjin deified), Ryūjin (dragon-king of the sea), Raijin (thunder god, drum-circle iconography), Fūjin (wind god, sack-of-winds iconography), Konohanasakuya-hime (cherry-blossom / Mt. Fuji), Iwanaga-hime (her stone-eternal sister), Ōkuninushi (lord of Izumo)
- **Mythic heroes:** Yamato Takeru, Jimmu (first emperor), Ame-no-Uzume (dance-goddess of the cave-myth)

**Historical / semi-legendary figures (PD):**

- **Abe no Seimei** (921–1005), onmyōji to the Heian court, mastered 12 shikigami, founded the Tsuchimikado lineage. Shrine at Kyoto still active. ([Wikipedia — Abe no Seimei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abe_no_Seimei))
- **Minamoto no Yorimitsu / Raikō** (948–1021), Heian samurai, slayer of Shuten-dōji at Mt. Ōe with the Four Heavenly Kings (Watanabe no Tsuna, Sakata Kintoki / Kintarō, Urabe no Suetake, Usui Sadamitsu). ([Wikipedia — Minamoto no Yorimitsu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minamoto_no_Yorimitsu))
- **Sakata Kintoki / Kintarō** — child-of-the-mountain folk hero, axe-bearer, raised by Yamauba per legend.
- **Watanabe no Tsuna** — slayer of the Ibaraki-dōji oni at Rashōmon gate.

**Top 8–10 Moments (deep-PD, source-attested):**

- **Susanoo slays Yamata-no-Orochi** at the Hi River — sake-drugs the eight heads, beheads serially, finds Kusanagi-no-Tsurugi (the imperial regalia sword) in the tail. *Kojiki* 712 + *Nihon Shoki* 720. ([Yamata no Orochi — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yamata_no_Orochi))
- **Amaterasu hides in the Heavenly Rock-Cave** (Ame-no-Iwato) after Susanoo's outrage; world plunges into darkness; Ame-no-Uzume's lewd dance + the bronze-mirror trick draws her out — origin of the imperial-regalia mirror.
- **Yorimitsu and the Four Heavenly Kings vs Shuten-dōji** at Mt. Ōe — Abe no Seimei's divination locates the lair, three old men (Hachiman/Sumiyoshi/Kumano in disguise) provide the magical sake, Yorimitsu beheads the demon-king mid-stupor.
- **Abe no Seimei's twelve shikigami** — court onmyōji binding paper-fold familiars to perform divination, exorcism, household tasks.
- **The Hyakki Yagyō (Night Parade of One Hundred Demons)** — Muromachi-period scroll tradition (Tosa Mitsunobu attribution at Daitoku-ji Shinju-an), Sekien-codified — the procession of yokai through the streets at night, mortals who witness it die unless protected by sutra. ([Wikipedia — Hyakki Yagyō](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyakki_Yagy%C5%8D))
- **Yuki-onna** — Hearn 1904 *Kwaidan* Mosaku-and-Minokichi version: snow-woman spares the young man, returns years later as his wife O-Yuki, vanishes when he tells. ([Project Gutenberg — Kwaidan](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/1210))
- **The Crane Wife (Tsuru no Ongaeshi)** — folktale of crane in human form weaving feather-cloth, vanishes when seen.
- **Urashima Tarō at Ryūgū-jō** — fisherman to the dragon-palace; the box of years.
- **Momotarō / Peach Boy** to Onigashima — folk-hero subdues oni with monkey/dog/pheasant retinue.
- **Princess Kaguya / Tale of the Bamboo Cutter** (*Taketori Monogatari*, ~10th c.) — moon-princess Earth-cycle.

## 2. PD Status

**Bedrock textual layer — deepest PD in any candidate:**

- **Kojiki** (712 CE) — earliest extant Japanese mythography. Public domain globally.
- **Nihon Shoki** (720 CE) — second imperial chronicle. PD.
- **Konjaku Monogatarishū** (~1120) — Heian-era "Tales of Now-and-Long-Ago," 1,000+ stories including yokai/oni accounts. PD.
- **Uji Shūi Monogatari** (~1212) — Kamakura-era tale collection. PD.
- **Otogizōshi** (Muromachi) — Shuten-dōji + Tamamo-no-Mae attested. PD.
- **Tale of the Bamboo Cutter / Taketori Monogatari** (~10th c.) — PD.

**Visual woodblock canon — deep PD, the safest possible aesthetic:**

- **Toriyama Sekien's four bestiaries (1776–1784)** — *Gazu Hyakki Yagyō* (1776), *Konjaku Gazu Zoku Hyakki* (1779), *Konjaku Hyakki Shūi* (1781), *Hyakki Tsurezure Bukuro* (1784). The codifying canonical-PD encyclopedia of yokai, predating every modern adaptation by 200+ years. **THE differentiation register.** ([Wikipedia — Toriyama Sekien](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toriyama_Sekien)) ([Nippon.com — Sekien](https://www.nippon.com/en/people/e00124/))
- **Hokusai's *Hyaku Monogatari* (One Hundred Ghost Stories, ca. 1830)** — only five completed (Sarayashiki, Laughing Hannya, Oiwa-san, Kohada Koheiji, Obsession), but each iconic; Berlin-blue palette. PD. ([Public Domain Review — Hokusai's Ghost Stories](https://publicdomainreview.org/collection/hokusai-s-ghost-stories-ca-1830/)) ([Art Institute of Chicago — Oiwa-san](https://www.artic.edu/artworks/47403/oiwa-oiwa-san-from-the-series-one-hundred-ghost-tales-hyaku-monogatari))
- **Tsukioka Yoshitoshi** (1839–1892) — *One Hundred Aspects of the Moon* (1885–92), *New Forms of Thirty-Six Ghosts* (1889–92), *Twenty-Eight Famous Murders with Verse* (1866–67). PD. The most cinematic of the woodblock register; LoC and Internet Archive confirm no copyright restrictions. ([LoC — One Hundred Aspects](https://www.loc.gov/item/2021668853/)) ([Wikipedia — One Hundred Aspects of the Moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_Hundred_Aspects_of_the_Moon))
- **Utagawa Kuniyoshi** (1798–1861) — *Minamoto no Yorimitsu Haunted by the Earth Spider* (1843), *Sōma Old Palace*, Suikoden warrior-prints with yokai. PD. ([Wikipedia — Kuniyoshi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utagawa_Kuniyoshi)) ([Fuji Arts — Kuniyoshi Yorimitsu Shuten-dōji 1851–52](https://www.fujiarts.com/19th-century-originals-japanese-prints/kuniyoshi/488621-minamoto-yorimitsu-and-his-retainers-attack-shuten-doji-1851-1852))
- **Kawanabe Kyōsai** (1831–1889) — Sekien-disciple (via Kuniyoshi); *Kyōsai Hyakuzu* (1863–66), *Kyōsai Gadan* (1887). PD. ([Collecting Japanese Prints — Kyōsai](https://www.collectingjapaneseprints.com/kawanabe-kyosai))
- **Sawaki Suushi** (1707–1772) *Hyakkai Zukan* (1737) — pre-Sekien yokai scroll. PD.
- **Hyakki Yagyō Emaki** (Tosa Mitsunobu attrib., Muromachi 1336–1573, Daitoku-ji Shinju-an) — original night-parade scroll. PD.

**Lafcadio Hearn (1850–1904)** — *Kwaidan: Stories and Studies of Strange Things* (1904), *In Ghostly Japan* (1899), *Kotto* (1902), *Glimpses of Unfamiliar Japan* (1894). Hearn died in 1904; works are PD globally and on Project Gutenberg. ([Wikipedia — Kwaidan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kwaidan:_Stories_and_Studies_of_Strange_Things)) ([Project Gutenberg — Kwaidan](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/1210))

The literary and visual substrate is the deepest, oldest, and most pictorially-rich PD set in the LoreVault candidate roster. The risk is entirely downstream.

## 3. Derivative-Rights Minefield — 15 Specific Locks To Avoid

Critical principle: **No one owns yokai. Everyone owns *their specific designs.*** Names, myths, story-shapes are PD. Every visual signature below is owned and must NOT be replicated. ([SCBC Law — Re-Spirited Away on Ghibli IP](https://www.scbc-law.org/post/re-spirited-away-can-intellectual-property-law-protect-against-openai-s-ghibli-fication-trend))

1. **Studio Ghibli — Totoro (1988)** — the round grey forest-spirit with circular belly-pattern, leaf-umbrella, two ear-tufts, ovate-eyed Catbus, owl-cry. Hard avoid the entire silhouette. Forest-kami exist in source as *kodama* (tree-spirits) — render as Sekien-bestiary woodblock-cut tree-figures, not Miyazaki's plush-toy shapes. ([Ghibli Wiki — Susuwatari](https://ghibli.fandom.com/wiki/Susuwatari))
2. **Studio Ghibli — Spirited Away (2001) No-Face / Kaonashi** — black robed silhouette + white nō-mask. Kaonashi is original Miyazaki, not folkloric. Avoid the silhouette completely; if depicting a faceless spirit, use the source-canon *Nopperabō* (Hearn 1904) which has flat blank skin, not the Kaonashi-mask register.
3. **Studio Ghibli — Susuwatari / soot sprites** — small black ball-bodies + two big white eyes + spider-leg wisps. Wikipedia and Ghibli's own materials confirm these are **Miyazaki-original**, not folkloric. Do not render any small black sprites in this design. ([Wikipedia — Susuwatari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susuwatari))
4. **Studio Ghibli — Princess Mononoke (1997)** — the kodama (specific clicking-head Mononoke design, white with rotating clack-head), Shishigami (deer-faced forest-spirit / Night-Walker), San's red-painted face + wolf-ears + dagger, Moro the white wolf-mother, Lady Eboshi silhouette. Avoid all. Source-canon kodama in Sekien is a wholly different visual.
5. **Studio Ghibli — Howl's Moving Castle / Calcifer** — flame-with-eyes fire demon. Avoid this fire-spirit silhouette specifically; if rendering a fire-yokai, use Hokusai's *Laughing Hannya* or Yoshitoshi's onibi (will-o'-wisp) prints instead.
6. **Studio Ghibli — Ponyo / Ponyo's father Fujimoto** — fish-girl + green-coated wizard. Avoid; use Ryūjin sea-dragon woodblock register instead.
7. **Pokémon — Vulpix / Ninetales** (Game Freak/Nintendo, gen 1, 1996) — the kitsune-derivative red-orange six-tailed → nine-tailed fox with curl-tipped tails and gem-on-forehead. Hard avoid that exact design family. Use Yoshitoshi's *Tamamo-no-Mae* or Hokusai's kitsune prints — court-woman/fox-shadow register, not chibi-mascot. ([Sakuraco — Pokémon Inspired by Yokai](https://sakura.co/blog/pokemon-inspired-by-classic-japanese-myths))
8. **Pokémon — Ludicolo, Lombre, Lotad** — kappa-derivative pineapple-sombrero green creatures. Avoid mascot-kappa register entirely.
9. **Pokémon — Froslass, Jynx, Articuno** — yuki-onna / cold-spirit derivatives with specific anime-feminine silhouettes. Avoid; use Hearn's Yuki-onna-as-described (white kimono, long black hair, no specific anime shape) plus Yoshitoshi snow-woman prints.
10. **Pokémon — Gengar, Haunter, Gastly** — yūrei/onryō derivatives in chibi-purple-blob register. Hard avoid. Render ghosts in Hokusai's *Oiwa-san* or Yoshitoshi onryō register — pale, hair-trailing, lantern-emergent.
11. **Pokémon — Mimikyu** — tsukumogami-derivative cloth-disguise creature. Avoid the silhouette — but tsukumogami broadly is PD-Sekien-canonical.
12. **Yo-kai Watch — Jibanyan and the entire roster** (Level-5, 2013) — chibi-mascot two-tailed cat with red-flame-cheeks, plus 350+ specific designs. Level-5 trademarked the entire Yo-kai Watch IP in the US Jan 2014. Hard avoid every Level-5 design; the franchise's *premise* is yokai-as-collectible-monster, exactly LoreVault's mechanic — extreme parallel. **Lean hard on woodblock-print register to make the visual gulf undeniable.** ([Anime News Network — Level-5 trademark filing](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2014-01-23/level-5-files-yo-kai-watch-trademark-in-the-u.s)) ([Wikipedia — Yo-kai Watch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yo-kai_Watch))
13. **Demon Slayer / Kimetsu no Yaiba** (Shueisha 2016+) — Tanjiro's green-and-black checkered haori, Nezuko's pink hemp-leaf pattern, Zenitsu's yellow scale-pattern, all Hashira character designs (Giyū's half-and-half haori, Shinobu's purple butterfly-print, Rengoku's flame-pattern). Shueisha has ACTIVE trademark registrations on the Hashira clothing patterns at USPTO + EUIPO, defended in 2021. **Notable**: the 18th-century *original* checkered/hemp-leaf patterns Nezuko's costume was drawn from are deep-PD, but Shueisha's specific application-as-character-mark IS protected. Use the source patterns (asanoha, ichimatsu) freely, never on a character-shaped silhouette in Demon Slayer's specific character configurations. ([Anime News Network — Shueisha trademarks](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/interest/2021-06-11/shueisha-trademarks-clothing-patterns-of-3-demon-slayer-kimetsu-no-yaiba-characters/.173823)) ([Justia — Demon Slayer trademark](https://trademarks.justia.com/903/99/demon-slayer-kimetsu-no-90399372.html))
14. **Inuyasha** (Rumiko Takahashi 1996–2008, Shogakukan/Sunrise) — Inuyasha (white-haired half-dog-yokai with red kimono + sword), Sesshōmaru (stoic full-yokai brother), Kagome's school-uniform + sacred-arrow, Shippō (chibi kitsune), Naraku, Kikyō, Sango-and-Kirara. All Takahashi-copyright-protected. ([Wikipedia — Inuyasha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inuyasha))
15. **Mushishi (Yuki Urushibara, 2000–2008)** — Ginko (one-eyed white-haired mushi-master with cigarette + traveler's pack), specific *mushi* spirit designs (river-of-light, kokemushi). + **Natsume's Book of Friends (Yuki Midorikawa, 2003+)** — Madara/Nyanko-sensei (squat lucky-cat with distinctive markings), specific yokai-of-the-week designs. Protected. ([Wikipedia — Natsume's Book of Friends](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natsume's_Book_of_Friends))

**ODIN ESCALATION — three highest-risk overlap zones, in priority order:**
- **(A) Ghibli kodama / forest-spirits / soot-sprites** — Mononoke and Totoro have *culturally-dominant* visual ownership of "small Japanese forest spirit." Any LoreVault forest-yokai card that even adjacent to those silhouettes triggers instant lawyer-and-internet-mob risk. Lock to Sekien woodcut register.
- **(B) Pokémon and Yo-kai Watch yokai-as-collectible-monster premise** — LoreVault's *mechanic* is collectible-creature-cards. Yo-kai Watch IS that, with yokai. The aesthetic gulf must be undeniable. Mascot-chibi register is a complete no-go zone; we are explicitly *not* in cute-monster cartoon space.
- **(C) Demon Slayer trademarked patterns** — asanoha, ichimatsu, seigaiha, etc. are PD textile patterns, but Shueisha has weaponized them when used in character-mark configurations. Never combine these patterns on a haori-clad young-man silhouette.

## 4. Pre-Modern PD Register — The Sekien-and-Lampblack Vocabulary

Recommended visual stack — every layer pre-1925 and PD-clean:

- **Toriyama Sekien (1712–1788) four bestiaries (1776–1784)** — the foundational PD canon. Black-line woodblock with limited color washes; figure-with-name-cartouche bestiary layout. Sekien named and codified more than half of all yokai we know today (including inventing Nuppeppō and the modern Tsukumogami-set). LoreVault's primary register. ([Nippon.com — Sekien](https://www.nippon.com/en/people/e00124/)) ([Japan Society Boston — Sekien](https://www.japansocietyboston.org/post/toriyama-sekien))
- **Tsukioka Yoshitoshi (1839–1892)** — the cinematic apex. *One Hundred Aspects of the Moon* (1885–92, 100 prints), *New Forms of Thirty-Six Ghosts* (1889–92), *Twenty-Eight Famous Murders* (1866–67 with Yoshiiku). Bold compositional drama, finely-drawn ghost figures, Meiji-era refined block-cutting. PD. ([Wikipedia — Yoshitoshi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsukioka_Yoshitoshi))
- **Hokusai's *Hyaku Monogatari* (~1830)** — only 5 prints exist but each iconic, Berlin-blue. PD. ([PD Review — Hokusai's Ghost Stories](https://publicdomainreview.org/collection/hokusai-s-ghost-stories-ca-1830/))
- **Utagawa Kuniyoshi (1798–1861)** — Yorimitsu/Earth-Spider triptych (1843), Takiyasha-the-Witch-and-the-Skeleton-Spectre (1844, with the giant articulated skeleton), warrior-vs-yokai battle prints. PD.
- **Kawanabe Kyōsai (1831–1889)** — Sekien-Kuniyoshi disciple, supernatural-comic register, dynamic ink-painting tradition.
- **Sawaki Suushi *Hyakkai Zukan* (1737)** — pre-Sekien scroll, also PD.
- **Muromachi Hyakki Yagyō Emaki (Tosa Mitsunobu attrib.)** — the original Daitoku-ji night-parade scroll — for cross-Pane Lampblack-event visualization.
- **Hylestad-equivalent for Japan: Heian-period emaki** (*Genji Monogatari Emaki* 12th c., *Shigisan Engi Emaki* late-Heian, *Bandainagon Ekotoba* 12th c.) — narrative scroll-painting register.
- **Edo-period byōbu screen paintings** — the gold-leaf six-fold screen of Hyakki Yagyō, Tawaraya Sōtatsu / Ogata Kōrin Rinpa-school for higher-register kami imagery.
- **Lafcadio Hearn 1904 first-edition typographic + decorative ornament** — for typographic register on collector-card reverse layout.

**Recommendation**: Sekien-bestiary as primary creature-card register + Yoshitoshi cinematic dramatic-print as Moment/Story register + Hokusai Berlin-blue for ghost/onryō register + Edo gold-leaf byōbu for Hyakki Yagyō night-parade Pane events. The result is unmistakably **not** Ghibli, **not** Pokémon, **not** Demon Slayer — it is Edo woodblock and Meiji ink, ink-line and washi-paper, brush-stroke and cartouche.

## 5. Tone, Themes, and Affordance

**Tonal vocabulary (woodblock-canon-grounded):**

- **Animism** — every object, mountain, river, tool, and even old slipper carries spirit; the world is densely-populated by the unseen.
- **Liminality** — yokai live at edges (twilight, river-banks, mountain-passes, doorways, abandoned houses); the *border* is the canonical setting.
- **Comic-grotesque dignity** — Sekien's yokai are grotesque AND humorous AND named with cartouche-formality; not horror-genre, not cute-genre, a third register that's unique to this canon.
- **Mono no aware** — the pathos of impermanence; even the Crane Wife and Yuki-onna stories are sad-wonder, not action.
- **Tsukumogami ethics** — household objects mistreated come back as yokai; care-for-things has cosmic consequence.
- **Onmyōdō / divination** — Heian court mysticism, paper-fold shikigami, geomantic bearings, almanac taboos — the fantasy-procedural register.

**LoreVault-specific affordances:**

- Sekien's bestiary cartouche is **literally a collectible-card layout from 1776**. The format is native.
- The Hyakki Yagyō night-parade is a **natural cross-Pane Lampblack mass-event**: multiple yokai appear simultaneously in moving procession.
- Tsukumogami are a **uniquely-Japanese mechanic hook**: artifacts that age into agents. Cards with literal aging-mechanics that turn them into yokai.
- Onmyōdō shikigami are **paper-card-as-summon**: Abe no Seimei literally folds shikigami from paper to summon. Card-game native.
- Kitsune nine-tail progression is **a literal evolution-rank ladder**, PD-canonical.

- **Oni ↔ Norse Jotunn / Greek Titan / Arthurian giants** — the horned-club-bearing colossal-other. Shuten-dōji as oni-king parallels the giant-king motif universally.
- **Yamata-no-Orochi ↔ Greek Hydra / Norse Jörmungandr / Arthurian Pendragon-dragon / Egyptian Apophis** — the hero-vs-many-headed-serpent moment is cross-pantheon canonical; LoreVault can run the Pane-comparison directly.

- **Hyakki Yagyō night-parade ↔ Wild Hunt (Norse Odin's host) ↔ Arthurian Faerie Rade** — the supernatural-procession-at-night is a deep-comparative-myth structure. **Designate Hyakki Yagyō as a primary cross-Pane Lampblack mass-event** that surfaces in multi-Universe play.
- **Tsukumogami artifact-spirits ↔ Arthurian sentient swords / Norse Mjolnir-as-character** — animism of objects.
- **Inari foxes ↔ Wonderland White Rabbit / EK familiar-companion** — the bridging-animal-spirit at threshold.
- **Abe no Seimei ↔ Solomon (Arabian) / Faust (Gothic) / Merlin (Arthurian)** — the binding-of-spirits magus. Shikigami-as-summon is the Japanese variant of the goetic summon-tradition.

## 7. Series-Slot Recommendation

**Series 2 candidate, contingent on cultural-advisor engagement.** The yokai/Shinto canon is the richest mechanic-fit in the entire candidate roster (cards-as-yokai is the native-format), but it carries the heaviest derivative-design risk and the only living-religion sensitivity in the set. **Do not greenlight without (1) Sekien-Yoshitoshi register lock, (2) explicit Shinto-cultural-advisor sign-off, (3) Ghibli/Pokémon/Yo-kai Watch IP-clearance pass by Japan-qualified counsel.**

If Series 1 closes successfully and Norse + Greek serve as the proven mythology-set, Yokai-Shinto is the natural Series 2 expansion — same myth-genre, complementary aesthetic register (woodblock vs. medieval-Romantic), strongly differentiated palette.

**Bench-rank rationale:**
- **Mechanic fit**: A+. The Sekien bestiary is *literally* a collectible-card book from 1776. Tsukumogami + Hyakki Yagyō + onmyōdō shikigami map directly onto card-game mechanics.
- **PD depth**: A+. Source layer is 712–1904, all uncontested PD.
- **Derivative IP risk**: F (uncategorically the worst in the candidate set). Ghibli + Pokémon + Yo-kai Watch + Demon Slayer + Inuyasha + Natsume + Mushishi together own most of the modern visual register.
- **Cultural sensitivity risk**: Highest in candidate set. Shinto is a living indigenous religion + onmyōdō practitioners still active.
- **Visual differentiation potential**: A+ if locked to woodblock. Sekien register makes the gulf to Ghibli/Pokémon undeniable.
- **Aesthetic ceiling**: A+. Sekien + Yoshitoshi + Hokusai is a gallery-grade pictorial register no other modern collectible-game owns.

## 9. Risk Register

- **Ghibli cultural-dominance** (HIGHEST). Totoro and Spirited Away own the global-public mental image of "Japanese spirit." Mitigation: Sekien woodblock register, explicit no-soot-sprite no-Catbus-no-Kaonashi rule, lawyer pre-clearance every concept card.
- **Yo-kai Watch premise-collision** (HIGH). They are literally yokai-as-collectible-monster. Mitigation: aggressive aesthetic differentiation (woodblock vs chibi-mascot is the only safe gulf); explicit positioning as "the woodblock canon, before everything cute."
- **Pokémon yokai-derivative creatures** (HIGH). Many gen-1+ creatures are yokai-derived; Game Freak has aggressively-defended IP (Palworld lawsuit ongoing 2024–2025 over patents). Mitigation: stay in the *original* yokai naming + woodblock register, never echo Pokemon-typology silhouettes. ([Lexology — Nintendo v Palworld](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=80348dc0-007e-4db0-9bc8-29f14b205a1a))
- **Demon Slayer trademark-pattern collision** (MED). Shueisha is enforcing. Mitigation: never put their pattern-character configurations on card silhouettes; use the underlying PD textile patterns freely on backgrounds/borders.
- **Cultural-appropriation risk** (HIGH). Yokai/Shinto is *not* dead-pantheon material. Mitigation: cultural-advisor on permanent retainer (Japanese folklorist + a Shinto-priest consultant); sensitivity-read every set; budget for Japan-counsel pre-clearance.
- **Translator-rights gotchas** (MED). Donald Keene / Helen McCullough / Royall Tyler translations of Konjaku, Heike, Genji are in copyright. Use Hearn 1904 (PD) or commission new translations from-source.
- **Hearn-as-orientalist critique** (LOW–MED). Hearn is loved in Japan but contemporary academic critique flags Western romanticization. Use Hearn as a source not a frame; cite Japanese folklorists (Kunio Yanagita 1875–1962, Komatsu Kazuhiko) where possible.

## 10. Cultural-Advisor & Living-Religion Notes (CRITICAL)

**Shinto is a living, currently-practiced indigenous religion with ~80,000+ active shrines in Japan and 100+ million casual / cultural adherents.** This is structurally unlike Greek, Norse, Egyptian, or Arthurian — those are dead-pantheon canons where mythological figures have no current worship community. Shinto kami have priests, shrines, ongoing ritual calendar, and worshippers TODAY. Inari's Fushimi Inari Taisha hosts ~3M visitors/year. Abe no Seimei's shrine in Kyoto is active. ([Britannica — Shinto](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Shinto))

**This means:**

- **Not all kami are equally appropriate for collectible-card commodification.** Inari, Amaterasu, Hachiman, Susanoo are commonly-depicted in art and merchandise within Japan, mostly fine. The imperial-house-direct-ancestry of Amaterasu warrants extra care (Imperial House symbolism). Kami-of-living-shrines-with-active-worship require sensitivity in stat-line/flavor-text framing — kami are not just "characters."
- **Yokai are different from kami.** Yokai are folkloric supernatural creatures (closer to "monsters" in Western D&D-vocabulary terms); they are not actively worshipped and have a long folklore-merchandise tradition in Japan (mizuki-shigeru's GeGeGe no Kitarō has run since 1960; museums sell yokai-figurines; the Mizuki Shigeru Road in Sakaiminato is built around yokai-collectibles). Yokai-as-collectibles is *culturally established* in Japan in a way LoreVault can lean on.
- **Onmyōdō (Abe no Seimei lineage) is also still practiced.** Tsuchimikado lineage continues; treat Seimei as a respected historical-religious figure with active veneration, not just a mage-character.

**Required engagement (non-negotiable for greenlight):**

1. **Japanese-folklorist cultural advisor** — ideally a Komatsu-Kazuhiko-trained academic; long-term retainer (not one-shot consultation); sign-off on every card.
2. **Shinto-priest consultant** — for kami-card framing; Jinja Honchō (Association of Shinto Shrines) or independent.
3. **Japan-qualified IP counsel** — separate from US counsel; Japanese trademark and unfair-competition law differs (the Demon Slayer trademark-pattern case is in Japanese law).
4. **In-Japan creative director or partner studio** — co-development register, not foreign-imposition register. Consider co-publishing partnership with a Japanese illustrator-studio rooted in the woodblock tradition.
5. **Sensitivity-read cycle** built into card-development pipeline (not bolted-on at end).

**Naming convention guard-rails:**

- Use **macron-correct romanization** (Yōkai, Tsukumogami, Onmyōdō, Sekien) on collector-grade card-text; not folk-Anglicizations.
- Use **Japanese yokai names**, not English approximations ("Snow-Woman" as flavor, *Yuki-onna* as canonical).
- Cite Sekien / Yoshitoshi / Hokusai by name on card-back source-attribution.
- Honor the **kami / yokai / yūrei distinction** — three categories, not interchangeable.
