## 2. PD Status

- **Pyramid Texts (~2400–2300 BCE)**: deep PD. Carved on Old Kingdom tomb walls; 4,400+ years old. Samuel A.B. Mercer's 1952 English translation has fallen into US PD. ([Wikipedia — Pyramid Texts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_Texts), [Internet Sacred Text Archive](https://sacred-texts.com/egy/pyt/index.htm))
- **Coffin Texts (Middle Kingdom, c. 2055–1650 BCE)**: deep PD as artifacts; Faulkner's Aris & Phillips translation 1973–78 still in copyright — use older translations.
- **Book of the Dead (c. 1550 BCE – 50 BCE)**: deep PD. Papyrus of Ani (~1250 BCE) is the most-reproduced specimen; held at the British Museum. E.A. Wallis Budge's 1895 translation is firmly PD. ([Wikipedia — Book of the Dead](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_of_the_Dead))
- **Plutarch, *De Iside et Osiride* (~120 CE)**: deep PD. William Watson Goodwin's 1878 translation PD; Babbitt's 1936 Loeb translation entered US PD per the 1978 Copyright Code revision (copyright expired 1964). ([LacusCurtius / Penelope](https://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Roman/Texts/Plutarch/Moralia/Isis_and_Osiris*/home.html), [Wikisource Loeb Babbitt](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Plutarch's_Moralia_(Loeb)/Isis_and_Osiris))
- **Herodotus, *Histories* Book II (~440 BCE)**: deep PD; Rawlinson 1858–60 translation PD.
- **Pharaonic-period art and architecture**: all PD. Tomb-paintings, papyri, statuary, hieroglyphs — the entire visual register is multi-millennial PD. (Caveat: modern photographs of artifacts can carry photographic copyright depending on jurisdiction; in the US, *Bridgeman v. Corel* says faithful 2D reproductions don't get fresh copyright, but in the UK/EU the position is murkier and museum licensing programs assert rights.)
- **19th-c Egyptology illustrations**: PD. David Roberts 1838–49 lithographs — explicitly PD, distributed via Library of Congress and PICRYL ([LOC Public Domain Archive — David Roberts](https://loc.getarchive.net/collections/david-roberts-lithographs)). Karl Lepsius *Denkmäler aus Aegypten und Aethiopien* 1849–59 — PD ([Smithsonian Libraries](https://library.si.edu/exhibition/artists-books-and-africa/denkmaeler-aus-aegypten-und-aethiopien-full)).
- **Late-19th / early-20th c Egyptological art**: Lawrence Alma-Tadema (d. 1912) PD; Edwin Long PD; James Tissot Old Testament watercolors PD.

## 4. Pre-Modern Canonical Register — The Visual Goldmine

This is where Egyptian Myth pulls decisively ahead of Marvel/Stargate. The actual ancient Egyptian visual idiom is unmistakable, deeply PD, and *opposite to the dimensional-fantasy register* of every locked derivative.

**Source registers to lean into:**
- **Tomb-painting flatness.** Profile-view bodies, eye-and-shoulders frontal, twisted-perspective register lines. New Kingdom tomb paintings (Valley of the Kings — Seti I, Nefertari, Ramesses VI). Deep PD.
- **Papyrus illumination.** Papyrus of Ani Book of the Dead (British Museum, ~1250 BCE) — the Weighing of the Heart vignette is the canonical image. Hunefer's papyrus (also British Museum, ~1275 BCE). PD.
- **Hieroglyphic register.** Cartouche-frames, vertical and horizontal hieroglyph columns, ka-double imagery. Native to the canon.
- **David Roberts (1838–40 Egypt)**: 247 lithographs of pyramids, temples, Karnak hypostyle hall, Abu Simbel colossi, Philae, Dendera. Romantic-era European but PD and visually authoritative. ([LOC](https://loc.getarchive.net/collections/david-roberts-lithographs))
- **Karl Lepsius *Denkmäler*** (1849–59): 900 plates of scientifically-accurate hieroglyphic and architectural drawings. PD. ([Smithsonian](https://library.si.edu/exhibition/artists-books-and-africa/denkmaeler-aus-aegypten-und-aethiopien-full))
- **Lawrence Alma-Tadema** (Victorian Egyptian-classical scenes; d. 1912): "The Finding of Moses" 1904, "Antony and Cleopatra" 1883. PD.
- **James Tissot Old Testament watercolors** (1896–1902): the Exodus sequence. PD.

**Recommendation:** LoreVault Egyptian Universe should adopt **tomb-painting flatness as the house style.** Profile-view figures. Hieroglyphic vertical columns flanking each card. Cartouche-stamp signature. Lampblack-and-gold-leaf palette. This is the *aesthetic anti-Marvel* — Marvel Moon Knight is dimensional-realist cosmic horror, Stargate is sci-fi-dimensional, Gods of Egypt 2016 is CGI-action. Tomb-painting flatness is unmistakable, deeply PD, and totally distinct from every locked derivative.

## 6. LoreVault Original Expression (3–5 unique angles)

1. **Anubis-as-cross-Universe Judge.** Make Anubis the LoreVault meta-figure who weighs the heart of every other Universe's protagonist — Sherlock's cold-rationality heart, Wonderland-Alice's curiosity-heart, the Wolf's (Fairy Tale Universe) hunger-heart. The Hall of Two Truths becomes a cross-Universe procedural location. *This is original; no derivative claims it.*
2. **Hieroglyphics-as-flavor-text.** Where Magic-The-Gathering uses italicized prose at card-bottom and Top Shot uses stat blocks, LoreVault Egyptian uses *actual ancient Egyptian hieroglyphic columns* as flavor-text frames, with English translation in cartouche format. Card-as-papyrus.
3. **The Pythia-Thoth Cross-Tether.** If the Greek Universe (a separate slot in LoreVault's 5-of-N) includes the Pythia oracle, Thoth becomes her cross-Universe mirror — both are scribes-of-prophecy. A "Lattice" mechanic where Pythia reading + Thoth reading on the same card unlocks deeper canon.
4. **Set-as-Wolf-shadow.** Set is the desert what the Wolf (Fairy-Tale Universe) is the forest — both are necessary-disorder figures the canon needs to *not* destroy. Cross-tether: Set/Wolf shared "trickster-as-stabilizer" mechanic.
5. **Akhenaten as the Fork-in-Canon.** The heretic monotheist who tried to replace the entire pantheon with Aten and was erased after his death. LoreVault-original: a "heretic" card mechanic where Akhenaten cards temporarily blank the rest of the deck. This dramatizes a real historical event with no derivative claim.

## 7. Aesthetic Totalism

**Palette:** gold-leaf, lapis-lazuli blue, carnelian-red, malachite/Nile green, desert-ochre, ivory, obsidian-black (the Lampblack tone is *literal lampblack*, the same soot-pigment used on actual Book of the Dead papyri).

**Biome:** Nile river (papyrus reeds, blue-green water, lotus blooms), eastern desert (red rock, ochre dunes), western desert (entrance to Duat), pyramid plateau (Giza), temple-pylon entrance with flanking obelisks, hypostyle hall (forest of papyrus-bundle columns, Karnak), tomb-chamber (registers of painted figures), papyrus-marsh, sphinx-avenue, primordial mound (creation site), twelve gates of night.

**Iconography:** ankh, was-scepter, djed-pillar, scarab, wedjat-eye, hieroglyph, lotus, papyrus-bundle, ouroboros, falcon (Horus), jackal (Anubis), ibis (Thoth), crocodile (Sobek), cat (Bastet), lion (Sekhmet), cobra (uraeus), scales, feather, mummy-bandage, canopic jar.

**Typography:** hieroglyphic-margin in vertical columns flanking the card art; cartouche-stamp at card bottom containing the figure's name; English text in a serif-with-incised-stroke face suggesting carved-stone (Trajan-adjacent, but LoreVault should commission a proprietary face).

**Set-glyph:** single hieroglyph (the Eye of Horus is the most legible candidate; the ankh is the back-up; the Set-animal would be a deeper-cut option).

**Sample Moment cards at Common / Rare / Legendary / Ultra-Legendary tiers:**
- **Common:** "Apophis Repelled" — anonymous boatman with a spear over Apophis-serpent. Tomb-flat profile.
- **Rare:** "The Weighing" — heart on left pan, feather on right, Anubis adjusting, Thoth recording. Papyrus-of-Ani composition.
- **Legendary:** "Isis Reassembles Osiris" — the kite-form Isis hovering over the wrapped body, Nile-mud column. Late-Period stela composition.
- **Ultra-Legendary:** "Atum on the Primordial Mound" — the First Time. Self-creation in the waters of Nun. Single figure, lotus opening, sun-disk emerging. Spare, hieratic, gold-on-lampblack.

## 9. Recommendation

**STRONG candidate for LoreVault Series 2 or Series 3.** The case:

- **PD is the deepest possible.** 4,400-year-old source material. No living-rights-holder for Pyramid Texts, Plutarch, Herodotus, Roberts lithographs, Lepsius plates, tomb paintings, papyri.
- **The aesthetic is unmistakably distinct from every locked derivative**, IF LoreVault commits to tomb-painting flatness + hieroglyphic-margin + lampblack-and-gold rather than dimensional-fantasy realism. This is a real fork.
- **Audience legibility is top-tier.** Anubis, sphinx, pyramid, ankh, Eye of Horus all read at glance globally.
- **Cross-Universe Lattice mechanics are obvious.** Anubis-as-judge across all Universes; Thoth-as-scribe across all Universes; ankh-as-life-symbol; Eye-of-Horus-as-protection. These are natural LoreVault meta-cards.

**Sequencing recommendation:** lock Egyptian for **Series 2** rather than Series 3. The cultural pre-load is at peak (GEM inauguration cycle, Mummy 4 production, ongoing repatriation news). The 2027 PD release of the 1932 Mummy is a *threat* to differentiation, not an opportunity for LoreVault, because it will wash the visual market with Karloff-era horror Imhotep imagery. Better to ship with the LoreVault tomb-flatness register *before* the PD wash.

**Failure mode to pre-empt:** the team designing the cards will, by default, drift toward Gods-of-Egypt-2016 / Moon-Knight / Stargate visual register because that is what is in their visual short-term memory. Bake the David Roberts / Lepsius / Papyrus-of-Ani register into the brief and lock it before pencils touch paper.

## 10. Cultural Sensitivity

- **Modern Egyptian and Coptic communities** are living descendants of the civilization. Egyptian myth is not a "dead" religion in the sense Greek/Roman myth is — there are esoteric and Coptic-Orthodox continuities. Treat the canon as someone's actual heritage, not a free-for-all theme park.
- **Egyptology decolonization conversation is hot.** The repatriation movement is accelerating (Egypt-US Nov 2025 return; Netherlands Thutmose III head; ongoing Nefertiti-bust and Rosetta-Stone debates). Brittle takes here will draw fire. ([Travel and Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/how-egypt-antiquities-restitution-efforts-rekindle-heritage-recovery-all-you-need-to-know/), [Heritage Management Org](https://heritagemanagement.org/beyond-access-rethinking-ownership-justice-and-decolonization-in-digital-repatriation-initiatives/))
- **Mummies, human remains, and tomb-imagery.** Museum decolonization has shifted toward re-humanizing remains as ancestors with continuing dignity-claims, not "specimens." Avoid playing mummies for camp-horror in the Karloff/Universal register; LoreVault should treat Osiris-as-mummy as theological-archetype, not horror.
- **Egyptian-revival vs cultural-appropriation.** There is a real critique-history of European Egyptian-revival aesthetics (Napoleonic *Description de l'Égypte*, Hollywood Cleopatra-cosplay) being extractive. LoreVault should consult with **at least one Egyptian or Egyptian-diaspora cultural advisor** before launch — not as a fig-leaf but for actual content review.
- **Whitewashing risk on historical figures.** Cleopatra VII, Bayek-style Medjay characters, Akhenaten — the racial-representation conversation is live (see academic critique of Bayek depictions in AC Origins, [SAGE journal Becoming Bayek](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/15554120231152755)). Cast-and-illustrate accordingly.
- **Hebrew/Exodus overlay.** Anything touching Moses / Exodus / Ramesses-as-pharaoh-of-Exodus collides with the Prince of Egypt lock AND with sensitive Jewish/Christian/Muslim canonical territory. Recommendation: stay inside Egyptian-canon proper; do not import Exodus.

## ODIN ESCALATION — Marvel Moon Knight Brand-Confusion Flag

**Flag is real and live.** Marvel confirms no Moon Knight Season 2, but Oscar Isaac returns as the Fist of Khonshu in a future MCU project (Midnight Sons or otherwise) ([animenextseason.com](https://animenextseason.com/moon-knight-season-2/), [MovieWeb](https://movieweb.com/marvel-no-moon-knight-season-2-confirmed/)). The character remains active and the Khonshu-as-cosmic-horror visual is in active culture-cycle.

**Specific overlap zones to monitor:**
1. **Khonsu / Khonshu naming and depiction.** LoreVault must use the *historically accurate* hieroglyph-anchored Khonsu (moon-son of Amun-Mut, falcon-with-moon-disc or mummiform youth with side-lock). NOT the bird-skull mummy-bandaged Marvel Khonshu. This is the single highest brand-confusion risk in the entire candidate Universe.
2. **Ammit depiction.** LoreVault Ammit must follow the Papyrus-of-Ani composition (small-scale crocodile-lion-hippo waiting beside the scales). NOT the Ethan-Hawke-cult avatar from Moon Knight S1.
3. **"Avatars of the gods" framing.** Marvel's Moon Knight built a god-avatar mythology around Khonshu/Ammit/etc. LoreVault should *not* use "avatar" language; use Egyptological terms — *ka, ba, akh, sheut* (the five parts of the Egyptian soul) — which are PD canon and visibly distinct from Marvel's framing.
4. **Multiple-personality / dissociative-identity.** Steven Grant / Marc Spector / Jake Lockley is Marvel-specific. Avoid any LoreVault Egyptian figure with a multi-identity mechanic that could read as Khonshu-avatar.
5. **Mummy-suited urban-vigilante silhouette.** The white-cloaked-mummy hero-pose is locked Marvel. LoreVault Egyptian figures should be tomb-painting-frontal, not action-pose-urban-rooftop.
6. **"Egyptian gods walk among us in modern day"** is an active Marvel framing. LoreVault Egyptian Universe should stay diegetically inside ancient-Egyptian timeframe (or, if cross-Universe Lattice mechanics pull Anubis into modernity, do so in the Lattice's abstract-realm framing, not in a Marvel-style street-level urban setting).

**Recommended action before Series 2 ship:** legal review specifically of Khonsu, Ammit, and any character with both an Egyptian name and a Marvel comic appearance in the last 60 years. Marvel's Egyptian roster also includes Sekhmet, Bast, Atum, Set, Anubis, Horus, Thoth, Ra, Khonshu, Ammit, Tefnut, and others as Avengers/Moon-Knight-adjacent characters — the underlying figures are PD but specific Marvel design treatments are not.
