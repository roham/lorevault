# 1. Universe Identity (the 50-word elevator)

**Baker Street is the Pane of Argument: the Universe in which seeing-clearly is the only god, the cost of seeing is loneliness, and the Lattice itself can be reasoned about.** Every other Pane has gods, monsters, fairies, or fates. This one has a man with a magnifying lens, a friend with a service revolver, and a method that pretends the universe is solvable. Holmes is the Lattice's epistemologist: the figure who arrives at every other Pane to ask, *"How do you know?"*

This is not "Sherlock Holmes the property." This is **Doyle's 60 stories, 1887–1927, fully US-PD as of 1 January 2023, anchored visually on Sidney Paget (1891) + Frederic Dorr Steele (1903) + Frank Wiles (1914)** — the three first-publication illustrators whose work entered the world before any actor's likeness. The Universe's distinctive bet inside the LoreVault portfolio is that **deduction itself is a cosmological role** — Holmes is the Pane where Method is the divinity, and the breaking of Method (Reichenbach, the failed prophecy at Delphi, the Cottingley fairies that don't yield) is the load-bearing tragedy.

# 3. Biome Library (~16 named locations)

Each biome includes lighting register, atmosphere, and sound-cue. FLUX prompt-engineers paste these wholesale; flavor writers reference them by name.

1. **221B Sitting Room** — gas-lamp + hearth-fire, smoke-saturated air, sound-cue: clock-tick + violin-string. The 17 steps from the street; bow window over Baker Street; jack-knife pinning correspondence to the wooden mantelpiece; Persian slipper on the fender.
2. **221B Chemistry-Corner** — single oil-lamp at the deal table, vapour rising off retort, sound-cue: bubble-and-hiss + tinkling glass. Test-tube rack, chemical-stained pine table; Bart's-laboratory transposed to domestic scale.
3. **221B Bow-Window-at-Night** — gaslight-sepia spilling onto wet cobble below, fog-blue exterior, sound-cue: hansom-hooves + far-off paperboy. Holmes silhouette behind muslin.
4. **The V.R. Wall** — patriotic plaster pocked with bullet-holes spelling Victoria Regina; dim lamp-glow grazing pock-marks; sound-cue: powder-smell-after, dust-fall.
5. **The Strand at Dusk** — gas-lamp every 30 yards, fog-blue diffuse, sound-cue: hansom-clop + newsboy-cry + omnibus-bell. Yellow-fog "London Particular" tinting all whites toward sepia.
6. **Charing Cross Station** — train-hall iron-and-glass, coal-smoke + pearl-grey daylight from the clerestory, sound-cue: whistle-blast + porter-bark.
7. **Scotland Yard, Lestrade's Office** — green-shaded banker's lamp + green-painted iron staircase; deduction-sharp white at the lamp, blood-pearl absent; sound-cue: nib-on-paper + boot-step on stone.
8. **The Diogenes Club, Stranger's Room** — only-permitted-talking room; soft fog-blue + leather-amber; **no sound-cue** (the cue is the silence; the *cue itself* is the absence). Mycroft's club; the Empire-as-furniture.
9. **Mycroft's Whitehall Office** — ledger-stacked study, pearl-daylight through tall sash; sound-cue: clock-tick (longer interval than 221B's), single dipped pen.
10. **The Lyceum Theatre, Third Pillar** — gas-foot-lamps spilling amber under the portico, fog-blue beyond, sound-cue: orchestra-tuning leaking from the doors. *Sign of the Four* meet-point.
11. **St Bartholomew's Hospital, Chemical Laboratory** — high-window cold daylight + brass spirit-burner, deduction-sharp white pole, sound-cue: glass-clink + footstep-echo on tile. The meet-cute.
12. **The Stoke Moran Chamber** — single-candle, blood-pearl heavy, sound-cue: bell-rope-whistle + low hiss. Speckled-band claustrophobia.
13. **Baskerville Moor / Grimpen Mire** — moonlight on peat-water, fog-blue dominant, blood-pearl on the hound's eyes, sound-cue: distant bay + wind-on-tor + sucking mire. Phosphorus paint allowed.
14. **Reichenbach Falls** — alpine cataract, Reichenbach-black void below, deduction-sharp white spray, sound-cue: water-roar that erases voice. **Ultimate-tier biome.**
15. **The Thames Embankment / Steam-Launch** — fog-blue water, gas-amber ferry-lanterns, sound-cue: paddle-thump + low fog-horn. *Sign of the Four* chase.
16. **Baker Street Fog-Lane (the alley behind)** — Reichenbach-black gutter, single hooded gas-lamp, sound-cue: drip + boot-on-flag. The Irregulars' approach.
17. **Sussex Down / Holmes's Bee-Cottage** — chalk-down sunlight, Persian-slipper amber, sound-cue: bee-drone + sea (distant). Retirement biome — Unique-tier only.
18. **Pondicherry Lodge / Norwood Garden** — overgrown laurel, gas-amber on damp stone, sound-cue: cricket + spade-on-earth. Buried-treasure register.

# 5. Typography

**Font-family direction:** period-appropriate Victorian newspaper + Watson casebook serif. **Two-register system:** Strand-Magazine-headline plate vs. Watson-casebook-hand.

**Recommended families (free or licensable):**
- **Headline / casebook serif:** **Caslon** (Adobe Caslon Pro, or libre alternative *Libre Caslon Text*) — *Strand Magazine*'s actual register, 1891. Use for figure name + Set glyph caption.

# 8. Per-Tier Art-Density Rubric

Density is a count of cinematic-vocabulary techniques (per LoreVault Art Direction V3): chiaroscuro, scale-contrast, alvinizing, moment-of-decision-framing, cross-hatch overload, double-exposure-via-color, witness-perspective, environmental-staging, relational-dyad, iconographic-symbol, color-block, atmospheric-fog, halo-light, off-balance-tilt, ground-plane-extension, foreground-prop-anchor.

### Common (12 Moments × 15K mint)
**Floor:** plain, single-figure-or-prop, low atmosphere, gaslight-sepia register, no Parallel art treatment, **≤4 cinematic techniques.**
**Density example (sample direction):**
> *The Lamp at the Corner of Marylebone Road.* Holmes silhouette under a gaslamp, Paget single fog-tone wash, Persian-slipper amber on cobble. Techniques used: silhouette + fog-atmosphere + ground-plane-extension + halo-light. **No** chiaroscuro, no relational-dyad, no scale-contrast. 4/16.

### Rare (5 Moments × 2.5K)
**+ environmental staging, + relational dyad, + iconographic symbol, ≤8 techniques.**
**Density example:**
> *You Have Been in Afghanistan, I Perceive.* Holmes in profile at Bart's chemistry-bench; vapour rising off the beaker; Watson at the doorway in middle-distance; jack-knife in foreground pinning a slip of paper; Steele palette. Techniques: chiaroscuro + relational-dyad + foreground-prop-anchor + environmental-staging + iconographic-symbol (test-tube) + halo-light + cross-hatch + witness-perspective. 8/16.

### Legendary (2 Moments × 250)
**+ scale contrast, + chiaroscuro full, + moment-of-decision, + alvinizing, ≤14 techniques.**
**Density example:**
> *The Final Problem.* The Reichenbach struggle, mid-fall; Moriarty's hand catching Holmes's coat; Wiles cinematic framing; Reichenbach-black against deduction-sharp-white spray; falling water rendered as Paget cross-hatching; the cliff face dwarfs both figures. Techniques: chiaroscuro + scale-contrast + moment-of-decision + alvinizing + relational-dyad + atmospheric-fog + ground-plane-extension + cross-hatch + halo-light + off-balance-tilt + iconographic-symbol (the cascade) + double-exposure-via-color (water/sky) + foreground-prop-anchor (Holmes's stick) + witness-perspective. 14/16.

### Ultimate (1 Moment × 25)
**Full cinematic deployment, ALL relevant techniques OK, defining moment of the Set.** Registers Drew Struzan + Caspar David Friedrich + Jeremy Jarvis ceiling.
**Density example:**
> *The Last Bow.* Holmes at his Sussex bee-cottage, beekeeper's veil pushed back, single bee landing on a magnifying lens that has begun to fog over; sea visible in the deep distance; chalk-down sunlight. The only LoreVault Moment that depicts Holmes after the case is over. Struzan-poster compositional gravity + Friedrich Rückenfigur on the down + Jarvis card-art finish. 16/16. **This is the Series 1 Baker-Street ceiling.**

**Death-arc reservation:** Hester does NOT die in BS-4. She survives into Series 2+ as Watson's partner-in-archive. (Reichenbach kills Holmes-the-figure briefly; Reichenbach must NOT also kill Holmes-the-archive, or the Universe collapses. Hester is structural insurance.)

# 14. Cultural-Sensitivity Gates (mandatory pre-ship checklist)

Every Baker Street Moment passes through these four filters. **Failure on any single filter = block.**

1. **Tonga / Andamanese caricature in *Sign of the Four* — EXCLUDE.** No Tonga card; no Andamanese figure rendering. *Sign of the Four* Moments anchor on the Agra-treasure / Jonathan Small / Thames-chase axis (BS-2 Rare *The Lyceum Pillar*). The Andamanese are a real, currently-living, legally-protected Indigenous people; do not render them as caricature. The Mode-3 narrative-frame for any *Sign of the Four* card omits the Andaman flashback entirely.

2. **Mormon-cult flashback in *Study in Scarlet* — EXCLUDE; anchor on London-half only.** No Utah-revenge sequence; no Brigham-Young-coded antagonist; no LDS-Church-as-kidnapping-cult imagery. *Study in Scarlet* Moments anchor on the meet-cute (BS-1 Legendary 18 *You Have Been in Afghanistan, I Perceive*), the Brixton lodging-house, and the "RACHE"-written-in-blood scene. Jefferson Hope appears as London cabman-revenger only; revenge-motive named generically ("a cult-marriage in the West"), not LDS-specifically.

3. **Period antisemitic / xenophobic asides in flavor copy — FILTER.** Several Doyle stories carry casual anti-Jewish, anti-Italian, anti-Eastern-European descriptors. Standard practice: paraphrase the plot beats; **never transcribe period slurs into card flavor copy**. Scrub by greppable list (slurs, ethnic descriptors used as slurs); writer-room editor checks every flavor file pre-Council-Lock.

4. **Imperial-British framing — INTERROGATE; do not naturalize.** Doyle's London is the imperial capital; Watson is an Afghanistan veteran of an imperial war; the "Empire" is the room's subtext. We do not erase this — but we do not romanticize it either. Mycroft as "the British government" is rendered with Lattice-clerk irony, not patriotic gloss. The V.R. wall is a *bullet-pocked* V.R. — devotion-and-violence at once. Watson's Afghanistan wound is a real war-wound, not a costume detail.

# 15. Voice Register Rules (per FLAVOR-TEXT-DOCTRINE inheritance)

**Watson is the primary chronicler.** Watson's voice is **forensic-confessional**, Yawgmoth+Karn-adjacent in MTG terms (the witness who is also the participant; the doctor-narrator). Specific gravity: late-Victorian London, *Strand* serial, written-after-the-fact reflection, gentle reverence for Holmes broken by exact medical observation.

### Phrases that ARE the register
- *"It was the November of '94..."* / *"In the autumn of '87..."* — temporal-anchor opener, never a year alone (always month + apostrophe-year).
- *"I had not understood him at the time."* — retrospective-recognition pivot.
- *"The gold-cased watch was wound for..."* — material-detail entry into deduction.
- *"Holmes was, at that hour, in one of those dark moods which..."* — characterological turn with semicolon-or-em-dash.
- *"My friend, leaning forward, observed quietly:"* — quote-frame.
- *"There are, as you know, three sorts of..."* — Holmes's preferred enumeration cadence (always odd-number — three, five, seven; never four/six).
- *"You will recall..."* / *"As I have elsewhere recorded..."* — case-cross-reference; presupposes the reader-as-fellow-archivist.
- **The em-dash for the turn.** Never the colon. The em-dash is Watson's signature typographic move — it is the moment Holmes turns and Watson catches up.

### Forbidden register
- **No modern slang.** "vibe", "energy", "instinct", "gut", "totally", "frankly", "awesome", "epic", "iconic", "honestly".
- **No exclamation marks.** Watson does not exclaim. Watson observes.
- **No first-person plural collapse.** Watson is "I"; Holmes is "he"; never "we" except in literal action.
- **No anachronism.** No "vintage", "retro", "classic" — these are 21st-century retrospectives. The cards are written *from inside the period*, not about it.
- **No genre-tag self-reference.** No "mystery", "detective story", "case-file" used as labels — these are textual artifacts, not 21st-century genre badges.

### Mode mix (every Set audited to)
- **70% Mode-1** — attributed quote (Watson, Mycroft, Moriarty's letters, Hester Quill, Hopkins, Lestrade, Mary Morstan, Mrs. Hudson, Wiggins). Voice register varies by attributor; Watson's is dominant.
- **25% Mode-3** — in-world artifact: telegram form, *Strand* obituary clipping, casebook page, Watson's notebook, Hopkins's Yard report, Moriarty's letter, Mycroft's Whitehall memo, the V.R.-wall photograph, the buff-paper telegraph slip, the *Strand* archive index card. Each Mode-3 artifact uses the typography from §5 (Cutive Mono / IM Fell English Italic / Spencerian copperplate as appropriate).
- **5% Mode-2** — unattributed; reserved for the four 1/1 Ultimates only. Mode-2 is the *Lattice's own voice* breaking through; use sparingly.

### Per-Tier flavor-density floor
- **Common:** 1 sentence Mode-1 OR a single-line Mode-3 artifact.
- **Rare:** 2–3 sentences Mode-1; OR a multi-line Mode-3 artifact (e.g., a four-line telegram, a casebook fragment).
- **Legendary:** 3–5 sentences; relational; the figure-and-companion both audible; cross-tether Mode-3 echoes permitted.
- **Ultimate:** flavor-text is *the artifact* — the card BECOMES the casebook page, the *Strand* opener, the Whitehall memo, the bee-cottage letter. Mode-2 reserved here.
