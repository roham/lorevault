## 1. Universe Identity — The Pane of Categorical Failure

Wonderland is the Lattice's fault zone — the Pane where the categories that hold every other Universe together (*size*, *time*, *cause*, *identity*) stop holding. Other Panes obey local canonical physics: Baker Street, *deduction-from-evidence*; Greek Myth, *fate-as-debt*; Gothic Horror, *return-of-the-repressed*. Wonderland obeys nothing. Its only law is that every law is suspect, and the suspicion is dispatched in courtroom register, by figures who use jurisprudence to certify that nothing can be certified.

Wonderland is **Blue register** — *blue as Liddell-pinafore*, the colour of the unflappable child-logician who walks through nonsense answering on its own terms. The register is logic-puzzle-under-nonsense-surface: every Moment is a syllogism whose premises don't hold but whose form is impeccable. The Hatter's six o'clock tea is a logical solution to a punishment by Time-as-a-person. The Queen's "Off with their heads —" is grammatically complete and operationally empty. The Cheshire Cat is the difference between a property and its bearer.

Spine of every Wonderland Moment: *a category fails, a small unflappable figure notices the failure, and the failure becomes the point*. Cheshire-as-pure-grin is its iconographic literalisation; Alice (the categories' last loyalist) its dramatic spine; the Queen (sovereign of empty sentence) its political spine; the Hatter (pinned to one moment by another figure's punishment) its temporal spine; the White Rabbit (threshold-crosser pulling the protagonist into the Pane) its summons spine.

Wonderland is the only Pane in Series-1 where **Lattice-traversal is canon-internal**. Stepping through the Looking-Glass is the in-fiction explanation for how Panes connect. WL-4 *The Looking-Glass Pawn* therefore carries structural load no other Set carries — the Set in which the doctrine of the Lattice is, for the first time, *shown rather than declared*.

## 2. Palette

**Base substrate:** Tenniel ink-line on cream rag-paper. Every artwork begins as wood-engraved black-on-cream. Color is *added*, never *replaced*. The line stays visible through every saturation pass.

**Primary triad — locked across all 80 Moments:**
- **Pinafore-blue** — `#1F3A6B` — Liddell-blue, navy-leaning, Tenniel-canonical. The colour Disney inherited from Tenniel via Mary Blair, not the other way round. Worn only by Alice; appears nowhere else in the deck.
- **Apron-white** — `#F4EFE3` — cream rag-paper white; the page itself made into garment. Reads as paper.
- **Rose-red** — `#B8252A` — the painted rose, the heart-suit pip, the Queen's lips, the jam-tart filling. Carmine with Tenniel-engraver depth, never Disney scarlet, never Burton lacquer.

**Secondary palette — biome- and figure-keyed:**
- **Tea-stain amber** — `#9E6A2C` — every Tea-Table Moment; the Dormouse; the Hatter's coat-tea-ring; the rim of every cup.
- **Jam-tart crimson** — `#7A1018` — deeper than rose-red; reserved for Trial Moments and the sealed evidence of the Queen's court.
- **Bandersnatch violet** — `#4A2A5C` — the *Looking-Glass* edge-of-the-board colour; the frumious silhouette in the Tulgey Wood; the bruise-purple of the chess-piece night-side.
- **Cheshire-pink-not-magenta** — `#D77A8E` — Tenniel's tabby gets a *pink* register, not Disney's magenta-fuchsia. This is the single most-policed colour in the deck; every Cheshire artwork passes a "would Disney's IP team flag this?" check before mint.
- **Mushroom-green** — `#5C7239` — Caterpillar's seat; the underside of the impossible garden; chartreuse-adjacent but desaturated to engraver-green, never Disney chartreuse.
- **Hookah-blue** — `#2C5570` — smoke-rings only; never figure-cloth.
- **Looking-Glass silver** — `#9DA4A8` — the mirror-Pane itself; the Lampblack-traversal surface (see §11).

**Forbidden colours:** Disney 1951 Cheshire magenta-fuchsia (`~#D62D8B`) — banned outright; Disney Hatter chartreuse-orange (Hatter is engraver black-and-white plus tea-amber); Disney Queen black-bobbed-hair red-and-black valentine-collar (Queen is cream-and-rose, tall and playing-card-flat); Burton Underland mossy palette, Marmoreal white-and-pearl, Bonham-Carter blue-vein makeup; McGee blood-on-apron crimson-on-white — our apron stays clean.

## 3. Biome Library

Thirteen biomes, each tied to specific Moments and each with a Tenniel-anchored composition rule.

1. **The Rabbit-Hole** *(WL-1 anchor)* — vertical descent shaft lined with bookshelves, marmalade-jars, looking-glass fragments, drawer-fronts. Camera falls *with* Alice; horizon is the page-margin; light source is the diminishing circle of daylight overhead. Tenniel 1865 falling-Alice composition.
2. **The Long Hall of Doors** *(WL-1)* — corridor of locked doors, glass table with the small golden key. Doors at impossible heights to make Alice's size-failure visible *before* she drinks.
3. **The Pool of Tears** *(WL-1)* — interior flood; furniture half-submerged; Mouse + Dodo + Lory + Eaglet float. Only biome where ink-line dissolves at the water-surface — a Lampblack signal marking the threshold.
4. **The Caterpillar's Mushroom-Pillar** *(WL-1)* — mushroom rendered tall enough that the Caterpillar's "Who are *you*?" descends from above. Hookah smoke-rings are perfect circles — never letters (smoke-letter exhalation is Disney choreography, banned).
5. **The Duchess's Kitchen** *(WL-2 deep cut)* — pepper-mill, cradle, fish-footman/frog-footman pair at the doorway. Heat-haze in line-density only, never colour-bloom.
6. **The Infinite Tea-Table** *(WL-2 anchor)* — table extends past the visible horizon; cups, pots, sugar-bowls multiply toward the vanishing point. The clock reads six o'clock and only six o'clock; no second-hand.
7. **The Croquet-Ground** *(WL-3)* — flamingo-mallet curves are the line-rhythm; hedgehog-balls inert engraver-cross-hatch; arches are card-soldiers bent at the waist. Checker-pattern visible through the grass — pre-figuring WL-4.
8. **The Courtroom-of-Cards** *(WL-3 anchor)* — stadium where audience and jury are themselves cards. King's bench small and fussy; Queen's bench tall and severe; the Knave centre; verdict hung in mid-air on a card-fan above the jury-box.
9. **The Garden of Live Flowers** *(WL-4)* — Tenniel's Tiger-Lily anchors. Flowers are flowers with mouths — never pansy-faced ingenues (Disney 1951 banned).
10. **The Looking-Glass Parlor** *(WL-4 anchor)* — domestic Victorian sitting-room with the mirror-frame as Pane-shape. The mirror surface IS the Lampblack-silver Pane through which other Universe Shells can be glimpsed (see §13).
11. **The Chessboard-Landscape** *(WL-4)* — rivers as black squares; brooks Alice must jump as rank-divisions; horizon is a board edge. The country *is* a chess game.
12. **The Tulgey Wood / Wood-Where-Things-Have-No-Names** *(WL-4)* — Bandersnatch-violet shadow biome; Jubjub silhouette in the canopy; trees lose their labels at chest-height. The Jabberwock lives here.
13. **The Snark-Island / Mock Turtle's Beach / Lobster-Quadrille Shore** *(WL-4)* — Bellman's chart has nine vertical lines and is "perfectly and absolutely blank." Beach grey-beige; the Boojum implied by absence — never drawn.

## 4. Iconography Lexicon

Every glyph **Tenniel 1865/1871** unless noted; every entry passes the Disney/Burton/McGee echo-check.

- **Pocket-watch (too-many-hands)** — White Rabbit, Hatter; impossible hand-positions; no second-hand; "10/6" price-card optional.
- **Drink-Me bottle** — small glass, paper label tied with string, "DRINK ME" in courtroom-record typography.
- **Eat-Me cake** — small box, currants spelling "EAT ME".
- **Mushroom (Caterpillar's)** — spotted cap, pillar-tall stalk; Caterpillar atop, never beside.
- **Hookah** — long stem; smoke in *rings*, never letters. Wielded only by the Caterpillar.
- **Vorpal Sword** — Tenniel 1871 *Jabberwocky* plate. Plain short-sword. Banned: any glow, rune-engraving, "named-and-singular" framing (Burton's state-artifact lock).
- **Looking-Glass frame** — 1871 mantelpiece mirror with pendulum-clock above. Surface is Lampblack-silver Pane (Universe-tier only).
- **Teacup + teapot** — six o'clock service; Dormouse-in-teapot is a separate canonical glyph.
- **Croquet-flamingo** — held by the neck, body curving as mallet. Bird is calm; never Disney's panicked.
- **Playing-card-soldier** — flat-pip body, human face on the pip, stiff-collared.
- **Fish-footman / frog-footman pair** — livery, oversized envelope, Duchess's-doorway composition.
- **Bandersnatch silhouette** — Tenniel 1871 implied; render *silhouette only*, never fully-resolved (avoids Burton boar-bear-rat lock).
- **Snark / Boojum** — Newell 1903 + Furniss-adjacent register; Snark is *implied through its hunters* (Bellman, Baker, Beaver, Butcher, Banker — the B-list); Boojum is *never drawn*. Encounter with the Boojum deletes a figure from the Lattice (§11). Image-language: a bell, a chart, an empty space where a figure should be.
- **Key (small golden)** — on the glass table at the door-hall.
- **Jam-tart** — crimson filling; courtroom-evidence prop.
- **Baby-pig-baby** — Duchess's kitchen → baby becomes pig. The Universe's quietest Lampblack icon: identity-failure mid-frame.
- **Bellman's bell** — Newell 1903.
- **Top-hat with "10/6" card** — Hatter's signature (NOT Disney's chartreuse hat with yellow bow-tie).
- **Chess-piece (White Knight, Pawn-becoming-Queen)** — 1871; White Knight whose horse is also a fish is the Universe's set-glyph anchor (§6).
- **Painted rose** — brush mid-stroke; red leaking onto white petal.

**Iconography hard interdictions** (mint checklist; full list in §13 negative-prompt): Disney 1951 Doorknob, Cheshire magenta-fuchsia stripe, chartreuse-orange Hatter, pansy-girl Talking-Flowers; Burton's Bayard, Marmoreal, glowing Vorpal Sword, Tarrant Hightopp; McGee's lank-black-hair Alice, blood-apron, Vorpal-knife, asylum framing — all banned.

## 5. Typography

**Tenniel-engraving + court-record + rhetorical-question margin.**

Title-card type is a Victorian wood-engraver display face — narrow, high-contrast, all-caps, hairline serif. Reference: 1865 + 1871 Macmillan title-pages + *Strand Magazine* courtroom-transcript inset. Must read *engraved*, never *handwritten* (Carroll's manuscript hand is interdicted as too-Liddell-coded; see §14).

**Caption / flavor-text register:** courtroom-transcript. Every flavor-text candidate sits inside a ruled box, led by italic *Q:* or *A:* on Trial-coded cards; led by small-cap COURT RECORDER OF HEARTS — on Lampblacker-Spine voicings (§11). Otherwise: single paragraph, dry, exact, Victorian-engraver register.

**Italics rule (binding):** italics mark **categorical words** — *time*, *size*, *court*, *rule*, *name*, *self*, *place* — as suspect. Italics are never decorative; they are the Universe's typographic Lampblack-signal.

**No exclamation marks at all** (Decision 5). The Queen shouts via dashes — "Off with their heads —". Excitement is conveyed through *what* is said, not *how* it is punctuated.

**Pun absolutely forbidden** (Decision 5). Wonderland's wit is logic-puzzle wit, never joke-wit. Carroll's own source-text puns (Mock Turtle's "tortoise / taught us") are referenced *via the Moment they appear in*, never reproduced as flavor-text. Zero tolerance.

**Set-title type:** narrow Victorian display-cap with a single ornamental rule beneath, drawn freehand in Tenniel-engraver register. Pinafore-blue on cream.

## 6. Set-Glyph Design

**Universe set-glyph (signs every Wonderland card):** an **impossible chess-piece** — Tenniel-1871 White Knight whose horse is also a fish, single-line wood-engraver style, ~12mm tall, lampblack on cream. The join-line where horse becomes fish is the Lampblack-signal: categorical-failure made glyph.

**Per-Set glyphs (8mm, beneath the Universe glyph):**

- **WL-1** — **rabbit-hole-spiral**: descending Archimedean spiral with a pocket-watch dial at the centre, counterclockwise.
- **WL-2** — **teapot-pouring-time**: Tenniel teapot in profile, spout pouring not liquid but a clock-hand exiting at the six-o'clock position.
- **WL-3** — **playing-card-tipping**: a card seen edge-on, mid-fall, Heart pip at the apex, tipped over the courtroom-bench horizon.
- **WL-4** — **looking-glass-frame**: mantelpiece-mirror frame with Lampblack-silver surface; inside the frame, a pawn-becoming-queen silhouette.

Universe-glyph and Set-glyph stack lower-left of every card; reader reads Universe first, Set second.

## 7. Shell × Universe Interaction Rules

Wonderland is the most Shell-restricted Series-1 Universe — most Shells require categorical coherence Wonderland refuses to grant.

| Shell | Wonderland status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **PRIME** | Mandatory baseline. | Every Moment ships in PRIME first; Tenniel 1865/1871 + §2 palette. |
| **DREAM** | **Native shell.** | Reserved for Alice, Hatter, Cheshire. DREAM is not a re-render — it is the deepening of the categorical-failure PRIME already shows. PRIME shows failure as event; DREAM shows it as substrate. |
| **HOLLOW** | **PERMANENTLY FORBIDDEN** (Cosmology Decision 13). | Apocalypse implies coherent before-and-after; Wonderland denies coherent time. Any HOLLOW Wonderland card is a line-failure. |
| **CYBER** | **Hatter only.** | Six-o'clock-punishment is a logic-puzzle; logic-puzzle in datafall is the cleanest CYBER reading. Tea-Table as a server-room frozen at a single timestamp; Dormouse as a sleeping cron-job. Alice and Cheshire are CYBER-banned. |
| **MIRROR** | **Snark + Looking-Glass figures only.** | *Through the Looking-Glass* is already a MIRROR-shell of *Wonderland*. Reserved for Snark figures (Bellman, Baker, Boojum) and Looking-Glass figures (Red Queen, White Queen, Tweedles, Humpty, Lion-and-Unicorn). MIRROR-Alice is the Pawn. MIRROR stacks with PRIME for additional Lampblack-density. |
| **AETHER** | **Very rare.** | Reserved for Snark/Boojum (cosmic-edge) and Cheshire-as-pure-grin (grin-as-mathematical-property). One AETHER per Set maximum. |
| **SAINT** | **Cheshire only.** | Cheshire is the saint of the categorically-lost — patron of every figure who has stopped meeting their own criteria. Single Universe-tier card across the four-Set arc. |
| **MODERN** | **Banned for protagonist figures.** | MODERN-Alice fails (a 2026 schoolgirl is a meme, not Wonderland's logician). Allowed only for summons figures (White Rabbit as courier-app notification) at Common only. |

Shell-discipline protects Wonderland from drifting into "trippy aesthetic" — every Shell choice does work on the figure's Spine, not skin.

## 8. Per-Tier Art-Density Rubric

Five tiers; the rule is not "more colour at higher tier" — it is "more *categorical-failure visible per square inch* at higher tier."

- **Common** *(48 of 80 — 12 per Set)*. Tenniel-line single figure on cream; no biome beyond an engraved horizon-line; primary triad + one accent. One categorical-failure visible.
- **Rare** *(20 of 80 — 5 per Set)*. Two-or-three-figure composition with partial biome; full triad + one secondary. Two categorical-failures visible.
- **Legendary** *(8 of 80 — 2 per Set)*. Tenniel-meets-impossible-color-saturation; full biome; cinematic composition. Three+ categorical-failures stacked. Defining canon-beats (Cheshire's grin-fade, Mad Tea-Party at six o'clock, Trial verdict, Looking-Glass passage).
- **Ultimate** *(4 of 80 — 1 per Set)*. Full cinematic deployment. Universe-defining beat anchoring marketing for the Set. Lampblack-density visibly higher than other tiers; Spine *and* Lampblacker-Spine references stacked.
- **Universe (1-of-1)** *(1 across the four-Set arc)*. Reserved for **Alice Steps Through the Looking-Glass** (WL-4). Single panel; mirror-surface as Lampblack-silver Pane through which Baker Street, Enchanted Kingdom, Gothic Horror, and Greek Myth are visible as faint silhouettes inside the silver. The only canonical Wonderland Moment that explicitly stages cross-Pane traversal.

## 9. Per-Parallel Reservation Rules

Five Parallels (per ART-DIRECTION schema): ARCANA, AETHER, WITNESS, NEON, 1/1.

- **ARCANA** *(Rare+ only, when categorical-failure is mathematized)* — Moment as mandala-glyph: Cheshire-as-geometric-pattern, Caterpillar's smoke-rings as recursive mandala, chessboard as grid-of-grids. Six across the four Sets. Never Common.
- **AETHER** *(Snark/Looking-Glass cosmic-edge)* — deep-cosmos render: Boojum's absence as Pane-shaped void in star-field; Looking-Glass parlor opened onto infinite mirror-corridor. Three across, all in WL-4.
- **WITNESS** — **permanently disabled for Wonderland.** Wonderland is non-realist by canon; WITNESS would force a photoreal register that pulls toward the Liddell biographical surface (§14). Zero WITNESS Moments — Wonderland-specific Parallel-table override, locked.
- **NEON** *(CYBER-shell Hatter only)* — Tea-Table as server-room with neon-pink Tenniel-line tracery. Two NEON Moments in the four-Set arc, both WL-2 Hatter.
- **1/1** *(one per Set, four total — independent of §8's Universe 1-of-1)*. WL-1 *Alice Falls Past the Bookshelves*; WL-2 *Six O'Clock Forever*; WL-3 *You're Nothing But a Pack of Cards*; WL-4 *The Pawn Becomes Queen*.

Tiers and Parallels stack (Legendary AND ARCANA, Ultimate AND NEON). The Universe-tier 1-of-1 card is exempt from Parallel — it is its own Parallel.

## 11. Lampblacker-Spine Candidate

**Decision: the Glass-Polisher** — voiced as **the Court Recorder of Hearts**.

Per Master Synthesis Part 3 §3, every Series-1 Universe needs an original load-bearing centre to give public-domain figures emotional gravity. For Wonderland: the figure who cleans the Looking-Glass and is the only one who knows it's two-way. The Universe's maintenance-of-reality character — her work is *literally keeping the Pane-surface clear*.

Spine line: *"I keep the surface clear. What looks back is not my doing."*

She has no name in canon. In Wonderland she is *the Glass-Polisher*. In WL-3 (Trial Set) and across all four Sets in flavor-text, she signs as **the Court Recorder of Hearts** — chosen because the Trial is the canonical Moment with a recorder in attendance; the sleight is that the Recorder is the Polisher seeing her work from the other side.

Register: **unsigned-correspondent** — third-person observation, courtroom flat-tone, no exclamation, italics on categorical words. The only Wonderland figure who uses second-person *plural* ("the court will note"); second-person *singular* is reserved for the Queen as threat.

She appears partially in seven Moments across the four Sets (never as figure; always as byline). In the Universe 1-of-1 *Alice Steps Through the Looking-Glass*, she is the hand just leaving the silver-frame in the bottom-margin — implied, not depicted.

Voice exemplars:

> *Q: Was the cat there before the grin? A: The court has not been able to establish which side of the question is the question. — The Court Recorder of Hearts*

> *The verdict came first. The trial took longer, because the verdict had to be defended.*

> *I keep the surface clear. What looks back is not my doing. The Pane is not the painter. — The Court Recorder of Hearts*

## 12. Cross-Universe Lampblack Tether Map

Wonderland's cross-Universe tethers are richer per-figure than any other Series-1 Pane, because every Wonderland figure is already a *categorical-failure* — and categorical-failure is what the Lattice is built out of.

- **Cheshire as Lampblack-incarnate cross-Pane *residue*** *(north-star)* — **Pattern 9 / Deep / Lampblack Trade (residue, not figure)** per Cross-Universe-Storyline-Patterns Dossier #21 §2. The grin-without-cat is the literalisation of Lampblack-residue. **No figure crosses; only residue.** Cheshire stays in Wonderland; the *grin-as-fog-shape*, *grin-as-canopy-gap*, *grin-as-bed-shadow-curl*, *grin-as-asphodel-horizon-line* gestures at Cheshire without rendering Cheshire-as-figure on the cross-Pane card. Series-1 deployment: a faint smile-shape in the negative space — a *recognition-tier residue*, not a Cheshire-as-figure cameo — on one card per Universe (Baker Street: Reichenbach fog; EK: Black-Forest canopy; Gothic Horror: shadow under Mina's bed; Greek Myth: asphodel-meadow horizon). **Recognition-only; never explained.**

**RECLASSIFICATION PER V2 REVIEW** — was previously declared as *4 cross-Pane Echoes* (which collided with Phase-05 §14.1 Series-1 Echo cap of 1-2 across the entire Series). Resolution per V2 Review §6 + §9 Rewrite 3: classified as **Pattern 9 / Deep / Lampblack Trade** (no figure crosses; only residue). Per-Series Deep budget is 0-4 (Cross-Universe-Storyline-Patterns Dossier #21 §2 — *"the lowest-stakes, highest-density crossover Moments — should make up the bulk of crossover production"*). 4 Cheshire-grin residues fits within 0-4 Deep budget perfectly. On-card visibility reduced from *Cheshire-grin-cameo* to *residue-only smile-shape in fog/canopy/shadow/horizon*. See REVIEW-V2-QUALITY.md §6 + §9 Rewrite 3.
- **Alice ↔ Persephone** — girls who fall through portals. Pomegranate-seed and Drink-Me bottle are the same binding-object viewed sideways. Twin-Soul candidate, Series 2.
- **Alice ↔ Snow White** — Underworld-Bride / Looking-Glass-Pawn pair (Apple ↔ Looking-Glass; bitten-fruit ↔ stepped-mirror). Twin-Soul candidate, Series 3.
- **Hatter ↔ Hyde** — broken-by-time vs broken-by-self; both are surfaces pinned to a punished interior. Cross-tether Moment: *The Hatter's Watch on Hyde's Cabinet-Shelf*.
- **Hatter ↔ Miss Havisham** *(Dickensian, Series 2)* — both pinned to a single moment of betrayal-by-Time. The Tea-Table at six o'clock and the Wedding-Cake with cobweb are the same Moment in two registers.
- **Queen of Hearts ↔ Stepmother (EK) ↔ Furies (Greek)** — female-plural-vengeance archetype. Painted-roses ritual is the poison-apple ritual viewed sideways. Tri-Pane candidate, Series 3.
- **Queen of Hearts ↔ Klytemnestra ↔ Lady Macbeth** *(Shakespearean META-tether)* — sovereigns whose sentences carry the threshold from order to chaos. The META-shadow under the Queen is Lady Macbeth ("out, damned spot" reads as a paint-the-roses-red gesture).
- **Mushroom ↔ Soma ↔ Poisoned Apple** — substance-of-categorical-passage. Caterpillar's mushroom, Persephone's pomegranate, Snow White's apple, Aladdin's lamp-rub: all the same Lattice-work, crossing a figure from one category-state to another.
- **Pocket-watch ↔ Time-Machine ↔ Tablet of Destinies** — time-as-artifact across Wonderland + Verne/Wells + Mesopotamian. The Hatter's broken pocket-watch is the proto-form of every cursed time-instrument in the deck.
- **White Rabbit ↔ Hermes ↔ Manannán mac Lir** — threshold-crossing summons-figure. The pocket-watch is the Hermes-staff in domestic register.
- **Looking-Glass ↔ Perseus's Polished Shield ↔ EK's Magic Mirror ↔ Dracula's Failing Mirror** — the four-Pane reflective-surface object; per Master Synthesis Part 3 §1.2, the most-recurring Echo-Object in the Lattice.
- **Snark / Boojum ↔ Lovecraft's "softly and suddenly vanish away"** *(Series 3)* — the Boojum is the proto-Lovecraftian deletion-event; the only canonical Wonderland artefact that *removes a figure from the Lattice*.
- **Loki ↔ Cheshire** *(Series 2)* — Loki = transformation; Cheshire = vanishment-as-mode. Complementary, not redundant.

The Lampblacker-Spine (Glass-Polisher / Court Recorder of Hearts) is herself a cross-Pane voice: her byline appears once in Baker Street flavor-text and once in Gothic Horror flavor-text across the Series-1 arc — unannounced. The Lampblack signal that the same hand has been on every Pane-surface.

## 14. Cultural-Sensitivity Gates

**The Carroll-Liddell biographical hard rule** *(non-negotiable; supersedes every other rule in this Bible)*:

- Never reference Alice Liddell as the model.
- Never reference Charles Lutwidge Dodgson as Lewis Carroll except in legal-disclosure copy at the back of Set-art books.
- Never use Carroll's actual photographs of Alice Liddell, even though they are in the public domain.
- Never use Carroll's manuscript hand (*Alice's Adventures Under Ground*) as typography — too-Liddell-coded.
- Never use a photoreal Alice register at all (why §9 disables WITNESS for Wonderland).
- Treat Alice as a fictional figure: a seven-year-old child-logician in pinafore-blue and apron-white, drawn from Tenniel.

**Caucus-Race / Dodo:** render straight from Tenniel; the Dodo is a Dodo. Do not editorialise the Carroll-as-Dodo self-portrait — it is biographical.

**Psychedelic-readings:** the hookah-smoking Caterpillar and size-changing mushrooms have been read as drug-allegory since the 1960s. LoreVault does not lean in — it is a 1960s overlay, not Carroll. Render straight from Tenniel; let viewers project. Banned: *trip*, *trippy*, *drug*, *acid*, *psychedelic*, *high*. Acceptable: *the Caterpillar's smoke*, *the mushroom*, *the size-passage*.

**Madness and the Hatter:** the Hatter is *time-broken*, never *mentally ill*. The Gothic-Horror cross-tether frames the condition as a temporal injury inflicted by Time-as-a-person — Carroll-canonical, avoids the comic-mental-illness pattern. Banned: *crazy*, *insane*, *mad as a*, *psychotic*, *unhinged*. Acceptable: *time-broken*, *pinned to six o'clock*, *punished by Time*.

**Disney trademark line** *(legal-binding)*: never use bare "Alice in Wonderland" as product-line. The Pane is **Wonderland**; the figures are *Alice*, *White Rabbit*, *Cheshire Cat*, *Hatter*, *Queen of Hearts*. Disney holds USPTO marks on "Alice in Wonderland" in specific Nice classes; the figures themselves are deep-PD.

**Brand-collision check** *(every commission)*: artwork must (a) read as Tenniel, and not read as (b) Disney 1951, (c) Burton 2010/2016, (d) McGee 2000/2011, (e) Dalí 1969, (f) photoreal child Alice. Any of (b)–(f) triggered, the artwork does not ship.

## 15. Voice Register Rules

**Liddell-logic.** Every flavor-text candidate is tested by one question: *would a seven-year-old child-logician concede the form of this sentence even while its content is impossible?* If yes, ships.

**Niv-Mizzet + Liliana adjacency.** The Hatter is mathematic-pedant Niv-Mizzet-register ("*time* is a person; his absence is a person-shaped hole; the hole is at six o'clock"); the Queen is sovereign-of-empty-sentence Liliana-register ("the verdict — first. The trial — afterward. The Knave —"). Carry the *register*, never the *content*.

**Reversal Joke archetype mandatory.** Every Legendary or Ultimate sentence built on a logical inversion — the second clause breaks the expectation set by the first.

- *The verdict came first. The trial took longer, because the verdict had to be defended.*
- *The cat was not there. The grin had not been informed.*
- *Six o'clock is a long time. Longer, when it is the only time.*
- *The pawn became a queen. The queen became a pack of cards. The cards remembered being a pawn.*

**Three-beat structure:** statement, restatement, snap. Or: question, answer, second question that breaks the answer.

- *The Hatter poured tea. The cup did not fill. The cup had agreed not to.*
- *Q: Where is the Cheshire Cat? A: Elsewhere. Q: Then what is here?*

**Second-person address.** Allowed only from the Queen, only as threat: *you will join the gardeners*; *you have not been heard from the dock*; *you — off —*. Never *we*, never plural-you-as-flattery. Every other speaker uses third-person observation or first-person courtroom-witness.

**Italics on categorical words** (binding): *time*, *size*, *court*, *rule*, *name*, *self*, *place*, *day*, *cause*, *head*. Every time, no exceptions.

**Lampblacker-Spine voicing.** Court Recorder of Hearts byline appears only at Legendary+ tier. At Common and Rare, the Recorder voices flavor-text *anonymously*; the reader recognises the voice across the deck before recognising the byline — the intended Lampblack effect.

**Banned words** *(writers' room)*: *whimsy*, *whimsical*, *magical*, *dreamy*, *zany*, *bonkers*, *kooky*, *lol*, *trippy*, *psychedelic*, *high*, *crazy*, *insane*, *unhinged*, *psychotic*, *mad as a*. Acceptable: *peculiar*, *curious*, *singular*, *insupportable*, *categorical*, *the case*, *the matter*, *time-broken*.

**Voice exemplars per Set:**

- **WL-1** — *The Rabbit checked his watch. The watch was not the issue. The Rabbit was late for the issue, and the issue had not waited.*
- **WL-2** — *The clock was at six. The Hatter was at six. The Time who had punished them had moved on.*
- **WL-3** — *The court has heard the verdict. The court has not yet heard the trial. The court will hear it, in due time, if there is time. — The Court Recorder of Hearts*
- **WL-4** — *The pawn went forward one square. Then another. Then she was a queen, and the squares were a kingdom, and the kingdom was a game she had not been told she was playing.*
