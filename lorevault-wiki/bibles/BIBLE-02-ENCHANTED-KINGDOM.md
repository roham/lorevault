## 1. Universe Identity

The Enchanted Kingdom is the **Pane of Inheritance**. Cosmologically it is where the Lattice stores its bloodlines, its curses, and its consequences. Stepmothers, wolves, and witches in this Pane carry full Spines because every act here is *justified by its own logic* — survival logic, hunger logic, the morality of what gets eaten. This is the Pane where the wolf is right to be hungry and the stepmother is right to be jealous, and the cards are not asking you to forgive either of them.

**Register:** Black/Green. Black for the iron, the soot, the forest dark, the closed eye behind glass. Green for the moss, the foxglove, the pine-needle floor of the Schwarzwald, the witch's herb-rope. Never the saturated emerald-and-pink princess palette of the post-1937 Disney register; that is the ghost we are exorcising on every card.

**Pre-Disney darkness is mandatory.** The 1812 first edition of *Kinder- und Hausmärchen* is the textual seam, not the 1857 sanitized seventh edition that German parents demanded. Mothers, not stepmothers, in some 1812 variants. Eye-pecking doves at the wedding. Hot iron shoes on the Queen at the wedding feast. Cinderella's stepsisters mutilating their own feet. The Pane *remembers* this and the cards *imply* it; we soften the explicit gore at the surface (per §14) but we never soften the moral weight beneath.

**Lampblack at this Pane:** the apple, the spindle, the breadcrumb, the red cloak, the glass slipper, the iron key, the bone-comb, the mirror. These are the residue burned when the Pane touches every other Pane in the Lattice. Snow White's apple is *also* Pandora's jar, *also* Eden's apple, *also* the apple of Eris, *also* Wonderland's *Drink-Me*. The Pane of Inheritance is where the Lattice reveals that every fruit-of-binding is the same fruit refracted.

**Tonal signature:** forest at dusk, woodsmoke under a slate sky, iron-bound chests, candle-stubs guttering in lanterns, the sound of an axe somewhere out of frame.

**Cosmological role in the Series-1 lattice:** EK supplies the curse-and-bloodline mechanic that the other four Universes inherit. Holmes does not investigate curses; the Wolf carries the curse to him. Wonderland does not write contracts; the Witch's contract is the cosmic ancestor of every Wonderland-bargain. EK is upstream of all four other S1 Panes.

## 3. Biome Library

Twelve biomes, locked. Every Moment lands in exactly one biome (the Convergence-Cathedral exception aside, capped at one per Series). Biomes are visual rooms — they fix the lighting, the depth-cue stack, and the iconographic vocabulary.

1. **The Deep Black Forest.** Schwarzwald canopy seen from below, gnarled-Rackham silhouettes, pine and beech, mist-pooling on the forest floor, sunlight in narrow shafts. Light source: above and behind, never frontal. Reference: Rackham 1909 Grimm; Vogel Munich KHM 1894.
2. **The Witch's Cottage (gingerbread/cake-and-sugar).** Exterior of the Hänsel und Gretel hut — caramel-shingle, sugar-pane window, gumdrop-eaves rendered as Vogel cross-hatched grotesque, NOT as Disney candy-cartoon. Smoke from a crooked chimney.
3. **The Glass Mountain.** Faceted ice-and-quartz peak, vertical-sublime, Nielsen 1925 register: tiny figure, vast geometric mountain, gold-leaf accents on the facets. The mountain is a *shape*, not a picture.
4. **The Tower (Rapunzel / Briar Rose).** Single circular masonry stack rising out of bramble; window halfway up; ivy and brier rose at the base. Crane 1875 heraldic-pageant register for Briar Rose; Vogel grimmer for Rapunzel.
5. **The Woodcutter's Hut.** Single-room timber cottage, smoking chimney, axe-block outside the door, deer-hide nailed to the wall. Honest poverty register. Vogel + Rackham midground reference.
6. **The Ash-Hearth / Cinder-Corner.** Cinderella's domain. Stone hearth with iron firedogs, ash-mound on the floor, the daughter sleeping on the warm stones, lentils sorted into bowls, doves on the windowsill. Crane register, ash-grey ground.
7. **The Gingerbread House (interior).** Inside the witch's hut: the iron oven crouched in the corner, herb-rope, suspended cages, jarred eyes-and-teeth, the kettle, the bone-comb. Vogel cross-hatched plus Bilibin folk-frame ornament.
8. **The Dwarves' Cottage (seven beds).** Small interior — seven small beds in a row, a single round table, copper pots, mountain-pickaxes leaning by the door, mineral specimens on a windowsill. **Dwarves UNNAMED or numbered I–VII; never Disney-named, never Disney-silhouette.** Jüttner 1905 silhouette register.
9. **The Frozen Palace (Snow Queen).** Andersen 1844 register — vast hall of mirror-shard floors, ice-pillars, blue-violet light from above, single throne of polished glass. Dulac 1911 + Nielsen 1925 stack. The shard-of-troll-mirror motif everywhere.
10. **The Bramble-Thicket (Briar Rose's hundred-year hedge).** Walled wood of black-thorn, knife-thorns turned outward, briar-rose bloom only at the very top. Crane 1875.
11. **The Queen's Bedchamber.** Stone bedchamber, the heavy bed with embroidered curtains, the magic mirror facing the bed, the ironwork chest at the foot, the spinning wheel in the corner casting its long bone-shadow. Vogel + Doré register. **NEVER a Disney palace ballroom.**
12. **The Seven-Doors Corridor (Bluebeard).** Stone corridor with seven iron-banded doors; the seventh keyhole leaks blood-shadow at floor-line. Doré 1862 *Contes* register; chiaroscuro-vertical, single candle, the iron key in the foreground glinting. **The defining Bluebeard biome.**

**Auxiliary biomes** (Common-tier only, low-density use): the village square (Hamelin); the underwater coral palace (mermaid; Dulac); the woodcutter's clearing; the ash-hearth (interior wide); the kingdom road at twilight; the church-yard well (frog-prince).

## 4. Iconography Lexicon

Eighteen objects, every one PD-source attributed. Every Set's prop budget pulls from this list. New props require Council Set-Lock signoff. Each entry: object · canonical Source · positive-prompt anchor · negative-prompt veto.

1. **Poisoned Apple.** Grimm KHM 53 (1812). *Russet-on-oxblood with one bite mark and a single curling leaf, Jüttner 1905 silhouette, hag's hand entering frame from below.* **Veto:** Disney 1937 deep-red drip-shape with purple cauldron drip; smooth waxen single-color symmetry; "Evil Queen" hag-prosthetic.
2. **Spinning Wheel + Spindle.** Perrault 1697 / Grimm KHM 50. *Heavy oak treadle wheel, hand-forged iron spindle with blackened tip, spool of grey flax, Vogel cross-hatched.* **Veto:** Disney 1959 Aurora's tall-thin slender stylized "magical" wheel.
3. **Breadcrumb Trail.** Grimm KHM 15 (1812). *Dark coarse rye crumbs scattered across moss, two birds in mid-stoop in a second panel, Rackham line.* **Veto:** stylized bread cartooned as Disney loaf-cubes.
4. **Red Cloak (Rotkäppchen).** Perrault 1697 / Grimm KHM 26. *Coarse oxblood-red wool with a peaked hood, pulled close at the throat, the girl's basket on her arm. Doré 1862 register — small figure, vast forest.* **Veto:** Disney velvet-bright candy-red; modern fashion-illustration cape.
5. **Glass Slipper.** Perrault 1697 (Cendrillon — *pantoufle de verre*, NOT *de vair*; the "fur slipper" theory is folklore-wrong, Perrault wrote glass.) *Tall-heeled glass mule catching candle-amber light, single shoe on flagstone, Crane 1875 register.* **Veto:** Disney 1950 specific powder-blue-tinted, jewelled-arch geometry; modern Swarovski-crystal interpretations.
6. **Magic Mirror.** Grimm KHM 53. *Hand-forged iron oval frame on a stone wall, the surface a polished obsidian-black not silver, the Queen seen from behind speaking into it, Doré shadow-stack.* **Veto:** Disney 1937 wall-mounted Art Deco frame with masked smoke-face; any rendering with the smoke-face speaking; "Mirror, mirror" recited as chiseled-text in frame.
7. **Iron Key (Bluebeard's seventh).** Perrault 1697. *Single hand-forged iron skeleton key on a red ribbon, a single drop of blood on the bow of the key that will not wipe off. Doré 1862.* **Veto:** ornamented heraldic-fantasy key; goldwork.
8. **Woodcut Twig (Spine glyph).** LoreVault original. *A single broken twig, two thorns, one leaf, engraved as a marginal ornament in Rackham/Vogel register.* This is the EK set-glyph (see §6).
9. **Axe (the Woodcutter's).** Grimm KHM 26 / KHM 5. *Heavy single-bit felling axe with a worn ash-haft, the head black with use. Vogel register.* **Veto:** Disney *Frozen* Kristoff-style ergonomic; axes that look like merchandise.
10. **Iron Shoes (the Stepmother's punishment).** Grimm KHM 53 (1812 ending). *Glowing-orange iron dance-shoes on tongs over a bed of coals — implication only; we never show feet inside (see §14).* **Veto:** any image that *uses* this implement on a body in-frame. Implication only.
11. **Bone-Comb.** Grimm KHM 53 (one of three poisonings). *Carved-bone comb, white-yellow ivory, a tooth missing, a curling lock of black hair caught in it. Bilibin/Vogel register.*
12. **Candle-Stub.** Multi-source (Snow White wake; Tinderbox; Bluebeard corridor). *A guttering tallow candle with a long-collapsed taper, wax run to the holder. Candle-amber light source; the rest of frame in iron-black.*
13. **Dwarf-Pickaxe.** Grimm KHM 53. *Short-handled mining pickaxe, iron head, leather-wrapped grip, leaning by a small bed. Jüttner 1905.* **Veto:** seven color-coded pickaxes; cartoon-stout dwarves wielding them; Disney whistle-while-you-work composition.
14. **Ice-Shard (Andersen's troll-mirror splinter).** Andersen 1844. *A single razor-thin glass splinter, glass-blue, casting an asterisk-glint. The shard in Kai's iris is the canonical Spine deployment.* Dulac/Nielsen.
15. **Hand-Mirror (the Queen's vanity).** Grimm KHM 53. Oval iron-framed hand-mirror, polished obsidian face. Distinct from §4.6 wall-mirror; this is the portable variant used at the moment of "who is the fairest."
16. **Swan-Feather.** Andersen *Wild Swans*; *Ugly Duckling*. *A single white feather, the quill blackened at the cut, lying on grey water or stone.* Dulac jewel-tone register.
17. **Raven-Feather.** Multi-source (Grimm KHM 25 *Seven Ravens*; messenger-of-doom across canon). *Glossy iron-black feather, the barbules catching iron-black-violet sheen.* Tethers to American Gothic Raven (see §12).
18. **Wolf-Tooth.** Perrault 1697 / Grimm KHM 26 / KHM 5. *Single yellowed canine, root-stained dark, sometimes mounted on a leather thong. Doré 1862 / Bilibin Slavic-adjacent.*

**Banned trade-dress objects** (will never appear on an EK card): Disney's apple-with-purple-drip; Disney's powder-blue-with-bow Cinderella slipper; Disney's wand of the Fairy Godmother; Sleeping Beauty's spindle "magical glow"; Aurora's tiara; Frozen Olaf's carrot nose; Frozen ice-magic blue tracery; *Tangled* glowing-hair effect; *BatB* Lumière candelabra silhouette and Cogsworth pendulum-clock-face — these are the Disney 1991 sentient-objects, all of which are out of bounds (see §14).

## 5. Typography

Three faces, locked. No fourth. Card chrome typography, set-name display, attribution body, and marginalia are exhausted by these three.

1. **Display (Set name + Universe-mark): Fraktur-adjacent.** **Berthold Mainzer Fraktur (PD)** or **Theuerdank-derived woodcut Fraktur (1517 PD)** as the canonical reference. Use modern PD-cleared digitization (e.g. *Walbaum Fraktur*, *Unifraktur Maguntia*). Heavy-stroke, broken-form, absolutely *not* the rounded "fairy-tale faux-medieval" Disney *Cinderella* / *Sleeping Beauty* opening-title silhouette family (Andy / Waltograph / Storybook Casual — banned).
2. **Caption (Moment title + flavor attribution): Woodcut-block caption.** Reference: late-19th-c German chapbook caption faces; **Caslon Antique-adjacent** with rougher impression. Slight ink-bleed and broken-edge implied. Sets the body-of-text register at small sizes.
3. **Marginalia (mode-3 woodcut-inscriptions, recipes, ballad-margins): Ballad-margin italic.** Reference: 19th-c. ballad broadsheet italics; treat as scribbled-on-the-margin. Used for the ~50% mode-3 flavor-text deployment (§15).

**Ligature and ornament rule:** every Set name is set with at least one *Versal* (woodcut decorated initial) — square-block four-line versal, foliated, in the Vogel/Rackham marginal-ornament tradition. The versal is the place where the Lampblack-twig glyph sometimes hides as a ghost-watermark.

**Banned typography signatures:** anything reading as Waltograph, Andy, Storybook Casual, Disney Princess wordmark family, *Frozen* engraved-with-frost letterforms, Mary-Poppins/Saving-Mr-Banks chalk-script, *Wicked* musical-poster sans.

## 6. Set-Glyph Design (Per 4 Sets)

Each Series-1 EK Set ships under a single unifying Universe-mark — **the woodcut-twig (a broken branch, two thorns, one leaf)** — *plus* a per-Set distinguishing element nested into the twig. The Universe-mark is the Lampblack residue stamp; the per-Set element is the local seal.

### EK-1 — *Snow Black* (Topsy-mode)

**Set-glyph:** woodcut-twig + a *bitten apple silhouette* fitted into the negative space between the two thorns. The twig is rendered in iron-black; the apple is oxblood. The apple shows one bite-mark.

**Tonal note:** Topsy-mode Set (see §10). The set-glyph carries a tiny ghost-footnote dagger (†) tucked into the twig's bark — the Footnoter narrator's mark. This is the *only* EK Set that carries the dagger.

### EK-2 — *The Wolf at Seven Doors*

**Set-glyph:** woodcut-twig + a *wolf-tooth* threaded through the leaf as if pinning it. Iron-black twig, candle-amber tooth. The seven-doors corridor is implied by a faint vertical bar-pattern behind the glyph at high resolution (drops out at favicon size — production-correct).

### EK-3 — *The Apple and the Mirror*

**Set-glyph:** woodcut-twig + a *small obsidian oval* (the hand-mirror) where the leaf used to be — the leaf is reflected *in* the obsidian instead of attached. Glass-blue rim around the obsidian. Reads as "the Pane sees itself."

### EK-4 — *The Snow Queen*

**Set-glyph:** woodcut-twig + an *ice-shard* through the leaf, fracturing it into seven crystalline points. Glass-blue shard, foxglove-violet at the pierce-point. The twig itself is dusted with frost (tiny pewter-white speckle).

**Universal rule:** every set-glyph is engraved as if by Vogel/Rackham — visible chisel-marks, slight imperfection. The Lampblack residue stamp (the twig-only version, no per-Set element) appears on every card-back in the Pane regardless of Set.

## 7. Shell × Universe Interaction Rules

Per the Multiverse Shells doctrine (PRIME / CYBER / MODERN / AETHER / HOLLOW / MIRROR / DREAM / SAINT). Series 1 ships **PRIME-only** for EK as for the other four Universes; the rules below govern Series 2+ Shell-deployments and the rare Series-1 Shell-experiments that pass Council Set-Lock.

| Shell | Verdict for EK | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **PRIME** | **MANDATORY for every figure.** Series 1 = PRIME-only. | Rackham/Nielsen/Jüttner/Dulac stack. The base register. |
| **HOLLOW** | **HEAVY USE.** EK is a natural HOLLOW host. | The forest after the wolf has fed; the cottage after the witch has burned; Briar Rose's hundred-year-overgrown court; the kingdom *after the curse held*. Every HOLLOW-EK Moment is *the bloodline that didn't continue*. |
| **MIRROR** | **HEAVY USE — primary for Snow-Black register.** | Snow-Black IS the Mirror-shell of the Snow White Spine (the corruption that looks back from smoked glass). The Stepmother is herself a Mirror-shell of the Mother. Magic-mirror is the Mirror-shell's iconographic anchor. |
| **DREAM** | **WORKS for Andersen Snow Queen + Mermaid + Tinderbox.** Restrained for Grimm/Perrault. | Andersen's prose is already half-DREAM — the soldier and the dog with eyes-as-saucers, the mermaid's voice exchange, the hundred-year Brier Rose hedge. DREAM-shell honors Andersen's surrealism without flattening the dread. **DREAM is BANNED for the Wolf, the Witch, and Bluebeard** — those Spines must not soften into reverie. |
| **AETHER** | **RESTRAINED. Reserve for Snow Queen + Andersen cosmic-scale.** | Snow Queen is the only EK figure with cosmic-scale Pane-of-Inheritance reach (winter as world-condition; the troll-mirror as cosmic instrument). **AETHER is BANNED for the seven dwarves, Cinderella, the Wolf, Hänsel & Gretel.** Those are *small-life* figures; cosmic registers betray them. |
| **CYBER** | **NEAR-BAN.** Reserve for 1/1 only (single Mirror-Tinderbox card, possibly). | Fairy tale's chassis is pre-industrial; CYBER-shell EK reads as cosplay. Single-card 1/1 experiments only with explicit Council approval. |
| **MODERN** | **BAN for all canonical figures.** | A modern-2026 Snow White is Disney's commercial register; we don't go there. Modern-shell EK material is reserved for the Lampblacker-Spine anchor (§11) and the Cartographer/Mapmaker; never the public-domain figures themselves. |
| **SAINT** | **WORKS for Briar Rose + Ugly Duckling + Cinderella (transfiguration moments).** | The "and she became revered" beat. SAINT-shell EK is the iconographic rendering — Pre-Raphaelite reverence-pose, gold-leaf nimbus, name-banner above the head. Used sparingly; ~1 per Set max at Legendary tier. |

**Cross-shell-deployment rule:** any EK figure may receive at most three Shell deployments across Series 1–3 (PRIME + 2). The Wolf takes PRIME + HOLLOW + MIRROR (the three are the canonical predator stack). Snow White takes PRIME + MIRROR (Snow-Black) + AETHER reserved-for-Series-3-only. The Snow Queen takes PRIME + AETHER + DREAM. Briar Rose takes PRIME + SAINT + HOLLOW.

## 8. Per-Tier Art-Density Rubric

Per the canonical 20-Moment-per-Set distribution (12C / 5R / 2L / 1U), each rarity tier has a fixed visual-density target. Density is the **pictorial labor budget** — the number of distinct compositional elements, the depth of palette, the rendering polish.

### Common (12 per Set, 15,000 mint) — SINGLE-PROP / SINGLE-FIGURE

- **Density:** lampblack-on-paper register only. One prop OR one figure, not both. Two-tone palette max (iron-black + one accent from §2).
- **Rendering:** pen-and-ink line; flat ash-grey ground; minimal cross-hatching.
- **Reference register:** Vogel marginal-ornament; Bilibin folio-frame at smallest scale.
- **Examples:** *The Crumb Trail* — moss path with crumbs, two birds. *The Spindle* — single spinning wheel against ash-grey wall. *The Axe* — a single axe in a stump, no figure.
- **Function:** Common cards are the **breadcrumbs of the Pane** — they hint, they archive, they don't perform.

### Rare (5 per Set, 2,500 mint) — TWO-ELEMENT COMPOSITION

- **Density:** figure + prop, OR figure + biome-fragment. Three-tone palette including one full §2 color.
- **Rendering:** Crane heraldic flat-color plus one inked accent; or Vogel cross-hatched figure on flat ground.
- **Reference register:** Crane 1875 *Beauty and the Beast*; Vogel KHM 1894 mid-page composition.
- **Examples:** *The Fitting* — glass slipper held to a foot at the hearth; figure + prop + ground. *Rapunzel's Hair* — the figure at the tower window, the rope-of-hair descending; figure + biome.
- **Function:** Rare cards are the **named scenes** — the moments a fan recites by heart.

### Legendary (2 per Set, 250 mint) — FULL JEWEL-TONE PLATE

- **Density:** figure(s) + biome + iconographic prop + atmospheric event. Full §2 palette deployed; gold-leaf accents permitted.
- **Rendering:** Dulac 1911 jewel-tone watercolor plate; Nielsen 1925 Aubrey-Beardsley-Japanese-woodblock; Jüttner 1905 oil-paint register; Rackham 1909 tipped-in color plate. Tipped-in-plate compositional language — vignette frame, captioned bottom edge.
- **Reference register:** Jüttner *Sneewittchen* 1905; Dulac *Andersen* 1911; Rackham *Grimm* 1909; Nielsen *East of the Sun* 1914 + *Hansel and Gretel* 1925.
- **Examples:** *Apple at the Door* — Jüttner-register plate; the hag's hand, the bitten apple, Snow White's eye half-closed behind glass. *The Snow Queen on Her Throne* — Dulac+Nielsen, full ice-palace, foxglove-violet light.
- **Function:** Legendary cards are the **plates the fans frame** — they are the canvas-prints the collectors hang.

### Ultimate (1 per Set, 25 mint) — FULL RACKHAM-NIELSEN PRE-RAPHAELITE-DARKNESS

- **Density:** maximum. Multi-figure narrative, multi-plane depth, full atmospheric weight, gold-leaf and metallic-ink finishes acceptable.
- **Rendering:** the deepest Pre-Raphaelite-darkness deployment we make. Pre-1929 Rackham at his most Constable-tipped-in. Nielsen at his most *East of the Sun*-precious. Pre-Raphaelite tonality (Burne-Jones twilight, Waterhouse jewel-mood) is permitted as cross-bleed at this tier only.
- **Reference register:** Rackham 1909 Grimm full-plate; Nielsen 1925 *Hansel and Gretel* gatefold; Jüttner full-plate oil; Dulac frontispiece.
- **Examples:** *The Glass Coffin Vigil* (Snow White Ultimate); *The Mirror in the Eye* (Snow Queen Ultimate). One Ultimate per Set, period.
- **Function:** Ultimate cards are the **founding plates** — the single-image-per-Set that defines the Pane's entry to the Lattice.

## 9. Per-Parallel Reservation Rules

Parallels are the orthogonal artistic registers (ARCANA / AETHER / WITNESS / NEON / 1/1) deployed as Rare-and-above variants only. Per the canonical economy, parallels do not appear at Common tier.

ARCANA-EK is always *grimoire-aesthetic* — alchemical sigil borders, Latin-pseudepigraphic-margin Fraktur, hand-drawn ritual-mandala backgrounds. **Mandatory for any "the curse takes effect" Moment.** |
| **AETHER** | **Reserved for Andersen Snow Queen cosmic-scale ONLY.** | The cosmos-as-mirror; winter-as-world-condition; the troll-mirror shattering across the night sky. WITNESS-EK reads as *cottage-floor-camera*, ethnographic, the way Maria Tatar would teach a class. Used for the Cartographer (§11), the Charcoal-Burner's Daughter, the woodcutter's wife. **2–3 deployments across Series 1, ALL anchored on the Lampblacker-Spine anchor or the household-witness archetype.** Never deployed for Snow White / Cinderella / the Snow Queen herself. |
| **NEON** | **CYBER-shell ONLY. Near-banned in EK.** | Synthwave-magenta-and-cyan against forest-green is a tonal collision the Pane cannot survive. **Reserved for one experimental 1/1 across Series 1**, almost certainly the Topsy-mode Snow-Black Set's joker-card. |
| **1/1 (ONE-OFF)** | **One per Set. Four total in Series 1 EK.** | Reserved for: EK-1 Topsy-mode Footnoter narrator's self-revealing card (the Footnoter speaking in first person); EK-2 the seventh door opening; EK-3 the Stepmother's transformation into the hag; EK-4 Kai-and-Gerda recognized at the palace. All four 1/1s are commissioned single artist-statements, no shell or parallel constraints. |

WITNESS never overlaps with the §11 Lampblacker-Spine when that Spine is at SAINT-shell; the documentary register and the iconographic register are mutually exclusive.

## 10. Per-Set Narrative Arc (4 Sets × 20 Moments)

Series 1 ships four EK Sets sequenced May 2026 → April 2027. Each Set is 20 Moments (12C / 5R / 2L / 1U). Voice-register, biome anchor, and tier candidates locked below.

### EK-1 — *SNOW BLACK* (Topsy-Mode)

**Anchor figure:** Snow White in the corrupted-reflection register — the Snow-Black variant. The Pratchett-grade satirical lead per the Topsy-Register doctrine (Mode-2 register).

**Voice register:** **Mode-2 satirical with The Footnoter as narrator.** Per Topsy-Register doctrine — the Pratchett-caliber footnoting voice that makes the dark visible by understating it. Every Common card and 50% of Rares carry a footnote-marginalia in the §5 ballad-margin italic. Footnotes are signed `— the Footnoter, m.s.` (mortuus sed scribens — "dead but writing"). The Footnoter is EK-1's voice spine.

**Biome anchors:** the seven-bedded cottage; the queen's bedchamber; the deep Black Forest; the glass coffin clearing.

## 11. Lampblacker-Spine Candidate

Per Synthesis Part 3 §3 — every Series-1 Universe must commission a single original Lampblacker-Spine anchor for Series 2 (no staggering). The EK candidate per the Synthesis is **The Cartographer**: "the woman who draws the Forest's maps. Maps don't survive in canon fairy tales; she is why."

**This Bible accepts The Cartographer and proposes a refined name and Spine:**

### The Charcoal-Burner's Daughter

A second-generation child of a forest charcoal-burner. Apprenticed not to her father but to the *unmaking* of her father's labor — she gathers the lampblack soot from the kiln-walls and grinds it into ink. With that ink she draws the Forest's maps. She is the only mapmaker the Pane has, and the only reason maps don't survive in canon fairy tales is that she keeps them.

**Role:** the EK *epistolary witness* — the silent index of every fairy tale. She is the Pane's archivist; the woodcutter's wife who watched Hänsel and Gretel pass on the path she drew; the unnamed crone in the corner of the village who handed Red Riding Hood her cloak; the maid who polished the Queen's mirror once a fortnight and saw what spoke back. She is what Vanessa Ives is for Gothic and the Footnote is for Baker Street.

**Spine (locked):**
> "The wolf walked past me. I had already drawn his shadow."

**Visual anchor:** mid-30s woman, weathered face, ink-stained hands, a leather satchel of folded vellum, a single eagle-feather quill behind her ear. Iron-black + ash-grey + candle-amber palette. Vogel/Rackham midground reference.

**Why "Charcoal-Burner's Daughter" over "Cartographer":** the noun *Cartographer* is a Latinate professional title — too clean for the Pane. *Charcoal-Burner's Daughter* is folkloric, anonymous-archetype phrasing (per the Mode-1 attribution rule, §15), and the *charcoal* is the literal Lampblack of the Pane. Her ink IS the Pane's residue. She is the figure who literally turns Lampblack into image.

**Series-2 deployment:** The Charcoal-Burner's Daughter ships as the WITNESS-parallel anchor across all four Series-2 EK Sets. She is never the Legendary or the Ultimate (the canonical figures own those tiers); she is the Rare-tier Lampblacker-Spine card, and her parallels propagate through the Set as marginalia, attribution, and ghost-presence at the edge of frame in other figures' Moments. She dies in the Series-3 finale Set the way Vanessa Ives dies in Penny Dreadful Season 3 — not because the Pane requires it, but because the Lampblacker-Spine doctrine permits the willing-substitute beat that ends the figure's voice while leaving the Pane intact.

**Alternate name kept on bench:** *The Third Sister Who Walked Out* — folkloric-anonymous in the same Mode-1 register, but loses the literal lampblack-ink connection. Reserve for a possible companion-figure (sister-in-archive); do not use as the primary Spine.

## 14. Cultural-Sensitivity Gates

The Pane's editorial filter, locked. Every Set passes through every gate; failures route back to revision before mint.

### Permanently Excluded

- **KHM 110 *Der Jude im Dorn* ("The Jew Among Thorns")** — antisemitic by Wilhelm Grimm's deliberate editorial design across successive editions. **Permanently excluded** from all LoreVault EK material in any Series, any Shell, any Parallel. The figure does not exist in the Pane.
- **KHM 7 *Der gute Handel* ("The Good Bargain")** — antisemitic content. **Permanently excluded.**
- **Brothers Grimm's antisemitic asides in 1812 footnotes and 1857 editorial notes** (specifically the "Zigeuner" stock-villain framings) — **permanently excluded.** Where we adapt tales with itinerant figures, we use neutral descriptive terms ("travellers," "the wandering musician," "the road-people"). The Bremen Town Musicians is fine; the framing is what we filter, not the figure.
- **The hooked-nose / antisemitic-caricature visual register** (cross-tether to Cruikshank's Fagin in Dickensian) — **EDITORIAL FILTER, not subject to PD-source-neutrality argument.** Even when the source is PD, the visual register is excluded.
- **MZB *Mists of Avalon* tether-bait** — Marion Zimmer Bradley's documented complicity in serial child sexual abuse renders even passing reference catastrophic. EK does not tether to MZB material; the Briar Rose ↔ Avalon-mist tether stops at PD-Pre-Raphaelite source (Burne-Jones *The Last Sleep of Arthur in Avalon* 1881–98) and never crosses into MZB phrasing.

### Softened (the darkness preserved, the gore not staged)

- **Cinderella eye-pecking doves at the wedding** (Grimm 1812 ending) — *implied, never staged.* The doves appear; their action is off-frame. The Mode-3 marginalia may carry a single-line ballad-fragment that lets the reader complete the scene; the image never does.
- **Snow White Stepmother iron-shoes-of-vengeance at the wedding feast** (Grimm 1812 ending) — *the iron shoes appear on tongs over coals in §4.10; the wearing is implication only; we never stage feet inside.*
- **Cinderella stepsister toe-and-heel mutilation** to fit the slipper — *the bloody slipper on the stair is allowed; the mutilation is off-frame, marginalia-only.* The Mode-3 ballad-margin in EK-3 may carry the four-line PD ballad-fragment that names the act; the image never does.
- **Hänsel and Gretel oven-shove of the witch** — *the oven mouth is shown lit; the witch's silhouette in the doorframe is shown; the shove is the cut; the resolution is implied by the next-card transition (the children walking out).* Vogel register supports this exactly — Vogel did not stage the shove either.
- **Bluebeard's bloody chamber** — *the seventh door is shown closed with shadow leaking under; the corridor is shown; the chamber's contents are implied only.* The iron key's drop-of-blood that won't wipe off is the entire visual proof.

Where Grimm tales include itinerant figures coded "Zigeuner": neutral-descriptor substitution applied at flavor-text level (Mode-1 attribution: "the road-people," "the wandering musician," "the third-village stranger"). Visual register: never hooked-jewelry-and-shawls stock-villain caricature; honest-poverty register only.

## 15. Voice Register Rules

EK's voice register is **bone-rhyme, Greven-il-Vec-adjacent, with Song-Fragment archetype (A12 Echo) carrying ~25% of cards.** Per the LoreVault Flavor-Text Doctrine — Mode-3 dominates at ~35% Set composition (woodcut-inscriptions, marginalia, recipes); Mode-1 attribution to anonymous archetypes carries another ~40%; Mode-2 (signed first-person) is reserved for Topsy-mode (EK-1) and the Lampblacker-Spine (§11) at ~25%.

### Mode-1 — Anonymous Archetype Attribution

The Pane speaks as anonymous folk. Attribution lines (when used): "*— the third sister*," "*— the charcoal-burner*," "*— the hangman's wife*," "*— the woodcutter who didn't return*," "*— the goose-girl, after*," "*— the child who came home*," "*— the maid who polished the mirror*," "*— the road-people, in passing*."

Mode-1 is the spine of EK-2 (*The Wolf at Seven Doors*) and EK-3 (*The Apple and the Mirror*). Anonymous-archetype attribution is the doctrinal opposite of Baker Street's signed-Watson register — and that contrast is intentional: BS = chronicled, EK = remembered.

### Mode-2 — Signed First-Person (Topsy + Spine Only)

Mode-2 EK voices are strictly two: **The Footnoter** (EK-1 Topsy-mode narrator, signing `— the Footnoter, m.s.`) and **The Charcoal-Burner's Daughter** (Lampblacker-Spine, signing `— the C.-B.'s daughter, by the kiln-wall`). No other figure signs in first person across Series 1 EK. Snow White does not speak in first person. The Snow Queen does not speak in first person. The Wolf does not speak in first person. **The Pane is too old for first person.**

### Mode-3 — Woodcut-Inscriptions, Marginalia, Recipes

Mode-3 carries ~50% of EK cards (above the LoreVault average of 35%) because EK is a Pane *of inscribed objects* — the witch's recipe-books, the cottage's herb-rope inventories, the queen's mirror-engravings, the woodcutter's account-margins, the ballad-broadsheets pinned in village squares. Mode-3 deployments include:

- **Recipes** (the witch's): "*Take a child by the wrist. Coax with sugar. Heat the oven a full afternoon.*"
- **Marginalia** (the queen's mirror-engraving border): "*Spieglein, Spieglein, an der Wand* ・ *Wer ist die Schönste im ganzen Land?*"
- **Ballad-fragments** (the village broadsheets, two-line PD ballad couplets): "*The miller's wife was sweeping ash, / She did not see the wolf go past.*"
- **Inventories** (the woodcutter's day-book): "*One axe re-handled. Two deer skinned. The deer-heart returned to the queen, by message.*"
- **Inscriptions** (Bluebeard's seventh door, etched in iron): "*Was hier ist, ist nicht für dich.*"

Mode-3 is set in §5's ballad-margin italic and renders as if scribbled into the artwork's edge. The reader has to lean in.

### Bone-Rhyme

The Pane's signature meter. Two-stress alternating lines, end-rhymed on closed syllables (bone-words, iron-words, blood-words). The voice of Grimm-tradition minus the songbook softening — closer to the Border Ballads in cadence than to the chapbook-prettified later Grimm editions. Bone-rhyme is the voice baseline; the Andersen-tone (EK-4) is the deliberate departure from it (prose-poem cadence, melancholy-from-above; never bone-rhyme).

### Greven-il-Vec-Adjacent Register

The flavor-voice archetype borrowed from MTG's Greven il-Vec: imperative-clipped, predator-wisdom, no introspection, no apology. The Wolf speaks Greven-il-Vec verbatim ("Open the door."). The Witch speaks a compressed-conditional variant ("If you eat, you stay."). The Stepmother speaks third-person passive (*"It was given. The girl was sent."*). These three figures together carry the Pane's predator-class voice register.

### Song-Fragment Archetype (A12 Echo)

~25% of EK Common-tier and ~40% of Rare-tier cards carry an A12 Echo — a single PD ballad-fragment, two to four lines, attributed to "the village" or "the road" or unsigned. Source pool: Border Ballads (Child); Des Knaben Wunderhorn (1805–08); Gammer Gurton's Garland (1810); the German *Volkslied* corpus. The A12 Echo is the cross-Pane Lampblack signal — the same ballad-meter shows up under Mina's diary in Gothic, under the Greek chorus, under Wonderland's Mad Tea songs. The Pane's ballad-fragment IS the residue.

### Per-Figure Voice Locks (Hard Rules)

- **Wolves speak imperatives.** "Open the door." "Climb the bed." "Look at me." "Come closer." Never reflective, never plaintive, never courtly. Maximum eight words per utterance.
- **Witches speak conditionals and contracts.** "If you eat, you stay." "Bring me three things." "Sleep one hundred years and the prince will come — or not." Always conditional-mood; always carrying an implicit signature line.
- **Stepmothers speak third-person passive.** "The mirror was asked." "The apple was prepared." "The girl was sent into the wood." The grammatical recession IS the Spine.
- **The Snow Queen speaks declarative-cosmic.** "It will be winter again." "He will spell *eternity* and forget you." Andersen-omniscient cadence, never folkloric.
- **Briar Rose does not speak in Series 1.** Sleep-Spine; the silence carries the figure. Series-3 SAINT-shell deployment may unlock a single-line Mode-2 utterance ("*I dreamt of you in passing.*") at 1/1 only.
- **The seven dwarves speak in collective third-person.** "*They had counted seven beds.*" Never named; never individuated dialogue. Numbered I–VII when reference is unavoidable.
- **The Footnoter speaks in marginal-italic Mode-2.** Signs every footnote `— the Footnoter, m.s.` Per Topsy-Register: Pratchett-grade satirical, the dark made visible by understating it. Operative across EK-1 only.
- **Gerda speaks plain prose.** Andersen-direct, child's clarity. The single Mode-2 child-voice in the Pane.
- **Kai speaks in fragments after the splinter.** Single nouns, ice-letters, half-words. The fracture of voice IS the curse.

### Voice-Compliance Gate

Every Set's flavor-text package routes to the LoreVault writer-room for Mosaic-Test verification per the Flavor-Text Doctrine: archetype variety check (no mode/archetype repeated more than 3× per Set), rhythm distinctness (no two consecutive cards in the same meter), voice consistency (per-figure locks above), and PD-source attribution on every quotation. Pratchett-caliber bar applies — if a flavor line could read as filler, it is rewritten or cut.
