# LoreVault Aesthetic Bible — 04 Gothic Horror

**Date:** 2026-04-26
**Status:** Production-binding. Decisions, not options.
**Universe slot:** 04 — Series-1 lock; "The Whitby Ledger" anchor Set; four Sets total in Series 1; **2027 Liberation Drop** strategic anchor.
**Sources:** Dossier 04-gothic-horror.md; Master Synthesis Parts 1–3 (2026-04-25); LoreVault wiki cosmology / shells / flavor / set-roadmap doctrine.
**Single most important date:** **January 1, 2027** — Universal *Dracula* (1931) and *Frankenstein* (1931) enter US Public Domain. The Liberation Drop (Section 13) is pre-built Q3 2026 and ships day-of.

This is the heaviest Universe in the slate. Stoker, Shelley, Stevenson, Leroux, Le Fanu, Polidori sit in deep PD. Universal Pictures sits as the most-litigious studio in the entertainment industry, and four of the most-recognised silhouettes in pop culture (Lugosi, Karloff, Pierce, Lanchester, Lloyd-Webber's Björnson half-mask) are landmines layered on top of those texts. The job of this Bible is to render the texts unmistakably and the silhouettes never — until 2027 cracks two of those landmines open and we ship a press-release-ready expansion in the same hour the cliff turns.

## 1. Universe Identity

**Pane name:** Gothic Horror.
**Pane register (Aesthetic Totalism doctrine):** **Pane-of-Transformation.**
**Lattice colour-class:** **Black** (the Black-register Pane in the five-Universe Series-1 set: Black for Gothic, Crimson-Brown for Baker Street, Forest-Green for Enchanted Kingdom, Ivory-and-Tea-Stain for Wonderland, Marble-and-Olive for Greek).
**Doctrinal one-line:** *The body as betrayer. The mirror that fails. The ledger that survives the wreck.*

The Gothic Horror Pane is the only Series-1 Pane where the central question is not "what is true" (Baker Street), "what is enchanted" (EK), "what is sense" (Wonderland) or "what is fated" (Greek) — it is **what does the body become when something else gets inside it.** Every Spine figure in this Pane has a body that has stopped being theirs:

- Dracula's body has stopped ageing and stopped reflecting.
- The Creature's body was never one body.
- Mina's body has the wafer-burn at the forehead.
- Jekyll's body becomes Hyde's at the cost of a salt.
- Erik's body has the death's-head face beneath the mask.
- Carmilla's body comes back under anagram.
- Lucy's body walks Whitby cemetery as the bloofer-lady before Arthur drives the stake.

The Pane is built on the **Pane-of-Transformation register** — chiaroscuro, candle-amber, single-prop legibility, epistolary fragment-as-card-back. Every card asks: **what came in? what went out? what's left?** This is the doctrinal binding logic across all four Series-1 Sets and the 2027 Liberation Drop.

**Flavor mode default:** Mode-3 (the canon's source texts ARE epistolary — Stoker's Dracula is letters, telegrams, journals, phonograph cylinders; Shelley's Frankenstein is Walton's letters; Stevenson's Jekyll is Utterson's narrative + Lanyon's letter + Jekyll's confession; Leroux's Phantom is the Persian's narrative + Christine's testimony; Le Fanu's Carmilla is Laura's case-history). **Mode-1 (signed letters) ~50%, Mode-3 (epistolary fragment / opera-libretto / ledger) ~35%, Mode-2 (close-third) ~15%.** This is the only Series-1 Pane where mode-3 dominates source-faithful canon — lean into it.

## 3. Biome Library

Thirteen named biomes. Every Moment in this Pane stages in one of these. No biome outside this list ships without an aesthetic-deviation memo.

1. **Castle Dracula, Borgo Pass, Carpathians.** Romanian-folkloric specificity (NOT generic-scary-Eastern-Europe; see §15). Stone-and-pine, snow-line at sundown, the wolves below.
2. **Whitby graveyard cliff + 199 steps + abbey ruin.** Yorkshire chalk-grey, North Sea spray, the Caedmon-stone register. The Pane's spiritual home.
3. **The Demeter, ship-deck and below.** Ship-tar-Black field; mast, lashed-wheel, log-book; the storm at Whitby Pier.
4. **Carfax Abbey, Purfleet.** Ruined-ecclesiastical English Gothic; ivied stone; the bricked-up cask of Transylvanian earth.
5. **Geneva alpine lab + the Mer de Glace.** Friedrich-blue ice and indigo crevasse; the Glacier-Argument biome; Holst-frontispiece-derivable interiors.
6. **Arctic ice-pack and lashed longboat.** Walton's frame; *The Sea of Ice* Friedrich register; the Creature's exit-biome.
7. **Soho laboratory + Cavendish Square consulting room.** Gas-lamp, fog, the locked door, the cane on the cobbles.
8. **Palais Garnier + the lake-cellars beneath.** Opera-velvet, gilt-and-shadow, the catacomb-water indigo, the chandelier-rope cut at the prologue.
9. **Innsmouth-adjacent fishing village.** **Use carefully — this biome touches Lovecraft-Pane territory.** In Gothic Horror this is the *Demeter's-port-of-origin* / *Whitby-adjacent-fishing-village*, NEVER Innsmouth-named. Lovecraft is a separate Pane (Series 3+); the boundary is hard.
10. **Seward's Asylum corridor.** Morgue-teal and ledger-yellow, the Renfield-cell, the keys on the warder's belt.
11. **The Demeter's Wheel.** Single-card biome — the captain lashed, dead, log in coat-pocket.
12. **The Morgue-Teal Corridor.** Asylum-and-mortuary register; the cold institutional Gothic. Cross-tether biome to Dickensian Hollow-Institution (Series 2).
13. **Lucy's Bedroom-Tomb.** Two-state biome — bedroom in life (lace + whitework + lamp), tomb at Hampstead Heath; the same room, twice. The Pane's clearest "what came in / what went out" image.

**Adjacent permitted biomes (Carmilla, Polidori sub-Universe):**
14. Styrian Schloss Karnstein and graveyard.
15. Polidori London-and-Greece (Lord Ruthven travel-circuit; Byron-prototype biome — single-card use only in S1).

**Border with Lovecraft Pane (hard line):** Antarctic-sublime in Gothic = Walton's Arctic, Friedrich-register, **never** *At the Mountains of Madness* register. The geometric-non-Euclidean is Lovecraft. The frozen-grief-of-creation is Gothic.

## 5. Typography

Three coordinated registers, deployable per card-archetype.

**5a. Epistolary-Fragment Serif (primary).** Reference: 1890s Pall Mall Gazette + 1830s Bentley's Standard Novels. Lining numerals, stressed-old-style serif, slight ink-bleed at descender; size hierarchy carries the page. **All Mode-3 flavor text uses this register.** Cards using this register: Letters, Journals, Telegrams, Christine's-locket-script.

**5b. Ship's-Log Clinical Date-Stamp.** Reference: Royal Navy 1880s log-book hand; Stoker's *"3 May. Bistritz."* construction. Sans-serif accent only on date and place; epistolary-serif body. **All Demeter-set Moments use this register.** Cards: Demeter log-pages, Captain's-narrative, port-records.

**5c. Opera-Libretto-Margin.** Reference: Garnier-era 1860s programme-typography; bilingual French/Italian margins (Leroux text); ruled-line above and below. **All Phantom-set Moments use this register.** Cards: Christine's role-cards, the chandelier-fall, Erik's organ.

**Special:**
**5d. Asylum-Ledger Clinical.** Seward's hand on phonograph-transcript; the Archivist's ledger entries (§11). Tighter leading, single-column, page-number bottom-centre. **Used for Lampblacker-Spine cards across all four Sets.**

**Forbidden typography:**
- Universal Pictures studio-block "FRANKENSTEIN"/"DRACULA" title-card type.
- Penny-Dreadful brutalist-serif show-card.
- Hammer-poster slab-serif red.
- Coppola 1992 Bram-Stoker's-Dracula-album hand-drawn calligraphic title.

## 6. Set-Glyph Design

**Universal Pane-glyph: the broken-mirror-shard.** Triangular, single-corner-fracture, no reflection inside. This is the Pane-anchor across all four Sets, all five Series. Render: Ship-tar-Black field, Whitby-cliff-Grey shard, Candle-Amber edge-light, no figure visible inside the silvering. The single most important visual identifier of the Pane.

**Per-Set sub-glyph:**

- **GH-1 The Demeter — Ship's-Wheel-Bound.** Six-spoke wheel with rope-lashing across two adjacent spokes; broken-mirror-shard nests in the hub.
- **GH-2 Arctic Mirror — Stitched-Suture.** Single horizontal incision-line with three sutures; broken-mirror-shard nests above the suture, glacier-blue.
- **GH-3 The Opera House — Full-Mask + Organ-Pipes.** Persian/Venetian full-mask in profile against three vertical organ-pipes; broken-mirror-shard nests where the mouth would be.
- **GH-4 The Two Doctors — Two Vials Crossed.** Jekyll's clear flask and Hyde's amber flask crossed at the neck; broken-mirror-shard nests at the cross-point.

**Liberation-Drop sub-glyph (post-2027):** **The Cliff-Cleared Shard** — the broken-mirror-shard but with the Lugosi widow's-peak silhouette and the Karloff flat-skull silhouette visible inside the silvering, both rendered as expression-discipline opt-ins (see §13).

## 8. Per-Tier Art-Density Rubric

Five tiers; density scales by tier. The density-scale is THE single most important production-discipline lever for this Pane — Gothic Horror cannot afford to render every Common at Ultimate-density (cost) or every Ultimate at Common-density (loss-of-impact).

**Common (atmospheric, near-engraving):**
Single prop OR single shadowed figure on an empty field. Ship-tar-Black + one warm-pole + one cold-pole. Reference: Cruikshank single-figure plate; Tenniel-spareness adapted to Gothic. Examples: *The Letter Posted from Bistritz* (a hand, a letter, an inkwell); *The Bricked Cask* (one cask, brick-line, lamp-shadow); *The Asylum-Key on the Belt* (key-on-leather, no figure).

**Uncommon (atmospheric + secondary element):**
Single figure + one prop + biome-suggestion (one architectural line). Examples: *Renfield at the Window* (figure + window-bars + a fly); *The Wafer in the Lid* (Van Helsing's hand + Communion-tin + lamp-edge).

**Rare (full Moment, restrained):**
Two-figure composition OR single-figure-with-action; biome rendered to mid-depth; chiaroscuro fully deployed. Examples: *Three Drops on the Nightgown* (Mina + window + the three drops); *The Chandelier Falls* (chandelier-mid-fall + audience-blur).

**Legendary (full Moment, atmospheric, source-text-anchored):**
Multi-figure-composition with full biome; the Holst-register or the Doré-register fully unleashed. Source-text-quoted in flavor. Examples: *The Demeter at Whitby Pier* (captain + wheel + dog + log + cliff); *The Glacier Argument* (Victor + Creature + Mer-de-Glace).

**Ultimate (full Coppola-Goya-Friedrich-Caspar deployment):**
Maximum density. Atmospheric AND figural AND iconographic AND cross-Pane-resonant. The card is a painting. Reference-stack includes **Goya's *The Sleep of Reason***, **Friedrich's *The Sea of Ice***, **Fuseli's *The Nightmare***, **Holst 1831**, **Doré's *Inferno***, **Harry Clarke 1919 Poe**. Every Ultimate carries the Pane-anchor broken-mirror glyph somewhere in the field. Examples: *The Castle Wall, Lizard-Fashion* (Jonathan's POV + Dracula descending headfirst + the cliff-drop + the Borgo Pass moonlight); *The Phantom at the Organ* (Erik unmasked + Christine reaching + the lake + Doré-organ-loft).

**Density-discipline rule:** at any Set's 100-card-grid, Common:Uncommon:Rare:Legendary:Ultimate density-cost ratio targets 1:2:5:12:25. Violating this ratio either bloats Set production cost or loses tier-legibility — both kill the Pane's chiaroscuro register.

## 9. Per-Parallel Reservation Rules

LoreVault's five Parallels — ARCANA, AETHER, WITNESS, NEON, and 1/1 — get distinct reservation rules in this Pane.

**ARCANA Parallel (the binding/curse-as-frame):**
- Available **Rare+**.
- Use when the binding (the wafer-burn, the Karnstein curse, Jekyll's salt, the Phantom's Persian-music-charm, Carmilla's anagram-cycle) is the frame of the card, not the action.
- Visual treatment: ornate occult-frame border (Beardsley + Clarke + Friston/Fitzgerald borrows); the figure inside the frame; the curse rendered as marginal-glyphs.
- Cap: 12% of Rare+ cards per Set carry ARCANA Parallel.

**AETHER Parallel (Dracula-cosmic-scale; Carmilla-time-displaced):**
- Available **Legendary+ only**.
- Reserved for Spine-figures operating across centuries. Dracula speaking-from-1462. Carmilla-as-Mircalla. The Creature's "I shall be with you on your wedding-night" prophecy.
- Visual treatment: time-stratified background (multiple eras visible as palimpsest); Dracula's shadow longer than Dracula.
- Cap: 1 AETHER per Legendary tier per Set; 1–2 AETHER at Ultimate per Set.

**WITNESS Parallel (ship's-log-register; epistolary; deposition):**
- Available **all tiers** — this Pane is the Lattice's strongest WITNESS register.
- Visual treatment: card-front rendered AS a log-page, journal-spread, telegram, phonograph-cylinder-transcript. Frame is the document.
- The Demeter Set anchors WITNESS-Parallel doctrine; expect 18–22 of 80 Demeter cards to be WITNESS-Parallel-eligible.
- This is the Pane's *house Parallel* — promote it heavily in the marketing arc.

**NEON Parallel (CYBER-shell only; Frankenstein-laboratory):**
- Available **CYBER-shell-Ultimate only** in S1.
- Reserved for Frankenstein-cluster lab-apparatus cards rendered with electric-arc-and-coil register.
- Visual treatment: galvanic-apparatus + sulphur-yellow accent + the Holst-frontispiece-light; **NOT** Strickfaden 1931 sparking-towers (Universal-locked until 2027).
- Cap: 1 NEON per Set at Ultimate tier.

**1/1 Parallel (the Lampblacker-Mythic):**
- **Exactly 1 per Set.** Mythic-tier; non-reprintable; on-chain singular.
- GH-1's 1/1 = *The Captain's Last Entry* (Demeter-log final-page; the captain's signature breaks mid-letter).
- GH-2's 1/1 = *The Creature on the Ice* (Walton's frame; the Creature speaking over Victor's corpse).
- GH-3's 1/1 = *The Persian's Footnote* (Erik's death-mask; the Daroga's witness-paragraph).
- GH-4's 1/1 = *The Ledger-Keeper Opens the Ledger* (Lampblacker-Spine reveal-card; see §11).
- 2027 Liberation Drop 1/1 = *The Cliff Turns* (a single Moment of the calendar-flip from Dec 31 2026 → Jan 1 2027; the Lugosi silhouette and Karloff silhouette visible for the first time inside LoreVault, both as expression-discipline opt-ins).

## 10. Per-Set Narrative Arc

Four Series-1 Sets × 20 Moments each = 80 Moments. Plus the 2027 Liberation Drop (15 Moments, see §13). Each Set is a complete arc — entered cold and exited resolved — that also threads to the next via the Lampblacker-Spine.

## 15. Cultural Sensitivity Gates

The Gothic Horror canon encodes 19th-century prejudices the Pane must navigate without sanitising the canon out of existence. Every gate below is **production-binding** with named-reviewer requirement before the relevant Set ships.

**1. Stoker's Romanian / Eastern-European stereotypes.** Stoker's *Slovaks "more barbarian than the rest"*; the *"pollution of pure Anglo-Saxon blood"* passages; the Balkans-as-superstition-Other; the racialized-Romani bystanders. Modern Romanian critics (Todorova, Pechko Perspective, The Gothic Wanderer) have argued Stoker's text grafted Western anxieties onto a country he never visited. **LoreVault posture:**
- The Carpathian biome is rendered with Romanian-folkloric specificity — real Bistritz, real Borgo Pass, real Romanian Orthodox iconography (not generic Catholic crucifix-pomp), real Carpathian-shepherd dress. Reference: Constantin Daniel Rosenthal + Nicolae Grigorescu (both d. before 1907, deep PD, Romanian Romantic-painter register).
- **No Romani-coded villains.** The bystanders at Castle Dracula are Slovak peasants in canon; render them as folk-specific, not as caricature. The "Szgany" in Stoker are problematic; LoreVault reframes them as named-individual carters with names from Romani-language-consultant-approved naming-list, depicted as bystanders carrying out a contract, not as Dracula's accomplices-by-blood.
- **Reviewer:** Romanian cultural advisor on retainer pre-GH-1 ship; Romanian-language fluency requirement for any Romanian-text on a card; consultation with the Romani Council of UK or equivalent organization for Szgany-rendering.

**2. Shelley's "monster" dehumanization.** The 1931 film locked the brute-monster reading; modern readings (Mellor, Levao, Hitchcock) restore the Creature's articulacy. **LoreVault posture:**
- The Creature is **the figure who reads Milton on a glacier and argues with his maker.** He speaks. The Glacier Argument is the Pane's most-quoted Moment.
- Holst-register render in S1 — long-haired, beautiful-and-wrong, recoiling Creator. The flat-skull-Karloff joins post-2027 as a **second expression**, not as the default. The shift is doctrinal: 1931 framed the Creature as inarticulate, Shelley framed him as articulate — LoreVault sides with Shelley.
- The 1931 Liberation Drop carries a paired-render card (*The Two Renders, Side by Side* — card #9) that makes the differentiation legible to collectors who have only ever seen Karloff. The Holst-Creature is on the left.
- **Reviewer:** Shelley scholar of choice (Charlotte Gordon / Fiona Sampson level) read of GH-2 narrative arc pre-mint.

**3. Le Fanu's *Carmilla* and the lesbian-vampire trope.** Carmilla 1872 pre-codes a century of "predatory queer woman = monster" cinema (Hammer's *Vampire Lovers* 1970, Argento's *Daughter of Darkness* 1971, etc.). **LoreVault posture:**
- Carmilla is rendered as a tragic-romantic figure with full interiority, not as Hammer-style erotic spectacle. Her relationship with Laura is depicted as **what Le Fanu wrote** — affection, terror, devotion, and the Karnstein curse — not as 20th-century-male-gaze-horror-spectacle.
- The Karnstein-portrait-1698 card frames the curse as the lineage's burden, not Carmilla's sexuality.
- No Hammer-cleavage; no nightgown-décolletage-as-spectacle; the figural rendering is restrained and the horror is the curse.
- **Reviewer:** queer-horror scholar (Carmen Maria Machado, Sarah Waters scholar, or comparable) read of Carmilla cards in GH-4 pre-mint.

**4. The Demeter's Captain.** Stoker's captain is heroic; he lashes himself to the wheel and dies at his post. **LoreVault posture:**
- Render the captain heroically, full-canon. Russian-Orthodox-cross around the neck (canon detail — the captain prays); his hand still on the wheel. The crew's deaths are by Hollow-shell predation, not by ethnic-coded vulnerability.

**5. Antarctic-expedition colonial-era framing.** Walton's 1816 expedition is colonial-explorer Romantic-sublime; Shelley framed it sympathetically but it is still a colonial expedition. **LoreVault posture:**
- Walton's frame is preserved as the canon's frame. The Arctic is rendered as Friedrich-sublime, not as conquest-narrative. There are no indigenous-Arctic-people in Shelley's text — keep it that way; do not invent stand-ins.
- The expedition's hubris is rendered through Walton's letters; his men's near-mutiny is a canon detail that pulls against Walton's ambition. Lean into that detail.

**6. Phantom's deformity.** Erik's death's-head face is grounded in 19th-century beliefs that physical difference encodes moral horror. **LoreVault posture:**

The face beneath is rendered as sorrow (per Leroux's text — the Persian's witness frames Erik as a man who has been hurt, not a brute), not as spectacle. ## 16. Voice Register Rules

The Pane's voice is **operatic-exhaustion + Sorin-Markov-adjacent clinical-detachment** — Mode-1 / Mode-2 / Mode-3 split per §1. Specific voice rules per Spine-figure:

**Dracula speaks in centuries.** Time markers in his dialogue and flavor text scale from years to centuries; he refers to the present in terms of the past. *"Three hundred years ago I sat at this table and there was a different king on the throne."* He never speaks of the future in years. The cliff-turn moment in the Liberation Drop is the only time he is present-tense.

**The Creature speaks in negations.** "I ought to be thy Adam, but I am rather the fallen angel." "I am not what you think I am." "I shall be with you on your wedding-night." Always frames himself by what he is not. Articulate, formal, source-text-syntactical (Shelley wrote him reading Milton; he sounds like it).

**Hyde speaks in present-tense imperatives.** Short, urgent, no past-tense. "Go." "Move." "Out of my way." The cane is the punctuation. Jekyll, by contrast, speaks in long-conditional past-tense — "I had thought, perhaps, that if I had not — " — the voice-contrast IS the transformation.

**Phantom Erik speaks in incomplete sentences interrupted by music.** Em-dashes mid-line. The libretto-margin typography (§5c) carries this. *"Christine, I — listen — I have written this for you — "* The music is what completes him; the silence between is what we render.

**Mina writes.** Not speaks — writes. Her flavor text is **always** in the typewriter-stenography register; her shorthand callouts in the margins. Mode-3 native.

**Van Helsing speaks in Dutch-inflected English.** Stoker's text-syntax: "Friend John, my dear, my poor, my friend John." Possessive-stacking, Dutch-cognate vocabulary (gemeenschap, vrouw, kamer for community, woman, room — single-word imports as flavor accent), polymath-citations (he quotes Latin, Greek, German in single phrases). **Never Van-Sloan-1931-vaudeville-Dutch.**

**Carmilla speaks in slow, affectionate, present-tense drift.** *"You are mine, you shall be mine, you and I are one for ever."* (Le Fanu, direct quote.) Repetition; possessive; tonal calm against horror.

**The Persian (Daroga) writes in mode-3 deposition register.** Witness-statement; first-person; corroborated. Always cites where he was when he saw what he saw.

**Asha Caedmon (Ledger-Keeper) writes in clinical century-scale archive-register.** Mode-3 dominant; epistolary-diarist. Sentences end on the date. Lampblack-residue catalog-style. *"Box of Transylvanian earth: bricked into Carfax cellar, 1897, by Harker per Stoker. Verified at the Carfax dig, 1923. Sample taken; provenance secure. — A.C., Whitby Abbey, 14 October 1927."* This is the Pane's house-voice-of-the-archive.

**Voice-discipline rule:** every flavor text passes through the Pane's voice-register check before mint. Cards quoting source-text directly are flagged "PD-quoted" (Stoker, Shelley, Stevenson, Leroux, Le Fanu, Polidori — all clear); cards in original LoreVault voice are flagged "LoreVault-original" and reviewed by the Pane's writer-room. **Asha Caedmon's voice is NEVER in the source-text quote frame** — she is LoreVault-original always.
