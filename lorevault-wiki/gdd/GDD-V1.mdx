## 1. The Two Nouns

**Every figure you half-remember is a figure with a Spine. The Lampblack is what proves they are.**

LoreVault is an illustrated trading-card multiverse built from public-domain literature. Sherlock Holmes deduces; Persephone descends; the Wolf comes to the door; Alice falls; Dracula is found out. These are not characters borrowed from somewhere else. They are figures held in common, returned to circulation, painted at MTG-mythic density and written at Pratchett caliber. The product is built on one cosmological premise, two proper nouns, and three load-bearing laws — and everything else that ships descends from them.

The first noun is **the Lattice**. The Lattice is the geometry of the multiverse. Every story humans have ever told that survived into the public domain is a single, fragile, finite reality cut from older glass — what we call a *Pane*. The character you know is a figure caught mid-gesture inside one Pane. The same figure exists in other Panes, frozen in different gestures, wearing different chassis (different *Shells*), but always recognisably itself. There is no Earth-Prime. There is no canonical Sherlock. Each shell is equally real. The Lattice is the architecture inside which Holmes-PRIME and Holmes-CYBER and Holmes-SAINT all exist as siblings rather than as adaptations of a master copy.

The second noun is **Lampblack**. Lampblack is the residue burned off when one Pane brushes against another. A drop of Lampblack on a Pane is what lets a Victorian Sherlock recognise a robot Sherlock as kin and not as costume. It is the Lattice's only physics. We will not invent a second. Lampblack is what the gold-cased pocket-watch carries when it appears in three Sets across two Universes. It is what the deerstalker silhouette carries when it surfaces, hatless-and-faceless, in the Black Forest at dusk. It is what the half-tooth carries when the Wolf returns in HOLLOW shell after the burn. Lampblack is residue in origin but force in effect — a pressure one Pane exerts on its nearest counterparts through accumulated contact.

Three laws govern. The first, **the Spine** — every figure has a non-negotiable beat-list they must enact, in every shell, or they stop being that figure. Sherlock's Spine: *deduces, falls, returns, refuses domesticity.* Alice's Spine: *crosses a threshold, distrusts size, accepts nonsense as law, walks back unchanged in form but changed in seeing.* Dracula's Spine: *is found out.* The second, **the Lampblack** — cross-Pane recognition is communicated through visible residue: a recurring symbol, prop, line, or scar that survives translation. The third, **Panes Touch, They Do Not Merge** — there is no canonical universe and no Crisis-style collapse. Two Panes can leave Lampblack on each other; they cannot be unified. This forecloses the *Crisis on Infinite Earths* failure mode where the multiverse periodically collapses into one timeline. That move is structurally banned in the Lattice, in writing, before any Set ships.

The cosmology reveals on a fixed ratio — the Iceberg, 1:2:4 (see §7) — and is enforced by a Canon Council that overrides the founder. The Three Laws are *felt*, never *stated*, until at least Year 3, and even then we confirm at most one per year on the Law the community has already named. The Lattice's first principle is that every fact named narrows the multiverse, and every visible-edge-of-the-unsaid enlarges it.

The collector who pulls a Reichenbach card and loses a morning to the Watson casebook fragment that came folded inside it — that collector is the audience. The Dracula-Daily reader who, on May 3, opens their inbox and finds 365 letters waiting in canonical-date order — that reader is the audience. The Sherlockian completist who notices a chessboard pattern in the rocks at the Falls and is never told why — only that the same chessboard appeared three Sets earlier on Moriarty's floor — that completist is the audience. The gambler who pulls the Apex Ultimate on Day 0 of BS-4 *Reichenbach* and watches the Council confirm, live on stream, that the smudge on the card is a residue they will not name — that gambler is the audience too. Two nouns hold the geometry. Three laws hold the physics. Everything below — the Shells, the Parallels, the Tiers, the packs, the ratio, the Council — descends from those five facts and adds nothing the cosmology did not already imply.

## 2. The Five Panes

Series 1 ships five permanent Universes, locked, and they are deliberately the five most-recognisable Panes in the public-domain canon. Recognition is the point. Lampblack only resonates against figures the audience already knew before they got here. The first job of a multiverse property is to be legible at the threshold; the second is to deepen on return. The five Panes share a common chassis (eight Shells, five Parallels, four Tiers) but each speaks a different literature, holds a different cosmological role, and renders in a palette no other Pane is allowed to use. Below, one row per Universe.

### 2.1 Baker Street — the Pane of Argument

Baker Street is the Lattice's prosecuting attorney. Its cosmological role is to test reality against logic and force it to confess. Every other Pane has gods, monsters, fairies, or fates; this one has a man with a magnifying lens, a friend with a service revolver, and a method that pretends the universe is solvable. Holmes is the Lattice's epistemologist — the figure who arrives at every other Pane to ask, *How do you know?* The palette is locked to seven anchor colors: **Gaslight Sepia** `#C9A26B`, **Persian-Slipper Amber** `#B5651D`, **Fog-Blue** `#6B8FA8`, **Deduction-Sharp White** `#F4F0E6`, **Reichenbach Black** `#1B1814`, **Tobacco-Stain Brown** `#5C3A1E`, and **Blood-Pearl** `#8B1A1A` (used sparingly — never more than 5% of any composition). The biome library is gas-lamp London by way of Sidney Paget's 1891 *Strand* plates, Frederic Dorr Steele's 1903 *Collier's* covers, and Frank Wiles's 1914 cinematic framing — 221B's sitting room with the Persian slipper on the fender, the V.R. wall pocked with bullet-holes, the Lyceum Theatre's third pillar, the Reichenbach cataract, the Sussex bee-cottage. The iconography is the magnifying lens tilted thirty degrees, the curved calabash (always credited to Steele/Gillette, never to Doyle, because Doyle never wrote it), the Inverness cape (Paget invention, 1893), the Stradivarius and bow, the gold-cased pocket-watch with its visible engraving, the V.R. monogram itself. The voice register is forensic-confessional, Watson chronicling: *"It was the November of '94. I had not understood him at the time."* The Lampblacker-Spine for Baker Street is **Hester Quill**, the female clerk whose Spencerian-cipher initials appear in the margins of every Holmes case — the figure who keeps every case retrievable, the archive that survives Reichenbach. (See §9.)

### 2.2 Enchanted Kingdom — the Pane of Inheritance

The Enchanted Kingdom is the Pane through which generational consequence flows. It is where the Lattice keeps its bloodlines, its curses, and its unpaid debts. Stepmothers, wolves, and witches in this Pane carry full Spines because every act here is *justified by its own logic* — survival logic, hunger logic, the morality of what gets eaten. The wolf is right to be hungry; the stepmother is right to be jealous; the cards are not asking you to forgive either of them, only to recognise them. The register is **Black/Green** — black for the iron, the soot, the closed eye behind glass; green for the moss, the foxglove, the pine-needle floor of the Schwarzwald, the witch's herb-rope. The palette anchors on **Forest-green** `#2C3A23`, **Apple-red oxblood** `#7A1F1F` (never candy-red), **Iron-black** `#0F1112`, **Ash-grey** `#9C9388`, **Candle-amber** `#D9A648`, **Glass-blue** `#7FA9C4` for Snow Queen ice, and **Foxglove-violet** `#553A6B`. Pre-Disney darkness is mandatory; the 1812 Grimm is the textual seam, not the 1857 sanitized seventh edition. The visual register stack is Arthur Rackham 1909, Edmund Dulac 1911, Kay Nielsen 1914+1925, Franz Jüttner 1905 (the primary Snow White anchor, never Disney's), and Hermann Vogel 1894 — registers chosen because they are deep PD, Disney-immune, and structurally darker than the post-1937 register the audience has been laminated with. The biome library runs from the Deep Black Forest through the Witch's Cottage gingerbread to the Glass Mountain to the Frozen Palace to the Bramble-Thicket to the Bluebeard Seven-Doors corridor. The iconography is the poisoned apple bitten once, the spinning wheel and forged spindle, the breadcrumb trail, the red cloak, the glass slipper (Perrault wrote *verre*, not *vair*; the fur theory is folklore-wrong), the iron key with its blood-drop that will not wipe off. The voice is bone-rhyme Greven-il-Vec adjacent — wolves speak imperatives ("*Open the door.*"), witches speak conditionals ("*If you eat, you stay.*"), stepmothers speak third-person passive ("*The girl was sent.*"). The Lampblacker-Spine is **The Charcoal-Burner's Daughter**, the woman who gathers lampblack soot from the kiln-walls and grinds it into the ink with which she draws the Forest's maps — the only mapmaker the Pane has, and the literal reason maps don't survive in canon fairy tales.

### 2.3 Wonderland — the Pane of Categorical Failure

Wonderland is the Lattice's fault zone. The categories that hold every other Universe together — *size*, *time*, *cause*, *identity* — stop holding here. Other Panes obey local canonical physics; Wonderland obeys nothing. Its only law is that every law is suspect, and the suspicion is dispatched in courtroom register, by figures who use jurisprudence to certify that nothing can be certified. The Pane is rendered Discworld-adjacent in its self-awareness but never Pratchett-cosplay; it is wit, not joke, and the difference is load-bearing. The base substrate is John Tenniel ink-line on cream rag-paper. Color is *added*, never *replaces* line. The primary triad is locked: **Pinafore-blue** `#1F3A6B` (worn only by Alice; appears nowhere else in the deck), **Apron-white** `#F4EFE3` (cream rag-paper), **Rose-red** `#B8252A` (the painted rose, the heart-suit pip, the jam-tart filling — carmine with Tenniel-engraver depth, never Disney scarlet, never Burton lacquer). Secondaries include **Tea-stain amber** `#9E6A2C`, **Cheshire-pink-not-magenta** `#D77A8E` (the single most-policed colour in the deck — Disney 1951 magenta-fuchsia is the line we will not cross), and **Looking-Glass silver** `#9DA4A8` (the Lampblack-traversal surface). The biome library runs from the Rabbit-Hole's vertical descent shaft through the Long Hall of Doors, the Pool of Tears (the only biome where ink-line dissolves at the water-surface, marking a Lampblack threshold), the Caterpillar's Mushroom-Pillar, the Infinite Tea-Table (the clock reads six o'clock and only six o'clock, no second-hand), the Courtroom-of-Cards, the Looking-Glass Parlor, the Chessboard-Landscape, the Tulgey Wood, and the Snark-Island where the Boojum is implied by absence and never drawn. The voice is Liddell-logic — every flavor line is a syllogism whose premises don't hold but whose form is impeccable. *The verdict came first. The trial took longer, because the verdict had to be defended.* No exclamation marks; the Queen shouts only via dashes. No puns, ever. The Lampblacker-Spine is **The Glass-Polisher**, voiced as the **Court Recorder of Hearts** — the figure who cleans the Looking-Glass and is the only one who knows it's two-way, the maintenance-of-reality character whose work is literally keeping the Pane-surface clear.

### 2.4 Gothic Horror — the Pane of Transformation

Gothic Horror holds the Lattice's body-horror archive. Every figure here has a body that has stopped being theirs — Dracula's no longer ages and no longer reflects; the Creature's was never one body to begin with; Mina's carries the wafer-burn at the forehead; Jekyll's becomes Hyde's at the cost of a salt; Erik's has the death's-head face beneath the mask; Carmilla's comes back under anagram; Lucy's walks the Whitby cemetery as the bloofer-lady before Arthur drives the stake. The single doctrinal line is: *the body as betrayer; the mirror that fails; the ledger that survives the wreck*. The palette is the Lattice's only true chiaroscuro Pane, with **Ship-tar Black** `#0A0908` as field, **Whitby-cliff Grey** `#4A4E51` as the cold pole, **Geneva-glacier Blue** `#6E8FA3` as the alpine cold, **Carfax Bronze-tarnish** `#6F4F1F` as metalwork, **Candle-Amber** `#D9A24E` as the warm pole, **Demeter Crimson** `#7B1E1E` as the strict accent, and **Sulphur-Yellow** `#C9B43A` reserved for the Creature's eye and the elixir phial only. The visual register stack is Theodor von Holst's 1831 *Frankenstein* frontispiece (the long-haired Creature recoiling on slab — *not* the 1931 Karloff flat-skull, which is locked until January 1, 2027), Caspar David Friedrich for *Sea of Ice* and *Wanderer Above the Sea of Fog*, Henry Fuseli's 1781 *Nightmare*, Aubrey Beardsley's black-line-on-white decadence, Sidney Sime's ink-and-wash phantasmagoria, Harry Clarke's 1919 stained-glass Poe, and Doré's *Inferno* steel-engraved chiaroscuro. The biome library runs Castle Dracula, Whitby graveyard cliff and 199 steps, the Demeter, Carfax Abbey, Geneva alpine lab and the Mer de Glace, the Walton Arctic, the Soho laboratory, the Palais Garnier and the lake-cellars, Seward's Asylum corridor, Lucy's Bedroom-Tomb (a two-state biome — bedroom in life, tomb at Hampstead, the same room twice). The Lampblacker-Spine is **Asha Caedmon**, the Ledger-Keeper of Whitby — the female survivor pulled from the *Demeter* wreck as a child in 1897, raised in the Whitby Abbey ruin, trained in classics and stenography (a Mina-lineage), runs a private archive of the supernatural across the Stoker / Shelley / Stevenson / Leroux / Le Fanu canon. Her bound ledger has entries from every Pane. She is the Pane's binding figure.

### 2.5 Greek Myth — the Pane of Fate

Greek Myth is the Pane where the gods themselves carry the debt. Sherlock pays — London does not. The Big Bad Wolf pays — the Forest does not. Here, Zeus owes Demeter a daughter; Hades owes Persephone six months; Prometheus owes the eagle a liver every dawn; Sisyphus owes the stone the slope, in perpetuity. Every Greek Myth Moment must read as the inside of a ledger that began before the figure was born and continues after. The register splits **White and Red** — White for the marble-and-libation register (temple, oracle, chiton, olive-branch, chorus in third-person plural); Red for the pottery-and-blood register (Achaean spear, sacrificial bowl, pomegranate-juice, the wound that does not close). White is the Pane in repose; Red is the Pane in the act. Every Set must pull from both. The palette anchors on **Bronze (Riace patina)** `#7A6A3F`, **Wine-dark sea** `#3B2A4A`, **Pottery black figure-ground** `#1A1410`, **Pottery red ground** `#A14B2C`, **Persephone-pomegranate-red** `#7E0F1B`, **Chiton-white chalk-marble** `#F1ECE0`, **Mediterranean sun-gold** `#E6C26E`, **Tyrian purple deep** `#3E1C46`, **Ash-grey asphodel** `#A8A199`, and **Dawn-rose** `#D9A8A0` (Homer's rose-fingered, eastern horizon only — never the figure). The visual register stack is Bouguereau, Waterhouse, Burne-Jones (the Perseus cycle 1875–1898), Bernini's marble photo-real (*Apollo and Daphne*, *Rape of Proserpina*), Caravaggio's 1597 *Medusa* shield, Frederic Leighton, Evelyn De Morgan, Cabanel, classical statuary (Riace bronzes, Doryphoros, Discobolus, Belvedere Apollo, Laocoön, Nike of Samothrace), and Athenian red- and black-figure pottery (Berlin Painter, Euphronios, Exekias). The voice is bronze-tongue, dactylic-hexameter Hesiod-cadence; every flavor line scans long-short-short long-short-short six feet before it ships. *Six were the seeds, and the year was a husband and not a husband.* The banned word is *destiny* — we use *moira*, the share-out, the portion-cut, the thread already spun. The Lampblacker-Spine is **Melaina**, the Pythia's Apprentice — the only first-person-singular voice on the Pane, the priestess-in-training learning the chamber, the gas, the silence between. *She breathes the smoke. I learn what to carry.*

## 2.6 Navigation Architecture — The Bottom-Tab Floor Plan

The product navigates on four tabs. No more. The tab-bar is a floor plan, not a menu — it names the four things a collector does here, in the order they do them, without burying any of them behind a hamburger or a secondary drawer.

The four tabs are **Home** (house icon), **Collection** (grid/binder icon), **Open** (pack icon), and **Profile** (person icon). There is no Market tab in the tab-bar. The marketplace is secondary navigation, reachable from a tap on any greyed-out gap slot in the sticker-book (see §2.7 for the gap-tap flow). Putting Market in the tab-bar would make the product feel like a trading desk the moment a new collector lands. It does not belong at floor level.

**Home** carries the hero drop card — the active Set's marquee Moment, displayed full-art — plus a countdown to the next storefront refresh and a scrollable feed of recently activated collectors: who pulled what, without dollar amounts, without P&L. It is the product's front window, not its dashboard.

**Collection** opens the sticker-book view by default, set to the current active Series. Progress reads as a fraction — "12 of 20" — above the grid. Cards the collector owns render full-art; cards they don't own render as grey silhouettes, reversed-out, present enough to register as shapes but absent enough to pull. Tapping a grey silhouette enters the buy flow for that Moment. This is the Zeigarnik mechanism — the gap does the work.

**Open** is the pack-picker landing. It is the ritual entry point: choose your Series, choose your Pane, choose your tier, confirm, rip. The full pack-rip ritual plays here, full-screen at every breakpoint, no modal, no sidebar (see §7.5 for the mobile-first constraint and §6.6 for the picker flow detail).

**Profile** holds the collector's showcase — their highest-rarity pulls arranged as a gallery — plus their parallel-completion progress across all active Sets and any serial-number-set pursuits they have flagged.

The bottom-tab bar respects iOS safe-area-inset-bottom on all devices. Active state renders in the Universe-accent color with a filled icon and a visible label. Inactive tabs render in muted grey with a visible label — never icon-only, because icon-only requires the collector to have already memorised the map. On desktop the tab-bar collapses to a left-sidebar nav carrying the same four items in the same order.

The following are banned from the tab-bar for the duration of Series 1: Market, Explore, Discover, Search. The marketplace is secondary navigation. Search is a utility, not a destination. Explore and Discover are names that belong to a different product category.

## 3. The Eight Shells

Eight Shells are locked across Series 1 through Series 6. Each is a chassis for the figure — *who they are*, not *how the painting reads*. Shells and Parallels (§4) are orthogonal axes. New Shells require unanimous Canon Council approval and a shipped justification doc — including for the founder.

**PRIME** is the figure in their canonical period, faithfully rendered, Spine fully present, no relocation. Victorian Sherlock; medieval Wolf; Bram Stoker's 1897 Dracula. PRIME is mandatory baseline — every figure ships PRIME first. Series 1 ships PRIME-only across all five Universes. The art density at PRIME is the canonical-illustrator inheritance: Paget cross-hatched ink for Baker Street, Rackham/Nielsen tipped-in plate for Enchanted Kingdom, Tenniel wood-engraving for Wonderland, Holst-Friedrich-Fuseli chiaroscuro for Gothic Horror, Bouguereau-Bernini-Waterhouse for Greek Myth. Scarcity-wise, PRIME is the Lattice's most abundant Shell (every figure gets one); what the collector feels when they pull a PRIME is *recognition* — the figure restored to its source register, before any reinterpretation has been laid over it.

**COMMON** is the Tier inside PRIME, not a Shell — clarification, since the audience often confuses Tier and Shell. (See §5 for the Tier rubric.) But the *sensation* of Common — the everyday-scene, the lived-in moment, the figure at rest — is what every Shell builds atop. The collector pulls a Common and feels an entry: a door opening, a ledger entry made.

**RARE** — same caveat. Rare is a Tier, not a Shell. Inside any Shell, Rare is where *the named scene* lives — the moment a fan recites by heart. The collector pulls a Rare and feels recognition deepen: *I know this moment*. Inside PRIME's eight Shells, Rares are the figure-with-environment compositions, the dyad cards, the relational-triad Mrs. Hudson tolerating the V.R. wall, the Cottingley investigation Mode-3 photograph, the pomegranate-seed close-up.

Eight Shells answer the question *who is the figure*. Four Tiers answer *how rare is the artifact*. The two axes are orthogonal and must not be conflated — a PRIME Holmes can ship at any Tier; an APEX pull is a Tier outcome, not a Shell. What follows is the eight-Shell roster; the Tier rubric is §5.

**EPIC** in the felt-experience sense is the moment a Rare card carries an A12 Echo — when the flavor on a card you hold quotes, verbatim or inverted, the flavor on a card you held last month, and the collector reads them as a chord. Epic is not a rarity; it is what happens when Lampblack lands. The art at Epic-feel level is full Symbolist density: Burne-Jones plus Waterhouse plus Caravaggio chiaroscuro on a single figure; the chiton edge gold-leafed; the eye carrying the line that carries the case across two Sets.

**LEGENDARY** is the figure-in-defining-moment. Two per Set, 250 minted, ~3.5% of Set supply. The collector who pulls a Legendary feels they have caught a load-bearing Moment — *The Hound on the Moor*, *Apple at the Door (Snow-Black variant)*, *The Mad Tea-Party*, *The Glacier Argument*, *Apollo and Daphne*. Density is full jewel-tone plate, gold-leaf accents permitted, scale-contrast and chiaroscuro and moment-of-decision framing all stacked. The collector hangs the Legendary in their gallery; it is the canvas-print they want enlarged.

**MYTHIC** is the felt-experience name for what the spec calls Ultimate. One per Set, 25 minted, the defining Moment of the arc — *The Last Bow*, *The Footnoter Reveals Themselves*, *Alice Steps Through the Looking-Glass*, *The Ledger-Keeper Opens the Ledger*, *The Mirror Held*. Density is maximum: Drew Struzan compositional gravity, Caspar David Friedrich Rückenfigur, Jeremy Jarvis card-art finish — all relevant cinematic techniques permitted. The collector who pulls a Mythic loses a morning. The Mythic is the founding plate of the Set.

**APEX** is the 1/1 ONE-OFF — one per Set, no exceptions, mint of one, the Pane's marquee. Bespoke per commission; no template; the Frame is the card, the doctrine *this card has no frame; it is the frame*. The collector who pulls an Apex pack at $999 and lands the ONE-OFF doesn't tweet about it — they screenshot it, then they sit with it. ### 3.2 The Six Mid-Shells — Collector Registers

**CYBER** is the near-future high-tech low-life chassis — rust-and-amber server-rooms, cyberdeck consoles, cooling-vents cast in the shape of recognisable props from the figure's PRIME period. The Spine-residue requirement: the deduction beat, the descent, the refusal of domesticity must survive translation into the silicon chassis — a Holmes who cannot deduce is not Holmes-CYBER, he is a costume. The collector who pulls a CYBER feels like finding out the detective still solves crimes in the rubble.

**MODERN** is today's urban world in Veep-grey realism — no blue Belstaff, no glamorised contemporary styling, the figure rendered in the register of a 2026 Sunday supplement photograph. The Spine-residue requirement: the figure's foundational tension — the thing they cannot stop doing even when the world has changed around them — must read in a single glance, with no period costume to carry it. The collector who pulls a MODERN feels the figure arrive uninvited into the present tense.

**AETHER** is the mythic-cosmic register, the Olympians' native home — aurora-cosmic particulate, nebula-bleed at the corners, the figure at its furthest remove from the biographical and closest to the archetypal. The Spine-residue requirement: at least one canonical Spine-beat must be implied in the cosmic register, or the figure dissolves into decoration. The collector who pulls an AETHER feels the scale of the figure's debt — how long the debt has been running before any human story began.

**HOLLOW** is the post-apocalyptic shell, Gothic Horror's native register — ruined biomes, ash-grey skies, the figure in survival posture with PRIME-era props eroded but recognisable. The Spine-residue requirement: the defining wound, the defining tool, or the defining relationship of the PRIME-period must be visible in some degraded form — Dracula's mirror still fails to reflect, even in the ruin. The collector who pulls a HOLLOW feels the version of the story where the wrong person survived.

**MIRROR** is the identity-inverted shell — gender- or role-flipped, but only when the inversion is answered by a Spine-event, never as costume. The Spine-residue requirement is the strictest of the six: every Spine-beat from the PRIME must be present in the inversion, or the card fails the commissioning check. A female Sherlock who does not deduce, fall, and refuse domesticity is not Holmes-MIRROR. The collector who pulls a MIRROR feels recognition without comfort — the sense that the figure was always capable of being this.

**DREAM** is the psychedelic-spiritual shell, Wonderland's home register — dissolution of category, impossible geometry, the figure operating in the logic of its own sleep. The Spine-residue requirement: for Universes where Dream is permitted, at least one Spine-beat must survive the categorical dissolution, anchoring the figure's identity against the register's tendency to absorb everything into pattern. Dream is structurally banned for Holmes by Spine — Holmes's Spine requires deduction, and deduction requires stable categories. The collector who pulls a DREAM feels the figure unguarded, which is the rarest pull of the six.

## 5. The Four Tiers + Art-Density Rubric

Four Tiers, locked: **I (Common)**, **II (Rare)**, **III (Legendary)**, **VOID (Ultimate)**. The fourth carries a special name in the Council's internal lexicon — VOID — because the Ultimate is the card the Set is built around, the gravitational center, the place where the Pane's theme collapses into a single image. Every Set ships 12 Common + 5 Rare + 2 Legendary + 1 Ultimate = 20 Moments. Mint counts: Common 15,000 (12 × 15,000 = 180,000 Common cards per Set), Rare 2,500 (5 × 2,500 = 12,500), Legendary 250 (2 × 250 = 500), Ultimate 25 (1 × 25 = 25). Total base supply per Set: 193,025 cards. With Parallels layered on top of Rare+ Moments, the catalog grows to 1,200 distinct (Moment × Tier × Parallel) entries for Series 1.

**Tier I — Common** is the breadcrumbs of the Pane. Twelve per Set; 75% of supply; ~70% pack-slot rate. Intended archetype: the everyday scene, the supporting character, the lived-in moment. Density rubric: single-figure-or-prop, plain composition, low atmosphere, ≤4 cinematic techniques (chiaroscuro, scale-contrast, alvinizing, moment-of-decision-framing, cross-hatch overload, etc.). Background fill ≤30%; figure count 1; typography weight is the Universe nameplate face at weight 500. The frame is plain ledger-paper, single 1px hairline at the bottom in muted-amber. The Common is the floor. Nothing below it ships.

**Tier II — Rare** is the named scenes. Five per Set; 21% of supply; ~25% pack-slot rate. Intended archetype: the moment a fan recites by heart. Density rubric: figure-with-environment OR figure-with-prop, three-tone palette including one full anchor color, ≤8 cinematic techniques. Background fill 50–60%; figure count 1–2 (relational dyad permitted at this Tier); typography weight 600 with 1px hairline border. The frame is linen-weave with 12×12 filigree corner-ticks at all four interior corners and a warm-gold outer glow. The Rare is where the Pane's relational geometry first appears — the dyad cards, the antagonist-shadow cards, the Mode-3 artifact cards.

**Tier III — Legendary** is the plates the fans frame. Two per Set; 3.5% of supply; ~4% pack-slot rate. Intended archetype: second-tier iconic, the moments fans remember by name. Density rubric: full jewel-tone plate; full palette deployed; gold-leaf accents permitted; ≤14 cinematic techniques. Background fill 70–85%; figure count 2–4 (central plus chorus/antagonist/witness); typography weight 700 with 1px solid bronze border and inset shadow. The frame is silk plus engraved bronze with 24×24 Beardsley-line corner ornaments and a hard outer glow. The Legendary is the canvas-print the collector hangs.

**Tier VOID — Ultimate** is the founding plate. One per Set; 0.5% of supply; ~1% pack-slot rate (the rest of the Apex-pack mathematics is in §6). Intended archetype: the defining Moment of the arc — *The Last Bow*, *The Glacier Argument*, *The Bow Bent*, *The Reichenbach Plunge*, *The Mirror Held*. Density rubric: full cinematic deployment, all relevant techniques permitted, Drew Struzan compositional gravity + Caspar David Friedrich Rückenfigur + Jeremy Jarvis card-art finish ceiling. Background fill 90%+; figure count 3–6 with a clear central figure; typography weight 700 small-caps in engraved-gilt fill on Whistler-velvet inset. The frame is engraved gilt on Whistler-velvet plus a 1px outermost Lampblack hairline; corners carry full Beardsley/Burne-Jones leaf-and-vine work; the Lampblack-cosmic-cascade glow drifts at 2-second period. Always-on. The Ultimate's flavor-text is the artifact — the casebook page, the *Strand* opener, the Whitby Ledger, the bee-cottage letter, the Bernini-tableau caption. Mode-2 (the Lattice's own voice breaking through, unattributed) is reserved for the Ultimate exclusively.

The Universe-tier 1/1 — Wonderland's *Alice Steps Through the Looking-Glass* — is structurally above VOID. It exists as a single card, mint-one-of-one, in which the mirror-Pane shows Baker Street, Enchanted Kingdom, Gothic Horror, and Greek Myth as faint silhouettes inside the silver. It is the Pane's structural admission that the Lattice is real. It is the only Universe-tier card in Series 1.

## 7. The Iceberg Doctrine

The Iceberg is the rate-limiter that prevents the Lattice from collapsing into soup. The principle: at any moment in LoreVault's life, the audience can perceive three layers of the cosmology — the **Surface** (what is shipped and explained), the **Echo** (what is shipped and unexplained), and the **Deep** (what is referenced but never shown). The ratio is fixed: for every unit of Surface, ship two units of Echo and gesture toward four units of Deep. **1:2:4.** This is the doctrine; this is the audit. The Council enforces it quarterly. Any quarter that breaches is failed; Sets hold until correction. Per the V2 Rewrite, Series 1 caps at 12 Echo + 24 Deep across 12 Sets.

The 1:2:4 ratio is not a marketing flourish. It is the mechanism by which the Lattice stays bigger than what we have shown. Every Surface fact narrows; every visible-edge-of-the-unsaid enlarges. Tolkien's appendices (vast unwritten history glimpsed through footnotes), Le Guin's anthropological reticence (the Hainish are not explained), Miyazaki's no-exposition entry (Totoro is never explained, and the absence is the asset) — these are the lineages we descend from. The opposite lineage — Pottermore-style expansion-by-decree, "actually, Sherlock has always been left-handed" press releases, the Wiki-as-truth — is what we structurally refuse.

A worked example: a Baker Street EPIC card from BS-2 *The Cases* — say, *The Cottingley Investigation* (Rare, Mode-3 photograph artifact, A12 Echo). What the collector sees first, on the 375px-mobile viewport, in the first second of glance: the figure (Holmes seated at a deal-table, lens raised over a small albumen print), the title (*The Cottingley Investigation*), the flavor (a single Watson sentence in serif italic: *"Holmes returned the photograph at length. 'It is the camera, not the fairies, that requires investigation.' — casebook, March 1921"*). That is the Surface — explained, named, attributed.

What is one click deeper, on the lore-note row: the casebook fragment, the tether-trail. *The albumen print is real; Doyle was the one investigated, by his own friends. The lens is the same lens that would not yield the Reichenbach silhouette twenty-eight years earlier.* That is the Echo — visible, present, unexplained. The collector reads it twice. The collector does not get told why the lens is the same lens.

What is in the vault — the third click, the wiki page, the long-form reading — is the Deep. The albumen print's serial number matches the *Strand* archive's index card from BS-1 Card 6 *The Casebook in Repose*. The lens the photograph was examined under is rendered in three other cards across BS-1 and BS-3, each time with the same hairline crack at the eight-o'clock position. Hester Quill's marginalia at the bottom of the Cottingley plate is the same Spencerian cipher *HQ* that watermarks 6 of the 20 BS-1 cards. The whole thing tethers, three layers down, to the Cheshire-grin-residue in the EK Black Forest canopy of EK-2 *The Wolf at Seven Doors* Card 5 — where the smile-shape in the negative space between branches is the same smile-shape in the fog at Reichenbach. None of this is explained. The Council does not confirm it. The collector who finds it posts it on the Canon Theory Wall. The Council reads the Theory Wall and, three years from now, may promote one Echo to Surface in Series 4. May. One Law per year, starting Year 3, on whichever Law the community has already named.

The 1:2:4 ratio is what makes the multiverse legible at thumbnail and inexhaustible at gallery. The Surface gives the new collector a footing. The Echo gives the returning collector a chord. The Deep gives the completist a forever.

## 7.5 The 375px Constraint — Mobile-First Without Apology

The primary viewport is 375px. Every surface in LoreVault is designed at 375px first — iPhone SE, iPhone 14 Mini, mid-range Android — then scaled up to tablet and desktop. There is no desktop-first fallback. There is no "we'll fix it on mobile later." The 375px grid is the design authority. Desktop is a consequence of that authority, not its origin.

Layout rules, non-negotiable:

Card detail renders the full-width image at 2:3 aspect ratio, flush to the screen edges. Flavor text appears below the image in a single-column reading column. Metadata chips — serial, tier, shell, parallel, set — appear in a single scrollable horizontal row beneath the lore-note. No metadata chip wraps to a second line. If the chip row overflows, it scrolls. It does not wrap.

Collection / sticker-book renders a 2-column grid at 375px. At 768px it expands to 3 columns. The gap between cells is 8px at 375px, 12px at 768px. No 1-column mobile fallback — the sticker-book's visual logic requires at least 2 columns to register as a grid, and the grid is the product's primary retention surface.

Pack ritual plays full-screen at every breakpoint. No modal. No sidebar. No card-in-modal. The ritual takes the screen because the ritual is the moment. Interrupting the ritual to preserve a nav element is the wrong trade.

Storefront renders a single-column card list at 375px — one card per row, full-width, image at 1:1 crop for list density — and expands to a 2-column grid at 640px.

Bottom-tab nav is fixed to the bottom of the viewport, 44px touch targets per Apple HIG minimum, safe-area-inset-bottom respected on all iOS devices. The bar does not overlap content. Content scrolls above it.

No horizontal scroll anywhere in the product. If a surface requires horizontal scroll to function, the surface is redesigned before it ships. This is not aspirational — it is a hard constraint enforced at design review.

Text sizing: body copy minimum 16px, no exceptions for secondary labels or metadata. Headings scale from 24px at 375px to 48px at desktop using fluid typography (`clamp`). No type below 14px ships.

Images: WebP only, lazy-loaded on all non-critical surfaces, with `sizes` attributes tuned to the 375px primary viewport. The 375px image is the default fetch; larger images are served only when the viewport confirms it.

## 8. The Voice

Voice is the second floor of the property; only art ranks above it on the Yardsticks. The bar is unambiguous: *Mark-Rosewater-survives-edit-pass for ≥80% of texts*. Each line must sit alongside the best Magic: The Gathering quotes and not flinch. Five registers, one per Universe. Twelve archetypes across all five. The Mosaic Test, applied at every Set lock.

**The five registers:**

1. **Bone-rhyme** (Enchanted Kingdom). Two-stress alternating lines, end-rhymed on closed syllables — bone-words, iron-words, blood-words. Border Ballads in cadence, Grimm-tradition minus the songbook softening. *"Three loaves she gave the road. The road ate two and kept the third for winter."* Wolves speak in imperatives ("*Open the door.*"). Witches speak in conditionals ("*If you eat, you stay.*"). Stepmothers speak in third-person passive. The grammatical recession is the Spine.

2. **Dry Wit** (Wonderland). Liddell-logic — every flavor line is a syllogism whose premises don't hold but whose form is impeccable. Three-beat structure: statement, restatement, snap. Or: question, answer, second question that breaks the answer. *"The Hatter poured tea. The cup did not fill. The cup had agreed not to."* Italics on categorical words — *time*, *size*, *court*, *rule*, *name*, *self*, *place* — to mark them as suspect. No exclamation marks. No puns. Ever.

3. **Elegy** (Baker Street, on the Watson axis). Forensic-confessional, retrospective-recognition, written-after-the-fact reflection. *"It was the November of '94. I had not understood him at the time."* The em-dash for the turn — never the colon. Watson does not exclaim; Watson observes. Two-clause structure: observation comma deduction. Period-precise adjectives (a watch is *gold-cased*, never *gleaming*). Emotion implied by the omission of the obvious — Watson never says he is afraid; he says the fire was lit and they did not approach it.

4. **Menace** (Gothic Horror). Operatic-exhaustion, Sorin-Markov-adjacent clinical detachment. *"For three hundred years I have been the answer to a question no one knew to ask. — Dracula"* Time markers in centuries. The Creature speaks in negations ("*I ought to be thy Adam, but I am rather the fallen angel*"). Hyde speaks in present-tense imperatives ("*Go.* *Move.*"). The Phantom speaks in incomplete sentences interrupted by music. Mina writes — never speaks. Asha Caedmon's sentences end on the date.

5. **Wonder** (Greek Myth). Bronze-tongue, dactylic-hexameter Hesiod-cadence, chorus-narrator dominant. *"…and so the third spring. She ate the seeds because she was hungry, and was hungry because she had eaten the seeds."* Capitalised abstract nouns when gods are doing the noun's work. Ordinal time markers (*the third spring, the seventh libation*). Ellipsis-openings encouraged. The banned word is *destiny* — we use *moira*. The chorus speaks in third-person plural; mortals in third person; only Melaina (the Lampblacker-Spine) speaks first-person singular.

**The twelve archetypes** (per Flavor-Text Doctrine §3): A1 The Deduction, A2 The Prophecy, A3 The Lover's Letter, A4 The Curse, A5 The Riddle / Apple-Test, A6 The Logbook, A7 The Hesiod Catalog, A8 The Pamphlet / Newspaper Cutting, A9 The Wisecrack-That-Lands, A10 The Last Word, A11 The Anonymous Witness, A12 The Echo. Sets must mix at least four archetypes per twenty cards.

Five sample lines, one per register:

> *Bone-rhyme:* "He had seven and a half teeth. The half was the one that mattered."

> *Dry Wit:* "The verdict came first. The trial took longer, because the verdict had to be defended."

> *Elegy:* "Holmes had not slept. He had only become more accurate. — Watson, casebook, March 1895"

> *Menace:* "3.14 AM. The lamp went down twice and I did not light it the second time. — Mina H., journal, fragment"

> *Wonder:* "He chose glory over length. The choice had been made for him in the cradle; he discovered it on the field. — chorus, on Achilles"

**The Mosaic Test.** At every Set lock, the Voice Lead reads all 20 cards' flavor in sequence, blind to art. Six checks: (1) Could a fan reading these twenty quotes blind, without art, name the Universe? Pass at ≥18 of 20. (2) Are at least four archetypes represented? (3) Does any quote paraphrase its own mechanic? Cut. (4) Does any quote violate the Forbidden Register (modern slang, AI-tells, joke punchlines, generic fantasy mush, fourth-wall breaches)? Rewrite. (5) Does at least one quote in the Set Echo a prior Set (A12)? If not, consider whether one card should carry an Echo. (6) Read the twenty quotes aloud. Do any two sound identical in rhythm? If yes, rewrite one. The Set is a *mosaic*, not a *wallpaper*.

What passes the Mosaic Test: *"He had told me, the night before, that the cataract was loud enough to drown a smaller voice than his. I had not understood him at the time. — Watson, casebook, May 1891."* (Twenty-five words; A1 Deduction; em-dash turn; period-precise; emotion implied by omission; Lampblack — the *cataract* — survives translation to the BS-2 CYBER Echo two Sets later.)

What fails: *"Sherlock used his powers of deduction to figure out the mystery, frankly!"* (Modern intensifier; paraphrases mechanic; names "deduction" instead of doing it; exclamation mark; Watson would not say *frankly*; the Lattice does not know it is being read.)

## 9. The Five Lampblacker-Spines

Per V2 Decision 11 — the Penny Dreadful diagnostic extends beyond Gothic Horror to all five Series-1 Universes. Public-domain figures alone don't sustain emotional gravity (too pre-loaded, too distributed across rival media). Each Universe needs an original load-bearing center. **Decision: commission all five simultaneously for Series 2 (no staggering — staggered Spines create uneven Universe gravity).** The five names are locked, and they are the keystone of LoreVault's IP-original moat.

**Hester Quill** (Baker Street). A female clerk-archivist whose Spencerian-cipher initials appear in the margins of every Holmes case — a Doyle-canon-adjacent figure who, in LoreVault canon, is the figure who keeps every case retrievable. Watson is the witness; Hester is the archive. The surname encodes the writing-implement function (the quill-as-tool); the H matches the Hudson initial-rhyme of 221B. She survives Reichenbach. She edits Watson's casebook into the *Strand* layout, checks the Mode-3 artifacts for plausibility, files Moriarty's posthumous letters (one of which is addressed to her by Spencerian-cipher initials *HQ*), and is the in-world reason the Pane is *retrievable*. **The Charcoal-Burner's Daughter** (Enchanted Kingdom). A second-generation child of a forest charcoal-burner, apprenticed not to her father but to the *unmaking* of her father's labor — she gathers lampblack soot from the kiln-walls and grinds it into ink, with which she draws the Forest's maps. **The Glass-Polisher / Court Recorder of Hearts** (Wonderland). She has no name in canon; in Wonderland she is *the Glass-Polisher* (the figure who cleans the Looking-Glass and is the only one who knows it's two-way), and signs as *the Court Recorder of Hearts* in WL-3 and across all four Sets in flavor-text. She uses second-person plural (*the court will note*); second-person singular is reserved for the Queen as threat. She appears partially in seven Moments across the four Sets, never as figure, always as byline. In the Universe 1-of-1 *Alice Steps Through the Looking-Glass*, she is the hand just leaving the silver-frame in the bottom-margin, implied not depicted. Spine line: *"I keep the surface clear. What looks back is not my doing."*

**Asha Caedmon** (Gothic Horror). The Ledger-Keeper of Whitby. Pulled from the *Demeter* wreck at Whitby Pier (1897) as a child; raised in the Whitby Abbey ruin under an unnamed Yorkshire schoolmistress; trained in classics, modern languages, and stenography (a Mina-lineage). By the time of the Pane's later Sets she is in her mid-thirties, lives at the abbey-cliff cottage, and runs a private archive of the supernatural across the Stoker / Shelley / Stevenson / Leroux / Le Fanu canon. She is the only character who appears in every Gothic Set without being from any source novel. Her bound ledger is kept in a Carfax-bronze-tarnish lock-box. Her cross-Pane cameos: a single Common-tier card in Baker Street ("*A Letter from Whitby Arrives at 221B*"), a Rare-tier card in Enchanted Kingdom ("*The Cartographer Trades Pages with the Ledger-Keeper*"), a single Echo-card in Wonderland ("*A Ledger-Page Falls Through the Looking-Glass*"). The Asha-Cartographer trade is the Lattice's first explicit Spine-to-Spine cross-Pane handshake. Spine line: *"I came up from the wreck of the Demeter. I have been writing it down ever since."*

**Melaina** (Greek Myth). The Pythia's Apprentice. *Melaina* is Greek for *the dark one*, literally encoding the Lampblack-residue function in the name. She breathes the smoke-room with the Pythia, learns what to carry from the chamber to the supplicant. She is never the answer — only the route the answer takes. The only first-person-singular voice on the Pane; the Pane's epistolary-witness, the Greek-Myth equivalent of Watson and Hester and the Cartographer and the Glass-Polisher and Asha. In her final arc (GM-4 Common #20, paired with the 1/1) she takes the Pythia's seat for the first time and breathes the smoke. Spine line: *"She breathes the smoke. I learn what to carry. The supplicant hears her; is also hearing me."*

## 10. The Cross-Tether Map

A tether is a cross-Universe Lampblack residue trail — a recurring symbol, prop, line, or scar that surfaces across Panes. Tethers are the proof of the Lattice. They are how the completist knows two cards are siblings without being told. Series 1 ships near-zero crossover (1–2 Echoes maximum across the entire Series), per Master Synthesis Part 3 §10. The marquee tether — held to Series 2 / 3 — is **Auguste Dupin ↔ Sherlock Holmes: the Method-Lineage.** Poe wrote Dupin in 1841; Doyle wrote Holmes in 1887; in *A Study in Scarlet* Holmes dismisses Dupin, and Doyle is on record about the anxiety-of-influence wink. This is the single richest cross-Pane spine in the corpus. The Series-1 teaser is BS-4 Common Card 9 *The Footnote at the Margin*, which contains a Mode-3 letter-artifact addressed to *HQ* — Hester Quill — and signed by Dupin. The Series-2 / 3 American Gothic Pane will host the full Surface card *Dupin Sends a Card* — a marquee Legendary Moment that anchors the dual-Universe launch.

How a tether appears across cards: the same object, line, or gesture surfaces in two Sets in two Universes, usually a season apart, with no explanation. The first appearance is canonical (the lens, the cane, the gold-cased pocket-watch). The second is the Echo (the same lens, in a different Pane, with a hairline crack at the same eight-o'clock position). The collector who owns both reads them as a chord. The collector who notices the chord and posts about it is the funnel. The Council, watching the Theory Wall, reads which tethers the community names — and at quarterly audit may promote one Echo from Series N to Surface in Series N+1.

Why tethers matter for the completist: they convert a card-binder from a list into a constellation. A Reichenbach Ultimate is one card; a Reichenbach Ultimate held alongside a Greek Myth card in which Achilles dies on a parallel cliff (the *Achilles at Reichenbach* tether) is two cards that read as a paired death-on-cliff, two Panes' worth of Lampblack converging on a single canonical gesture. The completist hunts tethers because the tether is the moment the multiverse becomes one room.

Two more canonical tether examples (held to Series 2 / 3, teased in Series 1):

**Persephone ↔ Snow White: the bitten-fruit tether.** The pomegranate seed (Greek) and the bitten apple (Enchanted Kingdom) are the same fruit-of-binding refracted. Both are eaten by a girl who descends; both bind her to a cycle; both are the hinge of the year. Series-1 teaser: a single Common-tier Echo card placed near the pre-Series-2 bridge — *"The Pomegranate Seed (Persephone) ↔ The Apple Bite (Snow White)"* — the only card in Series 1 that openly stages the cross-Pane single-bite-of-binding pair. Series-3 ships the Convergence card *Snow White Asks Persephone About Apples*.

**The Mirror.** Four Panes carry mirror-residue: EK's Magic Mirror, Wonderland's Looking-Glass, Greek Perseus's polished shield, Gothic Dracula's mirror that fails to reflect. Per Master Synthesis Part 3 §1.2, this is the most-recurring Echo-Object in the Lattice. Series 3 will ship a four-Pane Convergence-Cathedral card; in Series 1 the four mirrors appear as residues in their own Sets without being named as a set.

The **Cheshire-grin** is the marquee Pattern-9 / Deep / Lampblack-Trade residue. No figure crosses; only residue. Cheshire stays in Wonderland; the *grin-as-fog-shape*, *grin-as-canopy-gap*, *grin-as-bed-shadow-curl*, *grin-as-asphodel-horizon-line* gestures at Cheshire without rendering Cheshire-as-figure on any cross-Pane card. Series-1 deployment: a faint smile-shape in the negative space — a recognition-tier residue, never a Cheshire-as-figure cameo — on one card per other Universe (Baker Street: Reichenbach fog; EK: Black-Forest canopy; Gothic Horror: shadow under Mina's bed; Greek Myth: asphodel-meadow horizon). Recognition-only; never explained.

The fifth canonical tether class — *the Children of Lir doctrine* — is the doctrine of Lampblack-residue creatures that surface in every Universe in cross-tether Sets. The deerstalker silhouette is *the* Baker-Street Lampblack-residue object — the cap, never the man, never the face. It surfaces in EK, Greek, Gothic Horror, and Wonderland as a faint silhouette across Series 2+.

## 14. The Differentiator

LoreVault has no Universes Beyond. Wizards of the Coast's *Universes Beyond* — Final Fantasy in Magic, Lord of the Rings in Magic, Doctor Who in Magic — was a $200M+ success precisely because it didn't pretend to be canon. Magic's bible disallows it. Magic's primary world is theirs; the Beyond is licensed. We do not need a Beyond. **Public-domain is our moat, not our compromise.** Every figure on every card in every Set across every Series is in the public domain at point of ship. We do not pay licensing. We do not litigate use. We do not negotiate appearances. The figures are already archetypes; that is *why* they survived; that is *why* they are public-domain. Holmes is a public-domain figure because the canon outlived its copyright. Snow White is a public-domain figure because the tale outlived its tellers. Persephone is a public-domain figure because nobody has *ever* owned her.

The combinatorics are infinite. Twenty Moments per Set times five Universes per Series times eight Shells times five Parallels times four Tiers times the Lampblacker-Spine cross-Pane cameos — the catalog is structurally unboundable. We will ship at one Set per month for ten years and not run out of canonical figures, much less Lampblack residues.

**The negative space — what we are NOT.** No battle stats on literary figures. Holmes is not Charm-60 / Intelligence-59 / Mystery-85 / Legend-95 / Power-45. Holmes deduces. The 4-Layer Council (Silhouette / Props / Gesture / Spine) replaces the stat-block. No burn-for-XP destructive CTA. No card is consumed for points. No XP path. The future forge ties to Lampblack-tether discovery, not stat-grind. No "Estimated Value History −69.6%" red-trend price chart. No 30-day or 90-day price chart. No Smart-money pill. No comparable-floor-price strip. No movers-strip. No Comfy / Bulk / Need / Own marketplace tabs. No Top-Shot trader-vernacular sub-tabs. No daily-streak gambler chrome. No FOMO countdown timer on the card itself. No "log in 7 days for X." No prestige-grind ribbon.

**The positive affirmation.** Object, not asset. Every card on `/v3/card/:cardId` reads as an object first — the painted FLUX render at 2:3, the serif pullquote, the lore-note, the Lampblack smudge in the corner, the elemental serif at the lower-right, the figure-name at the top — and only after the object has been seen does the metadata-chip-row appear (serial · tier · shell · parallel · set), and only after the metadata does the optional Council toggle appear (collapsed by default). The card detail page reads as an *object* before it reads as an asset. The word *floor* never appears on a card detail page. The phrase *Smart money* never appears anywhere.

LoreVault is what happens when you take Top Shot's set/tier/parallel discipline, strip out everything that reads as trader-screen, and replace it with what trading cards looked like *before* they were also stocks. The literary register is what Top Shot doesn't have and structurally cannot acquire — its IP licensing forbids it; ours requires it.

### 14.2 The Phase-2 Marketplace — Trading Without the Stock Screen

The marketplace ships in Phase 2. It is not in Series 1 launch. The decision is doctrinal, not logistical: a marketplace on Day 1 signals that the product expects cards to be traded before they have been read. Series 1 is for reading. The marketplace opens when the binder has had time to fill.

The listing flow is four steps: seller sets an ask price, reviews the listing preview, confirms, and the listing goes live. The listing itself shows six things, in this order: the card thumbnail at 1:1 crop, the serial number, the seller's display name, the listing age, a scarcity reminder stating how many of this Moment are currently listed ("4 of 25 listed"), and the lore-note — one flavor line from the card, in the card's native register.

The lore-note appears above the price. This is the layout rule that does the doctrinal work: the object appears before the asset. The seller is selling a thing that has a voice; the buyer is buying a thing that has a voice; the price is the third fact, not the first.

Each card shows its owner trail — the chain of prior owners as display names, scrollable, with no wallet addresses visible. The platform facilitates P2P trades; there are no gas fees and no wallet connection required. Custody is held by the platform and transferred on-platform. The owner trail is a provenance record, not a flex ledger.

The following are banned on every marketplace surface: P&L charts, "Estimated Value" trend lines, "Smart money" pills, red-number change indicators, Comfy / Bulk / Movers / Need strips, Top Shot sub-tab vocabulary, 7-day volume, market cap, and average-sale aggregation. What is permitted on a listing: the ask price, the listing date, the seller's display name, and the scarcity reminder. That is sufficient information to make a decision. Anything added beyond it is trader-vernacular, and trader-vernacular is banned.

## 15. The Anti-Vision

Six anti-patterns, banned from the rebirth. Specific. Enumerated. Their absence is the product's identity as much as their absence is the product's discipline.

**Charm-60 / battle stats on literary figures.** The canonical specimen of v1's failure: Holmes given a stat-block with Power 45 / Intelligence 59 / Mystery 85 / Legend 95 / Charm 60. The reader-harm is that the stat-block reads Holmes as a unit in a Battle game, not as a figure in a literature. The literary reader closes the tab the moment they see Charm-60 on Holmes; the gambler-collector reader does not pull a Premium pack twice for a figure who has been reduced to numerical values they could compare on a screen. The alternative is the **4-Layer Council**: Silhouette · Props · Gesture · Spine — collapsed by default on the card detail page, expandable by user toggle, never auto-shown. Each row carries a shell-color left-rule. The Council answers a different question — *is this the figure?* — that the stat-block cannot.

**Burn-for-XP destructive CTA.** v1 carried a "burn this card for XP" path on Common cards, with the Common consumed permanently in exchange for a small bump on a Legacy Score ladder. The reader-harm is that it converts a card from an object to a disposable resource — and Decision 9 (no Pane is ever decanonized) means *every card sold remains canon to its own Pane forever*. Burn-for-XP violates the Trust Contract before the contract has been signed. The alternative is the future **Lampblack-Tether Discovery** mechanic (Series 2+) — collectors who hold both halves of a tether earn an unlock that surfaces a new lore-page or a new Pane-detail, but the cards themselves remain. The forge ties to discovery, not stat-grind.

**"Estimated Value −69.6%" / red-trend price chart.** v1 carried a 30/90-day price chart on every card detail page, complete with red-trend lines and percentage-loss callouts. The reader-harm is that it converts a card from an object to a position — and a position with a visible loss is a position the reader is ashamed to own. The alternative is **no price on card-detail.** Trading lives on a dedicated view (not Series 1). The card detail page shows the card. The vault routes are by Universe / Shell / Parallel / Elemental / Narrator — collector-curation, not trader-positioning. The owner trail shows display-names, never wallet addresses, and shows current avatar plus history scroll, never a P&L number.

**Comfy / Bulk / Movers / Smart-money chrome.** v1 carried four marketplace tabs — Need / Own / Comfy / Bulk — plus a Movers strip showing which cards "Smart money is accumulating." The reader-harm is that *every word* in that interface is trader-vernacular pulled from poker-table and stock-screener language; none of it is the language a literature reader uses. The alternative is the **vault facets**: `/v3/vault/universe/{slug}`, `/v3/vault/shell/{slug}`, `/v3/vault/parallel/{slug}`, `/v3/vault/elemental/{slug}`, `/v3/vault/narrator/{slug}`. Each facet is a way of looking at the binder; none of them is a way of looking at the market.

**Daily-streak gambler chrome.** v1 carried the 13-module retention dashboard home — daily-login strip, calendar-grid with 7-day flame, *DON'T BREAK YOUR STREAK* push, FOMO Pulse, season-countdown ticking by the second. The reader-harm is that all of it is built to manufacture anxiety in the user; the moment the user misses a day, the streak resets and the user is punished. The alternative is the **reading-pact ticket** (§11) — three actions, any one earns a ticket, tickets accumulate up to 5 max in a wallet, do not expire, cap. The pact is a *reader's reward*, not a *gambler's daily-quota*. The user can do nothing for 4 days and lose nothing; on day 5 they are back at the cap.

**Top-Shot trader-vernacular sub-tabs.** v1 carried sub-tabs on every card detail labelled *Listings / 7D Volume / Market Cap / Avg Sale*, plus portfolio P&L tiles on the home dashboard. The reader-harm is total: the entire interface organizes itself around the question *what is the asset doing?* rather than *what is the object?* The alternative is the **lore-first card detail** — pullquote above the lore-note above the tether row above the metadata chips above the optional Council toggle above the optional owner trail. The lore-note appears above the price on every listing in Phase 2, when the marketplace finally ships. The word *floor* never appears on a card detail page. The phrase *Smart money* never appears anywhere. The card-detail page's identity is built as much by what it refuses to be as by what it ships.

A useful test for any proposed feature, line, or shipped artifact: *does this read as one of the six?* If yes, hold it. If unclear, hold it.
